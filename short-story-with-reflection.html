14. V. Reflection A short story is The elements of a story are: The setting is the: The characters are Plot is made up of :​


Short story-A short story is a fully developed story which is shorter than a novel and longer than a fable. It typically takes just a single sitting for reading. Short Story focuses on the incidents bigger or smaller and evokes strong feelings from its readers. A short story often has a few characters in the plot.

Elements of a story are:

Setting

Character

Point of View

Conflict

Plot

Theme

Setting-A setting (or backdrop) is the time and geographic location within a narrative, either nonfiction or fiction. It is a literary element. The setting initiates the main backdrop and mood for a story.

Character-A character is a person, animal, being, creature, or thing in a story. Writers use characters to perform the actions and speak dialogue, moving the story along a plot line. A story can have only one character (protagonist) and still be a complete story.

Plot-In a literary work, film, story or other narrative, the plot is the sequence of events where each affects the next one through the principle of cause-and-effect. The causal events of a plot can be thought of as a series of events linked by the connector "and so".

The Little Prince

The story of The Little Prince simply teaches us one thing, children are really the purest men on earth. They seem so innocent yet they have learned many things when their innocence and curiosity knocks. Their open mindedness and willingness to explore the world makes little children more learned than the adults which appears to be a hopeless and narrow minded one. This scenario seem to be very common when I wish to get something that my parents would not agree nor approve for me to have. I used to think that they are beginning to lack some sort of understanding and acting strangely on things that I consider so simple. For instance, spending the night on a friend's house is something that we would always argue with since they do not approve of it. Another part of the story that seem remarkable for me is the lines of the fox which says "It is only with the heart that one can see rightly: what is essential is invisible to the eye.” Often, things unseen remains unseen because men are not using their hearts to see rightly. For example, the love we have for our parents and their love for us are sometimes ignored because either parties are trying not to see the manifestations thereof or are sometimes in denial since they are unable to compensate the feeling. What happens is that people begin to pretend, assume, and later regret since they were not able to express or give back a portion of their heart for those who are waiting for them to express that love. In the story, the narrator never show concern for the little prince except he knew that the prince wanted to go back home. He only search for his missing body after learning that he is no longer with him. He assumes that he had returned to his planet but he wanted to hear anything from him so he told the readers to notify him in case they have seen the little prince which seem for me to be too late to return the concern the little prince had shown him in the desert.


29. Read the story of the "Parable of the Prodigal Son," and write a short reflection about it.​


Answer:

In the story, a father has two sons. The younger son asks for his portion of inheritance from his father, who grants his son's request. This son, however, is prodigal (i.e., wasteful and extravagant), thus squandering his fortune and eventually becoming destitute. As consequence, he now must return home empty-handed and intend to beg his father to accept him back as a servant. To the son's surprise, he is not scorned by his father but is welcomed back with celebration and a welcoming party. Envious, the older son refuses to participate in the festivities. The father tells the older son: "you are ever with me, and all that I have is yours, but thy younger brother was lost and now he is found."
