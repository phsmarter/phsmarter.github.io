Playing online games has a negative effect on the academic performance of students, as measured by their class participation, due to the distraction and time management challenges that online games pose.

This hypothesis suggests that playing online games is associated with lower levels of academic achievement, as measured by class participation. The hypothesis also proposes a possible explanation for this relationship, namely that online games may be distracting and negatively impact time management skills, which in turn affects a student's ability to participate actively in class.

To test this hypothesis, researchers could conduct a study that compares the academic performance and class participation of students who play online games regularly with those who do not. The study could also include additional variables, such as the amount of time spent playing online games, to further explore the relationship between online gaming and academic performance.

Independent Variable

Independent variables are variables that are not influenced by other variables in the study. These are variables that does not depend on another variable and is not changed by any factors the study is trying to measure. These are the variables that are changed or controlled in a scientific research to test its impact on the dependent variables. These are often denoted by x in an experiment or graph.
