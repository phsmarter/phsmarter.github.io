16. 1. What are the effects of bullying on students during class?2. What are the factors that contribute to bullying in the school?3. In what way we could stop preventing bullying in school or public places?​


1. The effects of bullying on students during class can be detrimental to their physical, emotional, and academic well-being. Students who are bullied may experience fear, anxiety, depression, and low self-esteem, which can negatively impact their ability to concentrate, participate, and learn in class. Bullying can also lead to physical injuries, absenteeism, and a hostile classroom environment, affecting the overall classroom dynamics and disrupting the learning process for all students involved.

2. There are several factors that contribute to bullying in schools, including:

a) Social dynamics: Power imbalances, social hierarchies, and peer pressure can contribute to bullying. Students who perceive themselves as more powerful or popular may bully others to exert dominance or gain social status.

b) Lack of awareness and education: Lack of awareness about the consequences and impact of bullying, as well as limited education on conflict resolution and social skills, can contribute to the occurrence of bullying in schools.

c) Home environment: Factors such as parenting styles, family dynamics, and exposure to violence at home or in the community can influence a student's behavior and contribute to their engagement in bullying behavior.

d) School culture and climate: An unsupportive school culture and climate, where bullying is tolerated, ignored, or not addressed, can perpetuate the occurrence of bullying in schools.

3. To effectively prevent bullying in schools or public places, a multi-faceted approach is needed. Here are some ways to stop and prevent bullying:

a) Education and awareness: Educate students, teachers, parents, and the community about the consequences and impact of bullying. Raise awareness about the importance of respect, empathy, and kindness.

b) Positive school culture and climate: Foster a positive and inclusive school culture and climate where bullying is not tolerated. Establish clear anti-bullying policies, procedures, and consequences for those who engage in bullying behavior.

c) Support systems: Provide support systems for victims of bullying, such as counseling services, peer support groups, and safe reporting mechanisms.

d) Social-emotional learning: Incorporate social-emotional learning programs in schools to promote empathy, conflict resolution skills, and positive relationships among students.

e) Collaboration and community involvement: Involve parents, teachers, school administrators, law enforcement, and the community in efforts to prevent bullying. Collaborate with local organizations and agencies to raise awareness and implement anti-bullying initiatives.

f) Early intervention: Intervene early when bullying is identified, and provide appropriate consequences for those engaging in bullying behavior.

Bullying in Elementary Schools: Its Causes and Effects on Students By MS. Afroz Jan Abstract: Bullying is an everlasting problem in the lives of school kids. It is a problem that affects all students, the person who bully, those who are victims, and the persons who witnesses to interpersonal violence. Bullying may include verbal and physical assaults, threats, 'jokes' or language, mockery and criticizing, insulting behavior and facial expressions. These factors work individually, or collectively, to contribute to a child's likelihood of bullying. Bullying is difficult to eradicate in schools because it is so often effectively used by students. Teachers as professionals have to deal with the consequences of pupil-on-pupil bullying. The impact of prejudice-related bullying should not be underestimated. Bullying must be recognized, understood and taken seriously. The major objectives of this study were (i)To understand the nature of bullying (ii)To find out the causes of bullying (iii) To find out impact of pupil-on-pupil bullying on students 'learning. 10 teachers and 40 students were constituted as sample of the study. Collected data were analyzed through SPSS by running frequencies, mean score, percentage and standard deviation.
