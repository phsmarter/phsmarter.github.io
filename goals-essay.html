6. what is the main goal of an argumentative essay?​


The purpose of an argumentative essay is to organize and present your well-reasoned conclusions in order to persuade the audience to accept—or at least seriously consider—your point of view.

About Argumentative Essay

Argumentative writing is typically used in academic writing in the humanities and social sciences. Argumentative essays generally require extensive research, which may involve gathering data and examining and interpreting various forms of evidence. In general, the author presents his argument at the beginning of the essay and lays out the evidence supporting their thesis in the body.

Argumentative essays generally have a serious and impartial tone. They are based on disclosing evidence and avoiding emotional appeals in favor of facts and statements. By definition, an argumentative essay focuses on a controversial topic that inspires debate and disagreement. However, they avoid using hyperbolic or impassioned language and opinions. Additionally, argumentative essays are almost always written in the third person and avoid subjective voices.

Argumentative essay structure

Argumentative essays tend to follow a general structure featuring an introduction containing the thesis, a body paragraph containing the evidence, and a conclusion outlining broader implications. While they don't need to follow this structure strictly, it is the most effective structure for clear and compelling arguments, and most essays in the genre are organized this way.

17. What is SMART Goals? In a Essay form


Answer:

SMART (Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, and Time-Bound) goals are established using a specific set of criteria that ensures your objectives are attainable within a certain time frame.

Examples of SMART goals

Measurable: Success can be measured by the number of applications, interviews and job offers. Achievable: The goal setter will have the appropriate degree for the job. Relevant: The goal setter is planning to get a job in the education industry after getting an education degree.

"S" stands for specific. Make your goal or objective as specific as possible. ...

"M" stands for measurable. Include a unit of measure in your goal. ...

"A" stands for achievable. Be realistic. ...

"R" stands for realistic. ...

"T" stands for time-bound.
