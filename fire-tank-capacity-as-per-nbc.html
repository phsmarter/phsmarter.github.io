25. a tank is 1/6 full of gasoline when two liters are added the tank becomes 1/4 full what is the total capacity of the tank ?​


Answer:

→2/10

Step-by-step explanation:

1 +1 = 26+4=10you just have to add the denominatorbut if the denominator is the same you have to copy the denominator,

REACT ,FOLLOW,RATE,BRAINLIEST

Assume that the total capacity of the tank is represented by x liters. the tank initially had 1/6 of gasoline. this can be represented by (1/6)x.

When the two liters of gasoline got added to the tank, the capacity of the tank became 1/4 full. This can represented by the expression (1/4)x.

With all this knowledge, we can solve for x.

(1/6)x + 2 liters = (1/4)x

2 liters = (1/4)x - (1/6)x

Find the common denominator for the right side fraction(s):

2 liters = (3/12)x - (2/12)x

2 liters = (1/12)x

24 = x

Therefore, the total capacity of the tank is 24 liters.
