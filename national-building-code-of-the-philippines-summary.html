20. How does Nationalism influences nation-building in the Philippines?


Nationalism has played a significant role in the Philippine nation-building process since the country gained independence from Spain in 1898. The idea of Filipino nationalism emerged during the Spanish colonial period when Filipinos began to collectively resist Spanish rule and seek independence.

Nationalism in the Philippines has influenced nation-building in several ways, including:

1. Formation of a national identity: Nationalism has played a crucial role in forming a sense of national identity among Filipinos. It has helped to create a shared sense of history, culture, and tradition that binds Filipinos together as a nation. Nationalism has been used to express the idea of a united Philippines, free from the colonial powers.

2. Promotion of democracy and freedom: Nationalism in the Philippines has been closely linked to the promotion of democracy and freedom. It has played a key role in challenging authoritarian regimes and advocating for democratic governance.

3. Development of the economy: Nationalism has also had a significant impact on the country's economic development. It has been used to promote the growth of domestic industries, protect local businesses from foreign competition, and promote economic self-sufficiency.

4. Advocacy for territorial integrity: Through nationalism, Filipinos have been able to assert their sovereignty and territorial integrity. It has played a critical role in the country's territorial disputes with neighboring countries over disputed islands in the South China Sea.

In conclusion, nationalism in the Philippines has influenced nation-building in several ways by giving the Filipinos a sense of shared identity, promoting democracy and freedom, aiding in the country's economic progress, and advocating for territorial integrity.

Answer:

Nationalism has played a significant role in the nation-building process in the Philippines. Since the country's independence from colonial powers, nationalism has been used as a unifying force to rally the people towards a common goal, which is to build a strong and independent nation.

Firstly, nationalism promotes a sense of unity and pride among Filipinos. It encourages the people to embrace their cultural heritage, values, and history. This sense of pride and belongingness creates a strong bond among Filipinos, which is essential to nation-building. It fosters a feeling of community and shared responsibility towards the country's development.

Secondly, nationalism promotes the idea of self-determination. It emphasizes the need for Filipinos to take control of their own destiny and not to be dependent on other countries for their progress. This idea has led to the development of policies and programs that prioritize the country's interests and promote economic self-sufficiency.

Thirdly, nationalism has been used to challenge existing power structures and promote social justice. It has been used to fight against oppression and inequality, which are seen as obstacles to nation-building. Nationalist movements in the Philippines have been instrumental in fighting for the rights of marginalized groups and promoting a more equitable society.

In conclusion, nationalism has played an important role in shaping the nation-building process in the Philippines. It has promoted a sense of unity and pride among Filipinos, encouraged self-determination and challenged existing power structures. These values have been critical in shaping the country's political, economic, and social development.

26. Explain th background of the national code of the philippines.


Answer:

What is the National Building Code of the Philippines?

Building codes serve as the foundation guidelines of architects, builders, developers, and engineers in designing and constructing for any safe and secure built environment. Rather than serving as legally binding regulations, building codes serve as prototypes for legal jurisdictions to refer to when composing new statutes.

They shall become laws upon being formally enacted by the appropriate governmental or private authority. However, it is important to note that the strength of their jurisdictions heavily influences the competitiveness of builders and suppliers alike.

Since they primarily relate to the proper planning, construction, and occupancy of buildings and structures, building codes, like the National Building Code of the Philippines, are also highly vital in protecting public safety, health, and general welfare.

Is the Philippines bound for Another Version of the National Building Code?

Nearing two decades since its latest revision in 2005, both lawmakers and experts in the industry classify the present building code as “outdated” and “antiquated.” The growing trends in the construction industry, modern urban planning, population growth, and disaster risk reduction further strengthened the concerns from both Houses of the legislative branch to amend the present building code into something more in-line with the country’s current status in sustainability issues, infrastructure, and building technology, professional politics, among others.

At present, two legislative bills were filed dealing with the strengthened implementation of the building code. Albeit both with different approaches.
