2. identify and define at least five personal identification techniques​


Answer:

1. Knowledge-based authentication (KBA) - This technique involves asking the person being identified to provide specific information, such as a personal identification number (PIN) or a security question answer, that only they would know.

2. Token-based authentication - This technique involves the use of a physical token, such as a key fob or smart card, that the person being identified must possess in order to gain access.

3. Biometric authentication - This technique involves the use of a person's unique physical characteristics, such as a fingerprint, facial recognition, iris scan, or voice recognition, to confirm their identity.

4. Two-factor authentication - This technique involves the use of two different types of identification, such as a password and a fingerprint, to confirm a person's identity.

5. Public Key Infrastructure (PKI) - This technique involves the use of digital certificates and a public key infrastructure (PKI) to authenticate the identity of a person or device.

Family therapy considers the psychological and behavioral disorders of a member of a group as a symptom of the dysfunction of the group - generally the family (parents, siblings, etc.). Its goal is to bring a different reading of the problem and to give back to the family their functioning abilities.

Answer:

1. What is the literary device that uses the letter sounds of a word to imitate the natural sound emitted from an object or action?

Answer: Onomatopoeia

2. What coordinating conjunction is used in the sentence: "The Twin Towers were destroyed by terrorists, who thought they could break the US apart, but instead, this tragedy brought the US people together"?

Answer: "but"

3. Which word in the sentence "My mother always reminds me that I must practice honesty in all endeavors" has an affix?

Answer: "endeavors" (the suffix is "-s")

4. What was the name of the cyclone that tore the roof off Aung San Suu Kyi's house in 2008?

Answer: The statement does not provide the name of the cyclone.

5. What does the underlined word "glean" mean in the sentence "Ruth went to the field of Boaz to glean with the other widows"?

Answer: To gather leftover crops or grain from a field after the harvest.

6. What are the two basic kinds of morphemes, which are minimal units of meaning?

Answer: Free morphemes and bound morphemes.

7. What technique is being described in the statement: "A famous person, or someone you respect or like, claims that something is good or advertises or promotes a product or idea, even though they are not an expert"?

Answer: Appeal to authority or celebrity endorsement.

8. What literary device is being used in the sentence "On the Fourth of July, the air is filled with bangs and booms of fireworks"?

Answer: Onomatopoeia.

9. Why did Naomi say they should call her "Mara" when they arrived at her hometown?

Answer: Because "Naomi" means pleasant, but she had experienced much hardship and suffering, so she wanted to be called "Mara," which means bitter.

10. What kind of complement is the underlined phrase in the sentence "The man sounded angry on the other end of the line"?

Answer: Predicate noun (or predicate nominative).
