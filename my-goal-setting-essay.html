13. What is my goal in life? Essay 150 words


Goal of My life

Life without a noble objective is like a ship without a rudder, yet choosing a goal is a difficult undertaking. Because nothing is good or evil; thinking alone makes anything good or bad, different people have various goals depending on their preferences. Everyone who has a seeing eye, a thinking mind, and a feeling heart has some sort of ambition.

Serving my nation is my life's biggest ambition. I am aware that this is not a simple task and that there are several methods to serve one's nation. I am aware that becoming a decent citizen would be the finest service I could provide to my nation. I might decide to become a doctor, engineer, or teacher. I may go into business or into politics. But no matter who I become, if I don't understand my rights and obligations, I won't be of any service to my nation.

Making myself an excellent citizen would thus be my first step toward achieving my life's purpose. I'll never be corrupt, and I'll never put up with other people being corrupt. I'll always treat people fairly and justly. I will always put the good of the community above my own interests in all I do. I won't push my claims if there is a conflict between my personal and societal interests. I could have a positive impact on everyone if I can live up to this ideal.

29. My gaols are the reflections of what. i want to be my goals are?​


Yes, my goals are the reflections of my story. I want to be my goals as follow:

My main goals right now are to be good husband, a father, a head of household and the most important to contribute to the community.

THE REFLECTION IN MY STORY:

I was reminded of the advice of my spiritual teacher in Manila. He once whispered to me. The point is that life is about living it. Whatever God's commandment, must be carried out properly. So, wherever or as anything, we should carry out our duties well because it will contribute to our environment. If I develop: I now play the role of a husband, a father, a head of the household. I also play the role of a student. So I have to carry out my roles well so that it will have a good impact on my environment. A simple phrase, but has a deep meaning.

I was stunned, contemplating what I had lived so far. From the material side, it seems to be sufficient, from the knowledge side it is also not bad. But have I made any contribution to the environment?

Of course, as the head of the household, my contribution is quite large. The indicator: if I die, maybe my children and wife will feel a huge loss. Then what is my contribution to the larger environment. Is there? I tried to see one by one. For example in the neighborhood where I live or the institution where I work, have I contributed?

How do I contribute to the environment I live in? I have never been active in activities in my village. Even now I am rarely involved in the management of the students at my college because of my busy schedule. It doesn't seem like it has contributed yet. But at least I've never made a tantrum in my neighborhood. So that's fine.

In the institution where I work, it feels like I have a contribution. During this time I worked on making reports and economic analysis with my friends for the bosses to make decisions. Am I contributing enough there? It seems yes. I have also been involved in making economic models to be used as a tool in estimating future economic movements. I think it's quite a contribution.

At this point I remember one of Bhagavad Geeta's verse: "your duty is only to work, not to your work". In Geeta it is advised to be selfless in work. The philosophical reason is that work is a form of offering to God. The thing called offering is offering, not business or take and give. The practical reason: when we think about results or gains, then our concentration on what has to be done, becomes less. In addition, if for example we fail to achieve the goal, then frustration welcomes us.

There is a quote: don't ask what the State has given you, but what you have given the State. This expression also means the same: contribute without expecting anything in return.

Maybe that's why rich people end up being philanthropists. Previously, what he did was only for profit, for himself, but in the end, it all might not give happiness, so he ended up becoming a philanthropist. The profits earned are finally returned to the community.

Then, have I contributed? That question is still dancing in my mind.
