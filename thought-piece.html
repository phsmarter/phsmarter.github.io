10. Before one may assert that a piece of specific knowledge is true, this thought requires empirical and visible evidence.


Pragmatism

Pragmatism is a school of thought that needs empirical and observable evidence before one can claim that a piece of particular knowledge is true.

What is pragmatism?

Pragmatism refers to the assessment of a theory whether it is true or not based on the observed evidence that can be claimed. It is a philosophical tradition or norm that uses or considers words as a medium for prediction, actions, problem-solving, and ideas.

What is the importance of pragmatism?

This approach believes that children should not follow or be asked to do things with predetermined work. This means the approach should depend on what their needs and goals are after self-evaluation or self-realization.

When we talk about the education system, pragmatism refers to the free way of learning using a cooperative and purposeful approach. The institution is expected and is sometimes required to provide learning experiences that are beneficial to the students.

28. how can I communicate my thoughts and feelings using prose literary pieces? ​


Answer:

We want to see, hear, smell, taste and feel what you write. Use the active voice, not the passive voice. We want the subject to do the action, which draws us into the emotions. Utilize action verbs, not linking verbs. Avoid gerunds (the -ing words). Avoid adverbs (those pesky -ly words). Use metaphors over similes
