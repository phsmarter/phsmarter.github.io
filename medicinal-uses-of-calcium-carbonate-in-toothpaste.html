23. Carbon dioxide can be removed from a stream of gas by reacting it with calcium oxide to form calcium carbonate. If we react 5.50 L of CO2 at STP with excess CaO, what mass of calcium carbonate will form? Use the equation : CO2 (g) + CaO (s)CaCO3 (s)


Answer:

mass CaCO3 = 24.6 g

Explanation:

Note: The molar volume of an ideal gas at STP is 22.414 L.

mass CaCO3 = 5.50 L CO2 × (1 mol CO2 / 22.414 L) × (1 mol CaCO3 / 1 mol CO2) × (100.09 g CaCO3 / 1 mol CaCO3)

mass CaCO3 = 24.6 g
