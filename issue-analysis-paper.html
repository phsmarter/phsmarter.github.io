12. Current Issues in the Philippines ECONOMIC TERM PAPEREMPIRICAL PAPER > INDRODUCTION      A. Statement of the topic           In the form of question      B. Rationale ( Choice of topic )           Reason            What makes it interesting      C. Organization           Outline of argument           > BODY OF THE PAPER      A. Related literature ( Indicate Gap )      B. Application of economic theory      C. Analysis of data ( Summary of statistics)           Results            Findings > CONCLUSION       - Objective > REFERENCE​


Answer:

Introduction:

A. Statement of the Topic:

What are the current economic issues in the Philippines and how are they affecting the country's development?

B. Rationale:

The Philippines is a developing country with a population of over 100 million people, and its economy has been experiencing both progress and setbacks. Some of the current issues facing the country include a high poverty rate, income inequality, unemployment, and underemployment, among others. These issues have the potential to hinder the country's progress and development, making it an interesting topic to explore.

C. Organization:

This paper will first review the relevant literature and identify the gaps that exist in current research. Then, economic theory will be applied to the issues at hand. The paper will also analyze the latest data and statistics, summarize the results, and present the findings. Finally, the conclusion will discuss the objectives of the paper.

Body of the Paper:

A. Related Literature:

Previous studies have highlighted the economic challenges in the Philippines, such as poverty, income inequality, and unemployment. However, there is a need to update the literature and explore the current state of these issues. For example, the COVID-19 pandemic has exacerbated the poverty rate, causing more people to lose their jobs and income. Another gap in the literature is the lack of attention to underemployment, which is a prevalent issue in the country.

B. Application of Economic Theory:

The economic theory can be applied to explain the issues facing the Philippines. For instance, the poverty rate can be explained by the lack of access to education, healthcare, and employment opportunities, among others. Income inequality can be attributed to the unequal distribution of resources and opportunities. The high unemployment and underemployment rates can be linked to the country's inadequate infrastructure, low productivity, and poor business environment.

C. Analysis of Data:

According to the latest data, the poverty rate in the Philippines increased from 16.6% in 2018 to 23.1% in 2020 due to the COVID-19 pandemic. Income inequality remains a significant concern, with the top 10% of the population earning more than 40% of the total income. The unemployment rate increased from 5.1% in 2019 to 7.1% in 2020, and underemployment reached 14.4%. The pandemic has also caused a decline in the country's GDP growth rate, which was -9.6% in 2020.

Results and Findings:

The analysis shows that the economic issues in the Philippines have been exacerbated by the COVID-19 pandemic, leading to a significant setback in the country's development. The poverty rate, income inequality, unemployment, and underemployment are some of the challenges that the country needs to address to achieve sustainable development.

Conclusion:

The objective of this paper was to examine the current economic issues in the Philippines and their impact on the country's development. The analysis reveals that these issues are a hindrance to the country's progress, and there is a need for policies and interventions that can address them effectively. The government needs to prioritize economic growth and job creation to reduce poverty and improve the standard of living for all Filipinos.

References:

- Philippine Statistics Authority. (2021). Philippine Economic Indicators. Retrieved from https://psa.gov.ph/statistics/survey/peei

- World Bank. (2021). Philippines Economic Update. Retrieved from https://www.worldbank.org/en/country/philippines/publication/philippines-economic-update-march-2021
