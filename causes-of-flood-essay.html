15. What is causes floods?


Answer:

Floods can be caused by various factors, both natural and human-induced. Some of the common causes of floods include:

Heavy rainfall: When there is excessive rainfall in a short period of time, it can lead to flooding.

River overflow: When the water level in a river rises above its banks, it can cause flooding in nearby areas.

Coastal flooding: Strong winds, high tides, and storm surges can cause flooding in coastal areas.

Dam or levee failure: If a dam or levee breaks, it can result in sudden and widespread flooding.

Urbanization: Paved surfaces in urban areas can increase the speed at which water runs off, causing flooding in low-lying areas.

Deforestation: Removal of forests and vegetation can reduce the ability of the land to absorb water, leading to increased flooding.

Climate change: Changing weather patterns and rising sea levels due to climate change can also contribute to more frequent and severe flooding events.

29. The thing that can cause a flood?​


What Causes a Flood?

Severe flooding is caused by atmospheric conditions that lead to heavy rain or the rapid melting of snow and ice. Geography can also make an area more likely to flood. For example, areas near rivers and cities are often at risk for flash floods.

A flood is an overflow of water onto land that is normally dry. Floods can happen almost anywhere. They can cover an area with just a few inches of water or they can bring enough water to cover the roof of a house. Floods can be dangerous for communities, lasting days, weeks or sometimes even longer.

Many different situations can cause a flood. Here are just a few:

Heavy rainfallOcean waves coming on shore, such as a storm surgeMelting snow and ice, as well as ice jamsDams or levees breaking

Geography can also make an area more likely to flood. For example, areas near rivers are often at risk for floods. Urban areas (areas near cities) are also at higher risk for floods because rooftops funnel rainfall to the ground below, and paved surfaces such as highways and parking lots prevent the ground from absorbing the rain.
