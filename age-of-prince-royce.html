"The Prince of Egypt" is about the story of Moses, an Egyptian prince, that is based on the biblical book of Exodus.

Here are the answers to your activity:

Exposition:

The film introduces the main characters, Moses and his adoptive brother Ramses. They have their privileged life as princes of Egypt. It also introduces the audience to the cruelty done by Egyptian leaders during the time.

Rising Action:

When Moses discovers his true identity as a Hebrew and witnesses the suffering of his people. The Pharaoh requested Moses to leave the palace and went to the desert for a new start.

Climax:

It happened when God speaks to Moses through a burning bush. He was told that he will be the one who will lead Hebrews out of the Egyptian leaders' hands. Moses returns to Egypt and informed Ramses, demanding that he free the Hebrew slaves, and if he do not follow, plagues will come.

Falling Action:

The falling action begins as the plagues are delivered by God on Egypt such as the death of the firstborn sons. Also, when the Hebrews find themselves trapped between the Red Sea and the Egyptian army with nowhere to go.

Resolution:

The resolution comes when Moses divides the Red Sea with God's help, allowing them to cross safely to the other side. The Egyptian army is drowned as the waters close in on them. 14. 12) Ralph, Royce, and Randolf weigh 119 kgs altogether. Royce weighs twice as heavy as Ralph. Ralph weighs 7 kg less than Randolf. What is the mass of Randolf?​


Answer:

Let's start by assigning variables to each person's weight.

Let's call Ralph's weight "R", Royce's weight "O", and Randolf's weight "D".

From the problem, we know that:

R + O + D = 119 (they weigh 119 kgs altogether)

O = 2R (Royce weighs twice as heavy as Ralph)

R = D - 7 (Ralph weighs 7 kg less than Randolf)

We can use the second equation to substitute "2R" for "O" in the first equation:

R + 2R + D = 119

Simplifying:

3R + D = 119

We can also use the third equation to substitute "D - 7" for "R" in the above equation:

3(D - 7) + D = 119

Simplifying:

4D - 21 = 119

Adding 21 to both sides:

4D = 140

Dividing both sides by 4:

D = 35

Therefore, Randolf weighs 35 kgs.

Prince Bantugan and Prince Madali are both popular characters in Philippine folklore, but they belong to different tales and have distinct characteristics.

Differences between Prince Bantugan and Prince Madali:

1. Origin: Prince Bantugan is a mythical hero from the Maranao people of Mindanao, while Prince Madali is a character from the Maguindanaon epic "Biag ni Lam-ang".

2. Personality: Prince Bantugan is known for his bravery, wisdom, and kindness, while Prince Madali is portrayed as cunning and deceitful.

3. Abilities: Prince Bantugan possesses supernatural powers, such as being able to fly, make himself invisible, and control the elements. In contrast, Prince Madali is not depicted as having any supernatural abilities.

4. Story: The story of Prince Bantugan revolves around his journey to recover his stolen royal sword, while the story of Prince Madali centers around his attempts to marry a wealthy and powerful woman.

Similarities between Prince Bantugan and Prince Madali:

1. Royalty: Both Prince Bantugan and Prince Madali are princes from noble families.

2. Importance: They are important figures in their respective tales and are often praised for their heroic deeds.

3. Love: Both characters have romantic interests in their stories. Prince Bantugan falls in love with the daughter of a Sultan, while Prince Madali courts the wealthy and powerful Alokotan.

4. Conflict: Both stories have conflicts that the characters must overcome to achieve their goals. Prince Bantugan faces obstacles in his quest to recover his sword, while Prince Madali must overcome Alokotan's father's objections to their marriage.
