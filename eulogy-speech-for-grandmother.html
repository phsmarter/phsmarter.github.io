1. Eulogy about grandmother


Answer:

To my grandmother

Explanation:

I was born into this world without my parents taking care of me. I was abandoned by my father and a few months after giving birth to me, my mother had to go somewhere else to work--an act considered as good by many but I felt it was another form of abandonment. You were my father, you were my mother.

Even though we did not have the most money and at times we have to eat  just rice and no viand on certain days, we survived. You loved me more than anyone else could, despite all my shortcomings. You were there for me when I was lost. You were there for me even when you were sick.

I feel the loss now that you are gone and I wish I could have done more for you. I wish that I could turn back time and be with you once again. But no, time has expired for us and that I could not do anything about anymore. As we more forward with our lives I could only pray that you rest in peace as I cherish our times together.

Note: This is a real and heartfelt eulogy I would have given my grandmother, had we had eulogies back then.

3. What is eulogy speech?​


Answer:

A eulogy is a speech given at a memorial or funeral service. It can be delivered by a family member, close friend, priest, minister or celebrant and it commemorates and celebrates the life of the deceased.

Eulogies began in ancient Greece and the tradition continues today as it is still very common practice, although not all cultures and religions include eulogies in their funeral proceedings.

27. eulogy speech about mother earth


Eulogy for Mother Nature



Mother Nature is slowly fading
Into the emptiness of "civilization."
The birds no longer sing of freedom,
But of sorrow and pain.
Lands are mangled and eaten by the steel-toothed tractors of man
And in their places are put condominiums and parking lots,
Shopping malls and golden arches.
Then, we try to connect with Mother Earth,
Interspersing bits and pieces of "untouched" land—
A few standing trees and shrubs, with a family of cardinals,
          Allowed to keep their home for today—
          Put on display as a dying part of our world
Between some new mega chain store and a strip mall.

She is lonely now,
Nobody to keep her company,
As everyone has somewhere they should’ve been five minutes ago.
They fly by in their mass-produced cars,
Polluting the world around them.
“There’s plenty of trees.
Let our children worry about that.”

Now, everywhere you look:
Concrete, asphalt, chain link fences.
The air is gray and brown with the pollution of years gone by.
It’s hard to breathe.
There is no more beauty in the world.

30. what is the purpose of eulogy in speech​


Answer:

A eulogy is a way to honor the memory of a lost loved one at their funeral and is one of the most important elements of a service. It is truly a farewell tribute speech to family and friends, expressing how much this person means to you and information about who they were and what they did in their lifetime.
