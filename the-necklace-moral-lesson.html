1. moral lesson of the story the necklace​


THE NECKLACE

The story The Necklace revolves around a middle class family, the family of Madame Mathilde Loisel who strives to become or to appear rich or wealthy. And such action of Madame Mathilde Loisel brought her to sufferings.

MORAL LESSON OF THE STORY THE NECKLACE

WEALTH AND HAPPINESS DO NOT CORRELATE

It is very evident in the story The Necklace hat the main character is very much into becoming or appearing wealthy, in fact she equates wealth to happiness. That is, one lesson to get from the story is that do not equate wealth to happiness. A lot of people who have everything but are not happy.

DO NOT BE GREEDY

In the story The Necklace, clearly Madame Mathilde Loisel was not satisfied of the life she has. She was clearly the opposite of her husband who was satisfied in his post.

BEAUTY IS ONLY SKIN DEEP

This proverbial expression is the story's main lesson, meaning that a pleasing appearance is no guide to character.

5. Edna already owns 4 necklaces, and additional necklaces are priced at 2 for a dollar. Is the total number of necklaces Edna owns proportional to the amount of money Edna spends on additional necklaces?


Answer:

Yes.

Explanation:

Two terms are proportional if one term increases with the other, or as one term increases, the other decreases.

Using this basis, we know that the number of necklaces is proportional to the amount of money spent.

As the amount increases by a dollar, the necklaces increase by 2.

We can also determine if this is proportional if we can come up with a linear equation that relates the two terms.

Let x = necklaces; let y = amount spent for additional necklaces.

Using what I previously said in the third paragraph, y = x/2. But, we need to add 4 to this since there are 4 necklaces already to start with. Thus, y = x/2 + 4.

To summarize, yes; the number of necklaces is proportional to the amount spent on additional necklaces.

10. The necklace what moral lesson you get in the strory ​


The moral of "The Necklace" is that we should be grateful for what we have rather than constantly seeking to acquire more. Mathilde Loisel is married to a man with a good job in the Ministry of Education, she has a servant girl who helps her with housework, and her husband is happy with their modest life and exclaims "delightedly" when presented with even simple dinners. However, Madame Loisel is miserable because she does not have fancy clothes or expensive jewels, "And these were the only things she loved." She wants to be "desired" and "sought after" and to charm other people with how "wildly attractive" she is.

Madame Loisel is unable to find happiness, or even contentment, with all that she has, and so she tries to create a night in which she can pretend that she lives the life to which she believes she is entitled. She gets a new dress, goes to a fancy party, and borrows a lavish diamond necklace to wear. It is this failure to appreciate a husband who has worked to secure an invitation and who has offered her money he was saving for himself so that she can get a dress to wear for one night that gets Madame Loisel into trouble. She could have been satisfied with her life, but she was not. She could have been happy with her dress, but she was not. She had to have jewels, too, though flowers would have done just as well. If she had been grateful for what she had and not sought to have more, then she could have lived happily and contentedly with her loving husband.

Answer:

"The Necklace" is a short story by French author Guy de Maupassant. The main characters in the story are Mathilde Loisel and her husband, Monsieur Loisel.

Mathilde Loisel is the protagonist of the story, a young woman who is unhappy with her lower-class life and longs for a life of luxury. She is described as pretty, but dissatisfied with her modest existence and her drab and simple wardrobe. She is very vain and wants to be admired and envied by others. She is willing to go to great lengths to obtain what she wants, and her obsession with social status and material possessions leads to her downfall.

Monsieur Loisel is Mathilde's husband, a government clerk. He is described as a kind and patient man who is content with his life and his modest means. He is devoted to his wife and does everything he can to make her happy, including lending her money to buy a dress for a fancy party. He is patient and supportive of his wife, but his support doesn't help her to change her attitude towards the life they have.

Madame Forestier, a friend of Mathilde's, lends her a necklace for the party. She is described as a wealthy and elegant woman, who is unaware of the sacrifices and difficulties that Mathilde endures to replace the lost necklace.

The Necklace is a story that centers on the main character's struggles and the consequences of her actions. It's a cautionary tale of how the desire for material possessions and social status can lead to self-destruction.

In "The Necklace" by Guy de Maupassant, Mathilde Loisel's problem is that she lost a borrowed necklace from Madame Forestier, which was a very valuable and expensive one. To solve this problem, Mathilde takes several actions, but ultimately, it ends in a tragic way. Here are some of the actions she took:

She initially tries to hide the truth from Madame Forestier and pretends that the necklace is still in her possession.

She and her husband, Monsieur Loisel, spend all their savings and borrow money to buy a replacement necklace.

They work hard and make sacrifices for the next ten years, with Mathilde taking on menial jobs such as washing dishes and ironing clothes to pay off their debt.

They move to a small, cramped apartment in a poor neighborhood and give up their social life and luxuries.

Finally, after ten years, when the debt is finally paid off and they can finally afford to move to a better place, Mathilde reveals to Madame Forestier that the necklace was lost and replaced with a replica. Madame Forestier reveals that the necklace was not real and that it was worth only a fraction of the cost of the replacement.

As a result of her actions, Mathilde's life is destroyed and she lives in poverty, regretting her past choices and wishing she had been content with her simple life.

The story illustrates the destructive power of vanity, the desire for material possessions, and the false illusion of social status, that can lead to self-destruction.

22. A necklace was sold for Php3150.00 with a 40% discount.what was the original Price of the necklace?​


ANSWER


Php5250.00

SOLUTION

To determine the original price of the necklace, you can use the following formula:

Original price = selling price / (1 - discount%)

Plugging in the given values, you get:

Original price = 3150 / (1 - 40%) = 3150 / (1 - 0.4) = 3150 / 0.6 = 5250

Therefore, the original price of the necklace was Php5250.00.

Required:

percent by mass

Formula:

[tex]\text{percent by mass} = \dfrac{\text{mass of solute}}{\text{mass of solution}} \times 100\%[/tex]
