8. "reflection" Insight of developing a prototype of ICT project for social change


Answer:

I’ve learned about creating a concept paper. A concept paper is a document used to convince a panel of potential funders to help a product, program, or service become reality. It has five elements: introductions, purpose, description, support, and contact.

There are five elements of a concept paper. First, INTRODUCTION, it includes your group’s mission and vision. Next, PURPOSE, includes why this project is worth your group and sponsor’s time.

Then, DESCRIPTION, includes all the necessary information about the project. SUPPORT, contains the budget needed for the project. CONTACT INFORMATION, includes information on how the group can be contacted.

I’ve learned that ICT Project Process involves planning, development, release and promotion, and maintenance. In the planning stage, your group should be able to conceptualize your project, research about your topic, set meetings and deadlines, assign tasks, and find a host, create a sign map, list down all application and output that you need, and consider funding. The development stage involves the actual creation of the website or blog.

The release and promotion stage involves the actual release and promotion of the campaign. Finally, maintenance involves responding to feedback and continuing to improve and update the website. That’s what I’ve learned.

26. write an reflection about Language concept (ICT)​


Language is a fundamental concept in information and communication technology (ICT) as it plays a vital role in communication between individuals, groups, and machines. The development of ICT has enabled people from different cultures and backgrounds to communicate and exchange information effortlessly, breaking down geographical barriers and promoting global collaboration.

In the context of ICT, language has become more than just a tool for communication; it is also a medium for the creation and transmission of knowledge. The internet, for example, has allowed for the creation of vast amounts of information, much of which is written in various languages. Machine translation and natural language processing (NLP) technologies have made it possible to translate and process this information, making it accessible to people who speak different languages.

However, while ICT has made communication and knowledge exchange more accessible, it has also presented new challenges related to language. For instance, the use of computer-mediated communication (CMC) tools such as instant messaging and social media has given rise to new forms of language use and communication styles that may be difficult for some individuals to understand. Moreover, the use of ICT has also brought about new forms of language-based crimes such as cyberbullying and online harassment, which pose a threat to individuals' mental health and wellbeing.

In conclusion, language is a crucial concept in ICT as it enables communication, knowledge creation, and information exchange. However, the development of ICT has also presented new challenges related to language, which need to be addressed to ensure that everyone can benefit from the advantages that ICT provides.

28. are ict knowledge and skills enough to manage the website? and why reflection ​


No, ICT (Information and Communications Technology) knowledge and skills alone are not enough to fully manage a website.

While ICT knowledge and skills are necessary for technical aspects of website management, such as website design, development, and maintenance, they do not cover other important aspects of website management, such as content creation and management, digital marketing, and user experience design. In addition, understanding of the business or organization and its goals, as well as knowledge of relevant laws and regulations, is also important for effectively managing a website.
