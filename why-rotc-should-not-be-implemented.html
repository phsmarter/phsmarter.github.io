12. Why do you think ROTC is important?​


The Reserve Officers Training Corps (ROTC) is charged with educating and molding college students in the fundamentals of military service in order to generate competent Armed Forces of the Philippines reservists, which is why I believe ROTC is crucial.

The ROTC specifically seeks to:

College students should be prepared for potential duty in the defense establishment in the case of a crisis.Train officers and enlisted troops for the Philippine Armed Forces reserve components (AFP).Train prospective officers in preparation for future direct commissioning in the AFP.

Through the Reserve Officers' Training Corps (ROTC), students can transition from college to the military and get both academic and military training. Cadets agree to spend eight years in the military in exchange for employment as a commissioned officer after graduation. The possibility of having their tuition paid in full for cadets who apply for and are awarded an ROTC scholarship frequently draws students interested in military professions.

Depending on how much time is left in your studies, ROTC programs provide two-, three-, and four-year scholarships. The majority of ROTC scholarships cover all college costs in full, including tuition, room & board, and textbooks. Although all majors are eligible to join ROTC and apply for an ROTC scholarship, not all institutions offer the program, and particular majors—such as business, engineering, and public policy—get preference for the money awarded through ROTC scholarships.

To prepare for work as a commissioned officer, ROTC cadets study military organization, military tactics, and military technology in addition to their degree-related curriculum. They also receive military instruction. Some graduates go on to serve in the military in specialized roles such an analyst, nurse, or educator.

18. create an argument about the "Proposed Mandatory Implementation of ROTC in Senior High School"


The proposed mandatory implementation of ROTC in senior high school is a controversial issue that has sparked debates among educators, parents, and students. Supporters argue that ROTC provides numerous benefits, including promoting patriotism, discipline, physical fitness, and leadership skills. However, opponents argue that mandatory ROTC violates human rights, promotes militarism, and puts students at risk of physical and mental harm.

On the one hand, proponents of mandatory ROTC argue that it instills a sense of pride and loyalty to the country. The program also teaches discipline and respect for authority, which can benefit students in their future careers and personal lives. Moreover, ROTC can provide students with physical fitness training, which is essential to maintaining a healthy lifestyle.

On the other hand, opponents argue that mandatory ROTC violates students' human rights, as it forces them to participate in a program that may conflict with their beliefs and values. They also argue that mandatory ROTC promotes militarism and glorifies war, which is not appropriate for a civilian educational institution. Moreover, students may be at risk of physical and mental harm, as ROTC involves military-style training that can be physically and emotionally demanding.

In conclusion, while the proposed mandatory implementation of ROTC in senior high school may have some benefits, it also raises significant concerns about human rights, militarism, and the safety of students. The decision to implement ROTC should be made with careful consideration of these issues, and alternative programs that promote discipline, physical fitness, and leadership skills should also be considered. Ultimately, the goal should be to provide students with a well-rounded education that prepares them for success in their future careers and personal lives, without compromising their safety and well-being.

19. Do you think ROTC program should be mandotary/ voluntary​


Answer:

The Reserve Officers' Training Corps (ROTC) is a program that trains students to become officers in the U.S. Armed Forces while they pursue a college degree. Currently, ROTC participation is voluntary, and students can choose to enroll in the program if they wish.

Some argue that making ROTC mandatory could have benefits for both the military and the students. Proponents of mandatory ROTC argue that it could help to increase the number of officers in the military, which could be particularly important during times of war or national emergency. Additionally, they argue that ROTC provides valuable leadership and teamwork training that could benefit students regardless of whether they end up pursuing military careers.

However, others argue that mandatory ROTC could be problematic, as it could be seen as forcing students to pursue military careers against their will. Some students may also have religious or ethical objections to military service, which could conflict with mandatory ROTC. Furthermore, mandatory ROTC could be seen as infringing on individual freedoms and limiting the choices and opportunities available to students.

Ultimately, whether ROTC should be mandatory or voluntary is a complex issue with arguments on both sides. The decision would likely depend on a variety of factors, including national security needs, public opinion, and the values and priorities of the educational institutions involved.
