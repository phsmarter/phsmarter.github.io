8. Additional ActivityDirection: List down the similarities and differences of RADAR and LASER in the Venn Diagram in terms of transmission and reception of signals.​


Answer:

DIFFERENCES

RADAR

• It produces Radio waves

•Accepts weak target signals and amplify them to a functional level and convert the information stored in them from the Radio Frequency (RF) to the baseband.

• uses radio waves to determine the range, angle, or velocity of objects

• Radio waves (pulsed or continuous) from the transmitter reflect off the object and return to the receiver, giving information about the object's location and speed

LASER

•Contains a circuit for signal processing and a laser that is used to produce laser signal

•Catches and reads incoming laser signals

•Can relay more information with a stronger signal per second

• The carrier used for the transmission signal is typically generated by a laser diode

SIMILARITY

The way they work is similar. It’s just that Radar uses radio waves while Laser uses laser beams/laser signal
