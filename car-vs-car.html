13. BUS VS CAR when the bus collides head on with a var, which vehicle would undergo a greater change in its motion? why? write your prediction and explanation.


Answer:

The car

Explanation:

In a head-on collision the forces on the two vehicles are constrained to be the same by Newton's third law. But from the both Newton's second law and the work-energy principle It becomes evident that it is safer to be in the bigger truck.

Even though the forces are equal in magnitude on the two trucks, the smaller truck experiences the greater acceleration.

The change in velocity of the driver will be the same as the truck in which he/she is riding. A greater change in velocity implies the greater change in kinetic energy and therefore more work than on the driver.
