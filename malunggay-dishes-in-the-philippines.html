Answer:

Malunggay reproduces ether asexually and sexually. Asexually, is a process that does not involve meiosis or syngamy. It reproduces by having spores/pollen in the flower being transported to another flower by animal transport or wind pollination. The pollen lands on the stigma. Then the spores/pollen goes down the pistil into the ovary fertilizing the seeds. Sexually, the plant reproduces when two plant exchange genetic material fertilizing a seed.

11. some dishes that Filipino's consider as taboo is that;any dish that have dog and cat. In what act in the Philippines constitution prohibits this law? ​


Answer:

Animal Welfare Act (Republic Act No. 8485)

Answer:

The Philippines has an objective of eliminating rabies by 2020, a target that cannot be achieved unless the dog meat trade is eradicated—a fact that lawmakers understood when they included a prohibition in the trade of dog meat in the 2007 Rabies Act.

Explanation:

The killing and selling of dogs for food was banned in the capital city of Manila in 1982. A similar ban was enacted nationally in 1998, with the Animal Welfare Act (Republic Act No. 8485). The Act prohibits killing dogs for food, with minimum penalties set at 1,000 pesos (equivalent to about $US22 at the time) and not less than six months in prison.

30. description of malunggay​


Malunggay

Malunggay is classified as a tropical plant that can reach a height of 9 meters. The most prized part of this tropical tree are the Malunggay pods, which contain essential oils, vitamins and nutrients. The Malunggay pods are very long, have a green to brown outer skin and contain small seeds that are winged and angled.
