17. what's the difference between neoclassicism and romanticism?​


Answer:

Neoclassicism (1660 - 1790s) is the period of time where the main focus is the revival of Greek and Roman arts; They emphasized on structure and objectivity. Also, logic and universal truths are more important than human emotions.

Romanticism (1790s - 1830s) is more dramatic than Neoclassicism. They drew inspiration from the Medieval and Baroque eras, and their tone is more spontaneous. Finally, their main theme is nature, mythologies and love.

The two eras are completely different. If I should summarize, Neoclassicism is more formal while Romanticism is more unrestricted.

Explanation:

Whereas Neoclassicism focused on universal truths, historical examples, and logic, Romanticism focused on the personal subconscious, contemporary events, and the sublime. Romanticism also looked for more inspiration outside of Western aesthetics.
