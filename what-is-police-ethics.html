1. Law vs. Ethics

People cannot be made fair, kind, or honest by the law. For instance, while lying or breaking confidence is not against the law, they are immoral.

Example: Lies, deception, and tricks Lies undermine public trust in law enforcement. Because they are an improper method to aim, lies are unethical.

2. Ethics vs. Psychology

Ethics is the study of moral behavior. The study of human nature, experience, and thought processes is known as psychology. The relationship between psychology and ethics is one of a normative to a constructive science.

Example: Ethics is the study of human behavior that adheres to certain ethical norms. Psychology can be used in the police force to manipulate and yield answers.

3. Ethics vs. Religion

Religion has the power to inspire strong ethical impulses and to create high ethical standards.

Example: Intimidation based on religion. intimidation based on religion. the exploitation of religion for profit. resource abuse in religion. Sects' financial manipulation.

IMPACTS OF MORALITY ON LAWThe moral tenets of society serve as the broad foundation for laws. Both control how an individual behaves in public. They significantly affect one another. For laws to be successful, those moral principles must be reflected in them.The law deals with a person's right to privacy, whereas morals are involved with the public perception of right and wrong. Law controls a man's conduct while he is a part of a certain community, but morals control a man's conduct even when he is by himself.

20. What is ethics? What are the tyes of ethics​


What is Ethics?

Ethics is a set of moral beliefs and principles that are the basis of a person's behavior and decisions. Other term for ethics is "moral philosophy". When we say ethics, what is good for more people and to himself is always the center of it. Ethics always determined what is right and wrong based on human responsibility and rights and how would the society or community would be benefit from it.

Types of Ethics Personal Ethics - a type of ethics which serves a moral guide of a person. Social Ethics - a type of ethics which includes justice and fairness for members of society. Social ethics are also based on customs and norms of a certain society. Religious Ethics - an ethics based on beliefs of a certain religion. Business Ethics - composed of guidelines and actions of what is acceptable and unacceptable in the field of the business. Companies always follow their own business ethics standards.
