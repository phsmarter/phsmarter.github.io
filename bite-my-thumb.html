Answer:

THE WHISPER

By Melchor F. Cichon

https://brainly.ph/question/8698336

(Analogy)

Let us study line by line the poem:

Aboy-aboy, batya and patadyong  

(This describes the scenario of the speaker who is somewhat depicting an image of a woman doing laundry in the springs)

The chain I have with you

Is a cross to my calvary.

The speaker is somewhat pertaining to an agony against someone. This is evident in the use of the word "chain" and "cross to my calvary" which is a symbol of pain and suffering)

My parents told me

To just wipe away my biting tears

And put ice on my swollen lips,

The speaker seems to be in pain in these words with a sense of feeling of pitty to oneself that even the parents would not even try to protect their child

A gift from my husband's quick fists.

This statement signifies that the speaker is a woman suffering from domestic violence.

I have become his tail, my parents say,

After I have pressed my thumb

On our marriage contract

In these words, it shows why her parents cant seem to protect their child. It is because she is already married to the person causing her pain and agony.

Aboy-aboy, batya and patadyong,

It's not a sin, isn't it

To put off my chain from you

And march in the streets

With closed fists

The last 5 lines signify the woman's decision to stand on the ground and her suffering by leaving the marriage. The word with closed fists symbolizes that she is ready to fight.

The speaker of the poem is a battered wife - a victim of domestic violence in women.
