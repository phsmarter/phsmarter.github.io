24. Morality or moral standards are just mere human invention and considered as a social convention.​


Answer:

Prima facie, no. It would seem to depend on what our conventions are. I.e. "do we have a moral right to do anything we wish as long as we can get away with it?" would then be equivalent to "is it our convention to do anything we wish as long as we can get away with it?" If, for example, our convention is to live as in a Hobbesian state of nature, then our doing anything we wish as long as we can get away with it would be in accordance with such a convention (but so would our doing anything we wish even if we can't get away with it, confronted with revenge-seeking retaliators). Whether there even could be such a convention is moot. Our existing moral codes across all cultures aren't Hobbesian (Hobbes himself regarded morality as the way out of a state of nature) and those moral codes don't allow us to do anything we wish. If those moral codes are mere conventions, then those conventions don't allow us to do anything we wish either.
