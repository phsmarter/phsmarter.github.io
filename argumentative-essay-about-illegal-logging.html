1. Persuasive essay in no to illegal logging​


Answer:

Illegal logging is a serious problem that affects not only the environment but also the livelihoods of people who depend on forests for their survival. It is a destructive practice that involves the cutting down of trees without proper authorization, which leads to the destruction of natural habitats, loss of biodiversity, soil erosion, and climate change. Therefore, it is essential to say no to illegal logging and promote sustainable forest management practices to protect our planet and its inhabitants.

Firstly, illegal logging destroys natural habitats and affects the biodiversity of forests. Forests are home to millions of plant and animal species, and when they are destroyed, it leads to the loss of habitats and the extinction of species. This, in turn, affects the balance of ecosystems, leading to further environmental degradation. It is, therefore, crucial to protect our forests through legal and sustainable means.

Secondly, illegal logging contributes significantly to soil erosion and climate change. Trees serve a crucial role in preventing soil erosion and absorbing carbon dioxide. When trees are cut down illegally, it leads to soil erosion, which affects the fertility of the soil and affects agriculture. Additionally, the loss of trees leads to an increase in carbon dioxide in the atmosphere, which contributes to climate change.

Thirdly, illegal logging has adverse effects on the economy and the livelihoods of people who rely on forests. The forest industry provides employment to millions of people worldwide, and illegal logging destroys these jobs. It also leads to the loss of revenue for governments and local communities, which rely on the forest industry for their economic growth.

In conclusion, saying no to illegal logging is crucial for the protection of our planet and its inhabitants. We must promote sustainable forest management practices and enforce laws to prevent illegal logging. It is our responsibility to ensure that future generations have access to healthy forests, clean air, and water, and we must take action now to protect our environment.

18. what's is illegal logging ​


Answer:

The illegal logging is the harvest, transportation, purchase or sale of timber in violation of laws. The harvesting procedure itself may be illegal, including using corrupt means to gain access to forests; extraction without permission, or from a protected area; the cutting down of protected species; or the extraction of timber in excess of agreed limits. Illegal logging is a driving force for a number of environmental issues such as deforestation, soil erosion and biodiversity loss which can drive larger scale environmental crisis such as climate change and other forms of environmental degradation.  Illegality may also occur during transport, such as illegal processing and export (through fraudulent declaration to customs) the avoidance of taxes and other charges, and fraudulent certification. These acts are often referred to as "wood laundering. Illegal logging is driven by a number of economic forces, such as demand for raw materials, land grabbing and demand for pasture for cattle. Regulation and prevention can happen at both the supply size, with better enforcement of environmental protections, and at the demand side, such as a increasing regulation of trade as part of the international lumber Industry.
