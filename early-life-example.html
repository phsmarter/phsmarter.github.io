1. Give examples of the uses of constellations in the life of the early people​


Explanation:

Constellations have been used by early people for various practical purposes, such as:

Navigation: Constellations were used as a guide for navigation and helped early sailors and travelers to determine their direction and location. For example, the North Star (Polaris) was used as a reference point for determining the direction of north.

Agriculture: Early people used constellations to track the seasons and the movement of the sun, which helped them in planting and harvesting crops at the right time.

Religion: Many early civilizations worshipped the stars and the constellations as gods or symbols of their gods. For example, the ancient Egyptians associated the constellation Orion with the god Osiris, and the Greeks associated the constellation Cassiopeia with the queen of the same name.

Timekeeping: Constellations were used to measure time, as they appear in the sky at predictable times throughout the year. This allowed early people to create calendars and schedule important events.

Storytelling: Many cultures used the stars and constellations as the basis for myths and legends, which were passed down through generations as a way of explaining the natural world and the mysteries of the universe.
