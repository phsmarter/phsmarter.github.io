14. how are you going to convince people with sport persuasive speech english​


Answer:

I can provide you with some tips on how to create a persuasive speech on sports that can help you convince your audience.

1. Start with a strong opening: Begin your speech with a powerful statement or a question that captures your audience's attention. This can be a quote from a famous athlete, a statistic about the benefits of sports, or a personal anecdote that illustrates the importance of sports in your life.

2. Use facts and statistics: Incorporating factual information and statistics can add credibility to your argument. For example, you could highlight the positive impact of sports on physical and mental health, academic performance, and overall well-being.

3. Appeal to emotions: People often make decisions based on their emotions, so it's essential to connect with your audience on an emotional level. You could share stories of athletes who have overcome adversity or talk about the joy and camaraderie that comes with playing sports.

4. Address counterarguments: Acknowledge potential counterarguments and address them in your speech. This shows that you understand the issue from all sides and have considered different perspectives.

5. Provide a call to action: End your speech with a call to action that motivates your audience to take action. This could be encouraging them to join a sports team, support local sports programs, or advocate for increased funding for sports in schools.

Remember that a persuasive speech is all about convincing your audience to take action or change their point of view. By using these tips, you can create a compelling argument that will persuade your audience to see the value of sports.

What is Speech Style?

Speech style is a variation of the language used when people communicate with others. According to Joos (1976), the way of speaking is the format of the language used by the speaker and depends on the degree of format.

What are the Type of Speech Style?

According to Joos (1976), language styles can be divided into five forms. Frozen style, formal style, consultative style, casual style, and intimate style. So if you want to communicate with others, you have 5 style options.

Frozen. This is the most formal communication style commonly used in respectful events and ceremonies. It is also used to show hesitation, indifference, or prejudice. Frozen speech is commonly used in very formal settings and does not require feedback from the audience.

Formal style is a broad term for speech or writing marked by an impersonal, objective, and precise use of language. A formal prose style is typically used in orations, scholarly books and articles, technical reports, research papers, and legal documents.

A conversational style in which the speaker constantly observes the listener's linguistic and non-verbal clues for feedback. -What the speaker says next depends on the listener's feedback

Casual speaking is a form of communication. This is a relaxed or informal conversation between friends, peers, colleagues, or family members who share common knowledge and interests and use jargon, slang, or vernaculars.

Intimate speech is used in conversation between people who are very close and know each other quite well because they have the maximum of shared background information.
