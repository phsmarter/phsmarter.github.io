10. what do you think is the importance social idea of jose rizal which we think is a still useful to our society today​


Explanation:

ILOILO CITY, Dec. 30 (PIA6) -- Amidst the presence of modern technology and social media that have changed the lifestyle of young people at present, the millennials of today can still learn from the life and works of Dr. Jose Rizal.

Prof. Rejie Palmos, Coordinator of the Center for Peace Education at West Visayas State University here, said that our national hero is still relevant to the millennials today.

“Rizal served as an inspiration for many people during his time. For him, a revolution is not only about using bolos or firearms, but by using the pen which is mightier than the sword,” he said.

Palmos said that it is in the power of writing as a means to effect change that millennials can learn from Rizal.

“The theme for the 121st commemoration of Rizal’s martyrdom is “Rizal 2017: Mabuting Kaisipan, Susi sa Kaunlaran. It is about positive thinking or having positive minds,” he said.

He said that millennials should bear in mind that whatever they feel, whatever they do, whatever they act are a very important part of their way of life.

“If you always think of good things about people, if we do things that are good about people, we feel the positive vibes, the positive energy. But if we don’t think well about them, if we always feel negative towards them, this is something that is not good,” he said.

At present, Palmos said that young people are very much engaged in social media and in other forms of entertainment but there is a need to ‘check and balance’ on what we think, what we say, and what we write about other people.

“This is also a challenge for teachers and for educators that we have to counteract the culture of violence in social media and in many things that we do”, he said.

He said that Rizal was always pointing out that he did not want violence so he insisted that the pen should be used to propagate what was to be done for our country.

“We have to make him as an inspiration so before we speak bad words, before we post on social media, and before we interact with people in a not so good tone, we have to reflect Rizal’s relevance,” he said.

He said Rizal wanted change that was founded on a peaceful process because he opted for peace by using his pen to propagate what was peace and what was to be done during his generation.

Isabelo de los Reyes y Florentino, also known as Don Belong (July 7, 1864 – October 10, 1938), was a prominent Filipino politician, writer, and labor activist in the 19th and 20th centuries. He was the original founder of the Iglesia Filipina Independiente, an independent Philippine national church.
