What is Speech in Communication?

Speech communication is the study of how individuals use verbal and nonverbal signals to create shared meaning. Majors in speech communication seek to improve their public speaking, interpersonal, and small group communication skills. The emphasis is not on training students for a specific career but on developing interpersonal, organizational, intercultural, and public presentational skills to improve their lives in various scenarios (e.g.,  school, within the workplace, family, and social situations).

23. speech about school election as a grade 9 representative


When writing a speech about school elections as a Grade 9 representative, you are in essence writing a persuasive speech. This type of speech is meant to convince the audience to believe what you are saying. Oftentimes, there is also a call to action--for the students to vote for you as a Grade 9 representative. For this speech to be considered effective, you should have made them take your side by the end of your speech. Below is an example of a speech for Grade 9 representative.

Good morning fellow students at ____________ School. My name is _________ (insert your name). As we hold the elections today, I would like you to think hard of who you will vote as your Grade 9 representative. I may not have been your Grade 8 representative last year but what I plan to do is to make sure that all students in our level are heard. I will listen to your concerns regarding school-related issues. I will work with our student council and our school administration to make sure that concerns that could be corrected are addressed. So vote for me today and never regret why you did. Thank you!

When writing speeches like this, highlight what you want to accomplish and provide details as to how you can achieve those.

24. speech about school election as a grade 10 representative


A speech about school election as a a Grade 10 representative is an example of a persuasive speech. Persuasive speeches are meant to make your listeners take your side. It could also be a call to action, such as voting for you during the student election. Speeches such are these are very common during local and national elections. Below is a sample speech.  

Good morning my fellow students. Today, we will elect our representatives. It is a critical moment as we seek to put in position those who would act as our representatives and work with the school administration and the rest of the studentry.  

I appeal to you to look at the platform as well the track record of each individual student in front of you today. When I was the Grade 9 representative, these were the project that I either led or co-sponsored with our fellow student government officials.

Number 1: ____________________.

Number 2: ____________________

and finally ____________________.

This coming year, as we face issues related to climate change, I would like that to be the focus. We need to act against climate change hence if elected, I would make sure that information related to sea-level rise and its effect to the Philippines are distributed to everyone. Our party, likewise, would want to ensure that more people are represented, hence we selected students who come from various backgrounds.

So, as we vote today, please do not forget my name, _____________ as your Grade 10 representative.

Thank you very much!

When trying to persuade someone to vote for you make sure that you highlight what you have already accomplished and what you want or envision to achieve if ever elected.
