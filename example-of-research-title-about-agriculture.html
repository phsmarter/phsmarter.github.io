10. Example research paper on inquiries investigation and immersion, yung topic po sana nya is agriculture​


Introduction

Agriculture is an important sector in the economy of many countries around the world. It plays a crucial role in providing food, fiber, and fuel for the population. However, the sector faces numerous challenges such as climate change, soil degradation, and water scarcity, among others. To address these challenges, it is important to conduct research on different aspects of agriculture and identify innovative solutions to enhance productivity, sustainability, and resilience. In this paper, we will discuss the concepts of inquiries, investigations, and immersion in agricultural research and provide an example of how these concepts can be applied in a research project.

Inquiries

Inquiries refer to the process of asking questions and seeking answers to understand a phenomenon or a problem. Inquiries involve critical thinking, analysis, and synthesis of information to generate new knowledge or insights. In agricultural research, inquiries can be used to identify research gaps, formulate research questions, and develop hypotheses. For example, an inquiry-based approach can be used to investigate the factors that affect crop yields in a particular region. The researcher can ask questions such as: What is the current state of agriculture in the region? What are the main challenges facing farmers in the region? What are the factors that contribute to low crop yields? By answering these questions, the researcher can identify the key variables that need to be investigated further.

Investigations

Investigations refer to the process of collecting and analyzing data to test hypotheses or answer research questions. Investigations involve the use of different research methods such as surveys, interviews, experiments, and observations. In agricultural research, investigations can be used to collect data on different aspects of agriculture such as soil quality, crop management practices, and pest control methods. For example, an investigation can be conducted to determine the effectiveness of different irrigation methods in improving crop yields. The researcher can collect data on the amount of water used, the frequency of irrigation, and the quality of the soil to determine the impact of different irrigation methods on crop yields.

Immersion

Immersion refers to the process of engaging in a particular activity or environment to gain a deeper understanding of a phenomenon. Immersion involves observation, participation, and reflection on the experience. In agricultural research, immersion can be used to gain insights into the daily activities of farmers and understand their perspectives on different issues. For example, a researcher can immerse themselves in the life of a farmer for a week to understand their farming practices, challenges, and aspirations. By doing so, the researcher can gain a deeper understanding of the social, economic, and cultural factors that influence agricultural practices in the region.

Example of a Research Project

To illustrate how inquiries, investigations, and immersion can be applied in agricultural research, we will provide an example of a research project on enhancing the productivity and sustainability of small-scale farmers in a particular region.

Inquiries: The first step in the research project would be to conduct inquiries to identify the key research questions and variables that need to be investigated. The researcher can ask questions such as: What are the main challenges facing small-scale farmers in the region? What are the factors that contribute to low crop yields? What are the existing agricultural practices and technologies used by farmers in the region? By answering these questions, the researcher can identify the key variables that need to be investigated further.

Investigations: The next step would be to conduct investigations to collect data on different aspects of agriculture in the region. The researcher can use different research methods such as surveys, interviews, and experiments to collect data on soil quality, crop management practices, pest control methods, and other relevant variables. 25. Give research title about agriculture strand​


Answer:

TOP 9 AGRICULTURAL RESEARCH PAPER TOPICS

Agriculture, for many people, is not the most interesting of topics. Turning a topic that revolves around farming, plants, growth, agricultural technology and other agricultural concerns may seem nearly impossible to some. However, there are actually dozens of intriguing and valid agriculture research paper topics that students can choose from. These topics are both relevant to the agricultural field, and highly intriguing – even to those that may not have any love for agriculture! If you’re looking for a great agriculture research paper topic, check out the top nine topics listed below.

1.Agricultural technology. Technology is popping up in every single academic and work field under the sun, but agriculture is benefiting very obviously. Much of today’s agricultural technology is intriguing and effective. Exploring technological advancement in agriculture, as well as its economic and social effects, makes for a great paper.

2.Renewable energy. Astoundingly, many agricultural products are now being considered as possible energy sources – for example, a corn-based ethanol as a replacement for gasoline. Agriculture is fast becoming an important factor in the future of clean, renewable and alternative energy.

3.A dying art – the agricultural work force. In reality, there is an insanely small amount of people that work in agriculture. This tiny minority works to feed nations, and exploring this workforce – as well as the future of such a workforce – leads to startling discoveries.

4.Environmental issues. There has always been debate over the rules of agricultural expansion and environmental protection. When does agriculture rule, and when does the environment? How does agriculture affect ecosystems and the surrounding land? What are the environmental implications of commercial agriculture?

5Supply lines or finances. Ever consider the lengthy supply lines of agriculture? Every consider how much money goes in to producing agricultural products? Look it up – you’ll find some fascinating and unbelievable research that could contribute to your paper.

6Animal rights. Much of agricultural is based on animal products, and animal rights are a serious and constant issue in this market. Address laws, regulations, standards and conditions regarding animals in the agricultural business – you’ll find some interesting thesis starters.

7Pesticides. The effect of pesticides, safe choices for pesticides and alternative methods to pesticides have all been hot topics for many years. Pick something in this real to research for an interesting paper.

8Organic vs. Inorganic. There has been a rise in awareness on what is ‘organic’ vs. ‘inorganic.’ What are the health, financial, and business implications of this recent schism? How might it affect individual farmers or even larger productions?

9Cultural and political effects. Believe it or not, agriculture shapes much of today’s political and social movements. Find those connections for an interesting research paper read.
