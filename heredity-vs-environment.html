11. Explain the effect of heredity and environment to the human life.​


Answer:

Heredity and environment are two major factors that influence human development and behavior.

Heredity refers to the genetic traits that we inherit from our biological parents. These traits include physical characteristics such as eye color, height, and body type, as well as personality traits, intelligence, and predisposition to certain health conditions. Heredity can have a significant impact on our lives because it determines our biological makeup and can influence our behavior and abilities.

Environment, on the other hand, refers to the external factors that surround us, including the physical, social, and cultural surroundings. The environment includes everything from the air we breathe and the food we eat, to the people we interact with and the social norms and values we are exposed to. Environmental factors can have a significant impact on human development and behavior, shaping our personalities, beliefs, and habits.

Both heredity and environment interact with each other to shape human life. For example, a person with a genetic predisposition to obesity may be more likely to become overweight if they are exposed to an environment with high-calorie food and a sedentary lifestyle. Similarly, a person with a genetic predisposition to alcoholism may be more likely to develop the condition if they grow up in an environment with a culture of heavy drinking.

In conclusion, heredity and environment are two key factors that influence human life. While heredity determines our biological makeup and can influence our behavior and abilities, the environment can shape our personalities, beliefs, and habits. Both factors interact with each other to shape our lives, and understanding the complex interplay between the two can help us understand human development and behavior.

30. Essay:Is it heredity or environment that determines how an organism will develop?​


Answer:

Heredity explains why offspring resemble, but are not identical to, their parents and is a unifying biological principle. Heredity refers to specific mechanisms by which characteristics or traits are passed from one generation to the next via genes. Genes encode the information for making specific proteins, which are responsible for the specific traits of an individual. Each gene can have several variants, called alleles, which code for different variants of the trait in question.

Genes reside in a cell’s chromosomes, each of which contains many genes. Every cell of any individual organism contains the identical set of chromosomes. When organisms reproduce, genetic information is transferred to their offspring. In species that reproduce sexually, each cell contains two variants of each chromosome, one inherited from each parent. Thus sexual reproduction gives rise to a new combination of chromosome pairs with variations between parent and offspring. Very rarely, mutations also cause variations, which may be harmful, neutral, or occasionally advantageous for an individual. Environmental as well as genetic variation and the relative dominance of each of the genes in a pair play an important role in how traits develop within an individual. Complex relationships between genes and interactions of genes with the environment determine how an organism will develop and function.
