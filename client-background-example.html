Information Technology:

In working with clients from other cultural backgrounds, the IT specialist may find that the client is not fluent in English. The IT specialist recognizes this as an appropriate strategy for communicating with clients who are not fluent in English. This statement is false.

It may seem inappropriate for the IT specialist to communicate with clients using the English language when these clients are actually not fluent in English. It will be very hard for them to respond and give their honest opinions. At this time, the IT specialists might use sign languages or body languages instead or have somebody to translate for them.

25. It is a part of the ISBAR communication where information like name of client and caregivers, and time of shift should be first introduced. a. Situation b. Assessment O c. Background d. Introduction​


hope it help pa star heart and brainlist

The answer is d. Introduction.

In the ISBAR communication model, the first step is to introduce yourself and provide essential information such as your name, role, and time of shift. This step is important to establish clear communication and identify the parties involved in the conversation. It also helps to ensure that the information exchanged is accurate and relevant to the patient's condition.

The full ISBAR model includes the following steps:

Introduction: Introduce yourself, your role, and the time of shift.

Situation: Describe the current situation and why you are calling.

Background: Provide relevant background information about the patient, such as their medical history, medications, and allergies.

Assessment: Report your assessment of the patient's current condition, including vital signs and any changes in symptoms or behavior.

Recommendation: Make a recommendation for the next steps in the patient's care, such as transfer to another department or consultation with a specialist.

By following the ISBAR model, healthcare providers can improve communication and ensure that critical information is conveyed accurately and efficiently.
