About Level of measurements

Types of Measurement Scale

1 . Nominal scale

Nominal scale is a type of measurement scale that belongs to a category or group of a subject. For example, you can look at the gender variable, where the grouping is generally only into two, namely male and female which are coded 1 and 2.

The number only serves as a category label, without having a numeric value like a true number. These numbers do not have the same properties as numbers in general, so that on variables with a nominal scale, mathematical operations such as subtraction, addition, multiplication, etc. cannot be applied.

2. Ordinal scale

The ordinal scale is one type of measurement scale where the number symbols of the measurement results are in the form of a sequence or level. Appropriate statistical tests are mode, median, frequency distribution and non-parametric statistics such as rank order correlation.

3. Interval Scale

Is a type of measurement scale that has characteristics similar to the ordinal scale, which has a certain order. Another characteristic inherent in the interval scale is the existence of a scale unit. Appropriate statistical tests are all statistical tests except those that are based on ratios such as the coefficient of variation.

4. Ratio scale

Ratio scale is a type of measurement scale that produces data of the highest quality. The difference between the ratio scale and the interval scale lies in the presence of a zero value (based value). On a ratio scale, zero is absolute, unlike on an interval scale. The data generated by the ratio scale is ratio data. There are no restrictions on the appropriate statistical test tools.
