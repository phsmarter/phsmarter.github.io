6. the youth should be in school essay ​


Answer:

Education is a fundamental right for every individual, and it plays a crucial role in shaping one's future. The youth, being the future of the world, should be in school. In this essay, we will explore the reasons why the youth should be in school

Firstly, education provides young people with the necessary knowledge and skills that are essential for their personal and professional growth. A school is a place where children can acquire fundamental skills such as reading, writing, and mathematics. They also learn about science, history, social studies, and other subjects that contribute to their intellectual development. These skills and knowledge are critical in preparing the youth for future careers and personal success.

Secondly, schools provide an environment that encourages social interaction and networking among young people. In school, children interact with peers from different backgrounds and learn how to develop relationships with people from diverse cultures. This exposure to different ideas, beliefs, and perspectives enables them to build a strong sense of community and respect for others, which is essential in building a more tolerant and peaceful society.

Moreover, being in school provides the youth with a structured routine and discipline. Schools have rules and regulations that promote discipline and structure in the daily lives of young people. This routine helps young people to develop good habits such as time management, punctuality, and responsibility, which are essential in building a successful career and personal life.

Lastly, education is essential in promoting economic development. Education is a key driver of economic growth and development, as it provides the necessary skills and knowledge that contribute to innovation, technological advancements, and productivity. Education also increases the earning potential of individuals, which contributes to their economic well-being and that of their families and communities.

In conclusion, education is a fundamental right for every individual, and the youth should be in school. Education provides young people with the necessary knowledge and skills that are essential for their personal and professional growth. It also provides an environment that encourages social interaction and networking among young people. Education promotes discipline, structure, and routine, which are essential in building a successful career and personal life. Finally, education is essential in promoting economic growth and development. Therefore, governments, parents, and all stakeholders must work together to ensure that the youth receive quality education that prepares them for the future.

Out-of-School Children and Youth Prevalence in the Philippines

The 2013 Functional Literacy, Education and Mass Media Survey (FLEMMS) covered around 36 million population aged 6 to 24 years. According to the survey, one in every ten or about 4 million Filipino children and youth was out-of-school in 2013. Out-of-school children is defined in the FLEMMS as persons aged 6 to 14 years who are not attending school while out-of-school youth as persons aged 15 to 24 years who are not attending school, have not finished any college or post secondary course, and are not working.

Out-of-school children and youth in ARMM comprised 14.4 percent, which is the highest across regions. Six regions have proportion of out-of-school children and youth higher than the national figure at 10.6 percent, namely, SOCCSKSARGEN, Davao, Central Luzon, Zamboanga Peninsula, Caraga and MIMAROPA (ranging from 11.2 to 12.3 percent). Meanwhile, the proportion of out-of-school children and youth was lowest in CAR at 7.1 percent.

14. An expository essay on “The youths should be in school”


Education is one of the most important aspects of human development. It is the foundation upon which individuals can build their lives, careers, and contribute to society. However, despite the numerous benefits of education, many young people around the world are not in school. In this expository essay, we will explore the importance of education for youth and why it is essential that they should be in school.

Firstly, education provides young people with the knowledge and skills they need to succeed in life. It equips them with the tools to navigate the complexities of the world and to pursue their goals and aspirations. Education also helps young people to develop critical thinking skills, problem-solving skills, and the ability to communicate effectively. These skills are crucial for success in many areas of life, including the workplace, social interactions, and personal growth.

Secondly, education is a powerful tool for social and economic mobility. It opens doors to opportunities and allows individuals to break the cycle of poverty. Education is associated with higher incomes, better health outcomes, and greater social mobility. It also provides individuals with the skills and knowledge they need to contribute to their communities and to make a positive impact on society.

Thirdly, education is a fundamental human right. All young people have the right to access quality education that meets their needs and aspirations. Education is a key enabler of other human rights, including the right to health, the right to work, and the right to participate in civic life. Education also helps to promote tolerance, respect, and understanding among individuals and communities.

Despite the importance of education, many young people around the world are not in school. Some are prevented from attending school due to poverty, conflict, discrimination, or other barriers. Others drop out of school due to a lack of support, motivation, or interest. However, it is essential that young people should be in school for several reasons.

Firstly, being in school provides young people with a safe and supportive environment where they can learn and grow. It offers them the opportunity to interact with peers, learn from experienced teachers and mentors, and engage in extracurricular activities that foster personal growth and development.

Secondly, being in school helps to prevent social exclusion and marginalization. It provides young people with a sense of belonging and community, which is essential for their well-being and social development. It also helps to promote social cohesion and understanding, which is crucial for building peaceful and inclusive societies.

Finally, being in school helps to prepare young people for the challenges and opportunities of the future. It equips them with the skills and knowledge they need to pursue their goals and aspirations, and to contribute to the greater good. Education also helps young people to develop a sense of purpose and direction, which is essential for personal growth and fulfillment.

In conclusion, education is a fundamental human right and a crucial aspect of human development. It provides young people with the knowledge, skills, and opportunities they need to succeed in life and to make a positive impact on society. Therefore, it is essential that young people should be in school, where they can learn, grow, and thrive. By investing in education for young people, we can create a better future for all.
