The answer is A. The Anthropocentric

One of the contributing factors to the environmental damage (crisis) that has continued to occur so far is an error in perspective (paradigm), which refers to the ethics of anthropocentrism. As a result of this perspective, it has led humans to behave in certain ways, both towards each other and towards the natural environment. The Anthropocentrism paradigm views humans as the center of the universe and only humans have value, while nature and everything in it is merely a means of satisfying the interests and needs of human life.

Anthropocentrism is a theory of the relationship between humans and the environment that views humans as the center of the universe's systems. Humans and their interests are considered the most decisive in the order of ecosystems and in the policies taken in relation to nature, either directly or indirectly. The greatest value is people and their interests.

Only humans have value and get attention. Everything else in this universe will only get value and attention insofar as it supports and is in the interest of man. Therefore, nature is only seen as an object, a tool, and a means to fulfill human needs and interests. Nature is only a tool for the achievement of human goals. Nature has no value in itself.

Anthropocentric, which emphasizes the aesthetic aspect of nature and anthropocentric ethics that prioritizes the interests of the next generation, Meanwhile, anthropocentric ethics that emphasize the welfare of the next generation is based on the protection or conservation of nature aimed at the next generation of humans. This anthropocentric ethic understands that nature is the source of human life. Therefore, this will encourage the emergence of a new human who will reflect prudence and moderation or frugality towards the environment.
