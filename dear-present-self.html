15. what is the tone use in poem dear self​


Answer:

The poet's attitude toward the poem's speaker, reader, and subject matter, as interpreted by the reader. Often described as a “mood” that pervades the experience of reading the poem, it is created by the poem's vocabulary, metrical regularity or irregularity, syntax, use of figurative language, and rhyme.

Dear Self,

I hope this letter finds you well. I know it's been a while since we've had a heart-to-heart talk, and I think it's time we had one.

Firstly, I want to commend you for all the hard work you've been putting in. You've been through so much, yet you still manage to get up every day and give your best. I'm proud of you for pushing through your struggles and never giving up. Keep up the good work, and know that your efforts do not go unnoticed.

However, I also want to remind you that it's okay to take a break. You don't always have to be on the go. Rest is just as important as work, and taking a step back to recharge can do wonders for your mental and physical health.

I also want to remind you that it's okay to make mistakes. You're not perfect, and you never will be. Embrace your imperfections, and use them as opportunities to learn and grow. Remember, every mistake is a chance to do better the next time around.

Don't forget to take care of yourself. Eat well, exercise regularly, and get enough sleep. You can't pour from an empty cup, so prioritize your self-care and make it a non-negotiable part of your routine.
