3. The principal of Magtibay High School wants to estimate the average score of its students in the National Achievement Test with 90% confidence. He is willing to incur an error of only up to ten with the knowledge that the past results yield a standard deviation of 22.5. Find the minimum sample size that he must obtain to construct this confidence interval.


Answer:

To estimate the average score of the students in the National Achievement Test with 90% confidence and an error of only up to 10, we can use the formula for sample size calculation in estimating a population mean.

The formula is:

n = (z^2 * σ^2) / E^2

where:

n = minimum sample size

z = the z-score corresponding to the desired level of confidence (90% confidence corresponds to a z-score of 1.645)

σ = the population standard deviation (22.5)

E = the maximum error margin or tolerance level (10)

Plugging in the given values, we get:

n = (1.645^2 * 22.5^2) / 10^2

n = (2.722 * 506.25) / 100

n = 136.805 or 137 (rounded up)

So the minimum sample size that the principal must obtain to construct this confidence interval is 137.
