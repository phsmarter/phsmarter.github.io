23. Write one of your experiences in barangay health centers, clinics or hospitals.​​


Answer:

As my friends were applying for jobs in retail stores and offices, I decided to go a different route and apply to work in the field of healthcare. Little did I know that decideing to work in a nursing home would change my entire perception of the world. Seven months ago, I walked into to the nursing home and ever since then they have been like my second family. The staff and every resident are welcoming and loving. I can confidently say no other profession connects you with the people more than the nursing job.

Among the things I have learned in my practice, one of the most important is that helping people is extremely rewarding. On numerous occasions when on duty, someone is brought in who is almost nearing death. The quicker I respond to the situation will determine whether the resident lives or not. It is as if the lives of the people are given to you to take care. Most of the people I take care of are the elderly who are past their 80s. I have received a great deal advice and wisdom in my interaction with them and this has made me take life differently and carefully. Sometimes they smile at me from their beds and sometimes they will hold my hand and say, ‘thank you’. It is such an increadble experience to know that I can help someone . Even to see them walk out on their own, recovered and strong is enough consolation for my entire practice in nursing. Sometimes the patients feel lonely and in need of company. Maybe someone to sit by and talk to them. In these seven months, I have come to know that these little acts of kidness and attention we give to our patients makes a big differences.
