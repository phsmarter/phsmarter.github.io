SPEECH ON DRUG ABUSE 1

Dear Students – Warm Greetings to all of you! I welcome everyone to the school seminar hall.

Today, we are here to discuss about the fatal consumption of drugs and how it is destroying the lives of our youth. But before the discussion begins, I would like to deliver a short speech on drug abuse and would like to enlighten our students on this sensitive subject matter.

Drugs, as we all know, are an illegal matter which some people drink, smoke, inject or eat for the mental as well as physical effects that it leaves. There are several students who consume drugs out of fun or for various other reasons. People who deal in the selling of drugs create a network and mainly target students in order to make them addicted to drugs. Initially, the drugs are sold to the students for free and gradually when they get addicted to it, they start buying and consuming it. In fact, the students also eventually become a part of their network and start dealing in it.

It is observed that students start consuming drugs out of stress or unfair expectations of their teachers as well as parents. Lack of emotional support and disorder in their families make them vulnerable and increase their dependency on drugs. They use it in order to calm down their state of mental agitation. Other than this, it is poverty that compels some students to become a part of the selling and dealing network of drugs and derive their pocket money. Therefore, it becomes like a vicious circle of the drug addicted students from which they seem to find no escape unless the society comes for their rescue.

Sadly, the students fail to realize that the consumption of drugs has a severe impact on their physical and mental health, such as they start experiencing mental disorder, their intelligence level decreases, experience deadly diseases and untimely death. When one completely succumbs to the temptation of drugs, it gets very difficult for him/her to recover from its impact except in the centers for drug rehabilitation where they gain a new life, but which involves a high cost. In fact, there are many cases too where even rehabilitation centers fail because of the worst condition of the patients.

I, therefore, request all the students to refrain themselves completely from the consumption of drugs before it destroys their health, future and takes away their lives. I want to conclude by saying that drug abuse should be an absolute ‘No’ for all and I sincerely hope that our students will never ever try to consume drugs and will completely stay away from its use. Remember that our country needs you as you are its future and harbinger of progress.

Thank You!

17. Short speech about culture​


Answer:

Friends, I wish to say a few words about "Culture". It is vast topic really. But I will say a little of what I know.

Introduction : what is it and what do we understand by saying culture

Culture comprises of all the good ways of living of people in a civilization. Culture consists of the beliefs, customs, traditions, ethical and moral values of groups of persons. People with similar culture live together in general. People of a region have a culture that evolves over many decades.

Culture also means various performing arts like dance, instrumental music, singing, painting, drama, creating writing, craft making, debating, elocution and many more. These are often characteristic of a civilization.

Continuing on :

Culture of a person means the way a person speaks, behaves, responds to various incidents and the conduct of the person including his manners. Culture has a very important impact on the growth of a person or a society. When there is no culture, people in the society do not have a common understanding of what is good and what is not good.

Our culture tells us what practices, if we follow, will enable us to live better in our region. Nowadays, the fast growing India has adopted several cultures and has forgotten many of its traditions and cultures. The olden traditions and value systems are being forgotten and new values systems are coming in from all parts of the world. This may be good for some Indians. But respecting one's own culture and upholding is be our responsibility.

The culture is changing from village and small town living style to city and apartment in micro families, shopping malls, pubs, cinemas, entertainment etc.

conclusion :

Often we try to praise western culture and insult our own. Our culture gives us a great identity. Be proud of it. Protect it and uphold it.

22. speech about relationships 250 w0rds


Answer:

Good day everyone,

Today I want to talk to you about relationships. Relationships are a fundamental part of our lives, and they come in many different forms. We have relationships with our family, friends, partners, colleagues, and even with ourselves.

But what makes a good relationship? I believe that the key to a healthy and fulfilling relationship is communication. Communication involves listening, understanding, and expressing oneself in a way that is honest, respectful, and compassionate. It is about being open and receptive to the thoughts, feelings, and needs of others, and being willing to share our own thoughts, feelings, and needs.

Good communication is the foundation of a strong and healthy relationship. It allows us to build trust, connection, and intimacy with others, and to resolve conflicts and challenges in a constructive and positive way.

But communication is not the only important aspect of a good relationship. It is also important to show empathy, kindness, and support to those we care about. This might involve offering words of encouragement, lending a helping hand, or simply being there for someone when they need us.

In addition, it is also important to set boundaries and maintain our own sense of self-respect and self-worth in our relationships. This means standing up for ourselves and our values, and not sacrificing our own needs and well-being for the sake of someone else.

In conclusion, relationships are an essential part of our lives, and good relationships require communication, empathy, kindness, and self-respect. By fostering these qualities in our relationships, we can build strong, healthy, and fulfilling connections with others. Thank you.

28. speech about relationships 250 words


Answer:

Good morning/afternoon everyone. Today I would like to talk about relationships, and the importance of them.

Relationships are an important part of our lives. They provide us with companionship, emotional support, and a sense of belonging. They can also help us develop self-confidence and emotional resilience.

We all have different kinds of relationships in our lives - family, friends, colleagues, partners. Each of these relationships has its own set of challenges and rewards. It’s important to nurture these relationships and to be open and honest with those closest to us.

When we are in healthy relationships, we are more likely to feel supported, respected and valued. We can share our feelings and experiences with those around us, and we can rely on them to be there for us when we need them.

Unfortunately, not all relationships are healthy. If a relationship becomes abusive, or if we feel neglected or taken for granted, it can cause us to feel isolated, anxious, or even depressed. In these cases, it is important to seek help and support, and to get out of the unhealthy situation as soon as possible.

No matter what kind of relationship we have, it’s important to remember that relationships are a two-way street. We all need to be willing to put in effort and to give and take, in order for the relationship to be successful.

In conclusion, relationships are an important part of our lives, and we should always strive to keep them healthy and positive. Thank you for listening.
