Sure, here are five examples of personification:

"The wind whispered through the trees." In this sentence, the wind is personified as if it has the ability to whisper, which is a human characteristic.

"The sun smiled down on us." The sun is personified as if it can smile, which is a human expression of emotion.

"The flowers danced in the breeze." The flowers are personified as if they have the ability to dance, which is a human activity.

"The waves crashed angrily against the shore." The waves are personified as if they are capable of feeling anger, which is a human emotion.

"The clock ticked away the hours." The clock is personified as if it has the ability to tick away time, which is a human activity.

In each of these examples, non-human entities or objects are given human characteristics or abilities. Personification is a literary device used to create a more vivid and engaging description of the world around us.
