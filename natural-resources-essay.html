14. write a short essay on how will you protect a plant that is a natural resources.nonsence-report​


Plants are a valuable natural resource that play a significant role in our lives. They provide us with food, medicine, oxygen, and many other essential products. As such, it is crucial to protect and conserve plants to ensure that they remain available for future generations.

To protect a plant as a natural resource, the first step is to identify the species and determine its ecological importance. Once the plant is identified, it is necessary to ensure that its natural habitat is preserved and that it is not destroyed by human activities such as logging, mining, or urbanization.

One way to protect plants is to create protected areas such as national parks, nature reserves, and wildlife sanctuaries where they can grow and thrive undisturbed. These areas provide a safe haven for plants and other wildlife to flourish without the threat of human interference.

Another way to protect plants is through sustainable harvesting practices. If a plant is used for its medicinal or economic value, it is essential to ensure that the harvest is done in a way that does not harm the plant's health or survival. This may involve implementing regulations that limit the amount of harvest allowed, the timing of the harvest, and the use of sustainable harvesting methods.

Finally, educating people about the importance of plants as natural resources and the need to protect them is crucial. Public awareness campaigns, school programs, and community initiatives can be used to create awareness about the value of plants and the need to protect them.

In conclusion, protecting plants as natural resources requires a collective effort that involves government, communities, and individuals. By creating protected areas, implementing sustainable harvesting practices, and promoting public awareness, we can ensure that plants remain available for future generations.

21. claim of policy and claim of value essay about natural resources ​


Answer:

A claim of policy essay about natural resources could argue for specific actions that need to be taken to conserve or manage these resources. On the other hand, a claim of value essay would make an argument about the inherent worth or importance of natural resources.

Claim of policy essay:

Natural resources are finite and valuable, and it is our responsibility to ensure that they are conserved and managed sustainably for future generations. To achieve this goal, we must take action to reduce waste, protect ecosystems, and promote sustainable development practices.

One way to reduce waste is to implement more efficient technologies that use natural resources more judiciously. For example, we can encourage the development of renewable energy sources like wind and solar power to reduce our reliance on non-renewable resources like coal and oil. We can also promote the use of recycled materials to reduce waste and conserve natural resources.

To protect ecosystems, we must ensure that human activities do not cause irreversible harm to biodiversity and natural habitats. This requires careful planning and management of land use, as well as the establishment of protected areas and wildlife corridors. Additionally, we must work to mitigate the impact of climate change on ecosystems, which threatens to cause widespread extinction of plant and animal species.

Finally, to promote sustainable development practices, we must prioritize the use of natural resources in ways that are equitable and do not harm future generations. This requires a shift in the way we think about economic growth and development, moving away from short-term gains and towards long-term sustainability.

Claim of value essay:

Natural resources are invaluable treasures that provide essential services to humanity and support all life on earth. They are the foundation of our existence, providing us with food, water, energy, and materials for shelter, clothing, and medicine. Natural resources also provide recreational and aesthetic value, enriching our lives and connecting us to nature.

However, despite their immense value, natural resources are often taken for granted and exploited without regard for their long-term sustainability. This leads to a loss of biodiversity, degradation of ecosystems, and depletion of finite resources.

It is essential that we recognize and appreciate the inherent value of natural resources, not just in terms of their economic worth but also in terms of their ecological and social importance. We must protect and conserve these resources for their own sake, as well as for the sake of future generations.

Moreover, we must shift away from an exploitative and extractive mindset towards a more holistic and sustainable approach to resource management. This requires a fundamental change in our values and attitudes towards nature, recognizing that natural resources are not commodities to be consumed and discarded but rather gifts to be treasured and stewarded responsibly.

27. make an essay about natural resources of the philippines 10 sentence​ minimum​


Answer:

The Philippines is a country that is rich in natural resources. Its location in Southeast Asia makes it a hub of biodiversity, with a varied and unique ecosystem. The country's natural resources include minerals, forests, marine resources, and agricultural land.

One of the most significant natural resources of the Philippines is its mineral deposits. The country has vast reserves of minerals such as gold, copper, nickel, and chromite. These minerals are found in various parts of the country and are important sources of income for the mining industry. However, mining activities have also led to environmental degradation and loss of biodiversity.

The Philippines is also known for its diverse forests. The country has a range of forest types, including tropical rainforests, mangrove forests, and dipterocarp forests. These forests provide habitats for wildlife, regulate water flow, and are important sources of timber. However, illegal logging has led to deforestation and loss of biodiversity in the country.

The marine resources of the Philippines are also significant. The country's waters are home to a variety of fish species, including tuna, sardines, and mackerel. These fish are important sources of food for the Filipino population and are also exported to other countries. However, overfishing has led to a decline in fish stocks, posing a threat to the country's fishing industry.

Agricultural land is another essential natural resource of the Philippines. The country's fertile soil is suitable for growing crops such as rice, corn, and sugarcane. The agricultural sector is a vital contributor to the country's economy, providing employment opportunities for millions of Filipinos.

The Philippines is also rich in geothermal energy and natural gas. The country has several geothermal power plants that provide energy to the country's grid. The country also has natural gas reserves that are currently being developed, providing an alternative source of energy.

Despite the abundance of natural resources in the Philippines, there are challenges in their management and conservation. Illegal logging, overfishing, and mining activities have led to environmental degradation and loss of biodiversity. It is essential to implement sustainable practices to ensure that these resources are used responsibly and preserved for future generations.

In conclusion, natural resources are vital for the economic growth and development of the Philippines. The country's abundant mineral deposits, forests, marine resources, and agricultural land provide opportunities for growth and prosperity. However, it is crucial to balance economic development with environmental protection to ensure sustainable use of these resources.
