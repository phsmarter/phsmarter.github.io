20. how to improve uncomfortableness (fear) with public speaking?


Answer:

1. Prepare: Take the time to prepare your speech and practice it out loud. This will help you become more comfortable with the material and reduce your fear of forgetting something or saying something wrong.

2. Visualize: Visualize yourself giving a successful speech in front of an audience. Imagine how you will look, sound, and feel when you are speaking confidently and successfully.

3. Breathe: Take deep breaths before you start speaking to help calm your nerves and relax your body.

4. Focus on the Audience: Instead of focusing on yourself, focus on the audience and how you can help them understand what you are saying. This will help take the pressure off of yourself and make it easier to speak confidently in front of them.

5. Talk to Someone: Find someone who is also nervous about public speaking and talk to them about it before your speech. This can help both of you feel more comfortable with the situation and give each other support before going up to speak in front of an audience.

24. persuasive speech of fear of public speaking please help me with this :)​


Answer:

Good evening, everyone. I would like to talk to you today about the fear of public speaking. This is a common fear that affects many people, and it can be a major obstacle to achieving success in both personal and professional life.

First of all, it's important to understand that the fear of public speaking is completely normal. In fact, it's been said that public speaking is the number one fear among people, even more common than the fear of death. This is because when we speak in front of others, we put ourselves in a vulnerable position. We open ourselves up to criticism and judgement, and it can be very scary.

But here's the thing: the fear of public speaking can be overcome. And the key to overcoming it is to understand that it's not the speaking itself that we're afraid of, but rather it's the fear of the unknown, the fear of the unknown outcome.

One way to overcome this fear is to prepare yourself well. Practice your speech multiple times, know your material, and be familiar with the audience. With good preparation, you will feel more confident and relaxed when it's time to speak.

Another way to overcome the fear of public speaking is to focus on the audience. Instead of thinking about yourself and your own fears, think about the people who are listening to you. When you focus on the audience, you will be less likely to get caught up in your own thoughts and fears.

Finally, remember that it's okay to make mistakes. Nobody is perfect, and it's important to remember that it's not the end of the world if you make a mistake. The important thing is that you learn from it and move on.

In conclusion, the fear of public speaking is a common fear, but it can be overcome. With good preparation, focus on the audience, and a willingness to learn from mistakes, you can conquer your fear and become a confident and effective speaker. Thank you for listening
