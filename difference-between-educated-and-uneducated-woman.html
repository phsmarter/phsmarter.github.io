30. the mangyan tribe of mindoro are backwards people because they are uneducated.


Yes, the Mangyan people in Mindoro are backward people because they are not educated. The Mangyan tribe inhabits the island of Mindoro. Called uneducated because they do not get a proper education. The Mangyan tribe is increasingly marginalized because it is pressured by mining activities. Young Mangyans don't want a school that is surrounded by concrete and closed walls, like a school built by a German priest.

Mangyan Tribe Education

Mangyan Tribe Community Places Mangyans children are often placed second in public school relationships. Finally, many of them are traumatized by the unfair treatment of a more advanced civilized society.

Tribal School for Mangyans, Mangyan Tribe has its own way of maintaining its original culture. This school is not like the schools founded by the German clergy. This school is named Tugdaan, standing on a land grant that opened in 1989 with a total of 12 students. Until 2007 has had 120 students.

This school is not like the public schools established by the government with concrete walls. The walls and roofs of the classrooms are made of thatch as is their traditional house. This school was built on the basis of the need for a type of education that can prepare young people to develop according to the Mangyan culture.

Mangyan people in Mindoro

Mangyan is a collective name referring to the eight tribes of indigenous peoples native to the island of Mindoro and southwest of the island of Luzon in the Philippines. The total population may be around 280,000, but official statistics are hard to come by in remote areas, where some tribal groups are secretive and isolated from the outside world.

Mangyan is a native of Mindoro.

At first, they mostly inhabited the coast. Then moved to the interior and mountains to avoid the arrival and influence of foreign settlers such as Tagalog, and Spain and the conquest and spread of religion; also plunder and slave trade by Moro pirates.
