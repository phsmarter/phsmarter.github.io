19. In biology, what is meaning of the ATP?


Answer:

What is the meaning of ATP?

- ATP is a nucleotide derivative so it has those 3 parts you'd see in DNA and RNA nucleotides: phosphate, sugar, base, but it actually has 3 phosphates. ATP is short for its full name, ADENOSINE TRIPOSPHATE. Its full name is helpful as it tells you that it contains the nitrogenous base known as adenine, and 3 phosphates, hence the "tri" in Adenosine Triposphate. Its sugar is ribose.
