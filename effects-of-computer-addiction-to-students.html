14. what is the effect of mobile gamingaddiction in the mental health of seniorhigh school student?​


Mobile gaming addiction can have negative effects on the mental health of senior high school students.

Firstly, excessive mobile gaming can lead to a lack of sleep and poor sleep quality. This can lead to problems concentrating, increased irritability, and changes in mood, which can affect academic performance and day-to-day functioning.

Secondly, mobile gaming addiction can lead to social isolation, anxiety, and depression, as students may prioritize gaming over socializing with friends and family. This lack of social interaction can also lead to a lack of exposure to outside interests, leading to the development of a narrow range of interests.

Finally, mobile gaming addiction can also lead to substance abuse, as students may use gaming to self-medicate and escape reality. If left unchecked, addiction to mobile gaming can lead to more serious addiction disorders.

It is important for students and their parents to monitor and regulate mobile gaming habits to maintain a healthy balance in their lives, and to seek professional help when an addiction or mental health issue is suspected.

25. write a 500 word synthesis and summary of EFFECT OF ADDICTION TO ONLINE GAMES TO STUDENTS ACADEMIC PERFORMANCE ​


Answer:

Addiction to online games is a growing problem among students that can have negative effects on their academic performance. With the rise of technology and easy access to online games, students are becoming increasingly addicted to these games, which can lead to poor grades, lack of motivation, and even drop out of school. This essay will examine the effects of addiction to online games on student academic performance and how it can be prevented or managed.

The addiction to online games can have a significant impact on student academic performance. Studies have shown that students who spend a lot of time playing online games tend to have lower grades, reduced motivation, and higher rates of absenteeism. These students often neglect their studies and fail to meet academic expectations. The problem of addiction to online games is also associated with psychological and social issues, such as depression, anxiety, and social isolation, which can further impact academic performance.

The negative effects of addiction to online games on student academic performance can be mitigated through various interventions. One approach is to limit students' access to online games and monitor their use. Schools and parents can work together to develop strategies for reducing the amount of time students spend on online games, such as setting limits on the amount of time students can spend on games per day or week. Additionally, schools can offer support services to students struggling with addiction to online games, such as counseling, education, and alternative activities to help them build more positive and productive habits.

Another approach is to incorporate online games into the learning process, such as using educational games to teach important concepts and skills. This approach can help students see the value of online games beyond mere entertainment and can help them develop a more positive attitude towards learning. By incorporating online games into the curriculum, schools can also help students build the skills necessary to succeed in a digital world, such as critical thinking, problem-solving, and collaboration.

Schools and parents can work together to create a supportive environment for students struggling with addiction to online games. This includes providing emotional support, setting achievable goals, and celebrating successes. By creating a supportive environment, students can feel more motivated and empowered to overcome their addiction to online games and improve their academic performance.

Summary:
Online gaming has become a popular leisure activity among students, but its negative effects on academic performance have been a concern for educators and parents. This paper aims to summarize and synthesize recent research on the impact of addiction to online games on students' academic performance. The paper found that addiction to online games is associated with a decline in academic performance, as students become distracted, experience sleep deprivation, and become disengaged from their academic responsibilities. Additionally, addiction to online games can also lead to social isolation and interpersonal conflicts, further impacting academic performance.

Synthesis:
A number of studies have been conducted on the effects of addiction to online games on students' academic performance, with many highlighting the negative consequences. For example, a study conducted by Li and colleagues (2020) found that addiction to online games was associated with poor academic performance and lower grades. The study involved 369 students and found that those who reported higher levels of addiction to online games had lower GPA scores than their peers who did not play games excessively.

Similarly, a study by Chiu and colleagues (2017) found that addiction to online games was negatively correlated with academic achievement. The study involved 2,484 high school students and found that those who reported higher levels of addiction to online games were more likely to have lower grades and lower academic aspirations.

One of the main reasons for the negative impact of addiction to online games on academic performance is that it can lead to distraction and procrastination. When students become addicted to online games, they may spend more time playing than studying, leading to poor time management and reduced academic engagement. A study by Pontes and Griffiths (2016) found that online gaming addiction was associated with decreased academic engagement and lower academic performance, as students spent more time gaming and less time studying.

In addition, addiction to online games can lead to sleep deprivation, which can further impact academic performance. A study by Khazaal and colleagues (2016) found that online gaming addiction was associated with poor sleep quality and shorter sleep duration, which in turn can lead to decreased cognitive functioning and impaired academic performance.

Moreover, addiction to online games can lead to social isolation and interpersonal conflicts, further impacting academic performance. Students who become addicted to online games may withdraw from social activities and relationships, which can impact their social and emotional development. Additionally, students who become addicted to online games may experience interpersonal conflicts with peers and family members, which can cause stress and further impact academic performance.

In conclusion, addiction to online games can have a negative impact on students' academic performance. Studies have shown that addiction to online games is associated with distraction, procrastination, sleep deprivation, social isolation, and interpersonal conflicts, all of which can lead to poor academic performance. Educators and parents need to be aware of the negative consequences of addiction to online games and take steps to help students balance their leisure activities with their academic responsibilities. This may involve setting limits on screen time, encouraging physical activity and social engagement, and providing support for students who may be struggling with addiction. By taking a proactive approach, educators and parents can help students achieve their academic goals and develop healthy habits for the future.
