2. Choose the letter of the sentence that shows the correct sentence construction. Select one: a. Using drones can report stories without getting risks of reporter's personal injuries. b. Using drones can report stories without getting risks of personal injury to reporters . c. Using drones can report stories without getting risks to reporters of personal injury. d. Reporters can report using drones without getting risks to personal injury to reporters.


Answer: B. Using drones can report stories without getting risks of personal injury to reporters.

In a sentence construction, the order of words in order to support the preceding idea (subordinate clauses, direct and indirect objects) and the parts of speech used (articles, prepositions, conjunctions) are all important.

For this statement, “Using of drones can report stories” is already a complete sentence (because it has a subject which is “using of drones” and predicate which is “can report stories). The clause “without getting risks of personal injury to reporters” is a supporting idea for the complete idea to deliver a clearer message. “Personal injury” refers to what will reporters get in the middle of doing their work (especially covering risky and life-staking events). That is why, “to” is used to connect the phrases. “Without getting risks” is what describes “personal injury” that can be get by reporters. One can interchange the order of the clauses or even the words, for as long as the words proceeding and/or preceding it are the supporting idea and direct object and some words are restated or even added or changed, the sentence will still make sense.
