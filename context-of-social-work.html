What context is being hooked with the social context when the literary work or era

The social context of a text refers to how the characteristics of the society in which it is placed influence its meaning. The social context refers to both the society in which the characters live and the society in which the author's material was written in a text.

Jane Eyre, written by Charlotte Bront, was set in the same socioeconomic milieu as she did. It was the time when women were regarded as the inferior sex, when there was a significant split between a wealthy and the poor, and when tight class divisions were maintained. All of these societal characteristics have a role in influencing Bront's opinions on the necessity of social inclusion and her support for women's strength.(text)
