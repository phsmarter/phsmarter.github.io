8. advantages and disadvantages of part time jobs.(pasagot po ng maayos.)​


Answer:

One of the many types of flexible jobs is called a part-time job. A person who has a part-time job, compared to a full-time job, can work in a fewer working hours. Typically, part-time jobs require working hours of not more than thirty five hours per week. Most of the time, the shifts are rotational. It often involves working in shifts like within morning, afternoon, and evening. The employment entitlements received by the part-time employees are the same as the full-time workers on a pro-rata basis.

When it comes to part-time work just like in any other works, the advantages and disadvantages are always present for employers and employees.

The advantages of part-time work include:

The busy periods may be covered with part-time workers.As for the part of the employees, having a part-time job where they can choose their own schedule, is more attractive for it allows them to have more time doing things or more work at home or in their full-time jobs. Thus, employees can possibly have a work-life balance.

The disadvantages of part-time work include:

Scheduling meetings and coordinating projects may be difficult having the fact that these part-time employees also have other priorities.

Lots of people from different walks of life, due to the various daily needs that need to be met, are involved in part-time jobs. They include young moms and dads, students, retirees, people who want to start their own business and need more time, and all other workers who can’t work, or don’t need, a full-time job.

People who have part-time jobs are somehow goal-oriented and hard-working people. They are often the people who use their spare time to work and earn more money in order to survive every single day.

15. reclamation of some parts of manila bay will cause serve floods in coastal area in cavite.​


Explanation:

The reclamation of some parts of Manila Bay can have severe implications on the surrounding coastal areas, particularly in Cavite. The process of reclamation involves filling up coastal areas with sand and other materials, which can have a significant impact on the natural drainage and flow of water in the area. This, in turn, can cause flooding in nearby communities.

Cavite is known to be one of the most flood-prone provinces in the country, and the reclamation project can exacerbate the situation. The loss of natural wetlands and mangroves due to the reclamation can also have an adverse impact on the ecosystem, as these areas serve as natural buffers against flooding and storm surges.

It is essential to consider the long-term consequences of the reclamation project, particularly in terms of its impact on the environment and the communities that surround the area. There needs to be a comprehensive plan that takes into account the potential risks and provides adequate measures to address them.

Alternative solutions should also be explored, such as the rehabilitation of existing coastal areas and the promotion of sustainable development practices. By prioritizing the preservation of the environment and the protection of the communities, we can create a more sustainable and resilient future for everyone.

In conclusion, the reclamation of some parts of Manila Bay can indeed cause severe floods in coastal areas in Cavite. It is crucial to take a holistic and proactive approach to address the issue and ensure that the project's impact is carefully considered and mitigated. We must prioritize the protection of the environment and the well-being of the communities in our efforts to promote development and progress
