10. example of TOS in Elementary​


Context 1

... TOS provides a two-way chart to help teachers relate their instructional objectives, the cognitive level of instruction, and the amount of the test that should assess each objective ( Nortar et al., 2004). Table 3, illustrates a modified TOS used to develop a summative test for a unit of study in a 5 th grade Social Studies class. The TOS provides a framework for organizing information about the instructional activities experienced by the student. ...

View

Context 2

... review of Table 3 reveals a 7 column TOS (labeled A-G). The information in columns A, B, and C are taken directly from the teacher's lesson plans and reflective notes. ...

View

Context 3

... the top of Column E of Table 3, you should note that for this test the teacher has decided to use 10 items. The number of items to include on any given test is a professional decision made by the teacher based on the number of objectives in the unit, his/her understanding of the students, the class time allocated for testing, and the importance of the assessment. ...

View

Context 4

... we believe that the ideal test is one that students can complete in the time allotted, with enough time to brainstorm any writing portions, and to check their answers before turning in their completed assessment. The creator of the TOS in Table 3 decided to create a Fives & DiDonato-Barnes, Table of Specifications 10 item test that would include 7 multiple choice items and 3 short answer items. Just a reminder, this is a professional decision made by the teacher based on his/her knowledge of the students, the classroom context, and the role of this test in relation to other assessments for the grade period. ...

View

Context 5

... making this decision a teacher should consider the best way to get the desired information from the student. For instance, in Table 3, the teacher has indicated that he/she will use a short answer question to assess objective 2 "Summarize the contributions of different religious and cultural groups to the settlement of the Middle Atlantic Colonies." While this is considered a low level thinking item, simply rephrasing the material taught in class, it lends itself to a short answer item because students are required to put these descriptions in their own words rather than just selecting from a series of choices. ...

25. B. DIRECTIONS: 8 A speech about why a town needs a new elementary school


Ladies and gentlemen,

I stand before you today to talk about an issue that is of utmost importance to the future of our town - the need for a new elementary school.

As a community, we all know how crucial education is to the development and well-being of our children. Quality education is the cornerstone of a successful and prosperous society, and it is our duty as adults to provide our youth with the best possible education. Unfortunately, our current elementary school is no longer up to the task. It is old, understaffed, and ill-equipped to meet the needs of the growing number of students in our town.

Not only is this a detriment to our children's education, but it also hinders the town's economic and social development. A lack of education will lead to fewer job opportunities, lower wages, and a general decline in the quality of life for our community. Moreover, if we fail to invest in the education of our children, we are effectively closing the door on their future, and the future of our town.

It is for these reasons that we must seriously consider the need for a new elementary school. By investing in our school, we are investing in the future of our community. We are ensuring that our children are equipped with the knowledge and skills they need to succeed in the modern world, and we are providing them with the foundation for a better future.

Of course, this project will not be easy or inexpensive, but the benefits far outweigh the cost. The long-term benefits to our community will be immense, and the investment in our children's education will pay dividends for generations to come.

In conclusion, the need for a new elementary school in our town is clear. It is crucial for the growth and development of our community, and it is our responsibility as adults to ensure that our children receive the best possible education. Let us not falter in our commitment to our children and our community. Let us work together to make this project a reality, and let us build a brighter future for ourselves and for our children. Thank you.
