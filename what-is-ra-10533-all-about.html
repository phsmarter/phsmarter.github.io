2. Why was RA 10533 legislated?


Why was RA 10533 legislated?

10533, entitled “An Act Enhancing the Philippine Basic Education System by Strengthening Its Curriculum and Increasing the Number of Years for Basic Education, Appropriating Funds Therefor and for Other Purposes,” otherwise known as the “Enhanced Basic Education Act of 2013.”


3. Relationship of RA 10533 and the K to 12 framework​


Answer:

The passage of the Enhanced Basic Education Act, or Republic Act 10533 aims to ensure the continuity of the reform beyond this generation, and into the next. The K to 12 system was signed into law with the passage of the Enhanced Basic Education Act of 2013 (Republic Act 10533).

The K to 12 Program covers Kindergarten and 12 years of basic education (six years of primary education, four years of Junior High School, and two years of Senior High School [SHS]) to provide sufficient time for mastery of concepts and skills, develop lifelong learners, and prepare graduates for tertiary education, ...

9. what is this RA 9003 all about?


RA 9003 also known as the Ecological Solid Waste Management Act of 2000 is an act that aims to solve problems related to Solid Waste Management.  In the Philippines, Solid Waste Management (SWM) is considered to be one of the most serious environmental issues. This act mandate local government to reduce waste reduction. It makes provision for efficient solid waste management and for environment-friendly disposal of waste. This act describes solid waste management as a discipline associated with the control of generation.

Things to consider under this act:

Ensure the protection of public health and environment.Utilize environmentally-sound method.Set guidelines and targets for solid waste.Ensure the proper waste segregation, collection, and disposal.

Steps to manage and segregate waste:

Make sure to separate containers for dry and wet waste in the kitchen.Always keep two bags for dry and wet waste collection.For sanitary waste, keep a paper bag at hand at all times.
