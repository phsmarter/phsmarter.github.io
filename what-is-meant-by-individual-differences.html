5. How is heat different from temperature? What is meant by heat capacity? Why is the heat capacity of water unique?​


Answer:

Heat describes the transfer of thermal energy between molecules within a system and is measured in Joules. Heat measures how energy moves or flows. ... Temperature describes the average kinetic energy of molecules within a material or system and is measured in Celsius (°C), Kelvin(K), Fahrenheit (°F), or Rankine (R)

Heat capacity is defined as the amount of heat required to raise the temperature of a mass of substance by 1°C.

Water has the highest specific heat capacity of any liquid. ... Specific heat is defined as the amount of heat one gram of a substance must absorb or lose to change its temperature by one degree Celsius. For water, this amount is one calorie, or 4.184 Joules.

26. what is meant by bayanihan during the pre-colonial philippines different from buying the pandemic​


Answer:

Bayanihan is a Filipino custom of communal cooperation or mutual aid among people in a community. In pre-colonial Philippines, bayanihan was often practiced in rural areas where members of a community would come together to help a neighbor move their house or farm to a new location. This involved physically carrying the whole house or farm to the new site, with everyone in the community lending a hand.

During the pandemic, the term "bayanihan" was used again, but in a different context. It referred to a collective effort to help those who were affected by the pandemic, whether through donations, volunteering, or following health protocols to prevent the spread of the virus. While the spirit of communal cooperation and mutual aid was still present, the actions taken during the pandemic were more focused on addressing a crisis rather than a specific task or need.

Overall, bayanihan in pre-colonial Philippines and during the pandemic both involve the idea of coming together as a community to help one another, but the specific actions and context in which it was practiced were different.
