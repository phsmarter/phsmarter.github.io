Explanation:

Child development is a process every child goes through. This process involves learning and mastering skills like sitting, walking, talking, skipping and tying shoes. Most children learn these skills, called developmental milestones, during predictable time periods. Milestones develop in a sequential fashion. This means that a child will need to develop some skills before he or she can develop other skills. For example, children must first learn to crawl and to pull up to a standing position before they are able to walk. Each milestone that a child acquires builds on the last milestone developed.

20. Scenario: The students are giving each other informal and peer feedback on their essays. They start by offering positive feedback on what they liked about each other's essays, then move on to suggestions for improvement. The feedback is specific and actionable, focused on areas where the essays can be strengthened. The teacher reinforces the importance of feedback and encourages a positive and open-minded approach to it.​


This scenario is a great example of how peer feedback can be effective in improving students' writing skills. By starting with positive feedback, students are encouraged and motivated to continue working on their essays. This approach helps build confidence and fosters a positive learning environment. Moreover, the feedback being specific and actionable provides students with specific areas for improvement, making the process more effective. It also helps them develop critical thinking skills and the ability to evaluate and improve their own work.

The teacher's reinforcement of the importance of feedback and the encouragement of a positive and open-minded approach to it helps create a safe and supportive classroom environment. It also helps students learn the importance of giving and receiving feedback, not just in writing, but in all aspects of their lives.

Articulating positive feedback and offering suggestions for improvement are important skills that can benefit students in many areas of their lives. By providing specific and actionable feedback, students can grow and improve in their writing, communication, and critical thinking skills, which are essential for success in the academic and professional worlds.
