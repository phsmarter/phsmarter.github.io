Here's an example of an autobiography for a student:

My name is John, and I was born and raised in a small town in the midwest. Growing up, I was always a curious kid and loved exploring my surroundings. I remember spending hours outside, climbing trees and playing with my friends.

As I got older, my curiosity turned into a love for science and technology. I spent countless hours tinkering with computers and learning how they worked. I knew from a young age that I wanted to pursue a career in the tech industry.

In high school, I took every computer science class available and even started my own coding club. I spent most of my free time working on personal projects, and even created my own app that was featured in a local tech magazine.

After graduating, I was accepted into a prestigious computer science program at a top university. The coursework was challenging, but I loved every minute of it. I joined several student organizations, including a robotics club and a coding team, and even interned at a tech startup.

Outside of school, I enjoy hiking and camping in the nearby mountains. I also love playing video games with my friends and exploring new technologies.

Looking towards the future, I hope to continue my education and earn a master's degree in computer science. My dream is to one day work for a major tech company and create products that make a positive impact on people's lives.

Overall, my passion for technology and curiosity about the world around me have shaped me into the person I am today, and I can't wait to see what the future holds.

18. how hard is it to be a college student ​


Answer:

It’s hard if you have poor time management skills, take too many classes, try to work too many hours at a separate job, don’t plan your class selection well so you can graduate on time, or if you try to cram for tests on the last week of classes rather than across the term.

It’s quite manageable if you are willing to cut back your social life, do school work, read, and study from 8:00 a.m. until 5:00 p.m. each week day, and review materials over the course of the whole semester rather than cramming.

However, even if you do it right, it will still be harder than high school for the gen ed courses. Many students pass high school if they show up and are breathing. College won’t work that way, and there won’t be people watching out for you to force you to do the work.

So, a better question isn’t, “Is being a college student hard?” The better question is, “Is that college student self-motivated and disciplined?” That is the more important thing to ask when it comes to success.
