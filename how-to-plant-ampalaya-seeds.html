What is the rate of seeding that he needs to apply Required distance planting 500cm x 200 cm 2. Villa Quintana Farm has an area of land of 8000 cm x 7000 cm, the management wants to plant carrots on half of the land and wants to plant calamansi on the other half. Compute the rate of seeding for both crops. Carrots: Required distance planting 200cm x 200 cm calamansi: Required distance planting 4500cm x 300 cm 3. Manang Elsa planted ampalaya on her farm with an area of 900 cm x 800 cm, what is the rate of seeding that she applied? Ampalaya: Required distance planting 300cm x 250 cm 4. A sugar cane owned by the Dantes family has an area of 2000 cm x 3500 cm. Compute the rate of seeding. Required distance planting 800cm x 200 cm Compute for the following show your solution:​


Answer:

1.Mang Caloy has a piece of land that measures 1900 cm by 1500 cm. If he wants to plant onions on this land and the required distance for planting is 500 cm by 200 cm, he will need to apply a seeding rate of [(1900 cm x 1500 cm) / (500 cm x 200 cm)] = 48 plants.

2.Villa Quintana Farm has a piece of land that measures 8000 cm by 7000 cm. If they want to plant carrots on half of this land and calamansi on the other half, they will need to apply a seeding rate of [(8000 cm x 7000 cm) / 2] / [(200 cm x 200 cm) + (4500 cm x 300 cm)] = 20 plants for the carrots and [(8000 cm x 7000 cm) / 2] / [(200 cm x 200 cm) + (4500 cm x 300 cm)] = 5 plants for the calamansi.

3. the area of the land on which Manang Elsa planted ampalaya is 900 cm x 800 cm = <<900800=720000>>720000 square centimeters.

The required distance planting for ampalaya is 300 cm x 250 cm = <<300250=75000>>75000 square centimeters.

Therefore, the rate of seeding for Manang Elsa's ampalaya farm is 75000 / 720000 = 0.10416667.

4. the area of the Dantes family's sugar cane farm is 2000 cm x 3500 cm = <<20003500=7000000>>7000000 square centimeters.

The required distance planting for sugar cane is 800 cm x 200 cm = <<800200=160000>>160000 square centimeters.

Therefore, the rate of seeding for the Dantes family's sugar cane farm is 160000 / 7000000 = 0.02285.

Answer:

Flowering plants are divided into two groups - monocots and dicots. Recognizing which of these two

groups a plant belongs to is a great time saver when you are out in the field trying to identify a plant

using a key. The names or these groups are derived from the number of

cotyledons or seed leaves that the embryonic seedling has within its seed. A

monocot, which an abbreviation for monocotyledon, will have only one cotyledon

and a dicot, or dicotyledon, will have two cotyledons. However, this distinction will

not help you when you are trying to determine which group a plant belongs to if it

is no longer a seedling. And, sometimes doesn’t even help if you are looking at a

seedling, because many plants do not bring their cotyledon(s) above the soil

surface when they germinate as the seedling shown to the left did.

Here are some key features to look for to determine whether a plant is a monocot or a dicot.

Leaves of dicots have veins that are branched. Sometimes the veins branch out on either side of the leaf

from a middle vein, in an arrangement resembling a feather (pinnate arrangement) shown in the picture

below left. Other dicots have veins that branch out from a single vein, like
