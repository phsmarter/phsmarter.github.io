It is difficult to identify illegally traded timber. Legally, there are no laws against timber products or imports made out of illegal wood. Illegal logging leads to the destruction of forests and loss of biodiversity. It also has economic and social impacts on the poor. Many poor communities living near forests benefit from forest products. However, as demand for timber continues to rise, these poor communities lose out to logging companies and powerful interests.

Furthermore, illegal logging is often associated with organized crime, corruption, human rights abuse, violent conflict and money laundering. It undermines proper forest management, reduces the income of the producer countries and encourages tax evasion and corruption.

Deforestation caused by illegal logging leads to soil erosion. Scientists have also found links between deforestation and global warming. Illegal logging affects indigenous people, as they have no legal claim to the land on which they live. Therefore, logging companies and governments are able to profit from the forests by evicting these people.

11. What are the good and bad effects of a total log ban?​


Explanation:

1. Reduced air pollution from burning wood.

2. Reduced deforestation and habitat destruction from logging activities.

3. Increased water quality in rivers and streams due to reduced sediment runoff from logging activities.

4. Increased biodiversity as forests are allowed to regenerate naturally without human interference.

5. Reduced risk of forest fires due to reduced fuel sources from logging activities.

Bad Effects:

1. Loss of jobs in the forestry industry, which could have a negative economic impact on local communities that rely on the industry for their livelihoods.

2. Increased cost of lumber and other wood products due to reduced supply and increased demand for these products in the market place.

3. Potential for illegal logging activities as some people may attempt to circumvent the ban by harvesting wood illegally or through black market channels.

4. Reduced access to wood for traditional uses such as heating, cooking, and construction in rural areas where alternative sources of energy are not available or affordable
