The friction occurs between the skateboard and the pavement. This fluid friction is responsible for allowing the boy to move because the skateboard's wheels are able to grip onto the pavement's surface, creating a frictional force that propels the skateboard forward as the motion is transferred from the rider's feet to the wheels. Without this friction, the skateboard would not be able to move and the boy would remain stationary.
