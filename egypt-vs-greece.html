1. what is the character in prince of egypt?what is the protagonist in prince of egypt?what is the antagonist in prince of egypt?what is the conflict in prince of egypt?what is man vs. man, man vs. self, man vs. society, man vs. supernatural in prince of egypt?what is the point of view in prince of egypt?what is the first person and third person in prince of egypt?what is the theme in prince of egypt?​


Answer:

1.The main character in Prince of Egypt is Moses.

2.The protagonist in Prince of Egypt is also Moses, as the story follows his journey from a prince of Egypt to a leader of the Hebrews.

3.The antagonist in Prince of Egypt is Pharaoh Ramses II, who refuses to release the Hebrews from slavery.

4.The main conflict in Prince of Egypt is the struggle between the Hebrews and the Egyptians, with Moses caught in the middle.

5.There are elements of man vs. man (Moses vs. Ramses), man vs. self (Moses struggling with his identity and purpose), man vs. society (the Hebrews' struggle for freedom and equality), and man vs. supernatural (the plagues sent by God).

6.The point of view in Prince of Egypt is third person, with occasional glimpses into Moses' thoughts through voiceover narration.

7.The theme of Prince of Egypt is the transformative power of faith and the importance of freedom and equality for all people.

9. it is indoor game that was Known in ancient Greece and Egypt​


Answer:

Battledore and shuttlecock, children’s game played by two persons using small rackets called battledores, which are made of parchment, plastic, or rows of gut or nylon stretched across wooden frames, and shuttlecocks, made of a base of some light material, such as cork, with trimmed feathers fixed around the top. Players try to bat the shuttlecock back and forth as many times as possible without allowing it to fall to the ground. Ancient Greek drawings represent a game almost identical with battledore and shuttlecock, and it has been popular in China, Japan, India, and Thailand for at least 2,000 years. It has been played in Europe for centuries. Badminton is a further development of the game.

24. 1What is the role of art in the rich History of Egypt, Greece and Romans? ​


Answer:

Some of the first mummies, monumental sculptures, and other ancient Egyptian artifacts to come to America became part of the holdings of the Museum of Fine Arts, Boston, as early as 1872. Today, with over 60,000 objects, the MFA's Egyptian collection is one of the largest and most comprehensive in the world. This volume features over 100 of the most important of these objects, with texts by three prominent Egyptologists. Introductions to each major period of ancient Egyptian history provide an overview of the worshiped rulers, regional lords, and myriad laborers who commissioned and created these beautiful works, as well as the political events, religious rituals, and technical accomplishments that helped shape them. Individual descriptions of each object tell of their original use and significance. Some, such as the rare bust of Ankhhaf, or the monumental stone sculpture of King Menkaure and his queen, have no equal anywhere. Others boast a unique local history, such as the Torso of King Achoris that was purchased as ballast by a Yankee sea captain and later displayed for sixty years on a postmaster's front lawn. Whatever one's interest, this book provides a tantalizing glimpse into the civilization that has fascinated the west since Antony first wooed Cleopatra.
