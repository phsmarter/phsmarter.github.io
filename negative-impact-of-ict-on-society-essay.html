6. ACTIVITY 6.2. – Positive and Negative Impacts of ICT​


Positive Impact:

•Improved access to education, such as distance learning and online tutorials. Access to information: Possibly the greatest impact of ICT on individuals is the exponential change in access to information and services that has accompanied the growth of the Internet. new methods of education, such as virtual reality and interactive multimedia. new employment possibilities, such as positions in the communications sector, virtual offices, and flexible and mobile working. The second main impact of ICT is the availability of new instruments that are not really fully possible, which also opens up new options. Many of them are related to the previous access to information, but there are lots of examples of stand ICT systems as well.

Negative Impact:

One of ICT's most serious side effects is the possibility of job loss. This has negative social and economic effects, including lost status and self-esteem and income loss. Several factors, such as the digitalization of manual tasks, might result in job losses. I.e., workers on an assembly line could be replaced by technology. Reduced interpersonal contact: Working from home is typically considered as a benefit of utilizing ICT, however there can also be drawbacks. The majority of people involve a lot of social interaction in their everyday lives, and if they are unable to interact with others they may feel lonely and miserable.
