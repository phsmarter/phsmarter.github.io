Character trait of Ephemera helped itself to enjoy life by:

—Be Humble. The highest form of humility is the acceptance of our limitations – even if acknowledging our limitless potential – while unflinchingly recognizing the truth of humanity’s interdependence and our reliance on something or someone higher than us.

—Be Courageous. At exactly that point where courage falters, is the point at which all other character traits fail as well. In other words, courage is needed to nail every other character trait to the wall of integrity. Loving the loveable is easy. But loving the unlovable takes courage. Be Grateful.

18. The setting describes where and when the story takes place. It helps build background and create images in the mind. Elements of Setting 1. Place - This tells the geographical location of the story. It is where the story is taking place. Examples: in a village, in the house, in the backyard 2. Time - It refers to the length of time in which the story unfolds (as short as a day or as long as 1000 years or more. It also refers to time period. It tells when the story takes place. a. historical period examples: long time ago, middle ages, colonial time b. time of the day examples: in the morning, at twelve o'clock, late in the afternoon c. year examples: a year ago, next year 3. Weather Condition - Tells the condition of the atmosphere Examples: rainy, sunny, stormy, etc. 4. Social Condition - It refers to the daily life of the characters. Does the story mention the speech, dress, mannerisms, customs, etc. of a particular place?​


Historical events - This refers to the significant events that have taken place in the time period in which the story is set. It helps create a context for the story and can impact the characters and the events in the story.

Physical environment - This refers to the natural and man-made features that surround the characters in the story. This can include mountains, oceans, forests, cities, towns, etc.

Political and economic conditions - This refers to the political and economic systems in place in the time and place in which the story is set. This can impact the characters and events in the story.

Culture and tradition - This refers to the beliefs, customs, and traditions of the people in the time and place in which the story is set. This can also impact the characters and events in the story.

Overall, the setting is an important element in a story as it helps to create a sense of place and time, and it can have a significant impact on the events and characters in the story.
