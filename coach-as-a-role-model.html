5. In the learner-centered teacher takes on the role of facilitator: guide, coach, conductor, midwife, gardener. In what way you can explain the above profession to be as facilitator?​


Answer:

As a facilitator, the teacher takes on the role of guiding, coaching, and supporting students in their learning journey. Instead of being the sole source of knowledge, the teacher encourages and facilitates active and collaborative learning among students. The teacher creates a learning environment that fosters exploration, inquiry, and critical thinking. The teacher also serves as a conductor, orchestrating and coordinating learning activities and resources to ensure that students achieve their learning goals. Like a midwife, the teacher nurtures and supports the growth and development of students, helping them to overcome obstacles and challenges. Finally, like a gardener, the teacher cultivates and tends to the learning environment, providing the necessary resources and support for students to thrive and flourish. Overall, the teacher as a facilitator empowers students to take ownership of their learning, and encourages them to become lifelong learners.

15. What are the roles of management, teachers, trainers, coaches, and workers in the effective use of personal protective equipment maintenance before, during and after job performance? pa help!​


Answer:

By law, workers must use personal protective equipment in the workplace when it is required. Employer responsibilities include providing instruction on what PPE is needed, maintenance and cleaning of the equipment, and educating and training workers on proper use of PPE. In every jurisdiction, it is clear that the employer is responsible for making sure these requirements are met.

22. Is the athletes/ or someone undergoing coaching accountable to a desirable coaching outcome? Or is it only to the coach solely? Why?​


Answer:

Both the athletes and the coach are accountable for a desirable coaching outcome.

The coach is responsible for creating and implementing effective training plans, providing feedback, and motivating the athletes. They are also responsible for creating a positive and supportive team environment where athletes feel encouraged to reach their full potential.

On the other hand, athletes are responsible for putting in the effort, following the training plans, and communicating effectively with the coach. They are also responsible for taking ownership of their own development and progress towards their goals.

Ultimately, the success of coaching is a collaborative effort between the coach and the athletes. Both parties must work together, communicate effectively, and hold each other accountable to achieve the desired outcomes.
