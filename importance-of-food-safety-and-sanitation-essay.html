12. explain the importance of proper sanitation and safety of food that we eat everyday​


Answer:

Proper sanitation and safety of the food we eat every day are critical for ensuring good health and preventing foodborne illnesses. Here are some of the key reasons why:

Prevents the spread of harmful bacteria and viruses: Proper sanitation practices help to eliminate or reduce the presence of harmful bacteria and viruses that can cause foodborne illnesses. These include Salmonella, E. coli, Listeria, and Norovirus.

Maintains food quality: Maintaining proper sanitation and safety practices helps to preserve the quality of food, preventing spoilage and contamination that can lead to food waste and economic losses.

Reduces the risk of food poisoning: Eating contaminated food can lead to food poisoning, which can cause symptoms such as nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, and fever. In severe cases, it can lead to hospitalization or even death.

Protects vulnerable populations: Infants, young children, pregnant women, the elderly, and people with weakened immune systems are particularly vulnerable to foodborne illnesses. Proper sanitation and safety practices can help protect these populations from harm.

Ensures compliance with regulations: Food safety regulations are in place to protect consumers and ensure that food is safe to eat. Adhering to these regulations helps to maintain the public's trust in the food supply and protects the reputation of food businesses.

Overall, proper sanitation and safety practices are essential for protecting public health, preventing foodborne illnesses, and ensuring the quality and safety of the food we eat every day.

As students learn the skills required to become future food handlers, it's essential to emphasize the importance of maintaining high standards of food safety and sanitation.
