18. Give me 5 folk crafts of Siquijor and leyte. ​


Answer:

Siquijor:

Bolo Making - Handmade knives with intricately designed wooden handles are a popular craft in Siquijor.Banig Weaving - Banig mats are woven from indigenous materials such as pandan leaves and are used for sleeping, sitting, and decoration.Coconut Shell Craft - Coconut shells are transformed into bowls, plates, and various household items through intricate carving and design.Tuba Basketry - Baskets made from woven bamboo strips are used for storing and transporting Tuba (coconut wine) in Siquijor.Folk Dance Crafts - Traditional dances such as the Kuratsa and Tinikling are popular in Siquijor and the costumes, props, and decorations used for these dances are handmade.

Leyte:

Banig Weaving - Similar to Siquijor, Leyte is also known for its banig mats woven from pandan leaves.Tinalak Weaving - Tinalak is a traditional fabric woven by the T’boli people of Mindanao and is also produced by some artisans in Leyte.Coconut Shell Craft - Leyte's artisans also use coconut shells to create various handicrafts including lamps, bowls, and vases.Talisay Weaving - Talisay leaves are used to make hats, bags, and other woven items.Beadwork - Beadwork is a popular folk craft in Leyte, and local artisans create intricate designs on clothing, jewelry, and home décor using colorful beads.

Refers to the dances created and performed collectively by the ordinary people. it is diverse, and include religious, ceremonial, courtship, funeral, combative, exorcism, torture, comic and game dances.Tinikling is one of the most popular folk dances in the Philippines which originated during the Spanish colonial era – particularly in Leyte, one of the Visayan Islands.The Tinikling is said to be named after the long-legged bird called the tikling in the Philippines.For the dance, females traditionally wear a dress called balintawak or patadyong, and males wear an untucked embroidered shirt called the barong Tagalog. The balintawak are colorful dresses with wide arched sleeves and the patadyong is a pineapple fiber blouse paired with checkered skirts.To dance the Tinikling, you need two pairs of bamboo poles that are each 6–12 feet long (about 1.8–3.7 meters). Female dancers usually wear a dress called a Balintawak (a colorful dress with arched sleeves) or a Patadyong (a checkered loose skirt that's often worn with a thin-fibered blouse).

28. It is a dance that describe the social life of a peasants ofLeyte.​


Answer:

Ang sagot po jan ay Tiklos.

Explanation:

The Tiklos is a native peasant dance of Leyte. During breaks the peasants enjoy themselves with tuba (a native wine) and the rest dance the tiklos accompanied by the subing (plawta), guimbal and tambora drums or when available, the “sista” played by the band. The Tiklos music is also played to call them back to work.
