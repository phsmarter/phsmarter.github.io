25. What characteristic of global citizenship do I possess and what characteristics do I still need to develop in order to be a global citizen? Cite one real experience wherein you have applied or not applied one ethical obligation of a global citizen.​


Answer:

Global citizenship refers to a sense of belonging to a broader community beyond national borders and recognizing the interdependence of people and nations. It involves a commitment to promoting peace, justice, and sustainability, as well as respecting and valuing cultural diversity.

One characteristic of global citizenship is an awareness of global issues and an ability to analyze and evaluate information from diverse sources. Another characteristic is a willingness to take action to address global problems, whether through personal or collective efforts.

To become a global citizen, one may need to develop skills such as intercultural communication, empathy, and critical thinking. Additionally, a global citizen should have a deep sense of social responsibility, respect for human rights, and a commitment to ethical values.

One ethical obligation of global citizens is to promote social justice and equality. This can be applied in many ways, such as supporting efforts to reduce poverty, promoting access to education and healthcare, or advocating for human rights.

One real experience of applying an ethical obligation of a global citizen could be volunteering for a non-profit organization that works towards improving the lives of marginalized communities. On the other hand, not applying an ethical obligation of a global citizen could be ignoring or denying the impact of climate change and failing to take actions to reduce our carbon footprint.

Overall, becoming a global citizen is a lifelong journey that requires continuous learning and growth, and a commitment to promoting a more just, sustainable, and peaceful world.
