Thesis statement of death penalty for sex offenders?

In the 1990s, states enacted a plethora of new "get tough" laws targeting sex crime. These included extending the death penalty--a punishment typically reserved for murderers--to convicted sex offenders. Little attention, however, has been given to explaining why these tougher responses emerged and, in particular, whether the public supported extending the use of the death penalty to sex offenders. The goal of this paper was to examine whether public perceptions about executing sex offenders accorded with the punitive shift in policy and, more broadly, to contribute to scholarship on the death penalty. To this end, this paper examined data from a 1991 national public opinion poll, conducted just prior to the punitive shift in sex crime policies. The study found that views about executing sex offenders depended heavily on whether the victim was a child, that support for executing sex offenders was substantially lower than for executing murderers, and that few social and demographic divides differentially predicted support for executing sex offenders versus murderers. Implications of the study are discussed.

14. What is death penalty? What are the arguments in favor of death penalty? Discuss. What are the arguments against death penalty? Discuss.


WHAT IS DEATH PENALTY?

- capital punishment, also known as the death penalty, is the state-sanctioned killing of a person as punishment for a crime. The sentence ordering that someone is punished with the death penalty is called a death sentence, and the act of carrying out such a sentence is known as an execution.

WHAT ARE THE ARGUMENTS IN FAVOR OF DEATH PENALTY?

- aguments in favour of capital, punishment: retribution, deterrence, rehabilitation, prevention of re-offending, closure and vindication, and incentive to help police.

What are the 3 arguments against death penalty?

If someone murders someone else, they have given up their human rights, including the one to stay alive themselves.The punishment should 'fit the crime' - if you have killed someone, you should be killed too.Giving a killer the death sentence will stop them - and others - doing it again.

WHAT ARE THE ARGUMENTS AGAINST DEATH PENALTY?

Capital punishment is cruel and unusual. Capital punishment denies due process of law. The death penalty violates the constitutional guarantee of equal protection. The death penalty is not a viable form of crime control. Capital punishment wastes limited resources.

22. make an argumentative essay ... should death penalty be used in penalizing criminals? compose with thesis statement and evidences and introduction, body,conclusion at least 150 words ​


Answer:

The death penalty, also known as capital punishment, is a controversial topic that has been debated for decades. While some argue that it is a necessary tool to punish the most heinous crimes and deter criminal behavior, others believe that it is an ineffective and inhumane punishment that should be abolished. In this essay, I will argue that the death penalty should not be used in penalizing criminals.

First and foremost, the death penalty is ineffective as a deterrent to crime. Many studies have shown that states with the death penalty have higher rates of murder than those without it. This suggests that the threat of the death penalty does not effectively prevent people from committing crimes. In addition, the death penalty is often applied unfairly and disproportionately to certain groups of people, such as people of color and those with mental illnesses. This is a clear violation of the principle of equal justice under the law.

Furthermore, the death penalty is costly and time-consuming. The appeals process for death penalty cases is lengthy and expensive, and it often takes years or even decades for a condemned person to be executed. This means that a significant amount of taxpayer money is being spent on a punishment that has been proven to be ineffective.

In conclusion, the death penalty is an ineffective, inhumane, and costly punishment that should be abolished. Instead of focusing on retribution, we should be investing in programs that address the root causes of crime and provide support and rehabilitation for those who have committed crimes. By doing so, we can create a more just and humane society for all.
