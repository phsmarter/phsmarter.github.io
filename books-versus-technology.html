15. in your own idea explain picture books versus illustrated books ​


Picture books and illustrated books are both forms of children's literature, but they have distinct differences.

A picture book is a book where the pictures play an equal role to the text in telling the story. The illustrations in a picture book are often colorful, detailed, and imaginative, and they often help to convey the mood and atmosphere of the story. Picture books are designed for children, and their simple language and colorful illustrations make them appealing to young readers.

Illustrated books, on the other hand, are books that contain illustrations to accompany the text. The illustrations in an illustrated book serve as a visual complement to the text and help to bring the story to life. Illustrated books can be aimed at children or adults, and the illustrations can vary in style from simple drawings to complex, detailed illustrations.

In summary, while both picture books and illustrated books contain illustrations, picture books place equal emphasis on the pictures and the text to tell the story, while illustrated books use the illustrations as a supplement to the text
