Summary:

"Rise" is a short film that tells the story of a young boy named Ahmad, who lives in a poor community in Singapore. Ahmad aspires to be a football player, but his father forbids him from pursuing his dream, insisting that he should focus on his studies instead. Despite his father's disapproval, Ahmad remains determined to achieve his goal.

Characters:

The main character, Ahmad, is played by a young actor named Adib Kosnan. He effectively portrays the character's determination and passion for football. Ahmad's father, played by actor Tony Eusoff, is a strict and traditional man who believes that education is more important than sports.

Setting:

The film is set in a poor community in Singapore, where Ahmad and his family live in a small apartment. The setting contributes to the film by showing the challenges that Ahmad faces in pursuing his dream due to his family's financial struggles.

Lesson:

The film teaches us that we should pursue our dreams and passions, even if others discourage us from doing so. It also shows the importance of family support in achieving our goals.

Likes/Dislikes:

I liked the film's message of perseverance and determination in pursuing one's dreams. I also appreciated how it portrayed the challenges that come with living in a poor community. However, I felt that the film's pacing was a bit slow and the ending was somewhat predictable.

Recommendation:

I would recommend this film to anyone who enjoys inspirational stories and coming-of-age tales. It is also a good movie to watch with family and friends, as it can spark discussions about pursuing one's dreams and the importance of family support.
