5. How does an artist convey his or her meaning through materials and techniques? Search and look for a Rizal Monument and describe it to illustrate your answer. Write your answer in a 50-100 words essay that describes the process of making the monument, what it is made of, and how it is made.​


Answer:

An artist can convey meaning through materials and techniques by using them to create a visual representation of their ideas and emotions. For example, the Rizal Monument in Rizal Park, Manila, Philippines was built to commemorate the executed Filipino nationalist, José Rizal. The monument was designed by Swiss sculptor Richard Kissling and unveiled in 1913. It consists of a standing bronze sculpture of Rizal with an obelisk set on a stone base within which his remains are interred ¹. The materials used, such as bronze and granite, convey a sense of strength and permanence, while the techniques used to create the sculpture, such as casting and carving, allow for intricate details and lifelike representation. Through these materials and techniques, the artist is able to convey the importance of Rizal's legacy and the impact he had on Philippine history.

Answer:

topic sentence

Dr. Jose Rizal was a great Filipino hero. He is the smart and brave. He the love his country. He does it through peaceful means. He is the greatest Filipino hero of all time this is why he was considered as our national hero.

supporting details

Dr. Jose Rizal was a great Filipino hero. He is the smart and brave. He the love his country. He does it through peaceful means. He is the greatest Filipino hero of all time this is why he was considered as our national hero.

concluding sentence

Dr. Jose Rizal was a great Filipino hero. He is the smart and brave. He the love his country. He does it through peaceful means. He is the greatest Filipino hero of all time this is why he was considered as our national hero.

Explanation:

In expository writing, a topic sentence is a sentence that summarizes the main idea of a paragraph. It is usually the first sentence in a paragraph. Also known as a focus sentence, it encapsulates or organizes an entire paragraph.

Supporting details are reasons, examples, facts, steps, or other kinds of evidence that explain the main idea. Major details explain and develop the main idea.

A concluding sentence indicates that you are bringing closure to a paragraph. It should not include any information that was not discussed in the paragraph.
