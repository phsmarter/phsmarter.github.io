23. please patulong Di ko kasi maintindihan.Transform the following direct speech to indirect speech.1.Carmi said, "My parents are the best in the world".2.Henry mentioned, "My father is my idol".3."He is working very hard to provide our needs", Henry added.4."They have always been around when we need them", Donna reiterated.5."I can't tell you the secret", Christina said.​


When we are reading stories or novels, or when we watch movies, we would read or hear the characters saying, "She said," or "He said," when they talk about what other people said. When we speak, it's the same. We may say, "Ang sabi nga ni..."

The speaker's word may be written or spoken in two ways. It could be a direct speech or an indirect speech.  

A direct speech is the exact words said by the speaker. It is usually enclosed into quotation marks (" ").  

Examples:  

(Note: Verbs are underlined)

Present Tense: My mom always says, "The world does not revolve around you, my daughter. Love your neighbor."

Future Tense: I know that when I go to her place later, my grandma will tell me, "You need to be good girl."

Past Tense: She said, "My life is not easy to live because I have a lot of problems."

An indirect speech is also saying what the speaker said, only it does not use his or her exact words.  

Examples:  

(Note: verbs are underlined)

Present tense: My mom always says that the world does not revolve around me, so I must love my neighbor.  

Future Tense: I know that when I go to her place later, my grandma will tell me that I need to be a good girl.  

Past Tense: She said that her life was not easy to live because she had a lot of problems.

If we will analyze the tense, verbs in present and future tense did not change. Only those verbs in past tense are changed.

Answers to your questions:

1. Carmi said that her parents were the best in the world.

Extra explanation: We changed the possessive pronoun from my to her.

2. Henry mentioned that his father was his idol.

Extra explanation: We changed the possessive pronouns from my to his.

3. Henry added that he was working very hard to provide for their needs.

Extra explanation: We changed the possessive pronoun our to their.  

4. Donna reiterated that they had always been around when they needed them.  

Extra explanation: We changed the tense from it being present perfect to past perfect.

5. Christina said that she can't tell me the secret.  

Extra explanation: We changed the object pronoun me to you.
