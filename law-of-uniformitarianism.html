4. what is the definition of uniformitarianism


Answer:

Uniformitarianism, in geology, the doctrine suggesting that Earth's geologic processes acted in the same manner and with essentially the same intensity in the past as they do in the present and that such uniformity is sufficient to account for all geologic change.


5. Who proposed the principle of uniformitarianism?


Coined by William Whewell, the word was proposed in contrast to catastrophism by British naturalists in the late 18th century, starting with the work of the geologist James Hutton. Hutton's work was later refined by scientist John Playfair and popularised by geologist Charles Lyell's Principles of Geology in 1830.James HuttonCoined by William Whewell, the word was proposed in contrast to catastrophism by British naturalists in the late 18th century, starting with the work of thegeologist James Hutton. 16. definition of uniformitarianism


Answer:

The theory that changes in the earth's crust during geological history have resulted from the action of continuous and uniform processes.


17. what are the similarities of catatrophism and uniformitarianism​


Answer:

Both theories acknowledge that the Earth's landscape was formed and shaped by natural events over geologic time. While catastrophism assumes that these were violent, short-lived, large-scale events, uniformitarianism supports the idea of gradual, long-lived, small-scale events.
