4. How is life on Earth affected by the star and the moon?


The star and the moon have significant impacts on life on Earth:

Star (Sun): The sun provides the Earth with light and heat, which are essential for supporting life on the planet. It drives the Earth's climate, seasons, and weather patterns, providing energy for photosynthesis in plants, which forms the base of the food chain.

Moon: The moon influences the tides and ocean currents, affecting marine life and coastal ecosystems. It also affects the Earth's rotation and stability, helping to regulate the length of the day and maintaining the planet's axial tilt, which affects the climate.

In summary, the star and the moon play a crucial role in maintaining the delicate balance of life on Earth, and any changes in their movements and interactions could have significant impacts on the planet's climate and ecosystems.

The Gravitational Pull

The Moon exerts a strong gravitational pull that causes the changing tides in our oceans and seas. This gravitational pull is at its strongest during the New Moon and Full Moon, so here we experience the highest and lowest tides.

It is the pull of the Moon's gravity on the Earth that holds our planet in place. Without the Moon stabilising our tilt, it is possible that the Earth's tilt could vary wildly. It would move from no tilt (which means no seasons) to a large tilt (which means extreme weather and even ice ages).

The brightest and largest object in our night sky, the Moon makes Earth a more livable planet by moderating our home planet's wobble on its axis, leading to a relatively stable climate. In this film, Ishaan, a dyslexic teenager, can't seem to get anything right at his school. Ram Shankar Nikumbh, a new unique art teacher, soon assists him in discovering his hidden potential. This film is about the hope of a student, passion for teaching and learning, caring for a student, and patience in the process.

Explanation:

There is a very good message of education portrayed in the film "Like Start on Earth." The plot of the film is around a young boy named Ishaan Awasthy who has difficulty reading and writing. His family has to send him to a special school because he is believed to be such a foolish kid. However, his life was completely transformed when he met a talented painting teacher and enlisted his assistance. The film appears to expand on a new component of educating children. It is preferable for us to identify children's strengths and weaknesses, and then construct varied activities to assist them to improve those skills.
