10. explain the following values and traditions of celebration of fiesta that are common to Filipino people ​


Answer:

The following are some of the common values and traditions associated with the celebration of fiesta in the Philippines:

Explanation:

Community: Fiesta is a time for Filipinos to come together and celebrate as a community. Neighbors and relatives usually visit each other's homes during the fiesta to share food and drink, and to participate in various festivities.

Hospitality: Filipinos are known for their warm hospitality, and this is reflected in the way they celebrate fiesta. It is customary for families to open their homes to guests and offer them food and refreshments. In fact, it is considered impolite to refuse an invitation to a fiesta celebration.

Faith: Fiesta is a religious celebration, and it is an important time for Filipinos to express their faith and devotion. Many fiesta celebrations are centered around a church or a religious icon, and there are usually processions and other religious ceremonies that take place during the fiesta.

Food: Food is an important part of the fiesta celebration, and there are usually plenty of traditional Filipino dishes served. Some of the most popular fiesta foods include lechon (roast pig), adobo, pancit, and various types of rice cakes.

Music and dance: Filipinos love to sing and dance, and this is reflected in the fiesta celebrations. There are usually live bands or musical performances, and people often participate in traditional dances such as the tinikling or the pandango sa ilaw.

Overall, fiesta is a time for Filipinos to come together as a community, express their faith, and celebrate their culture and traditions. It is a joyous occasion that is deeply ingrained in the Filipino way of life.
