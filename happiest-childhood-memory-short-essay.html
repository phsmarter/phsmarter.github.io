9. childhood memories with passive voice


Passive voice is used when we want to emphasize the verb or the object rather than the doer of the action. The difference of passive and active voice is that active voice emphasizes the doer of the action than the verb being used.  

Example of passive vs. active voice

Active voice: John is dribbling the ball.

Passive voice: The ball was dribbled by John.  

Childhood memories with passive voice:

When I was a child I was always being caressed by my mother. She was always there for me and supports me in everything that I do. She is known as a loving and caring mother.

13. learning task Using the literary elements we discussed, write a three-paragraph essay about the happiest/saddest moment of your childhood. Please underline all the literary devices you used in your essay. ​


Answer:

I grew up in a small town where life was simple and uncomplicated. One of the happiest moments of my childhood was when my parents surprised me with a brand new bicycle on my 10th birthday. The shiny, red bike with silver rims and a bell was everything I had wished for. The moment I laid my eyes on it, my heart leaped with joy, and my face broke into a wide smile. I could feel my cheeks hurting from the excitement, and I couldn't wait to take it out for a ride. As I rode the bike, the wind blowing in my face, I felt a sense of freedom and happiness that I had never experienced before.

On the other hand, the saddest moment of my childhood was when I had to say goodbye to my best friend, who was moving to another city. We had been inseparable since kindergarten, and the thought of not seeing her every day made me feel empty inside. I remember the day she left; I tried to be strong, but as soon as she hugged me, tears started streaming down my face. It felt like a piece of my heart was being ripped away from me. The pain of losing my best friend was overwhelming, and I spent days crying and feeling miserable.

Explanation:

imagery (visual description of the shiny, red bike with silver rims and a bell, the wind blowing in my face)

metaphor (a piece of my heart being ripped away)

hyperbole (my cheeks hurting from smiling too much, the pain of losing my friend was overwhelming)

18. childhood memories essay in english (kahit ikwento mo nalang ung iyo haha)​


Answer:

Childhood is a time of joy, innocence, and wonder. It is a time when we are free to explore and discover the world around us without any worries or responsibilities. My childhood memories are a treasured part of my life, and they have shaped who I am today.

One of my fondest childhood memories is of spending time with my grandparents. They lived in a small town in the countryside, and every summer, my family and I would visit them. My grandparents' house was a place of warmth and love. My grandfather would always have a big smile on his face, and my grandmother would cook delicious food for us. We would spend hours playing in the garden, picking flowers, and chasing butterflies. I remember feeling so happy and free when I was with them.

Another childhood memory that stands out to me is of playing with my friends. We would spend hours playing hide-and-seek, tag, and other games. We would make up our own rules and create imaginary worlds. I remember feeling so alive and carefree when I was with my friends. We would laugh and play until it was time to go home.

I also have memories of my parents reading stories to me before bed. I loved the way their voices would change when they read different characters. I would get lost in the stories and imagine myself in the worlds they described. Those moments were so special to me, and they sparked my love for reading and storytelling.

In conclusion, my childhood memories are a precious part of my life. They have shaped who I am today and have given me a sense of joy and wonder that I carry with me into adulthood. I am grateful for those memories and for the people who were a part of them.

Write short biography essay that compare the students early childhood with rizals own?

 

José Protasio Rizal Mercado y Alonso Realonda He was born on June 19, 1861, some 35 years before that fateful day. The seventh of the eleven children born to a comparatively well-to-do family in the Dominican lands of Calamba, Laguna, Jose Rizal lived and died in the Philippines during the Spanish colonial era.  

Jose's father, Francisco Mercado Rizal, was a prosperous farmer from Binan, Laguna, while his mother, Teodora Alonzo y Quintos, was an accomplished and highly educated woman from Sta. Cruz, Manila.

In his early childhood, Jose had perfected the alphabet, learnt to write and read books like the Spanish translation of the Vulgate Bible. At a young age, he had always shown a tendency towards the arts. His family was fascinated by his pencil paintings, sketches, and clay moldings. Later in his youth, he displayed a special talent in painting and drawing, wrote a Tagalog play, which was presented at the Calamba Festival, and penned a short Spanish play, which was presented at the school.

He went to Spain on 3 May 1882 and enrolled at the Universidad Central de Madrid. He obtained a degree in medicine in June 1884 at the age of 23. A year back, he finished his course of Philosophy and Letters with the rank of "excellent." Wanting to treat his mother's advanced blindness, Rizal went to Paris, Heidelberg, and Berlin to gain more experience and expertise in ophthalmology. He finished his eye specialization in Heidelberg.  

Well-traveled, he is said to have mastered 22 languages. He wrote remarkable poetry, contributed nationalist essays to magazines, maintained his diary religiously, and corresponded to his friends and relatives. In March 1887, in Berlin, he published his first controversial book, Noli Me Tangere, which exposed the injustice and greed of the Spanish clergy and officials in the Philippines. To prove that the Filipinos had an outstanding culture long before the Spanish occupation, Morga's Successos De Las Islas Filipinas was annotated and reprinted in Paris. On September 18, 1891, Rizal's more militant book, El Filibusterismo, was published in Ghent.

In 1896, Rizal was granted permission by the governor general to become a volunteer military physician for the war in Cuba, which at the time still raged from yellow fever. But the 'Katipunan' launched the Philippine Revolution on August 26, 1896. The influential people who were provoked by Rizal's hostility used the opportunity to include him in the revolt. He was guilty of revolt and sentenced to death by firing squad at Bagumbayan Field after a trial in the Kangaroo court (now Luneta).
