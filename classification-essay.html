11. Searle's Classifications of Speech Act Art Theory. essay​


Answer:

John Searle's "Classifications of Speech Act" is a detailed and nuanced exploration of the various ways in which we use language. Searle begins by distinguishing between two different kinds of speech acts: constative and performative. Constative speech acts are those in which we make a statement or claim about the world. Performative speech acts, on the other hand, are those in which we do something by saying something.

Searle then goes on to explore the different types of performative speech acts. He distinguishes between four different kinds: locutionary, illocutionary, perlocutionary, and ulocutionary.

Locutionary speech acts are those in which we simply utter a sentence. Illocutionary speech acts are those in which we perform an action by uttering a sentence. For example, when I say "I promise to do the dishes," I am performing an illocutionary speech act.

Perlocutionary speech acts are those in which we cause someone to do something by uttering a sentence. For example, when I say "You're fired," I am causing the person I'm speaking to to lose their job.

Ulocutionary speech acts are those in which we achieve our goal by uttering a sentence. For example, when I say "I'm sorry," I am trying to achieve the goal of apologizing.

Searle then goes on to explore the different types of constative speech acts. He distinguishes between five different kinds: assertion, description, prescription, explanation, and report.

Assertions are those speech acts in which we make a claim about the world. For example, when I say "The sky is blue," I am making an assertion.

Descriptions are those speech acts in which we describe something. For example, when I say "The sky is a deep blue," I am describing the sky.

Prescriptions are those speech acts in which we prescribe or proscribe something. For example, when I say "You should do the dishes," I am prescribing that you do the dishes.

Explanations are those speech acts in which we explain something. For example, when I say "The sky is blue because of Rayleigh scattering," I am explaining why the sky is blue.

Reports are those speech acts in which we report on something. For example, when I say "I saw a bluebird," I am reporting on something I saw.

24. classification of sstocks essay 150 words​


There are several different types of stocks that investors can choose from, and each has its own distinct set of characteristics. The three main categories of stocks are common stock, preferred stock, and convertible securities.

Common stock is the most basic type of stock and typically represents ownership in a company. Preferred stock usually offers some sort of preferential treatment to shareholders, such as higher dividends or priority in the event of liquidation. Convertible securities can be exchanged for another type of security, such as bonds or common shares.

Each type of stock has its own set of pros and cons that should be considered before making any investment decisions. However, by understanding the difference between these types of stocks, investors will be better equipped to make informed investment choices that fit their individual needs and objectives.

Stocks can be classified in several ways, but the most common classification is based on the size of the company, industry, and growth potential.

Firstly, stocks can be classified by the size of the company, which is commonly measured by market capitalization.
