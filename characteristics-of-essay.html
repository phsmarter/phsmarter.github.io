The Purpose of Argument in Writing

The idea of an argument often conjures up images of two people yelling and screaming in anger. In writing, however, an argument is very different. An argument is a reasoned opinion supported and explained by evidence. To argue in writing is to advance knowledge and ideas in a positive way. Written arguments often fail when they employ ranting rather than reasoning.

A Tip: Most of us feel inclined to try to win the arguments we engage in. On some level, we all want to be right, and we want others to see the error of their ways. More times than not, however, arguments in which both sides try to win end up producing losers all around. The more productive approach is to persuade your audience to consider your opinion as a valid one, not simply the right one.

14. CHARACTERISTICS OFARGUMENTATIVE ESSAY​


Answer:

1. What are the Characteristics of an Argumentative Essay?

Before starting the writing process, you should be aware of structuring your argumentative essay in the right way. To help you out, we have done the research part and compiled a list of must-haves in your essay.

An argumentative essay has the following characteristics:

1.1 Selecting a Topic

One of the most important argumentative essay characteristics is the topic selection. The ultimate focus should be on choosing a topic that is:

1.1.1 Researchable

For a topic to be researchable, the writer should be able to find credible sources and enough research material to support its claim and position.

1.1.2 Debatable

A debatable topic is a controversial topic with several different points of view. For instance, writing about the consequences of rape on society isn’t debatable because no one is going to disagree with this statement. However, if you surround your essay with a particular topic, whether the punishments for rapists are effective in controlling the situation or not is a debatable topic. Another point could be whether the girl is to be blamed for what happened with her, which is something always debatable and you will find different supporting and opposing statistics surrounding it.

1.1.3 Current

A current topic that hasn’t been discussed for years, such as legalization of marijuana, abortion, death penalty, etc.

It is new, relevant to the society and is still discussed in different contexts.

1.1.4 Manageable

It shouldn’t be too broad that it gets impossible to cover all aspects in your essay. Global or national issues are often difficult to manage so you may want to avoid them and narrow down your focus to one aspect of it.

1.2 Debatable Thesis

A strong thesis statement is of utmost importance for an essay to be effective. Your thesis statement should make the reader aware of what the argument is and its importance. It should be clear, clean from any ambiguities and leaving no room it must be debatable, if you are aiming for an in-depth and contemplative type of essay.

1.3 Background Information

Although an argumentative essay differs from a research paper but providing the basic background information on the topic is necessary. Giving the background information in the start of your essay is essential for the understanding of your reader.

1.4 Transitions

The overall structure and smooth transitions are important for a well-structured argumentative essay. This is due to the nature of such essays where multiple reasons and evidence is presented to support the thesis and state counter arguments for comparison.

1.5 Exhaustive Research

Performing thorough and extensive research is incredibly important when it comes to argumentative essays. You can’t defend your point of view and persuade the reader when you aren’t well-researched yourself and don’t have the material to prove your point logically.

Don’t start writing on a topic without looking for valid research material if you don’t want to reach in the middle of the writing process, only to realize that there aren’t enough sources.

1.6 Logos, Pathos and Ethos

Including logos, pathos and ethos in an argumentative essay can be an effective tactic when used logically.

Logos refers to the logic of the debate. To convince someone writers must use logical explanation throughout their essay.

Pathos is the use of emotion. Getting the reader to become emotionally invested in the argument increases the writer’s chances of persuading them.

Ethos is the use of credibility, which can be achieved by presenting credible sources and examples to support your claim.
