Answer:

Instruments can be categorized according to typical use. Instruments are classified using four different categories depending on the manner in which the instrument creates the sound.

Explanation:

CLASSIFICATION OF MUSICAL INSTRUMENTS

Chordophones are instruments that produce sound by vibrating strings. Simple chordophones are sometimes referred to as zither type instruments. Most western chordophones, excluding the piano and harpsichord, fall into the composite chordophone category. Composite chordophones can be broken down into lute type and harp type instruments.

Idiophones are instruments that create sound through vibrating themselves without the use of air flow.

Membranophones are instruments that make sound from the vibrations of stretched skins or membranes. Drums, tambourines, and some gongs are common examples of membranophones.

Electrophone, any of a class of musical instruments in which the initial sound either is produced by electronic means or is conventionally produced (as by a vibrating string) and electronically amplified.
