10. the home economics club made brownies to sell to raise funds for the upcoming school fair. the cafeteria contributed two trays brownies to the club. each tray has 16 pieces of brownies. there were a total of 96 of brownies pieces to sell. how many brownies did the club make ?​


Answer:

The home economics club made 64 brownies.

Step-by-step explanation:

Home economics club = x

cafeteria = y

x+y=96       (adding the brownies made by the club and cafeteria gives us 96    brownies)x+(16*2)=96      (The brownies made by the cafeteria is equal to 2 trays. Each tray is equivalent to 16 brownies. We will multiply the number of brownies in each tray to the number of trays to get the amount of brownies the cafeteria created.)x+32=96      (16 times 2 = 32)x=96-32          (We can move 32 to the other side of the equation to isolate x. When a number is moved to the other side of the equation the sign of the number is changed from a positive to a negative or vice versa. Example: 1+1=2 ---> 1=2-1. We moved the positive 1 to the other side and it gave us a negative number.)x=64               (96 minus 32 is equal to 64)

16. Harry and Ben baked some brownies. When Harry gave Ben 25 brownies, both of them had the same number of brownies. If both of them had an average of 155 brownies each, how many brownies did Harry have at first?​


[tex]\blue{\huge {\mathrm{PROBLEM}}}[/tex]

[tex]\blue{\huge {\mathrm{SOLVING}}}[/tex]

[tex]======================[/tex]

[tex]{\underline{\huge \mathbb{P} {\large \mathrm {ROBLEM : }}}}[/tex]

– Harry and Ben baked some brownies. When Harry gave Ben 25 brownies, both of them had the same number of brownies. If both of them had an average of 155 brownies each, how many brownies did Harry have at first?

[tex]{\underline{\Large {\mathbb{A \normalsize{\mathrm {NSWER : }} {\tt{\green{\; \; 130 \; brownies \; \; }}}} }}}[/tex]

————————————————–—

[tex]{\underline{\huge \mathbb{S} {\large \mathrm {OLUTION : }}}}[/tex]

╰➤ To be able to solve this problem, just subtract the 25 brownies that Harry gave to Ben to 155 average brownies that they both had before Harry gave 25 brownies to Ben. So, therefore;

(Let N be the unknown value)

N = [tex] \tt 155 \; brownies \; - \; 25 \; brownies [/tex]

N = [tex] \boxed{\underline{\tt{\green{130 \; brownies}}}} [/tex]

∴ Thus, Harry has 130 brownies before giving 25 brownies to Ben.
