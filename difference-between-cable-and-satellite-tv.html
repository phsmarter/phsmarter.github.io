2. what is the difference between a satellite and a star?​


Answer:

A satellite is an object that orbits around a planet, while a star is a luminous celestial body that generates its own energy through nuclear fusion.

Satellites can be natural (like the Moon) or artificial (like communication and weather satellites), while stars are naturally occurring celestial bodies that can emit light and heat.

Satellites are much smaller than stars, with the largest natural satellites being moons like Ganymede (the largest moon in the solar system) and the smallest artificial satellites being only a few centimeters wide. Stars, on the other hand, can be massive, with the largest known star being VY Canis Majoris, estimated to be over 1,000 times the size of our sun.

15. What is the difference between UTP and STP cable?​


Answer:

UTP is a cable with wires that are twisted together to reduce noise generated by an external source. On the other hand, STP is a twisted pair cable with an additional mesh shield that guards the cable against electromagnetic interference.


16. what is difference between ethernet and patch cables?


Answer:

Ethernet is a protocol that describes how information travels through a cable. Ethernet cables are simply wires that comply with Ethernet protocols. These cables are made of copper or optical fiber. Patch cables are simply ethernet cables which have connectors at both ends.
