3. What are the basic problems of economics


Answer:

The basic problems of economics are: 1. the problem of scarcity

The first problem is that there are only a limited number of goods and services in the world. Most people would agree that if we were to double the amount of resources that we can use, then our standard of living would double as well. However, most people do not realize just how much we have already taken from the earth. There are only so many trees in the world, and it takes hundreds of years for a tree to grow into a mature forest. If we cut down all those trees, then our standard of living will drop dramatically for thousands of years ahead because we cannot produce enough food without them.

The second problem is one where there is always more than enough money to pay for everything that people want to buy, but people do not understand what they really need because they do not know what will be better tomorrow than today (inflation). For example, if everyone could afford an iPhone 5s tomorrow and they were satisfied with it forever after that, then no one would ever remember how good their old phone was because they would never go back to it again!

13. Every country faces basic economics problems. What are these. How are we going to solve these problems ?​


If there is a central economic problem that is present across all countries, without any exception, then it is the problem of scarcity. This problem arises because the resources of all types are limited and have alternative uses. If the resources were unlimited or if a resource only had one single use, then the economic problem would probably not arise. However, be it natural productive resources or man-made capital/consumer goods or money or time, scarcity of resources is the central problem. This central problem gives rise to four basic problems of an economy.
