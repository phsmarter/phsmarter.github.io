15. Figthing hunger essay based on module


Answer & Explanation:

Fighting hunger is a crucial issue that affects millions of people around the world. Hunger is a problem that not only affects a person's physical health but also has a profound impact on their emotional, social, and cognitive well-being. In this essay, I will discuss the causes of hunger, the impact it has on individuals and communities, and what can be done to fight it.

Hunger is caused by a variety of factors, including poverty, lack of access to food, and natural disasters such as drought and famine. In many developing countries, people do not have the resources to grow or purchase enough food to feed themselves and their families. This is often due to economic and political factors that limit access to resources and markets. In addition, natural disasters can destroy crops, disrupt food systems, and increase the risk of hunger.

The impact of hunger on individuals and communities is far-reaching and devastating. Hunger can lead to malnutrition, which can cause a range of health problems, including weakened immunity, increased risk of disease, and even death. Hunger also has a negative impact on cognitive development, affecting a person's ability to learn and perform tasks. On a larger scale, hunger can lead to poverty and social unrest, as people struggle to meet their basic needs.

Fighting hunger requires a multi-faceted approach that addresses its root causes. One solution is to improve food security by increasing access to resources and markets, and promoting sustainable agriculture practices. Another approach is to provide emergency food aid to communities affected by natural disasters or conflict. In addition, improving access to education, healthcare, and job opportunities can help people lift themselves out of poverty and achieve food security.

In conclusion, hunger is a major global issue that affects the well-being of millions of people around the world. It is caused by a range of factors, including poverty, lack of access to food, and natural disasters. The impact of hunger on individuals and communities is far-reaching and devastating, and requires a comprehensive approach to fight it. By addressing the root causes of hunger, promoting sustainable agriculture practices, and providing emergency aid and support, we can work towards a world where everyone has enough to eat.

27. What are the differences between emotional hunger and physical hunger?​


Answer:

Emotional hunger means that you turn to food either to avoid uncomfortable emotions or to heighten a pleasurable one. Typically, it means that you eat based on how you feel, rather than what your body needs. Physical hunger means that you eat when your body signals to you that you are actually hungry.
