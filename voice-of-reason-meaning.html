10. Using the SOAPSTone Method with Print, Media and Internet Sources Element of Source Description/meaning Critical Questions Verification 1. Is the author in the Speaker viewed material speaking for himself or using the voice or opinion of a particular person or group? 1. What has triggered the author's material (presentation, website, or Occasion document)? 2. Are the reasons for creating the material based on events, emotions, data, or economics?


Answer:

The SOAPSTone method is a useful tool for analyzing various types of sources, including print, media, and internet sources. The first element of this method is Speaker, which refers to the author or creator of the source. The following are some critical questions to consider when analyzing the Speaker element:

1. Is the author in the material speaking for himself or using the voice or opinion of a particular person or group?

- This question aims to determine whether the author is expressing his or her own ideas or representing the views of a particular person or group. It is important to consider the author's background, affiliation, and possible biases that may influence his or her perspective.

2. What has triggered the author's material (presentation, website, or document)?

- This question aims to identify the occasion or event that prompted the author to create the material. It could be a current issue, a recent event, or a long-standing problem that the author wants to address.

3. Are the reasons for creating the material based on events, emotions, data, or economics?

- This question aims to identify the reasons or motivations behind the author's material. It could be based on personal emotions, objective data, economic factors, or a combination of these factors. It is important to assess the validity and reliability of the sources of information used by the author.

By considering these critical questions, one can better understand the Speaker element of a source and its potential impact on the message conveyed by the source.
