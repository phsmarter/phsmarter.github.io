4. Write 3 claims (fact, value, and policy) on the issue of social media and bullying.


When someone bullies another person using electronic or remote media, this is known as cyberbullying. This typically occurs as a result of intimidating or threatening texts. Cyberbullying should be taken seriously at all times, particularly by educators. Educators can help reduce cyberbullying by teaching their pupils to be kind and compassionate, as well as monitoring how students communicate to see if there are any signs of bullying. They will also be a listening ear for students who are being bullied online who need assistance.

Facts:  Every day, more children than we realize are cyberbullied. Cyberbullying affects almost half of LGBTQ+ pupils. Cyberbullying is considered unethical by the majority of young people.

Values: Bullies are using social media to harass others by cyberbullying. Parents can effectively avoid cyberbullying for their children by keeping a close eye on how much time they spend on the internet and seeing who they are talking with while they are having an online discussion. Always reach out to someone you meet who has been the victim of cyberbullying.

Policies: (RA 10627) Sec. 2 When these attacks are carried out online, they are referred to as "cyberbullying." (RA 10627) Sec. 2-D This includes social bullying, which aims to humiliate another person or community, as well as gender-related bullying, which humiliates others based on their assumed gender identity.  In Dagupan City, Pangasinan, parents can now call a hotline to report bullying.

16. why do bullying, ethnic issues and gender issues be considered in the curriculum?


Answer:

To realize equality among human beings.

Explanation:

Bullying, ethnic issues, and gender issues should be included in the curriculum to teach students that equality in society is very important.

In schools, bullying occurs in all areas. It can occur in nearly any part in or around the school building, though it more often occurs during school breaks, in hallways, bathrooms, on school buses, and waiting for buses, classes that require group work, and/or after-school activities.

Gender issues talk about equal relations between men and women, especially in the context of treatment, access, and opportunities in various fields of life. While gender inequality is closely related to discrimination, both de jure and de facto discrimination. De jure discrimination in the context of education is closely related to the rules that distinguish women and men in entering certain fields of education. In de jure, there is no gender discrimination in education because every human has the right to education and to do what they want. De facto has a perception that women and men are different and that certain fields that are only suitable for women and other fields for men. For example, women are more suitable in the field of literature or social, and men in the field of engineering or exact sciences.

Meanwhile, ethnic issues talk about a form of conflict in which the objectives of at least one party are defined in ethnic terms, and the conflict, its antecedents, and possible solutions are perceived along ethnic lines. The conflict is usually not about ethnic differences themselves but over political, economic, social, cultural, or territorial matters.

From the explanation above, bullying, gender issues, and ethnic issues must be considered in the curriculum because to avoid social fracture and split. All at once, give students an understanding of things between people.

26. social issue is cyber bullying human sex trafficking and malnutrition why?​


Answer:

There are many reasons why cyberbullying, human sex trafficking, and malnutrition are considered social issues.

Cyberbullying is a social issue because it can have serious negative effects on mental health, self-esteem, and social relationships. It can also lead to anxiety, depression, and even suicide. With the rise of social media and online communication, cyberbullying has become more prevalent and difficult to control.

Human sex trafficking is a social issue because it involves the exploitation and abuse of vulnerable individuals, mostly women and children, for profit. This illegal industry is a violation of human rights and can lead to physical, emotional, and psychological trauma for the victims.

Malnutrition is a social issue because it is a widespread problem in many parts of the world, particularly in developing countries. Malnutrition can lead to stunted growth, cognitive impairment, and weakened immune systems. It is often linked to poverty and lack of access to adequate food and healthcare. Addressing malnutrition requires social and economic interventions to improve access to food, education, and healthcare.

Social issues refer to problems that affect a significant number of people in society. Cyberbullying, human sex trafficking, and malnutrition are all considered social issues because they negatively impact individuals and communities.

Cyberbullying involves using technology to harass, intimidate, or harm others, often through social media platforms. This can lead to emotional distress, depression, and even suicide among victims.

Human sex trafficking involves the illegal trade and exploitation of individuals for sexual purposes, often through force or coercion. This is a violation of human rights and can lead to physical and psychological harm.

Malnutrition occurs when individuals do not have access to or cannot afford nutritious food, leading to health problems and developmental issues. This is a problem that affects millions of people, particularly in developing countries.

Addressing these social issues requires collective efforts from individuals, organizations, and governments to raise awareness, provide resources, and implement solutions to prevent and combat these problems.
