2. A toddler repeating her parent's sounds is an example of what stage of the development of the self?*1 pointPlayLanguageTutorGame​


A toddler who repeats the voice of his parents is an example of the stage of self-development in the preparatory stage.

There are four main stages of socialization development for the formation of the child's self and personality:

1. Preparatory stage.

This stage begins at birth. At this stage, children will imitate their parents imperfectly. For example, imitating certain words with unclear pronunciation and without understanding the meaning of the word.

2. Play stage.

At this stage, children will begin to imitate behavior and learn to socialize. He will observe the character and habits of those around him and imitate them.

3. Game stages.

At this stage, the child has grown up and has started to play his own role and also understands how to socialize with their peers.

4. Generalized Stage.

At this stage, individuals who are growing up will begin to be able to interact in society and understand well the existing norms and rules.

According to the four stages above, imitating sound should be included in the preparatory stage, but because the options given do not have a preparatory stage option, the answer can be play stage.

DEVELOPMENTAL STAGES

Developmental stages are when changes in the person's life are experienced. Developmental stages are shown through the transformation of the physical, mental, social, sexual, and emotional aspects of an individual. Developmental stages are important to understand people around us in connection with what stage they are in throughout their development.

Here are the development stages and their characteristics.

Early Childhood is the pre-gang age where the child questions a lot and is exploratory. Infancy is the foundation age when the child’s fundamental behavior is organized. Adolescence is the transition age as it is the child’s stage of transforming from a child to an adult. Late Childhood is the gang and creative age wherein the child develops multiple necessary life skills. Pre-natal is the stage where hereditary endowments and sex are developed because of conception. Old Age is the rapid decline of physical and mental capabilities. Middle Age is when adjustments for the decline of physical and mental capabilities occur. Early Adulthood is the adjustment to new life patterns and roles.
