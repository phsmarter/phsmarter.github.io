If you decide you would not have conducted marketing research in your community, explain why. ​


Answer:

If I were managing a business in my community, I would definitely conduct market research to determine the products and services that the business should offer, as well as the appropriate design, features, prices, and promotion strategies for those products and services. Marketing research helps businesses gather important information about their target market, competitors, and industry trends, which can be used to make informed decisions about the direction and positioning of the business.

For example, I might conduct market research to identify customer needs and preferences, assess the competitive landscape, and determine the most effective pricing and promotion strategies for my products or services. This could involve conducting surveys or focus groups with potential customers, analyzing sales data and market trends, and gathering input from industry experts and other stakeholders.

Overall, conducting marketing research is an essential part of managing a successful business, as it helps to ensure that the products and services offered meet the needs and expectations of the target market, and that the business is positioned effectively in the market to attract and retain customers
