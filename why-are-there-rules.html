18. Why not all rules are moral rules; not all standards are moral standards?


Answer:

In my opinion, not all rules are moral rules because some rules have no bearing on morality. Grammar rules are an example. It may be poor grammar, but it is not unethical or immoral. Morality may or may not be a factor in etiquette rules. Religion-based rules are also ambiguous; what is wrong in one religion may be permitted in another—for example, eating pork.

A non-moral standard refers to a standard by which we believe what is good and right, bad or wrong in a non-moral way. A moral standard is a set of guidelines that we use to judge what activities are morally acceptable and what actions are morally wrong. Moral standards, in particular, are concerned with issues that have the potential to gravely damage or benefit people. Not all standards are moral standards for some standards are rules of behaviour that we set only for ourselves.
