4. what are the computers deployed by national aeronautics and space administration (NASA) for high-end computing​


Answer:

High-end computers are being used to accelerate discoveries that will improve life on Earth and answer questions about our solar system and the universe. They are also used to conduct rapid engineering analysis to reduce design cycle times and cost. The 10,240-processor Columbia supercomputer at the NASA Advanced Supercomputing (NAS) facility enables scientists and engineers to model and analyze data up to 10 times faster than before, and to view their results at much higher fidelity. Dramatic improvements in turnaround time and accuracy mean that answers to problems that were until recently considered unsolvable are now within possible reach. Results obtained on Columbia are translating to such practical benefits as: reduced design-cycle time for aircraft and aerospace vehicles, more accurate climate and weather prediction, and rapid diagnosis of space vehicle problems for assessing descent options or making inflight repair decisions.

17. Definition of aeronautical light beacon with picture? Where is the aeronautical light beacon located? Why is it used? How can it be used properly?


Answer:

An aeronautical light beacon is a visual navigational aid that emits a beam of light to guide pilots in the air. It is typically a tall tower, with a high-intensity light that rotates or flashes in a distinctive pattern. Here is a picture of an aeronautical light beacon:

![Aeronautical Light Beacon](https://www.faa.gov/documentLibrary/media/Advisory_Circular/150_5345_43H_Chg1_Figure_2.jpg)

The aeronautical light beacon is usually located at or near airports, helipads, and other aviation facilities. It is used to provide pilots with a visual reference point to help them navigate and locate the airport or other aviation facility. The beacon can be seen from miles away, and pilots use it to orient themselves and align their approach to the runway.

To use the aeronautical light beacon properly, pilots must be familiar with the beacon's distinctive pattern and color. The pattern and color of the beacon can vary depending on the type of facility it is located at, and pilots must be able to recognize these differences to ensure they are heading to the correct airport or facility. Pilots should also use the beacon in conjunction with other navigational aids, such as radio communication and GPS, to ensure they arrive at their destination safely and efficiently
