1. Make an explanation about our polluted riverPlease make a long and short version of the explanation ​


Short version:
Our river is polluted due to the indiscriminate disposal of waste from households and businesses. This has led to the accumulation of trash and pollutants in the water, which has negative impacts on aquatic life and human health. It is important to address this issue by implementing proper waste management practices and educating the public on the importance of taking care of our environment.

Long version:
Our river is currently facing pollution problems brought about by the unregulated disposal of waste from households and businesses. The lack of proper waste management systems has led to the accumulation of trash and pollutants in the river, which has negative impacts on aquatic life and human health.

The accumulation of waste in the river, particularly plastic waste, has been shown to cause harm to aquatic life. Marine animals such as fish and turtles mistake plastic for food, which can lead to ingestion and suffocation. Additionally, the presence of pollutants in the water can cause long-term damage to the aquatic ecosystem, affecting the health of the river and all the species that depend on it.

Moreover, the pollution of the river also poses a threat to human health. The contaminated water can pose a risk of waterborne diseases such as cholera, typhoid, and hepatitis A. Ingesting contaminated water and consuming fish from polluted waters can also lead to serious health problems.

To address this issue, it is important to implement proper waste management practices such as waste segregation, recycling, and proper disposal of hazardous wastes. There is also a need for public education campaigns to raise awareness among the community about the importance of proper waste disposal and responsible environmental practices.

Overall, the pollution of our river is a serious issue that requires immediate attention and action. By taking the necessary steps to address this problem, we can help protect the health of our rivers and the environment, and ensure a better future for generations to come.

The Negro Speaks of River" is a poem written in 1920 by the American poet Langston Hughes. One of the key poems of a literary movement called the "Harlem Renaissance," "The Negro Speaks of River" traces black history from the beginning of human civilization to the present, encompassing both triumphs (like the construction of the Egyptian pyramids) and horrors (like American slavery). The poem argues that the black "soul" has incorporated all of this historical experience, and in the process has become "deep." The poem thus suggests that black cultural identity is continuous, that it stretches across the violence and displacement of slavery to connect with the past—and that black people have made vital, yet often neglected contributions to human civilization.
