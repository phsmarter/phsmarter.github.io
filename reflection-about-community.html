9. Make a reflection about communicative strategy​


Answer:

A communicative strategy is a conscious approach or plan that an individual or group uses to communicate effectively with others. It involves choosing the right words, tone, and body language to convey a message in a way that is clear, concise, and effective.

In my experience, using a communicative strategy can greatly improve the effectiveness of communication. It allows me to consider my audience and tailor my message to them, ensuring that it is relevant and easy for them to understand. It also helps me to be more aware of my own communication style and to make adjustments as needed to be more effective.

One of the key elements of a successful communicative strategy is to listen actively and attentively to the other person. This helps me to better understand their perspective and to respond in a way that is respectful and constructive. It also helps to build trust and rapport, which are essential for successful communication.

Overall, I believe that using a communicative strategy is an important skill that can help to improve the quality of communication in any situation. It requires thoughtfulness and mindfulness, but the benefits are well worth the effort.

A deliberate method or strategy used by an individual or group to interact with others effectively is known as a communication strategy. It entails using the proper words, body language, and tone to effectively and clearly communicate a message.

In general, I think that employing a communicative strategy is a crucial ability that may enhance the effectiveness of communication in every circumstance. Thoughtfulness and mindfulness are necessary, but the rewards are well worth the effort.

Introduction

Intercultural communication competence refers to the ability of an individual to adapt and communicate appropriately and effectively across a wide array of cultural contexts. That is to say, for an individual to be considered an intercultural communicator they must understand other’s cultures as well as they understand their own, and apply this understanding to communication (Chen 1-2). 22. own reflection about vertical communication​


Answer:

Vertical communication refers to the flow of information between different levels of an organization, such as between superiors and subordinates. It is an essential aspect of organizational communication, as it helps to ensure that everyone is on the same page and working towards the same goals.

However, vertical communication can sometimes be hindered by power dynamics and a lack of transparency. Subordinates may feel hesitant to speak up or share their ideas with their superiors, fearing negative consequences or being dismissed. This can lead to a lack of innovation and creativity within the organization.

On the other hand, effective vertical communication can promote collaboration and a sense of unity within the organization. When superiors are open to feedback and suggestions from subordinates, it can lead to a more inclusive and democratic workplace culture. This can ultimately lead to better decision-making and improved organizational performance.

Overall, vertical communication is a crucial aspect of organizational communication that requires open-mindedness, transparency, and a willingness to listen to all voices within the organization.

30. reflection about communicative styles​


Answer:

After reflecting on this question and trying to decide on what my communication style is, I realized that my communication style changes based on the situation that I am in. One of the things that I learned early in my career as a sales representative was that your communication style needs to change to match the style and preferences of the customer you are speaking with. Those lessons from that training have stayed with me throughout my career. As an HRBP your day can vary. I can go from one meeting with our executive team to a training class with a group of engineers. How I speak, my communication style and body language need to adapt to those differences for me to be effective in my job. I also feel that how you act at work is not how you carry yourself in your personal life, and your communication style differs in your personal life as well. Who I am speaking with there will alter my communication style as well. I have to communicate differently with my three children as they are all different ages. At times I need to alter my communication styles with my spouse. Depending on what friends I am with will also alter my communication style

So in short, I think that I do not have one communication style, but a blend of styles that I can utilize depending on the person or people that I am speaking with or the situation (business or professional) that I am in.
