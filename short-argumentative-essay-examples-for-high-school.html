1. Disagreeing the "No homework policy" argumentative essay for debate.Introduction:Argument1:Argument2:Argument3:Conclusion:​


Answer:

Introduction:

As students, we are accustomed to receiving homework as part of our academic life. However, the debate over the effectiveness of homework has been ongoing for years, with some advocating for the abolishment of homework altogether. While the idea of no homework policy may sound attractive to students, it is not a feasible solution to improve the educational system. In fact, homework is an essential component of learning, and it helps students develop crucial skills that they will need in the future.

Argument 1: Homework Helps Students Learn and Retain Information

One of the primary reasons why homework is essential is that it reinforces what students have learned in the classroom. By completing homework assignments, students are more likely to remember the information they have learned and retain it for a more extended period. Moreover, homework provides an opportunity for students to apply what they have learned in the classroom to real-life situations. This helps them develop a better understanding of the subject matter, and it enables them to see the practical applications of the knowledge they have gained.

Argument 2: Homework Develops Key Skills

Homework also helps students develop key skills that are essential for success in both their academic and personal lives. By completing homework assignments, students learn how to manage their time effectively, prioritize tasks, and meet deadlines. These are skills that will serve them well in college and beyond, as well as in their careers. Additionally, homework encourages students to think critically and independently, as they must solve problems on their own without the help of a teacher.

Argument 3: Homework Prepares Students for the Future

Homework is not just about learning subject matter; it is also about preparing students for the future. In college and the workforce, students will be required to complete assignments independently and manage their time effectively. Homework is an excellent way to develop these skills, and it provides students with a taste of what they can expect in the future. Additionally, homework teaches students responsibility and accountability, as they are expected to complete assignments on time and to the best of their abilities.

Conclusion:

While it may be tempting to do away with homework altogether, this is not a practical or effective solution. Homework is a critical component of learning, and it provides numerous benefits to students. It reinforces classroom learning, helps students develop key skills, and prepares them for the future. Rather than abolishing homework, we should focus on improving the quality of homework assignments and finding ways to make them more engaging and relevant to students' lives. Homework should be seen as an opportunity for students to continue their learning outside of the classroom, and as a way to develop essential skills that will serve them well in the future

10. Make an argumentative essay about this argument:"Should students be allowed to enter into a relationship during high school?"AGREE or DISAGREE.​


Answer:

The issue of whether or not students should be allowed to enter into a relationship during high school is a highly debated topic. While some argue that relationships can be a distraction from academic pursuits and can lead to negative consequences, others believe that they can provide valuable learning experiences and contribute to emotional development.

Those who argue against allowing high school students to enter into relationships often cite concerns about the potential negative impact on academic performance. They contend that students who are in relationships may be more focused on their partners than on their studies, leading to lower grades and decreased motivation. Additionally, they argue that relationships can be a source of drama and conflict, which can further detract from a student's ability to focus on their academic pursuits.

On the other hand, those who support allowing high school students to enter into relationships argue that these experiences can provide valuable learning opportunities. Relationships can help students develop important social and emotional skills, such as communication, empathy, and conflict resolution. By learning how to navigate the complexities of relationships, students can develop a better understanding of themselves and others, which can contribute to their personal growth and development.

Furthermore, relationships can provide a sense of stability and support for students during what can be a challenging and stressful time. Students who feel connected to others and who have positive relationships in their lives may be better equipped to handle the challenges and pressures of high school.

In conclusion, while there are valid arguments on both sides of the debate, it is ultimately up to individual schools and communities to decide whether or not to allow high school students to enter into relationships. While it is important to acknowledge the potential risks and distractions associated with relationships, it is equally important to recognize the potential benefits and learning opportunities that they can provide. Ultimately, the decision should be based on what is in the best interests of the students and their overall well-being.

Animal testing has been a controversial issue for many years. Those in favor of animal testing argue that it is necessary for the development of new drugs and medical treatments, and that it has contributed to many important scientific discoveries. However, opponents of animal testing argue that it is cruel and inhumane, and that it is not necessary to test on animals when alternative methods, such as computer modeling, are available.

In addition to the ethical concerns surrounding animal testing, there are also practical considerations. Many animals used in testing are not biologically similar to humans, and the results of these tests may not accurately predict how a drug or treatment will affect humans.

In conclusion, while animal testing has contributed to many important scientific discoveries, it is important to consider the ethical concerns and practical limitations of this practice. Alternative methods, such as computer modeling, should be explored and utilized whenever possible in order to minimize harm to animals while still advancing scientific knowledge.
