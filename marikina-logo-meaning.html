1. WHHAT IS THE DESIGN IN THE LOGO OF MARIKINA


The official seal of the City of Marikina bears the inscriptions Lungsod ng Marikina, Pilipinas, the year 1630 referring to the founding date of the municipality, and 1996 the year of approval of the City Charter.The two mountains represent the majestic twin ranges of the Cordillera and Sierra Madre between which the Marikina Valley nestles, traversed by the Marikina River.

The rising sun points to the eastern location of the city, with each ray representing the city’s barangays.

The machinery gear symbolizes its industries and the shoelast represents the traditional manufacture of the city. The torch focuses on the lofty and noble ideals for human development and a better quality of life, and is symbolic of its Hispanic culture and tradition.

The bamboo underscores a mixture of the people’s natural humility and strength of character and also emphasizes the city’s transition from an agricultural past to the urbanized, industrial present; while the leaves and branches symbolize order and serenity. The bamboo and the wheel represent the Marikina and its people’s respect for, and protection of the environment so as to remain in harmony with progress.


2. marikina logo with explanation of its importance​


Answer:

Lungsod ng Marikina, Pilipinas, 1630, the year the municipality was founded, and 1996, the year the City Charter was approved, are all inscribed on the official seal of the City of Marikina.

The two mountains stand in for the magnificent Cordillera and Sierra Madre twin ranges, between which the Marikina Valley is nestled and the Marikina River flows.

Each ray of the rising sun, which points to the city's eastern location, corresponds to one of the barangays.

The shoelace symbolizes the city's traditional manufacturing while the machinery gear represents its industries. The torch is a representation of the Hispanic culture and tradition and focuses on the lofty and noble ideals of human development and a better quality of life.

The bamboo emphasizes the city's transition from an agricultural past to an urbanized, industrial present, while the leaves and branches stand for order and tranquility. It also highlights the people's inherent humility and strength of character. The wheel and the bamboo stand for the Marikina and its people's commitment to preserving the natural world while advancing.

16. meaning of-trademark-copyright-plagiarism-logo-logo maker​


Answer:

1.Trademark- refers to a recognizable insignia, phrase, word, or symbol that denotes a specific product and legally differentiates it from all other products of its kind. A trademark exclusively identifies a product as belonging to a specific company and recognizes the company's ownership of the brand.

2.Copyright- refers to the legal right of the owner of intellectual property. In simpler terms, copyright is the right to copy.

3.Plagiarism- the practice of taking someone else's work or ideas and passing them off as one's own.

4.Logo- a symbol or other design adopted by an organization to identify its products, uniform, vehicles, etc.

5.Logo maker- A recognizable graphic design element, often including a name, symbol or trademark, representing an organization or product.
