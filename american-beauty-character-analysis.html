9. C Character Analysis Fill out the character analysis sketch to give characterization to the play. "While the Auto Waits 1 Character Traits Evidence Part of the p it was reveal​


Answer:

As I read through the play "While the Auto Waits," I began to notice certain traits that define each character. The first character that stood out to me was the protagonist, John. From his actions and dialogue, it is clear that he is a cautious and logical person, always thinking before he acts. His tendency to overthink and analyze situations can sometimes make him seem hesitant, but it is ultimately what keeps him safe. Additionally, John has a strong sense of responsibility, which is evident when he takes charge of the situation after the accident.

On the other hand, the other driver involved in the accident, Mrs. Judson, comes across as impulsive and careless. She does not take responsibility for her actions and instead tries to shift the blame onto John. Her behavior throughout the play is selfish and unreasonable, which makes her a frustrating character to interact with.

Finally, there is the auto mechanic who appears towards the end of the play. He seems friendly and helpful, but there is also an underlying sense of greed and opportunism in his actions. He wants to take advantage of John's vulnerability and charge him more than necessary for the repairs.

Overall, the character analysis sketch reveals that each character in "While the Auto Waits" has distinct personality traits that shape their actions and interactions with others. These traits create a dynamic and engaging story that keeps the audience invested until the very end.
