The K to 12 Basic Education Curriculum for Senior High School in the Philippines requires a Grade 12 student to undergo Work Immersion in an industry that directly relates to the student's post-secondary goal in order to graduate.

Through Work Immersion, the students are exposed to and become familiar with work-related environment related to their field of specialization to enhance their competence. Specifically, the students are able to: (1) gain relevant and practical industrial skills under the guidance of industry experts and workers; (ii) appreciate the importance and application of the principles and theories taught in school, enhance their technical knowledge and skills; (iv) enrich their skills in communications and human relation; and (v) develop good work habits, attitudes, appreciation, and respect for work. These prepare them to meet the needs and challenges of employment or higher education after employment. Work Immersion provides the students with a venue to test themselves and apply what they have learned in a non-school scenario. The learners are not only able to apply their previous training but are also able to experience the social interactions in a work environment. Their experiences during work immersion will develop many skills and values that would help them as they transition from high school to real life.

To assure the achievement of the objectives of Work Immersion in the Philippines, the Department of Education issued DepEd Order No. 30, s. 2017 which serves as the basis in the implementation of work immersion in all Senior High Schools (SHSS). The policy serves to guide SHSS in: (i) creating flexible work immersion arrangement for their learners; (ii) providing options for work immersion that are relevant to learners' purposes and needs; (iii) organizing work immersion opportunities for learners that are consistent with the diverse human resource requirements for partner institutions for work immersion; and (iv) articulating the scope and limits of work immersion in the context of basic education when building relationships with work immersion partners. To assess the learners' performance during the Work Immersion, the Work Immersion Teacher and the Work Immersion Partner Institution Supervisor will jointly assess the learners' performance following DepEd Order No. 8, 2015.

As a Grade 12 student. I was given the chance to undergo the Work Immersion for a duration of two weeks. In preparation, I accomplished the needed requirements and documents first before I was qualified for the immersion. I was assigned to the Philippine National Police station. In our Work Immersion, I was able to experience the day-to-day activities of the PNP such as road checkpoint, flyer distribution, moral activity, community service, gun assembling, and earthquake drills. During this period, I learned a lot of things that helped me understand and appreciate the work of our police officers. I got first-hand experiences wherein I was able to apply and improve my prior knowledge and skills.

On the positive side, the pandemic provided me with an opportunity to reflect on my priorities and re-evaluate what was truly important to me. I became more appreciative of the small things in life, such as spending time outdoors, cooking meals with my family, and pursuing creative hobbies.

Peer reviews can have their positive and negative effects.  Therefore, these may or may not work for students.  The positive and negative effects are listed below for your reference.

Positive:

Peer review allows the students to discuss among themselves or to evaluate each other’s performances that have contributed in the success of their project, or research for example.  There will be an equal recognition of efforts and contributions such that those who worked so hard in the project will receive their due achievements.  Hence, peer review provides room for growth, understanding and collaboration among teams and members.

Negative:

18. Write an essay of about 200 (TWO HUNDRED) words on: “Which is more beneficial to work: skills or experience? Why?”


Answer:

The debate on whether skills or experience is more beneficial for work has been ongoing for years. Both are vital and complementary aspects of career development, and it can be challenging to determine which is more valuable. However, it is essential to note that the answer to this question may vary depending on the industry, job type, and personal preferences of individuals.

Skills are the abilities and knowledge that individuals possess and are essential for performing a specific task or job. In contrast, experience is the practical knowledge gained by working in a particular field or industry over time. While skills may be more important in certain industries like technology, healthcare, or finance, experience is often valued more in fields like education, politics, or management.

Having the necessary skills can give job seekers a competitive edge in the job market. Employers are constantly seeking individuals with relevant skills for their company, and having the right skills can open doors to new job opportunities. On the other hand, experience allows individuals to understand the intricacies of a particular industry or company and develop a more nuanced perspective of the job they are performing.

In conclusion, both skills and experience are crucial for career development. While skills are essential for individuals to stand out in the job market, experience is valuable in developing a deeper understanding of an industry or company's inner workings. Ultimately, what is more beneficial to work depends on the individual and their career aspirations. It is necessary to strike a balance between the two to achieve success in any profession.
