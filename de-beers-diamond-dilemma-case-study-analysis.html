1. how did the de beers cartel try to maintain control of the price in the diamond market __________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________​


Answer:

The De Beers cartel tried to maintain control of the price in the diamond market by using various tactics such as:

1. Controlling the supply: The De Beers cartel controlled the supply of diamonds in the market by buying diamonds from other mines and stockpiling them. This helped them regulate the quantity of diamonds in the market and maintain prices.

2. Creating an illusion of scarcity: The De Beers cartel also strategically released diamonds into the market to create an illusion of scarcity. This created a perception that diamonds were rare and valuable, and people were willing to pay more for them.

3. Marketing: The De Beers cartel invested heavily in marketing to create a demand for diamonds. They created the famous slogan "A diamond is forever" to convince people that a diamond was a timeless and valuable investment.

4. Controlling distribution: The De Beers cartel also controlled the distribution of diamonds by only allowing authorized dealers to sell diamonds. This helped them control the supply chain and maintain prices.

5. Price fixing: The De Beers cartel also engaged in price fixing by setting a minimum price for diamonds. This prevented competition and ensured that prices remained stable.

23. industry background of De Beers


Answer:

De Beers diamond history.

De Beers is a diamond mining and trading company that was founded in 1888 by Cecil Rhodes. The company has a long and complex history, and has played a major role in shaping the global diamond industry.

In the early years, De Beers controlled a significant portion of the world's diamond production, particularly in South Africa, where many of its mines were located. The company also played a key role in developing the modern diamond market, by promoting the idea of the diamond engagement ring and creating demand for diamonds as a luxury product.

In the mid-20th century, De Beers became embroiled in controversy over allegations of price-fixing and monopolistic practices. The company eventually settled a lawsuit in the United States, which resulted in the dismantling of its monopoly and the opening up of the diamond trade to new players.

Today, De Beers is a subsidiary of Anglo American plc and is one of the world's largest diamond producers. The company operates mines in Canada, Botswana, Namibia, and South Africa, and also has a significant presence in diamond trading and retail through its subsidiary, Forevermark. De Beers is committed to sustainable mining practices and ethical sourcing, and has implemented programs to support the communities in which it operates.

27. SWOT analysis of the film The social dilemma


Strengths:

1. Provides a thought-provoking and eye-opening exploration of the impact of social media on society.

2. Features interviews with experts and insiders from the tech industry, providing valuable insights and perspectives.

3. Raises awareness about issues related to privacy, data collection, and manipulation by social media platforms.

4. Empowers viewers to make informed decisions about their social media usage and take control of their digital lives.

5. Uses engaging and visually compelling storytelling techniques to capture and hold the viewer's attention.

Weaknesses:

1. May oversimplify some of the complex issues related to social media and its impact on society.

2. May focus too much on the negative aspects of social media and not highlight enough of its positive contributions.

3. May not provide enough concrete solutions or actions that viewers can take to address the issues raised in the film.

4. May not appeal to all audiences, particularly those who are not interested in technology or social media.

5. May be criticized for using fear-mongering tactics to generate attention.

Opportunities:

1. Can serve as a catalyst for discussions and debates about the role of technology and social media in our lives.

2. Can inspire individuals and organizations to take action to address the issues raised in the film.

3. Can encourage policymakers to enact regulations that protect users' privacy and prevent the spread of misinformation.

4. Can lead to the development of new technologies that prioritize user well-being and social responsibility.

5. Can contribute to a greater understanding of the social and psychological implications of digital technologies.

Threats:

1. May face backlash or criticism from the tech industry or social media companies who feel targeted by the film's message.

2. May be seen as overly negative or alarmist by some viewers, who may dismiss the film's message or choose to ignore it.

3. May not reach a wide enough audience or may be overshadowed by other media content.

4. May be vulnerable to censorship or suppression by social media platforms or other entities that do not want the issues raised in the film to gain attention.

5. May be seen as just one of many documentaries or media content focused on similar issues, leading to audience fatigue or saturation.
