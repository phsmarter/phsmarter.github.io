23. how does the concept of freedom affect the life and values of a person​


Answer:

The concept of freedom affects the life and values of a person by giving them the ability to make their own choices and decisions without external constraints or limitations. It allows them to pursue their goals and desires according to their own will and to express their individuality and uniqueness.

Freedom also fosters a sense of responsibility and accountability, as it requires individuals to take ownership of their choices and their consequences. This can shape their values by encouraging them to prioritize personal growth, empathy, and respect for others.

Overall, the concept of freedom plays a significant role in shaping the life and values of a person by providing them with the autonomy to make their own decisions and to define their own path in life.


24. How I value and and Practice Freedom​


Answer:

You are free to choose YOUR WORDS – you decide what you say to yourself and others. Are your words a reflection of who you are and the impact you intend to create on yourself and those around you?

You are free to choose YOUR ATTITUDE – is your glass half full or half empty? You decide your perspective. If the one you are using isn’t working, try a new one!

You are free to choose YOUR ACTIONS – seemingly insignificant choices turn into small actions. The small actions you do regularly become your habits. Your habits shape your character and create your life. You make choices every day about what you will and will not do. Here are some matched pairs for contemplation: let go or control others; walk or drive; cash or credit; prepare or procrastinate; be fit or be fat; progress or perfection; talk or listen…what actions are you choosing?

You are free to choose YOUR BELIEFS – do you realize that what you believe drives how you behave? In Atomic Habits, James Clear does an excellent job explaining how your identity (the sum of your beliefs) is essential to lasting change. You get to decide what you believe. If you are behaving in ways that aren’t working for you, examine what belief is causing that behavior. Then decide whether you want to keep the belief or change it. This is a powerful exercise that many of our coaching clients do to radically improve their leadership, performance and productivity!
