14. what are the degrees of the holy orders?


There are three degrees of the Holy Orders:

Deacons (Diaconate)
Priests (Presbyterate)
Bishops (Episcopate)

Each degrees have different roles and power toward the church.

Deacons: 

Roles:
He is called to serve to the Church as Jesus served.

Involvement to the church:
Helping the priest/bishop in celebrating the Eucharist.
Care for the sick 
Helping to the poor 

Priests:

Roles:
After being a deacon and finishing studies in Theology and Philosophy, he can be promoted into a priest. A priest can celebrate the holy Eucharist, unlike the deacon who needs the supervision of a priest.

Involvement to the church:
Can baptize children and adults
Can celebrate the Eucharist 
Catechize children
Can do exorcisms 
Hear confessions

Bishops:

Roles:
He is the chief of priests within the diocese. 

Involvement to the church:
Can ordain priests and deacons
Can celebrate all the sacraments
Confirms people as part of the Church of Christ

26. what do you think will happen if there will be no man who will undergo the holy sacrament of holy order?


Answer:

The Holy Sacrament of Holy Orders is a sacrament of the Catholic Church that ordains men to the priesthood. If there were no men who underwent this sacrament, it would mean that there would be no male Catholic priests.

This would have significant implications for the Catholic Church, as the priesthood is a crucial part of the Church's structure and mission. Without priests, there would be no one to celebrate the sacraments, offer spiritual guidance and counsel, or lead the faithful in worship. This would mean that the Church would have to find alternative ways to meet the spiritual needs of its members.

However, it's important to note that the Catholic Church is not the only Christian denomination, and there are other churches and religious organizations that do not have a male-only priesthood. Therefore, the absence of male Catholic priests would not necessarily mean the end of Christianity or religion altogether.
