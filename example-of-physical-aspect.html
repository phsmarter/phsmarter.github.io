18. Based on your experience, describe some examples of the close, interactive relationship between the physical dimensions of yourself and the psychological aspects of your mind and experience. ​


Examples of interactive relationships between psychological aspects and physical aspects.

1. Physical health affects mental health and vice versa, mental health affects physical health.

This is the most basic interactive relationship between the physical and psychological aspects of human beings. When we keep our bodies healthy, we also take care of the health of our minds. For example, such as eating healthy foods, getting enough rest and sleep, and exercising regularly. Because our bodies are fit, our minds will also be fresh. But if we don't take care of our bodies, our minds will not be fresh because of unfit body conditions.

And vice versa. By keeping our minds healthy, we also take care of our bodies. Because if our minds experience mental problems, then it can have an impact on the physical. We can feel tired easily and not have the energy to do activities. On a more severe level, this can also cause physical pain.

2. Loving yourself = accepting your own physic

We may often compare ourselves with others or feel less beautiful and perfect. This may make us want to change our physique to become more beautiful. But one of the ways we can do that without changing our physique is to accept and love ourselves. Realize that everyone has their own strengths and weaknesses, and beauty is not always about physical appearance.

When we practice self-love, we will also feel more accepting of our physique and feel beautiful just the way we are. We will also feel more confident and comfortable with our own bodies.

3. Our mindset determines our physical health

The relationship between mindset and physical health is when we have the mindset that exercising, eating healthy foods, and adopting a healthy lifestyle are investments in body health. Then we will start living with a healthier and fitter body.

24. How physical education develop the 4 aspects of the individual?A. Physical-B. Emotional-C. Social-D. Mentally-Explain and cite example for each one.​


Answers and Explanations:

A.) Physical

Physical education improves children's physical development such as muscular strength, flexibility, muscular endurance, body composition, and cardiovascular endurance. It also improves skill development such as motor skills, which allow for safe, successful, and satisfying participation in physical activities.

B.) Emotional

Physical education also improves emotional development which involves learning what feelings and emotions are, understanding how and why they occur, recognizing your own feelings and those of others, and developing effective ways of managing those feelings.

C.) Social

Exercise helps increase social skills and the prevention of social conflicts. In sports and group activities, people learn to help others, have consistency with the group, cooperation, forgiveness, dedication, independence, self-confidence, respect for the law, and friendship with others. The social development objective is concerned with helping an individual in making personal adjustments, group adjustments, and adjustments as a member of society.

D.) Mental

Exercise also improves mood and mental health. Exercise promotes chemicals in the brain that improve your mood and make you more relaxed. Specifically, the brain releases feel-good chemicals throughout the body. Physical activity reduces anxiety and depressed mood and enhances self-esteem.

28. As a student can you give some example about the traits or physical aspect that you can visualize in your mind in winning candidates.​


ANSWER

-Confidence: Successful candidates are often seen as self-assured and confident in their abilities and their message. They may hold themselves in a commanding or assertive manner and speak with authority.

-Charisma: Successful candidates are often viewed as having a certain charm or magnetism that draws people to them. They may have the ability to connect with people and inspire them to support their cause.

-Intelligence: Successful candidates are often seen as intelligent, well-informed, and capable of making complex arguments and decisions. They may possess a wide range of knowledge and be able to think critically about important issues.

-Physical fitness: Successful candidates are often seen as healthy and physically fit. They may be physically active and maintain a healthy lifestyle.

-Presentation: Successful candidates are often seen as polished, well-groomed, and dressed professionally. They may have good posture and hold themselves in a manner that commands attention.

-Communication skills: Successful candidates are often good at communicating effectively, be it speaking, listening, writing and having the ability to connect with different audiences.

It is important to note that the traits mentioned above are general stereotypes, not necessarily reflect the qualities that make someone a good candidate, as many other factors like policies, ideologies, and past performance may come into play. People's opinions might also vary.
