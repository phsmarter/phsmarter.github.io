1. The typical university business school is organized on a functional basis, with departments heads in accounting, finance, information systems, management, and marketing all reporting to a dean. You are on your alma mater’s advisory board and the dean is asking for advice. What suggestions might you give for redesigning this structure to increase communication and collaboration across departments, as well as improve curriculum integration for students in all areas of study?


Answer:

As a member of the advisory board, I would suggest the following redesigns to increase communication and collaboration across departments, and improve curriculum integration for students in all areas of study:

1. Cross-functional teams: Encourage the creation of cross-functional teams that bring together faculty members from different departments to work on common projects or initiatives. This will help break down silos between departments and encourage collaboration.

2. Interdisciplinary courses: Develop interdisciplinary courses that require students from different departments to work together on projects or case studies. This will help students see the connections between different business functions and develop a more holistic view of the business world.

3. Shared teaching and research spaces: Encourage the creation of shared teaching and research spaces that can be used by faculty members from different departments. This will facilitate informal communication and collaboration between faculty members.

4. Joint faculty appointments: Consider joint faculty appointments between departments. This will help break down departmental barriers and encourage collaboration between faculty members.

5. Curriculum integration: Work to integrate the curriculum across different departments. For example, accounting courses could include more information on marketing or management, and marketing courses could include more information on finance or accounting. This will help students develop a more well-rounded understanding of business.

By implementing these redesigns, the business school can encourage greater collaboration between departments, and provide students with a more integrated and well-rounded business education.
