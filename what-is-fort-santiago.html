1. A. What is the historical significance of Fort Santiago in relation to Intramuros? B. Why is Fort Santiago considered to be one of the dark tourism in Intramuros? C. Why is Fort Santiago considered historical and famous? D. When was Fort Santiago declared as the "Shrine of Freedom", what does this mean in relation to Philippine History?


Answer:

A. Fort Santiago is historically significant in relation to Intramuros because it served as a military fortress during the Spanish colonial period in the Philippines. It was built in 1571 as a defense against foreign invaders and was later used as a prison for political prisoners during the Spanish and American colonial periods. The fort also played a major role in the Philippine Revolution and the Philippine-American War.

B. Fort Santiago is considered one of the dark tourism sites in Intramuros because of its dark history as a prison and torture chamber during the Spanish and American colonial periods. Many political prisoners were subjected to inhumane treatment and were executed within the walls of Fort Santiago. The fort has also been associated with the brutal Japanese occupation of the Philippines during World War II.

C. Fort Santiago is considered historical and famous because of its significance in Philippine history. It has been witness to various important events such as the arrival of Spanish conquistador Miguel López de Legazpi, the imprisonment of Philippine national hero Jose Rizal before his execution, and the torture and execution of Filipino revolutionaries during the Philippine Revolution and Philippine-American War.

D. Fort Santiago was declared as the "Shrine of Freedom" on April 9, 1951, by President Elpidio Quirino. This declaration recognizes the fort's historical significance as a symbol of the struggle for Philippine independence and as a site where many Filipino heroes fought and died for freedom. The declaration also signifies the importance of preserving and protecting the fort as a national heritage site and a testament to the resilience and bravery of the Filipino people throughout history.

7. Give a Fort Santiago Historical


Answer:

Fort Santiago (Spanish: Fuerte de Santiago; Filipino: Moóg ng Santiago), built in 1593, is a citadel built by Spanish navigator and governor Miguel López de Legazpi for the new established city of Manila in the Philippines. The defense fortress is located in Intramuros, the walled city of Manila.

Fort Santiago Historical

On September 24, 1762, British forces led by Brigadier-General William Draper and Rear-Admiral Samuel Cornish invaded and captured Manila, and along with it Fort Santiago. It was during this time that the fort served as a base of operations for the Royal Navy until April 1764 when they agreed to a ceasefire with the Spanish.

American occupied Fort Santiago in 1940

The raising of the American Flag at Fort Santiago.

On August 13, 1898, the American flag was raised in Fort Santiago signifying the start of the American rule in the Philippines. The fort served as the headquarters for the U.S. Army and several changes were made to the fort by the Americans. One of these changes included the draining of the moats surrounding the fort. The grounds were then transformed into a golf course.

World War II

For the Japanese Invasion of the Philippines, see Philippines Campaign (1941–42).

During World War II, Fort Santiago was captured by the Japanese Imperial Army, and used its prisons and dungeons including the storage cells and gunpowder magazines for hundreds of prisoners who were killed near the end of the war (see Manila massacre). The fort sustained heavy damage from American and Filipino military mortar shells during the Battle of Manila in February 1945. Also, approximately 600 American prisoners of war died of suffocation or hunger after being held in extremely tight quarters in the dungeons at Fort Santiago.

22. Describe Rizal's life at Fort Santiago


Answer:

The fort is one of the most important historical sites in Manila. Several lives were lost in its prisons during the Spanish Empire and World War II. José Rizal, one of the Philippine national heroes, was imprisoned here before his execution in 1896. The Rizal Shrine museum displays memorabilia of the hero in their collection and the fort features, embedded onto the ground in bronze, his footsteps representing his final walk from his cell to the location of the actual execution.

It is only a few meters away from the Manila Cathedral and the Palacio del Gobernador (lit. Governor's Palace, currently the office of the Commission on Elections).
