12. List down at least three (3) potential hazards induced by extreme atmospheric and hydrologic conditions that is applicable to your community. ​


Answer:

Atmostpheric:

1. Tropical Cyclones

2. Tornadoes

3. Severe thunderstorms and lightning

Hydrologic:

1. Flood

2. Tsunami

3. Drought (shortage of water)

Explanation:

Atmospheric:

(Before)

1. Listen to a radio or TV for information. Secure your home and move to the evacuation center while bringing evacuation and emergency kits with you.

2. Tornadoes cannot be predicted as of now.

3. Listen to a radio or TV for the latest weather forecast.

(During)

1. You must stay indoors and keep away from windows and glass doors. Even if you could see that the skies have cleared up, you must not go outside, unless the authorities have told you do so and it's safe.

2. Remain calm, and avoid panic. Seek shelter in a sturdy basement. If there is no basement, go to an interior room such as the bathroom, and closets. If possible, avoid sheltering in the room with windows.

3. When the thunder roars, go indoors ASAP. Avoid using electronic devices that is connected to an electrical outlet. Also, avoid using the running water because lightning could pass through the plumbing pipes.

(After)

1. Stay out of floodwater. Stay away from power lines; there is a possibility you might get electrocuted. Also, be careful when you are near of a damage building.

2.  Give first aid when appropriate. Stay away from damaged buildings, and turn on the radio to get more emergency information.

3. Listen to the authorities and latest weather forecast for more information on wether it is safe to go outside.

Hydrologic:

(Before)

1. Be alert, prepare your disaster supplies, protect your home and your family, and be prepared to evacuate.

2. Protect yourself from the earthquake. Get to the higher ground as possible. Be alert for a sign of tsunami, such as a sudden rise or draining of the ocean waters. Do not wait for the tsunami to arrive, evacuate ASAP. Also, listen for emergency information and alerts

3. Repair any leaks from your pipes and faucets. Start learning to conserve water.

(During)

1. Move to a higher ground or higher floor. Do not swim, or dive through flood waters, especially during flash floods. Drowning is mostly occured during this scenario.

2. Do not go near a shore to watch a tsunami hit.

3. Avoid letting the tap water turned on during brushing your teeth, or washing your face and hands. Take short showers, and collect the water that you have used to water your plants and crops.

(After)

1. Avoid floodwaters. Standing water might be electrically charged from a downed power lines. Listen for local warnings and information.

2. Avoid disaster areas. Check yourself for injuries and get first aid when needed. Help others who requires special assistance - like helping the infants, or the elderly.

3.Understand how the drought has affected you and create a management plan for a next drought to occur.
