6. Which barrier is the number one enemy of communication?Select one:a. USE OF JARGONb. LACK OF CONFIDENCEc. EMOTIONAL BARRIERSd. NOISY ENVIRONMENT


Barriers in communication are all things that become barriers to effective communication.

Let's look at the options given one by one to get the answer.

1. Use of jargon.

Jargon can be interpreted as a special term for an activity or group. If jargon is used in communication without a prior explanation of its meaning, then this can lead to confusion.

2. Lack of confidence

A lack of confidence in communication can cause a person to find it difficult to express his message or desire properly.

3. Emotional barriers

This can be interpreted as a condition in which one or all of the communicating parties are not in a good emotional situation so it can cause communication to not run effectively, even to the point that it cannot be continued.

4. Noisy environment

Noisy environments can cause barriers to verbal communication because the voices used to convey messages can become inaudible.

Of the four options above, the number one enemy of communication is the emotional barrier because the emotional barrier can make the conversation completely unsustainable. Meanwhile, other barriers such as the use of jargon, lack of confidence, and a noisy environment can still be overcome if the people communicating are in a good emotional situation.
