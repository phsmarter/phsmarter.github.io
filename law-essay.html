Newton is famous for many things, such as being the first to theorize about gravity after an apple fell on his head. Another one of his famous works includes theorizing and publishing his 3 laws of motion. These laws were first theorized by Newton in 1666, then formally published in his book Principia Mathematica Philosophiae Naturalis. The three laws are: objects will stay at rest or be moving unless acted upon by an unbalanced force, acceleration depends on an object’s mass and the force acted upon the object, and if one object exerts a force on another object then the second object exerts a force of equal strength in the opposite direction on the first object.

   The first law, which states that objects will stay in its course of action, whether it is still or moving, unless acted upon by an unbalanced force. An unbalanced force can make an object speed up, if still, slow down, if in motion, or stop the object altogether. 15. rice teriffication law essay


Answer:  The main economic issues that arise with tariffication stem from the nonequivalence of tariffs in NTBs in a number of scenarios. The issue analyzes nonequivalence arising from the existence of imperfect competition in importing countries, price instability in importing and exporting countries, and inefficient allocation of quantitative restrictions. It is shown that in all these cases the definition of an appropriate "equivalent tariff" to be used in tariffication is not straightforward, and that in general this equivalent tariff cannot be computed on the basis of only observed price differences between countries. Tariff-rate quotas, which are meant to be the main tool in implement tariffication according to the existing proposal, are analyzed in some detail. Concerning the relationship between tariffication and the other elements of the trade liberalization package, it is shown that tariffication would limit the scope of export subsidy policies, and that the existence of production and export subsidies makes observed price gaps between countries of questionable value in setting equivalent tariff levels. Finally, it is argued that the main focus on tariffication should be the conversion of NTBs to acceptable long-run (bound) tariffs rates, and considerable flexibility in the conversion process could be exercised during the transition period.

25. persuasive essay about law​


Answer:

Law is an essential part of any society. It is the foundation of a civilized society and provides order and stability. Without law, chaos would ensue and the world would be a much more dangerous place.

Law serves many purposes in our society. It protects citizens from harm, ensures justice is served, and helps to maintain order. Laws are also important for protecting our rights as citizens. They provide us with protection from discrimination, exploitation, and abuse of power. Laws also help to ensure that everyone is treated fairly and equally under the law.

In addition to providing protection for citizens, laws also help to promote economic growth and development. By providing a stable legal framework for businesses to operate within, laws help to create an environment where businesses can thrive and grow. This in turn leads to increased economic activity which benefits everyone in society.

Finally, laws are important for promoting social cohesion and harmony within a society. By establishing clear rules of conduct that everyone must follow, laws help to ensure that people respect each other’s rights and live peacefully together in harmony. This helps to create a strong sense of community which is essential for any successful society.

In conclusion, law is an essential part of any society as it provides protection for citizens, promotes economic growth, and helps to promote social cohesion and harmony within a society. Without law there would be chaos and disorder which would lead to a much less safe world for all of us to live in.

Laws are the backbone of any society. They are put in place to maintain order, protect the rights of citizens and prevent chaos. In this essay, I will discuss why laws are important and the benefits that they bring to society.

Body:

Firstly, laws provide a framework for a society to function smoothly. Without laws, there would be no order, and society would descend into chaos. Laws set the standards for behavior, outline what is acceptable, and what is not, and provide penalties for those who break them. For example, traffic laws are put in place to prevent accidents and ensure that everyone follows the same rules of the road. Without these laws, there would be no structure, and people would drive recklessly, putting themselves and others in danger.

Secondly, laws protect the rights of citizens. They ensure that everyone is treated fairly, regardless of their race, gender, religion or social status. Laws prevent discrimination and provide a means for individuals to seek justice if their rights have been violated. For example, the civil rights laws of the 1960s gave African Americans equal protection under the law and ended segregation in schools and public places.

Thirdly, laws create a sense of accountability. They hold individuals and organizations responsible for their actions and provide consequences for their wrongdoings. This helps to deter people from breaking the law and ensures that justice is served when laws are broken. Without accountability, people would feel free to do as they please, leading to chaos and disorder.

Lastly, laws help to regulate and control the actions of those in power. They provide a means for citizens to check the actions of their government and prevent abuse of power. For example, the Constitution of the United States limits the powers of the government and provides a system of checks and balances to ensure that no one branch becomes too powerful.

Conclusion:

In conclusion, laws are essential for a functional and peaceful society. They provide structure, protect the rights of citizens, create accountability and help to regulate those in power. Without laws, there would be chaos, and the world would be a much more dangerous and unpredictable place.
