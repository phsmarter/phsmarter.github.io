2. money want give happiness argumentative essay ​


Answer:

Money is often seen as a path to happiness, but is this really the case? While it is true that money can provide individuals with access to certain luxuries and opportunities, it is not a guarantee for happiness. In fact, many individuals who possess great wealth still struggle with mental health issues and feelings of unhappiness. Therefore, the idea that money can buy happiness is a flawed one.

Firstly, money can provide individuals with access to material possessions, which can provide temporary satisfaction. However, studies have shown that material possessions do not lead to long-term happiness. The initial excitement and pleasure of purchasing something new wears off quickly, leaving individuals feeling unfulfilled and searching for the next thing to buy. Additionally, the constant pursuit of material possessions can lead to a never-ending cycle of wanting more, which can actually lead to feelings of stress and unhappiness.

Secondly, money can provide individuals with experiences that can bring happiness, such as traveling or attending events. However, it is important to note that experiences are only enjoyable if they are shared with loved ones. No matter how much money one has, if they are unable to share experiences with those they care about, they may still feel lonely and unfulfilled.

Furthermore, studies have shown that individuals cwho prioritize money over relationships and experiences are less happy than those who prioritize social connections and personal growth. This is because social connections and personal growth provide individuals with a sense of purpose and fulfillment that cannot be achieved through material possessions or money.

In conclusion, the idea that money can buy happiness is a flawed one. While money can provide individuals with temporary satisfaction and access to certain experiences, it is not a guarantee for long-term happiness. Instead, individuals should prioritize social connections and personal growth, which can provide them with a sense of purpose and fulfillment that cannot be achieved through money alone.

Answer:

What does that mean? The longer version of the quote (included at the bottom of the post) goes on to state that once one can no longer be happy, in concrete form, the person will focus solely on money. Happiness comes to humans in many forms. A pleasant day, a warm smile from a friend, or the scent of your favorite food hanging in the air. Money can buy one of those three examples. But when you no longer care about the weather, have alienated all your friends, and can no longer truly enjoy food, art or music, to what do you turn? Money is the abstract form of happiness, in that when you have enough money, you can buy many things to make you happy. But focusing on the money, and not the happiness all around us, can be problematic.

Why is concrete happiness important? While it is true that money cannot buy true happiness, it can be useful for getting a moment of it here and there, from time to time. In this manner, money is actually an abstract of it, something which can be exchanged for a form of it on a short-term basis. But that form is only temporary.

The more concrete types of happiness are much longer lasting and more satisfying. Relationships with other people is one of the deeper and longest lasting of the ways we can find it in our lives. Others include an appreciation of various art forms, from sculpture and painting to music and singing.
