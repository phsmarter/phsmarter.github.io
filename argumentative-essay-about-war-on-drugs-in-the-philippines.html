2. Disagreeing the "No homework policy" argumentative essay for debate.Introduction:Argument1:Argument2:Argument3:Conclusion:​


Answer:

Introduction:

As students, we are accustomed to receiving homework as part of our academic life. However, the debate over the effectiveness of homework has been ongoing for years, with some advocating for the abolishment of homework altogether. While the idea of no homework policy may sound attractive to students, it is not a feasible solution to improve the educational system. In fact, homework is an essential component of learning, and it helps students develop crucial skills that they will need in the future.

Argument 1: Homework Helps Students Learn and Retain Information

One of the primary reasons why homework is essential is that it reinforces what students have learned in the classroom. By completing homework assignments, students are more likely to remember the information they have learned and retain it for a more extended period. Moreover, homework provides an opportunity for students to apply what they have learned in the classroom to real-life situations. This helps them develop a better understanding of the subject matter, and it enables them to see the practical applications of the knowledge they have gained.

Argument 2: Homework Develops Key Skills

Homework also helps students develop key skills that are essential for success in both their academic and personal lives. By completing homework assignments, students learn how to manage their time effectively, prioritize tasks, and meet deadlines. These are skills that will serve them well in college and beyond, as well as in their careers. Additionally, homework encourages students to think critically and independently, as they must solve problems on their own without the help of a teacher.

Argument 3: Homework Prepares Students for the Future

Homework is not just about learning subject matter; it is also about preparing students for the future. In college and the workforce, students will be required to complete assignments independently and manage their time effectively. Homework is an excellent way to develop these skills, and it provides students with a taste of what they can expect in the future. Additionally, homework teaches students responsibility and accountability, as they are expected to complete assignments on time and to the best of their abilities.

Conclusion:

While it may be tempting to do away with homework altogether, this is not a practical or effective solution. Homework is a critical component of learning, and it provides numerous benefits to students. It reinforces classroom learning, helps students develop key skills, and prepares them for the future. Rather than abolishing homework, we should focus on improving the quality of homework assignments and finding ways to make them more engaging and relevant to students' lives. Homework should be seen as an opportunity for students to continue their learning outside of the classroom, and as a way to develop essential skills that will serve them well in the future

14. Critical Essay about Drug Addiction. • Argumentative• Informative • Persuasive ​


Drug addiction is a serious problem that affects millions of people around the world. It is a complex condition that can have devastating physical, psychological, and social consequences. Drug addiction can lead to a range of health problems, including heart disease, stroke, liver failure, and even death. It can also lead to financial ruin and broken relationships. Despite the dangers associated with drug addiction, many people continue to use drugs in an attempt to cope with life’s challenges or escape from reality.

The causes of drug addiction are varied and complex. Genetics may play a role in some cases, as certain individuals may be more prone to developing an addiction than others. Environmental factors such as poverty or living in an area where drugs are readily available can also increase the risk of developing an addiction. Additionally, mental health issues such as depression or anxiety can contribute to drug abuse and addiction.

In order to effectively address the issue of drug addiction, it is important to understand its causes and effects. Education about the dangers of drug use is essential for preventing future generations from becoming addicted. Treatment programs should be available for those who are already addicted so they can learn how to manage their cravings and live a healthier lifestyle without drugs. Finally, society must work together to reduce the stigma associated with drug addiction so that those struggling with this condition feel comfortable seeking help without fear of judgment or discrimination.

It is clear that drug addiction is a serious problem that requires immediate attention from both individuals and society as a whole. By understanding its causes and effects, we can work together to reduce its prevalence and help those who are already struggling with this condition find hope for recovery.

Summary

Thousands of people in the Philippines have been killed since President Rodrigo Duterte launched his “war on drugs” on June 30, 2016, the day he took office. Among those who died have been dozens of children under age 18 who were either specifically targeted or were inadvertently shot during anti-drug raids, what authorities have called “collateral damage.” Philippine children’s rights nongovernmental organizations (NGOs) put the total number of child fatalities at 101 from July 2016 through December 2018, both targeted and killed as bystanders. More deaths of children have been reported in the media in 2019 and 2020.

More broadly, official figures from the Philippine National Police and the Philippine Drug Enforcement Agency put the number of “drug war” casualties at 5,601 deaths as of January 31, 2020. In virtually every case, police claimed they killed a drug seller or user during a raid after the suspect resisted arrest and fought back. The national Commission on Human Rights and domestic human rights groups believe many thousands more – estimated at more than 27,000 – have been killed by the police, agents of the police, or unidentified assailants.

The overwhelming majority of these killings have not been properly investigated. According to the Philippine Department of Justice in January 2019, just 76 deaths have led to investigations. Even then, only 33 resulted in court cases and 5 were pending before the Office of the Prosecutor, while the prosecutor dismissed half – 38 cases. At time of writing, only one case – the killing of 17-year-old Kian delos Santos by three police officers in August 2017, which happened to be captured on video – has resulted in a trial and conviction.

Title: The Legalization of Drugs: A Dangerous Path to Follow

Introduction:

The debate over drug legalization has been ongoing for years, with supporters arguing that it would reduce crime and drug-related deaths, while opponents argue that it would lead to increased drug use and addiction. Despite these arguments, I firmly believe that legalizing drugs would be a dangerous path to follow.

Body Paragraph 1:

One of the main reasons why drugs should not be legalized is that it would lead to increased drug use and addiction. Studies have shown that when drugs are legalized, their use increases significantly, and this can have serious consequences for individuals and society as a whole. For example, drug use can lead to impaired judgement, poor decision making, and a host of other negative outcomes.

Body Paragraph 2:

Another reason why drugs should not be legalized is that it would lead to increased crime. While supporters of drug legalization argue that it would reduce crime rates, the reality is that legalizing drugs would only lead to more drug-related crimes, as addicts would still need to fund their addiction, and drug gangs would still exist to profit from the sale of drugs.

Body Paragraph 3:

Finally, the legalization of drugs would have a negative impact on public health. Legalizing drugs would send a message to society that drug use is acceptable, and this could lead to more people using drugs and suffering the negative health consequences that come with drug use. Moreover, the healthcare system would be burdened with the costs of treating drug-related illnesses and injuries.

Conclusion:

In conclusion, while the debate over drug legalization is complex, I believe that legalizing drugs would be a dangerous path to follow. The increased drug use, crime rates, and negative public health outcomes that would result from drug legalization far outweigh any potential benefits. Instead, we should focus on prevention, treatment, and education to reduce drug use and addiction.
