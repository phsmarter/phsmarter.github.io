2. explains how the research was conducted. These include the research design, population and sampling procedure, research instrument, data gathering procedure, and data analysis procedure


Answer:

Research Design:

The research followed a mixed-methods approach, incorporating both qualitative and quantitative methods to gather comprehensive data. The study employed a descriptive and exploratory research design, aiming to understand the contributions of households, schools, and communities to individual well-being and development.

Population and Sampling Procedure:

The target population for this research comprised individuals across various age groups residing in households, attending schools, and living within communities. A purposive sampling technique was used to select diverse households, schools, and communities to ensure representation of different demographics and socio-economic backgrounds. The sample included households from different regions, schools with varying educational systems, and communities with distinct characteristics.

Research Instrument:

Multiple research instruments were utilized to collect data. A questionnaire was designed to gather quantitative data, including demographic information, household dynamics, school experiences, and community perceptions. The questionnaire consisted of structured and close-ended questions, allowing for statistical analysis. Additionally, semi-structured interviews were conducted to obtain qualitative data, enabling a deeper understanding of participants' experiences, perspectives, and narratives. The interviews provided rich, contextual information that complemented the quantitative data.

Data Gathering Procedure:

The data gathering process involved several steps. Firstly, the research team identified and selected households, schools, and communities that represented diverse populations. Informed consent was obtained from participants, ensuring their voluntary participation and confidentiality. Questionnaires were distributed to the selected individuals, either in-person or through online platforms, and participants were given an appropriate timeframe to complete the questionnaires. Concurrently, semi-structured interviews were conducted with a subset of participants, chosen through purposive sampling. The interviews were audio-recorded and transcribed for later analysis.

Data Analysis Procedure:

The collected data underwent a systematic analysis process. For the quantitative data, statistical analysis techniques such as descriptive statistics, correlation analysis, and regression analysis were employed to identify patterns, relationships, and trends within the data. The qualitative data from the interviews were subjected to thematic analysis, involving coding and categorization of the responses to identify common themes, emerging patterns, and significant insights. The qualitative and quantitative findings were then integrated to provide a comprehensive understanding of the research question and objectives. The research conclusions were drawn based on the analysis of the combined data, supplemented with relevant literature and theoretical frameworks.

6. Be able to define the following: 1. Research design 2. Research approach 3. Population and Sample 4. Sampling techniques/ strategies 5. Data gathering methods 6. Instruments 7. Data gathering procedure 8. Data analysis (statistical and thematic) 9. Ethical consideration​


Answer:

1. Research design - refers to the overall plan or blueprint for conducting a research study, including the methods and procedures to be used for data collection and analysis.

2. Research approach - refers to the general framework or perspective from which a research study is conducted. Common research approaches include qualitative, quantitative, and mixed methods.

3. Population and Sample - population refers to the entire group of individuals or objects that a researcher is interested in studying, while a sample is a subset of the population that is selected to participate in the research study.

4. Sampling techniques/strategies - refer to the methods used to select participants for a research study. Common sampling techniques include random sampling, stratified sampling, and convenience sampling.

5. Data gathering methods - refer to the techniques used to collect data for a research study. Examples include surveys, interviews, observations, and experiments.

6. Instruments - refer to the tools or devices used to collect data for a research study, such as questionnaires, interview guides, and tests.

7. Data gathering procedure - refers to the specific steps taken to collect data for a research study, including the recruitment of participants, data collection methods, and data recording procedures.

8. Data analysis (statistical and thematic) - refers to the methods used to analyze the data collected in a research study. Statistical analysis involves using mathematical and statistical tools to analyze numerical data, while thematic analysis involves identifying patterns and themes in qualitative data.

9. Ethical consideration - refers to the principles and guidelines that researchers must follow to ensure that their research is conducted in an ethical and responsible manner. This includes obtaining informed consent from participants, protecting their privacy and confidentiality, and minimizing any potential risks or harm.
