5. theoretical framework about vaping​


Answer:

There are several theoretical frameworks that can be used to understand the behavior and attitudes related to vaping.

One commonly used framework is the theory of planned behavior (TPB), which suggests that behavior is driven by intention, which in turn is influenced by attitudes, subjective norms, and perceived behavioral control. Another framework is the protection motivation theory (PMT), which suggests that behavior is influenced by perceived vulnerability to a health threat, the perceived severity of the threat, the perceived efficacy of the behavior in preventing the threat, and the perceived self-efficacy to perform the behavior. Other frameworks that have been used to study vaping include the social cognitive theory, the social ecological model, and the health belief model. These frameworks can provide valuable insights into the factors that drive vaping behavior and can inform interventions aimed at reducing vaping and improving public health.

Answer:

The conceptual framework for online games involves the process of creating, developing, and distributing games over the internet. This process includes several key components, such as game design, programming, testing, marketing, and distribution.

The game design phase involves conceptualizing the game, defining its mechanics and aesthetics, and creating a storyboard or prototype. Once the design is finalized, the game development team can begin programming the game using appropriate coding languages and platforms.

Self-directed learning theory posits that learners should be given the freedom and autonomy to take control of their own learning process. This theory emphasizes the importance of learners' motivation, self-regulation, and metacognition in the learning process. As such, the study can examine the impact of self-directed learning modules on students' motivation, self-regulation, and metacognition.

Constructivism, on the other hand, asserts that learners construct their own knowledge through active engagement with the learning materials. This theory emphasizes the importance of learners' prior knowledge, beliefs, and experiences in shaping their learning process. The study can examine how self-directed learning modules can facilitate students' construction of knowledge and how their prior knowledge, beliefs, and experiences can influence their learning outcomes.

Finally, adult learning theory emphasizes the unique characteristics of adult learners, including their motivation, experience, and self-direction. The study can examine how the design and implementation of self-directed learning modules can take into account these unique characteristics and how these modules can effectively engage adult learners.

Overall, the theoretical framework for the study on the effectiveness of self-directed learning modules can draw from several theoretical perspectives, including the self-directed learning theory, constructivism, and adult learning theory. 21. What is the Theoretical framework of the effect of mass media to moral development of grade 11 student​


Answer:

The theoretical framework for studying the effect of mass media on the moral development of grade 11 students can draw upon several theories from different fields, such as psychology, sociology, and media studies. Some of the theoretical frameworks that could be used to investigate this topic include:

Social Learning Theory: This theory suggests that people learn by observing and imitating the behavior of others. In the context of media, this means that students may learn moral values from the characters they see on TV shows, movies, and social media platforms.

Cognitive Development Theory: This theory emphasizes the role of cognitive processes in shaping moral development. It posits that as students mature, they develop increasingly complex ways of understanding moral concepts and making moral judgments. Exposure to media can provide opportunities for students to engage in moral reasoning and reflect on their own values.

Cultivation Theory: This theory suggests that media exposure shapes individuals' perceptions of reality over time. Exposure to media that presents certain values or behaviors as normative can lead individuals to adopt those values or behaviors as their own. In the context of moral development, exposure to media that portrays certain behaviors as acceptable or desirable could influence students' own moral values and decision-making.

Uses and Gratifications Theory: This theory focuses on the ways in which individuals use media to fulfill their own needs and desires. In the context of moral development, students may seek out media that aligns with their own moral values, or they may use media to explore different perspectives on moral issues.

Media Ecology Theory: This theory emphasizes the ways in which media and technology shape social interactions and cultural norms. In the context of moral development, media exposure can influence the cultural values and norms that students are exposed to, potentially shaping their own moral beliefs and behaviors.

These are just a few examples of theoretical frameworks that could be used to study the effect of mass media on the moral development of grade 11 students. The specific framework chosen would depend on the research question and the hypotheses being tested.

26. theoretical framework about bullying input,process, output,​


Answer:

Input:

Individual factors: These include personal characteristics such as temperament, self-esteem, and social skills. Individuals who have low self-esteem, poor social skills, and difficulty regulating their emotions are more likely to engage in bullying behavior.

1.Social factors: These include family dynamics, peer relationships, and cultural norms. Children who grow up in households with high levels of conflict, have limited social support, and are exposed to aggressive behavior in the media are more likely to engage in bullying behavior.

2.Environmental factors: These include school and community factors such as school climate and neighborhood safety. Schools with high levels of bullying and violence may create an environment where bullying is more likely to occur.

Process:

The processing stage involves the cognitive and emotional mechanisms that underlie bullying behavior. These include:

1.Attitudes and beliefs: Individuals who hold negative attitudes and beliefs about others, such as intolerance or prejudice, are more likely to engage in bullying behavior.

Emotions: Emotions such as anger, frustration, and jealousy can trigger bullying behavior.

2.Social cognition: Social cognition refers to how individuals process and interpret social information. Children who have difficulty understanding others' emotions or intentions are more likely to engage in bullying behavior.

Output:

The output stage includes the effects of bullying on the victim, the bully, and the wider community. These include:

1.Victim effects: Victims of bullying can experience a range of negative effects, including emotional distress, physical harm, academic problems, and social isolation.

2.Bully effects: Bullies can also experience negative effects, such as academic problems, mental health problems, and increased risk of future delinquent behavior.

Community effects: Bullying can also have wider effects on the community, including creating a negative school climate, decreasing academic achievement, and increasing fear and mistrust in the community.
