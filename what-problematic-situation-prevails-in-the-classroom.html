1. What problematic situation prevails in the classroom?​


Problematic Situation Prevails in The Classroom

Classroom challenges are one of the adequate problems faced by teachers and a good teacher has the courage to overcome all these challenges bravely.

Some of the common classroom challenges faced by teachers include lack of teamwork, minimum personal time, working towards long term goals, arguments and student excuses, etc.

Addressing these common classroom challenges can not only help to improve teacher retention rates but also increase student success rates and the ultimate quality of education.

Teaching in a class where students are well disciplined will definitely feel easier than students who are not disciplined. Having students who lack discipline is a challenge for teachers. You should be able to change their behavior better.

Each student has different abilities in understanding and mastering lessons. Because of that the teacher cannot force students to immediately understand. Teachers must motivate and inspire students to learn and give them time to understand more.

Teachers who are fierce, tend to be stiff, and are not friendly with students will make their relationship feel distant. Confusion will occur in students so that students become passive, shy, and afraid to ask the teacher.

The solution is that the teacher must be warm and interact more with students. This will make students not afraid and more comfortable asking questions and increase student activity in learning. You also have to be able to recognize various student characters so that you can provide solutions to student problems.

26. What technique in family therapy cast a new light on a problem and provides a different interpretation of a problematic situation


One technique in family therapy that can help to cast a new light on a problem and provide a different interpretation of a problematic situation is reframing. Reframing involves helping the family to look at a problem or situation from a different perspective, often by reframing the way they understand or interpret the problem. This can involve reinterpreting the problem as an opportunity for growth or change, or by considering the problem in the context of larger systemic issues. By looking at the problem in a new way, the family may be able to find new solutions or approaches to addressing the issue.
