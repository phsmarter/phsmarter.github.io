Title: Exploring the Problem of Student Burnout: A Qualitative Study

Research Question: What are the experiences and perceptions of senior high school students regarding the problem of burnout, and how do they cope with it?

Background: Student burnout has become a significant concern for many high school students around the world, especially as academic demands and expectations continue to rise. Burnout can lead to negative consequences such as decreased academic performance, increased stress and anxiety, and even mental health issues. It is important to understand how students perceive and cope with burnout to help educators and parents address this issue effectively.

Methodology: This study will use a qualitative research design, specifically a phenomenological approach, to explore the experiences and perceptions of senior high school students regarding burnout. Participants will be selected through purposive sampling from a local high school, and data will be collected through semi-structured interviews. The interviews will be audio-recorded and transcribed verbatim for analysis.

Expected Findings: The study is expected to provide insights into how senior high school students experience burnout, including the factors that contribute to it, the symptoms they experience, and the coping strategies they use. The study may also reveal any differences in experiences and coping strategies based on gender, grade level, or academic track.

Significance: The findings of this study can be used to inform educators and parents on how to help students cope with burnout and promote better mental health and academic performance. By understanding the experiences and perceptions of students regarding burnout, educators and parents can develop more effective interventions and support systems to address this issue. The study can also contribute to the existing literature on student burnout and provide a basis for further research in this area. Ultimately, the study aims to improve the well-being and academic success of senior high school students.

16. what benefits can teachers get about the study of investigating students academic performance in online learning of college students through qualitative research approach?


Answer:

Teachers can benefit in several ways from investigating students' academic performance in online learning of college students through a qualitative research approach. Some of these benefits include:

1. Improved teaching strategies: Qualitative research can help teachers understand the factors that contribute to students' success or challenges in online learning. They can use this information to develop more effective teaching strategies that cater to different learning styles and needs, leading to improved academic performance among their students.

2. Enhanced student engagement: By understanding the factors that motivate students to engage in online learning, teachers can adapt their teaching methods to encourage greater participation and engagement from their students. This can lead to increased academic performance as students become more invested in the learning process.

3. Identification of student needs: Qualitative research can help teachers identify the specific needs of their students, such as language barriers or learning disabilities, which may be impacting their academic performance. Teachers can then implement appropriate accommodations or support services to ensure that all students have an equal opportunity to succeed.

4. Development of personalized learning plans: By analyzing student performance data through a qualitative research approach, teachers can develop personalized learning plans that cater to the unique needs of each student. This can lead to improved academic performance as students receive targeted support and instruction.

5. Professional development: Conducting qualitative research can help teachers develop their research skills and deepen their understanding of the factors that impact student academic performance in online learning. This can contribute to their professional development and improve their ability to support their students' academic success.

Possible qualitative research topic for Grade 11 Senior High School students about the problem of students:

Title: Understanding the Challenges Faced by Senior High School Students in Remote Learning During the COVID-19 Pandemic

Research Question: What are the challenges faced by senior high school students in remote learning during the COVID-19 pandemic and how do they cope with these challenges?

Introduction:

The COVID-19 pandemic has brought significant changes to the education system worldwide. In the Philippines, senior high school students have been affected by the shift to remote learning, which requires students to study from home using digital devices and online platforms. However, this mode of learning has posed several challenges for students, such as lack of access to technology and internet connectivity, distractions at home, difficulty in adjusting to online learning, and isolation from peers and teachers. This qualitative research aims to understand the challenges faced by senior high school students in remote learning during the COVID-19 pandemic and how they cope with these challenges.

Methodology:

The study will use a qualitative research design, specifically a phenomenological approach, to gain an in-depth understanding of the experiences and perspectives of senior high school students in remote learning during the COVID-19 pandemic. The participants will be Grade 11 students from different public and private schools in the Philippines who have been studying remotely for at least one academic year. Data will be collected through semi-structured interviews, which will be conducted online via video conferencing. The interviews will be audio-recorded and transcribed for data analysis.

Data Analysis:

The data collected from the interviews will be analyzed using a thematic analysis approach. The analysis will involve identifying recurring themes and patterns in the data related to the challenges faced by students in remote learning and their coping strategies. The themes and patterns will be organized into categories and subcategories, which will be used to generate an understanding of the challenges and coping strategies of the participants.

Conclusion:

This qualitative research aims to contribute to the understanding of the challenges faced by senior high school students in remote learning during the COVID-19 pandemic and how they cope with these challenges. The findings of this study may inform educational policies and practices that address the needs of students in remote learning and provide support to students who are struggling with the challenges of online education.
