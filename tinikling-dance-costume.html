TINIKLING DANCE

-originated during the Spanish occupation in the Philippines, during the 1500s when the Spaniards conquered the Philippines. It was started by farmers on the Visayan Islands of Leyte. The dance has also been said to have derived from a punishment that the Spaniards practiced. It imitates the movement of the tikling birds as they walk between grass stems, run over tree branches, or dodge bamboo traps set by rice farmers." Dancers imitate the tikling bird's legendary grace and speed by skillfully maneuvering between large bamboo poles.

WHAT IS THE CHARACTERISTICS/DESCRIPTION OF THE DANCE

-The dance involves two people beating, tapping, and sliding the bamboo poles on the ground and against each other in coordination or more dancers who step over and in between the poles in a dance

WHAT COSTUME/ACCESORIES, WHAT MUSIC OR RHYTHYM

For the dance, females traditionally wear a dress called balintawak or patadyong, and males wear an untucked embroidered shirt called the barong Tagalog. The balintawak are colorful dresses with wide arched sleeves and the patadyong is a pineapple fiber blouse paired with checkered skirts. The barong Tagalog is usually light long sleeved shirts and worn with red trousers. Dancers wear no footwear while performing.

In the United States, this dance has been altered into a four-beat rhythm to adjust to popular music. In some cases, it has been used in conjunction with traditional Filipino martial arts to demonstrate fleetness of foot and flow of movement.

14. TINIKLING 1. Why is the dance called Tinikling? 2. Music and Count (Time Signature)3. Costume (MALE & FEMALE)​


TINIKLING

Tinikling is a traditional Philippine folk dance and the national dance of the Philippines originated in the island of Leyte in the Visayas during Spanish colonial era. The performance involves the rhythmic clapping, tapping, and sliding of two bamboo poles against each other while one or more dancers step over in between the poles timed to the beat.

WHY IS THE DANCE CALLED TINIKLING?

Tinikling is said to be named after a long-legged bird called TIKLING.

The dance imitates the movement of the tikling birds as they dodge the bamboo traps set by the farmers. Dancer mimics bird's grace and agility by dancing between large bamboo poles.

MUSIC AND COUNT

The time signature of Tinikling is ¾ so we have 1, 2, 3, counts in every measure.

COSTUME

MALE- Barong Tagalog or camesa de chino and long red trousers.

FEMALE- Balintawak or Patadyong.

Answer:

Background and Origin: It historically originated during the Spanish occupation in the country specifically in Leyte, an island in the Visayas.

Description of the dance:The dance involves two people beating, tapping, and sliding bamboo poles on the ground and against each other in coordination with one or more dancers who step over and in between the poles in a dance.

Adaptation of the dance:Some schools in the U.S. include this dance into their curriculum using pop music to keep the dance modern.

Costume used in the dance:Female dancers usually wear a traditional dress called Balintawak (a colorful dress with arched sleeves) or a Patadyong (a checkered loose skirt that’s often worn with a thin-fibered blouse).

Males dancers wear a Barong Tagalog (which is the country’s national outfit for Filipino men) usually a light long sleeved shirt paired with red trousers.
