Explanation: Good afternoon everyone,

Today I will be presenting on the topic of fossil fuels. Fossil fuels are non-renewable sources of energy that are formed from the remains of plants and animals that lived millions of years ago. They include coal, oil, and natural gas.

Fossil fuels have played a crucial role in shaping our modern world. They have powered industries, transportation, and homes for centuries. However, as we continue to rely heavily on fossil fuels, we are facing a number of challenges.

One major issue is climate change. Burning fossil fuels releases greenhouse gases into the atmosphere, which contribute to global warming and climate change. As a result, there is a growing need to transition to cleaner sources of energy such as wind, solar, and hydropower.

Another challenge is the depletion of fossil fuel reserves. Fossil fuels are finite resources, and we are rapidly using them up. This means that we will eventually run out of these resources, which will have significant economic and social impacts.

Despite these challenges, fossil fuels will continue to be an important part of our energy mix for the foreseeable future. However, it is important that we find ways to use them more efficiently and responsibly.

In conclusion, while fossil fuels have played an important role in powering our modern world, we need to acknowledge their limitations and work towards a more sustainable and cleaner energy future. Thank you for listening.

19. presentation script for biomass(energy)​


Introduction:

Good morning/afternoon everyone. Today, I'd like to talk to you about a fascinating source of energy that is becoming increasingly popular around the world - biomass energy. Biomass energy is derived from organic materials, which can be used to produce electricity, heat, and fuel for transportation. In this presentation, we will explore what biomass energy is, how it works, and the advantages and disadvantages of using this renewable energy source.

What is Biomass Energy?

Biomass energy is derived from organic materials, such as wood, crop waste, manure, and municipal solid waste. It is a renewable energy source because these materials can be replenished over time. Biomass energy is created through a process called combustion, which involves burning the organic material to produce heat. This heat can then be used to produce electricity, heat homes, or power vehicles.

How does Biomass Energy work?

The process of generating biomass energy starts with collecting organic materials, such as wood chips or agricultural waste. The materials are then processed to remove any impurities, such as rocks or metals. Once the organic material is cleaned, it is burned in a biomass power plant. During the burning process, heat is produced, which creates steam. The steam then powers a turbine, which generates electricity.

Advantages of Biomass Energy:

Biomass energy is a renewable energy source, which means it can be replenished over time. Unlike fossil fuels, biomass energy does not produce greenhouse gases that contribute to climate change. Using biomass energy can also reduce the amount of waste that goes into landfills, which helps to reduce pollution.

Disadvantages of Biomass Energy:

One of the disadvantages of biomass energy is that it can be expensive to produce. It requires a significant amount of organic material to generate electricity, and the cost of collecting, processing, and transporting the material can be high. Biomass energy can also contribute to air pollution, particularly if the burning process is not controlled properly.

Conclusion:

In conclusion, biomass energy is a fascinating source of renewable energy that has the potential to help us reduce our dependence on fossil fuels. While there are some disadvantages to using this type of energy, the benefits, such as its renewability and its ability to reduce waste, make it a promising energy source for the future. Thank you for your attention.

1. The contest is open to all students-who are enrolled in GECC or General Education Core Curriculum under Arts and Sciences.

2. During the contest proper the following materials will be used A board (provided by the facilitator) > Writing material (chalk)

> Eraser (tissue) 3. The questions will be from General Information, Social Science, Natural Science,

Language and Mathematics.

4. The contest will have 3 rounds: Easy, Average, and Difficult round. There will be ten (10) questions for the EASY, five (5) questions for the AVERAGE, and five (5)

questions for the DIFFICULT round. 5. The quizmaster shall read each question twice. After the second reading, the quizmaster shall say the word "GO". Only then the contestants shall begin writing their answers on the boards provided. The time allotment is 10 seconds for regular questions

and 20 seconds for math questions. 6. After the time limit and buzzer sound, the contestant will automatically stop writing The contestants should raise their answer and the correct answer will be flashed on the

screen.

7. Any protest or appeals regarding the preceding question will be entertained before the

quizmaster proceed to the next question.
