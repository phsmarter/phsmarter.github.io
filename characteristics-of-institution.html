Problem

There are four options that describe family except one. That single difference is what we will be looking for.

a. a family is never isolated b. a family serves as an important universal social institution c. a family does not involve in creating and shaping self and collective identities d. a family can also interact with other social institutions and contribute to social stability and change

Explanation

Let's evaluate each given option.

A. A family is never isolated

No man is an island, as we all know. An individual or group of people needs interaction with other living entities in order to flourish and progress. However, there are instances where a family is isolated from others for a variety of reasons. One factor is that their home may be extremely remote from their neighbors or the busy neighborhood. Their home could be far from most people and located close to the top of a mountain or hill.

B. A family serves as an important universal social institution

As the definition above states, a family is the basic institution of society. Therefore, it is crucial in the development of society as a whole.

C. A family does not involve in creating and shaping self and collective identities

[tex]\textcolor{\text{It's a bluff!}}[/tex] A family has an influence on the identities of its members since it is an integral element of society. The mother and father provide their children with every opportunity to develop into healthy adults who contribute to the betterment of society. The children also add color and happiness to the family.

D. A family can also interact with other social institutions and contribute to social stability and change

Definitely! There are many social institutions in existence in society, including religions, hospitals, the government, non-profit organizations, and schools. These institutions would struggle to operate in the absence of families. Positive interactions between these social institutions have the potential to greatly contribute to social stability and societal progress.

Therefore, from the given explanation for each option, the letter C is not a characteristic of a family.

Answer:Abstract

Contemporary theories of institutions as clusters of stable solutions to recurrent coordination problems can illuminate and explain some unresolved difficulties and problems adhering to institutional definitions of art initiated by George Dickie and Arthur Danto. Their account of what confers upon objects their institutional character does not fit well with current work on institutions and social ontology. The claim that “the artworld” confers the status of “art” onto objects remains utterly mysterious. The “artworld” is a generic notion that designates a sphere of human activity that involves practices that create goals that have led to the emergence of formal and informal institutions. But those institutions, rather than magically “creating” objects subjected to esthetic appreciation, merely solve familiar and ubiquitous coordination problems created by artistic activity in ways other institutions in other areas (science, religion, education…) solve similar and/or analogous coordination problems.

17. Formal Institution Informal Institution 1. 2. Informal Institution 1. 2. diss


I can provide examples of formal and informal institutions to help illustrate the concept:

Formal Institutions:

1.) Government - this is an example of a formal institution because it is a structured and organized entity with specific rules, regulations, and laws that govern its operation.

2.) Educational institutions - these are also formal institutions because they have a clear structure, hierarchy, and formalized processes for teaching and learning.

Informal Institutions:

1.) Family - this is an example of an informal institution because it is based on social norms and customs rather than formal rules and regulations. Families have their own unique cultures, traditions, and values that are passed down from generation to generation.

2.) Peer groups - these are also informal institutions because they are based on informal social structures and relationships rather than formal rules and regulations. Peer groups can form based on shared interests, experiences, or affiliations, and can exert a strong influence on individual behavior and decision-making.


18. what did you understand by economic institutions religious institutionseducational institutions family institutions EXPLAIN IT BY YOUR OWN UNDERSTANDING. pa helppp​


Answer:

here's my explanation of these four types of institutions:

Economic institutions: These are the systems and structures that govern the production, distribution, and consumption of goods and services within a society. Examples of economic institutions include markets, financial systems, corporations, labor unions, and government agencies that regulate trade and commerce. Economic institutions can have a significant impact on the well-being and prosperity of individuals and communities.

Religious institutions: These are organizations or systems that support and promote a particular religion or set of beliefs. Religious institutions can include churches, mosques, synagogues, temples, and other places of worship. They can also include religious schools, charitable organizations, and social service agencies. Religious institutions often play an important role in shaping cultural values and practices, and can provide a sense of community and belonging for their members.

Educational institutions: These are organizations and structures that provide formal education and training to individuals. Examples of educational institutions include schools, colleges, universities, and vocational training centers. Educational institutions can have a significant impact on individuals' social mobility, as well as on the overall economic and intellectual development of a society.

Family institutions: These are the systems and structures that govern the relationships and responsibilities within families. Family institutions can include cultural norms and traditions around marriage, child-rearing, and caregiving. They can also include legal systems and government policies that affect families, such as laws related to divorce, adoption, and child support. Family institutions can have a profound impact on individuals' emotional well-being and social development, as well as on the overall stability and cohesion of a society.
