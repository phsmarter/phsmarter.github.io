One lesson that I have learned from his life and strategies is the importance of perseverance. Hershey faced numerous failures and setbacks before achieving success, including several bankruptcies and failed business ventures. However, he did not let these challenges deter him, and he continued to work hard and pursue his dreams. This resilience and determination is something that I can apply to my own life, both in personal and professional contexts.

Another lesson that I have learned from Hershey's life and strategies is the value of investing in one's employees. Hershey believed that a company's success was dependent on the happiness and satisfaction of its workers, and he implemented policies and programs that prioritized the well-being of his employees. For example, he established a company town for Hershey's workers, complete with housing, schools, and healthcare facilities. This approach not only ensured that his employees were well taken care of, but it also created a loyal and dedicated workforce that was committed to the success of the company.

Finally, Hershey's philanthropic efforts demonstrate the importance of giving back to the community. Hershey used his wealth to establish the Milton Hershey School, a boarding school for underprivileged children, as well as a variety of other charitable organizations. His commitment to social responsibility and community engagement is a reminder that success should not be measured solely in terms of financial gain, but also in terms of the positive impact that one can have on others. As I reflect on Hershey's life and strategies, I am inspired to work hard, treat others with kindness and respect, and use my resources to make a positive difference in the world.
