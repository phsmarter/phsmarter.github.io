11. Include your contact information. How to Write a Concept Paper A concept paper is a document written to convince potential funders to sponsor a product, program or service. Concept papers vary in format and specifics depending on the organization, but are generally concise documents containing information, statistics and persuasive arguments. Here are the steps that are required to write a concept paper. Writing Your Own Concept Paper 1 Acknowledge the role of the sponsoring organization. Demonstrate your knowledge of the private or public organization by summarizing the nature of its involvement in the project. For example, if the funders are dedicated to advancing literacy in developing nations, acknowledge their successful history in this area. 2 Describe your team or organization. Explain the core objective of your project. For example, you may be an instructional software company that uses software programs to teach reading skills to disenfranchised populations. Clearly convey your position as it relates to the goals of the funding organization. 3 Identify the problem. Funders read your concept paper because they have a shared interest in the problem. Describe the core issue. Explain the problem in enough detail to convey your background and expertise with the issue. If necessary, include data to demonstrate the extent of the problem. 4 Include an assessment of previously applied methods. This may be a report of your past successes with a similar project. Or it may be a reference to a history of successes experienced by various organizations who have applied your specific solution to the problem. 5 Explain how you will meet the need. Describe the product, service or program. Be clear and concise. Use language that the reader will easily understand. Avoid using technical language for a non-technical audience. 6 Outline your intended objective. Your solution to the problem may save money, advance learning or prevent health issues. Include quantitative details, such as the number of people who will benefit, how much time will be saved or the amount of profit that will be generated. 7 List the resources necessary to offer the services. This may include a list of materials to build a product or the type of personnel required to provide the services. A detailed list will demonstrate your grasp of the project's needs and your readiness to provide the product or service. 8 Establish a time line for delivery of services. Explain how long it will take to build the product, deliver the service or manage the project. The time line should be both realistic and efficient. 9 Specify the amount of funds you are requesting. If possible, research the amount of money that the funding organization has available to stay within its budget. Asking for an amount that exceeds the available funds may result in an immediate dismissal of your program, project or service. 10 Summarize the concept paper by describing the outcome. Close with a few sentences to ensure the sponsoring organization that an investment in your team or organization will meet objectives and solve the specific problem. 11 Include your contact information. List your name, address, website, phone number and email address

14. 2. How may the techniques for clarifying concepts assist you in writing your upcoming concept paper?​


Answer:

The techniques for clarifying concepts can be very helpful in writing a concept paper. Here are some ways that these techniques can assist you in writing your upcoming concept paper:

1. Conceptual analysis - This technique involves breaking down a concept into its constituent parts and examining each part in detail. By using conceptual analysis, you can better understand the concept you are writing about and identify any ambiguities or inconsistencies in your understanding of the concept. This can help you to refine your definition of the concept and ensure that you are using it consistently throughout your paper.

2. Definition by example - This technique involves defining a concept by providing examples of it. By using this technique, you can help your readers to better understand the concept you are writing about by providing concrete examples that illustrate its meaning. This can be particularly useful if the concept you are writing about is abstract or difficult to define.

3. Comparison and contrast - This technique involves comparing and contrasting a concept with similar or related concepts. By using this technique, you can help your readers to better understand the nuances of the concept you are writing about and distinguish it from other similar concepts. This can be particularly useful if the concept you are writing about is often confused with other concepts.

4. Operational definition - This technique involves defining a concept in terms of how it is measured or observed. By using this technique, you can provide a clear and objective definition of the concept you are writing about that is based on empirical evidence. This can be particularly useful if you are writing about a concept that is often debated or controversial.

Overall, by using these techniques for clarifying concepts, you can ensure that your concept paper is clear, accurate, and consistent in its use of concepts. This can help to ensure that your readers understand your ideas and arguments and can follow your reasoning throughout your paper.

25. How do we write a concept paper using explication?

Explanation:

A concept paper that uses explication as a strategy means that more details will need to be added. In ordinary language, the verbs "explicate" and "explain" are usually used interchangeably. But this is not the case with literary criticism--which I will explain below.

But first, let us define what a concept paper is. An academic concept paper is a brief summary written to outline a research project. In its limited definition, it can also be a paper that discusses an idea, concept or process in a orderly and clear manner. The purpose of a concept paper is to make the reader understand a process or a concept right away.

When explicating, you are laying what the concept is and generally answers the following questions:

What is the concept about?What is it made of?What are the principles behind it?How do you identify it?How do you differentiate it from other concepts?

Once the definition is made, then you can explain the process. This means that you outline the steps in details--the steps, including the materials needed. Think of it like a recipe. You need the ingredients, the measurements as well as the steps that are needed to make the perfect dish. In other words, explication is detailed and intense explanation.

After doing what was mentioned above, you can close with a summary of what has been discussed so far.

Going back to the other definition of explication--it can be used as an alternative to the term Explication de Texte in literary analysis. This technique calls for a "close reading" of the literary text and then explaining the style, the tone, structure and imagery of the text.

A concept paper has many applications in real life and is most notably used when creating a business proposal where explication is used.

Answer:

What are the Main Elements of a Research Concept Paper?

•Title page

— provides a tentative title for the dissertation. The title of the Concept Paper should be a stand-alone statement that can fully describe the project by summarizing the main idea of the manuscript.

•Statement of the Problem

— provides the purpose for the research.

•Preliminary Literature Review

— provides identification of major literature that supports and validates the topic.

•Goal Statement

— provides a broad or abstract intention, including the research goals and objectives.

•Research Questions

— provides a preliminary view of the questions the student will investigate. Questions are based on theory, past research, and need.

•An Abridged Methodology

— provides the student’s best idea on how to conduct the research and analyze the data.

•Timeline

— provides a range of time for completion of the project, highlighting key elements for each stage of the project. This element is unique to the Concept Paper and provides the student structure for managing sections of the project within a realistic time frame.

•References

— provides references to the material cited in the literature review and elsewhere in the Concept Paper.
