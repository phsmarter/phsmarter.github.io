On December 20, 2018, the owner withdrew cash in anticipation of the Christmas day celebration in the amount of Php 10, 000. No additional activities affected the owner's equity in 2018. By December 31, 2018, the restaurant had increased to Php 80, 000. In December 31, 2018 financial statements, how much is the total asset? And how much is the total owner's equity?​


Answer:

---------------------------------------------------------------------------

The total assets of Gutumin Restaurant on December 31, 2018 is Php 580,000. This can be calculated from the initial equity (Php 500,000 + Php 200,000) + net income (Php 120,000) - owner's withdrawal (Php 10,000). The total owner's equity on December 31, 2018 is Php 80,000 which is equal to the amount of increased equity as stated in the question.

14. You are a financial Manager of Chevron Corp. You need to assess the effectiveness of working capital management of the company for 2018 using the following data. What is the 2018 Receivable turnover? 2017 Account Receivable = 15,353,000 2018 Account Receivable = 15,050,000 2017 Inventory = 5,585,000 2018 Inventory = 5,704,000 2017 Accounts Payable = 14,565,000 2018 Accounts Payable = 13,953,000 2017 Sales = 134,674,000 2018 Sales = 158,902,000 2017 Cost of Sales = 95,114,000 2018 Cost of Sales = 113,997,000 2017 Purchases = 95,114,000 2018 Purchases = 123,435,000 10.20 O 10.45 10.60​


Answer:

The formula for receivable turnover is:

Receivable turnover = Sales / Average Accounts Receivable

To calculate the average accounts receivable, we add the beginning and ending balances and divide by 2:

Average Accounts Receivable = (Beginning Accounts Receivable + Ending Accounts Receivable) / 2

Therefore, the average accounts receivable for Chevron Corp. in 2018 is:

Average Accounts Receivable = (15,353,000 + 15,050,000) / 2 = 15,201,500

And the receivable turnover for 2018 is:

Receivable turnover = 158,902,000 / 15,201,500 = 10.45

Therefore, the 2018 Receivable turnover for Chevron Corp. is 10.45.

24. What is the accounting treatment when the financial statements of an associate are not prepared as of the same date as the financial statements of the investor


EQUITY METHOD

An investment in an associate is recorded using the equity method as of the day it meets the criteria for being considered an associate.

When an associate's financial statements are prepared as of the end of a reporting period different from the investor's, the gap cannot be more than three months when using the equity method. reporting comparable transactions and additional occurrences.

In any instance, there cannot be a more than three-month gap between the end of the reporting period of the associate or joint venture and that of the business. The reporting periods' lengths and any discrepancies in their endings must remain constant from one reporting period to the next.

When utilizing the equity technique, the investor consults the associate's most current financial accounts that are readily available. When the reporting date of the investor differs from that of the associate, the investor's reporting date is used to generate the associate's financial statements.
