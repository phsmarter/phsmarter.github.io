4. Cite some ethical issues in education in the Philippines? Explain​


Answer:

1. Conduct

Conduct is an important aspect of ethical practices. Conduct can include boundary violations, abuse or improper relationships. In the education context, teachers should have vision, hope and creativity. However, be cautious as sarcasm and cynicism tend to inhibit these qualities. Know also that cynicism and sarcasm are signs of anger or pain and they should be respectfully addressed so that possible deeper needs and concerns of instructors can be nurtured.

2. Assessment

Assessment of students is another “hot spot” of ethical practices. Teachers must be honest and promise this ethical habit. Falsification of data or information related to assessment violates every model of ethical conduct and law. While on the surface, in the moment, or initially such conduct may seem to benefit students or the school, it in fact injures not only both the student and school, but also the teacher and society as well.

3. Planning

Good planning is a part of the commitment that every teacher needs to make as a professional. Remember, teachers are committed to prudence. Prudence, for centuries meant the establishment of right goals and selecting the right means to achieve this goal. A prudent teacher creates the right goals and selects the best means to accomplish the goals.

Learning activities and lesson plans are developed in advance and the learning environment is created and assessed in a dynamic way so that it addresses the real needs of each student maximally, across all modes of learning.

6. 1. effects of educational social problems in the philippines 2. solutions of educational social problems in the philippines 3. causes of drugs in the philippines 4. effects of drugs in the philippines 5. solutions of drugs in the philippines


Answer:

1. Effects of Educational Social Problems in the Philippines: - High drop-out rates among students - Poor quality of education due to lack of resources and facilities - Limited access to education, especially for children from poor families - Inadequate training and support for teachers - Unequal distribution of educational opportunities across regions and social classes - Limited job opportunities for graduates, leading to unemployment and underemployment - Brain drain as highly skilled professionals seek better opportunities abroad - Limited innovation and economic growth due to lack of skilled workers

2. Solutions of Educational Social Problems in the Philippines: - Increase government funding for education to improve resources and facilities - Provide scholarships and financial assistance to students from poor families - Improve teacher training and support to enhance teaching quality - Address regional and social inequalities in educational opportunities - Encourage private sector investment in education - Promote technical and vocational education to address unemployment and underemployment - Enhance research and development in education to promote innovation and economic growth

3. Causes of Drugs in the Philippines: - Poverty and lack of economic opportunities - Weak law enforcement and corruption - Accessibility of drugs due to porous borders and inadequate border control - Influence of drug syndicates and organized crime - Peer pressure and social acceptance of drug use - Lack of awareness and education on the dangers of drug use - Mental health issues and trauma

4. Effects of Drugs in the Philippines: - Increased crime rates and violence - Health problems and addiction - Social disintegration and breakdown of families - Economic losses due to decreased productivity and increased healthcare costs - Stigmatization and discrimination against drug users - Undermining of national security and sovereignty

5. Solutions of Drugs in the Philippines: - Strengthen law enforcement and border control measures - Address poverty and provide economic opportunities for vulnerable communities - Increase awareness and education on the dangers of drug use - Provide access to mental health services and trauma support - Enhance rehabilitation and treatment programs for drug users - Address corruption and organized crime - Promote community-based approaches to drug prevention and rehabilitation.

27. give atleast 5 issues and trends in philippine educational system​


Answer:

-Quality of Education.

-Budget for Education.

-Affordability of Education.

-Drop-out Rate (Out-of-school youth)

-Mismatch.

Quality of education

First of which, is the quality of education. In the year 2014, the National Achievement Test (NAT) and the National Career Assessment Examination (NCAE) results show that there had been a decline in the quality of Philippine education at the elementary and secondary levels. The students' performance in both the 2014 NAT and NCAE were excessively below the target mean score. Having said this, the poor quality of the Philippine educational system is manifested in the comparison of completion rates between highly urbanized city of Metro Manila, which is also happens to be not only the country's capital but the largest metropolitan area in the Philippines and other places in the country such as Mindanao and Eastern Visayas. Although Manila is able to boast a primary school completion rate of approximately 100 percent, other areas of the nation, such as Eastern Visayas and Mindanao, hold primary school completion rate of only 30 percent or even less. This kind of statistic is no surprise to the education system in the Philippine context, students who hail from Philippine urban areas have the financial capacity to complete at the very least their primary school education.

Budget for education

The second issue that the Philippine educational system faces is the budget for education. Although it has been mandated by the Philippine Constitution for the government to allocate the highest proportion of its government to education, the Philippines remains to have one of the lowest budget allocations to education among ASEAN countries.

Affordability of education

The third prevalent issue the Philippine educational system continuously encounters is the affordability of education (or lack thereof). A big disparity in educational achievements is evident across various social groups. Socioeconomically disadvantaged students otherwise known as students who are members of high and low-income poverty-stricken families, have immensely higher drop-out rates in the elementary level. Additionally, most freshmen students at the tertiary level come from relatively well-off families.

Drop out rate (Out-of-school youth)

France Castro, secretary of Alliance of Concerned Teachers (ACT), stated that there is a graved need to address the alarming number of out-of-school youth in the country. The Philippines overall has 1.4 million children who are out-of-school, according to UNESCO's data, and is additionally the only ASEAN country that is included in the top 5 countries with the highest number of out-of-school youth. In 2012, the Department of Education showed data of a 6.38% drop-out rate in primary school and a 7.82% drop-out rate in secondary school. Castro further stated that "the increasing number of out-of-school children is being caused by poverty. The price increases in prices of oil, electricity, rice, water, and other basic commodities are further pushing the poor into dire poverty." Subsequently, as more families become poorer, the number of students enrolled in public schools increases, especially in the high school level. In 2013, the Department of Education estimated that there are 38, 503 elementary schools alongside 7,470 high schools.

Mismatch

There is a large mismatch between educational training and actual jobs. This stands to be a major issue at the tertiary level and it is furthermore the cause of the continuation of a substantial amount of educated yet unemployed or underemployed people. According to Dean Salvador Belaro Jr., the Cornell-educated Congressman representing 1-Ang Edukasyon Party-list in the House of Representatives, the number of educated unemployed reaches around 600,000 per year. He refers to said condition as the "education gap".

30. Problems and Solutions of education in the Philippines


Answer:

There are several problems facing the education system in the Philippines, including:

1. Lack of funding: The Philippine government allocates a relatively small percentage of its budget to education, which limits the resources available to schools and teachers.

2. Poor infrastructure: Many schools in the Philippines lack basic facilities such as classrooms, libraries, and laboratories, which can make it difficult for students to learn effectively.

3. Low teacher salaries: Teachers in the Philippines are often paid low salaries, which can lead to a shortage of qualified educators and lower the quality of education.

4. Inadequate curriculum: The curriculum in the Philippines is often criticized for being too focused on memorization and not providing students with the critical thinking and problem-solving skills they need.

5. Limited access to education: Many children in the Philippines are unable to attend school due to poverty, distance, or lack of transportation.

To address these problems, several solutions have been proposed, including:

1. Increased funding: The Philippine government could allocate a higher percentage of its budget to education, which would provide schools with more resources and improve the quality of education.

2. Infrastructure improvements: Investing in school infrastructure, such as building more classrooms and providing access to technology, could help create a more conducive learning environment.

3. Higher teacher salaries: Increasing teacher salaries would help attract and retain qualified educators, which would improve the quality of education.

4. Curriculum reform: The curriculum could be revised to focus more on critical thinking and problem-solving skills, which would better prepare students for the workforce.

5. Expanded access to education: Efforts could be made to provide more opportunities for children to attend school, such as providing transportation and expanding distance learning programs.

Overall, addressing these problems and implementing these solutions would require a concerted effort from various stakeholders, including the government, schools, teachers, and communities. By working together, it is possible to create a more effective and equitable education system in the Philippines.
