8. EssayHave you experience an earthquake? Cite some of your observation and damages that yousaw after the event. Explain your answer using specific examples. (10 points)​


Yes I already did experience an earthquake.

I was 13 years old back then. I was sleeping in my house, having bunked the republic day celebrations at school. The 2nd term examinations had just ended a day earlier and I just wanted to sleep. My parents were invited at a family friend's place for breakfast and they allowed me to take a holiday and rest in.

Suddenly the whole house started shaking. I tried getting up but couldn't. My sister shouted from the other room to come out. I made my way out of my room with great difficulty. The house was in a mess. All the kitchen items were on the floor. All the food that was kept in the fridge spilled out. I met my sister near the hall and along with my grandfather we came out of the house.

The moment we came out we heard a big loud sound and we saw the adjacent building had come down. I started crying. I had friends in that building and I was too much in shock to understand what was hapoening.

Just then my sister asked,

“Do you know where mummy papa have gone?”

I replied in the negative. My heart started racing. All the people were out on the streets and nobody wanted to go back inside. My sister suggested she would go look for our parents and told me to be strong and look after my grandfather.

Before my sister could leave we saw our parents returning. Thankfully they were alright. The few things that are still etched in my memory after so many years are,

People risking their lives to take out debris and trying to help out people who were stuck and couldn't move. In one such instance there was a man who kept talking to a woman stuck inside till the fire brigade came to rescue her. She had lost hope as she her husband lay beside her dead. The man kept motivating her that there is more to life.

Then there were people who rummaged inside the debris just to find some cash or jewellery. People took whatever valuable items they could find.

A friend of mine lost her right hand when a whole part of the lift fell upon her. She was rescued 24 hours later.

Families who were lucky to have survived helped others selflessly. Food was shared among those who were waiting for their loved one to make it out of the debris alive.

A pregnant woman in our locality had twins on that day. She named them ‘Erica’ and ‘Eric’. They must be 16 year olds now.

There were stories about people who escaped death by few seconds. A man was pushed and forced by his wife to go and wash his car. The moment he stepped out of his house he felt the earthquake and his whole building was razed to the ground.

Many pet owners shared their experiences of how they were warned by the pets to come out of their houses even before the earthquake happened.

An NRI couple in our neighbourhood had come to India after 20 years. They died while their relatives survived.

All the public gardens and parks were packed with people at night who were to too scared to go back in their houses to sleep.

My whole family slept in the hall, close to the main door with an emergency bag ready. It contained 2 pairs of clothes each, cash and jewellery that we had and all the documents needed.

We were the lucky ones who had not lost anything or anyone. I can't describe how it changed me. I had a new found respect for my life. At just 13 I realised I was lucky enough to be alive. And also for the fact that I still had my family with me. Family is everything.
