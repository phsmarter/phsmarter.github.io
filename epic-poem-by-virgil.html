7. summary of the epic poem the beowulf


In the poem, Beowulf, a warrior from Geats comes to the rescue of Hrothgar, the king of the Danes whose land is being attacked by a monster, Grendel. Beowulf succeeds and defeats Grendel. To show his prowess, he even kills Grendel with his bare hands. However, the tension of the story escalates, because Grendel's mother is now on the warpath to get revenge. As the story progresses, Beowulf kills Grendel's mother in her liar with a sword that giants of a past time once used.

After this victory, Beowulf returns home to Geats. Fifty years pass. However, there is one final battle. There is a dragon that plagues his people because of stolen treasure. More importantly, Beowulf now has to defend his people. He fails at first, but then with the help of Wiglaf, a servant, chases the dragon into its lair and kills it. However, in the process, Beowulf receives a fatal wound and dies. Finally, he is buried by the sea.


8. What is the difference between lyric poem and epic poem?


A lyric poem and an epic poem can be said to be the two most common types of poems. There are a number of differences between the two.  Here is a couple:

 

First of all, the purpose why it was written – the lyric poetry is focused on how the writer/speaker feels on a particular topic. An epic, on the other hand, is narrative and involves historical elements.

 

Second, the length of each poem. Epic poem is longer than the lyric poem.  

Third, the point of view. The usual speaker of a lyric is the speaker itself. In epics, these are oftenly written from a third person’s point of view.

20. what is an epic poem?


An epic poem is a long, narrative poem that is usually about heroic deeds and events that are significant to the culture of the poet. Many ancient writers used epic poetry to tell tales of intense adventures and heroic feats. Some of the most famous literary masterpieces in the world were written in the form of epic poetry.

Epic poems were particularly common in the ancient world because they were ideal for expressing stories orally. These works continue to be well regarded today. Many high school students read famous examples of epic poetry, such as Gilgamesh and The Iliad.

An epic poem is a long, serious, poetic narrative about a significant event, often featuring a hero. Before, epic poems were memorized and played an important part in maintaining a record of the great deeds and history of a culture.

Epics deal with legendary or historical events of national or universal significance. Epic poems are not merely entertaining stories of legendary or historical heroes; they summarize and express the nature or ideals of an entire nation at a significant or crucial period of its history.

22. similarities of epic and lyric poem


Answer:There are similarities between epic poetry and lyric poetry.

The main difference between epic poetry and lyric poetry is length. Epic poetry is typically lengthy and tells a story. Thus epic poetry will have characters and a plot. A lyric poem can be very short and may not tell a story but simply communicate an idea or feeling. For instance, haiku.

The similarites include such things as:

(1) The quality of writing. The best epic poems and the best lyric poems are writings of very high quality. For instance, “Paradise Lost” by John Milton is an epic poem that features marvelous writing. Milton’s best sonnets are lyric poems of similar quality.

(2) Meter or cadence. “Paradise Lost” was written in blank verse, or unrhymed iambic pentamenter. Milton’s sonnets were written in rhymed iambic pentamenter. And there have been epic poems that feature rhyme, such as Dante’s and Edmund Spenser’s most famous works. Granted, not all epic and lyric poems employe meter or cadance, but many of the most famous poems in both categories do.

(3) Poetic devices such as imagery, metaphor, humor, irony, alliteration, assonance, etc.

(4) Something beyond ordinary writing. The best writers of epic poetry and lyric poetry take us beyond ordinary writing. I would liken it to listening to the best music of Mozart or Prince. It’s hard to define what is so much better than ordinary music, but we recognize it when we hear it. The best poetry of both genres can elevate the heart, the mind, the soul.

Answer:

The sole similarity is that they are both poetry as opposed to prose. That is to say, that they both use highly artificial, stylized language rather than more natural language.

Prose is an attempt to artistically render language in a naturalistic, realistic way. Think of a novel. It’s art, but it’s an attempt to create a naturalistic art- the illusion of a real world. Prose is like photography.

Poetry is doing something very different. It isn’t trying to imitate natural speech, it’s an effort to artistically better it. This is what makes poetry so hard to define. It doesn’t always rhyme, it doesn’t always have rhythm. What it always does achieve is a stylized artifice of language. Poetry is like painting.

Give me a short summary of the epic poem Beowulf?

print Print document PDF list Cite

Quick Answer

The Old English epic poem Beowulf tells the story of a young Geatish warrior who comes to the aid of Hrothgar, the King of the Danes, whose kingdom is being terrorized by a monster named Grendel. Beowulf uses his epic strength and bravery to slay Grendel in Hrothgar’s mead hall, Heorot, and then to slay Grendel’s vengeful mother in her underwater lair. Beowulf’s fame spreads, and he returns home to Geatland laden with treasure for his king, Hygelac. Beowulf later becomes the king of the Geats and rules for a peaceful fifty years. When a dragon begins to pose a threat to Geatland, Beowulf and his servant Wiglaf set off to defeat it. Beowulf succeeds in slaying the dragon, but dies in the process.
