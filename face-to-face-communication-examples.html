The channel refers to the means by which the message is conveyed from the source to the receiver. In face-to-face communication, the channel is direct, as the source can use their intellect, sensory skills, and motor skills to encode and transmit their message. For example, a speaker might use gestures, facial expressions, and vocal inflections to convey their message to a listener.

The receiver is the person for whom the message is intended. In the context of teaching and learning, the student is considered the receiver of the message. The receiver must be able to decode the message and understand its intended meaning. This requires active engagement on the part of the receiver, as they must use their own intellect and knowledge to interpret the message.
