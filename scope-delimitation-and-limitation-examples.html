2. Scope of the study,Delimitations,Limitation,Scope Where?,Who?,Size?,Topic Limits?,Delimitation You will just explain this​


Scope of the study refers to the breadth and depth of the research topic that a researcher has chosen to explore. It defines the extent to which the researcher intends to investigate a particular phenomenon or subject matter. It determines the boundaries of the research and sets the direction for the study.

Delimitations are the specific factors or parameters that the researcher sets in advance in order to narrow the scope of the study. These are constraints that are put in place to define the boundaries of the research, and to ensure that the study remains focused and manageable. Delimitations can be determined by a variety of factors, such as time, location, sample size, or methodology.

Limitations, on the other hand, refer to the external factors that may affect the research outcomes, and are beyond the control of the researcher. These can include factors such as lack of resources, access to data, or external events that may impact the research.

The scope of a study can be defined by answering the following questions:

Where: What geographical or spatial boundaries will the study cover?

Who: What specific population or group will be included in the study?

Size: What will be the sample size of the study?

Topic Limits: What specific aspect or aspects of the topic will be explored?

Delimitation: What specific factors or parameters will be set in advance to narrow the scope of the study?

Delimitations and limitations are important to consider when conducting research, as they help to ensure that the study remains focused and feasible, and that the results are reliable and valid. By being clear about the scope, delimitations, and limitations of the study, the researcher can better communicate the significance and relevance of their research to the broader academic community.

Sure! I can explain the concepts of scope of study, delimitations, limitations, and scope in the context of the title "effectiveness of the ABM strand in developing entrepreneurship skills."

Scope of Study:

The scope of the study refers to the boundaries of the research or investigation. It defines the extent to which the research will cover the topic or subject matter. In this case, the scope of the study is limited to the effectiveness of the ABM strand in developing entrepreneurship skills.

Delimitations:

Delimitations are the parameters or constraints that the researcher imposes on the study. It sets the boundaries of the research by specifying what the study will include and exclude. For instance, delimitations for this study could be limited to a specific geographical location, certain age group, specific time frame, or specific ABM curriculum.

Limitations:

Limitations refer to the potential constraints or barriers that may affect the study's validity and reliability. These can be practical, theoretical, or methodological in nature. Some examples of limitations in this study could be the availability of data, access to participants, the reliability of measures, or biases in the data collection.

Scope Where, Who, Size, and Topic Limits:

The scope can be further defined by the following factors:

Scope Where: The study may focus on a specific location, region, or country where the ABM strand is implemented.

Scope Who: The study may target a specific group of individuals who have undergone the ABM strand, such as high school graduates, college students, or professionals.

Scope Size: The study may be limited to a specific sample size, such as a certain number of participants or a particular percentage of the population.

Topic Limits: The study may be limited to certain aspects of the ABM strand's effectiveness in developing entrepreneurship skills, such as its impact on financial literacy, decision-making, risk-taking, or innovation.

Overall, the scope of the study, delimitations, limitations, and scope where, who, size, and topic limits will depend on the research objectives and questions.

Scope:

The scope details how in-depth your study is to explore the research question and the parameters in which it will operate in relation to the population and timeframe.

Guidelines on How to Write a Scope -

What – the subject to be investigated, and the included variables.Where – the location or setting of the study, i.e. where the data will be gathered and to which entity the data will belong.When – the timeframe within which the data is to be collected.Who – the subject matter of the study and the population from which they will be selected. This population needs to be large enough to be able to make generalisations.How – how the research is to be conducted, including a description of the research design (e.g. whether it is experimental research, qualitative research or a case study), methodology, research tools and analysis techniques.
