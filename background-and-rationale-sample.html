10. "Influence of Family Background and Support on Students participation and success in Stem Academic Strand"Patulong po pagawa ng Rationale of the Study​


Answer:

Family background and support has a significant influence on students' participation and success in STEM (Science, Technology, Engineering, and Mathematics) academic strands. Parents or guardians play a vital role in instilling confidence and motivation in their children, which can be the difference between success and failure in STEM fields. Students from a disadvantaged background may lack the resources to access resources related to STEM courses, such as tutors and educational materials, thereby limiting their progress. Furthermore, some students may have family or cultural values that discourage their STEM participation or limit their access to STEM education.

On the other hand, supportive family environments can give students the confidence and support needed to achieve success in the STEM fields. Supportive family members can provide access to resources such as tutors, books, and other educational materials. Additionally, they may provide emotional and psychological support, which is vital to students who can become overwhelmed by the difficulty of the work in STEM studies. Furthermore, family members may provide advice and guidance through difficult academic periods and provide support during failure, both of which are essential for students to experience successful STEM learning outcomes.

Ultimately, family background and support can significantly influence students' participation and success in STEM academic strands. Disadvantaged students may lack the resources necessary to succeed while students with greater support may experience better outcomes. Therefore, it is essential that individuals, schools, and organizations work together to ensure that students from all backgrounds have equal access to resources which may facilitate better STEM learning outcomes.

Research Title: The Impact of Social Media on Mental Health: A Study on Young Adults

Main Topic: The relationship between social media use and mental health outcomes in young adults.

Central Question: What is the effect of social media use on the mental health of young adults?

Corollary Question: How do different types of social media use (e.g. passive scrolling, active posting) affect mental health outcomes?

Participants: Young adults aged 18-25 who regularly use social media platforms.

Background of the topic: Social media has become an integral part of daily life for many young adults, with research suggesting that they spend an average of 2-3 hours per day on social media platforms. While social media can provide benefits such as increased social connectedness and access to information, there are also concerns about its impact on mental health. Studies have found associations between social media use and mental health problems such as depression, anxiety, and poor sleep quality.

Rationale: The topic was chosen because of the growing concern about the effects of social media on adolescent mental health. While previous studies have explored this topic, there is still much to learn about the specific mechanisms by which social media use affects mental health outcomes. By conducting this study, we hope to contribute to a better understanding of the impact of social media on adolescent mental health and inform interventions and policies aimed at promoting healthy social media use among youth.


21. what is rationale and background of drinking alcohol​


Answer:

The rationale and background of drinking alcohol.

According to a review article published in the Journal of Studies on Alcohol and Drugs by Goodwin, Gabrielli, Erickson D., & Robinson (2015), "Alcohol use, both moderate and excessive, has been a part of human culture since the beginning of recorded history." They argue that there are several reasons why people choose to drink alcohol, including social, religious, and cultural reasons.

Alcohol's ability to reduce anxiety and promote social interactions also makes it a popular choice at gatherings and social events. Additionally, many people believe that moderate alcohol consumption has health benefits, such as reducing the risk of heart disease and stroke.

However, excessive alcohol consumption can lead to negative consequences, such as impaired judgment, increased risk of accidents, addiction, and health problems. The authors also point out that cultural and societal norms and values play a significant role in the patterns of alcohol consumption worldwide.

Source: Goodwin, V. J., Gabrielli, J. L., Erickson, D. J., & Robinson, T. L. (2015). Drinking alcohol: The health and social risks. Journal of studies on alcohol and drugs, 76(4), 541-550. https://doi.org/10.15288/jsad.2015.76.541.

Answer:

Introduction:

Deforestation refers to the large-scale removal of trees from forests, primarily for the purpose of commercial use of forest resources such as timber, pulp, and fuelwood. This study aims to explore the causes, impacts, and potential solutions to the problem of deforestation.

Rationale of the Study:

Deforestation is a serious issue that has various environmental, economic, and social consequences. The clearing of forests not only contributes to the loss of biodiversity but also to the release of greenhouse gases into the atmosphere, leading to global climate change. Additionally, deforestation often negatively impacts local communities who depend on forests for their livelihoods. Understanding the causes and impacts of deforestation is crucial to develop effective strategies to address this problem.

Theoretical Background:

There are various theories that attempt to explain the causes and effects of deforestation. The political economy perspective argues that deforestation is a result of uneven power relations between actors, with powerful actors benefiting from resource extraction. The ecological perspective emphasizes the importance of forests in maintaining ecological balance and biodiversity. The social justice perspective highlights the effects of deforestation on marginalized communities.

Abstract:

Statement of the Problem:

Deforestation is a growing concern worldwide, with an estimated 18.7 million acres of forest lost annually. This study seeks to understand the underlying causes and consequences of deforestation and explore potential solutions to mitigate its negative impacts.

Significance of the Study:

The study of deforestation is important due to its far-reaching impacts on the environment, economy, and social welfare. By examining the root causes and effects of deforestation, this study is expected to inform policy-makers and stakeholders on effective strategies to address this critical issue.

Research Methodology:

Research Design:

This study will employ a mixed-methods research design, using both qualitative and quantitative data. The research will collect data through literature review, interviews, and surveys. Data analysis will include both content analysis and statistical analysis.

Definition of Terms:

1. Deforestation: the large-scale removal of trees from forests for commercial purposes.

2. Biodiversity: the variety of plant and animal species in a given habitat.

3. Greenhouse gases: gases that trap heat in the Earth's atmosphere, leading to global warming and climate change.

4. Marginalized communities: groups of people who are socially, politically, and economically disadvantaged.
