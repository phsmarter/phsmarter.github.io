Scenario #2: Minchin is interested in buying a new smartphone since it will be needed for his online classes. She goes to the All Phone Store and inquires about the latest A Phone series. Minchin finds out that All Phone Store does not sell A Phone series.​


Explanation:

tatement and what makes it biased in Scenario #1:

Statement: "Vince Disel is a lousy actor who just paraded his muscled body."

What makes it biased: The statement is biased because the movie critic, Cameron Panis Jr., has a personal grudge against Vince Disel that has nothing to do with the quality of his acting. Cameron's negative comments about Vince Disel's acting are not based on the performance of the actor in the movie but on his personal bias. The bias affects the objectivity and fairness of the movie review.

The bias in Scenario #2 comes from Mr. Barnie, the manager of the All Phone Store. He is discouraging Minchin from buying the A Phone series and instead keeps showing her different models of phones. This indicates that Mr. Barnie may have a financial interest in promoting the other phone models, which can make his opinions biased. Moreover, it is also biased because he did not provide Minchin with complete and accurate information. He should have informed her right away that they do not sell the A Phone series, instead of trying to sway her to other phone models. This lack of transparency can mislead the customer and make them feel like they are being taken advantage of.
