3. give an answer about the conceptual framework Process about online games?​


Answer:

The conceptual framework for online games involves the process of creating, developing, and distributing games over the internet. This process includes several key components, such as game design, programming, testing, marketing, and distribution.

The game design phase involves conceptualizing the game, defining its mechanics and aesthetics, and creating a storyboard or prototype. Once the design is finalized, the game development team can begin programming the game using appropriate coding languages and platforms.

Throughout the development process, the game is tested to ensure its functionality and quality. This includes testing for bugs, errors, and glitches that could affect the gameplay experience. Once the game is fully developed and tested, it is marketed to potential players through various channels, such as social media, gaming websites, and app stores.

Finally, the game is distributed to players through online platforms, such as Steam, Google Play, or Apple App Store. The distribution process involves making the game available for purchase or download, managing player accounts, and providing customer support.

Overall, the conceptual framework for online games involves a complex process that requires collaboration between designers, programmers, testers, marketers, and distributors to create and deliver engaging and high-quality game experiences to players.

The conceptual framework for studying the impact of using online applications during the pandemic on the academic performance of all General Academic Strand (GAS) students involves several key components:

Independent Variable: Use of Online Applications during Pandemic - This refers to the extent to which GAS students utilized online applications for academic purposes during the pandemic. It includes various online tools and platforms used for virtual classes, online assignments, assessments, and communication with teachers and peers.

Dependent Variable: Academic Performance of GAS Students - This refers to the overall academic performance of GAS students, including their grades, test scores, and other measures of academic achievement. It is the outcome or result that is being investigated to determine how it is affected by the use of online applications during the pandemic.

Mediating Factors: Access to Technology and Internet, Digital Literacy, and Motivation - These are factors that may influence the relationship between the use of online applications and academic performance. Access to technology and internet refers to the availability of devices and reliable internet connection for students to access online applications. Digital literacy pertains to the students' ability to effectively navigate and utilize online tools and platforms. Motivation refers to the students' level of engagement and motivation to learn in an online environment.

Moderating Factors: Socioeconomic Status, Home Environment, and School Support - These are factors that may affect how the use of online applications during the pandemic impacts academic performance differently for different groups of GAS students. Socioeconomic status refers to the economic and social background of students, which may influence their access to resources and support. Home environment includes the availability of conducive learning environment at home, parental support, and other home-related factors. School support pertains to the level of support provided by the school, such as online teaching strategies, resources, and support services.

The relationships among these components will be examined through empirical research, such as surveys, interviews, and data analysis, to understand the impact of using online applications during the pandemic on the academic performance of GAS students, and how various factors may influence this relationship. The conceptual framework serves as a guide for conducting the research and understanding the complex interactions and dynamics involved in this topic.

16. can anyone make an example of conceptual framework of the effect of parental absence in academic performance? ​


Answer:

Sure, here's an example of a conceptual framework for the effect of parental absence on academic performance:

Conceptual Framework:

Independent Variable:

Parental absence (measured by the length of time parents are absent from the home)

Dependent Variable:

Academic performance (measured by grades, test scores, and other academic indicators)

Mediating Variables:

1. Family structure (single-parent or dual-parent households)

2. Parent-child relationship (quality of relationship between child and absent parent)

3. Socioeconomic status (income, education, and occupation of parents)

Moderating Variables:

1. Age and gender of child

2. Length of parental absence (temporary or long-term)

3. Coping mechanisms used by child (e.g., seeking emotional support from others, engaging in extracurricular activities)

Hypothesis:

It is hypothesized that parental absence negatively affects academic performance among children, and this effect is mediated by family structure, parent-child relationship, and socioeconomic status. Furthermore, the negative effect of parental absence on academic performance is expected to be stronger among younger children, female children, and those experiencing long-term absence, but may be mitigated by effective coping mechanisms.

This conceptual framework provides a theoretical foundation for understanding the complex relationship between parental absence and academic performance, and can guide future research on this topic.

25. conceptual framework in research about the effects of face to face learning modality to the working students​


The conceptual framework for researching the effects of face to face learning modality on working students can be presented as follows:

1. Independent variable: Face to Face Learning Modality

- This refers to a traditional classroom setting where students attend classes physically, interact with instructors and fellow students face to face.

2. Dependent variable: Working Students

- This refers to students who are employed while pursuing their education.

3. Mediating Variables:

- Academic Performance: The academic progress and achievement of working students can be measured in order to determine the effectiveness of the face to face learning modality.

- Work Performance: The impact of the face to face learning modality on the work performance of the students can be assessed.

- Time Management: The impact of attending face to face classes on the time management skills of working students can be examined.

4. Moderating Variables:

- Age: The age of the working students can affect the effectiveness of the face to face learning modality.

- Work Schedule: The work schedule of the working students can affect their attendance and participation in the face to face classes.

- Academic Level: The academic level of the working students can affect the extent of dependence on the face to face learning modality.

5. Hypothesis:

- Face to face learning modality has a positive effect on the academic performance of working students.

- Face to face learning modality has a positive effect on the work performance of working students.

- Face to face learning modality improves the time management skills of working students.

6. Research methodology:

- The research can be conducted using surveys to collect data from working students regarding their academic performance, work performance, and time management skills.

- The research can also involve collecting data on the attendance rates of working students who attend face to face classes and the impact of attending these classes on their work scheduling.

- A statistical analysis of the collected data can be carried out to determine the impact of the face to face learning modality on working students.
