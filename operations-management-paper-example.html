Supply Chain Company's Success Supply chain management has a big impact on a company's success.

      Systems for managing the supply chain efficiently reduce costs, waste, and manufacturing cycle time. A just-in-time supply chain, where retail sales automatically transmit replenishment requests to manufacturers, has become the industry standard. Then, retail shelves can be refilled virtually as soon as merchandise is sold. Analyzing the data from supply chain partners to determine where more changes can be made is one technique to further improve this process.

 There are three situations in which efficient supply chain management adds value to the supply chain cycle by analyzing partner data:

Recognizing potential issues. The buyer may complain of poor service if they order more stuff than the manufacturer can provide. Manufacturers could be able to foresee the shortfall through data analysis before the customer is dissatisfied.Dynamic pricing optimization Products that are in season have a short shelf life. These goods are often discarded or sold at steep discounts towards the end of the season. Prices are frequently dynamically adjusted to suit demand for perishable "products" by airlines, hotels, and other businesses. Similar forecasting methods can increase margins even for hard items by applying analytical software.Enhancing the distribution of merchandise that is "available to promise." Utilizing analytical software tools, it is possible to dynamically schedule tasks and distribute resources based on the sales estimate, actual orders, and promised raw material deliveries. When an order is placed, manufacturers can confirm the product delivery date, thereby lowering the number of orders that are incomplete.

Good supply chain management regarding purchasing as a supply chain.

    In business activities, including hospitality, purchasing operations are certainly very important to support business activities. To be able to implement a good and profitable purchasing system, the hotel must create a good supply chain. However, in practice, hotels may also experience difficulties due to a lack of control over the producers and sellers involved as suppliers in the supply chain.

    In this case, there are several things that can be done by the hotel to overcome and prevent losses that may be experienced by the actions of producers and/or sellers.

1. Planning process.

At this stage, the hotel can detail the quality and general specifications that must be met by the producers and/or seller, this is to prevent unwanted things as mentioned in the problem, where salespeople increase personal interests and detriment the hotel.

2. Purchasing process.

At this stage, the hotel can choose the producer and/or seller that is most profitable for the hotel. Hotel management can also prepare a formal agreement that must be followed by the seller to prevent losses on one side and maintain the quality of supply received by the hotel.

    If the hotel has gotten a loss due to detrimental things done by the producer and/or seller, then the hotel can take control again and make the above policies to restore an effective supply chain.
