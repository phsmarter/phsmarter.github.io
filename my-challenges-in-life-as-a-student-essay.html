4. Make an Essay (minimum of 200 words) Title: MY LIFE AS A STUDENT DURING PANDEMIC​


The effects of the COVID-19 panic has caused a massive changes to our daily lives, changes that have often happened rapidly and abruptly. Life adjustments often come with a wide range of experiences and emotions. Sometimes that transition can feel smooth and other times that journey to the new normal is choppy or downright bumpy!

Some people may find themselves in survival mode, gathering information and resources necessary to function at school, work, as a person, and in our relationships with others. Others may be attempting to settle into their new routines. For some, this means trying to combat social isolation. For many, it means learning how to live (or re-live) with friends, family members, partners, or roommates, which is not always easy. I hear a lot of people struggle to find a distraction-free space at home that is conducive to do productive and effective work. For myself, I feel like it takes twice as much time, effort, and energy to do even simple tasks, which can be quite draining. I’ve really had to be very compassionate and patient with myself lately. Others may be struggling with the fatigue, frustration, sadness, anger, grief, and anxiety (among other things) of adjusting. Some people (and I hope there are a lot of you out there), have gone through the adjustment process and are finding comfort in your new norm. In all of this, here are a few important things to remember.

This situation is both uncertain and temporary! It’s okay not to feel okay. It’s also okay (and encouraged) to seek help and support!
Consider this a time to reflect on who you want to be during this time.
Focus on what is within your control.
It’s good to be informed, and it’s a really good idea to take breaks from conversations, news, and information related to COVID-19.
Adjustment is a process that looks differently for most people. This process is not liner or well-defined. Patience and flexibility are really important.
Please be respectful of where you are in the process (some self-compassion can go a long way) and also of where others are.
It’s okay to be curious about where your friends, family, and others are in their process and to seek to understand their wants and needs at this time. Don’t forget to tell them what you want and need too!
Setting a routine can be really helpful! In addition to doing your schoolwork make sure to schedule time for your physical and emotional health, fun, creatively, social connection, and stress relief!
It’s okay to set boundaries, even with family members, friends, and professors.
I hope that when reading this you are beginning to see that you aren’t alone in the adjustment process. My wish for you is that you have found some peace in the chaos, and learned something about yourself and the world

14. - ESSAY (STUDENTS' CHALLENGES IN THE NEW NORMAL CLASSROOM) ​


Explanation:

The new normal classroom has brought about a lot of changes in the way students learn. With the introduction of online learning, students are now faced with a variety of challenges that they must overcome in order to succeed. These challenges include adapting to the new technology, managing their time effectively, and staying motivated.

Adapting to the new technology is one of the biggest challenges that students face in the new normal classroom. Many students are not used to using computers or other digital devices for learning and may find it difficult to adjust. Additionally, they may not be familiar with the different software programs and applications that are used for online learning. This can lead to frustration and confusion as they try to figure out how to use these tools effectively.

Time management is another challenge that students face in the new normal classroom. With online learning, there is no set schedule or structure for classes, which can make it difficult for students to stay on track with their studies. They must be able to manage their time wisely in order to complete assignments on time and keep up with their studies. Additionally, they must be able to balance their school work with other activities such as extracurriculars or part-time jobs.

Finally, staying motivated is a challenge that many students face when transitioning from traditional classrooms to online learning environments. Without the physical presence of teachers and classmates, it can be difficult for some students to stay focused and engaged in their studies. Additionally, without regular feedback from teachers or peers, it can be hard for some students to stay motivated and continue working towards their goals.

Overall, there are many challenges that students face when transitioning from traditional classrooms to online learning environments in the new normal classroom. However, by understanding these challenges and taking steps such as adapting quickly to new technology, managing their time wisely, and staying motivated, students can overcome these obstacles and succeed in this new environment.

Secondly, students may face distractions while studying at home, such as noise from family members or the temptation to use their devices for entertainment purposes. 24. Write a story or an essay about you and how you deal with challenges in life as a students​


Answer:

As a student, I have faced many challenges throughout my academic journey. From struggling with difficult coursework to dealing with personal issues, I have learned that life as a student can be challenging at times. However, I have also learned that it is possible to overcome these challenges and come out stronger for it.

One of the biggest challenges I have faced as a student was when I was taking a particularly difficult math class. The material was complex and I found myself struggling to understand the concepts. I spent countless hours studying and trying to grasp the material, but nothing seemed to click. I felt defeated and was ready to give up.

But I didn't give up, instead I sought help. I went to my professor's office hours and asked for extra help. I also formed a study group with my classmates and we helped each other to understand the material. I also found a tutor who helped me to work through the problems in a way that made sense to me.

Through hard work and determination, I was able to improve my understanding of the material and even ended up getting a good grade in the class. That experience taught me that it's okay to struggle and ask for help when you need it.

Another challenge I faced as a student was dealing with personal issues. I was going through a difficult time in my life and found it hard to focus on my studies. I was constantly feeling down and unmotivated. But I realized that I needed to take care of myself in order to be able to succeed as a student. I sought support from friends and family, and I also made an effort to take care of my mental and physical health.

Through it all, I learned that challenges are a part of life, and that it is how we deal with them that makes the difference. I learned that I can overcome any obstacle if I am willing to put in the work and ask for help when I need it.

In conclusion, as a student, I have faced many challenges, but I have also learned valuable lessons that have helped me to become a stronger and more resilient person. I know that there will be more challenges in the future, but I am confident that I have the tools to overcome them and succeed in my academic journey.
