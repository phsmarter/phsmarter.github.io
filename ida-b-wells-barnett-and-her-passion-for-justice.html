1. Summary of Ida b. Wells Barnett and Her Passion for Justice


Si Ida Bell Wells-Barnett, mas kilala bilang si Ida B. Wells, ay may dalawang lahi- Aprikano at Amerikano. Isa siyang journalist, guro at lider ng kilusan ukol sa karapatang pantao. Isa din siya sa nagtatag sa National Association for the Advancement of Colored People (NAACP). Naging bantog si Wells bilang isang Itim sa Amerika dahil ang kaniyang buhay ay nakasentro sa pakikipaglaban sa diskriminasyon dahil sa kulay at karahasan sa mga babae.

Ang pamilya ni Wells ay naging alipin at nakalaya lamang dahil sa Emancipation Proclamation noong American Civil War. Nang siya ay naging guro at nagkaraoon ng kasosyo sa publishing industry, isinulong na niya ang kaniyang adhikain. Noong taong 1890, binusisi niya sa publiko ang paghamak sa mga Itim at pagbibigay ng pabor sa mga Puti. Kasama na dito ang pag-aalis ng kalayaan ng Itim sa politika.

-Wells-Barnett's work challenged existing power structures by speaking out against lynching and segregation and by advocating for the rights of women and people of color.

-Wells-Barnett used various strategies to promote her causes, including investigative journalism, public speaking, and organizing boycotts and protests.

-Wells-Barnett faced many obstacles in her efforts to bring about social change, including threats of violence, blacklisting, and lack of support from mainstream organizations.

-Wells-Barnett's involvement with the NAACP helped to shape her activism by providing her with a platform to advocate for civil rights and by connecting her with other activists.

-Race and gender played significant roles in Wells-Barnett's activism, as she sought to address the intersecting forms of oppression faced by women and people of color.
