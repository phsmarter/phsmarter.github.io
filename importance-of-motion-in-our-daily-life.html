Motion

Motion is a very basic phenomenon of daily life. It can be defined as the change in location relative to surroundings over a period of time.

Motion is of various types:

Translational motion :Type of motion in which object moves along a path in any of dimensions. Example: man walking.

Rotational Motion: Type of motion in which an object moves along circular path about fixed axis. Example: Rotation of earth about its axis.

Linear Motion: Type of translational motion when body moves in unique direction along one dimension. Example: a train moving along straight path.

Periodic motion: Type of motion that repeats after regular intervals. Example: vibrating tuning fork.

Simple Harmonic Motion: Type of motion in which restoring force acts in direction opposite to the motion of the object. Example: Pendulum.

Projectile motion: This motion has horizontal as well as vertical displacement. Example: Javelin Throw.

Oscillatory motion: This motion is repetitive in nature within a time frame. Example: vibrating strings.

So, motion is applied in every aspect of life . There are infinite examples illustrating this .

26. Why is motion of object important? Cite intances that shows the importance of motion in our daily life? give 5 examplesplssss​


Answer:

The motion of an object is important because it helps us understand and predict the position and behavior of objects in the physical world, and plays an important role in many aspects of our daily life.

Examples of the importance of motion in our daily life:

(1) Transportation: Motion is essential for transportation. Whether it's a car, bus, train, or plane, all forms of transportation rely on motion to get us from one place to another.

(2) Communication: The motion of electrons in a wire is what allows us to send and receive messages through communication devices like phones and computers.

(3) Sports: Many sports, such as running, jumping, throwing, and catching, involve the motion of objects and people. These motions help athletes compete and entertain audiences.

(4) Manufacturing: The motion of machines and tools is crucial in manufacturing processes, such as assembly lines. These motions help us produce goods efficiently and accurately.

(5) Natural Disasters: The motion of wind, water, and earth can cause natural disasters like hurricanes, tsunamis, and earthquakes. Studying the motion of these events helps us predict and prepare for them, potentially saving lives.
