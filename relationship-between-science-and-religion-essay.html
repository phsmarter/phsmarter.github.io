16. write a short essay expressing your choice between SCIENCE and RELIGION. Be able to cite reasons why you choose it.​


SCIENCE AND RELIGION:

The ways that science and religion approach comprehending the world are different. Religion relies on faith, belief in a higher power, and allegiance to a set of doctrines, whereas science is founded on evidence, experimentation, and a process of constant questioning concerning statements that has facts.

Both science and religion have advantages and drawbacks. We have made significant advancements in our understanding of the natural world, the creation of new technologies, and the improvement of our standard of living thanks to science. Contrarily, religion has given people a sense of direction, moral guidance, and solace in trying times.

In the end, a person's decision between science and religion is personal and is influenced by their views, values, and life experiences. While some people might discover that religion gives them a more profound sense of meaning and purpose, others might discover that science gives them a more precise and fact-based understanding of the world.

Traditionally, the connection between science and religion has been seen as one of conflict, particularly when it comes to the origins debate (creationism vs. evolution). History demonstrates that science and religion have more frequently been complementary to one another and have had a fluid connection.

I think it's important to acknowledge that science and religion aren't always incompatible. Many religious leaders accept scientific explanations for natural events, while many scientists believe in a higher power. It is possible to accept and value both perspectives and use them in conjunction to develop a more thorough understanding of the world we live in.

26. essay about the relationship of ethics and religion​


Answer:

The Relationship Between Religion and Ethics

To answer this question, we must first understand what both ethics and morality are. As ethics is defined as the philosophical study of morality, those who study religion get their moral precepts from what they believe God says should be done. This perspective is not at all unexpected, because all religions apply a perspective on morality. Morality is defined as beliefs concerning right and wrong, good and bad- beliefs that can include judgements, values, rules, principles, and theories. Morals are what help us guide our actions, define our values, and give us reason for being the person that we are.

According to the U.S. Census Bureau, in 2009, over 228 million Americans classified themselves as practicing some type of religion. Due to the fact that most believe that their religion is the basis of their morals and values, this number would then also represent the number of people in our country who associate religion and Ethics.

The relationship between religion and ethics can be chronicled as how religion relates to the use of experience and critical reasoning to study morality. Many of those who practice religion believe that there is no need to understand why their religion believes in particular morals because all they need to know is what God says is morally correct. It doesn’t matter to them the reasoning behind why certain things are morally good, while other things are bad. This rejection of critical reasoning, however may prevent those who feel this way from truly understanding all the particulars of their own religion.
