2. argumental essay on social media


Answer:

Social media has become increasingly popular in recent years, with more and more people using it to connect and share information. While it can be a useful tool for connecting with friends and family, it can also have a negative impact on users. This argumentative essay will discuss the advantages and disadvantages of social media.

One of the main advantages of social media is that it allows users to stay connected with their friends and family. It can be used to share important news and events, or simply to stay in touch with loved ones. Additionally, it allows users to keep up with current events and trends. This can be especially useful for those who do not have access to traditional media sources.

However, there are some disadvantages to using social media. One of the biggest concerns is the potential for cyberbullying and other forms of online harassment. Social media can be a breeding ground for negative comments and behavior, which can be damaging to users’ mental health. Additionally, there is the potential for data breaches and privacy violations, as social media platforms have access to personal information.

In conclusion, while social media can be a useful tool for staying connected and informed, it can also have a negative impact on users. It is important to be aware of the potential risks and to use the platform responsibly. By being mindful of the potential pitfalls, users can take steps to ensure that they are using social media in a safe and responsible manner.

In the past decade, social media has taken the world by storm. With billions of people using platforms such as F/cebook, Tw/tter, and Inst/gram, it’s no wonder that social media has become one of the most popular topics of debate.

On one side of the argument, there are those who believe that social media is a force for good, connecting people from all over the world and giving them a voice. On the other side, there are those who believe that social media is a destructive force, causing people to become addicted and leading to a rise in cyberbullying.

So, what’s the truth? Is social media a force for good or bad?

There are pros and cons to social media, and it’s important to consider both sides of the argument before coming to a conclusion.

On the plus side, social media can be used to connect with friends and family, share important news and events, and build relationships. It can also be a great way to discover new things, meet new people and learn about different cultures.

On the downside, social media can be addictive, leading to people spending hours scrolling through their feeds. It can also be a breeding ground for cyberbullying, with anonymous users hiding behind their screens to say hurtful things.

At the end of the day, it’s up to each individual to decide whether social media is a force for good or bad in their life. If used in moderation and with caution, social media can be a great way to stay connected with the people and the world around you.

13. The social media do more harm or good argumentative essay​


Answer:

Social media has revolutionized the way we communicate, share information and connect with others. While some argue that social media has brought about a positive change in the world, others believe that it has done more harm than good. In this essay, I will argue that social media has both positive and negative effects, but ultimately the negative impact outweighs the positive.

One of the most significant negative effects of social media is the spread of misinformation and fake news. With the rise of social media, it has become easier for anyone to share information, regardless of its accuracy or truthfulness. This has led to the spread of conspiracy theories, propaganda and rumors, which can have serious consequences. Misinformation on social media has been linked to everything from the spread of diseases to political instability and social unrest.

Another negative effect of social media is the impact it can have on mental health. Social media has been linked to increased rates of depression, anxiety, and other mental health issues. This is partly due to the pressure to present a perfect image online, which can lead to feelings of inadequacy and low self-esteem. Social media can also be addictive, leading to a sense of social isolation and a lack of real-world connections.

Additionally, social media has been linked to cyberbullying, harassment and the spread of hate speech. Social media provides a platform for people to express their opinions and beliefs, but it can also be used to spread harmful and hurtful messages. Cyberbullying and harassment can have serious consequences on mental health and even lead to suicide in extreme cases.

On the other hand, some argue that social media has brought about positive change in the world. Social media has been used to raise awareness about important issues, connect people with similar interests and to mobilize communities for positive change. It has also provided a platform for marginalized communities to share their stories and advocate for their rights.

In conclusion, while social media has brought about some positive changes, its negative impact outweighs the positive. Misinformation and the spread of fake news, negative impact on mental health, cyberbullying, harassment, and the spread of hate speech are all significant negative effects of social media. It is important for individuals to use social media responsibly and for policymakers to take measures to combat the negative effects of social media.

23. Argument about Social Media can affect to teenager's 7-9 sentences


Answer:

Social media has become an integral part of many teenagers' lives. Many spend hours a day scrolling through various platforms. While social media can be a source of entertainment and connection, it can also have negative effects on teenagers. One way it can affect them is by influencing their mental health. Studies have shown that heavy social media use is linked to an increased risk of anxiety, depression, and other mental health issues. The constant stream of information and comparison to others' highlight reels can create unrealistic expectations and lead to feelings of inadequacy and low self-esteem. Additionally, social media can be a source of distraction and disrupt teenagers' ability to focus and be productive. It can also contribute to sleep problems, as the blue light emitted by screens can interfere with the production of melatonin. Finally, social media can expose teenagers to a wide range of dangers, such as cyberbullying, online predators, and inappropriate content. It is important for teenagers to be aware of these risks and take steps to protect themselves online. Overall, while social media can be a useful tool, it is important for teenagers to use it responsibly and in moderation to minimize its potential negative effects.

26. Argument essay about does social media fame impact one's life ​


Answer:

Social media, which represents the phenomenon of the modern era, has been proven to have a major influence on a person's way of life. Be that as it may, one of the main functions of social media alongside communication is making the private become public. People share events from their lives and personal photos; in other words, they become connected to others through sharing stories and images.

By considering the matter of ratings of social media that in fact are reflected in the phenomenon of "likes" and "comments," a person who sometimes has more "likes" and "comments" than other people accordingly becomes famous on social media. Apparently, fame on social media has a considerable influence on how a person perceives their status in real life as well.

The matter of fame has been proven to have an effect on people's lives since the existence of civil society. Usually, fame comes from many

areas of one's life, such as politics, social activity, public life in general, and the entertainment industry. It has become to be so that the entertainment industry is the one which provides the most considerable amount of people with fame. Other areas do also claim of making people famous, but what is more important is that in order to be famous a person must be public (Replogle). Fame influences the public status of a person, or in other words, the way other people perceive the person who is considered famous (Brim). Public perception, in turn, influences self perception of a person and raises a person's social status in the eyes of others, and in the eyes of that person as well.

By being called "social," the media have a noticeable effect on socializing. However, the real-life image comes to be entirely different. The impact of social media has also proven to depersonalize people in real life, and make them more estranged and alienated from one another. Be that as it may, social media and real-life events are connected because people post

things on social media that in fact occur in their lives. Nonetheless, the reality reflected on the screens of cell phones is the reality that comes to be distorted by the very same screen. This way, when a person who becomes famous on social media also happens to be arrogant because of it, it comes to be evident that fame and arrogance at the same time are fake, while also being built on false representation and perception. For this reason, one must claim that fame in social media does not always mean fame in real life. Moreover, fame in real life, even though it becomes fame on social media, always comes first, and this could not be considered as the one that influences a person's life in the first place.

All in all, it must be said that fame on social media influences a person's life significantly by changing the status in real life as well. Still, the fame on social media appears to be fake for most of the time, as it is perfected and distributed as the most efficient possible scenarios of reality. The second kind of fame that comes from real life, and is reflected in social media, could not be considered as fame on social media, which influences a person's life.
