15. what are the 8 steps in selection process for hiring employees? Explain Each.pahelp ​


Here are the 8 steps in the selection process for hiring employees:

Job analysis and description: Define the job requirements, duties, and responsibilities to determine the knowledge, skills, and abilities required for the role.

Recruitment: Advertise the job and attract potential candidates through various methods such as job boards, employee referrals, college recruiting, and social media.

Screening: Review resumes, cover letters, and applications to determine if the candidate meets the minimum qualifications for the role.

Interviews: Conduct in-person or virtual interviews with shortlisted candidates to evaluate their qualifications, skills, and fit for the role and company culture.

Assessment: Use objective methods such as aptitude tests, skills assessments, and work simulations to evaluate the candidate's suitability for the job.

Reference and background check: Verify the candidate's employment history, education, and criminal background.

Selection: Make a final decision on which candidate to offer the job based on the results of the selection process.

Onboarding: Integrate the new hire into the company by providing training, orientation, and support for a successful transition into their new role.

26. what is the process of selecting subjects for experiments


If we want to know if Billy performed better than Sally, or if boys scored higher than girls in our class, or even if Asian children receive higher grades in our school than Caucasian children, the selection of subjects is rather simple.  When we are testing the entire population of possible subjects, we are adequately assured that no subject bias has occurred.

A population refers to the entire pool of possible subjects.  In a classroom or other setting where the entire population is relatively small, testing all subjects may be simple.  However, if we are attempting to understand or gain knowledge related to a large population, such as all third grade children, all depressed adults, or all retail employees, gathering and testing everyone would be relatively impossible.  In this situation, we would need to gather a sample of the population, test this sample, and then make inferences aimed at the entire population of which they represent.
