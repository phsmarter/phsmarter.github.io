2. Essay importance of electromagnetic waves​


Essay importance of electromagnetic waves

Electromagnetic waves or EM waves are waves that are created as a result of vibrations between an electric field and a magnetic field. In other words, EM waves are composed of oscillating magnetic and electric fields. The most important of these is visible light, which enables us to see. 15. essay about radio waves ​


Answer:

Scientifically, waves transfer energy through vibration from one area to another. Some types of waves need a medium in which to travel through; these waves are called mechanical waves. Other types of waves do not need a medium in which to travel through, they can travel in empty space; these waves are called electromagnetic waves. Mainly, there are two types of how the particles in the waves vibrate, they are called transverse waves and longitudinal waves. The particles in transverse waves vibrate in an up and down pattern. However, particles in longitudinal waves travel in the same direction the wave travels (back and forth movement).  The properties of waves are called amplitude, wavelength, and frequency. The amplitude is the measure of the distance of the wave from its normal rest position. The distance between any point on a wave and the same point on the next wave is called the wavelength. The number of waves produced in one second is called its frequency.

18. Create a short essay about the presence of wave around us.​


Explanation:

Waves are all around us, whether we are aware of them or not. They are an essential part of the natural world and play a vital role in many aspects of our daily lives. From the gentle ripple of water on a calm lake to the powerful sound waves produced by a thunderstorm, waves are all around us, shaping our world and providing us with valuable information about the environment we inhabit.

One of the most common types of waves that we encounter are electromagnetic waves. These are the waves that make up light, radio signals, and x-rays. They travel through the vacuum of space, allowing us to observe distant galaxies and communicate across vast distances. We use electromagnetic waves for a wide range of applications, from cooking food in our microwave ovens to transmitting data wirelessly through our phones and computers.

Another type of wave that is prevalent in our environment is the sound wave. We are surrounded by sound waves, from the chirping of birds to the hum of traffic on the street. We rely on sound waves for communication, music, and entertainment. Sound waves also provide us with important information about our surroundings, allowing us to locate objects and navigate our environment.

In addition to electromagnetic and sound waves, there are also mechanical waves, such as ocean waves and seismic waves. Ocean waves are caused by the wind and tides and shape our coastlines, while seismic waves are produced by earthquakes and can cause destruction and loss of life.

Overall, waves are an essential part of our world, providing us with information about our environment and enabling us to communicate and navigate through it. As we continue to explore and understand the properties and behavior of waves, we can harness their power and potential to improve our lives and the world around us.

24. Essay Can sound waves travel in other media like solids and liquid?​


Answer:

Sound travels more quickly through solids than through liquids and gases because the molecules of a solid are closer together and, therefore, can transmit the vibrations (energy) faster. Sound travels most slowly through gases because the molecules of a gas are farthest apart.
