Answer:

1. There are various ways to identify the economic status of one's family. Here are some common methods:

Income: One of the most common indicators of economic status is income. Income can be determined by looking at the amount of money that a family earns from various sources such as salaries, wages, bonuses, and investments.

Education: Education can also be a good indicator of economic status. Families with higher levels of education tend to have higher paying jobs and better access to resources.

Occupation: The occupation of the head of the household or the primary breadwinner can also give an indication of economic status. Certain occupations such as doctors, lawyers, and engineers tend to have higher incomes than others.

Assets: The assets that a family owns can also provide an indication of economic status. Assets may include real estate, vehicles, stocks, bonds, and other investments.

Living conditions: The living conditions of a family can also give an indication of economic status. Families living in larger houses or in expensive neighborhoods may have a higher economic status.

It is important to note that these indicators should not be used in isolation and should be considered together to get a more accurate picture of a family's economic status.

2. The relationship between wealth and happiness is complex and not straightforward. While it is true that having a certain level of financial security can alleviate some of the stresses and anxieties associated with poverty, it does not necessarily guarantee happiness.

Research has shown that once basic needs are met, such as food, shelter, and safety, additional wealth has diminishing returns in terms of happiness. This means that beyond a certain point, having more money does not necessarily lead to greater happiness. In fact, studies have shown that some extremely wealthy individuals experience higher levels of anxiety, stress, and depression than their less affluent counterparts.
