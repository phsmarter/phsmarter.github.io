Cyberbullying is a form of harassment that occurs online or through electronic devices such as smartphones, computers, and tablets. This type of bullying can have a profound negative impact on the lives of young people, and it is important to take steps to control it to prevent any further harm. While some may argue that cyberbullying is simply a part of modern life and that children should just toughen up, it is essential to acknowledge the severity of the issue and work to prevent it from happening in the first place.

One of the most significant concerns about cyberbullying is the impact it has on young people's mental health. Constant harassment and humiliation can lead to depression, anxiety, and other mental health problems. Cyberbullying can also lead to physical harm, including self-harm and suicide. The rise in cyberbullying-related incidents over the years is a clear indication that this is a serious issue that needs to be addressed.

Some may argue that cyberbullying is just a part of growing up, and children need to learn to deal with it. However, this is not a valid argument. Bullying of any kind should never be accepted, and we should work to prevent it from happening altogether. Children should not be subjected to the pain and suffering that comes with cyberbullying, and it is our responsibility to protect them.

The issue of cyberbullying can also have a ripple effect on the community. Children who are bullied may become bullies themselves, perpetuating the cycle of harassment and humiliation. This can create a negative environment, where children are afraid to speak out and seek help.

One way to prevent cyberbullying is to regulate online behavior. Parents, teachers, and other adults should monitor online activity and take action when necessary. Schools and organizations should also provide education on the dangers of cyberbullying and how to prevent it. We must create a culture where bullying of any kind is unacceptable, and there are severe consequences for those who engage in it.

In conclusion, cyberbullying is a serious issue that needs to be controlled to prevent a negative influence on youth. The impact of cyberbullying can be devastating, and it is our responsibility to protect our children from harm. While some may argue that cyberbullying is just a part of modern life, we must work to prevent it from happening and create a safe and positive environment for our youth. By taking action and regulating online behavior, we can make a difference and create a better future for our children.

6. What are the positive and negative influences of foreign films, series, or bands on Filipino youth? List 3 of each and support your answer.


Positive Influences

1. Cultural Awareness, helps to broaden their understanding and appreciation of the world.

2. Improved Language Skills, helps improve their language skills and familiarize themselves with different accents and expressions.

3. Increased Empathy, develop a greater understanding and empathy for people from diverse backgrounds, promoting social cohesion and cultural understanding.

Negative Influences

1. Distorted Image of Reality, can negatively impact the self-esteem and body image of Filipino youth.

2. Reduced Exposure to Local Culture, exposed to less of their own cultural heritage and traditions, leading to a loss of cultural identity.

3. Encouragement of Materialism, prioritize material success over other values, such as family, community, and personal growth.
