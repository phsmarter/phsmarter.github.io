CRASH COURSETYPES OF ESSAY

Argumentative:

An essay is a type of argumentative text where you have to express your personal opinion. It is an academic text which has no particular recipient (unlike the letter for example). It must include an introduction, a development in 2 or 3 parts and a conclusion.

Writing an argumentative paragraph means presenting your point of view, an opinion, a thesis and trying to convince the reader of their interest. → Each paragraph must contain an idea, developed by an argument, supported by an example which must clarify its meaning.

Informative:

Informational essay is a type of writing that provides information to educate readers on a topic. It presents valuable information focused on facts, details and evidence. At the same time, it should not be argumentative or convincing.

Persuasive:

A persuasive essay is a text that expresses a particular point of view based on an argument. Although similar to argumentative writing, the logic of this type of writing generally addresses the feelings of the reader instead of relying on concrete evidence.
