7. Paragraph on the Cause-Effect of Filipino Nationalism and Patriotism *for pt*​


Explanation:

Filipino Nationalism and Patriotism.

The cause of Filipino nationalism and patriotism can be traced back to the country's history of colonization and subjugation. For centuries, the Philippines was under the control of foreign powers, including Spain and the United States, which led to the suppression of the country's culture, traditions, and identity. The Filipinos' struggle for independence and self-determination sparked a strong sense of national pride and loyalty, which became the foundation of their nationalism and patriotism.

The effect of this nationalistic and patriotic sentiment is evident in various aspects of Filipino society. It has played a significant role in shaping the country's political, economic, and cultural landscape. Nationalism and patriotism have inspired Filipinos to fight for their rights and freedoms, leading to numerous revolutions and uprisings throughout the country's history. They have also driven Filipinos to work hard and excel in their respective fields, contributing to the country's development and progress.

In conclusion, Filipino nationalism and patriotism have been both a cause and effect of the country's struggle for independence and self-determination. They continue to shape the country's identity and inspire Filipinos to strive for a better future..

Filipino nationalism refers to the establishment and support of a political identity associated with the modern nation-state of the Philippines, leading to a wide-ranging campaign for political, social, and economic freedom in the Philippines. This gradually emerged out of various political and armed movements throughout most of the Spanish East Indies—but which has long been fragmented and inconsistent with contemporary definitions of such nationalism—as a consequence of more than three centuries of Spanish rule. These movements are characterized by the upsurge of anti-colonialist sentiments and ideals which peaked in the late 19th century led mostly by the ilustrado or landed, educated elites, whether peninsulares, insulares, or native (Indio). This served as the backbone of the first nationalist revolution in Asia, the Philippine Revolution of 1896.[1] The modern concept would later be fully actualized upon the inception of a Philippine state with its contemporary borders after being granted independence by the United States by the 1946 Treaty of Manila.

Feelings of patriotism are based on the positive values the country embraces—like freedom, justice, and equality. The patriot believes that both the system of government and the people of their country are inherently good and work together for a better quality of life.

In contrast, feelings of nationalism are based on a belief that one’s country is superior to all others. It also carries a connotation of distrust or disapproval of other countries, leading to the assumption that other countries are rivals. While patriots do not automatically denigrate other countries, nationalists do, sometimes to the point of calling for their country’s global dominance. Nationalism, through its protectionist beliefs, is the polar opposite of globalism.

16. Paragraph on the Cause-Effect of Filipino Nationalism and Patriotism *for my pt*​


Filipino nationalism and patriotism have been shaped by a complex interplay of historical, social, and cultural factors. One of the key factors that have contributed to the growth of Filipino nationalism and patriotism is the country's colonial past, particularly the Spanish and American colonial periods. During these periods, Filipinos were exposed to foreign cultures, languages, and ideas, which led to a growing sense of national identity and pride.

Another factor that has contributed to the growth of Filipino nationalism and patriotism is the country's struggle for independence and self-determination. The Philippine Revolution and the subsequent Philippine-American War were pivotal moments in Philippine history, as they marked the beginning of the country's fight for independence from foreign rule. The bravery and sacrifice of Filipino revolutionaries and patriots during these periods inspired generations of Filipinos to continue the struggle for national sovereignty and unity.

Moreover, Filipino nationalism and patriotism have been sustained and strengthened by various cultural and social movements, such as the promotion of the Filipino language and literature, the advocacy for social justice and human rights, and the celebration of Filipino traditions and customs. These movements have fostered a deeper appreciation and understanding of Filipino identity and heritage, and have inspired Filipinos to work towards a better future for themselves and their country.

In conclusion, the cause-effect relationship between Filipino nationalism and patriotism is a complex and multifaceted one. While historical, social, and cultural factors have played a significant role in shaping these values, it is ultimately the collective actions and aspirations of Filipinos that will determine the future of the country's national identity and pride.
