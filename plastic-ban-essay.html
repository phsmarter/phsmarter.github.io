3. write a argumentative essay of not less than 300 words about banning plastics in school​


The use of plastic in schools has been a hotly debated topic in recent years. While plastic products, such as water bottles and straws, are convenient for students and teachers, the environmental impact of plastic cannot be ignored. For this reason, I believe that schools should ban the use of plastic products.

The most obvious reason for banning plastic in schools is the environmental impact. Plastic is not biodegradable, which means that it does not break down naturally in the environment. Instead, it can take hundreds of years for plastic to break down into small pieces that can be harmful to wildlife and marine life. In fact, plastic pollution is one of the leading environmental issues facing our planet today. By banning plastic in schools, we can help to reduce the amount of plastic that ends up in landfills and in our oceans.

In addition to the environmental impact, banning plastic in schools can also have a positive impact on student health. Plastic products, such as water bottles and straws, can contain harmful chemicals such as BPA, which can be harmful to human health. By banning plastic, schools can ensure that students are not exposed to these harmful chemicals.

Another argument in favor of banning plastic in schools is that it can help to reduce costs. Plastic products are often more expensive than reusable or biodegradable alternatives. By banning plastic, schools can save money on the cost of plastic products and reduce the amount of waste that needs to be disposed of.

Finally, banning plastic in schools can serve as an educational opportunity for students. By banning plastic, schools can teach students about the importance of environmental conservation and the impact of plastic pollution on our planet. This can help to create a new generation of environmentally conscious citizens.

In conclusion, there are many reasons why schools should ban the use of plastic products. The environmental impact of plastic, the potential health risks, the cost savings, and the educational opportunities are all compelling reasons to make the switch to more sustainable alternatives. By taking action now, we can help to protect our planet for future generations.

Plastic bags are used in many everyday applications, from carrying home groceries to packing lunches, and have become an indispensable convenience for many Baybay City residents. Unfortunately, this convenience has also had some profound impacts on the environment due to plastic being non-biodegradable and clogging up drainage systems and waterways.
