Further explanation

Dilution is the process of adding solvents (generally water) that aims to obtain a solution with a concentration that is smaller than the initial concentration of a more concentrated solution

Can be formulated

C₁V₁=C₂V₂

C₁ the stock solution concentration

V₁ the volume of aliquoted from the stock solution.

C₂ the diluted solution concentration

V₂ the final volume of the diluted solution.

Given

C₁=0.1 N

V₁ = 50 ml

C₂=0.02 N

Required

Volume of water

Solution

Input the value :

V₂=(C₁V₁)/C₂

V₂=(0.1 x 50 ml)/0.02

V₂=250 ml

Volume of water needed :

= 250 ml - 50 ml

= 200 ml

Further explanation  

Solution properties are the properties of a solution that don't depend on the type of solute but only on the concentration of the solute.  

Solutions from volatile substances have a higher boiling point and lower freezing points than the solvent  

For freezing point can be formulated :  

ΔTf = Kf . m  

Kf = molal freezing point constant  

m = molal solution

Osmosis pressure is the minimum pressure given to the solution so that there is no osmotic displacement from a more dilute solution to a more concentrated solution.  

General formula:  

π = M x R x T  

π = osmosis pressure (atm)  

M = solution concentration (mol / l)  

R = constant = 0.08205 L atm mol-1 K-1  

T = Temperature (Kelvin)  

Given

5.0 g ethylene glycol

95 g water = 0.095 kg

Required

the

a. freezing point of the solution

b. osmotic pressure at 35°C

Solution

freezing point

= 5 g : 62 g/mol

= 0.081

= 0.081 : 0.095

= 0.853

Kf = 1.86 °C/m for water

Input the value :

ΔTf = Kf . m  

ΔTf = 1.86 x 0.853

ΔTf = 1.589

ΔTf = Tf solvent - Tf solution

Tf solvent = 0°C(water)

1.589 = 0 - Tf solution

Tf solution = -1.589 °C

9. The tetraethyl lead [Pb(C2H5)4] in a 25.00 mL sample of aviation gasoline was shaken with 15.00 mL of 0.02095 M I2. The reaction is Pb(C2H5)4 + I2 → Pb(C2H5)3I + C2H5I After the reaction was complete, the unused I2 was titrated with 6.09 mL of 0.03465 M Na2S2O3. Calculate the weight (in milligrams) of Pb(C2H5)4 (323.4 g/mol) in each liter of the gasoline.


The weight (in milligrams) of Pb (C₂H₅)₄ in each liter of the gasoline = 2700 mg/L

Further explanation

Iodometric titration is one of the titrations using a redox reaction iodine/iodide to determine the concentration of oxidation potential compounds smaller than the iodine-iodide system

In the above reaction there are two stages of reaction:

1. The reaction of TEL with iodine

Pb (C₂H₅)₄ + I₂ → Pb(C₂H₅)₃I + C₂H₅I

2. The excess iodine with thiosulfate reaction

I₂ + 2S₂O₃²⁻ → 2I⁻ + S₄O₆²⁻

Then to find the weight (in milligrams) of Pb (C₂H₅)₄ then we determine the calculation of the reaction stage 2 then proceed to the reaction stage 1

1. Reaction stage 2

I₂ + 2S₂O₃²⁻ → 2I⁻ + S₄O₆²⁻

The redox reaction coefficient above shows the ratio of mol I₂ to mol thiosulfate = 1: 2

mol thiosulfate = 6.09 mL of 0.03465 M = 0.211 mlmol

so that mole I₂ = 1/2 x 0.211 mlmol = 0.1055 mlmol

Reaction stage 1

Initial mole I₂ = 15.00 mL of 0.02095 M = 0.3142 mlmol

So that mole I₂ which reacts perfectly with Pb (C₂H₅)₄ = 0.3142 - 0.1055

= 0.2087 mlmol

Because the mole ratio of Pb (C₂H₅)₄ and I₂ in the reaction stage 1 = 1: 1, then mole Pb (C₂H₅)₄ = mole I₂ which reacts completely = 0.2087 mlmol = 0.0002087 mole

Then the mass of Pb (C₂H₅)₄

= mlmol x molar mass Pb (C₂H₅)₄

= 0,0002087 x 323.4

= 0.0675 g

= 67.5 mg

In 25 ml of the sample becomes:

[tex]\rm =\dfrac{67.5~mg}{0.025~L}\\\\=\boxed{\bold{2700~mg/L}}[/tex]
