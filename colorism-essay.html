14. 3 paragraph essay for skin color​


Explanation:

Skin color is a physical characteristic that can vary widely among individuals. It is determined by the amount of melanin, a pigment that is produced by specialized cells in the skin. People with more melanin tend to have darker skin, while those with less melanin have lighter skin. This variation in skin color is primarily due to evolutionary adaptations to different levels of ultraviolet radiation in different parts of the world.

Unfortunately, skin color has often been used as a basis for discrimination and prejudice throughout history. People with darker skin have been subject to unfair treatment and social exclusion, based on the erroneous belief that they are inferior to those with lighter skin. This type of discrimination, known as colorism, continues to be a problem today in many parts of the world.

It is important to recognize that skin color is just one physical characteristic among many, and it has no inherent value or meaning beyond its role in protecting the body from harmful UV radiation. It is not an indicator of intelligence, personality, or worth as a human being. By celebrating diversity and embracing differences in all their forms, we can create a more inclusive and accepting society where people of all skin colors are valued and respected.

18. Writing exercise on colors Directions: Using two primary colors (in any combination of yellow, red, and blue) write a personal essay in 250 words​


Seeing the world in color is something humans have been doing since the dawn of time. Colors have been used to distinguish the various aspects of our lives: the blue sky, the red flag and even our feelings of yellow happiness and red rage. As a child, I was fascinated by the various shades, hues and tints of yellow and red. I loved to play with my crayons, drawing whatever images my young imagination came up with, hues of yellow and red appearing unceasingly.

As I grew older, I began to attach deeper meanings to these two colors. Yellow became something of a cautious optimism, the hope that in spite of the unknowns, life can be filled with beautiful surprises. I chose to view this world in terms of yellow- a special reminder that the best moments in life are those to be cherished, reflecting the je ne sais quoi of this special hue.

Meanwhile, red has come to signify passion and courage for me. Red can be brave and fierce, uncomfortable yet its potency draws us in with its power. Whenever I face a challenge, I visualize myself donning a cape of red determination and strength before overcoming it. Red makes me feel alive.

In the end, the one thing connecting yellow and red is ambition. Together, they embody an energy, a noisy duo that drives me to reach the goals I’ve set out for myself. If yellow is the dream, then red is the reality, the tangibility that pushes me to act on what my heart desires.

The way I see the world may be different than others, but it’s these two colors that shape my experience. Together, they form a kind of power. By accessing yellow and red within me, I push my limits, daring to explore the boundaries of what’s possible and believing in the potential for a brighter future.
