17. 1.what is physical education?2. what Is the major goals of physical education ?3. what is the 4 objectives of physical education? Describe each.​


Answer:

1.) Physical education, often abbreviated to Phys Ed. or P.E., is a subject taught in schools around the world.

2.) The goal of physical education is to develop physically literate individuals who have the knowledge, skills and confidence to enjoy a lifetime of healthful physical activity.

3.) PHYSICAL DEVELOPMENT

Through the carefully selected physical an individual who participates actively will develop and maintain good health and a high level of physical fitness.

SOCIAL DEVELOPMENT

Participation in the physical education activities provides opportunities for the development of desirable social traits needed for adjustment to the social life in general.

EMOTIONAL DEVELOPMENT

The informal nature of physical education activity offers opportunities for self-expression and emotional mastery.

MENTAL DEVELOPMENT

Through participation in physical education activities the individual develops his mental capacities as he learns the mechanical principles underlying movement, as he acquired knowledge and understanding of rules and strategies of games and sports, and as he discovers ways of improving his movements in gymnastics and dance.

Answer:

The advantages of free education include increased access to education for individuals who may not have been able to afford it otherwise, as well as the potential for a more educated and skilled workforce. Additionally, free education can help to reduce the burden of student debt and improve social mobility.

However, there are also drawbacks to free education. One potential disadvantage is that it may lead to overcrowding in classrooms and a decrease in the quality of education. Additionally, free education may put a strain on public funds and could result in a decrease in funding for other public services.

In my opinion, free education in college does not necessarily decrease the quality of education. However, it is important for schools and universities to ensure that they have sufficient resources and support to provide high-quality education to all students.

Free education can benefit our society by increasing access to higher education and providing individuals with the skills and knowledge they need to succeed in the workforce. This can lead to a more educated and skilled workforce, which can in turn drive economic growth and development.

•Elementary education - it is typically the first stage of formal education coming after preschool and before •Secondary Education.

Secondary education - it is the final and second basic phase of the education which comes before tertiary education.

•Tertiary education - it is commonly referred to as post Secondary Education referred to academic persuits undertaken after High School.

•Vocational education - it is a kind of education that prepares people to work in various jobs, such as trade ,craft or as technician.

•Non formal education - non formal education is about acknowledging the importance of education, learning and training which takes place outside a recognised institution.

•Special education - it is a practice of educating students in a way that addresses their individual differences and needs.
