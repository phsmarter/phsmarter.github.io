1. is it possible for the world class organization to achieve superiority in all five major competitive priorities?​


Is it possible for the world class organization to achieve superiority in all five major competitive priorities?

Answer:

It is possible to improve on all five competitive Priorities. However, to achieve superiority on all five Major Competitive Priorities is not easy, as a firm has to trade one for another, to achieve superiority in another. In other words, a firm’s Operation Strategies often guide the firm towards a trade-off between the five Competitive Strategies. For example, Apple Inc is known for its superiority in terms of Delivery, Quality and Innovation. However, it lags behind in terms of Price and Flexibility in all its products. Taking another example :: Company –Dell Inc. Competitive Priorities Competitive Priority Applications Low Production Costs Tailored Manufacturing Reduced its production costs by cutting down on the number of vendors, thus warranting excellent bargains Pioneered the ‘Just in time’ production process. Delivery Performance On time delivery using online Purchasing system. Follows Make-to-order process, hence carries very low inventory. Customer Service and Flexibility Improved on both by providing online customization of products, and cutting down on the wait time for the customers. Quality Though it has been improving on the quality of its products in recent times, it still lags behind when compared to Sony or Apple. Innovation Scores very low on Innovation as its products are standardized across the table. To summarize, Dell as a firm scores high on 3 of the competitive strategies and is being competitive in the other two, it has not been able to perfect all five strategies, which is in line with its Operational.

(1) Maintain my own health and happiness. Because this is my top priority, first I schedule the things that most affect my happiness. I make time for sleep, exercise, creative work, personal growth, and my friends and family, and I say “no” to those activities—fun as they might be—that interfere with my sleep, exercise, and time with my closest friends and family. I simply say that I have a scheduling conflict.

In 2021, my goal is to learn everything about how the ways we work can be redesigned so that the work design itself—the structures and cultures organizations create—help people everywhere fulfill their potential for creativity, focus, productivity, purpose, meaning and joy.

(4) Coaching and speaking. These are the professional activities in my life that consistently remind me of my social value. I love creating coaching programs, like this one, and I love coaching teams and individuals. Every day that I coach I get feedback that I’m helping people become happier, healthier, and more productive.

(5) Give back. I challenge myself to do something every week, if not every day, to use my skills and passions to help someone else. Here is a prioritized list of the given words and the reasons for the order:

Save - This should be the first priority because it involves preserving the work that has already been done. Saving work ensures that any progress made is not lost and can be easily accessed in the future.

Work - This should be the second priority since the main purpose is to complete tasks and make progress. The work cannot be saved if it is not finished, so it is important to focus on completing tasks.

Borrow - This should be the third priority since borrowing involves taking something from someone else, which may not always be possible or feasible. It is important to try to complete work with the resources that are available before considering borrowing.

Other - This should be the last priority since it is a vague and general term. It is not clear what "other" refers to, so it is difficult to prioritize it. It is best to focus on the specific tasks at hand before addressing any other miscellaneous concerns.
