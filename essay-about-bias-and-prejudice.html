5. •Make and essay on what will you do to prevent bias and prejudice from happening.​


Answer:

Bias and prejudice are unfortunate realities in our world that can cause significant harm to individuals and communities. As a responsible and compassionate member of society, there are several things that I can do to prevent bias and prejudice from happening.

Firstly, I would make a conscious effort to educate myself about different cultures, lifestyles, and perspectives. This can be done by reading books, watching documentaries, attending workshops, and engaging in conversations with individuals from different backgrounds. By expanding my knowledge and understanding, I can reduce the likelihood of making assumptions or stereotyping people based on incomplete or inaccurate information.

Secondly, I would actively challenge bias and prejudice whenever I encounter it. This can be done by speaking out against discriminatory language or behavior, correcting misconceptions, and promoting empathy and understanding. Additionally, I would encourage others to do the same by modeling respectful and inclusive behavior and calling attention to instances of bias and prejudice that may be overlooked or ignored.

Thirdly, I would seek out opportunities to actively promote diversity and inclusion. This can be done by volunteering for organizations that work to combat prejudice and discrimination, attending cultural events, and supporting businesses and initiatives that promote diversity and inclusivity. By actively engaging with diverse communities and promoting their voices and perspectives, I can help to create a more equitable and just society.

Finally, I would be willing to reflect on my own biases and prejudices and take steps to address them. This can be done by engaging in self-reflection, seeking feedback from others, and being open to learning and growth. By acknowledging and addressing my own biases, I can become a more effective ally and advocate for diversity and inclusion.

In conclusion, preventing bias and prejudice from happening requires a commitment to education, advocacy, and self-reflection. By taking proactive steps to challenge bias and promote diversity and inclusion, we can create a more equitable and just society that respects and celebrates the diversity of all its members.

10. essay how will you stop bias and prejudice among youth?​


Answer:

Prejudice and bias are unfortunately still present in society today, and it is essential to address these issues with youth to promote a more inclusive and accepting future. There are several strategies that can be employed to help stop bias and prejudice among youth, including education, exposure, and community involvement.

Firstly, education is crucial in helping youth understand the harm that can come from bias and prejudice. Teachers, parents, and mentors can start by discussing the concept of prejudice and its negative effects on individuals and communities. Youth should be taught about the importance of valuing diversity and understanding different cultures and backgrounds. Schools can also offer programs that promote inclusion and diversity, such as multicultural clubs or events that celebrate different holidays and customs.

Secondly, exposure to diverse people and experiences can help youth challenge their preconceived notions and reduce bias. Youth should be given opportunities to interact with people from different backgrounds and cultures. This can be done through school exchange programs, community events, or volunteering in diverse communities. By interacting with people who are different from themselves, youth can learn about different perspectives and gain a deeper understanding and appreciation for diversity.

Finally, community involvement can also be an effective strategy for reducing bias and prejudice among youth. Community organizations can provide safe spaces for youth to discuss their beliefs and experiences and learn from one another. Youth-led initiatives can also be effective, allowing young people to take ownership of promoting inclusivity and tolerance. Organizations can also work with schools and parents to promote messages of acceptance and diversity, emphasizing the value of creating a community that celebrates differences.

In conclusion, stopping bias and prejudice among youth requires a multifaceted approach. Education, exposure, and community involvement are all critical components to reducing bias and promoting inclusivity. By teaching youth to value diversity, providing opportunities for exposure to different people and experiences, and fostering community involvement, we can help ensure that our future generations embrace inclusivity and acceptance.

An essay on the implication of bias and prejudice about society​:

- It is important to note that prejudice, stereotyping and discrimination are concepts classified under the term social bias. They are seen to have negative consequences on people. However, the impacts of the negative consequences differ. This is because the degree of social biasness in all of them differs as well. It is for this reason that subtle and blatant biases are key terms in social bias so as to explain the degree of biasness. In other words, subtle and blatant are two extreme categories in the bias spectrum.

Blatant biases are created in one’s mind. They are biases which are best explained by prejudice and discrimination acts. On the bias range, blatant biases are more severe and have severe negative consequences. That is, it can result to people hurting others simply because they belong to a different group of people. This is usually influenced by thoughts and feelings formed in the group against another group. This kind of bias is expressed by people who are aware of their actions and are proud of it.

On the other hand, subtle biases are not always intentional and thus their degree of biasness is usually low. However, subtle biases are also harmful just like any other bias. An example of subtle bias is when a person wants to be accepted in the society. In this case even though the person comes from the discriminated group, he/she may appear to be acceptable from outside but in the real terms the group has not fully accepted him/her.

The consequences of social biases are harmful to both groups, that is, the group biasing and the recipient. However, to the instigator the impact may be less harmful than the recipient. The extreme impacts are felt by the recipient. This is because they cause the emotional pain. In addition, social biases have negative impacts because they do not only affect the immediate persons involved but also the entire group.

Social biases result to hatred, anger as well as separation of people. Once developed, social biases become difficult to overcome. They have seen many people try to overcome them while others keep justifying the reasons for holding to them. It is important to understand that social biases do not lead to positive impacts and thus they should be avoided at all cost.

Individuals should know that differences exist among people and changes are bound to happen whenever different people come together. To overcome the social biases, people should know that it starts with self. No one should discriminate another on whatever basis. Instead, they should always seek to be united.
