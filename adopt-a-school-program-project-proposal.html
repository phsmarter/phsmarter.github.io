2. bakit mahalaga ang Adopt a school program?​


Answer:

Ang "Adopt a School" program ay isang programa na naglalayong magbigay ng tulong at suporta sa mga paaralan sa bansa. Mahalaga ang programang ito dahil mayroong maraming mga paaralan sa bansa na nangangailangan ng tulong upang mapabuti ang kalidad ng edukasyon na kanilang maibibigay sa mga mag-aaral.

Narito ang ilan sa mga mahahalagang dahilan kung bakit mahalaga ang Adopt a School program:

Nakakatulong sa pagpapabuti ng kalidad ng edukasyon - Sa pamamagitan ng tulong na ibinibigay ng mga organisasyon, pribadong sektor at mga indibidwal sa Adopt a School program, nabibigyan ng dagdag na pondo, libro, gamit at mga pasilidad ang mga paaralan. Sa ganitong paraan, nabibigyan ng mas magandang kalidad ng edukasyon ang mga mag-aaral.

Nakakatulong sa pagpapababa ng dropout rate - Dahil sa mga dagdag na pasilidad at tulong sa mga paaralan, mas maaring masiguro ang pag-aaral ng mga mag-aaral. Sa ganitong paraan, nababawasan ang dropout rate at mas marami ang nagtatapos ng kanilang pag-aaral.

Nagbibigay ng oportunidad sa mga mag-aaral - Sa pamamagitan ng Adopt a School program, nabibigyan ng oportunidad ang mga mag-aaral na makapagtapos ng kanilang pag-aaral. Nabibigyan sila ng mga kagamitan at mga pasilidad na nakakatulong sa kanilang pag-aaral.

Nagbibigay ng positibong epekto sa mga komunidad - Sa pamamagitan ng Adopt a School program, nakakatulong ang mga organisasyon, pribadong sektor at mga indibidwal sa pagpapabuti ng edukasyon sa kanilang komunidad. Sa ganitong paraan, nabibigyan ng positibong epekto sa kanilang komunidad.

Sa kabuuan, mahalaga ang Adopt a School program dahil sa nakakatulong ito sa pagpapabuti ng kalidad ng edukasyon, pagpapababa ng dropout rate, pagbibigay ng oportunidad sa mga mag-aaral, at pagbibigay ng positibong epekto sa mga komunidad.

6. propose means that can be adopted at home on how to mitigate the effects of climate change​


There are many things you can do at home to help mitigate the effects of climate change. Here are some suggestions:

Reduce energy consumption: This can be done in a variety of ways, such as turning off lights and electronics when not in use, using energy-efficient light bulbs, and adjusting your thermostat to conserve energy.

Use renewable energy sources: Consider installing solar panels or wind turbines at your home, or purchasing renewable energy from your utility company.

Reduce water consumption: Take shorter showers, fix leaks, and install low-flow showerheads and toilets.

Reduce waste: Recycle as much as possible, compost food waste, and use reusable bags, water bottles, and containers.

Eat a plant-based diet: Consuming less meat and dairy can significantly reduce your carbon footprint.

Use environmentally-friendly products: Choose products that are made from sustainable materials, such as bamboo or recycled plastic, and avoid single-use plastics.

Plant trees and other vegetation: Planting trees and other vegetation can help absorb carbon dioxide from the atmosphere.

Support climate-friendly policies: Write to your elected officials, vote for candidates who prioritize climate action, and support organizations that are working to combat climate change.

By adopting these practices at home, you can help mitigate the effects of climate change and contribute to a more sustainable future.

23. adoption of a rcycling program claim of policy?​


Answer:Since the 1990s, recycling of waste has become a core element of sustainable development. However, in this article, analysis of the flow modelling shows that recycling in itself is inefficient to perform the necessary "decoupling" of economic development and the depletion of non-renewable raw materials. The depletion of the natural resource of raw material is inevitable when its global consumption by the economy grows by more than 1% per annum. Recycling can delay for some years or decades at best. Decoupling the economy and its material needs must be understood as a double decoupling, the two components of which being inoperative if they are implemented separately. Only the combination of the two makes a significant impact on the problem of resources: (i) A fundamental decoupling, namely to restrain the growth of total consumption of raw material (virgin or recycled) (ii) A relative decoupling to reduce, through recycling and reuse, the share of primary resources (virgin) in the total production of raw material.

In this perspective, the actual role of recycling to protect the resources is not significant for non-renewable materials which consumption tends to grow more than 1% per year. Conversely, once the fundamental decoupling is performed by other means, so that the growth of total consumption of raw materials is reduced below 1% per year, recycling becomes indispensable if the rate of effectiveness is very high globally. Only recycling rates above 80% allow a significant slowdown of the depletion of natural resources.

In conclusion, sustainable development policies can not rely primarily on recycling, even though it is an important component. These policies should primarily aim at reducing the consumption of each non renewable raw matérial so that the annual growth rate remains under 1%. And in any case, to be efficient as the indispensable second part of these policies, recycling should be developed to much higher rates than the ones observed for most recycled materials in the world today.

29. propose review and adopt bills for enactment into​


Answer:

The procedures for introducing legislation and seeing it through committees are similar in both the House of Representatives and the Senate.

Legislative proposals originate in a number of different ways. Members of the Senate, of course, develop ideas for legislation. Technical assistance in research and drafting legislative language is available at the Senate Legislative Technical Affairs Bureau. Special interest groups—business, religious, labor, urban and rural poor, consumers, trade association, and the like—are other fertile sources of legislation. Constituents, either as individuals or groups, also may propose legislation. Frequently, a member of the Senate will introduce such a bill by request, whether or not he supports its purposes.

It must be noted also that much of the needed legislation of the country today considered by Congress originates from the executive branch. Each year after the President of the Philippines outlines his legislative program in his State-of-the-Nation Address, executive departments and agencies transmit to the House and the Senate drafts of proposed legislations to carry out the President’s program.
