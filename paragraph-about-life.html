1. make a paragraph about life​


Answer:

Life is a precious and complex phenomenon that encompasses all living beings on our planet. It is a journey filled with ups and downs, challenges, and opportunities. Life is not always easy, and it can be full of surprises and uncertainties. Yet, it is also full of beauty and wonder, and it offers us countless experiences and moments to cherish. Throughout our lives, we encounter different people, cultures, and experiences, which shape us and make us who we are. Our individual life paths may vary, but we all share a common desire for happiness, love, and fulfillment. Life is a precious gift, and we should strive to make the most of it by living it to the fullest and making a positive impact on the world around us.

10. Write a paragraph about the importance of the sun to your life.


IMPORTANCE OF THE SUN

When exposed to the sun, your skin can manufacture its own vitamin D. “We each have vitamin D receptor cells that, through a chain of reactions starting with conversion of cholesterol in the skin, produce vitamin D3 when they're exposed to ultraviolet B (UVB) from the sun,” .The sun's UV rays help your body make this nutrient, which is important for your bones, blood cells, and immune system. It also helps you take in and use certain minerals, like calcium and phosphorus.It radiates light and heat, or solar energy, which makes it possible for life to exist on Earth. Plants need sunlight to grow. Animals, including humans, need plants for food and the oxygen they produce. Without heat from the sun, Earth would freeze.

13. life lesson about Helen Keller 5 paragraph ​


Answer:

This week marks Helen Keller’s 136th birthday, and while she may no longer be with us, she certainly left a good deal of life lessons for us to learn from.

Not only famous for becoming a published author, Helen Keller was also the first deaf and blind person to earn a Bachelor of Arts degree, truly showing that disability – even blindness – is no match for a spirit of determination.

In fact, there are many things we can learn from Helen Keller.

The “unrealistic” can be realistic.

When you look at history, you’ll find that the true movers and shakers were not “realists.” Those figures that truly changed the course of history and, effectively, each of our lives, were overwhelmingly idealists.

Think about the people who have made a difference in our world, from Albert Einstein to Benjamin Franklin to the Wright Brothers to Steve Jobs; each of these people believed they could attain something more from life than what was presently accepted.

Helen Keller was exactly this type of person. She rose above what was “realistic” for someone with her disabilities at the time she lived.

21. create a paragraph about life cycle​


Answer:

Life cycle means the stages a living thing goes through during its life. In some cases the process is slow, and the changes are gradual. Humans have various stages of growth during their lives, such as zygote, embryo, child and adult. The change from a child to an adult is slow and continuous.

Explanation:

A life cycle is defined as the developmental stages that occur during an organism's lifetime. A life cycle ends when an organism dies. ... During the adult stage, an organism will reproduce, giving rise to the next generation. A life cycle can be comprised of more than the three basic stages depending on the species
