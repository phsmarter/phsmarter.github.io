10. what is the role of mass media in building nationalism in the philippines contemporary period?​


Answer:
Mass media has played a significant role in building nationalism in the Philippines during the contemporary period. The media, through various platforms such as television, radio, newspapers, and the internet, has helped to shape national identity and to promote a shared sense of national pride. It has also been used to educate the public about Philippine history and culture, and to promote the values and ideals that are important to the nation. Mass media has also been instrumental in covering and reporting on issues and events that are relevant to Philippine society, including issues related to nationalism and patriotism. Through its reporting, the media has helped to foster a sense of unity and belonging among Filipinos, and has helped to build a sense of national pride and identity. Additionally, mass media has also been used to promote political and social causes that are aligned with national goals and objectives, and to mobilize support for these causes among the public.

14. What are the contemporary art forms?


Answer:

to many people, coming up with a contemporary art definition can be a tricky task. While its title is simplistic and straightforward, its modern-day meaning is not as clear-cut. Fortunately, understanding what constitutes as “contemporary” is entirely possible once one traces the concept's history and explores its underlying themes.

In its most basic sense, the term contemporary art refers to art—namely, painting, sculpture, photography, installation, performance, and video art—produced today. Though seemingly simple, the details surrounding this definition are often a bit fuzzy, as different individuals' interpretations of “today” may widely and wildly vary. Therefore, the exact starting point of the genre is still debated; however, many art historians consider the late 1960s or early 1970s (the end of modern art, or modernism) to be an adequate estimate.
