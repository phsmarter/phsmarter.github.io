1. Journey essay for making artwork?​


My direction

(Own Experience)

I had only had a couple of friends and never really ventured out to make new friends. So, for the majority of that summer I kept to myself, and read the Twilight Saga. During my readings of the Twilight Saga, I became a major fan of the series. This was my very first ‘fandom’, of a long line that will soon come in the upcoming years. I was introduced to Tumblr, where all fan geeks like me gathered to share our feelings on books, music, and art that we liked. Through Tumblr, I was introduced fanfiction and fanart. This was how I got into drawing.

I still had no self esteem as an artist. When my mom would call me the little artist, I shrugged at this idea. In my mind, being an artist means you actually have to be good at.

The first couple drawings, as you would expect, were really terrible. I thought I was never going to become a good artist, but, for some reason, I kept trying. I needed to practice, so I looked up drawings from other people and tried to mimic. I would draw the same picture a couple times. By doing this, I was training my eyes to pick up details, without even realising at the time.

As time went on, I expanded my world by taking on more fandoms. By doing this, I expanded my fuel for more drawings. I continued to draw more often, and started to become better at it. Of course most of my drawings are fan art, but, it helped me improve my basic skills as an artist, such as detail, color, and proportion.

So we must all have perseverance. Not everything comes from luck, others come from hard work. I am a student, striving for what I want to achieve and what can i be achieved.

-Tumahik: A Symbolic Dance of Love and War.

The Tumahik is a mock war dance performed by the males of the Yakan tribe in order to practice their fighting skills. The movements, common among Southeast Asian martial arts, include traveling on the knees, tumbling, and high kicking.

-Tennun (Weaving)

Yakan people are recognised for their remarkable technicolour geometric weaves and the distinctive face decorations used in their traditional ceremonies. Yakan fabrics are known for their striking colors and geometric patterns.

-Tanyak-tanyak (Facial Art)

Yakan are more of a kind, aesthetic and rich in different beauties. During any other occasions, they are also engaged with make overs with their faces. This body art represent the traditional way of showing both physical and cultural beauty which is known as Tanyak-Tanyak. Tanyak-Tanyak is a face painting custom unique to tribal culture and is worn only for wedding ceremonies.

-Pagkawin (Yakan Traditional Wedding)

Yakan wedding is very unique. The bride and the groom are both dressed in Yakan attire and follow rituals inclined with Yakan culture.  

An agong chant is played to announce wedding celebrations. In Yakan, a pegkawin, usually lasts 3 days. On the day of the wedding, the bride and the groom and given separate baths in separate places, signifying purification of the body.

Not to be confused with contemporary art, the “modern art” label refers to late 19th and early-to-mid 20th century art. Works produced during this time showcase artists' interest in re-imagining, reinterpreting, and even rejecting traditional aesthetic values of preceding styles.

Although many different styles are encompassed by the term, there are certain underlying principles that define modernist art: A rejection of history and conservative values (such as realistic depiction of subjects); innovation and experimentation with form (the shapes, colours and lines that make up the work).Modern art includes artistic work produced during the period extending roughly from the 1860s to the 1970s, and denotes the styles and philosophies of the art produced during that era. The term is usually associated with art in which the traditions of the past have been thrown aside in a spirit of experimentation.Post modern art is a body of art movements that sought to contradict some aspects of modernism or some aspects that emerged or developed in its aftermath. In general, movements such as intermedia, installation art, conceptual art and multimedia, particularly involving video are described as postmodern.
