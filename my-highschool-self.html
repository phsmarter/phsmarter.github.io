1. Similarities in all stage of my self (elementary ,highschool to college)


Answer:

When I read my old report cards, I performed quite well overall. I started sometimes at satisfactory grades but progressed to outstanding grades by the end of each year in every multiple subject. My citizenship and related skills were also usually outstanding though some had satisfactory marks. The only multiple subject that I was never below outstanding, perhaps not even including outstanding minus, was spelling. During that elementary school study, I attended three schools, the first was demolished and a new school created, and then the next school was an older one. These were Fremont Older, Sedjwick, and Meyerholz respectively, and if I kept attending the next schools at the Cupertino Union School District, I would have gone to Lynbrook. Though that was when I was a commuter and so when I finished grade 1 to 6, I transferred back to Fremont Unified School District close to where I live. I was always quite good at academics if I focused and wanted to study the course material, though I was also involved with social life and making friends. I didn’t want to feel left out of the normal social groups, having breaks and activities, and having friends during academics. Middle school and high school were not all excellent, as I got some failures when I was in grade 8 due to socializing and having girlfriends. And it took me until grade 10 to 11, to bring up my studies and grades in the single subjects overall, by following the trends of my class. My high school grades were spread, and I did well in English and mathematics, good in science, mostly excellent in social science, and okay in foreign language in Spanish. I did not take many advanced placement classes, only 2 in statistics and U.S. history. My college intentions were not very focused on occupation, academic major and the courses to be taken. I always came into college the first time exploring and taking interesting courses in general education, while barely keeping my major in business economics up. I even took a minor in philosophy then. I had to go to college a second time to show that I had enough focus and attention to do outstanding in another major in mathematics and minor in computer science. My overall studies still in college have added a lot of courses and units or credits into my transcript, though I don’t have that much depth. I have gone to graduate school but it was not serious enough in statistics to establish an academic career, and so it has informed me by my actions and the consequences, that I am still interested in another subject at a lower level. These include Vietnamese because I enjoy learning the language, and also courses related to my past majors and to broaden out my general education while I have spare time. I'm actually very interested in the lower division right now, in prerequisite and course sequences in subjects I have not had the opportunity to develop much at all. College is very limited in that sense, when we may say we went to college but we really only studied a major or few majors, and also a selected, elected, or sampled set of courses for general education.

12. essay for my journey as a junior highschool​


Answer:

My journey as a junior high school student has been a time of growth, learning, and discovery. During these years, I have had the opportunity to explore new subjects, meet new people, and develop new skills that will serve me well throughout my life.

One of the most significant changes I experienced during my junior high school years was the shift from a more general education to a more specialized curriculum. As I moved from grade school to junior high school, I had the opportunity to study subjects in greater depth, such as science, math, and English. This allowed me to develop a deeper understanding of these subjects and prepare for the more advanced coursework that awaits me in high school and beyond.

Another important aspect of my junior high school journey has been the opportunity to make new friends and expand my social circle. As I entered a new school with new classmates, I had the chance to meet people from different backgrounds and with different interests. This allowed me to broaden my perspective, learn from others, and develop important social skills that will serve me well throughout my life.

Finally, my journey as a junior high school student has been characterized by a commitment to personal growth and development. I have learned the importance of hard work, dedication, and perseverance in achieving my goals, whether that be acing a test or mastering a new skill. I have also learned the value of seeking help when needed, whether that be from teachers, mentors, or peers, and the importance of taking care of my physical and mental health.

Overall, my journey as a junior high school student has been a time of growth, learning, and development. I am grateful for the experiences and opportunities that have come my way during these years and look forward to continuing my journey as I move on to high school and beyond

My childhood memories (personal recount)

Childhood is a precious time that shapes the person we become. Looking back, I can vividly recall many fond memories from my early years. The carefree days of playing with friends, exploring the outdoors, and enjoying the simple pleasures of life.

One of my fondest memories is the times I spent with my grandfather. He was a kind and gentle man, who always had a twinkle in his eye and a story to tell. Whenever I visited him, he would take me on long walks in the countryside, pointing out all the plants and animals we came across.

Another treasured memory is the summer vacations spent at my aunt's farm. We would spend the days picking fresh fruits and vegetables, feeding the animals, and playing in the open fields. At night, we would gather around the campfire and listen to my uncle's stories about his travels around the world.

I also have memories of my school days, the excitement of learning new things, the thrill of participating in sports, and the joy of making new friends. There were times when I struggled with my studies, but my parents and teachers were always there to support and guide me.

As I grew older, life became more complex, and I began to appreciate the simplicity of those childhood days. The memories of those carefree days have stayed with me, providing a source of comfort and inspiration during the challenges of adult life.

In conclusion, childhood memories are an integral part of our lives, shaping our identity and providing a foundation for the future. I cherish the memories of my childhood and the people who helped make it such a special time.
