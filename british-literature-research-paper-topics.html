Postmodern Style

Postmodern or abbreviated as Post-mo is an after-modern art style. Along with the development of culture, art will also experience a development of style.

If traditional art has ornamental characteristics, modern art has simplification of forms, then the postmodern art style has characteristics of a combination of simplified forms and a little ornamental.

postmodern style tends to be more free and does not have certain rules. Exploration of elements of fine art is mostly done for this style. Social and social criticism is a theme that is widely used in postmodern art works.

Naturalism

The term naturalism comes from two words, namely nature which means nature and ism which means flow or style. Naturalism is a style of art whose depiction is in accordance with existing natural conditions. Painters who use this style generally take natural landscapes as their objects.

Foreign artists who use this style include Rubens, Gainsborough, Claude, Turner, and Constable.

Realism

The term realism comes from two words, namely reak which means real and ism which means flow or style. realism is a style of art whose depiction is in accordance with the existing reality. .
