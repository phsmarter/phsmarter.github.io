1.) How does the Philippine government address different local and national health issues and concerns like HIV/AIDS cases and tobacco and alcohol related diseases?

The Philippine government addresses different health issues and concerns through various programs and initiatives. For instance, the Department of Health (DOH) has implemented the Philippine Health Agenda, which aims to improve the delivery of health services and to address the country’s health challenges, such as HIV/AIDS, tuberculosis, and non-communicable diseases. The government has also passed laws and regulations, such as the Sin Tax Law, which imposes higher taxes on tobacco and alcohol products to deter their consumption and reduce related diseases.

2.) Who do you think are the stakeholders or partners of the government in addressing health problems? How can they help curb various health problems?

The stakeholders or partners of the government in addressing health problems include healthcare providers, non-governmental organizations (NGOs), private sectors, and the community. Healthcare providers and NGOs can offer medical assistance, research and development, and community outreach programs. The private sectors can provide resources and funding for health programs, while the community can participate in health promotion activities and support the implementation of health policies.

Educating the youth can help in developing socially conscious and healthy individuals who are aware of the various global health threats and concerns. By educating the youth, they can become advocates for health and can help create a culture of health consciousness, which can lead to a healthier society in the future.
