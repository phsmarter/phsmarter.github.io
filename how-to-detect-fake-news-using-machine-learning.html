7. Essay: what fake news ? how are going to fight misinformation of fake news​


Answer:

Fake news refers to false or misleading information that is spread deliberately to manipulate public opinion or deceive people. The term is commonly used in the context of politics and media, where false stories are spread to advance a particular agenda or to discredit a particular individual or group. The rise of social media has made it easier for fake news to spread quickly and reach a large audience, making it a serious threat to the credibility of information and the public's ability to make informed decisions.

Fighting misinformation and fake news requires a multi-faceted approach that addresses both the root causes and the symptoms. Here are some ways to fight fake news:

Fact-checking: One of the most effective ways to fight fake news is to fact-check and verify information before sharing it. This means checking multiple sources and using tools like fact-checking websites and databases.

Media literacy: Media literacy education is essential in teaching people to critically evaluate information and identify fake news. This includes teaching people how to identify bias, understand the difference between facts and opinions, and recognize propaganda and misinformation.

Regulation: Governments can play a role in regulating the spread of fake news by imposing fines on media outlets that spread false information and by requiring online platforms to take measures to prevent the spread of fake news.

Technology: Artificial intelligence and machine learning can be used to detect and flag fake news and to remove false information from online platforms.

Community engagement: Communities can come together to fight fake news by promoting media literacy, reporting fake news when they see it, and supporting credible media sources.

In conclusion, fighting fake news is a collective effort that requires the cooperation of individuals, communities, media outlets, and governments. By raising awareness and taking action, we can help to create a more informed and trustworthy public discourse.

10. "As a student, how will you contribute to prevent the spreading of FAKE NEWS using the online community?​


Answer:

As a student, there are several ways you can contribute to preventing the spread of fake news online:

1. Verify sources: Before sharing any news or information, ensure that the source is reliable and trustworthy. Look for multiple sources to verify the information.

2. Check the facts: Always fact-check the information you come across online. Be wary of sensational headlines and clickbait articles that are designed to grab attention but may not have much substance.

3. Share responsibly: Only share information that you have verified and know to be true. Avoid sharing unverified information or rumors that could potentially be harmful or misleading.

4. Report fake news: If you come across fake news or misinformation, report it to the relevant authorities or platforms. This can help prevent its spread and ensure that others are not misled by it.

5. Educate others: Spread awareness about the dangers of fake news and the importance of verifying information before sharing it. Encourage your friends and family to be responsible online citizens.

By following these steps, you can help prevent the spread of fake news and contribute to a more informed and responsible online community.

Explanation:

As a critical reader you should reflect on:

What the text says:  after critically reading a piece you should be able to take notes, paraphrasing - in your own words - the key points.

What the text describes: you should be confident that you have understood the text sufficiently to be able to use your own examples and compare and contrast with other writing on the subject in hand.

Interpretation of the text: this means that you should be able to fully analyse the text and state a meaning for the text as a whole.

Critical reading means being able to reflect on what a text says, what it describes and what it means by scrutinising the style and structure of the writing, the language used as well as the content.
