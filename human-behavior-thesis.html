4. hypothesis about social media: Its Influence to the social behavior of the students pa help naman po thesis namin​


Answer:

The hypothesis about social media and its influence on the social behavior of students suggests that the use of social media platforms has a significant impact on the way students interact with others and behave in social situations. This hypothesis is based on the idea that social media has become an integral part of students' lives, affecting not only their communication habits but also their perceptions of self and others.

The hypothesis suggests that students who use social media regularly may exhibit certain behaviors such as increased aggression, decreased empathy, and a stronger focus on appearance and popularity. On the other hand, students who use social media in moderation or not at all may display more positive social behaviors such as improved communication skills, enhanced emotional intelligence, and a greater sense of community.

It is important to note that this hypothesis is not a blanket statement and may vary depending on the individual and their specific experiences with social media. Further research and analysis are needed to fully understand the relationship between social media and social behavior among students.

To create essays and theses about human experiences related to discrimination, gender, poverty, and economy in applied social science, it is essential to follow a structured approach. Here are some steps that can be helpful:

1. Identify the research topic: Choose a specific topic related to the subject matter. For example, the impact of gender discrimination on women's economic empowerment.

2. Conduct a literature review: Review academic sources and research studies related to the topic. This will provide a solid understanding of the current state of research, gaps, and areas that need further investigation.

3. Formulate research questions: Based on the literature review, formulate research questions that address the gaps in knowledge and contribute to existing research.

4. Develop a methodology: Select an appropriate research methodology and design the study. This will include selecting participants, data collection methods, and data analysis techniques.

5. Collect and analyze data: Collect data from participants using the chosen data collection method. Analyze the data using appropriate statistical techniques.

6. Interpret the findings: Interpret the results of the study in light of the research questions and literature review. Explain the significance of the findings and how they contribute to the existing body of knowledge.

7. Conclusion and recommendations: Conclude the essay or thesis by summarizing the main findings, discussing the implications of the study, and making recommendations for further research or policy changes.

When writing about human experiences related to discrimination, gender, poverty, and economy, it is important to approach the topic with sensitivity and empathy. It is also essential to maintain academic rigor and use credible sources to support arguments and findings.

17. Difference between thesis and thesis statement


Answer:

Your thesis is the main idea, point, incident, or argument of your paper. That is in your mind.

Your thesis statement is that idea expressed in a sentence. An important caveat: this sentence must conform to the class or the reviewer’s (professor’s) requirement. If there is a specific form required, that should have been explained to you and taught to you before you have to write it. In one of my classes, stdents were required to create thesis statements, bring them to class, and have the critiqued in class discussions. We also studied examples of both good and bad thesis statements from published papers.

Thesis — idea.

Thesis statement — a sentence giving the thesis (idea) which conforms to the established form stating that idea.
