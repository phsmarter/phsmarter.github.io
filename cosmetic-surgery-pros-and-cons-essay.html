27. persuasive topics Should children under the age of 18 be able to get plastic surgery for cosmetic reasons? 300-500 essay​


Answer:

Plastic surgery for cosmetic reasons is a highly controversial topic, especially when it comes to children under the age of 18. While some argue that it should be allowed, others believe that it is not appropriate for minors. In this essay, I will argue against the idea of allowing children under the age of 18 to undergo plastic surgery for cosmetic reasons.

Firstly, it is important to understand that plastic surgery is a serious medical procedure that should not be taken lightly. Children's bodies are still developing, and undergoing a major surgery can have long-term consequences. Cosmetic surgery is often irreversible, and it can lead to complications and risks such as infections, scarring, and anesthesia-related issues. These risks are even higher for children whose bodies are not fully developed, and whose emotional maturity may not be fully developed either.

Secondly, children may not fully understand the implications and consequences of undergoing plastic surgery. They may be more focused on their appearance and may not fully understand the risks involved. Moreover, they may not be emotionally mature enough to make such a decision. They may be influenced by their peers or social media, which can lead to unrealistic expectations and a negative body image.

Finally, allowing children to undergo plastic surgery for cosmetic reasons sends the wrong message about self-esteem and body image. Children should be taught to accept and love themselves for who they are, rather than to change their appearance to fit societal beauty standards. Allowing children to undergo plastic surgery for cosmetic reasons reinforces the message that appearance is more important than inner beauty and self-acceptance.

In conclusion, children under the age of 18 should not be allowed to undergo plastic surgery for cosmetic reasons. Cosmetic surgery is a serious medical procedure that comes with risks and long-term consequences. Children's bodies and emotional maturity are still developing, and they may not fully understand the implications of undergoing such a surgery. It is important to teach children to accept and love themselves for who they are, rather than to change their appearance to fit societal beauty standards.
