10. brief summary of the old man at the bridge​


Answer:

The story “Old Man at the Bridge” is about a conversation between a war correspondent and an old man who had to leave his hometown during the Spanish Civil War. Hemingway was appointed by the North American Newspaper Association (NANA) but he apparently decided to write it as a short story instead of a news article. As the title “Old Man at the Bridge” suggests, the story revolves around the old man. This very short and simple story is a powerful depiction of how war affects the lives of common people who are “without politics”. The old man has nothing to do with the impending war. But still, he had to leave his home and his beloved pets whom he regards as his family.

Ernest Hemingway’s “Old Man at the Bridge” is a short story that depicts the consequences of war on individuals and their communities. The story is set in a small Spanish town during the Spanish Civil War, and it tells the story of an old man who is forced to flee his home due to the war. The narrative explores the themes of loss, displacement, and the sense of hopelessness that many war refugees experience.

The story begins with an old man sitting at a bridge, waiting for the rest of his family to arrive. The man is exhausted and weak, and he has been walking for several days. He is unable to continue his journey due to his advanced age and physical limitations. The narrator, an American soldier, approaches the old man and strikes up a conversation with him. The old man tells the soldier that he is from a town several kilometers away and that he is the only one left there. The rest of his family has fled the town, and he is waiting for them at the bridge.

As the story progresses, it becomes clear that the old man is resigned to his fate. He knows that he is too old to keep walking, and he has no choice but to wait for his family. However, the soldier begins to feel a sense of guilt and helplessness as he realizes that he cannot do anything to help the old man. The soldier is sympathetic towards the old man’s plight, but he knows that he cannot interfere with the war or the old man’s family’s decisions.

The story ends on a somber note, as the soldier watches the old man sitting alone at the bridge, waiting for his family. The soldier reflects on the old man’s fate and the tragic consequences of war. He realizes that the old man’s situation is not unique, and there are countless others like him who have been displaced and left homeless by the war. The story highlights the human cost of war and the profound impact it has on individuals and communities.

In conclusion, “Old Man at the Bridge” is a poignant story that sheds light on the devastating effects of war on civilians. The story is a powerful reminder that war is not just about military battles and geopolitical power struggles; it is also about the lives of ordinary people who are caught in the crossfire. Hemingway’s spare, understated prose captures the sense of loss and despair that many war refugees experience. Ultimately, the story is a call to action, urging us to do everything in our power to prevent wars and to support those who are affected by them.
