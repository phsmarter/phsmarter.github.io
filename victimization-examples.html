2. What is the Example of Related Victims ​


Answer:

Related victims are people who are dependent on the primary victim, have a close relationship with the primary victim or are connected to the victim in some other way. For example, the neighbors of the robbery victim would be considered related victims if the crime occurred on their street

12. Answer the question below: 1. Based on the examples of intentional injuries given above what are the effects on intentional injuries on the victims?​


Intentional injuries can have a wide range of effects on the victims, depending on the type and severity of the injury. Some common effects of intentional injuries include physical harm, emotional trauma, and financial burden.

For instance, victims of physical assault may experience injuries such as broken bones, cuts, bruises, or even more severe injuries such as brain damage or paralysis. These physical injuries may require extensive medical treatment and rehabilitation, resulting in significant financial costs and long-term disability.

Victims of sexual assault may experience physical injuries as well as psychological trauma, including depression, anxiety, post-traumatic stress disorder (PTSD), and difficulties with intimacy and relationships.

Similarly, victims of domestic violence may suffer physical injuries as well as emotional trauma and financial instability. They may also experience a decreased sense of self-worth, shame, and fear, and may be trapped in a cycle of abuse.

In general, intentional injuries can have a significant impact on the physical, emotional, and financial well-being of victims, and may require extensive support and resources to aid in recovery.
