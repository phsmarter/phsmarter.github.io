27. 10 culture and traditional that filipino adopt because of globalization with explanation ​


Answer:

1. Overseas Filipino Workers (OFWs) - The Filipino culture has been greatly influenced by the millions of Overseas Filipino Workers (OFWs) who have left the country to work in other parts of the world. The Filipino culture has been enriched by the various cultures that OFWs have been exposed to.

2. Spanish Language - The Spanish language has been greatly influential in the Philippines. It was the language of the Spanish colonial masters and is still spoken by many Filipinos today.

3. Catholicism - The Catholic religion is the predominant religion in the Philippines. It has been influential in shaping the Filipino culture and values.

4. Family Values - The Filipino culture is strongly rooted in family values. Family is considered to be the most important institution in society.

5. Respect for Elders - Filipinos have a strong tradition of respecting their elders. This is reflected in the way they address their elders, and in the way they interact with them.

6. Hospitality - Filipinos are known for their hospitality. They are always willing to welcome guests into their homes and to treat them with respect and kindness.

7. Sense of Community - Filipinos have a strong sense of community. They are always willing to help out their neighbours and to work together for the common good.

8. Traditional Dress - Filipinos have a strong tradition of wearing traditional dress. This is especially common in rural areas, where people still wear traditional costumes for special occasions.

9. Folklore - Filipino folklore is rich and varied. It is reflective of the Filipino culture and the way of life in the Philippines.

10. Bayanihan - Bayanihan is the spirit of community cooperation and solidarity that is deeply ingrained in the Filipino culture. It is the belief that we are all in this together and that we can achieve great things when we work together.
