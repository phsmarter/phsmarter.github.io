19. In mice, the ability to run normally is a dominant trait. Mice with this trait are called running mice (R). The recessive trait causes mice to run in circles only. Mice with this trait are called waltzing mice (r). Hair color is also inherited in mice. Black hair (B) is dominant to brown hair (b). If you cross a heterozygous running black mouse with a waltzing brown mouse, what are the Parental genotypes?


Answer:

Therefore, the parental genotypes for this cross are RrBb (heterozygous running black mouse) and rrbb (waltzing brown mouse).

Explanation:

In this cross, the heterozygous running black mouse has the genotype RrBb, where R represents the dominant running trait, r represents the recessive waltzing trait, B represents the dominant black hair color, and b represents the recessive brown hair color. The waltzing brown mouse has the genotype rrbb, as it possesses the recessive traits for both running and hair color. Therefore, the parental genotypes for this cross are RrBb (heterozygous running black mouse) and rrbb (waltzing brown mouse).
