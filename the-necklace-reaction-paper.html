22. Edna already owns 4 necklaces, and additional necklaces are priced at 2 for a dollar. Is the total number of necklaces Edna owns proportional to the amount of money Edna spends on additional necklaces?


Answer:

Yes.

Explanation:

Two terms are proportional if one term increases with the other, or as one term increases, the other decreases.

Using this basis, we know that the number of necklaces is proportional to the amount of money spent.

As the amount increases by a dollar, the necklaces increase by 2.

We can also determine if this is proportional if we can come up with a linear equation that relates the two terms.

Let x = necklaces; let y = amount spent for additional necklaces.

Using what I previously said in the third paragraph, y = x/2. But, we need to add 4 to this since there are 4 necklaces already to start with. Thus, y = x/2 + 4.

To summarize, yes; the number of necklaces is proportional to the amount spent on additional necklaces.

24. "THE NECKLACE STORY"-Context -Authors intention -Your reaction -Literary device -Literary technique -Ending of the story​


Answer:

Context:

"The Necklace" is a short story by French author Guy de Maupassant, first published in 1884. The story is set in Paris in the late 19th century and follows a middle-class couple, Monsieur and Madame Loisel, who borrow a diamond necklace from a wealthy friend to wear to a fancy ball. Madame Loisel loses the necklace and spends ten years paying off the debt to replace it, only to discover that the original necklace was a fake, and their sacrifices were for nothing.

Author's intention:

The author's intention was to illustrate the themes of social class, materialism, and the consequences of dishonesty. Through the story, de Maupassant highlights the false sense of importance placed on material possessions and the dangers of deceit, as well as the crushing weight of poverty and how it can ruin people's lives.

My reaction:

I find the story to be a poignant reminder of the futility of materialism and the importance of honesty. It's a cautionary tale about the dangers of constantly striving for more and the consequences of not being content with what one has. The story is also a sad commentary on the societal expectations that force people to conform to a certain class and status, often at great personal cost.

Literary device:

One literary device used in the story is irony, as the necklace that Madame Loisel covets and sacrifices so much for turns out to be a fake, making all of her suffering pointless.

Literary technique:

One literary technique used in the story is symbolism, as the necklace represents Madame Loisel's desire for material possessions and her desire to be accepted into the upper class.

Ending of the story:

The story ends with Madame Loisel running into Madame Forestier, the friend who lent her the necklace, and telling her the truth about what happened. Madame Forestier is surprised and tells Madame Loisel that the original necklace was a fake, leaving Madame Loisel to realize that all of her suffering and sacrifices were for nothing. The story ends with a bitter irony, highlighting the dangers of materialism and the importance of honesty.


25. PERFORMANCE TASK 2 Compose a CRITICAL ANALYSIS on the suggested selection using the LITERARY APPROACHES Short bond paper learned.THE NECKLACE by GUY DE MAUPASSANT​


"The Necklace" is a short story written by Guy de Maupassant, a French writer who is known for his realistic and naturalistic style. The story tells the tale of Madame Loisel, a beautiful but discontented woman who is married to a clerk. She yearns for a life of luxury and glamour, and constantly dreams of being admired by the rich and famous. One day, her husband manages to obtain an invitation to a fancy ball, and Madame Loisel borrows a beautiful necklace from a friend to wear to the event. However, after the ball, she discovers that the necklace is missing, and the couple is forced to replace it at a great cost, which leads them into years of poverty and hardship. In the end, Madame Loisel learns that the necklace was not even real, and that her desire for material possessions had led her to ruin.

The story is a poignant commentary on the dangers of materialism and the pursuit of wealth at the expense of one's own happiness. Through his use of vivid imagery and descriptive language, Maupassant paints a vivid picture of the opulent world that Madame Loisel dreams of, and the stark reality of her own life. The contrast between the two is striking, and the reader is left with a sense of the futility of striving for material gain.

Maupassant's use of irony is also notable in the story. Madame Loisel's desire for a life of luxury is ultimately what leads to her downfall, as the necklace that she coveted so much turns out to be a fake. This irony highlights the danger of attaching too much importance to material possessions, and the emptiness that can result from pursuing wealth and status above all else.

In terms of literary approaches, "The Necklace" can be analyzed using a variety of techniques. From a structural perspective, the story is notable for its use of foreshadowing, as Madame Loisel's obsession with material possessions is introduced early on and sets the stage for the events that unfold. The story can also be analyzed from a feminist perspective, as it highlights the limited options available to women in the time period in which it is set, and the way that societal expectations can lead to feelings of dissatisfaction and unfulfillment.

Overall, "The Necklace" is a timeless tale that continues to resonate with readers today. Its message about the dangers of materialism and the importance of finding happiness in the present moment is as relevant now as it was when it was first published, and its literary techniques and approaches make it a fascinating subject.

29. A necklace was sold for Php3150.00 with a 40% discount.what was the original Price of the necklace?​


ANSWER


Php5250.00

SOLUTION

To determine the original price of the necklace, you can use the following formula:

Original price = selling price / (1 - discount%)

Plugging in the given values, you get:

Original price = 3150 / (1 - 40%) = 3150 / (1 - 0.4) = 3150 / 0.6 = 5250

Therefore, the original price of the necklace was Php5250.00.
