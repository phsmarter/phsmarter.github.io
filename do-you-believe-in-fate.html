9. Do you sympathize Fortunato or think that he deserved his fate?


Answer:

I sympathize with Fortunato.

Explanation:

This question is related to the short story "The Cask of Amontillado" written by Edgar Alan Poe. In this story we learn that a man named Montresor, wants to take revenge on his neighbor, Fortunato, because he was humiliated by him. This revenge causes Montresor to handcuff Fortunato and leave him trapped in a brick wall. He really does that and listens to Fortunato's screams, imprisoned, until he dies.

In this story I sympathize with Fortunato because I don't believe that torture, like what happened to him, is the right way to get revenge. Also, I don't believe that revenge is a good way to resolve grievances, as nothing will change what happened.

25. do you believe that poverty is neither a fate nor a punishment but a condition that can and must be change yes or no justify your answer ​


Answer:

Yes, it's a condition that can and must be changed.

Explanation:

POVERTY: A FATE OR A CHOICE?

Are you familiar with this famous phrase?

"It's not your fault if you're born poor; it's your fault if you die poor."

Our country has many poor people, or should I say, poorer than poor. They frequently murmur that they are impoverished because that is their path in life. They claim that they are poor because they have no other option.

Part of me wants to believe it, but I think the primary source of this view is a lack of knowledge. When a poor person restricts their information, they will remain stuck in their current position or belief. Poverty is not solely a government issue. Poverty begins with oneself.

How one arranges their life affects one's chances of living in luxury or poverty. Similarly, becoming wealthy is not a difficult task; all it takes is a conscious effort and determination to succeed. To put it another way, just because you come from a poor family doesn't mean you'll always be poor. The decisions and choices we make now, whether to save/invest or not, shape the story of poverty we will tell tomorrow. Yes, you may have come from a poor family, but the tale changes when you make a more educated decision to improve your life and achieve your goals. You alter your destiny in this way.

Poverty occurs when decisions that could have changed the situation are ignored due to the threat of immediate danger. You don't need to spend millions on improving your condition. All it takes is generating a small idea that can start with the pennies you have right now, having a positive mindset, and being committed to what you want to do or have created. The world is full of chances, but only those who take the risk to seize them will benefit.

There may be certain parts of this essay that you disagree with, but the central point is to show you that it just takes a little to get out of poverty. Poverty, on the other hand, isn't a spell set on you to completely control your life for the rest of your life; instead, the continuation of poverty on each successive day of your life signals that you haven't made a decision yet. So yes, it's a condition that can and must be changed. Some people are indeed born into better circumstances than you, but refusing to accept responsibility isn't going to change that.
