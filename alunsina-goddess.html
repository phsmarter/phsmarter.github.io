26. Goddess of the moon?


Many cultures, civilizations and religions have different deities, god or goddess, associated with or symbolizing the moon. However, the most known goddesses of the moon are from Greek and Roman mythology, as follows: 


a) Selene is the goddess of the moon based from Greek mythology. She is the dauther of Titan gods Hyperion and Theia, and sister of the sun-god Helios and Eos, who is the goddess of the dawn. She is often depicted as a goddess  who drives the moon chariot across the heavens, in attribution to how the moon passes across the sky from dawn to dusk. 

b) Luna is the Roman equivalent of the goddess of the moon. She is often presented in Roman art and literature as the female complement of the Sun (Sol).
