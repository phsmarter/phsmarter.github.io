2. The German philosopher who stated that one cannot fully live unless he confronts his own mortality. Select one: a. None of the choices b. Martin Heidegger c. Albert Camus d. Soren Kierkegaard


Answer:

B.martin heid

Explanation:

Martin Heidegger (1889-1976) was a German philosopher who is widely considered to be one of the most influential thinkers of the 20th century. He is best known for his book Being and Time, which is considered one of the most important philosophical works of the 20th century. Heidegger's thought is often associated with phenomenology and existentialism, although he himself rejected both labels. Heidegger's work focused on the analysis of human existence, and how it relates to the nature of being. He argued that existence is not an abstract concept, but rather something that is experienced and lived through. Heidegger's philosophy has had a profound influence on many disciplines, including philosophy, theology, psychology, sociology, and literature.

15. similarity of phenomenology and existentialism?​


Answer:

Together, Existentialism and Phenomenology move the focus away from facts about the world towards facts about the individual self. For Phenomenology, that means changing the way we view metaphysics and epistemological claims. For Existentialism, it generates a normative ethic on how to live a worthwhile life.
