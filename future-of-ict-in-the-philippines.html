9. discribe the status of ICT in the Philippines​


Answer:

Information Communications Technology is usually included in the Home Economics and Livelihood Education program in grade school and taught through the Technology and Home Economics program[1] in high school. The recent status of ICT education in the Philippines, along with other Southeast Asian countries, was surveyed by the Southeast Asian Ministers of Education Organization (SEAMEO) in 2011. Using the UNESCO model of ICT Development in Education, the countries were ranked as Emerging, Applying, Infusing or Transforming. The Philippines (with Indonesia, Thailand, and Vietnam) were ranked at the Infusing stage of integrating ICT in education, indicating that the country has integrated ICT into existing teaching, learning and administrative practices and policies. This includes components such as a national vision of ICT in education, national ICT plans and policies, complementary national ICT and education policies, professional development for teachers and school leaders, community or partnership and teaching and learning pedagogies.[2] A 2012 study reported that public high schools in Metro Manila had a computer to student ratio of 1:63. While 88 percent of schools have internet connections, half of the students claimed not to be using it.[3]

26. unlocking the full potential of ICT for a better future​


Answer:

Unlocking the full potential of information and communication technology (ICT) can lead to a variety of benefits for society. Some of these benefits include:

Improving access to information and education: ICT can help bridge the digital divide and provide access to information and educational resources to people in remote or underserved areas.

Facilitating communication and collaboration: ICT can make it easier for people to communicate and collaborate with one another, regardless of their location.

Enhancing productivity and efficiency: ICT can help businesses and organizations improve their productivity and efficiency by automating tasks and streamlining processes.

Improving healthcare: ICT can be used to improve the delivery of healthcare services, such as telemedicine, which allows doctors and patients to communicate remotely.

Enabling innovation: ICT can be a powerful tool for innovation, as it allows people to easily share ideas and collaborate on projects.

Addressing societal challenges: ICT can be used to address a wide range of societal challenges, such as climate change, poverty, and inequality.

Overall, unlocking the full potential of ICT can lead to a better future for all by providing access to information and resources, fostering innovation and collaboration, and improving the way we live and work.
