1. beauty in nature essay moralist ​


Nature is a work of art that is constantly evolving and changing, and its beauty is unparalleled. From the majestic mountains to the vast oceans and from the intricate patterns of a butterfly's wings to the magnificent colors of a sunset, nature has a beauty that is both mesmerizing and humbling. As a moralist, I believe that this beauty carries with it important moral lessons and values that we can learn from.

First and foremost, the beauty of nature teaches us the importance of humility. As humans, we often have a tendency to believe that we are the center of the universe, and that everything revolves around us. However, when we immerse ourselves in the beauty of nature, we are reminded of just how small we are in comparison to the vastness of the natural world. This realization can be humbling and can teach us to be more respectful of the environment and the creatures that inhabit it.

Secondly, the beauty of nature teaches us the importance of perseverance. Nature is a constant reminder that even in the face of adversity, life goes on. From the delicate flowers that bloom in the cracks of concrete to the towering trees that withstand harsh winds and storms, nature teaches us that resilience and determination are essential traits for survival.

Lastly, the beauty of nature teaches us the importance of balance. The natural world is a delicate balance of ecosystems and cycles that work together to create a sustainable environment. When we disrupt this balance through pollution, deforestation, and other harmful practices, we threaten the very beauty that we seek to admire. Therefore, we must learn to respect and preserve the natural world so that future generations can also appreciate its beauty.

In conclusion, the beauty of nature is not just a sight to behold, but it also carries with it important moral lessons and values that we can learn from. As a moralist, I believe that we must strive to respect and preserve the natural world so that we can continue to learn from its beauty for generations to come.

3. essay of beauty in nature by michael popejoy​


Beauty in Nature

Our senses, which are the doors that open to the inner world, are profoundly affected by the beauty of the natural world, and this impact can range from feelings of awe, wonder, or amazement to the inability to believe that it really exists.  

When we consider the beauty of nature, the things that dazzle the senses come to mind, such as the prominence of a mountain, the size of the sea, or the growth of a flower's life.  Simply appreciating beautiful objects can often make us joyful, and this emotional or affective response on our part seems to be crucial to how we perceive beauty.

The majority of the time, it seems to me, the reason we find these things to be beautiful is not because of something else they might bring us, like a piece of furniture or a "delicacy" to be consumed, but rather because of the way that the forms of these things immediately hit us upon sight. One can even argue that the appreciation of beauty itself serves as a foundation for the value of nature—that is, that the beauty of nature makes it valuable.
