3. paper 1 sample paragraph that has CAUSE-EFFECT SIGNALS and another paragraph that has PROBLEM-SOLUTION SIGNALS. Words​


Answer:

Sure, here are two sample paragraphs for you:

Paragraph with Cause-Effect Signals:

Many people suffer from obesity, which is a growing concern in today's society. The main cause of obesity is a sedentary lifestyle and unhealthy eating habits. People who consume high-calorie food and do not engage in physical activity tend to gain weight, which can lead to obesity over time. This, in turn, leads to various health problems such as diabetes, cardiovascular diseases, and joint pain. Therefore, it is important to adopt healthy habits such as regular exercise and a balanced diet to prevent obesity and its associated health risks.

In this paragraph, the cause of obesity is a sedentary lifestyle and unhealthy eating habits, and the effect is various health problems. The cause-effect signals used in this paragraph are "main cause," "tend to gain weight," "which can lead to," "in turn, leads to," and "associated health risks."

Paragraph with Problem-Solution Signals:

Traffic congestion is a major problem in many cities around the world. One of the main reasons for this is the increasing number of cars on the road. To solve this problem, governments can take several measures. One solution is to improve public transportation systems, such as buses and trains, to encourage people to use them instead of driving their cars. Another solution is to introduce carpooling programs, which can reduce the number of cars on the road during rush hour. Additionally, cities can invest in bike-sharing programs and create bike lanes to promote alternative modes of transportation. By implementing these solutions, cities can reduce traffic congestion and improve the quality of life for their residents.

In this paragraph, the problem is traffic congestion, and the solutions are improving public transportation, introducing carpooling programs, and promoting alternative modes of transportation. The problem-solution signals used in this paragraph are "problem," "solutions," "one solution," "another solution," "additionally," and "by implementing these solutions."

4. Activity C. Study HabitsWrite a 3-5 paragraph essay focusing on your study habits (how you study) as you go for distance learning. Underline and label the kind of grammatical signals you have used in the composition.Use the following guide questions as you work on the task and consider the sample and the rubric below.- What problems do you usually encounter in distance learning?- What causes the problems that you have?- What solutions do you have so you may learn best despite not having a face-to-face class?- What are the different study habits that you have? What do you do to understand the topics even without the presence of your teacher?- How will you prove to the other students that your study habits are effective, and they must follow your example?


Answer:

TOPIC : STUDY HABITS OF A JUNIOR STUDENT.

Explanation:

As a junior high school student, I have encountered some problems when it comes to distance learning. One of the main problems is distractions. When I'm studying, my phone keeps buzzing, and my siblings or parents keep talking to me. It's hard to concentrate, and I tend to get sidetracked easily. Another issue is the lack of motivation. Sometimes, I don't feel like studying, and I procrastinate until it's too late.

The problems that I have in distance learning are caused by the environment around me. Since I'm at home, there are a lot of things that distract me. My siblings are also studying from home, so it can get noisy and chaotic at times. It's also hard to maintain a routine since I don't have a strict schedule like in school.

To overcome these problems, I have come up with some solutions. First, I try to create a conducive environment for studying. I make sure that I'm in a quiet room, and I turn off my phone or put it on silent mode. I also try to study during hours when my siblings are not too busy with their online classes. Second, I try to motivate myself by setting goals and rewards. For example, if I finish studying for two hours straight, I reward myself with a snack or a short break.

My study habits involve a balance between studying and other activities. I make sure that I have enough time for my hobbies and interests, so I don't get burnt out. I also try to take breaks in between study sessions to rest my mind and recharge. When it comes to understanding topics, I read the materials thoroughly and take down notes. If there's something that I don't understand, I search for more information online or ask my teacher during our online class.

To prove that my study habits are effective, I would show my grades and how I was able to cope with the challenges of distance learning. I would also share my tips and tricks with my classmates and encourage them to try it out for themselves. Ultimately, the best way to prove that my study habits work is by consistently performing well in school and achieving my goals.
