4. let the student explain and describe the Tidal Theories and different Type of Tide 300 words


Tidal Theories and the Different Types of Tides

Tides are the increase and decrease of sea levels produced by the combined effects of the Moon's gravitational pulls, as well as the Earth and Moon circling each other. Below are some theories about the occurrence of tides:

Equilibrium Theory – This theory was developed by Sir Isaac Newton in 1687. In this theory, Newton assumed that there is an equal amount of ocean on the surface of the earth. Then he showed that the attractive force of the moon on the earth’s side that is near the moon is greater than the force on the opposite side of the earth, therefore the water would move away from the moon. This theory explains the occurrence of semi-diurnal tides but fails in explaining the time of occurrence of high water at a certain place.

Dynamic Theory of Tides – This was theorized by Laplace back in 1775. The tide waves in the ocean are produced by tide-producing forces that happen over a certain period. This theory used the effect of ocean depth and width, the earth’s rotation, and friction on fluids subjected to rhythmic forces in understanding the tides.

Types of Tides

Diurnal tide – This tide occurs when there is one episode of high and low water in a day. This type of tide usually happens in some places when the moon is positioned far from the equator, an example would be the Gulf of Mexico’s coast. Semi-diurnal Tide – This is the most common type of tide. This tide occurs when two high water and two low waters occur in a day and both rise and fall to the same level. This usually happens when the moon is directly above the equator and is very common along the Atlantic Coast.Mixed Tide – Just like the Semi-diurnal Tide, this tide has two episodes of high and low water in a day, however, they rise and fall at different levels. This tide happens when the moon is at the farthest from the equator either on the north or south side. This is usually seen along the coast of the Pacific.

Narrative or Traditional literature reviews

Narrative or Traditional literature reviews critique and summarise a body of literature about the thesis topic. The literature is researched from the relevant databases and is generally very selective in the material used. The criteria for literature selection for a narrative review is not always made open to the reader. These reviews are very useful in gathering and synthesising the literature located. Answer:

1. Educate all farm workers about the dangers of chemicals. Educating yourself and your farmworkers about the repercussions of poorly handling hazardous chemicals promotes a safer work environment by helping workers avoid injury.

For instance, some farm chemicals contain anhydrous ammonia. The compound doesn’t contain water, so when anhydrous ammonia contacts moisture, it rapidly combines with it.

This is great for crops and plants, as it quickly combines with the soil’s moisture to promote growth. But, if it comes into contact with our eyes, skin or mucous membranes, it can cause rapid dehydration and severe burns.

It’s also important to make sure all hazardous chemicals are labeled so workers know what they are dealing with. You should have Material Safety Data Sheets readily available and constantly reviewed.

2. Wear personal protective equipment. To avoid exposure to hazardous chemicals, wear chemical-proof goggles, rubber gloves, and a heavy-duty long-sleeved shirt. Certain chemicals may also call for a respirator.

3. Provide clean water for first aid. If a farmer comes into contact with a chemical, first aid must be administered immediately. Plenty of water should be on hand at all times to flush out the chemical after a person has come into contact with it.

All chemical nurse and applicator tanks should contain at least one five-gallon container of clean water, which should be changed out daily to ensure a fresh supply.

4. Store and handle chemicals properly. For instance, because anhydrous ammonia boils at minus 28 degrees Fahrenheit, it must be stored properly to maintain its liquidity and handled under high pressure with specially designed and well-maintained equipment.

All equipment should meet the appropriate design guidelines by the American National Standards Institute.

Keep equipment in good condition by maintaining an annual safety checklist, like this one, provided by your equipment supplier.

5. Observe proper procedures when transferring chemicals. Most accidents happen while transferring chemicals.

That’s why it’s critical to wear all proper protective equipment, and to make sure all farm workers are thoroughly familiar with the machinery and equipment necessary for transfer.
