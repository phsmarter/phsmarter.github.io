13. 1. how has globalization impacted the role and practice of religion in contemporary society?​


CONTEMPORARY SOCIETY

However, I can provide information that globalization has had a significant impact on the role and practice of religion in contemporary society. Here are some of the ways:

1. Increased religious diversity: Globalization has led to increased religious diversity due to the movement of people from one country to another. This phenomenon has resulted in the mixing and mingling of various religious practices, beliefs, and traditions.

2. Emergence of new religious movements: Globalization has provided the platform for the emergence of new religious movements. The internet, social media, and accessibility to information have played a vital role in the spread of new religious practices.

3. Religious tourism: Globalization has led to an increase in religious tourism. People travel to different parts of the world to experience different religious traditions and practices.

4. Religious conflicts: Globalization has also led to an increase in religious conflicts. The cultural clashes between religions, ethnic groups, and societies can lead to religious intolerance and violence.

5. Influence of secular culture: Globalization has promoted secularization, leading to a decline in traditional religious practices. Many people have turned to secularism, and this has impacted the practice of religion in contemporary society.

In conclusion, globalization has had a significant impact on the role and practice of religion in contemporary society. It has led to increased religious diversity, the emergence of new religious movements, religious tourism, religious conflicts, and the influence of secular culture.

29. How prominent are the roles of the monks and the temple in the dory? What does it sas about religion in Thai society? What parallelism does it have with religion in the Philippines?


Answer:

-How prominent are the roles of the monks and the temple in the dory?

•The roles of the monks and the temple are very prominent in the dory. The monks are responsible for conducting the traditional dory ceremony, which involves singing, chanting, and praying to the gods of the sea. The temple is the primary place of worship for the fishermen, and is where the dory rituals are performed. Additionally, the temple is a place of spiritual guidance for the fishermen, providing a sense of comfort and hope during hard times

-What does it says about religion in Thai society?

•Monks and temples play an important role in Thai society and religion. They are highly respected and revered by the people. In many ways, the monk serves as a spiritual and moral guide to the people of Thailand. Monks are often seen as the source of traditional values and represent wisdom, stability, and discipline. Temples are sacred places for many Thai people, representing a place of spiritual refuge, contemplation and peace. Monks offer religious services such as counselling, teaching meditation and providing spiritual guidance. Temples are often at the centre of local village life, where people come to perform rituals, receive spiritual guidance and learn about Buddhism. Visiting temples and making offerings is seen as a sign of respect to the monks and the Buddhas. In Thailand, religion plays a fundamental role in day-to-day life and is seen as a crucial component of the cultural identity.

-What parallelism does it have with

religion in the Philippines?

•Parallelism between religion in the Philippines and other cultures around the world is often seen in the way that religious beliefs and practices overlap and permeate aspects of everyday life. Common threads in religious practices include a belief in a higher power or spirit, recognition of special days or holidays, and the active community-building and charitable efforts of many churches and religious organizations.
