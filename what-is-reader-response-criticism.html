1. What is reader response criticism​


Answer:

Reader-response criticism is a school of literary theory that focuses on the reader and their experience of a literary work, in contrast to other schools and theories that focus attention primarily on the author or the content and form of the work.

Answer:

Reader-response criticism

Reader-response criticism is a school of literary theory that focuses on the reader and their experience of a literary work, in contrast to other schools and theories that focus attention primarily on the author or the content and form of the work.


2. What is reader-response criticism


Answer: Reader-response criticism focuses on the important role of readers' reactions in understanding and interpreting the meaning of a literary piece.

Explanation: The reader-response theorist believe that in understanding literature, we must not disregard the role of the readers.

8. What is the Reader's Response Critics to the song When I Met You by Ako Hiking Society. ​


Answer:

Reader's Response Criticism is an approach to literary criticism that emphasizes the individual reader's interpretation and emotional response to a text. Therefore, the response to the song "When I Met You" by Ako Hiking Society would differ from person to person, depending on their personal experiences, emotions, and cultural background. However, the song is generally seen as a heartfelt expression of love, with its simple yet powerful lyrics and melody resonating with many listeners. It is widely admired for its sincerity, emotional depth, and enduring popularity.

what are the distinct features of new historicist and reader response criticism

ANSWER:

New Historicism is an approach to literary criticism and literary theory based on the premise that a literary work should be considered a product of the time, place, and historical circumstances of its composition rather than as an isolated work of art or text.
