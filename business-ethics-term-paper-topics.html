6. Reaction of Ethical Issue In Business Ethics​


Explanation:

Ethical issues in business ethics are always a concern for society, as they can have a significant impact on individuals, communities, and the environment. When businesses prioritize profits over ethical behavior, it can result in exploitation, discrimination, environmental damage, and other negative outcomes. On the other hand, when businesses prioritize ethical principles, their actions can have a positive impact on society as a whole.

It is therefore important for businesses to consider the ethical implications of their decisions and actions, and to take steps to minimize harm and promote social responsibility. This can involve making ethical codes of conduct, ensuring compliance with laws and regulations, promoting transparency and accountability, and actively engaging with stakeholders to understand and address their concerns. Ultimately, prioritizing ethical behavior is not only the right thing to do but can also lead to long-term business success and sustainability.

20. explain the difference of business law and business ethics​


Answer:

Business law determines the minimum standards of behavior expected of companies and sole proprietorships. Business ethics go beyond simple legality. They describe the way a business should behave – how a business does what it is legally obligated to do.

21. Activity II TERMS OF BUSINESS. Give your own definition on the following words. (5pts each) 1. Virtue Ethics 2. Reciprocity Ethics 3. Dialectic Method​


Answer:

1. Virtue Ethics: An ethical theory that emphasizes the development of good character traits or virtues, such as honesty, courage, and compassion, to guide moral decision-making and behavior.

2. Reciprocity Ethics: An ethical theory that emphasizes treating others as we would like to be treated, based on the principle of reciprocity. This involves a mutual exchange of benefits or harms between individuals or groups, guided by a sense of fairness and justice.

3. Dialectic Method: A method of philosophical inquiry or debate that involves the exploration and resolution of opposing viewpoints or ideas through dialogue, argumentation, and critical thinking. It is a process of reasoning and analysis that seeks to establish truth through the clash of ideas and the synthesis of conflicting perspectives.

26. principles of business ethics


Business ethics refers to a set of moral principles and values that guide the behavior and decision-making of individuals and organizations in the business world. It involves treating all stakeholders fairly and with respect, such as employees, customers, suppliers, and the community. Honesty, integrity, and accountability are also important aspects of business ethics. Adhering to ethical principles can help build trust, reputation, and long-term success for businesses.
