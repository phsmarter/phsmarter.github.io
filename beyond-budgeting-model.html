1. The budget for 'baon' (b) is greater than budget for school supplies (s).? Inequality or not and what is the inequality model?​


INEQUALITYNAMING VERBAL INTO MATHEMATICAL INEQUALITY

Answer:

It is an inequality and the inequality model is b > s.

Step-by-step explanation:

The budget for 'baon' expressed as variable 'b' while the budget for school supplies expressed as variable 's'. The sign > is used when it is 'greater than'.

So the inequality would be like this:

b > s

Therefore, the inequality model is b > s.

When is an inequality sign can be used?

The inequality signs are:

< - This is 'less than' sign. It is used to compare when the first subject is smaller than the second one. It can be used when it expressed 'less than', 'fewer than' or 'smaller than'.> - This is 'greater than' sign. It is used to compare when the first subject is greater than the other one. It can be used when an expressions contain the phrases 'greater than' or 'more than' or the word 'exceeds'.≤ - This is 'less than or equal to' sign. It is also used to compare the two subjects if it is either 'less than' or 'equal to'. The expressions used in this mathematical sign are containing 'less than or equal to', 'at most', 'no more than', ' does not exceed' or 'a minimum of'.≥ - This is 'greater than or equal to' sign. It is used in comparing two subjects if it is either 'greater than' or 'equal to'. It expressed as 'at least', 'no less than', 'a maximum of' or including the phrase of itself.

Overtime rate beyond 8 hours: 1.5 (31.25)

Number of hours: x

Total earnings: f(x)

f(x) = 250 + 1.5 (31.25) (x - 8)
