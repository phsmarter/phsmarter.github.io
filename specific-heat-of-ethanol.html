Then, he heated a 42.31 mL of ethanol (C2H5OH) to 106.9°C. After the alcohol cools, the observed final temperature of ethanol and water is 18.7°C. What is the specific heat capacity of ethanol? The specific heat capacities of sunflower oil and coconut oil are 2.302 ∙℃ and 2.100 ∙℃, respectively. Answer:

1. To determine the specific heat capacity of ethanol, we can use the principle of energy conservation.

The heat lost by the ethanol is equal to the heat gained by the water in the calorimeter.

The heat lost by the ethanol can be calculated using the formula:

Q_ethanol = m_ethanol * c_ethanol * ΔT_ethanol

where:

- Q_ethanol is the heat lost by ethanol (in joules)

- m_ethanol is the mass of ethanol (in grams)

- c_ethanol is the specific heat capacity of ethanol (in J/g°C)

- ΔT_ethanol is the change in temperature of ethanol (in °C)

The heat gained by the water can be calculated using the formula:

Q_water = m_water * c_water * ΔT_water

where:

- Q_water is the heat gained by water (in joules)

- m_water is the mass of water (in grams)

- c_water is the specific heat capacity of water (in J/g°C)

- ΔT_water is the change in temperature of water (in °C)

Since all the heat released by ethanol is absorbed by water, Q_ethanol is equal to -Q_water.

Using the given values:

- m_ethanol = 42.31 mL * 0.789 g/mL = 33.37459 g

- c_water = 4.184 J/g°C (specific heat capacity of water)

- ΔT_water = 18.7°C - 18.7°C = 0°C (change in temperature of water)

- ΔT_ethanol = 106.9°C - 18.7°C = 88.2°C (change in temperature of ethanol)

Q_ethanol = -Q_water

m_ethanol * c_ethanol * ΔT_ethanol = -m_water * c_water * ΔT_water

Substituting the known values:

33.37459 g * c_ethanol * 88.2°C = -182 mL * 1 g/mL * 4.184 J/g°C * 0°C

Simplifying:

c_ethanol = (-182 mL * 1 g/mL * 4.184 J/g°C * 0°C) / (33.37459 g * 88.2°C)

c_ethanol ≈ -0.214 J/g°C

Therefore, the specific heat capacity of ethanol, based on the given experimental data, is approximately -0.214 J/g°C. Note that the negative sign indicates an exothermic reaction, where heat is released.

2. To determine the final temperature of the sunflower oil, coconut oil, and water mixture, we can use the principle of energy conservation.

The heat lost by the heated oils is equal to the heat gained by the water in the calorimeter.

The heat lost by the sunflower oil can be calculated using the formula:

- c_coconut is the specific heat capacity of coconut oil (in J/g°C)

The heat gained by the water can be calculated using the formula:

The specific heat capacities of sunflower oil and coconut oil are 2.302 ∙℃ and 2.100 ∙℃, respectively. The density of sunflower oil is 0.929 g/mL, while 0.926 g/mL for coconut oil.​


Answer:

1. 1.87 J/g°C specific heat of ethanol

2. Trying to solve

Explanation:

1. First, we need to calculate the amount of heat released by the ethanol (q):

q = mCΔT

where m is the mass of the ethanol, C is the specific heat capacity of ethanol, and ΔT is the change in temperature of the ethanol.

The mass of ethanol can be calculated using its density:

mass of ethanol = volume of ethanol x density = 42.31 mL x 0.789 g/mL = 33.41 g

ΔT = final temperature - initial temperature = 18.7°C - 106.9°C = -88.2°C (note that we use a negative value because the temperature of the ethanol decreased)

Now we can calculate the amount of heat released by the ethanol:

q = 33.41 g x C x (-88.2°C) = -2,946.5 g°C

Next, we need to calculate the amount of heat absorbed by the water (q):

q = mCΔT

where m is the mass of water, C is the specific heat capacity of water, and ΔT is the change in temperature of the water.

The mass of water can be calculated using its density:

mass of water = volume of water x density = 182 mL x 1 g/mL = 182 g

ΔT = final temperature - initial temperature = 18.7°C - 18.7°C = 0°C

Now we can calculate the amount of heat absorbed by the water: Specific heat of water is 4.184 J/g°C

q = 182 g x 4.184 J/g°C x 0°C = 0 J

Since the calorimeter is perfect and all the heat released by the ethanol is absorbed by the water, we can equate the two values of q:

-2,946.5 g°C = 0 J

We can solve for C:

C = -2,946.5 g°C / (33.41 g x -88.2°C)

C = 1.87 J/g°C

Therefore, the specific heat capacity of ethanol is 1.87 J/g°C.

17. Ethanol, C2H5OH, is used to introduce oxygen into some blends of gasoline • It has a heat of combustion of 1366.8 kJ/mol • What is the heat of formation of ethanol?​


Answer:

The heat of formation, ΔHf, is the enthalpy change associated with the formation of one mole of a compound from its elements in their standard states.

The balanced chemical equation for the combustion of ethanol is:

C2H5OH(l) + 3O2(g) → 2CO2(g) + 3H2O(l)

The standard heat of formation of a compound is defined as the enthalpy change for the reaction in which the compound is formed from its elements in their standard states, with all reactants and products at a pressure of 1 atm and a temperature of 25°C (298 K).

The standard heat of formation of the elements in their standard states is zero. Therefore, the standard heat of formation of ethanol, ΔHf(C2H5OH), can be calculated using the standard heat of formation values of carbon dioxide, water, and oxygen, which are -393.5 kJ/mol, -285.8 kJ/mol, and 0 kJ/mol, respectively.

ΔHf(C2H5OH) = ΣnΔHf(products) - ΣnΔHf(reactants)

where n is the stoichiometric coefficient of each substance in the balanced chemical equation.

ΔHf(C2H5OH) = [2ΔHf(CO2) + 3ΔHf(H2O)] - [ΔHf(C2H5OH) + 3ΔHf(O2)]

Substituting the values:

ΔHf(C2H5OH) = [2(-393.5 kJ/mol) + 3(-285.8 kJ/mol)] - [1366.8 kJ/mol + 3(0 kJ/mol)]

ΔHf(C2H5OH) = -104.7 kJ/mol

Therefore, the heat of formation of ethanol is -104.7 kJ/mol.
