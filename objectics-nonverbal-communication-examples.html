NON - VERBAL

There are many kinds of nonverbal communication, which include the following:

Expressions of the face. The human face is very expressive, capable of conveying many emotions without speaking a word. And, in contrast to specific other nonverbal modes of communication, facial expressions are universal. Happiness, sorrow, rage, surprise, fear, and contempt all have universal facial expressions.

Movement and posture of the body. Consider how the way individuals sit, move, stand, or hold their heads affects your impressions of them. Your movement and posture convey a lot of information to the world. This nonverbal communication style encompasses your posture, bearing, stance, and subtle motions.

Gestures. Our everyday lives are intertwined with gestures. When disputing or speaking animatedly, you may wave, point, call or use your hands, often expressing yourself without thinking. However, the meaning of some gestures varies significantly between cultures. While the hand gesture for "OK" communicates a good message in English-speaking nations, it is deemed insulting in Germany, Russia, and Brazil. As a result, it is critical to use caution while using gestures to prevent misunderstanding.

Maintain eye contact. Due to the fact that the visual sense is dominant in the majority of individuals, eye contact is a particularly significant kind of nonverbal communication. Your gaze may convey a variety of messages, including curiosity, love, anger, or desire. Sustaining eye contact is also critical for maintaining the conversation's flow and assessing the other person's attention and reaction.

Touch. Through touch, we communicate a tremendous lot. Consider the contrasting signals sent by a feeble handshake, a loving bear hug, a condescending pat on the head, or a dominating grasp on the arm.

Space. Have you ever felt uneasy during a discussion because the other person stood too near and encroached on your personal space? We all need physical space, but that need varies according to culture, circumstance, and the degree of intimacy in the connection. Physical space may be used to convey a variety of nonverbal messages, including indications of closeness and love, as well as aggressiveness and authority.

10. Differentiate verbal communication from nonverbal communication. Give evidences, examples and supporting sentences.​


Answer:

The intended message is transferred to people non-verbally using

proxemics (distance), kinesics (body language), haptics (touches), appearance eye contact, etc.

And verbally using words, which includes

speaking writing detonation (dictionary meaning) or connotation (feelings associated with meaning of words) tone and volume

Verbal communication

Verbal communication is auditory communication with words. It is mostly face-to-face or written with the use of language as a means. Nowadays, technologies like phone and internet have allowed oral communication to take place without being in the same place or writing.  Here, tone and volume of the spoken words or sound matter the most along with the words. Similarly, detonation and connotation are other factors that sends intended meaning of message. So, choosing right words according to situations is important.

Non-verbal communication

Wordless communications fall under non-verbal communication. It is conveyed as visual cues. Body language, gestures, facial expressions, touch, etc. are few examples of non-verbal communication.  It is noticed and interpreted more than words. It also communicates more messages.

It is used to interpret whether verbal communication is true and authentic,   or not.  Non- verbal communication can be different according to place, culture and individual differences. There is no specific interpretation, rather the interpretations are open.

Similarities between verbal and non-verbal communication

Verbal and non-verbal communications are not contradictory in their uses. They go side by side. Combination of verbal and non-verbal communication make communication effective. In most cases, one doesn’t exist without the other. For example, laughing at a joke and saying it is funny.

According to a researcher Wertheim, non-verbal communication plays different roles in communication. It can play one of these five roles: reinforcement, substitution, contradiction, accentuation and regulation.

Both forms of communications are learned and are not innate in most cases. There are some innate verbal or non-verbal communications like crying, groaning in pain, etc. Except those, people learn most of non-verbal communications like that of table manners and so on.

30. Using Effective and Appropriate Nonverbal communication Strategies: Example plss need 10 Example thanks​


During your next face-to-face conversation about mental health, keep these tips in mind:

Pay close attention to what the person says. Really listening to what the person is telling you is crucial to being a helpful listener. When you reply, it can be helpful to repeat some of what they told you, using the same language. This attention to detail demonstrates that you care.Maintain comfortable eye contact. Don’t avoid eye contact, but do avoid staring. It’s important to meet someone’s gaze. It shows you are interested and that your focus is on them – quite literally!Maintain an open body position. Avoid crossing your arms over your body – it may appear defensive. When your body position is open, it conveys that you are open to listening.Sit down, even if the person is standing. Being on the same level as someone appears less threatening and can make them feel more comfortable, while avoiding feelings of tension or nervousness when having personal conversations.Sit alongside and angled toward the person rather than directly opposite them. This allows the conversation to feel friendly and nonconfrontational. No one wants to feel like they’re being interviewed.Avoid fidgeting. During a sensitive conversation, it may be tempting to fidget, but it can be distracting to the person who is talking. It could also make it appear that you are uncomfortable, nervous or bored.
