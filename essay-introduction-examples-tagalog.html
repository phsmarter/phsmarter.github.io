4. examples of argumentative essay introduction,body and conclusionpasagot po​


Sure, I can provide you with an example of an argumentative essay structure, which typically consists of an introduction, body paragraphs, and a conclusion.

Introduction: The introduction of an argumentative essay should grab the reader's attention, provide background information, and present the thesis statement. Here's an example:

Topic: Should the government ban plastic bags?

"Plastic bags have become a ubiquitous part of our daily lives. However, they also have devastating effects on the environment. In recent years, there has been growing concern about the negative impact of plastic bags on our planet. Some people argue that the government should ban plastic bags to protect the environment, while others believe that this is an unnecessary interference in people's lives. In this essay, I will argue that the government should ban plastic bags to reduce the damage to our environment."

Body paragraphs: The body paragraphs of an argumentative essay provide evidence and support for the thesis statement. Each paragraph should focus on one main point and provide evidence to support that point. Here's an example of a body paragraph:

"Plastic bags are not biodegradable and can take hundreds of years to decompose. They also pose a serious threat to wildlife. Animals often mistake plastic bags for food and can suffocate or choke on them. According to the Ocean Conservancy, plastic bags are one of the most common items found in ocean debris, which has a devastating impact on marine life. Therefore, banning plastic bags would help reduce the amount of plastic waste in our environment and protect wildlife."

Conclusion: The conclusion of an argumentative essay should restate the thesis statement and summarize the main points of the essay. It should also provide a final thought or call to action. Here's an example of a conclusion:

"In conclusion, plastic bags have a detrimental impact on our environment and wildlife. By banning plastic bags, we can reduce the amount of plastic waste in our environment and protect wildlife. The government should take action to ban plastic bags and encourage people to use reusable bags instead. It's time for us to take responsibility for our planet and make a positive impact on the environment."

Certainly! This is an illustration of a strong argumentative essay on the subject of outlawing plastic bags:

Topic: Plastic bag ban

Introduction:
In spite of their widespread use and apparent convenience for carrying groceries or other items, plastic bags have a major negative impact on the environment. Billions of plastic bags pollute our oceans and damage wildlife every year by ending up in landfills or as public litter. We must take steps to lessen our dependence on single-use plastics in order to safeguard our planet and keep it intact for future generations. Bans on plastic bags are an important first move.

Body:
There are several strong arguments in favor of outlawing plastic bags. They are, first and foremost, a significant cause of pollution. Plastic bags do not biodegrade, so they will either wind up in our oceans where they can harm or destroy marine life, or they will sit in landfills for hundreds of years. Second, the manufacture of plastic bags uses a lot of fossil fuels, which increases greenhouse gas pollution and the effects of climate change. Third, plastic bags are frequently only used once before being tossed away, wasting precious resources and adding to the issue of overconsumption as a whole.

The prohibition on plastic bags does not, however, leave us without any other choices. Instead, use reusable bags made of fabric, canvas, or other materials. These bags are more durable than plastic bags and can be used repeatedly to cut down on trash and conserve resources. In reality, a lot of supermarkets and shops already give customers who bring their own bags discounts or other rewards.

Studies have shown that the long-term advantages of banning plastic bags far outweigh any short-term expenses, despite claims to the contrary. There has been a substantial decrease in plastic waste and litter in areas where plastic bag bans have been enacted, such as California and Hawaii. Additionally, companies have come up with inventive methods to comply with the ban, like selling reusable bags or switching to paper bags.

Conclusion:
In conclusion, outlawing plastic bags is a crucial move we can take to safeguard the environment and lessen our environmental impact. While changing our habits may require some work, the advantages of lowering plastic pollution and protecting our natural resources make it worthwhile. Reusable bag use and backing of plastic bag bans are two ways we can help the earth and leave a better world for future generations.
