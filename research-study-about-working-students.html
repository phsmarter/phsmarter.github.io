Ethical guidelines for research - Norms support the objectives of research, including knowledge, accuracy, and mistake prevention.

      By adhering to ethical standards, you can make sure that your study is accurate and authentic and increase your reputation and public support. Also, while presenting your findings in your publication, you must follow ethical standards.

Safeguarding human subjects:

Respect human dignity, privacy, and autonomy when doing research on living beings; limit risks and damages, and maximize benefits.

Courtesy for privacy:

Relationships between the rights to beneficence, respect for dignity, and loyalty and the question of secrecy and anonymity are intricate. Because personal comments cannot be connected to the identify of the subject, anonymity is safeguarded. If the researcher is unable to guarantee anonymity, he must deal with confidentiality, which is the handling of personal data by the researcher in order to safeguard the subject's identity.

Confidentiality:

As well as health information, commercial or military secrets, articles or grants that have been submitted for publication or that have been awarded confidentially should also be protected.

Colleagues should be respected:

You should respect and be fair to your coworkers.

Non-Discrimination:

Keep your coworkers from being treated unfairly
