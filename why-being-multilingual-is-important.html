2. why is it important to look into challenges in Mother tounge -Multilingual implementation?​


Answer:

It is important to look into challenges in mother tongue-multilingual implementation because it can have a significant impact on a child's educational outcome and overall success in life. Children who are taught in their mother tongue are more likely to have better literacy and numeracy skills, which can lead to better academic performance and greater opportunities for economic and social advancement. Additionally, preserving and promoting mother tongue languages can help to maintain cultural heritage and identity. Therefore, addressing the challenges in implementing mother tongue-multilingual education can lead to improved education and better life outcomes for children and communities.

There are several reasons why it is important to look into challenges in mother tongue-multilingual implementation. First, research has shown that students perform better academically when instruction is provided in their mother tongue. This is because they are more likely to understand and retain the material, which leads to better academic outcomes. Additionally, using the mother tongue as the primary language of instruction can help to preserve linguistic and cultural diversity. Furthermore, it can help to promote social inclusion and reduce the marginalization of certain linguistic groups. It also has been observed that when students are taught in their mother tongue, it creates a more positive and welcoming learning environment which in turn leads to better engagement and participation from students.
