Title: The Role of Inquiries, Investigation, and Immersion in Understanding the Educational Needs of Marginalized Students

Abstract:

This research paper explores the role of inquiries, investigation, and immersion in understanding the educational needs of marginalized students. The study focuses on the experiences of students from low-income families, immigrant families, and students with disabilities. The research methodology employed was qualitative, using interviews, observations, and document analysis to gather data. The study found that inquiries, investigation, and immersion are effective tools in understanding the educational needs of marginalized students. By engaging in these methods, researchers were able to gain a deeper understanding of the students' experiences, challenges, and strengths. The study also highlights the importance of cultural responsiveness in inquiry-based research and the need for ongoing collaboration between researchers and community stakeholders.

Introduction:

In recent years, there has been growing interest in the use of inquiries, investigation, and immersion in educational research. These methods involve engaging with the community, observing and documenting the experiences of individuals, and gathering data through interviews, surveys, and focus groups. The purpose of this research paper is to explore the role of inquiries, investigation, and immersion in understanding the educational needs of marginalized students. Specifically, the study focuses on students from low-income families, immigrant families, and students with disabilities. The research methodology employed was qualitative, using interviews, observations, and document analysis to gather data.

Literature Review:

The literature on inquiries, investigation, and immersion in educational research highlights the importance of community engagement and cultural responsiveness. These methods involve working closely with community stakeholders to gain a deeper understanding of the experiences, challenges, and strengths of marginalized students. Studies have shown that inquiries and investigation can lead to a more nuanced understanding of the educational needs of marginalized students and can inform the development of culturally responsive teaching practices (Banks, 2006; Gay, 2010). Immersion, on the other hand, involves spending time in the community and observing the experiences of individuals firsthand. This method has been shown to be particularly effective in understanding the experiences of immigrant and refugee students (Suarez-Orozco et al., 2010).

Methodology:

The study employed a qualitative research methodology, using inquiries, investigation, and immersion to gather data. The research was conducted in a school district with a high percentage of students from low-income families, immigrant families, and students with disabilities. The researchers conducted interviews with students, parents, teachers, and community stakeholders to gain a deeper understanding of the experiences of marginalized students. They also observed classrooms and school activities to document the experiences of students and teachers. In addition, the researchers analyzed documents such as school policies and curriculum materials to gain a broader understanding of the educational context.

Results:

The study found that inquiries, investigation, and immersion were effective tools in understanding the educational needs of marginalized students. By engaging in these methods, researchers were able to gain a deeper understanding of the students' experiences, challenges, and strengths. For example, through interviews with students, researchers were able to gain insights into the challenges faced by students from immigrant families, such as language barriers and cultural differences. Through observations of classrooms, researchers were able to identify areas where teachers could improve their cultural responsiveness.

Conclusion:

The study highlights the importance of inquiries, investigation, and immersion in understanding the educational needs of marginalized students. These methods are particularly effective in gaining a deeper understanding of the experiences, challenges, and strengths of students from low-income families, immigrant families, and students with disabilities. The study also underscores the importance of cultural responsiveness in inquiry-based research and the need for ongoing collaboration between researchers and community stakeholders. By engaging in these methods, researchers can develop more effective interventions and teaching practices that are responsive to the needs of marginalized students.

Introduction

Agriculture is an important sector in the economy of many countries around the world. It plays a crucial role in providing food, fiber, and fuel for the population. However, the sector faces numerous challenges such as climate change, soil degradation, and water scarcity, among others. To address these challenges, it is important to conduct research on different aspects of agriculture and identify innovative solutions to enhance productivity, sustainability, and resilience. In this paper, we will discuss the concepts of inquiries, investigations, and immersion in agricultural research and provide an example of how these concepts can be applied in a research project.

Inquiries

Inquiries refer to the process of asking questions and seeking answers to understand a phenomenon or a problem. Inquiries involve critical thinking, analysis, and synthesis of information to generate new knowledge or insights. In agricultural research, inquiries can be used to identify research gaps, formulate research questions, and develop hypotheses. For example, an inquiry-based approach can be used to investigate the factors that affect crop yields in a particular region. The researcher can ask questions such as: What is the current state of agriculture in the region? What are the main challenges facing farmers in the region? What are the factors that contribute to low crop yields? By answering these questions, the researcher can identify the key variables that need to be investigated further.

Investigations

Investigations refer to the process of collecting and analyzing data to test hypotheses or answer research questions. Investigations involve the use of different research methods such as surveys, interviews, experiments, and observations. In agricultural research, investigations can be used to collect data on different aspects of agriculture such as soil quality, crop management practices, and pest control methods. For example, an investigation can be conducted to determine the effectiveness of different irrigation methods in improving crop yields. The researcher can collect data on the amount of water used, the frequency of irrigation, and the quality of the soil to determine the impact of different irrigation methods on crop yields.

Immersion

Immersion refers to the process of engaging in a particular activity or environment to gain a deeper understanding of a phenomenon. Immersion involves observation, participation, and reflection on the experience. In agricultural research, immersion can be used to gain insights into the daily activities of farmers and understand their perspectives on different issues. For example, a researcher can immerse themselves in the life of a farmer for a week to understand their farming practices, challenges, and aspirations. By doing so, the researcher can gain a deeper understanding of the social, economic, and cultural factors that influence agricultural practices in the region.

Answer:

Research is a systematic investigative process employed to increase or revise current knowledge by discovering new facts. It can be divided into two general categories: (1) Basic research, which is inquiry aimed at increasing scientific knowledge, and (2) Applied research, which is an effort aimed at using basic research for solving problems or developing new processes, products, or techniques.

The first and most important step in any research is to identify and delineate the research problem: that is, what the researcher wants to solve and what questions he/she wishes to answer. A research problem may be defined as an area of concern, a gap in the existing knowledge, or a deviation in the norm or standard that points to the need for further understanding and investigation. Although many problems turn out to have several solutions (the means to close the gap or correct the deviation), difficulties arise where such means are either not obvious or are not immediately available. This then necessitates some research to reach a viable solution.

30. cluster random sampling or simple random sampling an inquiry of errors in writing academic text by home economics students at asia source icollege.​


Answer:

It depends on the specific research design and objectives of the study. Both cluster random sampling and simple random sampling have their own strengths and weaknesses, and the choice between the two will depend on the characteristics of the population being studied and the goals of the research.

Cluster random sampling is a sampling technique in which large groups or clusters of individuals are first identified, and then a random sample of these clusters is selected for further analysis. This method is useful when the population is geographically dispersed and it is difficult to obtain a complete list of all individuals. For example, if you are trying to survey home economics students at Asia Source iCollege, you could use cluster random sampling by first identifying the different departments within the college, and then randomly selecting a few departments to survey.

Simple random sampling, on the other hand, is a method in which every individual in the population has an equal chance of being selected. This method is useful when a complete list of individuals is available and the population is relatively homogeneous. For example, if you are trying to survey errors in writing academic text by home economics students at Asia Source iCollege, you could use simple random sampling by randomly selecting a certain number of students from the list of all home economics students at the college.

In this inquiry of errors in writing academic text by home economics students at Asia Source iCollege, Simple Random Sampling is the best method to use because we can have a complete list of students from home economics department and the population is relatively homogeneous.
