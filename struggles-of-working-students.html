30. struggles and challenges of FNb students​


Answer:

First-generation college students (FNb students) often face a variety of struggles and challenges as they navigate the higher education system. Some of the most common challenges include:

Lack of financial resources: Many FNb students come from low-income families and may not have access to the financial resources needed to pay for college.

Lack of academic preparation: FNb students may not have had access to the same level of academic resources and opportunities as their peers, which can make the transition to college more difficult.

Lack of social and emotional support: FNb students may not have family members or friends who can provide them with guidance and support as they navigate the college experience.

Difficulty in navigating the college bureaucracy: FNb students may be unfamiliar with the processes and procedures of higher education, making it challenging for them to access the resources and support they need.

cultural disconnection from majority students as well as from campus culture

Different expectations and academic culture that might be unfamiliar to them.

Limited understanding of career opportunities and lack of access to professional networks.

lack of mentorship and guidance on how to navigate the academic and professional world.

Overall, these challenges can make it difficult for FNb students to succeed in college, but many universities and organizations have developed programs and resources to support these students and help them overcome these obstacles.
