6. 5 example in each way to receive heat by Convection​


Answer:

boiling water - When water boils, the heat passes from the burner into the pot, heating the water at the bottom. This hot water rises and cooler water moves down to replace it, causing a circular motion.

radiator - A radiator puts warm air out at the top and draws in cooler air at the bottom.

steaming cup of hot tea - The steam you see when drinking a cup of hot tea indicates that heat is being transferred into the air.

ice melting - Ice melts because heat moves to the ice from the air. As a result, the ice melts from a solid to liquid.

7. what is an example of a receivable accounts for payment ​


Answer:

An example of a receivable account for payment is accounts receivable. Accounts receivable is the amount of money that a business is owed by its customers for goods or services that have been sold but not yet paid for. For example, if a company sells $10,000 worth of goods to a customer on credit, the customer owes the company $10,000. This $10,000 is recorded as an accounts receivable on the company's balance sheet. The company can then collect the money owed from the customer at a later date, either by receiving a check, bank transfer, or other means of payment. Once the payment is received, the accounts receivable balance is reduced by the amount of the payment received. Accounts receivable is an important asset for many businesses, as it represents money that is expected to be received in the future, and can be used to finance the company's operations or investments.

20. Example of receiving and sending chemical signals, where it found and what it does?​


Answer:

An example of receiving and sending chemical signals can be found in a process called quorum sensing, which is used by bacteria. Quorum sensing is a method of cell-cell communication in which a certain species of bacteria release and receive chemical signals. These signals allow the bacteria to detect the presence of other bacteria, sense their population density, and coordinate their activities. For example, when the bacteria reach a certain density, they can produce an antibiotic or enzyme that can be beneficial to them.
