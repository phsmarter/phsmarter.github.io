Answer:

Contemporary arts and traditional arts are both important aspects of a community's cultural heritage. In many communities around the world, traditional arts are still practiced and celebrated, while contemporary arts are also gaining popularity.

Traditional arts, such as folk music, dance, and crafts, have been passed down from generation to generation. They reflect the history, beliefs, and values of a community and are a way to keep cultural traditions alive. In some communities, traditional arts are still an important part of everyday life, such as in indigenous communities where traditional crafts are still used for daily necessities.

Contemporary arts, on the other hand, reflect the current trends, issues, and concerns of a community. They can take many forms, such as visual arts, performance arts, and digital arts. Contemporary arts are often seen as a way to express individuality and creativity, and they can also be used to create social change.

In many communities, there is a balance between traditional and contemporary arts. For example, a community may have a traditional festival that includes traditional music and dance performances, but also includes contemporary art installations and performances.

Overall, both traditional and contemporary arts are important to a community's cultural identity and should be celebrated and supported. They provide a way for individuals to express themselves and for communities to come together and celebrate their heritage.
