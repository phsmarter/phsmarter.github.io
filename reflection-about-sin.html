16. write a reflective essay about,“Every high priest is taken from among men and made their representative before God, to offer gifts and sacrifice for sins”. Hebrews 5:1​


Answer:

The fourth chapter ended with a depiction of Jesus' relationship with humanity. He can understand human temptation because of His whole humanity. As the rest of this passage explains, He is capable of acting as a legitimate high priest. This job necessitates the priest's ability to interact with both God and men. This includes making sin offerings.

Earlier parts of Hebrews explained that Messiah had to be a human creature, not just an angel (Hebrews 2:17). The topic discussed between the end of Chapter 4 and the beginning of Chapter 5 is one explanation for this. Messiah had to be human in order to genuinely represent humanity as a priest. He wouldn't be able to sympathize with our failings (Hebrews 4:15), operate as a mediator between God and men (1 Timothy 2:5), or be someone we could approach in trust if He didn't have humanity (Hebrews 4:16).
