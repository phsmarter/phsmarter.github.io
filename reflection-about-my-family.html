15. Answer the following questions based on the story "My Father Goes to Court"1. how is the poor man's family different from the rich man's family?2. what is ironic about the two families?3. is the rich neighbor's complaint valid? why?4. is the poor father's way of paying the rich man fair? why?5. how is social justice reflected in the story?​


1. A poor family is described as struggling financially and living a simple life, while a rich family is rich and lives in luxury.

2. The irony of the story is that, although the rich man's family is doing well financially, they are not happy and still feel the need to take advantage of the poor man's family. On the other hand, despite the economic difficulties, the poor man's family is content and happy.

3. The complaint of a wealthy neighbor who voluntarily lent money to his poor father and repaid it on time is not entirely justified. The only problem was that payments were made in small amounts, but the rich man did not set any repayment terms.

4. The way the poor father pays the rich is only because he paid on time and according to their covenant. It is unfair and unreasonable for a rich neighbor to demand full payment immediately, regardless of the poor person's financial situation.

5. Social justice is reflected in the story by describing the family of the poor man, who appears content and happy despite economic difficulties. The story emphasizes the importance of treating others fairly and not taking advantage of the underprivileged. It also emphasizes the need for empathy and understanding towards people in difficult situations.

Answer:

ACTIVE VOICE

Active voice is a grammatical voice common in many of the world's languages. It is the unmarked voice for clauses featuring a transitive verb in nominative–accusative languages, including English and most other Indo-European languages.

PASSIVE VOICE

A passive voice construction is a grammatical voice construction that is found in many languages. In a clause with passive voice, the grammatical subject expresses the theme or patient of the main verb – that is, the person or thing that undergoes the action or has its state changed.
