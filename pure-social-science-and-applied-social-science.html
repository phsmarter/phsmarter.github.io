16. The similarities and differences of Pure social and Applied Social Science​


Answer:

Social scientists conduct research on topics purely for their scientific and humanistic merits without needing any real-world application for their findings. A cultural anthropologist may study the kinship system in the Brazilian Amazon. A social psychologist may test people's faith in a just world hypothesis. Applied social scientists will do research to evaluate the potential uses of prior research. They discover how social scientific tools, data, and concepts can be used for practical, organizational, and societal needs, such as, for example, providing lawyers with tools to aid in the process of jury selection.
