11. who is Jose Rizal for you​


José Rizal to me is a national hero of the Philippines, a scholar and known as a fighter for the rights of the Filipino people who suffered the consequences of Spanish colonialism for approximately 300 years. He is a heroic figure who is very brave and has strong integrity.

EXPLANATION:

December 30th is a day of extraordinary tribute by the Filipino people to José Rizal. José Rizal is known as a Filipino national hero who was born in the 19th century. He is known as a fighter for the rights of the Filipino people who suffered as a result of Spanish colonialism for approximately 300 years. He is one of the most revered figures in Philippine history.

José Rizal is a man who has a variety of intellectual intelligence and a well-known political activist, because his political writings really inspired the Filipino people to create their nationalism, this caused him to be executed by the Spanish colonialists. He was one of the children of a very prominent family in the Philippines at that time. José Rizal is the seventh child of eleven children, his siblings have relatively high education. In his youth Rizal grew up as a very intelligent person, he was a very gifted student especially in the humanities and could master around 22 languages.

Long story short during his studies, Rizal finished his first novel entitled "Noli me tangere". In this book, Rizal tells about the social conditions in the Philippines as a result of Spanish colonialism. The Filipino people under Spanish colonialism experienced many injustices. This caused José Rizal to have political thoughts and inspired him to write the book "Noli me tangere" which is one of his most famous works. During his travels José Rizal created various works such as articles, newspapers, magazines to form political propaganda, he took advantage of the freer conditions in Europe to write about the suffering of the Filipino people due to Spanish colonialism.

At the same time, one of Rizal's most controversial works is El Filibusterismo which means pirate, the article was published in 1891, the contents of this work aroused hatred from the people of Spain. In writing this work, José Rizal was inspired by the state of terror perpetrated by the Spanish colonialists against the Philippines. Filibustersimo is Rizal's work which is widely read, shared, and discussed secretly in the Philippines which ultimately creates a sense of patriotism within the Filipino people.

How was the value of good education manifested in the life of Jose Rizal? > 3. What can you say about Jose Rizal relationship with his parents and siblings? ​


Of all the members of Jose Rizal's family, his mother, Teodora Alonso, had the most significant influence on his life. Teodora was a highly educated and intelligent woman who instilled in her children the importance of education and critical thinking. She was Rizal's first teacher and taught him to read and write at a very young age. She also exposed him to various intellectual and cultural influences that shaped his worldview and aspirations. For example, she encouraged Rizal's interest in literature and history and introduced him to the writings of great thinkers like Voltaire, Hugo, and Tennyson. Furthermore, Teodora's patriotism and courage in standing up against unjust authorities inspired Rizal's own commitment to fighting for social justice and national liberation.The value of good education was central to Jose Rizal's life and legacy. Rizal was a firm believer that education was the key to personal and societal development. He received a first-rate education from some of the best schools in the Philippines and Europe, which enabled him to become a skilled writer, linguist, scientist, and thinker. Rizal's education also exposed him to various intellectual and cultural influences, which broadened his horizons and shaped his worldview. Rizal believed that education should be accessible to all Filipinos, regardless of their social class or gender, and he used his writings and advocacy to promote this idea. He saw education as a tool for liberation and empowerment, which could help Filipinos break free from the colonial mindset and assert their identity and rights as a people.Jose Rizal had a close and loving relationship with his parents and siblings, despite the challenges they faced as a family. Rizal's parents, Francisco and Teodora, were both supportive of his educational pursuits and encouraged him to pursue his passions. Rizal was particularly close to his mother, who played a significant role in his intellectual and personal development. Rizal's siblings also played an important role in his life, with his older brother Paciano serving as a mentor and role model, and his sisters helping him with his studies and providing emotional support. However, the Rizal family also faced many hardships and injustices, such as the persecution of Paciano and Teodora by the Spanish authorities, which fueled Rizal's own commitment to social justice and reform. Overall, Rizal's relationship with his family was characterized by mutual respect, love, and support, which helped him overcome the many obstacles he faced in his life.
