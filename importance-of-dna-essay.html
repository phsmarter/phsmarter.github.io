During transcription, DNA is used as a template to make a molecule of messenger RNA (mRNA). The molecule of mRNA then leaves the nucleus and goes to a ribosome in the cytoplasm, where translation occurs. During translation, the genetic code in mRNA is read and used to make a protein.

30. Why is the DNA important?


Answer:

DNA is important because it contains all the genetic information that makes you, you. This information is necessary for their development and survival and can be passed on to the next generation. It also affects your traits with everything from your appearance to the food you like, with lots of things in between.

Given how different you are from someone else, it might seem like everyone's DNA must vary greatly between individuals. As incredible as it may seem, this is not the case. On average, you share about 99.5% of your DNA with someone you are not related to.

A big part of what makes you unique is in the 0.5% part of your DNA. And even though we are often more alike than different, everyone's DNA tells a different story about who their relatives are and where they come from.
