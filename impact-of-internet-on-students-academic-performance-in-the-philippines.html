1. The impact of sleep deprivation on academic performance of selected grade 11 students in holy rosary academy​


Answer:

Background: Sleep is an inseparable part of human health and life, which is crucial in learning, practice, as well as physical and mental health. It affects the capacity of individual learning, academic performance, and neural-behavioral functions. This study aimed to determine the relationship between sleep quality and students’ academic achievement among students at Kermanshah University of Medical Sciences.

Methods: In this cross-sectional study, 102 medical students from different fields, with maximum variation sampling, completed Pittsburgh Sleep Quality Index (PSQI). For data analysis, SPSS 19 was used through which Pearson correlation test, Spearman test, and t-test were employed.

Results: Based on the quality of sleep questionnaire scores, the results indicated no significant difference between students with high grades and those with low grades. However, there were moderate and sometimes severe sleep disturbances in both groups.

Conclusion: The results showed no significant difference between sleep quality and academic achievement. Nevertheless, longitudinal study should be performed to control for confounding factors.

15. The impact of gender discrimination on The academic performance if students​


There is a great deal of research that has been conducted on the impact of gender discrimination on the academic performance of students. The vast majority of this research has found that gender discrimination negatively impacts the academic performance of students. Some of the specific ways in which gender discrimination can impact the academic performance of students include :

Reducing the motivation of students to achieve in schoolIncreasing the likelihood of students feeling isolated and alone in the classroomLeading to students feeling less confident in their abilitiesPreventing students from taking advantage of opportunities to learn and grow

All of these impacts can have a serious negative effect on the academic performance of students. Gender discrimination is something that needs to be addressed in order to ensure that all students have an equal opportunity to succeed in school.

29. synthesis the Impact of Extracurricular Activities Participation on Students' Academic Performance​


Extracurricular activities, such as sports, clubs, music, and volunteering, can have a significant impact on students' academic performance. Research shows that students who participate in extracurricular activities generally perform better academically than those who do not.

One reason for this is that extracurricular activities can help students develop important skills, such as time management, organization, and teamwork. These skills are essential for academic success, as students who are able to manage their time effectively and stay organized are more likely to complete their assignments on time and perform well on exams.

Furthermore, extracurricular activities can also help students stay engaged and motivated in their academic work. When students are passionate about an activity, they are more likely to be interested in learning about related academic subjects. For example, a student who loves playing soccer may be more interested in learning about the history and culture of countries where soccer is popular.

Extracurricular activities can also provide students with opportunities to build relationships with peers and adults who share their interests. These relationships can be valuable sources of support and encouragement, which can help students stay motivated and engaged in their academic work.

However, it is important to note that the impact of extracurricular activities on academic performance can vary depending on a variety of factors, such as the type and frequency of activities, the student's level of commitment, and their individual learning style. Additionally, extracurricular activities should not come at the expense of academic responsibilities, and students should prioritize their studies above their extracurricular activities.

Overall, participation in extracurricular activities can have a positive impact on students' academic performance, as long as they are balanced with academic responsibilities and the student is able to effectively manage their time and commitments.
