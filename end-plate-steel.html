14. A machine gun is fired at a steel plate. is the average force on the plate from the bullet impact greater if the bullets bounce off or if they are squashed and stick to the plate?


Answer:

Bouncing or reflecting the bullet backwards would impart a greater force on the plate than if the bullet just flattened out and stuck to the plate. This is easily calculated using conversation or momentum combined with the Newton's third law and the fact that changes in momentum over time create force.

so: Force on steel plate= Derivative of bullets change in momentum over time.

When the bullet squashes and sticks the momentum of the bullet changes from Mass* Velocity to zero since the velocity of the bullet is zero after squashing and sticking. So that change in momentum is only M*V.

27. what is the weight of a steel plate 15 ft. long, 4 ft. wide, and 5/8 inch thick, If steel weighs 475 lb./ft.^3 ?


Given: 
l = 15 ft
w = 4 ft
h = 5/8 in ----> thick is the height of steel plate.
density = 475 lb/ft³

Solution:

Convert 5/8 in to ft.

= 5/8 * 1/12  
= (5/8)/12 
h = 5/96 ft

Find the volume of the plate by using the formula V = lwh.

V = lwh
= (15)(4)(5/96)
= 300/96
= 3.125 ft³  

3.125 ft³ is the volume of the plate.

To find the weight of steel plate, use the formula weight = volume * density.

475 * 3.125 = 1484.375 lbs.

The weight of a steel plate is 1484.375 lbs.
