3. Define the four marks of the Catholic Church(One, Holy, Catholic and Apostolic)​


One - There is one body and one Spirit just as you were called to the one hope that belongs to your call, one Lord, one faith, one baptism, one God and Father of all, who is over all and through all and in all.

Holy - The word holy means set apart for a special purpose by and for God. Christians understand the holiness of the universal Church to derive from Christ's holiness.

Catholic - The word "catholic" is derived from the Greek adjective καθολικός (katholikos), meaning "general", "universal". It is associated with the Greek adverb καθόλου (katholou), meaning "according to the whole", "entirely", or "in general", a combination of the preposition κατά meaning "according to" and the adjective ὅλος meaning "whole".

Apostolic - This describes the Church's foundation and beliefs as rooted and continuing in the living Tradition of the Apostles of Jesus.

23. - Why is it important to always keep in mind the 4 marks of the Catholic Church?


It is important to always keep in mind the 4 marks of the Catholic Church because they serve as a reminder of the essential characteristics that define the Church as founded by Jesus Christ. These marks include:

1. One: The Church is one in its mission, worship, and doctrine, despite the diversity of its members and traditions.

2. Holy: The Church is holy because it is the dwelling place of the Holy Spirit and leads people to holiness through sacraments and teachings.

3. Catholic: The Church is Catholic, meaning universal, because it encompasses all people from all nations and cultures and offers salvation to all.

4. Apostolic: The Church is apostolic because it is founded on the apostles and continues to be guided by their successors, the bishops.

By keeping these marks in mind, Catholics can better understand and appreciate the universality and unity of the Church, as well as its mission to bring people to holiness and salvation.
