1. What is the painful irony in the poem "Oh Captain! My Captain!"


Answer:

Though the speaker calls out for him and wants to celebrate his victory, the captain is unable to answer, as he has died on his ship on the way home. The irony of the situation is that he managed to protect his ship in battle and return it home safe and sound, yet he is no longer alive to enjoy his victory.

12. Walt Whitman wrote a famous poem about Lincoln entitled “o Captain! My Captain!​


Answer:

My Captain!" is an extended metaphor poem written by Walt Whitman in 1865 about the death of U.S. President Abraham Lincoln. Well received upon publication, the poem was Whitman's first to be anthologized and the most popular during his lifetime.

“O Captain! My Captain!” is an elegy written by Walt Whitman in 1865 to commemorate the death of President Abraham Lincoln. It was first published in Sequel to Drum-Taps (1865), a collection of Whitman’s poems inspired by the events of the American Civil War.

14. What is the meaning of the quote “I am the master of my fate, I am the captain of my soul.” By: William Shakespeare


Answer:

In the poem 'Invictus,' the speaker says at the end, "I am the master of my fate,/ I am the captain of my soul." The first line of this quote means that the speaker is fully responsible for whatever happens to him. The reason for this is that he has complete control over his destiny. So one's fate is in one's own hands. It all depends on how you make it work for you. The speaker then says in the final line that he is the captain of his soul. The person in command of a ship is known as the captain. The ship represents the speaker's soul in this context. So, the direction of the soul is determined by one's actions, not by predestination.

29. o captain,my captain" is an example of a/an​


Answer:

"O Captain! My Captain!" is an example of a poem. It was written by Walt Whitman in 1865 as a tribute to Abraham Lincoln, the 16th President of the United States, after his assassination. The poem speaks to the loss and mourning of a leader, but also the hope for a continued journey and progress. It is considered as one of the most famous works of Whitman and it's widely read and studied in American literature.
