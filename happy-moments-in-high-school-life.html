8. Many students said that "High School" was the best part of their life as students because of memorable experiences that they had gathered during this stage. How about you? Do you share the same idea? Create a photo essay of your journey as a Junior High School student showcasing your treasured moments and/or challenges that you have gone through.​


Answer:

As a Junior High School student, you have likely experienced a range of emotions, challenges, and moments that have shaped your academic journey. From making new friends to struggling with difficult subjects, your time in Junior High School has undoubtedly been filled with unforgettable experiences.

For many students, Junior High School is a time when they begin to discover who they are and what they are passionate about. Perhaps you joined a sports team or a club that allowed you to explore your interests and talents. Or maybe you had a teacher who inspired you to pursue a particular subject or career path.

On the other hand, Junior High School can also be a challenging time for many students. Balancing schoolwork, extracurricular activities, and social life can be overwhelming, and you may have faced obstacles or setbacks along the way.

Despite the highs and lows, Junior High School is a crucial period in your academic journey, and it is important to reflect on the experiences that have shaped you.

In your photo essay, you could include pictures of your friends, teachers, and classmates who have made an impact on your life. You could also include photos of memorable events such as school dances, sports games, or field trips.

Don't forget to include images that highlight the challenges you've faced and the lessons you've learned. Perhaps you have a photo of yourself studying late into the night, or an image of a project that you worked hard on but didn't turn out as you hoped.

Ultimately, your photo essay should showcase your growth and development as a Junior High School student, and provide a window into the experiences that have shaped who you are today.

16. make a short story it could be the saddest part of you life or your happy moment or story 300 words​


Answer:

Sad Story

Explanation:

In the winter of 2020, my baby cousin was diagnosed with ATRT (a rare brain cancer). My aunt and uncle did EVERYTHING they could to save her, but the things they tried only delayed the inevitable. She fought it tooth and nail for over a year and a half, but ended up dying in early September last year, just a month and 3 days short of her second birthday. The morning she died, I sat up in bed to start getting ready for work about an hour after her time of death, and was immediately struck with a terrible headache on the left side of my brain (which I later found out was the same side of the brain that her tumor was on). Of course, I was an idiot and chalked the headache up to hormones since it was almost that time of the month, but halfway through my work day is when I found out she'd died about an hour before I finally made myself get up for work. Of course, I cried and wanted to pack my bags to leave and go home for the funeral, but I couldn't Unfortunately for me, I was over 200 miles away from home, and couldn't leave the island I was on for the funeral. Even 5 months later, I still haven't visited her grave, and I still feel bad about it. I was 16 and a half when she was born, and now at almost 19, I still don't have my driver's license. When I finally get it, I'm going to make a point of visiting her. I have a general idea of where she's buried, so hopefully I be can use that to find her grave.
