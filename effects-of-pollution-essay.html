It works in mysterious ways, which sometimes cannot be seen by the naked eye.

One of these pollutions is 'Air Pollution'. It is made from harmful gases made by various harmful activities by humans. We know how important water is in our daily life, almost 70% of our body is water also more than 70% of earth is covered in water. Moreover, the way waste is dumped on the land eventually ends up in the soil and turns into toxic. If 'Land Pollution' keeps on happening at this rate, we won’t have fertile soil to grow our crops on. The main source of our food supply will be greatly affected.

Lastly, 'Noise Pollution' can be defined as any disturbing or unwanted noise that interferes or harms humans or wildlife. Although noise constantly surrounds us, noise pollution generally receives less attention than water quality and air quality issues because it cannot be seen, tasted, or smelled.

Therefore, serious measures must be taken to reduce these pollutions.

13. What is It How to write paragraphs with cause-and-effect, comparison-and-contrast, and problem-solution relationships? Cause and Effect Relationship A cause-and-effect text explains the reasons and results of events, situations, or trends. There are three patterns or types of cause-and-effect essays. Cause 1 Cause 2 Effect Cause 3 Multiple Causes-One Effect Cause 1 Cause 2 Cause 3 One Cause Effect 1 Effect 2 Effect 1 One Cause-Multiple Effects Effect 3 Effect 2 Effect 3 Cause-Effect Chain Read an example of a cause-and-effect paragraph below. Air pollution may be due to a lot of factors, the most common of which are exhaust gases from vehicles, factory gas emissions, and natural events. Exhaust gases from vehicles have continuously dominated the air especially in highly urbanized towns and cities mainly because the government lacks control and citizens prefer convenience to the harmful effects. Likewise, the government has not exercised good control over the location of factories that emit gas elsewhere. More often than not, air pollution is caused by natural events such as forest fires, volcanic eruptions, wind erosion, and pollen dispersal.​


Answer:

Writing paragraphs with cause-and-effect, comparison-and-contrast, and problem-solution relationships involves identifying the relationship between two or more concepts and presenting them in a coherent manner.

For a cause-and-effect paragraph, the writer should clearly explain the causes and effects of an event, situation, or trend. There are different patterns or types of cause-and-effect relationships, including single and multiple causes with one or multiple effects, as well as a cause-effect chain. The writer should use transition words and phrases to connect the causes and effects, such as "because," "therefore," "as a result," and "consequently."

Here is an example of a cause-and-effect paragraph on air pollution:

Air pollution may be due to a lot of factors, the most common of which are exhaust gases from vehicles, factory gas emissions, and natural events. Exhaust gases from vehicles have continuously dominated the air especially in highly urbanized towns and cities mainly because the government lacks control and citizens prefer convenience to the harmful effects. Likewise, the government has not exercised good control over the location of factories that emit gas elsewhere. More often than not, air pollution is caused by natural events such as forest fires, volcanic eruptions, wind erosion, and pollen dispersal.

For a comparison-and-contrast paragraph, the writer should identify the similarities and differences between two or more concepts. The paragraph should start with a topic sentence that clearly indicates the aspects being compared or contrasted. The writer should use transition words and phrases such as "similarly," "in contrast," "unlike," and "on the other hand" to signal the comparison or contrast.

For a problem-solution paragraph, the writer should identify a problem and propose a solution to it. The paragraph should start with a clear statement of the problem and its impact. The solution should be presented in a logical and organized manner, with supporting evidence and examples. The writer should use transition words and phrases such as "one solution is," "the best approach is," and "to solve this problem" to signal the solution. The paragraph should end with a call to action or a recommendation for the reader.

In conclusion, writing paragraphs with cause-and-effect, comparison-and-contrast, and problem-solution relationships requires a clear understanding of the concepts being discussed and their interrelationships. The writer should use appropriate transitional words and phrases to guide the reader through the text and provide supporting evidence and examples to enhance their understanding.

20. Write an essay about noise pollution - its causes, effects, and solutions.​


Answers: Noise pollution- unwanted or excessive sound that can have deleterious effects on human health, wildlife and environmental quality.

It is commonly generated inside many industrial facilities and some other workplaces, but it also comes from highway, railway, and airplane traffic and from outdoor construction activities . Noise is more than a mere nuisance.  It can cause physical damage to the eardrum and the sensitive hair cells of the inner ears and result in temporary or permanent hearing loss, known as noise-induced hearing loss.  Excessive noise exposure can raise blood pressure and pulse rates; cause   irritability; can anxiety and mental fatigue; and interfere with sleep, recreation and personal communication,  stress, impairments in memory and attention span. Noise pollution control is important in the workplace and in the community.  It can interfere with an animal’s ability to attract mate, communicate, navigate, find food or avoid predators.  It is serious for marine animals.  Noise control ordinances an laws enacted at the local, regional and national levels can be effective.  Low levels of noise may be overcome using additional absorbing material such as heavy drapery or sound absorbent tiles in enclosed rooms; using hearing protectors.

Supporters argue that school uniforms promote a sense of unity and equality among students. According to a study conducted by the National Association of Elementary School Principals, schools that require uniforms have seen a decrease in discipline problems and an increase in academic performance. By eliminating the pressure to wear the latest fashion trends, students can focus more on their studies and less on their appearance. However, opponents argue that mandatory school uniforms limit students' self-expression and creativity.
