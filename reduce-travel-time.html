12. 1. An arrow leaves a bow with a speed of 42 m/s. Its velocity is reduced to 34 m/s by the time it hits its target. How much distance did the arrow travel over if it were in the air for 2.4 seconds?​


Answer:

Explanation: To find the distance traveled by the arrow, we can use the formula:

Distance = (Initial Velocity + Final Velocity) / 2 * Time

= (42 m/s + 34 m/s) / 2 * 2.4 seconds

= 38 m/s * 2.4 seconds

= 91.2 meters

The arrow traveled 91.2 meters over the 2.4 seconds it was in the air.

14. Apply the concept of inverse variations in solving the following real-life problems:1. The volume of a gas V is inversely proportional to its pressure P. If there is a pressure at 20g/cm² of a container with a volume of 360 cm³, what is the pressure if the volume is 200 cm³?2. The temperature set on an induction cooker is inversely proportional to the cooking time of a one-kilogram meat. If a one-kilogram meat is boiled at a temperature of 1200°C, the cooking time is 20 minutes. To lessen the time, the temperature was set to 1600°C. How long will it take to cook the meat?3. The travel time around an island varies inversely as the speed of the bike. If it takes 4 hours to travel around the island on a speed of 25 kph, how long will it take a bike to travel the said island if the speed is reduced to 20 kph?​


Answer:

Let's assume the constant of proportionality is k. Since the volume and pressure are inversely proportional, we have:

V * P = k

We know that when the pressure is 20g/cm², the volume is 360 cm³, so we can plug in these values to find k:

360 * 20 = k

k = 7200

Now we can use k to find the pressure when the volume is 200 cm³:

V * P = k

200 * P = 7200

P = 36 g/cm²

So the pressure when the volume is 200 cm³ is 36 g/cm².

Let's assume the constant of proportionality is k. Since the temperature and cooking time are inversely proportional, we have:

T * t = k

We know that when the temperature is 1200°C, the cooking time is 20 minutes, so we can plug in these values to find k:

1200 * 20 = k

k = 24000

Now we can use k to find the cooking time when the temperature is 1600°C:

T * t = k

1600 * t = 24000

t = 15 minutes

So it will take 15 minutes to cook the meat when the temperature is 1600°C.

Let's assume the constant of proportionality is k. Since the travel time and speed are inversely proportional, we have:

t * s = k

We know that when the speed is 25 kph, the travel time is 4 hours, so we can plug in these values to find k:

4 * 25 = k

k = 100

Now we can use k to find the travel time when the speed is 20 kph:

t * 20 = k

t = 100 / 20

t = 5 hours

So it will take 5 hours to travel around the island when the speed is 20 kph

24. a.) If the brakes of an automobile can decelerate it at 7.00 m/s². What time is required to reduce the velocity of the automobile from 157.0 km/h to 75.0 km/h? b.) How many meters does the car travel while decelerating?​


Answer:

a.) To solve for the time required to reduce the velocity of the automobile from 157.0 km/h to 75.0 km/h, we first need to convert the velocities to meters per second (m/s) because the unit of acceleration given is in m/s².

157.0 km/h = 43.6 m/s (approx.)

75.0 km/h = 20.8 m/s (approx.)

The initial velocity, u = 43.6 m/s

The final velocity, v = 20.8 m/s

The acceleration, a = -7.00 m/s² (negative because it is a deceleration)

We can use the formula:

v = u + at

where t is the time required to decelerate the car.

Substituting the values, we get:

20.8 = 43.6 - 7.00t

Simplifying the equation, we get:

7.00t = 43.6 - 20.8

7.00t = 22.8

t = 3.26 seconds (approx.)

Therefore, it takes about 3.26 seconds to reduce the velocity of the automobile from 157.0 km/h to 75.0 km/h.

b.) To find out how many meters the car travels while decelerating, we can use the formula:

s = ut + (1/2)at²

where s is the distance travelled, u is the initial velocity, a is the acceleration, and t is the time.

Substituting the values, we get:

s = 43.6 x 3.26 + (1/2)(-7.00)(3.26)²

s = 141.9 meters (approx.)

Therefore, the car travels about 141.9 meters while decelerating.
