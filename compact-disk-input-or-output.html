22. A cartoon of 20 compact disks contains 5 pirate and 15 original disks. In how many ways can an inspector choose 3 of the compact disks and a. get one that is pirated? b. get two of the pirated disks? c. none of the disks is pirated?


a. To get one pirated disk, we have to choose 1 disk from the 5 pirated disks and 2 disks from the 15 original disks. This can be done in C(5,1) * C(15,2) ways (where C(n,r) denotes the combination of n items taken r at a time). So, the total number of ways to choose 3 disks with one pirated disk is:

C(5,1) * C(15,2) = 5 * (15 choose 2) = 5 * 105 = 525

Therefore, there are 525 ways to choose 3 disks with one pirated disk.

b. To get two pirated disks, we have to choose 2 disks from the 5 pirated disks and 1 disk from the 15 original disks. This can be done in C(5,2) * C(15,1) ways. So, the total number of ways to choose 3 disks with two pirated disks is:

C(5,2) * C(15,1) = (5 choose 2) * 15 = 10 * 15 = 150

Therefore, there are 150 ways to choose 3 disks with two pirated disks.

c. To choose none of the disks as pirated, we have to choose 3 disks from the 15 original disks. This can be done in C(15,3) ways. So, the total number of ways to choose 3 disks with none of the disks as pirated is:

C(15,3) = (15 choose 3) = 455

Therefore, there are 455 ways to choose 3 disks with none of the disks as pirated.

What is Computer Hardware insight?

Peripheral

A "device that is utilized to bring information into or obtain information out of the computer" is referred to as a peripheral.

Peripherals are divided into three categories:

Input is a type of data that is used to communicate with or transmit to a computer (mouse, keyboards, etc.)

The computer's output to the user is called output (monitors, printers, etc.)

A peripheral device is any auxiliary device that connects to and works with the computer in some form, such as a computer mouse or keyboard, and stores data processed by the computer (hard drives, flash drives, etc.). Expansion cards, graphics cards, image scanners, tape drives, microphones, loudspeakers, webcams, and digital cameras are all examples of peripherals. RAM (random access memory) is also storage device that is necessary for every significant function of a modern computer, and removing the RAM will practically cripple any modern machine. Many new gadgets, such as digital watches, cellphones, and tablet computers, include interfaces that allow them to be the used as a peripheral by a full computer, despite the fact that they are not host-dependent like older peripherals. The central processor unit, power supply, motherboard, and computer chassis are the only components of a computer that are not considered peripherals by the most technical definition.
