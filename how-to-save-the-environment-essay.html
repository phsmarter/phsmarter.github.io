2. As a student how are you going to help save the environment? (Essay)​


Answer:

clean the sorounding and planting trees

ANSWER:

As a student, there are many ways to help save the environment. One of the most effective ways is to reduce one’s carbon footprint by conserving energy, reducing waste, and reducing water consumption.

Conserving energy can be achieved by switching off electronics when not in use, using energy efficient appliances, and using natural light instead of electric light. Reducing waste can be done by reusing or repurposing items, reducing food waste, and recycling whenever possible. Reducing water consumption can be done by replacing water-intensive landscaping with drought-tolerant plants, using water-saving appliances, and taking shorter showers.

Being a student, one can also spread the word about the need to save the environment by participating in events such as beach clean-ups and tree planting drives. One can also join environmental groups or volunteer to help out with environmental initiatives.

By taking these simple steps, students can have a significant impact on the environment and help save it for future generations.

Cause

Direct causes of deforestation are agricultural expansion, wood extraction (e.g., logging or wood harvest for domestic fuel or charcoal), and infrastructure expansion such as road building and urbanization.

Effect

The loss of trees and other vegetation can cause climate change, desertification, soil erosion, fewer crops, flooding, increased greenhouse gases in the atmosphere, and a host of problems for indigenous people.

Solution

Environmental education (EE) refers to organized efforts to teach about how natural environments function and, particularly, how human beings can manage their behavior and ecosystems in order to live sustainably.

The term is often used to imply education within the school system, from primary to post secondary. However, it is sometimes used more broadly to include all efforts to educate the public and other audiences, including print materials, websites, media campaigns, etc. Related disciplines include outdoor education and experiential education.

Environmental education is a learning process that increases people’s knowledge and awareness about the environment and associated challenges, develops the necessary skills and expertise to address the challenges, and fosters attitudes, motivations, and commitments to make informed decisions and take responsible action (UNESCO, Tbilisi Declaration, 1978).

Environmental education generally refers to curriculum and programs which aim to teach people about the natural world and particularly about ways in which ecosystems work. Environmental education programs often aim to change people’s perceptions about the value of the natural world and to teach how to change environmental behavior, such as getting people to recycle or how to build eco-friendly dwellings.

Environmental education programs often aim to

(i) help students develop factual knowledge about the natural environment, particularly with regard to how ecosystems work and human impacts on the natural environment;

(ii) foster more positive perceptions about the value of the natural world

(iii) develop eco-friendly habits,

(iv) engage students in environmental rejuvenation projects and

(v) develop students’ psychological and spiritual relationship with nature.

(ii) Knowledge and understanding about the environment and environmental challenges

(iv) Skills to mitigate the environmental problems
