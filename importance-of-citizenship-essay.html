8. What is Citizenship ( the right and responsibility ? essay​


Citizenship is a legal status defining the relationship between an individual and the state, defining both rights and duties each bears to the other. The notion of citizenship is in sharp contrast to the historically rival notion of being a subject. In countries ruled by a powerful monarch, subjects owed their sovereign unquestioning obedience and allegiance, and could even be said to be his property. Citizenship is entirely the province of the granting power which can impose whatever requirements it chooses. Broadly speaking there are two forms of citizenship: jus solis and jus sanguinis. In the first case anyone born on the territory of the granting power is considered a citizen. In the second case the child takes the citizenship of the parents regardless of birthplace. As usual its not really that simple and there are a bunch of caveats. For immigrants there is some form of naturalization process. Rules are varied and depend on the individuals status (e.g. refugee, landed immigrant, whatever). Normally this includes at least some means of support and a period of residence on the national territory.

The rights and responsibilities for each country are enshrined in their constitution. Those constitutions can result from a democratic process, revolution or imposition by a foreign power. The other answers outline what those rights and responsibilities are for the Philippines.

One of the driving forces of immigration and emigration throughout history has been individuals wanting to move themselves and their families to a jurisdiction whose constitution reflect their personal values.

Our first and most important responsibility is to acknowledge those same rights in our fellow citizens. Second, we must defend those rights in others even if we find the manner in which they are exercised uncomfortableable.


9. Define and discuss global citizenship. What are the roles and importance of global citizenship in Global Development & Progress? ​


Answer:

Global citizenship refers to a concept that emphasizes the responsibilities and rights of individuals, organizations, and governments in promoting a just, peaceful, and sustainable world. It recognizes that we are all interconnected and that we share a common responsibility to address global challenges such as poverty, climate change, inequality, and conflict.

Global citizenship involves developing a sense of belonging to the global community, recognizing and respecting cultural diversity, promoting human rights, and taking action to address global issues. It includes being informed about global issues, engaging in dialogue with people from different backgrounds, and taking action to make a positive impact in the world.

The role of global citizenship in global development and progress is crucial. In today's interconnected world, global challenges require global solutions, and global citizens have a critical role to play in creating a more just and sustainable world. By promoting cultural understanding and respect, global citizens can help to reduce conflict and promote peace. By advocating for human rights, they can help to ensure that everyone has the opportunity to live a dignified life. By taking action to address global challenges, they can help to create a more sustainable and equitable world.

Global citizenship is also important for sustainable development, as it promotes a holistic approach to development that takes into account social, economic, and environmental factors. Global citizens recognize that development must be sustainable to ensure that future generations can also thrive.

In summary, global citizenship is a critical concept for creating a just, peaceful, and sustainable world. It involves developing a sense of belonging to the global community, recognizing and respecting cultural diversity, promoting human rights, and taking action to address global challenges. By promoting global citizenship, we can create a more equitable and sustainable world for all.

One of the key requirements for becoming a citizen is usually a demonstration of one's commitment to the country and its values. This can be done through various means, such as by showing knowledge of the country's history and government, or by participating in community service and other activities that contribute to the country's well-being.

29. it is important to learn citizenship education​

Democracies need active, informed and responsible citizens – citizens who are willing and able to take responsibility for themselves and their communities and contribute to the political process.

How does it benefit young people?

It helps them to develop self-confidence and a sense of agency, and successfully deal with life changes and challenges such as bullying and discrimination.

It gives them a voice: in the life of their schools, their communities and society at large.

It enables them to make a positive contribution by developing the knowledge and experience needed to claim their rights and understand their responsibilities. It prepares them for the challenges and opportunities of adult and working life.

Who else does it benefit?

Citizenship also brings benefits for schools, other educational organisations and for society at large.
