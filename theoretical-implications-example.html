30. give me some theories/theoretical framework in the implications of allowing smartphone in classes​


Explanation:

There are several theories and theoretical frameworks that can be used to understand the implications of allowing smartphones in classes. Here are a few examples:

Cognitive Load Theory: This theory suggests that our cognitive resources are limited, and when we are trying to learn new information, we need to use our working memory to process that information. Allowing smartphones in classes could increase cognitive load, as students may be distracted by notifications or tempted to use their devices for non-academic purposes, which could take up valuable cognitive resources.Self-Determination Theory: This theory suggests that people are more motivated when they feel a sense of autonomy, competence, and relatedness. Allowing smartphones in classes could increase students' sense of autonomy, as they are given more control over their learning environment. However, if students are distracted by their devices, they may feel less competent and less connected to their learning experience.Social Learning Theory: This theory suggests that people learn through observation and modeling. Allowing smartphones in classes could provide opportunities for students to learn from each other, as they can easily share information and resources on their devices. However, if students are distracted by their devices, they may be less likely to engage in collaborative learning activities.Attention Restoration Theory: This theory suggests that exposure to nature or other restorative environments can help restore attentional resources. Allowing smartphones in classes could interfere with students' ability to engage with their learning environment and could reduce opportunities for attention restoration
