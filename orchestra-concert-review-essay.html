15. what is the difference of gamelan and orchestra? essay 100 words​


Answer:

Gamelan and orchestra are two different types of musical ensembles. Gamelan is a traditional Indonesian ensemble that consists of percussion instruments such as gongs, metallophones, and drums, as well as string and wind instruments. The instruments in a gamelan are usually tuned to a specific scale or mode, and the ensemble plays compositions that are based on traditional Indonesian music.

On the other hand, an orchestra is a Western classical ensemble that typically includes string instruments, woodwinds, brass, and percussion. The instruments in an orchestra are usually standardized and have a specific range and timbre. Orchestral compositions are typically written in Western musical notation and follow a specific harmonic and structural framework.

Overall, the main difference between gamelan and orchestra is the instrumentation, tuning, and musical style. While gamelan is rooted in traditional Indonesian music, the orchestra is associated with Western classical music.
