The documentary runs for eleven minutes and fifty seconds (11:50). After watching the documentary, answer the following questions in short essay.1. What is globalization?2. Who are the key players in globalization?3. What are advantages of globalization?4. What are the impacts of globalization?5. Does globalization weaken the power of the state? Why or why not?​


Answer:

1.Globalization refers to the process by which countries, businesses, and people around the world become increasingly interconnected and interdependent. This can involve the movement of goods, services, and people across national borders, as well as the exchange of ideas and culture.

2.The key players in globalization include multinational corporations, international organizations such as the World Trade Organization and the International Monetary Fund, and governments around the world.

3.Some advantages of globalization include increased economic growth and development, greater access to a wider range of goods and services, and the ability to tap into global talent and expertise. Globalization can also lead to increased cultural exchange and understanding between different countries and peoples.

4.However, globalization can also have negative impacts, such as increased income inequality, the erosion of local cultures and traditions, and environmental degradation. Globalization can also create new challenges for governments, such as the need to regulate international trade and manage the movements of people and capital across borders.

5.Globalization can both strengthen and weaken the power of the state. On the one hand, globalization can give states access to new markets and sources of wealth, and can also facilitate the exchange of ideas and information. On the other hand, globalization can also challenge the power of states by creating new economic and political competition, and by putting pressure on states to adapt to changing conditions. In general, the impact of globalization on the power of the state will depend on a variety of factors, including the state's economic and political power, as well as its ability to adapt to changing circumstances.
