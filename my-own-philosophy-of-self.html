11. Which of the philosophy of the self relates to to your own belief and explain how each view impact your self understanding? What is your own philosophy of self? 3-5 paragraph​


Explanation:

My personal philosophy of self is most closely aligned with the existentialist view that emphasizes the individual's autonomy and responsibility for their own life. To me, this means that each person has the power to shape their own identity and determine their own values and beliefs. This view has had a profound impact on my self-understanding, as it allows me to live with purpose and meaning.

The existentialist view suggests that we are not defined by our circumstances or backgrounds, but rather by our own choices and actions. This emphasis on personal responsibility has helped me to recognize the potential for growth and change within myself. I have realized that I am in control of my own destiny and that my actions have consequences. This shift in perspective has encouraged me to take more ownership of my life and to strive for more authentic and fulfilling experiences.

At the same time, the existentialist view emphasizes the value of living a life that is true to yourself, regardless of societal expectations or pressure. This has given me the confidence to pursue my own passions and interests, even if they don't fit within conventional norms. I understand that my life is my own and that I must live it in a way that brings me joy and fulfillment.

In contrast, other philosophical views of the self have had a more negative impact on my self-understanding. For example, biological determinism suggests that we are solely products of our genetics and environment, which can lead to a sense of futility and powerlessness. I have struggled with this notion in the past, as I have felt that certain aspects of my personality and behavior are beyond my control. However, the existentialist view has empowered me to recognize that I have the power to shape my own identity and that I am not limited by my genes or past experiences.

Furthermore, cognitive positivism places utmost emphasis on objective knowledge and facts, which can lead to a lack of appreciation for other perspectives and experiences. I have found this worldview to be overly rigid and limiting, as it can discount the value of subjective experiences and emotions. The existentialist view, on the other hand, allows for a more holistic understanding of the self, acknowledging the importance of both objective facts and subjective experiences. I have come to appreciate that our sense of self is complex and multifaceted, encompassing both logical and emotional aspects.

Overall, my personal philosophy of self is one that emphasizes the importance of personal responsibility, authenticity, and growth. It allows me to live with purpose.

This encourages self-reflection and introspection, which can help individuals become self-aware of their strengths, weaknesses, and potential.

In terms of my own self-understanding, Humanism has enabled me to understand myself as a unique individual with the capacity to make my own choices. Overall, my own philosophy of self is rooted in Humanism which emphasizes the importance of individual autonomy
