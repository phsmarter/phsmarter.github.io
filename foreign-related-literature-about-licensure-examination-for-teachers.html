10. foreign literature about inflation rate​


Answer:

There is a vast amount of foreign literature about inflation rate, as it is a crucial economic concept that affects countries worldwide. Here are some resources that you may find helpful:

1. "Inflation Dynamics and Monetary Policy in India" by Dholakia, Ravindra H. (2011): This paper discusses the inflation dynamics in India and explores the effectiveness of monetary policy in controlling inflation.

2. "Inflation Targeting: Lessons from Four Countries" by Lars E.O. Svensson (1997): This paper examines the experiences of four countries that have implemented inflation targeting as a monetary policy framework and draws lessons for other countries.

3. "Inflation in Emerging and Developing Economies: Evolution, Drivers, and Policies" by International Monetary Fund (2018): This report provides a comprehensive analysis of inflation trends, drivers, and policies in emerging and developing economies.

4. "Inflation and Economic Growth: A Cross-Country Non-linear Analysis" by Olusegun A. Omisakin and Abimbola M. Oyinlola (2013): This study investigates the relationship between inflation and economic growth using a cross-country non-linear analysis.

5. "Inflation and Monetary Policy in Central and Eastern Europe" by Juraj Zeman and Lubos Komarek (2014): This book examines the inflation dynamics and monetary policy frameworks in Central and Eastern European countries.

6. "Inflation Targeting and Economic Growth" by Jonas E. Arias and Juan F. Rubio-Ramírez (2010): This paper explores the impact of inflation targeting on economic growth using a dynamic stochastic general equilibrium model.

These resources provide a sample of the foreign literature available on inflation rate, and there are many more publications and studies that you can explore depending on your specific research interests.
