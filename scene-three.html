1. three examples of set up of the scene​


Answer:The three types of setting are the elements of time, place, and environment (both physical and social). Each of these types contributes to building the setting of a story.

The definition of a scene is a place where something occurs or a setting in a story. An example of a scene is where a crime occurred. An example of a scene is the balcony episode in Romeo and Juliet. ... The place in which the action of a play, movie, novel, or other narrative occurs; a setting.

The three active parts of the scene: goal, conflict, and disaster.

Still, there's a way to write a good one, so here are the 5 elements of a scene. Time & Place. One of the first things you want to establish in your scene is the time and place. ... A Clear Goal. Something needs to be accomplished during the scene. ... Conflict & Action. ... Emotional Change. ... A Page-Turning Ending.

Example 1

Conflict: A character and her mother are upset with each other. The main character believes she must be an artist, whereas her mother does not support her career and would rather have her be an accountant.

Climax: The character and her mother have a large argument in which they both state their feelings.

13. Movie Mo 'To! Think of three scenes from your favoritemovies where different communicative strategies wereemployed in one conversation. Watch each scene andevaluate if it employs acceptable,​


Answer:

While all movies present characters using different communication strategies, the following movies present some of the memorable scenes that highlight different communication strategies by the characters.

Explanation:

1.  Movie: Four Sisters and A Wedding - The scene where the sisters are having a meeting at the dirty kitchen.

Communication Strategy - Nomination

Why acceptable: As the sisters are look for ways to keep their youngest brother from getting married, they thought of looking up Rebreb's past girlfriends to find out who can help them stop their brother from marrying Princess.  Nomination happens when they started looking up different names of the previous girlfriend and each time, they found reasons why they can't help them which presents different topics for discussion.

2.  Movie: Anak - the final confrontation scene between the eldest daughter and the mother, just before the mother leaves for Hong Kong.

Communication Strategy: Turn taking

Why acceptable:  The scene evokes powerful emotions which is common when people fight.  Every time the mother, Josie gives reasons for doing what she did for the good of the family, the angry daughter, Carla takes turn answering back which continued until the mother's released all her pent up frustrations on her ungrateful eldest child.

3. Movie: Legally Blonde 1 - The final court scene.

Communication strategy: Topic control

Why acceptable: The scene presents how Elle Woods, from being a nervous replacement lawyer has managed to turn the tables and continue pressing questions on the witness which turned out to to be the culprit all because she has found a flaw on her alibi. Topic control is common in interrogating individuals in court cases.
