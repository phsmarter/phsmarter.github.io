9. Why do they say time is gold?


Time is gold is among the most popular metaphors of all time which can be traced back all the way to Ancient Greeks. While the Greeks did not exactly say time is gold, a close translation is the most expensive cost is time or the most costly expense is time.

While not confirmed, the modern variation of the saying is credited to US President Benjamin Franklin in his 1748 essay written to address young tradesmen.

But why is time considered gold?

Putting aside its physical properties, let us take into consideration the monetary value of gold.

Gold is a finite resource.

Gold is not in continuous production, it is not man-made. It comes from nature, produced through hundreds of thousands of years under the right chemical and geological conditions.

Some gold mining towns are eventually abandoned because gold has run out and everything was mined to extinction. Some gold are eventually melted and re-purposed into a new item.

One may be able to replicate the color and shape of gold but never its other properties such as luster, malleability, corrosion resistance, and weight.

The same goes for time. While the universe may exist forever, humans do not live forever. They each only live for a number of years and will eventually "expire". We cannot produce more time or extend 24 hours of the day into 30 hours. We may be able to make time for some things by rearranging our priorities but we remain at 24 hours a day.

Gold is valuable due to its continuously rising value.

Over time, gold rises in value because it is a commodity that is scarce and in demand. People want gold and people need gold. Since there is only so much of it left in the world, any gold in existence rises in value as the demand grows stronger.

The same goes for time. As people grow older, they gain more knowledge and experience. This means they now have the ability to do more but the lack of time to do all they want remains a constant.

Some people value time and allocate it to things that will allow them to make more money. While others allocate their time to the things that they believe in or people they value the most.
