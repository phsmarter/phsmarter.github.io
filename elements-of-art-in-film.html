-A Shape is a bounding, closed line. Shapes other than circles and triangles can also take on abstract forms.

-A form Shape and line are combined to produce form, which adds depth. The appearance of three dimensions is produced by form.

-A Value is the seeming lightness or darkness of something. Value is a crucial factor in both shading and highlighting. The relationship between the light and dark values is contrast. There is a high concentration of contrast when there are both very light and very dark areas. There is little contrast if the values are mostly grey and the difference between the lightest and darkest values is not great.

-A Texture is how a surface appears, such as how bumpy or smooth a drawing appears to be. An artwork's texture is rarely truly felt in the piece. Instead, it is merely the appearance that the sketched item has this tactile character.

-A Color has three properties-hue, value, and intensity. In addition to these, there are primary and secondary colors. Red, yellow, and blue are the primary, and orange, green, and purple are the secondary. One can study the color wheel to see how these colors interact with one another, (complementary are opposite of each other, analogous sit next to each other.
