26. write a short speech about the peoples experiences when the bohol province was hit by the typhoon odette. as a student how will you overcome these challenges brought by the typhoon? (3 parahraphs: 7-10 sentences)​


With the lessons learned from the Typhoon Odette experience, Bohol province will strive to be more resilient and self-sufficient in times of man-made or natural calamity in the future, Governor Arthur Yap said Wednesday.

As the resources are being used to clean up and for the reconstruction effort in the entire province, Yap said the capitol will also embark on a program that will prepare all 47 municipalities and its capital, Tagbilaran City, in case another calamity hits the island.

“Let’s find whatever we can use in the future, as we study what we can do as a province, (after) "Odette". We cannot just rely always on others. We have to build our own capability and resiliency,” he said, as he attended a water purifying demonstration by a team from the Metropolitan Manila Development Authority (MMDA) in Loboc town, one of the most badly hit areas by the typhoon.

Personnel from the MMDA are in Bohol to help in the clearing operations. They also brought a handy, solar-powered water purifier that can be used in areas struck by the typhoon where damaged power lines forced water refilling stations to shut down.

As the local government units (LGU) witnessed how the localities tackle the effects of Typhoon Odette, Yap said the experiences and challenges in the aftermath of the calamity can be used to study what systems they could adopt in future responses against any forms of disaster.

“The Philippines (will have an) average of 22 to 25 tropical cyclones every year. We are one of the most climate change-affected countries in the world. Mangandam ta kanunay (Let’s always be ready) because we are (always) at risk,” Yap said.

28. significances about The challenges that ABM students encounter in being a classroom officer or SSG officer while focusing on their study.


As a student myself, I understand the challenges that come with being a classroom officer or SSG officer while also focusing on our studies. One significant challenge is the added responsibility and workload that comes with the position. As an officer, we are expected to attend meetings, organize events, and communicate with our fellow students and faculty members. This can take up a significant amount of time and energy that could have been allocated towards studying and completing assignments.

Another challenge is the need to balance our priorities. As classroom or SSG officers, we are expected to fulfill our duties and responsibilities to the best of our abilities, but at the same time, we must also prioritize our academic performance. This means that we have to learn how to manage our time effectively, set realistic goals, and prioritize our tasks accordingly.

Furthermore, being a classroom or SSG officer also requires strong leadership skills, which can be challenging to develop and maintain while also focusing on our studies. It takes a lot of effort and dedication to be an effective leader and to ensure that our peers are motivated and engaged in the activities that we organize.

Despite these challenges, being a classroom or SSG officer can also be a rewarding experience. It allows us to develop our skills in communication, leadership, and organization, which can be valuable in our future careers. It also provides an opportunity to make a positive impact on our school community and leave a lasting legacy.

Overall, the challenges that ABM students encounter in being a classroom or SSG officer while focusing on their studies should not discourage us from taking on these roles. Instead, we should embrace the opportunity to grow and develop as individuals while making a positive impact on our school community
