As a student, there are many ways to help save the environment. One of the most effective ways is to reduce one’s carbon footprint by conserving energy, reducing waste, and reducing water consumption.

Conserving energy can be achieved by switching off electronics when not in use, using energy efficient appliances, and using natural light instead of electric light. Reducing waste can be done by reusing or repurposing items, reducing food waste, and recycling whenever possible. Reducing water consumption can be done by replacing water-intensive landscaping with drought-tolerant plants, using water-saving appliances, and taking shorter showers.

Being a student, one can also spread the word about the need to save the environment by participating in events such as beach clean-ups and tree planting drives. One can also join environmental groups or volunteer to help out with environmental initiatives.

By taking these simple steps, students can have a significant impact on the environment and help save it for future generations.

Environmental education (EE) refers to organized efforts to teach about how natural environments function and, particularly, how human beings can manage their behavior and ecosystems in order to live sustainably.

The term is often used to imply education within the school system, from primary to post secondary. However, it is sometimes used more broadly to include all efforts to educate the public and other audiences, including print materials, websites, media campaigns, etc. Related disciplines include outdoor education and experiential education.

Environmental education is a learning process that increases people’s knowledge and awareness about the environment and associated challenges, develops the necessary skills and expertise to address the challenges, and fosters attitudes, motivations, and commitments to make informed decisions and take responsible action (UNESCO, Tbilisi Declaration, 1978).

Environmental education generally refers to curriculum and programs which aim to teach people about the natural world and particularly about ways in which ecosystems work. (ii) foster more positive perceptions about the value of the natural world

(iv) engage students in environmental rejuvenation projects and

16. Essay about producing new scientist through innovations, competitions and inventions for the sustainability for clean and green environment to save mother earth.


The making of a scientists often involves finishing at least a four-year degree from a tertiary education institution. Most of the time, students are immersed in books and lectures. What if we can produce new scientists through innovations, competitions, and inventions to guarantee the sustainability of a clean and green environment and thus saving mother earth?

Learning is never only about reading books; it is also about being motivated to remember and practice an information, concept, or skill. If students simply memorize theories from books, chances are they would forget this, and by the time they need it for actual use, they will have to review and thus waste time. An innovative way of making new scientists would mean straying away from the usual lecture and notes methods and moving to new methods like experiential learning. Producing new scientists through innovations can be tricky and doing so requires teachers to be creative enough to figure out new methods to educate students. However, this is important because the environment in which students grow and develop influences how they turn out, so if they are trained in an innovative manner, they are likely to be innovative.  

Similarly, when students are pitched against each other to compete, they are often motivated to do their best. Of course, who would say no to a quiz contest with an exemption in the final exam as a reward? However, while many students are motivated by competition, John Shindler (2009) in his book Transformative Classroom Management notes that makes learners focus on the outcome and not on the process; it also weakens the intrinsic motivation of students not to mention it increases anxiety. Worse, Shindler notes that it develops ‘fear of failure’ in students. This would not be a good quality for scientists since they should go through a series of failed experiments before they arrive at the appropriate outcome. Hence, in developing new scientists, competition may help in teamwork, but too much of it could be dangerous to the individual.  

In learning, assessment is the most important. The most common forms of assessments would be written exams. However, if we are breeding scientists, practical ones in which they apply what they have so far learned would be a guarantee of their potentials. An invention would be a suitable form of assessment. They must be able to put together something functional to prove that all their years of learning have not been put into waste.

Since we are breeding innovative, competitive, and inventive new scientists, we are given hope that the environment could be saved by this new blood of scientists. Exposing them at a young age to the value of protecting the environment will help them focus on agendas related to sustainability of  a clean and green environment. Hence, part of the innovative ways of teaching them would be best related to preserving the environment. They may be asked to compete on topics related to the environment. Finally, they may be asked to invent something for the environment. If such information is gradually implanted in them from a young age, students would grow with much concern for the environment, and these new scientists leave the institution ready to save mother earth.  

The new scientists bred through innovation, competition, and invention, could easily show how science could save humanity and mother earth.

21. Essay on Save Fuel For Better Environment & Health in 500 words​


Answer:

Fuel is very important for everyday activities. Day-by-day our fuel deposits are depreciating. We need to save fuel to have a better environment around us. The real danger in consuming fuel thoughtlessly is that it is drying up the earth’s resources. Fossil fuels like coal and oil and gas are non-renewable resources. Increasing usage is depleting their presence within the earth. To save our environment, we need to save fuel.

In today's world fuels are undoubtedly ruling our world. Our basic needs are Roti, Kapada and Makaan. And, we need fuel to cook, to manufacture fabric, to produce building material and to build our sweet homes. So willingly or unwillingly we are dependent on fuels to live.

We use fossil fuels such as coal, wood, natural gas, Petroleum and its derivatives like petrol, diesel, LPG, kerosene etc. Since the days of Industrial revolution, there has been a rapid growth and development in the entire globe. The machines in industries, transport vehicles and domestic appliances all use fossil fuels. Even though the world adopted electricity as a source of energy, we still need fossil fuels to produce electricity.

Environment is an essential part of our life. It includes surrounding water, air, trees, wildlife, birds, minerals etc. Quality of our life depends on the quality of our environment. Our health is dependent both on ourselves and our environment. We need a clean environment to live healthy.

Last four decades have seen tremendous growth in electric and electronic gadgets and appliances causing the demand for energy to go skyrocketing. Combustion of fossil fuels results in carbon dioxide and other greenhouse gases that have negative impact on environment and health. Overuse of fuels have initiated a breakdown of our ecosystem balance.

As a consequence air we breathe is polluted with CO2, soot particles and harmful gases. A large number of people suffer from respiratory diseases. The local temperatures have risen. Climatic balance is broken. Global warming is causing icebergs at the Poles and ice caps on mountain peaks to melt at a faster rate.

Many species of birds and oceanic creatures are getting extinct. Animals too suffer from pollution. The balance in Biodiversity has become an issue.
