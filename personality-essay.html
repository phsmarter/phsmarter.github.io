17. My personal values essay​


Personal values are the beliefs and principles that guide an individual's decision-making and behavior in life. They are the foundational principles that shape our lives and help us to find meaning and purpose in what we do. In this essay, I will discuss my personal values and the importance they have in shaping my beliefs and actions.

Honesty is one of the most important values for me. I believe that being honest with myself and others builds trust, respect, and helps to create harmonious relationships. In my personal and professional life, I have always strived to be honest in my interactions with others, even if it may be difficult or uncomfortable.

Integrity is another value that is important to me. Integrity involves having strong moral principles and being able to stand by them when faced with ethical dilemmas. I believe that integrity is what defines who we are and what we stand for. It is important that I act in a way that reflects honesty, respect, and fairness in all that I do.

Respect is another value that I hold dear. I believe that everyone should be treated equally, regardless of their background, culture, or lifestyle. Respecting others' opinions and values is an essential aspect of building a strong and harmonious community. It is important to me that I respect others and that I am respected in return.

Compassion is a value that helps me to connect with others on a deeper level. I believe that showing compassion towards others helps build relationships and shows that we care about one another.

26. describe Full by harmony hazard based on personal essay Genre:Personal Essay


FULL BY HARMONY HAZARD

"Full" by Harmony Hazard is a personal essay that addresses the topic of feeling fulfilled in life through tales and personal experiences.

Hazard opens the essay by explaining the concept of "hitting rock bottom," which refers to a state of profound despair or hopelessness. She recounts her personal experience of reaching rock bottom, feeling lost and unhappy despite having an apparently perfect existence on the surface. She considers how society frequently encourages us to pursue specific objectives or milestones, such as acquiring a good career or purchasing a home, in order to achieve pleasure and contentment. However, Hazard contends that these exterior indicators of achievement do not always lead to true fulfillment.Hazard underlines the necessity of finding one's own individual path to fulfillment throughout the essay. She urges readers to consider what actually provides them joy and significance in life, rather than simply conforming to the expectations of others or society. She discusses how she achieved personal fulfillment by pursuing her passion for writing and connecting with people via her work.
