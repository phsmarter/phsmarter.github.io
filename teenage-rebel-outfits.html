3. Reaction paper of memoirs of a teenage rebel. ​


Answer:

  Teenage revolt is not a new occurrence. Looking at classical writings confirms that every age group has disputed. Unfortunately, this doesn't mean that the juvenescence have to be the night terrors that everyone talks about. Parents can get great contentment from observing their youngster grow into a young adult and sharing a more uniform relationship. However, there are usually a few speed bumps along the way.

            One of the things many parents find hardest about the teenage years is that most teenagers go through a time of wanting to reject everything their parents stand for. Although it may not seem like it at the time, this sort of behavior is normal and healthy. The key task for young people in their teenage years is to find out who they are and what they stand for. It is almost impossible for a teenager to do this successfully if they just uncritically accept all of their parent's values and ways of thinking. However, because teenagers are teenagers they don't take the middle ground and establish values somewhere between conventional and radical.
