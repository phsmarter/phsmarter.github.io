6. compare and contrast that mark twain's the adventures of huckleberry finn and jk rowling's harry potter serie​


"Huckleberry Finn vs. Harry Potter"

Most likely, morality was the largest difference between Huck and Harry Potter. In the story, Huck frequently lies, steals, and threatens other people.

Harry Potter frequently engaged in wrong behavior, such as lying and stealing, yet he never went out of his way to cause harm to a student. Harry should try to had a zero tolerance for bullying and showed great compassion for everyone, especially his pals, from the start opposed to Huck.

Despite the fact that Huck and Harry had completely different adventures, saving someone or something was always the ultimate objective.

Huckleberry Finn's forty-three chapters are devoted to his adventures with Tom and Jim, whereas all seven of Harry Potter's books are about his adventures with his companions while defending the wizarding world.

Harry and Huck had quite dissimilar endings and futures. The author of Harry's story actually goes on to describe Harry, his friends, and family in the future. While Huckleberry Finn leaves his home and everything behind, Mark Twain left us wondering what will happen to him after that.

Harry Potter and Huckleberry Finn are such dissimilar works that comparing them to British and American children's literature is simply unfair, especially considering that Huck isn't even strictly a children's book.
