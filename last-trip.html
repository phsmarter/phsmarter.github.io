Answer:

a. distance covered = 380 km

b. average speed = 75 km/hr

Step-by-step explanation:

GIVEN:

t(1) = 2 hr

s(1) = 70 km/hr

t(2) = 3 hr

s (2) = 80 km/hr

REQUIRED:

a. What is the distance covered for Arthur's entire  trip?

b. What is his average speed of travel for his entire  trip?​

SOLUTION:

a)

distance (1)= speed × time

distance (1)= 70 km/hr × 2  hr

distance (1)= 140 km

distance (2) = speed × time

distance (2) =80 km/hr × 3 hr

distance (2) = 240 km

distance covered = distance (1) + distance (2)

distance covered = 140 km + 240 km

distance covered = 380 km

b.

average speed = (s(1) +s(2))÷2

average speed = (70 km/hr + 80 km/hr) ÷ 2

average speed = 150 km/hr ÷ 2

average speed = 75 km/hr

21. on an automobile trip, jessa maintained a steady speed for 30 minutes. rush hour traffic slowed her speed by 40 kph for the last part of the trip. the entire trip, a distance of 38 kilometers, took 1 hour and 15 minutes. what was her speed during the first part of the trip?


Answer:

54.4 kph

Step-by-step explanation:

We all know that problems involving rate, distance and time have a general formula called DISTANCE FORMULA

Distance = (Rate)(Time)

D = rt

Where D is the distance travelled at a certain time;

r is the rate or the speed at which the car is travelling; and

t is the time it took to cover a certain distance

STEADY SPEED is the speed at which for a certain amount of time, the speed remains constant. All we do is find the 3 values in the problem (rate, distance and time).

The problems says that Jessa was travelling at a steady speed of we still don't know (we let as X kph) in 30 minutes or 0.5 hours. Note that we converted minutes to hours to have uniform units.

We next interpret the next sentence. "Slowed her speed by 40 kph" means that her speed on the next distance is slower than her original speed (X) by 40kph. Thus, her present speed is X-40 kph. That is, the previous speed should be bigger in number.

"For the last part of the trip" is the time she travelled slower due to the traffic. Note that there are only two parts she travelled

1. She travelled with her original speed X; and

2. She travelled X-40 kph due to traffic

Thus, taking note of the total time she travelled (1 hour and 15 minutes, or 1.25 hours), we can now solve for the time she travelled while in traffic.

Total travel time - time at steady speed

1.25 hrs - 0.5 hrs = 0.75 hrs

Since we now have all the values and variables, we make a LINEAR EQUATION WITH ONE VARIABLE.

Distance 1 + Distance 2 = Total Distance

(0.5)(X) + (0.75)(X-40) = 38

0.5X + 0.75X - 30 =38

Transposing all variables to the left and simplifying

1.25X = 68

X = 54.4 kph

Therefore, her speed during the first part of the trip is 54.4 kph.
