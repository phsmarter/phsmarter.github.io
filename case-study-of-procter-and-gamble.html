4. Using the procter & gamble example from the text (in the entrepreneurial process box), explain how some programs may succeed or fail using this approach.


According to the case study, procter and gamble is investing substantially in one-on-one consumer research with the goal of satisfying the less evident demands of consumers with novel goods as one of the creative techniques being used to expand market share. According to the case study, P&G also employs a novel technique by drastically cutting back on test marketing in order to accelerate the launch of new product offerings. This tactic is predicated on the idea that "creating the finest product can come at a cost of being late to market or completely losing a market opportunity."

In actuality, Unilever invests a significant amount of time in each stage of the product development process, from concept demonstration in a lab setting through prototype demonstration in a real-world setting. Perhaps this innovation strategy contributes to Unilever's ability to surpass P&G in market share for its FMCG products in the UAE.

Last but not least, the case study has shown that P&G uses designer engagement in product packaging and branding to satisfy consumer experiences and so maintain growth. It may be claimed that in the UAE environment, such involvement has helped the business draw clients in a nation that primarily relies on cultural and religious values. The fundamental tenet at Unilever is that "packaging designers must constantly inject innovations to appeal to consumers in a constantly changing and fiercely competitive industry."

19. essay about gambling


Gambling is a bad habit. Yet it is an old habit that existed in ancient India. We learn from our epics that the Pandavas and the Kauravas used to gamble.

People gamble because they want to get rich quickly. Gambling is a kind of short cut to riches. Everyone would like to follow an easy way to make money.

There are different forms of gambling. Some may gamble with cards or dice, others may play 'matka' or bet on horses. Buying lottery tickets and trying cross- word puzzles are other forms of gambling. Some forms of gambling like cards, dice and matka are illicit. Others are permitted by the law. These are crosswords, lotteries and horse-racing.

Illicit forms of gambling should not be encouraged by people. Even buying lotteries or betting on horses should be done within limits. The disadvantage of gambling is that it becomes a craze. Gamblers neglect their families. They spend all their earnings on gambling. They hardly ever win any money. When they lose money by gambling, they sometimes commit suicide. Many families have been ruined by gambling. So gambling should be avoided.
