3. What are the examples of ethical and unethical behavior of a professional counselor?​


Explanation:

Unit 3: Characteristics of Effective Counseling

Neither the client nor the counselor is free of ethical views of life. Effective counseling deals with ethical understanding, legal responsibilities and moral realities. Counseling does not take place in a fantasy world, but in a world of reality where people are required to make ethical choices and decisions. Adherence to professional ethical standards protects both the public and the counselor.

Learning Outcomes

By the end of the unit, you should be able to:

describe unethical behaviour;

discuss ethical codes.

This unit includes unethical behaviour and ethical codes.

Unethical Behaviour & Ethical Codes

First let us consider what would constitute unethical behaviour from a counselor. Consider this list.

Incompetence, that is, inadequate knowledge and the absence of skills necessary for professional behaviour.

Lack of integrity, moral commitment and sound professional judgment to adhere to acceptable standards of right and wrong action.

Violating confidences. Information presented in a counseling relationship is confidential.

Exceeding the level of professional competence. Counselors must recognize their strengths and limitations in serving their clients in the most competent manner - or refer them to other experts.

Imposing values on clients. It is a responsibility of counselors to be aware of his/her values and of their impact on others.

Creating dependence on the part of the clients to meet the counselor's own needs, e.g., sexual relations and social interactions.

Improper advertising, especially advertising that presents the counselor as one who has the skills, competence and/or credentials, that he, or she, does not actually possess.

Charging fees for private counseling to those who are entitled to free services through the counselor's employing institution, and/or using one's job to recruit clients for a private practice.

Answer:

ACTIVITY 1:

Rights of Counselors:

Right to privacy and confidentiality of clientsRight to express their professional opinionsRight to decline to work with clients if it violates their personal values or beliefsRight to access professional development opportunitiesResponsibilities of Counselors:Responsibility to provide competent and ethical services to clientsResponsibility to maintain confidentiality and protect the privacy of clientsResponsibility to maintain appropriate boundaries with clientsResponsibility to continue to learn and develop professionally

Accountabilities of Counselors:

Accountability to clients for providing ethical and competent servicesAccountability to regulatory bodies for maintaining ethical and professional standardsAccountability to colleagues for maintaining ethical and professional behaviorAccountability to the profession as a whole for upholding its values and principles.

ACTIVITY 2:

Ethical Behaviors among Counselors:

Maintaining confidentiality and protecting the privacy of clientsProviding competent and evidence-based servicesEstablishing and maintaining appropriate boundaries with clientsBeing respectful and non-judgmental towards clientsContinuing to learn and develop professionallyAdhering to professional standards and guidelines

Unethical Behaviors among Counselors:

Breaching confidentiality or failing to protect the privacy of clientsProviding services that are not evidence-based or outside of their competenceEngaging in dual relationships with clientsBeing disrespectful or judgmental towards clientsFailing to engage in ongoing professional developmentViolating professional standards or guidelines.

It is important for counselors to be aware of their rights, responsibilities, and accountabilities to ensure that they are providing ethical and competent services to their clients. Being able to distinguish between ethical and unethical behaviors can help counselors to maintain high standards of professionalism and avoid behaviors that could harm their clients or damage the reputation of the profession as a whole.
