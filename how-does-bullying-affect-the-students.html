20. how does bullying affect junior high school students ? introduction ​,Body, Conclusion


Answer:

Introduction:

Bullying is a prevalent problem that affects many students, especially those in junior high school. It can take many forms, such as physical, verbal, or emotional abuse, and can cause long-term negative effects on the victims. This essay will explore how bullying affects junior high school students.

Body:

Bullying can have various negative effects on junior high school students. Firstly, it can lead to a decline in academic performance. Students who are bullied may have difficulty focusing on their studies due to the emotional distress caused by the bullying. They may also miss school, leading to a further decline in their academic performance.

Secondly, bullying can cause social isolation. Victims of bullying may feel ashamed or embarrassed about their situation, which can make them hesitant to seek out friendships or social interactions. This can lead to social isolation and feelings of loneliness.

Thirdly, bullying can have serious mental health effects. Students who are bullied may experience depression, anxiety, and low self-esteem. They may also develop post-traumatic stress disorder (PTSD) and have difficulty trusting others.

Conclusion:

In conclusion, bullying can have a significant impact on junior high school students. It can lead to a decline in academic performance, social isolation, and serious mental health effects. It is essential for schools to have effective anti-bullying policies and for teachers and parents to be aware of the signs of bullying and take action to prevent it. By working together, we can help create a safe and supportive environment for all students to learn and grow.
