4. Saint Patrick’s Cathedral in Manhattan, New York is a masterpiece of James Renwick. It is one of the many Neo-gothic buildings or Gothic revival architectures that feature castellation. Which of the following statements best describe a castellation?  A. It is characterized by light architectural movement using bricks and stones only. B. It is characterized by crenelated walls and towers in imitation of medieval castles. C. It is characterized by huge domes and pillars as a part of the decorative phase of the structure. D. It is characterized by a simple yet continuous lining of wall with flat roofs and small arches. ​


Answer:

C. It is characterized by huge domes and pillars as a part of the decorative phase of the structure.

Explanation:

The pointed arch, which is used for windows, doors, and architectural components such as porches, dormers, or roof gables, is the most defining feature of the castellationl style. Steeply pitched roofs and front-facing gables with delicate timber trim known as vergeboards or bargeboards are other distinctive elements.

The Palace of Westminster, constructed in 1835 for the Houses of Parliament, is one of the most well-known specimens of the neo-Gothic architecture. Romantic-era architects also experimented with new construction materials like as cast iron to play with Gothic shapes, as well as exotic themes inspired by Britain's worldwide empire.

6. what is the story of this art by conrad faber von kreuznach pa help po ​


Explanation:

Conrad Faber von Kreuznach's early works include illustrations that were published by the publisher Peter Schöffer between 1517-1522. In the 420 pages of the issue there are 153 Faber woodcuts and collaborations with other fellow workers for 250 pictures. Faber's works stand out as lively illustrations. Faber used sketches of Middle Rhine and Rhineland cities that he had made during his travels to make illustrations of ancient Greek Sieges while he depicted Princes of his time as ancient Roman warlords. A large number of the paintings by Conrad Faber are portraits of wealthy clients and also Landscapes and Cityscapes. He depicted the costumes of patrons with lavish and colourful detail. His paintings were signed as 'CVC' (Conrad von Creuznach).
