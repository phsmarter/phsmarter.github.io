15. How can a professional teacher violate sec. 6 and section 7 of Article II


Answer:

Section 6 and Section 7 of Article II of the Philippine Constitution provide the state's policy on education and the role of the teacher in the development of the youth.

Section 6 states that "the State shall encourage non-formal, informal, and indigenous learning systems, as well as self-learning, independent, and out-of-school study programs particularly those that respond to community needs." A professional teacher can violate this section by discouraging or prohibiting students from participating in non-formal or indigenous learning systems, or by failing to recognize and incorporate the unique cultural knowledge and practices of their students in their teaching.

Section 7 states that "the State shall provide for a comprehensive and integrated system of education and training which shall be responsive to the changing needs of society." A professional teacher can violate this section by failing to adapt their teaching methods to the changing needs of society or by promoting outdated or irrelevant information.

In summary, a professional teacher can violate Sections 6 and 7 of Article II by not supporting non-formal or indigenous learning systems, not incorporating cultural knowledge and practices in their teaching, not adapting their teaching to changing societal needs, or promoting outdated or irrelevant information.

One news article that involves a violation of human rights is titled "Myanmar's Military Crackdown on Protests Escalates, UN Says". The article, published by NPR on March 2, 2021, discusses the ongoing political crisis in Myanmar, where the military has taken control of the government in a coup and cracked down on protests by pro-democracy activists.

The military's actions in Myanmar have violated several articles of the Universal Declaration of Human Rights (UDHR). First, Article 3 of the UDHR states that "everyone has the right to life, liberty and security of person." However, the military's violent crackdown on protests has resulted in multiple deaths and injuries among demonstrators, as well as arbitrary arrests and detentions of activists and opposition leaders.

Second, Article 5 of the UDHR states that "no one shall be subjected to torture or to cruel, inhuman or degrading treatment or punishment." However, there have been reports of torture and mistreatment of protesters and detainees by security forces, including beatings, electrocution, and sleep deprivation.

Third, Article 19 of the UDHR states that "everyone has the right to freedom of opinion and expression." However, the military has imposed restrictions on the media and social media platforms, and arrested journalists and media workers who have reported on the protests and the military's actions.

Overall, the military's crackdown on protests in Myanmar represents a serious violation of human rights, as enshrined in the UDHR. The international community has condemned the military's actions and called for a restoration of democracy and the rule of law in Myanmar.
