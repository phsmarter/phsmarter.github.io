12. example of positive and negative externalities​


Examples of positive externality in production

A private school provides an external benefit because the workforce will be more educated in the future and it saves the cost of government education in a publically-funded school.Providing a cafe which employs previously homeless people helps to reduce the social problem of homelessness.

Examples of a negative externality in production

Non-organic vegetable growing. Farmers use fertilizers and pesticides which harm insects and also can get into the food chain, causing health problems in the future.

Negative externalities exist when individuals bear a portion of the cost associated with a goods production without having any influence over the related production decisions.For example,parents may have to pay higher-health-care cost related to pollution-induced asthma among their children because, of increased industrial activity in the neighborhood. Producers do not consider those costs to others in their decisions.As a result, they produce more goods with negative externalities than is efficient, which leads to more environmental degradation than is socially diserable.

Positive externalities also result in inefficient market outcome. However, goods that suffer from positive externalities provide more value to individuals in society than is taken into account by those providing the goods. An example of positive externalities can be seen in the case of college roommates sharing an off-campus apartment. Though a clean kitchen floor is not fully compensated for providing value to all roommates because, of that tho decision to clean the kitchen undervalues the benefits of such an action and the kitchen will go uncleaned more often than is socially diserable such is the case with environmental quality. Because markets tend undervalue goods with positive externalities, market outcome provide a level of environmental quality that is lower then is socially diserable.
