1. 3rd quarter speech about president if I am a president of this Philippines​


Answer:

My fellow Filipinos,

Today, I stand before you as the President of the Philippines, humbled by the trust and confidence you have bestowed upon me. I am honored to have been elected as the leader of this great nation, and I vow to work tirelessly to serve the people of the Philippines to the best of my abilities.

As we face the challenges of the modern world, we must come together as a nation to overcome them. The problems we face are daunting, but I am confident that with hard work and determination, we can overcome any obstacle.

One of the most pressing issues we face today is poverty. Too many of our fellow Filipinos are living in extreme poverty, struggling to make ends meet. As President, I pledge to prioritize poverty reduction efforts, including increasing access to education, job training, and economic opportunities. We will work to create jobs and stimulate economic growth, particularly in the rural areas of our country.

Another critical issue we must address is corruption. Corruption undermines our government's credibility and erodes the trust of our people. As President, I vow to root out corruption at all levels of government and implement measures to prevent it from occurring in the future. We will hold those who engage in corrupt practices accountable for their actions and ensure that justice is served.

We must also prioritize investments in infrastructure, particularly in rural areas, to improve connectivity and promote economic development. Our roads, bridges, and ports must be modernized to support the growth of our economy and improve the quality of life for our citizens.

Lastly, we must address the issue of climate change. The effects of climate change are already being felt in our country, and we must take action to mitigate its impact. As President, I will work to increase investments in renewable energy, reduce carbon emissions, and promote sustainable development practices.

My fellow Filipinos, the challenges we face are great, but I am confident that together, we can overcome them. Let us work hand in hand to build a brighter future for ourselves and future generations. Thank you, and Mabuhay ang Pilipinas


2. 3rd quarter speech about president if I am a president of this Philippines ​


Answer:

My fellow Filipinos,

I stand before you today as the President of this great nation, humbled by the trust and confidence you have placed in me to lead our country forward.

I believe that a strong and prosperous Philippines is within our reach, but it will require hard work, sacrifice, and unity. As your President, I am committed to working tirelessly to achieve our shared goals and aspirations.

One of my top priorities is to address poverty and inequality in our country. No Filipino should go hungry or without basic necessities. We will work towards creating more job opportunities and increasing access to education and healthcare for all Filipinos, regardless of their social status.

We will also prioritize the protection and promotion of human rights, and ensure that justice is served for all. No one should be above the law, including those in positions of power.

Another key area of focus will be the protection of our environment. We are blessed with beautiful natural resources, and it is our responsibility to preserve and protect them for future generations.

We will also work towards enhancing our international relationships and promoting regional stability. We recognize the importance of our relationships with other nations, and we will work towards building stronger partnerships and collaborations.

As your President, I believe in transparency, accountability, and open communication. I will be accessible to all Filipinos, and will listen to your concerns and suggestions. I am committed to working with all sectors of society to build a brighter future for our nation.

In closing, let me assure you that the Philippines has a bright future. Together, we will work towards achieving our shared goals and aspirations, and building a nation that we can all be proud of. Thank you.

Explanation:

Direct speech describes when something is being repeated exactly as it was – usually in between a pair of inverted commas. For example:

She told me, “I’ll come home by 10pm.”

Indirect speech will still share the same information – but instead of expressing someone’s comments or speech by directly repeating them, it involves reporting or describing what was said. An obvious difference is that with indirect speech, you won’t use inverted commas. For example:

She said to me that she would come home by 10pm.
