Subject: Salvation History

Topic: The principle of faith/results of Faith

1. What is a principle?

A principle is a fundamental truth or law that serves as the foundation for a belief or a system of thought.

2. Give three examples of principles as quoted in the module.

- "The righteous shall live by faith."

- "Without faith, it is impossible to please God."

- "The just shall live by faith."

3. Faith in God is trust, confidence in Him, and in His promises.

4. It is dependent on the response of the individual involved, be he a believer, a seeker, or the person himself.

5. The nine results of faith include:

- Salvation

- Justification

- Sanctification

- Righteousness

- Forgiveness

- Peace

- Access to God

- Hope

- Transformation

6. By faith, we trust in God's promises, believe in His existence, and rely on His guidance.

7. By unbelief, we will doubt God's promises, question His existence, and rely on our own understanding.

8. Word Watch:

A) Unbelief: The lack of faith or refusal to believe.

B) Justified: Being declared righteous or acquitted of guilt.

C) Destiny: The predetermined course of events in a person's life.

D) Sanctified: Set apart or made holy for a sacred purpose.

E) Eternity: Infinite or unending time.

9. Essay:

Faith plays a crucial role in salvation as it is through faith that we receive the gift of salvation. By believing in Jesus Christ and His sacrifice on the cross, we are justified before God and receive forgiveness for our sins. Faith enables us to trust in God's promises and surrender our lives to Him.

Not all people are saved because salvation requires a personal response of faith. Each individual has the freedom to accept or reject the offer of salvation. While God desires all to be saved, some choose to reject Him or place their faith in other things. Salvation is a result of both God's grace and an individual's faith working together.

It is important to note that salvation is not based on our own merit or good works but solely on faith in Jesus Christ. It is by God's grace that we are saved, and faith is the means by which we receive that salvation.
