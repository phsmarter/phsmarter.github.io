Deforestation, burning fossil fuels, vehicle pollution, quarrying, and other human activities are some of the primary causes of global warming. These activities release greenhouse gases such as carbon dioxide, methane, and nitrous oxide into the atmosphere, which trap heat and cause the Earth's temperature to rise.

To address these causes of global warming, the government could implement policies and regulations that limit the amount of greenhouse gases that industries and individuals can emit. This could include incentives for using renewable energy sources, such as solar and wind power, and penalties for excessive emissions.

The world could also take collective action to reduce greenhouse gas emissions by promoting sustainable practices, such as reforestation and conservation efforts, reducing meat consumption, and investing in public transportation and other low-carbon alternatives.

Individuals can also make a difference by making small changes in their daily lives, such as reducing energy consumption, using public transportation or carpooling, and supporting environmentally responsible companies.

Addressing the causes of global warming will require a collective effort from governments, businesses, and individuals around the world to reduce greenhouse gas emissions and promote sustainable practices.
