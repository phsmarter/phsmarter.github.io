Analogy plays a significant role in problem solving, as well as decision making, argumentation, perception, generalization, memory, creativity, invention, prediction, emotion, explanation, conceptualization and communication. 25. Does everyone need a working knowledge of accounting in pursuit to the practice of their professions? Why?​


Answer:

As I understand it, you are asking how businesses (or professions) can better understand the accounting function?

An example I can give you is at a law firm I recently worked for, where I was the Practice Manager (bookkeeper, office admin). We had an external accountant but I did the day to day bookkeeping for the firm. The owner of the business (a solicitor who was the Managing Partner) wanted to better understand the financial position of the business and not be reliant on just myself and the external accountants, the latter who charged a huge amount of money. She had good reason to do this, not only because she was ultimately responsible for the financial success and viability of the business, but also so that she could better understand why the accountants were charging such a hefty fee.

After completing an accounting course, she did feel it was a very worthwhile thing to have done. In my opinion a basic level of accounting is essential if you are running your own business, no matter what your profession is.

(OR)

T-accounts are symbolic representations of an account. The name of the account is written above a horizontal line and then a vertical line separates the two sides of an account: debit on the left and credit on the right. In a group of T-accounts, entries on the debit side of accounts must be balanced by credits on other accounts.

Economists use T-accounts to analyze financial situations and cash flows. For example, macroeconomics uses T-accounts to analyze cash flows in national economies to determine things like trade deficits or surpluses, and the effect on the money supply of a given currency. And on a microeconomic level, T-accounts are used to analyze a banks cash flows. In addition, T-accounts are used to estimate the multiplier effect of monetary flows.
