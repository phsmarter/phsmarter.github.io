Morality in the Titanic

The film "Titanic" is riddled with moral dilemmas. In one of the scenes, the owner of Star Line, the shipping company that owned the now-sinking Unsinkable, joins a lowered life-boat. The tortured expression on his face demonstrates that even he experiences more than unease at his own conduct. Prior to the disaster, he instructs the captain to adopt a policy dangerous to the ship. Indeed, it proves fatal. A complicating factor was the fact that only women and children were allowed by the officers in charge into the lifeboats. Another was the discrimination against Third Class passengers. The boats sufficed only to half the number of those on board and the First Class, High Society passengers were preferred over the Low-Life immigrants under deck.

Marxism in Titanic

Titanic makes the Marxist class struggle obvious, and it is one of the most obvious films that depicts a difference in class and the struggle between them.It's also a great example of capitalism. The Titanic itself is a symbol of capitalism and greed; the way the ship's owners worked to make a ship faster, stronger, and more sumptuous for the upper classes is a depiction of what Karl Marx meant when he talked about materialism.The ship itself showed the class struggle, with first-class passengers on the upper deck and third-class passengers unable to reach the higher decks. In terms of characters, the main story of this film revolves around the love of two passengers — Jack Dacuson and Rose DeWitt Bukater of different social classes.When the Titanic collides with an iceberg, the crew locks the steel doors below to prevent third-class passengers from boarding lifeboats. Because they prioritized boarding life boats with first class passengers, the crew also threatened to kill the passengers. This certainly paints a pessimistic view of the rich.Rose's mother, who is preoccupied with her daughter's marriage to Karl – a wealthy man – in order to sustain their depreciating family. Karl gives Rose a big diamond to pique her interests.Furthermore, the Titanic scenes indicate that, as Marx stated, "history is based on unending class struggle." This movie conveys the message that the inequality and unfair treatment of the bourgeoisie and proletariat must be addressed.
