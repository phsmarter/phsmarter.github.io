5. comprehensive report about online shopping​


The act of buying and selling goods and services over the internet is known as online shopping, or e-commerce. Online shopping has become increasingly popular in recent years, with an increasing number of customers preferring to shop online rather than in stores. We will examine the history, growth, advantages, challenges, and anticipated trends of online shopping in depth in this comprehensive report.

History of Online Shopping The idea of shopping online dates back to the early days of the internet, when Amazon launched the first online marketplace in 1995. However, due to the expansion of the internet and the development of more advanced e-commerce technologies, online shopping did not begin to gain widespread popularity until the latter half of the 1990s and the beginning of the 2000s.

Since then, the growth and development of online shopping has continued, with the introduction of new technologies and innovations to enhance the shopping experience for customers on a regular basis.

The expansion of online shopping has been nothing short of extraordinary. Global e-commerce sales were $4.28 trillion in 2020, up from $3.53 trillion in 2019, according to a Statista report. E-commerce sales reached $791.7 billion in the United States alone in 2020, up from $601.7 billion in 2019.

The increasing availability of high-speed internet, the proliferation of mobile devices, the ease of online shopping, and the capacity of e-commerce platforms to provide a wider range of products at lower prices are some of the contributing factors to this expansion.

Benefits of Online Shopping Convenience is one of the biggest advantages of shopping online. Customers can make purchases from the convenience of their own homes when they shop online, without having to worry about traffic, parking, or any of the other issues that come with going to a physical store. Additionally, online shopping provides a wider range of products at lower prices than traditional retail establishments.

Another advantage of shopping online is the ease with which prices and products can be compared. Customers can compare various products from various sellers and find the best deals with just a few clicks.

With the ability to track and save preferences, purchase history, and wish lists, online shopping also provides a more individualized shopping experience. This data can also be used by online retailers to provide customers with personalized recommendations and promotions.

Challenges of Online Shopping Online shopping has many advantages, but it also has a few drawbacks. The threat of identity theft and fraud is one of the most significant obstacles. When doing their shopping online, customers need to exercise extreme caution to use secure payment methods and only provide personal and financial information to reputable sellers.

The inability to touch or see products before making a purchase is yet another obstacle to online shopping. Customers may find it more challenging to make educated purchasing decisions as a result, which may result in a higher rate of returns.

Finally, as consumers become increasingly reliant on technology and may lose the social interactions that come with physical shopping, online shopping can also lead to social isolation.

Trends for the Future of Online Shopping The future of online shopping looks bright, with more growth and new ideas coming soon. The use of artificial intelligence and machine learning to provide more individualized shopping experiences, including personalized recommendations and targeted promotions, is one of the most significant trends in online shopping.

The use of augmented and virtual reality to enhance the shopping experience, giving customers a more immersive way to see and interact with products, is another trend.

Finally, it is anticipated that mobile commerce will continue to expand as an increasing number of customers use their mobile devices to make on-the-go purchases.

Conclusion Since its inception, online shopping has come a long way, and it continues to expand and change rapidly. Despite the benefits and drawbacks, online shopping has become increasingly popular due to its ease of use and extensive product selection.

16. statement of the problemdefinition of terms(operational definition andconceptual definition)Research title:FACTORS INFLUENCING STUDENTS'ONLINE SHOPPING BEHAVIORpa sagot please​


Answer:

The purpose of this research is to identify and understand the factors that influence students' online shopping behavior. The study will aim to investigate the different variables that affect online shopping behavior among students, such as age, gender, income, educational background, convenience, and internet access.

Definition of terms:

1. Online shopping behavior: The process of purchasing goods or services via the internet using electronic devices such as computers, smartphones, or tablets.

2. Factors: The different variables that contribute to the online shopping behavior of students, including demographic, social, and technological factors.

3. Students: Individuals who are currently enrolled in academic institutions, pursuing a degree or certification, and belong to the age group of 18 to 25 years old.

4. Age: A demographic variable that refers to the number of years a person has lived.

5. Gender: A demographic variable that refers to the social and cultural characteristics associated with being male, female, or non-binary.

6. Income: The amount of money earned or received by an individual or household.

7. Educational background: The level of education attained by an individual, such as high school, college, or postgraduate education.

Operational Definition:

The operational definition refers to how the researcher will measure or define the terms used in the study. In this research, operational definitions can be:

1. Online shopping behavior: The frequency and amount of money spent by students on online purchases in the last three months.

2. Factors: The demographic and psychographic variables that influence online shopping behavior, such as age, gender, income, educational background, convenience, and internet access.

3. Students: Individuals who are currently enrolled in a tertiary institution and belong to the age group of 18 to 25 years old.

4. Age: Categorized into three groups: 18-20 years old, 21-23 years old, and 24-25 years old.

5. Gender: Categorized into three groups: male, female, and non-binary.

6. Income: Categorized into three groups: less than $1000, $1000 to $2000, and more than $2000 per month.

7. Educational background: Categorized into three groups: high school, college, and postgraduate education.
