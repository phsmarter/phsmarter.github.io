21. background of the study of organic insecticide ASAP​


Answer & Explanation:

The use of insecticides is a common practice in modern agriculture to control pests and improve crop yields. However, the use of synthetic insecticides has been associated with numerous negative impacts on the environment, including the development of resistance in pest populations, the contamination of soil and water sources, and harm to non-target organisms such as pollinators and beneficial insects.

In response to these issues, there has been a growing interest in the use of organic insecticides, which are derived from natural sources such as plants, minerals, and microorganisms. Organic insecticides have the potential to be effective in controlling pests while also being less harmful to the environment and non-target organisms.

The study of organic insecticides involves exploring the properties and efficacy of various natural compounds and their potential as insecticides. This includes investigating the mechanism of action of these compounds, their toxicity to pests and non-target organisms, and their potential to be used in integrated pest management (IPM) programs.

Furthermore, the development of organic insecticides requires an understanding of the practical considerations involved in their use, such as formulation, application methods, and cost-effectiveness. In addition, the study of organic insecticides also involves investigating the potential for their use in alternative pest control strategies, such as biological control and pheromone-based pest management.

Overall, the study of organic insecticides is an important area of research that has the potential to offer sustainable solutions to pest management while minimizing the negative impacts on the environment and non-target organisms.
