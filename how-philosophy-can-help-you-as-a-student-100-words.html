In simple terms, philosophy can be explained as a science that looks for deep causes and effects of things. In several countries, including the Philippines, philosophy has become a field of science that is usually just starting to be studied among students. However, in some other countries, such as France, Italy, and Germany, it turns out that philosophy is included in the subject of secondary school subjects. And it is proven, high school students in these countries have very good critical thinking skills.

Actually there are many other advantages to be gained from studying philosophy from an early age and it is very helpful for students' lives. Below, I've listed seven reasons to study philosophy for students.

1. We will be a reliable problem solver.

From studying philosophy, we become accustomed to finding accurate causes and effects of a problem. We also find it easier to find solutions to existing problems.

2. Can be a potency to get along with many groups.

There are so many people in this era who flock to study philosophy. Well, if you have studied philosophy at a young age, it is very likely that you will be able to mingle with various types of circles.

3. Not easily trapped by rumors and gossip.

If we are accustomed to analyzing a problem or event critically when studying philosophy, then we will not easily believe and be trapped by issues (or even, conspiracies, haha) in everyday life that are not certain the truth.

4. So it is easier to follow lessons at school.

Philosophy contains the foundations of all sciences. Therefore, studying and applying philosophy means that we already hold important provisions to study many other sciences. So it's easier too, to absorb lessons at school.

5. Philosophy helps us take direction in life as adults.

The most important thing explored in philosophy is the question, "How should we live?" and studying philosophy will also help you find the answer.

6. Can grow the spirit of leadership.

Philosophy helps us see and analyze the good and the bad, especially for the benefit of many people. Hence, philosophers make great leaders.

The earlier you start, the better. As already mentioned, in our country, philosophy is usually only studied in the realm of universities. It's a shame, you know, even though we can start studying philosophy since we were in high school. That way, it will be easier for us to have a good performance in academics when we go to college.

4. How important it is for you to know Philosophy in 100 words?​


Philosophy” comes from Greek words meaning “love of wisdom.” Philosophy uses the tools of logic and reason to analyze the ways in which humans experience the world. It teaches critical thinking, close reading, clear writing, and logical analysis; it uses these to understand the language we use to describe the world, and our place within it. Different areas of philosophy are distinguished by the questions they ask. Do our senses accurately describe reality? What makes wrong actions wrong? How should we live? These are philosophical questions, and philosophy teaches the ways in which we might begin to answer them.

Students who learn philosophy get a great many benefits from doing so. The tools taught by philosophy are of great use in further education, and in employment. Despite the seemingly abstract nature of the questions philosophers ask, the tools philosophy teaches tend to be highly sought-after by employers. Philosophy students learn how to write clearly, and to read closely, with a critical eye; they are taught to spot bad reasoning, and how to avoid it in their writing and in their work. It is therefore not surprising that philosophy students have historically scored more highly on tests like the LSAT and GRE, on average, than almost any other discipline. Many of our students combine studying philosophy with studying other disciplines.

16. How can philosophy help you succeed as a student in obtaining a broad perspective in life.?


Answer:

Philosophy isn't just the most valuable way to obtain a broad perspective in life… it IS obtaining a broad perspective in life. Philosophy is the love of knowledge, the pursuit of truth, and the questioning of all ideas. By studying, discussing, or just thinking about the many ideas in the world, you can find questions you had never thought to ask, or perspectives you had never considered before. Or perhaps, you could find a theory or idea you had already been thinking about on your own, and read about the perspectives and arguments of others who thought along similar lines.

Philosophy is often regarded as some kind of snoody subject for "intellectuals" who want to put themselves above everyone else by knowing fancy words, or something like that. Granted, some people go into philosophy that way, but philosophy is so much more than they make it seem.

If you are in any way curious about the big picture of things, about the questions that no one can easily answer, then philosophy is the best way to feed that curiosity. Being a philosopher, when it comes down to it, is as simple as reading about or listening to the ideas and questions other people have had, and thinking about them for yourself.

There is no better way to broaden your perspective than to learn about the world's wide variety of ideas pertaining to the broadest subjects and greatest mysteries, and that's what philosophy is all about.
