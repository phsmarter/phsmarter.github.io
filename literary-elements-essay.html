Context: "Will of the River" is an essay written by Alfredo Gonzales Jr. about the author's experience living near a river in the Philippines.

Author's Intention: The author's intention is to reflect on the power and unpredictability of nature, as well as to convey a sense of respect and awe for the natural world.

Reaction: The reader is likely to feel a sense of wonder and reverence for nature, as well as a recognition of its ability to inspire fear and awe.

Literary Devices: The essay makes use of vivid sensory details, such as the description of the river's "deep, hollow roar," as well as metaphorical language, such as comparing the river's course to a "swinging scythe." The essay also uses personification, attributing human qualities to the river, such as when the author writes that "the river, like a living being, moves forward."

Literary Technique: The essay makes use of imagery, particularly visual and auditory imagery, to create a vivid sense of the river and its surroundings. The essay also employs the technique of foreshadowing, hinting at the dangers and unpredictability of the river early on in the text.

Beginning: The essay begins with a description of the author's childhood home near the river, as well as the river's power and the respect it inspired in the author and his community.

Middle: The essay continues with an exploration of the river's behavior and the dangers it posed, as well as the author's respect for its power and unpredictability.

Ending of the Story: The essay ends with a reflection on the role of the river in the author's life, and a recognition of the importance of nature in inspiring wonder, respect, and humility in humans.

Allegory
