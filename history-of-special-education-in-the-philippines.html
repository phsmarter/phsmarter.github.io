2. pahingi po ng sagot kailangan ko lang pogive the history of special education in the Philippines started in 2001 into 2023 ​


Answer:

Special education in the Philippines started long before 2001, but significant changes and developments occurred during that period and beyond. Here is a brief history of special education in the Philippines from 2001 to 2023:

2001-2004: The Department of Education (DepEd) initiated the Special Education (SPED) Program in public schools, aiming to provide appropriate education for children with disabilities. It was implemented through the establishment of SPED Centers in selected schools, and training of teachers and staff on inclusive education.

2005-2008: The Philippine government enacted the Magna Carta for Persons with Disabilities, which mandates equal access to education, employment, and social services for persons with disabilities. The law also created the National Council on Disability Affairs (NCDA) to promote the rights and welfare of persons with disabilities.

2009-2012: The DepEd issued policies and guidelines for the implementation of inclusive education in public schools. It includes the integration of children with special needs into regular classrooms, provision of accommodations and support services, and the use of the Individualized Education Program (IEP) for students with disabilities.

2013-2016: The K to 12 Basic Education Program was implemented, which included the integration of SPED into the regular education system. It aims to provide inclusive and quality education for all, including children with special needs. The DepEd also launched the Early Language, Literacy, and Numeracy Program to support the development of basic skills for children with disabilities.

2017-2020: The DepEd strengthened its efforts towards inclusive education by establishing more SPED Centers in public schools, providing training for teachers and staff, and launching programs for the education of children with hearing and visual impairments, autism spectrum disorder, and intellectual disabilities.

2021-2023: The COVID-19 pandemic affected the education sector, including the provision of special education. The DepEd implemented alternative modes of education, such as online and modular learning, to ensure the continuity of education for all learners, including those with special needs. The agency also launched the SPED Online Program, which provides access to online training courses and resources for teachers, parents, and caregivers of children with special needs.

In summary, the history of special education in the Philippines from 2001 to 2023 reflects the government's efforts to promote the rights and welfare of persons with disabilities and provide inclusive education for all learners. While there have been challenges and limitations, significant progress has been made in advancing the education and well-being of children with special needs in the country.


3. pahingi po ng policies na ginawa ng malacañang sa sped create a timeline chart that show the History of the special education in the Philippines started in 2000 and end to 2023​


Answer:

Here are some of the policies related to Special Education (SPED) that were implemented by Malacañang, the official residence of the President of the Philippines, in recent years:

1. Republic Act No. 10533 or the Enhanced Basic Education Act of 2013 - This law mandates the provision of inclusive education, which includes SPED, to all learners. It also provides for the establishment of SPED centers in every school division.

2. Executive Order No. 366 or the Rationalization Plan - This order mandates the Department of Education to allocate 2% of its budget for the development and implementation of SPED programs and services.

3. Department of Education Order No. 13, s. 2018 or the Policy Guidelines on the Implementation of SPED Program in Public Schools - This order provides guidelines on the provision of appropriate education to learners with disabilities in public schools.

4. Department of Education Order No. 20, s. 2018 or the Guidelines on the Conduct of SPED Identification and Placement Process - This order provides guidelines on the process of identifying and placing learners with disabilities in SPED programs.

5. Department of Education Order No. 32, s. 2019 or the Policy Guidelines on the Implementation of the Alternative Learning System for Persons with Disabilities - This order provides guidelines on the provision of alternative learning opportunities for persons with disabilities who are unable to attend regular school.

Here is a timeline chart showing the history of special education in the Philippines from 2000 to 2023:

2000 - Republic Act No. 8980, or the Early Childhood Care and Development Act, is signed into law, which includes provisions for the establishment of SPED programs in every barangay.

2013 - Republic Act No. 10533, or the Enhanced Basic Education Act, is signed into law, which mandates the provision of inclusive education, including SPED, to all learners.

2018 - Department of Education (DepEd) Order No. 13 and 20 are issued, providing guidelines on the implementation of SPED programs in public schools, and the identification and placement process of learners with disabilities.

2019 - DepEd Order No. 32 is issued, providing guidelines on the implementation of the Alternative Learning System for Persons with Disabilities.

2023 - Continuing efforts to improve SPED in the Philippines are expected to be implemented by the government and DepEd.
