Despite swift actions by many governments around the world to protect travel service providers, refocus destination marketing campaigns, and even implement more sustainable management strategies, a larger unknown looms in the back-ground — will tourists actually return? More precisely, when will they return, and will it be soon enough to save destination economies?

Notwithstanding the economic impact of lockdowns, and potentially high levels of unemployment and inflation long after they are eased (which will also infringe upon a person’s ability to spend money on a holiday), Covid-19 demonstrates all too well the unpredictability of exogenous factors and their implications on tourist motivation and tourism demand.

16. 300 essay words, what is Labor Laws in Tourism industry? ​


Labor laws are a set of rules and regulations that govern the relationship between employers and employees in the tourism industry. These laws cover a wide range of issues, including minimum wage, overtime pay, working hours, leave policies, safety and health standards, and discrimination and harassment.

In the tourism industry, labor laws are designed to protect the rights of workers and ensure that they are treated fairly and with respect. This is particularly important in an industry that often relies on low-wage, seasonal, and part-time workers.

Minimum wage laws set the minimum amount that an employer must pay an employee for their work. These laws vary by state and may also be different for different types of workers. For example, tipped employees, such as servers and bartenders, may be paid a lower minimum wage than non-tipped employees.

Overtime pay laws require employers to pay employees time and a half for any hours worked over a certain number in a week. These laws also vary by state, with some states requiring overtime pay for any hours over 8 per day or 40 per week, while others have different thresholds.

Working hours laws regulate the number of hours that an employee can be required to work in a day or week. These laws may also include provisions for breaks and meal periods.

Leave policies, such as vacation, sick leave, and family leave, allow employees to take time off work for personal or medical reasons. These policies may be required by law or may be offered by the employer as a benefit.

Safety and health standards are designed to protect workers from hazardous conditions and accidents on the job. These standards may include training programs, safety equipment, and procedures for handling emergencies.

Discrimination and harassment laws prohibit employers from treating employees unfairly on the basis of their race, religion, gender, age, or other protected characteristics. These laws also prohibit harassment or abuse of employees by coworkers or supervisors.

Overall, labor laws in the tourism industry play a critical role in protecting the rights and well-being of workers and promoting fair and equitable treatment in the workplace.

20. Essay:Identify and discuss the scope and market of tourism industry and how it contributes to the economy.​


Answer:

The scope of the tourism industry is incredibly broad and encompasses everything from traditional forms of leisure travel, such as beach vacations and sightseeing, to more specialized forms of travel, such as eco-tourism, cultural tourism, and adventure travel. The tourism industry market is composed of consumers (travelers), businesses (hotels, airlines, tour operators, etc.) and governments (local, regional, and national).

The global tourism industry contributes significantly to the world economy. According to the World Travel and Tourism Council, in 2018, the industry directly contributed to 10.4% of global gross domestic product (GDP). Furthermore, the industry is estimated to have indirectly contributed to another 4.5% of global GDP. In terms of global employment, the industry is estimated to have directly contributed to 319 million jobs in 2018, which represents 1 in 10 jobs in the world. These figures are expected to grow in the coming years, with the World Travel and Tourism Council estimating that the industry will create an additional 75 million jobs by 2028.

30. 2.WRITE AN ESSAY ABOUT TOURISM, CULTURE, ARTS AND RECREATION.​


Answer:

Tourism, culture, arts, and recreation are all interconnected aspects that contribute to the enrichment of our lives. Each element has its own unique features that attract people from all over the world, creating a global exchange of knowledge, experiences, and values.

Tourism is the act of traveling to a new place for leisure, business, or other purposes. It involves visiting different locations, experiencing new cultures, and interacting with local people. Tourism has a significant impact on the economy of many countries, as it generates employment opportunities, foreign exchange earnings, and investments in infrastructure development.

Culture, on the other hand, refers to the customs, traditions, beliefs, and values of a particular group of people. It encompasses various aspects such as language, food, clothing, music, dance, and art. Cultural tourism is a form of tourism that allows people to explore and learn about the cultural heritage of a place. It can include visiting museums, historical sites, festivals, and other cultural events.

Arts, which include music, visual arts, performing arts, and literature, are an important part of human culture. They provide a medium for self-expression and creativity, and they play a significant role in preserving the cultural identity of a community. The arts also play a crucial role in tourism, as many people travel to experience and appreciate different forms of artistic expression.

Finally, recreation refers to the leisure activities that people engage in for enjoyment and relaxation. This can include sports, outdoor activities, hobbies, and other forms of entertainment. Recreation is essential for maintaining physical and mental health, as well as social well-being.

In conclusion, tourism, culture, arts, and recreation are all interconnected and important aspects of our lives. They provide us with opportunities to learn, experience, and appreciate the diversity of the world we live in. By promoting and supporting these aspects, we can create a world that is more vibrant, inclusive, and fulfilling for everyone.
