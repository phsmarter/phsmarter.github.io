SAMPLING TECHNIQUE USED BY THE RESEARCHER  Systematic random sampling  Simple random sampling Stratified random sampling Systematic random samplng Stratified random sampling Sytematic random sampling Cluster random sampling   Systematic random sampling Systematic random sampling Cluster random samplingSampling Method

    The methods for selecting members of the population to participate in the study are known as sampling methods. Researchers use samples rather than working with the entire population since the majority of the time, the research's population is large, and it's impossible for them to collect all of the necessary data for each object in the study. The following are five methods for selecting a sample:

SIMPLE RANDON SAMPLING  A simple random sample is a subset of a population that has been randomly picked. Every member of the population has an exact equal probability of getting chosen using this sampling procedure.   A simple random sample is a randomly chosen subset of a population. This sampling process ensures that every member of the population has an exact equal chance of being chosen.SYSTEMATIC RANDOM SAMPLING Systematic sampling is a type of probability sampling in which people from a larger population are randomly selected from that group at a fixed, periodic interval. By dividing the population size by the desired sample size, a predetermined periodic interval known as the sampling interval is calculated. If the periodic interval is predetermined and the starting point is chosen at random, systematic sampling is still considered random.STRATIFIED RANDOM SAMPLING Researchers can use stratified random sampling to get a sample population that is representative of the overall population under study.CLUSTER RANDOM SAMPLING Cluster sampling is a technique for breaking down a population into smaller groupings called clusters. They then select clusters at random to create a sample. Cluster sampling is a sort of probability sampling that is widely used to investigate large populations, particularly those dispersed across a vast geographic area. Typically, researchers employ pre-existing units as clusters, such as schools or cities.

It has become increasingly common, especially among teenagers, as the digital sphere has expanded and technology has advanced.Cyberbullying is when someone, typically a teenager, bullies or harasses others on the internet and other digital spaces, particularly on social media sites.
