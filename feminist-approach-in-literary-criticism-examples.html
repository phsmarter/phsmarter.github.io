1. what is feminist approach in literary criticism ​


Answer:

Feminist literary criticism looks at literature assuming its production from a male-dominated perspective. It re-examines canonical works to show how gender stereotypes are involved in their functioning. It examines (and often rediscovers) works by women for a possible alternative voice.

Answer:

Feminist criticism is concerned with "the ways in which literature (and other cultural productions) reinforce or undermine the economic, political, social, and psychological oppression of women" (Tyson 83).

17. in what ways can the feminist approach in literary criticism help us better understand the representation of gender and the experiences of women in literature?​


Answer:

The feminist approach in literary criticism can help us better understand how gender is represented and constructed in literature, and how this representation reflects and reinforces cultural norms and power relations. By examining the portrayal of female characters, feminist critics can uncover the ways in which women are often marginalized, objectified, or stereotyped in literature. They can also explore how women's experiences and perspectives are often overlooked or misrepresented, and how this can perpetuate patriarchal attitudes and values.

Feminist literary criticism can also help us understand the historical and social context in which literature is produced and received, and how this context shapes the representation of gender. For example, feminist critics can analyze the ways in which literary works respond to or challenge social and political movements such as the women's suffrage movement or the feminist movement of the 1960s and 70s. They can also examine the ways in which literary works reflect or resist dominant cultural myths and ideologies about women, such as the idea of the "angel in the house" or the "femme fatale."

Furthermore, feminist literary criticism can help us recognize the diversity of women's experiences and identities, and the ways in which these experiences intersect with other social categories such as race, class, and sexuality. By examining the representation of women from different backgrounds and perspectives, feminist critics can challenge essentialist and stereotypical notions of femininity, and promote a more nuanced and inclusive understanding of gender and identity in literature.
