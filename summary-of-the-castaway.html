A Quote on Quote: "Faith is a house with many rooms."

This quote means that faith is like a house which confines many rooms. Which goes to show that our faith can be expressed in many different ways.  

"Doubt is useful; it keeps faith a living thing. After all, you cannot know the strength of your faith until it is tested."

Doubt makes people eager to know things. As they work on to know many different things, people keeps their faith working too. But once their faith is put under the test or they are being tried, then they will know for themselves whether they have been stronger or whether they are weak and lacks the faith.

"No one has seen that island since, and you’d never read about those trees in any book. And yet, if I hadn’t found those shores I would have died, if I hadn’t discovered that tooth I would have been lost alone forever."

According to Pi, there are things that people never realizes how beautiful they were and they were never noticed of. But, if he never got the chance to reach the shore, he might have died and without his tiger friend he would have been journeying alone.

"Even when God seemed to have abandoned me, He was watching. Even when he seemed indifferent to my suffering, and when I was beyond all hope saving, He gave me rest and gave me a sign to continue my journey.” “It happened, it happened. Why should it have to mean anything?"

Even if Pi felt that God does not seem to care for him or might have ignored him, he knew that God is looking down on him. God might seem to Pi that is not a caring God but the truth is, God gave him hope and the courage to continue with his journey. He had realized that everything happened for a reason but he cannot understand why since it does not mean anything to him.

"For castaways, who must share their lifeboats with large, dangerous carnivores, it is advisable to establish a territory as your own."

On this part of the story, Pi considers himself a castaway with many companions since he was riding a lifeboat together with his Bengal tiger, a zebra, an orangutan, and a spotted hyena. However, their ride together does not last since everyone needs to survive. Everyone needs to protect himself or oneself in order to survive. This is what Pi is referring to as establishing a territory of your own where nobody can enter or pass.

Explanation:

Choose a day when waves are moderate, but regular. Step two: with the lifeboat facing into the waves, making the ride as comfortable as possible, blow your whistle soothingly. Step three: turn the boat sideways to the waves, accompanied by harsh, aggressive use of the whistle. With sufficient repetition, the animal will associate the sound of the whistle with the discomfort of seasickness. Similar methods have long been used by circus trainers, though they generally lack access to rough seas.
