Filipino author and national hero Jose Rizal's book Noli Me Tangere describes the social and political climate in the Philippines during Spanish rule. The use of symbols, images, and gestures that convey a deeper meaning beyond their literal interpretation is referred to as indicated in the book.

They are the evaluative statements in the essay:

The book's dialogues and narration make extensive use of Spanish terminology and phrases to illustrate how the Spanish colonizers influenced Filipino culture and language.

The representation of characters - Rizal used different characters in the book to represent different social classes and political views prevalent in the Philippines during the Spanish colonization.

The book has many symbols that Rizal used to express various ideas, such as the sampaguita flower, which stands for innocence and purity, and the collapsing wall, which represents the decline of Spanish colonial control.

The way in which events are portrayed in the book highlights the injustices and oppression endured by the Filipino people under the rule of the Spanish colonists. Rizal.
