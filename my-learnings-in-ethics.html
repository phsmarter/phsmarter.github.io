11. lessons that you learned about professional ethics​


JOURNAL ARTICLE Lessons Learned from Ethics in the Classroom: Exploring Student Growth in Flexibility, Complexity and Comprehension Patricia J. Carlson and Frances Burke Journal of Business Ethics Vol. 17, No. 11 (Aug., 1998), pp. 1179-1187 (9 pages) Published By: Springer https://www.jstor.org/stable/25073949Abstract This study shows the link between teaching ethics in a college setting and the evolution of student thinking about ethical dilemmas. At the beginning of the semester, students have a rigid "black and white" conception of ethics. By the end of the semester, they are thinking more flexibly about the responsibilities of leaders in corporate ethical dilemmas, and they are able to appreciate complex situations that influence ethical behavior. The study shows that education in ethics produces more "enlightened" consumers of ethics information who are able to make sound determinations about responsibility in ethical dilemmas.

Journal Information The Journal of Business Ethics publishes original articles from a wide variety of methodological and disciplinary perspectives concerning ethical issues related to business. Since its initiation in 1980, the editors have encouraged the broadest possible scope. The term 'business' is understood in a wide sense to include all systems involved in the exchange of goods and services, while 'ethics' is circumscribed as all human action aimed at securing a good life. Systems of production, consumption, marketing, advertising, social and economic accounting, labour relations, public relations and organisational behaviour are analysed from a moral viewpoint. The style and level of dialogue involve all who are interested in business ethics – the business community, universities, government agencies and consumer groups. Speculative philosophy as well as reports of empirical research are welcomed. In order to promote a dialogue between the various interested groups as much as possible, papers are presented in a style relatively free of specialist jargon.

Publisher Information Springer is one of the leading international scientific publishing companies, publishing over 1,200 journals and more than 3,000 new books annually, covering a wide range of subjects including biomedicine and the life sciences, clinical medicine, physics, engineering, mathematics, computer sciences, and economics.

13. my expectation in ethics subject


The subject of ethics in education is considered as part of the human right to get an education. It aims to receive knowledge. Ethics has a very vital and productive role in the world of education. Ethics in education helps to run the system smoothly.

It sets applicable standards and protects the interests of students and teachers. To be a better citizen, ethics must be put as a way in the education system. Teachers are responsible for helping develop students' personalities, as well as acting as mentors for them. On the other hand, students must realize the importance of responsibility and move by implementing ethical practices that develop over time.

Although we know, it takes patience to practice ethics and understand that every student is different. In this way students not only know the function of ethics, but also understand the importance of learning responsibility and practicing self-discipline. Ethics is an important part of decision making, but unfortunately not all students understand what ethics is. Some even do not understand the difference between ethics and morals, distinguish between good and bad.

Teachers will seek to help students grow ethically, both in terms of awareness and understanding. So apply ethics at school and in the social environment.

There are five basic ethical principles in education for students, including:

Don't hurt: try not to hurt others with your actionsChange things for the better: help people, and know the difference between helping and hurting.Respect others: always speak the truth and keep promises. Be someone people can trust.Be fair: keep your emotional balance, don't punish people unnecessarily.Loving: being kind and loving to others by showing concern.

In the end, my expectation to the subject of ethics is the first to cultivate the intellectual dimension. Second, it is important to develop critical thinking skills. Especially the ability to reflect and effect theoretical and practical as a personal human being as well as a social being. Another ethical function is for ethical orientation, which is needed in taking a reasonable attitude in conditions of pluralism.
