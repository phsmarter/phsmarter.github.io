22. what does it mean whatever is right does not mean its good and whatever is good does not mean it is right​


Answer

To understand this statement, first, learn the difference between Right from Good.

In a simple explanation, RIGHT is related to a Person's action or duty, while GOOD is related to principles and beliefs. For example, you report a person to the store guard for stealing at an item. That is just the RIGHT deed to do. On the other hand, you try to cover up the unlawful act and try to persuade that person never to do it again. That is a GOOD deed to do.

So the statement, "whatever is right doesn't mean it is good and whatever is good doesn't mean it is right" is a case to case basis of what is morally correct in society. For example, what if that person stole again? Then, you did a GOOD thing by giving the person a chance. However, it became wrong because you realize it was not the RIGHT thing to do. What if that person stopped stealing? Then the GOOD deed you did became a RIGHT deed.

30. is it right to be dishonest in a good cause?​


Answer:

Is it right to be dishonest in a good cause?

- All humans are liars, and we lie in different ways for a variety of reasons.

Most of us, I assume, lie for polite or social reasons, i.e, to be nice, to be appropriate, to follow social norms, etc.

I personally often lie for those very reasons, but I never maliciously lie or tell lies designed to harm or hurt someone. I lie about seemingly small, innocuous things, but according to many ethicists, all lies are equally bad — so any type of lie told for any reason is equally malicious.

But I don’t see how the world (people) could function harmoniously and productively and equitably if we all told the complete, honest truth ALL THE TIME, in every situation, no matter how small or trivial. If we always broadcasted our genuine beliefs or opinions or feelings about something or someone, that would cause all kinds of mayhem —not only to ourselves but to those around us.

The truth is, not everyone wants the truth all the time, and not every situation demands the truth.

In fact, many of our social norms and social conventions dictate that we ARE NOT supposed to be truthful all the time if doing so would be unnecessarily rude or offensive or improper.

The truth is that we often have to lie and/or deceive in at least small ways in order for us to be polite and socially appropriate and inoffensive. A person who walked around being totally honest 24/7 about everything, in all situations, no matter the context, would be directed to the nearest psych ward for a mental evaluation.

From a social standpoint, yes, some lies are good because they are necessary to keep the peace and promote social stability and harmony, as well as to adhere to and meet the demands of social convention and social ritual
