25. write aN essay about Death penalty Using The INFORMATIVE , PERSUASIVE & ARGUMENTATIVE ​


Introduction:

The death penalty is a legal process whereby a person is put to death by the state as a punishment for a crime. The use of the death penalty has been a subject of debate for many years. Some people argue that the death penalty is necessary to deter crime and punish heinous crimes. Others argue that it is inhumane and ineffective. This essay will discuss the death penalty from three different perspectives: informative, persuasive, and argumentative.

Informative:

The death penalty is a controversial issue that has been debated for many years. Supporters of the death penalty argue that it is necessary to deter crime and punish heinous crimes. They also argue that it is a just punishment for murderers and other violent criminals. Opponents of the death penalty argue that it is inhumane and ineffective. They also argue that it is racially biased and unfairly targets the poor.

The use of the death penalty varies from country to country. Some countries, such as the United States, China, and Iran, continue to use the death penalty. Other countries, such as Canada, Australia, and most European countries, have abolished the death penalty.

Persuasive:

The death penalty is a necessary punishment for heinous crimes such as murder, rape, and terrorism. It serves as a deterrent to potential criminals and provides closure for the families of the victims. The death penalty is also a just punishment for those who commit these heinous crimes.

Opponents of the death penalty argue that it is inhumane and ineffective. However, there are methods of execution, such as lethal injection, that are humane and painless. Additionally, studies have shown that the death penalty does deter crime, particularly murders.

Another argument against the death penalty is that it unfairly targets the poor and minorities. However, this argument is not supported by the facts. The death penalty is applied equally to all races and socioeconomic groups. In fact, studies have shown that the death penalty is more likely to be applied to those who commit the most heinous crimes, regardless of their race or socioeconomic status.

In conclusion, the death penalty is a necessary punishment for heinous crimes. It serves as a deterrent to potential criminals, provides closure for the families of the victims, and is a just punishment for those who commit these crimes.

Argumentative:

The death penalty is an inhumane and ineffective punishment for heinous crimes. It is a violation of human rights and does not serve as a deterrent to potential criminals. Additionally, the death penalty is racially biased and unfairly targets the poor.

One of the main arguments against the death penalty is that it is inhumane. The use of the death penalty is a violation of the right to life and human dignity. It is also a cruel and unusual punishment that does not fit the crime. The use of the death penalty also carries the risk of executing innocent people.

Another argument against the death penalty is that it is ineffective. Studies have shown that the death penalty does not deter crime, particularly murders. In fact, states that have abolished the death penalty have seen a decrease in the murder rate.

The death penalty is also racially biased and unfairly targets the poor. Studies have shown that the death penalty is more likely to be applied to African Americans and those who cannot afford a good defense. This is a clear violation of the principle of equal justice under the law.

In conclusion, the death penalty is an inhumane and ineffective punishment for heinous crimes. It violates human rights, does not deter crime, and is racially biased. The use of the death penalty should be abolished in all countries.
