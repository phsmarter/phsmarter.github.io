Answer:

Research AIM:

The aim of this study is to investigate the effects of sleep deprivation on the academic performance of senior high school students.

Why the need for this study?

The need for this study arises from the growing concern about the impact of sleep deprivation on students' academic performance. Many senior high school students often sacrifice their sleep to cope with academic demands, extracurricular activities, and social engagements. This practice can have adverse effects on their academic performance, as sleep deprivation can lead to impaired cognitive function, memory consolidation, and attention span. Additionally, sleep-deprived students may experience mood swings, irritability, and decreased motivation, which can further impact their academic performance. Therefore, this study is necessary to investigate the extent of the relationship between sleep deprivation and academic performance and to provide insights into effective strategies for improving students' sleep quality and academic outcomes.

15. What is research gap of sleep deprivation ​


Answer:

The research gap in sleep deprivation refers to the lack of comprehensive understanding of the underlying causes, impacts, and effective interventions to address this issue. While numerous studies have been conducted on sleep deprivation, there is still much that is not known about the causes and consequences of chronic sleep deprivation, including the long-term health effects, impact on cognitive function, and links to mental health conditions. Furthermore, there is a gap in understanding effective interventions and treatments to address sleep deprivation, especially among high-risk populations such as shift workers, medical professionals, and students. Addressing the research gap in sleep deprivation is critical for developing evidence-based interventions to improve sleep quality and promote overall health and wellbeing.

24. The impact of sleep deprivation on academic performance of selected grade 11 students in holy rosary academy​


Answer:

Background: Sleep is an inseparable part of human health and life, which is crucial in learning, practice, as well as physical and mental health. It affects the capacity of individual learning, academic performance, and neural-behavioral functions. This study aimed to determine the relationship between sleep quality and students’ academic achievement among students at Kermanshah University of Medical Sciences.

Methods: In this cross-sectional study, 102 medical students from different fields, with maximum variation sampling, completed Pittsburgh Sleep Quality Index (PSQI). For data analysis, SPSS 19 was used through which Pearson correlation test, Spearman test, and t-test were employed.

Results: Based on the quality of sleep questionnaire scores, the results indicated no significant difference between students with high grades and those with low grades. However, there were moderate and sometimes severe sleep disturbances in both groups.

Conclusion: The results showed no significant difference between sleep quality and academic achievement. Nevertheless, longitudinal study should be performed to control for confounding factors.
