3. final exam should be abolished(possible question for debate)​


Answer:

The proposition that final exams should be abolished is a possible topic for a debate. Some possible arguments for this proposition could include:

Final exams are a stressful and high-stakes way of evaluating student learning, which can lead to anxiety and other negative mental health effects.Final exams often do not accurately reflect the full range of a student's knowledge and skills, and can lead to unfair or inaccurate grading.There are other forms of assessment, such as projects, papers, and presentations, that can provide a more comprehensive and authentic evaluation of student learning.Abolishing final exams could free up valuable time and resources that could be used for other forms of learning and assessment.

Some possible arguments against this proposition could include:

Final exams provide a consistent and standardized way of evaluating student learning, which allows for fair and accurate grading.

Final exams can serve as a useful review and synthesis of the material learned over the course of a semester or academic year.

Final exams can help students develop important skills, such as time management, organization, and study habits.

Abolishing final exams could result in a lack of accountability and motivation for students to consistently engage with their coursework throughout the semester.

14. bkit dapat sang-ayunan ang K-12 program? "debate"​


Answer:

Ang programang K-12 ay binuo upang mapataas ang kalidad ng edukasyon sa bansa. May ilang mga dahilan kung bakit dapat itong sang-ayunan:

1. Nakakatugon sa pangangailangan ng mga mag-aaral: Sa pamamagitan ng pagpapalawig ng kurikulum ng paaralan, nagbibigay ito ng mga oportunidad sa mga mag-aaral upang mas malawak na maunawaan ang mga konsepto at maipamalas ang kanilang kasanayan sa mga iba't ibang larangan. Hindi lamang ito nagbibigay ng mas malawak na kaalaman, ngunit nagbibigay rin ito ng mas maraming mga oportunidad sa mga mag-aaral na makapili ng kanilang karera o propesyon.

2. Nagpapakita ng internasyunal na pagkilala: Ang pagkakaroon ng K-12 program ay nagbibigay ng pagkakataon sa mga mag-aaral na magpatuloy sa pangkalahatang edukasyon sa ibang bansa, kung nais nilang gawin ito. Ito ay dahil sa karamihan ng mga bansa sa mundo ay gumagamit ng K-12 program, kaya't may magandang pagkakataon para sa mga mag-aaral na magpatuloy ng kanilang pag-aaral sa ibang bansa.

3. Pumapalakas sa ekonomiya ng bansa: Ang mga mag-aaral na nakapagtapos ng K-12 ay may mas malawak na kaalaman at kasanayan, kaya't may mas mabuting pagkakataon sa kanilang propesyon. Sa pamamagitan ng pagkakaroon ng mga propesyunal na ito, ang bansa ay maaaring magkaroon ng mas malakas na ekonomiya.

4. Nagbibigay ng pagkakataon sa mas mahusay na kasanayan: Sa pamamagitan ng K-12 program, mayroong higit na pagkakataon para sa mga mag-aaral na masanay sa mga praktikal na kasanayan. Sa halip na mag-focus lamang sa pagsusulat at pagbabasa, nagbibigay ang K-12 program ng pagkakataon para sa mga mag-aaral na masanay sa mga kasanayan sa paggawa, pagluluto, at mga iba pang praktikal na kasanayan.

Sa kabuuan, dapat sang-ayunan ang programang K-12 dahil ito ay nagbibigay ng mas maraming oportunidad sa mga mag-aaral upang mapataas ang kanilang mga kasanayan at kaalaman. Ito ay nagbibigay rin ng mga oportunidad sa mga mag-aaral na magpatuloy ng kanilang pag-aaral sa ibang bansa at makapagbigay ng mas malakas na ekonomiya sa bansa.

Answer:

1. Issue: The K to 12 education system has been a controversial topic in many countries, with some arguing that it is unnecessary and should be abolished.

2. Claim: K to 12 should not be abolished.

3. Argument: The K to 12 education system is important because it provides students with a more comprehensive education, better prepares them for college and the workforce, and promotes a more equitable and inclusive education system. The additional years of schooling in K to 12 allow students to develop critical thinking and problem-solving skills, as well as knowledge in specialized areas such as technical-vocational education. Moreover, the K to 12 system provides students with a smoother transition to college and the workforce, as they are better prepared for the demands and challenges of higher education and the job market. Lastly, the K to 12 system promotes a more equitable and inclusive education system by providing opportunities for all students, regardless of their socio-economic background, to receive a quality education.

4.Evidence:

According to the Department of Education in the Philippines, the K to 12 system has been successful in improving the quality of education, with more students passing the college entrance exams and being employed after graduation.

A study conducted by the World Bank showed that the K to 12 system has led to a more equitable education system, with increased access to education for students in rural and remote areas.

A survey conducted by the Philippine Statistics Authority showed that 71.2% of K to 12 graduates are employed, compared to 59.7% of high school graduates.

5. Counter argument: Some argue that the K to 12 system is unnecessary and places an additional burden on families, as they have to pay for an additional two years of schooling.

6. Rebuttal: While it is true that the K to 12 system may be an added expense for some families, the benefits of the additional years of schooling outweigh the costs. Moreover, the government provides financial assistance and scholarship programs for students who are unable to afford the additional years of schooling. Ultimately, the K to 12 system is an investment in the future of students and the country, as it provides a more comprehensive and equitable education system that better prepares students for success in college and the workforce.
