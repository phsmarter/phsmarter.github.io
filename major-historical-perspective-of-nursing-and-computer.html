The 18th century lasted from January 1, 1701 (MDCCI) to December 31, 1800 (MDCCC). During the century, slave trading and human trafficking expanded on a global scale. 30. the following are the three major perspectives in sociology​

Sociology includes three major theoretical perspectives: the functionalist perspective, the conflict perspective, and the symbolic interactionist perspective (sometimes called the interactionist perspective, or simply the micro view).

Answer: The people in every society have different characteristics and behaviors which gave birth to the scientific study called Sociology. In order for the Sociologists to easily understand the society, the following three major perspectives in sociology were made: Structural Functionalism, Symbolic Interactionism, and Conflict Perspective.  

Explanation:

The Structural Functionalism views society as a complex interconnected system of parts that work together in harmony. It approaches society from a macro-level perspective wherein society and its pieces are viewed as whole. Examples of structures or institutions of society include: education, health care, family, legal system, economy, and religion.

The Symbolic Interactionism is an individual level approach in a micro-level. The primary focus in this approach is communication and exchange of ideas using symbols. Here are some examples of symbolic interactionism in different societies: national flag, gender, colorism, relationship roles and rainbow.  

The Conflict Perspective is also a macro-level approach that focuses on inequality. Inequality is a never-ending issue especially the unequal distribution of desired resources which mainly and highly causes conflict within a society. Conflicts in a society basically arise when those that do not have resources create new ways to acquire resources that are equal and the same to those already have the resources. This has been viewed as a constant conflict in a society.
