1. 1) What are the differences between mitosis and meiosis? 2) Give the similarities between mitosis and meiosis. 3) What are the dangers of mitosis? 4) What is the importance of meiosis?


Answer:

1. Mitosis is a type of cell division for somatic cells and for the asexual reproduction of unicellular eukaryotic cells. Meiosis is the type of cell division for the production of gametes in sexual reproduction.

2. Both mitosis and meiosis involve cell division. Both the processes occur in the M-phase of the cell cycle. In both cycles, the typical stages are prophase, metaphase, anaphase and telophase. In both cycles, synthesis of DNA takes place.

3. Mistakes during mitosis lead to the production of daughter cells with too many or too few chromosomes, a feature known as aneuploidy.

4. Meiosis is important because it ensures that all organisms produced via sexual reproduction contain the correct number of chromosomes.

17. 1. What are the similarities and differences of mitosis and meiosis?​


Answer:

Similarities: Both Mitosis and Meiosis are types of cell division in which one cell gives rise to more than one cell. Both follow duplication of chromosomes. Both include breakdown of the nuclear membrane. Both require spindle fibers to move chromosomes around. Both require chromosomes to line up in the middle of the cell (although in mitotic division, the chromosomes line up single file, whereas in meiosis 1 of meiotic division, the chromosomes line up in pairs (homologous). Meiosis 2 of meiotic division is similar to mitotic division in that the chromosomes line up single file.

Differences: Mitosis gives rise to two identical cells- identical to each other and to the parent cell from which they came. Meiosis is a two- division process that gives rise to four cells that are not identical to each other (due to cross over events) nor are they identical to the cell from which they came. The daughter cells (the four produced) have half the number of chromosomes as the parent cell. This type of division gives rise to gametes (eggs and sperm) in animal life cycles, and spores in plant and fungal life cycles.
