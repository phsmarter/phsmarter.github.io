2. give examples of how Definition and Classification is applied beyond writing academic essays​


Answer:

1: Choose an abstract word with a complex meaning.[1]

A simple word that refers to a concrete word will not give you much to write about, but a complex word that refers to an abstract concept provides more material to explore.

Typically, nouns that refer to a person, place, or thing are too simple for a definition essay. Nouns that refer to an idea work better, however, as do most adjectives.

For example, the word “house” is fairly simple and an essay written around it may be dull. By switching to something slightly more abstract like “home,” however, you can play around with the definition more. A “home” is a concept, and there are many elements involved in the creation of a “home.” In comparison, a “house” is merely a structure.

 

2: Make sure that the word is disputable.

Aside from being complex, the word should also refer to something that can mean different things to different people.

A definition essay is somewhat subjective by nature since it requires you to analyze and define a word from your own perspective. If the answer you come up with after analyzing a word is the same answer anyone else would come up with, your essay may appear to lack depth.

 

3: Choose a word you have some familiarity with.

Dictionary definitions can only tell you so much. Since you need to elaborate on the word you choose to define, you will need to have your own base of knowledge or experience with the concept you choose.

For instance, if you have never heard the term “pedantic,” your understanding of the word will be limited. You can introduce yourself to the word for your essay, but without previous understanding of the concept, you will not know if the definition you describe is truly fitting.

 

4: Read the dictionary definition.

While you will not be relying completely on the dictionary definition for your essay, familiarizing yourself with the official definition will allow you to compare your own understanding of the concept with the simplest, most academic explanation of it.

As an example, one definition of “friend” is “a person attached to another by feelings of affection or personal regard.”[2] Your own ideas or beliefs about what a “friend” really is likely include much more information, but this basic definition can present you with a good starting point in forming your own.

 

5: Research the word’s origins.

Look up your chosen word in the Oxford English Dictionary or in another etymology dictionary.[3]

These sources can tell you the history behind a word, which can provide further insight on a general definition as well as information about how a word came to mean what it means today

3. ESSAY GIVE YOUR OWN EXAMPLE SENTENCES/DIALOGUES OF EACH CLASSIFICATION. 5 SENTENCES/DIALOGUES IN EACH CLASSIFICATION Oral Communication in Context​


Answer:

Explanation:

1. Directed Dialogues

It is considered as the simplest kind of interlocution that is used in literary writing. This interaction happens directly between two people – character A & character B.

“What did you eat for breakfast?”

“Eggs and ham.”

2. Misdirected Dialogue

This looks like a natural communication between two participants where they do not answer direct questions. The tone and the manner of the communication might vary only when a third participant interrupts in the conversation.

“It’s pretty sunny.”

“Then it’s a good day to have a picnic.”

3. Modulated Conversation

In this type of dialogue, the conversation provides important details. This generally increases the tension of narration or it might reveal the key aspect of the conversation. It is the gradual retrospection and a way to develop a plot or observation.

“You are never around when I was growing up.”

“I had to work very hard so you can grow up.”

4. Interpolation Conversation

This type of conversation is used for the insertion. In simple words, interpret some information where you are not given important details directly.  

“I’m having a fever.”

“You must have colds.”

5. Inner (Internal) Dialogue  

In inner (internal) dialogue, the people speak to themselves and express their attitudes or opinions.  People use inner dialogue to express their flow of consciousness and loneliness.  

Example:

‘Want to come over to my place and watch television?’ John asks as the train pulls out of town. ‘It’s Thunderbirds today.’

6. Outer Dialogue  

Outer dialogue is a dialogue where people talk to other people. It is a classical dialogue and this type of dialogue is seen most of the time.  

Example of dialogues:

• In first-person narration:

• I cannot believe I hurt my brother. Will he ever forgive me?

• In third-person narration:

• “I cannot believe I hurt my brother. Will he ever forgives me?” wondered James.

Give the 3 special classification of folk danceESSAYAs a student, how will you empower and nurture traditional folk dance and ethnic dances in the Philippines.​


Answer:

1. Philippine Folk Dance is a traditional dance form that reflects the distinct cultural heritage and identity of the Filipino people. It encompasses various indigenous artistic expressions, including music, literature, and visual arts.

2. The primary objectives of folk dance are to preserve cultural heritage, promote social cohesion and solidarity, and provide entertainment for the community. Furthermore, it aims to foster national pride and identity, promote physical fitness and well-being, and enhance individuals' self-esteem.

3. The three values of folk dance are cultural identity, diversity, and creativity. It reflects the unique cultural identity of the people of the Philippines, celebrates the country's diversity and promotes creative expressions of the art form.

4. The four factors affecting folk dance are historical context, cultural diversity, socio-economic conditions, and technological advancements.

5. The four classifications of Philippine Folk Dance are tribal or ethnolinguistic dances, rural or country dances, court or palace dances, and religious or ceremonial dances.

6. The two geographical extents of origin are Luzon and Mindanao.

7. The eight nature of folk dance are communal, traditional, expressive, symbolic, celebratory, rhythmic, creative, and participatory. Examples of communal dances are Pandango sa Ilaw and Tinikling. Examples of celebratory dances are Ati-Atihan and Sinulog.

8. The four movements of folk dance are turning, hopping, leaping, and sliding.

9. The three formations of folk dance are circular, square, and straight line.

10. The three special classifications of folk dance are ancient or pre-colonial dances, fusion or modernized dances, and revival or recreation dances.
