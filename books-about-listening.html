BIBLIOGRAPHY

Articles are cited using the author's name, followed by the year, the article's title, the publication's name in italics, the volume and issue number (if applicable), and the page range. The following is the format: Authors A. A. & B. B. Authors (Year).

A bibliography refers to a list of sources (e.g., books, articles, websites) utilized to complete a task (e.g., an essay). It often includes all sources considered, even if they are not acknowledged (included) directly in the assignment.

The steps involved in writing a bibliography for an assignment are:

- Name(s) of the author(s), editor(s), or publisher(s) of the book.

- Including the Subtitle and the Complete Title of the Book.

- If not the first edition, the volume number or a total number of volumes in a multi-volume work.

- Publisher, city of publishing, date of publication.

What is conjunctive adverb?

When connecting independent clauses (sentences), a special class of adverb known as a conjunctive is used. Contrary to the majority of adverbs, which alter verbs, adjectives, or other adverbs, conjunctive adverbs are used as conjunctions to connect phrases.

Conjunctive adverb identification should thus be simple. Conjunctive adverbs are used to connect concepts of equal importance if a conjunction is neither coordinating nor subordinating. But by combining these equal concepts, we hope to demonstrate a solid relationship.

Conjunctive adverbs are adverbs that connect sentences together. In order to illustrate linkages like cause and effect, contrast, and sequence, they can also be employed. Like other adverbs, conjunctive adverbs can be moved around inside a phrase or sentence.

Transitional words or phrases are known as conjunctive adverbs. Connective adverbs and connecting adverbs are other names for conjunctive adverbs. Conjunctive adverbs are conjunctions, however they are not employed in the same way as coordinating conjunctions and are different from one another.
