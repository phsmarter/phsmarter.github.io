22. Interview the entrepreneur with 4 stages idea stage , concept stage, product development stage, test marketing stage.​


-STAGES IDEA
the product life cycle are introduction, growth, maturity, and decline. Learn how to leverage this into your business strategy.
-CONCEPT STAGE
The conceptual stage creates the concrete product idea for a new project. It can refer to a new system or a functional extension of an existing system. The objective of a conceptual stage is to examine the product idea for domain-related consistency and basic technical feasibility.
-PRODUCT DEVELOPMENT STAGE

Answer:

Interviewer: Can you tell us about your journey as an entrepreneur and the four stages of idea development that you went through?

Entrepreneur: Sure, I'd be happy to. My journey as an entrepreneur began with the idea stage, where I came up with a concept for a new product or service. During this stage, I spent a lot of time researching the market and identifying a need that my product or service could fill. I also talked to potential customers and gathered feedback to refine my idea.

Interviewer: That sounds like a crucial stage. What came next?

Entrepreneur: The next stage was the concept stage, where I developed a more detailed plan for my product or service. I created a business plan, conducted more market research, and began to flesh out the specifics of the product or service. This stage was important because it helped me determine the feasibility of my idea and whether it would be financially viable.

Interviewer: So, after the concept stage, what happened next?

Entrepreneur: The third stage was the product development stage, where I began to develop the actual product or service. This involved creating prototypes, testing different materials, and refining the design. I also worked with a team of engineers and designers to ensure that the product met the needs of our target customers.

Interviewer: That sounds like a challenging stage. What happened after product development?

Entrepreneur: The final stage was the test marketing stage, where we launched the product in a limited market to gather feedback and refine the marketing strategy. We used this feedback to make improvements to the product and to refine our marketing message. After the test marketing stage, we were ready to launch the product on a larger scale.

Interviewer: That's great to hear. Can you share some of the challenges you faced during these stages?

Entrepreneur: One of the biggest challenges was funding the development of the product. We had to be creative in finding investors and securing funding to move the project forward. Another challenge was ensuring that the product met the needs of our target customers. We had to listen carefully to their feedback and make changes to the product based on their input.

Interviewer: Thank you for sharing your experience and insights. It's clear that developing a successful product or service requires a lot of hard work and careful planning.
