19. what is the root word of supervisor​


Answer:

Its base word, supervise, ultimately comes from the Medieval Latin verb supervidēre, meaning “to oversee.” In fact, the word supervise quite literally means the same thing as oversee—the prefix super- means “over” or “above,” and the Latin verb vidēre means “to see” (the English word vision is based on the same root).

24. your immediate supervisor assigned you to finish a certain task today however you do not fully understand the instructions given to you about the task what are you going to do in order to ensure that you will complete the task correctly ​


Answer:

If I do not fully understand the instructions given to me about the task, I would take the following steps to ensure that I complete the task correctly:

1. Ask for clarification - I would approach my supervisor and ask for further explanation or clarification regarding the task. I would ask specific questions about any part of the instructions that are unclear to me.

2. Repeat the instructions back - I would repeat the instructions back to my supervisor in my own words to ensure that I have understood them correctly. This also helps me to identify areas where I may need further clarification.

3. Research - I would conduct my own research to gain better understanding of the task. I would consult relevant documentation, guidelines, or procedures to help me understand the task better.

4. Seek guidance from colleagues - I would seek guidance from colleagues or other team members who might have more information or experience with similar tasks.

5. Take notes - I would take detailed notes of the conversation with my supervisor and any additional information I gather. This will help me to refer back to the information and ensure that I am on the right track while completing the task.

By taking these steps, I can ensure that I have understood the instructions correctly and complete the task accurately and efficiently.
