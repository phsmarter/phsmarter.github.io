15. A 0.406 g sample of magnesium reacts with oxygen, producing 0.674 g of magnesium oxide as the only product. What mass of oxygen was consumed in the reaction?​


0.2074 gr of Oxygen was consumed in the reaction

Further explanation  

A reaction coefficient is a number in the chemical formula of a substance involved in the reaction equation. The reaction coefficient is useful for equalizing reagents and products.  

The mole itself is the number of particles contained in a substance amounting to 6.02.10²³  

Mole can be found from the amount of substance mass and its molar mass  

[tex]\rm mole=\dfrac{mass}{molar~mass}[/tex]

A 0.406 g Mg reacts with O₂, producing 0.674 g of MgO

Reaction :

2Mg + O₂ ⇒ 2MgO

This reaction is a magnesium combustion reaction

We determine each mole first because the reaction coefficient shows the mole ratio

[tex]\rm mole~Mg=\dfrac{0.406}{24}=0.0169\\\\mole~MgO=\dfrac{0.674}{40}=0.0169[/tex]

These two moles show that Mg reacts entirely, so O₂ mole is based on Mg mole

The mole ratio of O2 and Mg of the reaction equation: 2: 1, so mole O₂ =

[tex]\rm mole~O_2=\dfrac{1}{2}\times MgO\\\\mole~O_2=\dfrac{1}{2}\times 0.0169\\\\mole~O_2=0.00845[/tex]

then :

[tex]\rm mass~of~O_2=mole\times molar~mass\\\\mass~of~O_2=0.00845\times 32\\\\mass~of~O_2=\boxed{\bold{0.2704~gr}}[/tex]

18. The number of moles and the mass of magnesium carbonate required to produce 298.0 g of carbon dioxide​


SOLUTION: (number of moles of MgCO₃)

Step 1: Balance the chemical equation.

MgCO₃ → MgO + CO₂

Step 2: Calculate the number of moles of magnesium carbonate produced by using dimensional analysis.

Based on the balanced chemical equation, 1 mole of MgCO₃ is stoichiometrically equivalent to 1 mole of CO₂.

The molar mass of CO₂ is 44.009 g/mol.

[tex]\begin{aligned} \text{moles of} \: \text{MgCO}_3 & = \text{298.0 g} \: \text{CO}_2 \times \frac{\text{1 mol} \: \text{CO}_2}{\text{44.009 g} \: \text{CO}_2} \times \frac{\text{1 mol} \: \text{MgCO}_3}{\text{1 mol} \: \text{CO}_2} \\ & = \text{6.77134 mol} \\ & \approx \boxed{\text{6.771 mol}} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, 6.771 mol of magnesium carbonate is required to produce 298.0 g of carbon dioxide.

SOLUTION: (mass of MgCO₃)

The molar mass of MgCO₃ is 84.313 g/mol.

[tex]\begin{aligned} \text{mass of} \: \text{MgCO}_3 & = \text{6.77134 mol} \: \text{MgCO}_3 \times \frac{\text{84.313 g} \: \text{MgCO}_3}{\text{1 mol} \: \text{MgCO}_3} \\ & = \text{570.912 g} \\ & \approx \boxed{\text{570.9 g}} \end{aligned}[/tex]
