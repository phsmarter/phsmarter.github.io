Answer:

Explanation:

Identifying the Type of Academic Writing

In identifying the type of academic writing used in the above examples, one must clearly know the difference among the four types of academic writing: descriptive (DE), analytical (AN), persuasive (PR), and critical (CT). The purpose of descriptive writing is to provide facts and information. Analytical writing is similar to DE writing but it re-organizes the facts and information into categories or groups. Meanwhile, persuasive writing has the elements of AE writing with the incorporation of the writer's point of view. Lastly, critical writing has the features of PR writing but includes more than one point of view.

In doing CT writing, one must thoroughly summarize important parts of work such as assumptions, methodology, and interpretation. It also determines the strengths and weaknesses of a study.

Identifying the Type of Academic Writing Used in the Above Examples

With the knowledge of the different types of academic writing, one can already identify the type of academic writing used in the above cases.

Analytical: categories are mentionedCritical: identifying parts of a work (interpretation, assumption, and methodology) Descriptive: plainly stating a report on the result of a class experiment Persuasive: discussing one's point of viewCritical: strengths and weaknesses are discussed
