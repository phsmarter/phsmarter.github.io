8. Information Communications and Technology is in the modern office now. What are the advantages and disadvantages of technology in the modern office/workplace?Pa help po​


Answer:

Advantages of technology in the modern office include increased productivity and efficiency, better communication and collaboration among employees, improved data storage and organization, and the ability to work remotely. Additionally, technology can also assist in automating repetitive tasks, provide a wider range of data analytics, and improve customer service and support.

Disadvantages of technology in the modern office include the potential for increased distractions, cyber security threats, and a lack of face-to-face interaction among employees. Additionally, technology can be expensive to implement and maintain, and there is a risk of employees becoming over-reliant on technology, which can negatively impact their problem-solving and critical thinking skills.

14. What are the advantages and risks of information and communication technologies? Explain each. ​


Answer:

Communication – with help of information technologies the instant messaging, emails, voice and video calls becomes quicker, cheaper and much efficient. Globalization and cultural gap – by implementing information systems we can bring down the linguistic, geographical and some cultural boundaries.

Top 10 risks for communications, media and technology companies

1. Data security and privacy- This new world of connected devices promises insights on which companies can base strategic decisions. But the ability to obtain more information brings the responsibility to use that data appropriately, maintain customers’ privacy, and practice effective cybersecurity.

2. Technology errors and omissions

Sixty-nine percent of companies said tech errors and omissions (E&O) risk was of great concern, yet only 45 percent of companies believe they are fully protected against tech E&O risk.

3. Intellectual property

Intellectual property (IP) theft doesn’t often result in a loss for CMT companies. In fact, only 6 percent of survey respondents said they had experienced such a loss in the past few years. But the severity of such a loss, while difficult to quantify, is potentially enormous, helping put IP theft at the number three spot on the list of top CMT risks.

4. Employee safety

The risk of employee safety was the highest in terms of reported losses among the Marsh survey respondents. The high level of concern points to an understandable desire to keep people safe and an acknowledgment that workplace injuries can lead to significant settlements.

5. Regulatory compliance

New regulations and contractual risks are increasing for CMT organizations. For example, the Federal Communications Commission (FCC) passed sweeping privacy rules for internet service providers, the European Union and the U.S. agreed to the Privacy Shield Framework and sharing economy regulations have increased.

6. Business interruption

Natural disasters, terrorism, riots and cyberattacks are examples of events that can cause a business to suspend operations or limit their hours.

7. Multinational exposure

In today’s global business environment, multinational corporations face heightened risks. An event overseas can trigger major disruptions, especially in a variety of key supply chains.

8. Directors and officers liability

Increased regulatory scrutiny is impacting the D&O loss landscape and driving coverage demands.

9. Mergers and acquisitions

Mergers and acquisitions (M&A) and restructuring increase uncertainty. Eleven megadeals valued above $10 billion each occurred in the technology industry in 2015, and the first three quarters of 2016.

In addition, communication services companies have stepped up acquisitions of content creators.

10. Contingent business interruption

Contingent business interruption results from an interruption of business at the premises of a customer or supplier.

What happens if a supplier’s data centers go down? In 2016, data center network outages and contractual obligations caused numerous disputes.

28. Give 1 advantage and disadvantage of Information Communication Technology. (2-5 sentences)


Advantage and disadvantage of Information Communication Technology.

Advantage:

Information Communication Technology has enabled a wide range of new communication options, allowing you to communicate with people almost instantly, for less money, and across longer distances than ever before. Texting, instant messaging, and video conferencing technologies enable users to connect in real time with individuals all over the globe for a little charge, an idea that may have appeared absurd prior to the invention of computers. Additionally, text-based computer communication can provide a level playing field for persons with speech or social difficulties when communicating with their peers.

Disadvantage:

Information Communication Technology allows criminals to access your personal information in a variety of methods, potentially causing you or your company to lose money and reputation. With the correct security details, a third party may impersonate you and obtain remote access to your funds via technologies like internet banking. This is in sharp contrast to the pre-Information Communication Technology age, when fraudsters had to persuade someone else (such as a bank teller or a phone operator) that they were you in order to gain access to your money.
