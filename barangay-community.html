21. community pantry early month of may community response to hunger barrios and barangay


Answer:

The Community Pantry as an alternative intervention against hunger was initiated to promote and make available basic goods and services in the community of North Caloocan, particularly those that are not accessible to the residents since the supermarket chains do not visit the areas of our concern. This is imminent because there are many cases of malnutrition due to lack of food and food that is out of reach for several individuals in poverty stricken areas. The Community Pantry also aims to provide a venue where small scale farmers can come together, support each other, trade and sell their products for higher value, such as in 'street-side markets' aka sidewalk or public markets.
