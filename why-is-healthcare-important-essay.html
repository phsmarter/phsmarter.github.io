14. Why is Providing access to quality healthcare and medical treatment for those who are infected with the virus important?


Answer:

Providing access to quality healthcare and medical treatment for those who are infected with the virus is important for a number of reasons:

To save lives: By providing medical treatment to those who are sick with the virus, we can help to prevent severe illness and death.

To reduce suffering: Medical treatment can help to alleviate the symptoms of COVID-19 and make the illness more bearable for those who are infected.

To prevent the spread of the virus: By providing medical care to those who are sick, we can help to reduce the transmission of the virus to others and slow the spread of the pandemic.

To protect public health: By providing quality healthcare and medical treatment to those who are infected, we can help to prevent the overloading of public health systems and protect the health of the broader population.

To ensure fairness and justice: Providing access to quality healthcare and medical treatment is important for ensuring that all people have the opportunity to receive the care they need, regardless of their ability to pay or their social or economic status.

22. why lack of access to healthcare is important issue in the philippines


Answer:

Access to healthcare is an important issue in the Philippines for numerous reasons. Firstly, the country has one of the highest poverty rates in the world, with more than 25% of the population living in extreme poverty. This means that many people lack the financial resources to pay for medical care and health services. Additionally, there is a severe shortage of healthcare professionals across the country, particularly in rural areas. This leads to a lack of access to quality healthcare, which can lead to poorer health outcomes. Finally, many areas lack infrastructure such as hospitals and clinics, which limits access to care even further. All of these factors mean that access to healthcare is an important issue in the Philippines and one that needs to be addressed.

Explanation:

The debate as to whether healthcare should be free or paid has been going on for a long time. Ultimately, the goal of a healthcare system should be to ensure that everyone has access to medical care when they need it. On one hand, providing free healthcare to individuals allows for equitable access to services, and thus can improve the public's health and wellbeing. On the other hand, a system with paid healthcare may ensure better quality care and a more efficient system, as well as serving as a source of funds for the healthcare system.

For a functioning healthcare system, it is important to maintain an adequate level of funding and a cost effective access to medical services. Therefore, a combination of free and paid healthcare may be the most beneficial option. By allowing individuals access to some basic medical care at no cost, it ensures that people with limited financial resources can still access vital healthcare services. This can be supplemented with private costs for more comprehensive care, specialized services, and treatments.

The cost of the healthcare system can also be reduced if regulations are in place to ensure that providers are not charging excessive costs to patients. Additionally, taxes could be placed on certain services or healthcare related items, such as alcohol or tobacco products, in order to increase the funds available to the healthcare system.

Ultimately, providing equitable access to healthcare will require an approach that involves a mix of free and paid healthcare. The decision about what services should be free and what should be paid for can and should be publically debated to ensure that the most efficient, affordable and equitable system is implemented.
