Creating An Idea with A Sentence

The first things or idea that comes to our mind:

1. My Father: My father is an architect

2. My mother: My mother has a beautiful face

3. My family: My family is diverse

4. My siblings: My siblings have good grades

5. My childhood: My childhood was wonderful

6. My friends: My friends are good to me

7. My school: My school held the independence day celebration

8. My dream: My dream is to receiving a scholarship

9. My weakness: My weakness is darkness

10. I am afraid: I am afraid of failing

To answer the question above, we have to know the structure of a sentence. In this question, we are told to create an idea (in a sentence), hence we have to create a sentence grammatically correct.

The Structure of a Sentence

A sentence is when a writing consist subject and verb (predicate).

Example :

I eat, I (subject) + eat (verb/predicate). using verbShe is beautiful, He/she/it + to be / auxiliary verb (is) + adj/adv/nounThey are married, you/they/we + to be/auxiliary verb (are) +  adj/adv/nounI am beautiful, I + to be/auxiliary verb (am) + adj/adv/noun

1. My Father: My father is an architect

My father (Subject) + is (auxiliary verb) + an (article) + architect (object)

2.  My mother: My mother has a beautiful face

My mother (subject) + has (verb) + a (article) + beautiful (adjective) + face (object/noun)

in this sentence, we use adjective to modify the noun. Remember, always put adjective before the noun.

3. My family: My family is diverse

My family (subject) + is (auxiliary verb/to be) + diverse (adjective)

Remember! always use auxiliary verb/to be before adjective, noun, and adverb.

4. My siblings: My siblings have good grades

My siblings (subject) + have (verb/predicate) + good (adjective) + grades (objective/noun)

5. My childhood: My childhood was wonderful

My childhood (subject) + was (auxiliary verb) + wonderful (adjective)

6. My friends: My friends are good to me

My friends (subject)+ are (to be/ auxiliary verb) + good (adjective) to (preposition) + me (object)

7. My school: My school held the independence day celebration

My school (subject) + held (Verb 2) + the independence day celebration (noun)

8. My dream: My dream is to receiving a scholarship

My dream (subject) + is (to be/auxiliary verb) + to (preposition) + receiving (gerund) + a (article) + scholarship (noun)

9. My weakness: My weakness is darkness

My weakness (subject) + is (to be/auxiliary verb) + darkness (noun)

10. I am afraid: I am afraid of failing

I (subject) + am (to be/auxiliary verb) + of (preposition) + failing (gerund)

Remember, using preposition 'of' + v-ing (gerund)!
