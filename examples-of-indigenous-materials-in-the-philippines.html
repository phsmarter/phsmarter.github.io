Storage basket

Example: Bayong

Type: Woven basket

Materials: Bamboo, rattan, or palm leaves

Uses: Used to store and transport goods, such as food, clothes, and household items.

Hats

Example: Salakot

Type: Conical hat

Materials: Bamboo or palm leaves

Uses: Worn as protection from the sun and rain, as well as a traditional accessory for ceremonial events.

Transportation Baskets

Example of basket: Bilao

Type: Flat basket

Materials: Bamboo or rattan

Uses: Used to transport goods, such as fruits, vegetables, and grains, from one place to another.

Personal Bag Basket

Example: Nito Bag

Type: Handheld basket

Materials: Nito vine or other natural fibers

Uses: Used as a personal bag to carry small items, such as personal accessories, snacks, and tools.

Pots Basket

Example: Takilong

Type: Basket used as a pot holder

Materials: Bamboo or rattan

Uses: Used to hold pots, especially for cooking or boiling water over a fire.

23. give 5 examples of indigenous baskets and what are the materials used to it?​


Kalinga baskets - these are woven baskets made by the Kalinga people of the Cordillera region in the Philippines. They are made from bamboo or rattan strips that are dyed with natural colors.T'boli baskets - these are intricately designed baskets made by the T'boli people of Mindanao in the Philippines. They are made from nito vines that are harvested from the forest.Ifugao baskets - these are baskets made by the Ifugao people of the Cordillera region in the Philippines. They are made from rattan strips that are woven into intricate patterns.Ilocano baskets - these are baskets made by the Ilocano people of the northern Philippines. They are made from dried pandan leaves that are woven into different shapes and sizes.Tausug baskets - these are baskets made by the Tausug people of Sulu in the southern Philippines. They are made from buri leaves that are dried, dyed, and woven into different designs.

28. give 7 examples of indigenous materials appropriate for types baskets.​


Here are 7 examples of indigenous materials appropriate for making baskets:

Rattan - a type of palm that is flexible and durable, commonly used for basket weaving

Bamboo - a fast-growing woody grass that is lightweight and strong, often used for making baskets with intricate designs

Abaca - a type of banana plant that produces long, strong fibers used for weaving baskets and other household items

Pandan - a tropical plant with long, narrow leaves that are used for weaving baskets, mats, and other handicrafts

Coconut leaves - the long, flexible leaves of the coconut palm are used for weaving baskets, hats, and other items

Nito - a type of vine that grows in the Philippines, commonly used for weaving baskets and other household items

Pine needles - the long, thin needles of pine trees can be coiled and woven into baskets, often used by Native American communities in the United States.

EXAMPLES:Bamboo: A fast-growing and abundant plant, bamboo is a popular material for making baskets in many parts of the world. It is lightweight, strong, and durable,making it ideal for baskets that need to be sturdy.Rattan: Rattan is a type of climbing palm that is native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa. Its flexible and durable stems can be woven into baskets of different shapes and sizes.Wicker: Wicker is a type of woven material that is made from natural fibers such as willow, cane, or reed. It is often used for making decorative baskets and storage baskets.Seagrass: Seagrass is a type of flowering plant that grows in shallow water near the coast. Its strong, flexible stems can be woven into baskets that are perfect for storing household items.Pine needles: Pine needles are often used to make coiled baskets. They are pliable and easy to work with, and can be dyed in a variety of colors to create beautiful patterns.Sweetgrass: Sweetgrass is a fragrant, native grass that is found in wetlands and marshes in many parts of North America. It is often used to make baskets with a distinctive, sweet scent.Palm leaves: Palm leaves are commonly used in many cultures to make baskets. They are often woven into intricate patterns to create beautiful and functional baskets for everyday use or for special occasions.
