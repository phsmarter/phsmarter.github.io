There are many aspects in self improvement; one is the physical improvement. We humans always care how we look because our physical appearance is a biological force that affects on how we deal with others. Our perception of our own attractiveness also has an effect on how we feel confident about ourselves. One way to improve your physical appearance is to getting into the habit of drinking more water. You need to be hydrated in order to have a fair, glowing skin, healthier hair and healthier body. Another way is to be always mindful of your hygiene. Another way for you to look attractive is to feel confident all the time. Don’t be cocky, this really helps. Always be in proper posture to show confidence. Smell of perfumes last longer when applied on the back of the neck or on your wrist. Next aspect of self development is the mental improvement. Mental strength or toughness is not something that you have to be born with as this is something that can be developed as you grow. One way to develop your mental strength is to listen to professionals and experts of this field. There are many available biographies and audio programs that will teach you to overcome obstacle and be successful in life. Another way is to always bear in mind that failure and handicaps are not hindrances in reaching your goals in life.

Topic: School Uniforms

Claim: School uniforms are a better choice for public schools.

Arguments:

a. Wearing school uniforms would simplify students' lives and eliminate the stress of deciding what to wear every day.

b. School uniforms help students to act responsibly in groups and as individuals, as they create a feeling of unity and give the message that school is a special place for learning. Additionally, statistics show that school uniforms have positive effects on violence, attendance, test scores, and student suspensions.

c. School uniforms would make all students feel equal, regardless of their financial status. It would promote pride and raise self-esteem among students who cannot afford to wear expensive clothing.

Counterarguments:

a. Some may argue that school uniforms limit students' self-expression and creativity. However, students can still express themselves through accessories or other means outside of clothing. Moreover, uniforms promote a sense of unity and belonging, which may be more important in a school environment.

b. Others may argue that implementing school uniforms can be costly for families who cannot afford them. However, many schools have programs to help families obtain uniforms at a low cost or for free. Additionally, over time, uniforms can save money for families who would otherwise have to purchase expensive clothing for school.

c. Some may argue that school uniforms do not address the root causes of student behavior problems and that other solutions should be explored. While school uniforms are not a panacea, they have been shown to have positive effects on behavior and academic performance, as well as promote a sense of community and pride among students.
