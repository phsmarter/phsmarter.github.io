Transitional Words and Phrases

Transitional words and phrases show the relationships between the parts of a sentence, between the sentences in a paragraph, or between the paragraphs in a longer piece of writing (i.e., an essay, short story, novel, magazine article, etcetera). Although transitional words and phrases mean little by themselves, they are very important in linking your ideas together smoothly and logically so that your paragraphs have coherence. Transitional words and phrases can be divided into categories according to the kind of relationship you as a writer are trying to show. There are eight (8) basic categories you must learn:

To Show Time. after, afterward, always, as soon as, at last, at once, briefly, eventually, finally, immediately, in the meantime, in the past (or future), last, later, meanwhile, next, never, now, often, once, promptly, sometimes, soon.
To Show Place. above, among, around, at this point, behind, below, beside, beyond, down, forward, from, here, in front of, inside, nearby, next to, on, on the other side, opposite, over, through.

To Add An Idea. again, also, and, as well as, besides, for one thing, further, furthermore, in addition to, last, likewise, more, moreover, next, similarly, too.

To Illustrate or Explain an Idea. for example, for instance, in other words, in particular, namely, specifically, such as, that is, thus, to illustrate.

To Compare or Contrast Ideas. but, even so, conversely, differently, however, in contrast, in spite of, in the same way, nevertheless, on the contrary, on the other hand, still,yet.

To Show a Result. accordingly, as a result, consequently, for that reason, hence, then, therefore, thus.

To Empasize an Idea. above all, especially, indeed, in fact, most important.

To Summarize an Idea. as has been noted, finally, in brief, in other words, in short, on the whole, to sum up.

These are not all of the transitional words and phrases in the English language that we use, but they represent a good sampling of those most often employed in writing. Remember that transitions are like bridges -- they link one thing with another. They can be used to go forward (on to the next sentence or paragraph) or to go backward (to refer to something that has just been stated). The following is a brief listing of commonly used transitional words and phrases one finds in daily speech:

or              otherwise                unfortunately         but

when         however                  recently                   for

after          therefore                although                  for example

so             consequently          at last                      in conclusion

1. Children can acquire illness and diseases due to several reasons. One possible cause of this is their exposure to germs and viruses. Kids who are constantly touching dirty surfaces or playing with toys that have not been cleaned properly may contract harmful illnesses. Additionally, lack of proper hygiene practices such as washing hands before meals or after coming from the outside may also make them susceptible to diseases. Poor nutrition and lack of sleep can also weaken their immune system and make them more prone to sickness.

2. My mother shows genuine love for our neighbor. Her kind and selfless actions towards our neighbor have a positive effect not only on the neighbor but also on our family. Seeing my mother's kind deeds encourages us to also be more compassionate and extend help to our neighbors. Furthermore, it creates a friendly and harmonious relationship within the neighborhood. This loving gesture also empowers our neighbor to do good deeds for others, creating a ripple effect of kindness and love.

3. Water shortage might happen in the future. The overuse and wasteful consumption of water are potentially significant reasons why this shortage could occur. This could result in the depletion of natural sources of water, such as lakes and rivers. Moreover, the current climate change that is being experienced in many parts of the world can cause droughts. This situation will lead to devastating effects such as crop failures, food shortages, and the scarcity of drinking water.

4. Scarcity of food happens to many Filipino families. One cause of this is poverty. Over four million Filipinos face extreme poverty, and with this condition comes hunger. Access to healthy food is quite challenging due to the high cost of food, so people often tend to consume unhealthy and cheap food. Moreover, the economic instability caused by frequent natural calamities also creates a negative impact on the country's food supply. This food scarcity puts Filipino families in a difficult situation and may significantly affect their lives and growth.

5. People have time to prepare and prevent damage from thunderstorms. Understanding the effects of thunderstorms is one of the essential ways to prepare and prevent damage. These storms are always accompanied by lightning, high winds, hail, and flooding. Preparing for these effects requires securing and reinforcing homes and buildings to withstand high winds; this includes trimming trees and cleaning roof gutters. Prevention may also include ensuring that weak structures and loose items are secured, as well as staying updated on weather bulletins to avoid being trapped in dangerous situations.

What is the Introduction? Body? Conclusion?

Answer: They work to book-end the argument made in the body paragraphs by first explaining what points will be made (in the introduction) and then summarizing what points were made (in the conclusion). 

What are written in the first paragraph?

Answer: The introductory paragraph should also include the thesis statement, a kind of mini-outline for the paper: it tells the reader what the essay is about. The last sentence of this paragraph must also contain a transitional "hook" which moves the reader to the first paragraph of the body of the paper.
