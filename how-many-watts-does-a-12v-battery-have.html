8. How much charge can a 4µF capacitor store on its plates when it is connected to a 12V battery?


Answer:

The stored electric charge in a capacitor, Q (in coulombs, abbreviated C) is equal to the product of the capacitance C (in Farads, abbreviated F) of the capacitor, and the voltage V (in volts, abbreviated V) across its terminals. That is, Q = C٠V.

For example, if C = 33μF and V = 20V, then Q = (33٠10^-6)٠(20) = 660μC.

If you take the same capacitor and increase the voltage from 20V to, say, 50V, then Q = (33٠10^-6)٠(50) = 1650μC.

10. A 12V battery is able to move a charge of 30C in a given unit of time. Solve for the energy stored in the battery


Answer:

The energy stored in a battery can be calculated using the formula: Energy = 1/2 * C * V^2, where C is the charge stored in the battery and V is the voltage of the battery.

Given, C = 30C and V = 12V.

Substituting these values into the formula, we get:

Energy = 1/2 * 30 * 12^2 = 1/2 * 30 * 144 = 8640 J.

So, the energy stored in the 12V battery is 8640 J.
