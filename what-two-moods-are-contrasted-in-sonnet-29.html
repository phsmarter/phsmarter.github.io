Answer:

basic pentameter rhythm

Sonnet 29 does have a basic pentameter rhythm; that is, each line is made up of five unstressed and five stressed syllables, making a total of ten syllables per line. But not all lines are iambic pentameter, notably lines 3,5,6,9,10 and 11.

Summary

25. what is the difference between the man with the hoe and sonnet 29


What is the difference between the man with the hoe and sonnet 29

Both the poem "The Man with the Hoe" by Edwin Markham and the Sonnet 29 by  Shakespeare speak of the agony of mankind when he is in a state of suffering and contention.  However, one difference is that Markham 's poem talks more fluently about the state of being subjected to work,  perhaps even simply about the state of slavery, whereas Shakespeare 's poem talks only of having an impoverished destiny. It finishes on  a happy note, too.

The man with the hoe  has to do with drudgery, equality.  How poorly the people are  handled by government, or society.  In the Sonnet 29, the person's jealousy, guilt, and insecurity towards the people around him was conveyed.

26. What is most likely predicament of the writer in sonnet 29?


Answer:

Anxiety, love, and jealousy

Explanation:

This poem is about a speaker, initially downcast about fate, but becomes excited when he recalls his beloved.

The poem begins with the description of the speaker’s discontent and his lamentation over the miserable plight of life. He curses his poor fate for his catastrophic condition. He feels abandoned and useless. He feels that even God is not answering his calls. Also, he is envious from the successful and talented people around him. He wishes to be rich, satisfied, and fortunate like them but meets only failures and disappointments. After cursing his present sad state, he suddenly talks about a special person, and his mood changes in a dramatic way. This thought of love provides him immense pleasure and removes his negative feelings. What stays in the minds of the readers is the magical effect of love that eradicates his sorrow and makes him feel special.

27. what are the rhyming words in the SONNET 29 by GOERGE SANTAYANA

Poor - Sure

The second stanza's pattern is c-d-c-d-c-d.

30. what seems to be his/her problem of sonnet 29​


Answer:

The emotional state of the speaker in Sonnet 29 is one of depression: in the first line, he assumes himself to be "in disgrace with fortune," meaning he has been having bad luck. He also feels in disgrace with "men's eyes," implying that the general public looks on him unfavorably.
