4. Make a reflective essay about the topic: "Stopping the Spread of Fake News"​


Answer:

Fake news has become a pervasive problem in today's society, with misinformation and disinformation spreading quickly and easily through social media and the internet. This phenomenon has serious consequences, from undermining public trust in institutions and the media to potentially even impacting the outcomes of elections. It is therefore crucial that we take steps to stop the spread of fake news.

One approach to combating fake news is to focus on education and media literacy. By teaching people how to critically evaluate the sources and accuracy of the information they encounter online, we can help them identify and avoid falling for fake news. This can involve providing resources and tools that help people assess the credibility of news sources, as well as educating them on the common tactics used by those who create and spread fake news.

Another important step in stopping the spread of fake news is to hold those who create and disseminate it accountable. This can involve implementing stricter regulations and penalties for those who deliberately spread fake news, as well as providing support for fact-checking organizations that can help identify and debunk fake news. Additionally, social media companies and other online platforms have a responsibility to combat fake news on their platforms, whether through implementing algorithms that identify and flag false information or by providing users with tools and resources to help them identify fake news.

Finally, it is important to recognize that fake news is often created and spread for profit or political gain. By being aware of these motivations and not falling for the tactics used to spread fake news, we can help to reduce its impact. This may involve being more skeptical of sensational headlines and evaluating the credibility of the source before sharing information online.

In conclusion, the spread of fake news is a serious problem that has the potential to undermine trust in institutions and the media, as well as potentially impacting the outcomes of elections. By focusing on education and media literacy, holding those who create and spread fake news accountable, and being aware of the motivations behind it, we can take steps to stop the spread of fake news and protect against its harmful effects.

27. construct an essay from the topic/theme,"The Resiliency of Filipinos".You may create a title from the given topic/theme.The essay must contain an introduction, Body and conclusion. Three hundred to five hundred words (300-500) are encouraged. Modals and communicative/speech style must reflect in your essay.​


“Resilience” or “katatagan” is the word that describes the “image of the Filipino people” in times of adversity. Often, traditional and social media depict us as a bunch of people smiling amid the face of adversity. It somehow fosters a national image of a strong nation--the one that bounces back quickly with a joke and a smile as his or her weapon. Seeing this somehow uplifts the spirit and hopeful that life is normal, or shall resume normally soon.

However, this “resilience” has also been contentious. People tend to dismiss adversities as something we should deal with everyday. Since we’re “resilient,” we often brush off risks in an advent of another event, and would do the same thing “over and over.” If you complain about the inconveniences, people would often see you as negative and a pessimist—not helping uplift the spirit and the society needed in these tough times. The image of “resiliency” being played over and over again seems to border “insanity” — doing the same thing while expecting a different result. Also, it has that flair of fatalism—“wala na tayong magagawa.” Dahil sanay naman daw tayo sa mga delubyo, “we should deal with it.”
