3. japanese colonization in the philippines


Around 1942 and 1945, when Imperial Japan invaded the Commonwealth Philippines during World War II, the Japanese invasion of the Philippines took place. It was the shortest occupation/colonization but was considered the most gruesome.

The Philippine invasion began on December 8, 1941, ten hours after the Pearl Harbor attack. As at Pearl Harbor, the initial Japanese attack caused severe damage to American aircraft. The American Asian Fleet in the Philippines, without air cover, returned to Java on December 12, 1941. General Douglas MacArthur was ordered to leave, leaving his men for Australia, at Corregidor on the night of March 11, 1942. Bataan's 76,000 starving and wounded American and Filipino defenders surrendered on April 9, 1942, and were forced to endure the notorious Bataan Death March of which 7,000–10,000 died or were killed. Corregidor's 13,000 prisoners surrendered on May 6.

For more than three years, Japan controlled the Philippines before Japan's surrender. Philippine resistance forces ' highly effective guerrilla campaign occupied 60% of the islands, mainly jungle and mountain areas. The Filipinos remained loyal to the Americans with the hope that they gain independence from their colonizers. The women also became "comfort women" for the japanese soldiers. Over a thousand women, some of them under the age of 18, were incarcerated as "comfort women" throughout the Philippines, held in sexual slavery for Japanese military personnel during the occupation. During the invasion, each of the Japanese military facilities in the Philippines had a site where the women were kept and they called it a "comfort station," like Bahay na Pula.

17. what are the contributions of the three races who colonized our country as stated in the story spaniards: japanese: americans:​


Spaniards: The Spanish colonial period in the Philippines lasted for over three centuries, from 1521 to 1898. During this time, the Spaniards introduced Christianity and Western culture to the country, leaving a lasting impact on Filipino society. The Spaniards also established a system of government and introduced the concept of land ownership, which had a significant impact on the country's political and economic development. However, the Spanish colonization also led to the oppression of the Filipino people, including forced labor, taxation, and the suppression of indigenous cultures.

Japanese: The Japanese occupation of the Philippines took place during World War II, from 1942 to 1945. During this time, the Japanese introduced several reforms aimed at promoting Filipino nationalism, including the establishment of a puppet government and the use of Filipino language in schools and government. However, the Japanese occupation also led to widespread human rights abuses, including forced labor, torture, and rape.

Americans: The American colonial period in the Philippines lasted from 1898 to 1946. During this time, the Americans introduced modern education, infrastructure, and governance systems to the country. They also helped to modernize the country's economy and improve public health. However, the American colonization also led to the suppression of Filipino nationalism and the exploitation of the country's natural resources. The American colonization period also had a significant impact on the country's political and economic development, leading to a legacy of dependence on foreign powers.

Overall, the contributions of the three colonial powers to the Philippines are complex and multifaceted, with both positive and negative impacts on the country's development.
