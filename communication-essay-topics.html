8. ESSAY & Sentence outline 500 words of essay Topic: Negative effects of social media: How to be reduced in face to face communication? introduction:Problem,Claim of factBody: Cause & effect,Claim of Policy conclusion:solution,Claim of Value ​


Answer:

Introduction:

Social media has revolutionized the way people communicate with each other, enabling them to share ideas, connect with friends and family, and interact with their favorite brands and celebrities. However, this technological advancement has also brought with it a host of negative effects that have greatly impacted our society. One of the most significant negative effects of social media is its impact on face-to-face communication. In this essay, we will explore the negative effects of social media on face-to-face communication and suggest some ways to reduce these effects.

Problem:

Social media has made communication easier and more convenient than ever before, but it has also had a detrimental effect on face-to-face communication. The constant need to check our phones, tablets, and laptops for social media notifications has made us distracted and disengaged from the people around us. We have become so accustomed to communicating through screens that we struggle to communicate effectively in person.

Cause & Effect:

One of the main causes of this problem is the addictive nature of social media. Social media platforms are designed to keep us engaged and coming back for more. This is achieved through a combination of addictive design features, such as infinite scrolling and push notifications, and the use of algorithms that personalize our feeds to keep us interested. This addictive nature of social media can be so overwhelming that it distracts us from face-to-face conversations.

Another cause of the problem is the social comparison that social media facilitates. People often present their best selves on social media, and this can lead to unrealistic expectations and feelings of inadequacy. This, in turn, can lead to social anxiety and a lack of confidence in face-to-face interactions.

The effect of social media on face-to-face communication is significant. It can lead to a lack of engagement and a sense of disconnection from the people around us. It can also contribute to a decrease in social skills and a lack of confidence in social situations.

Claim of Policy:

To reduce the negative effects of social media on face-to-face communication, we need to take action. One solution is to limit our social media use. We can set aside specific times of the day to check our social media accounts and turn off notifications during other times. This will help us stay present and engaged in our face-to-face interactions.

Another solution is to develop social skills. Social skills are essential for effective face-to-face communication, and they can be improved with practice. We can develop our social skills by attending social events, joining clubs or groups that share our interests, and volunteering in our communities.

Conclusion:

In conclusion, social media has had a significant impact on face-to-face communication, and its negative effects cannot be ignored. However, there are ways to reduce these effects. By limiting our social media use and developing our social skills, we can become more engaged and effective communicators in our face-to-face interactions. Ultimately, the key to reducing the negative effects of social media is to recognize its limitations and to prioritize the relationships that matter most to us.

“Resilience” or “katatagan” is the word that describes the “image of the Filipino people” in times of adversity. Often, traditional and social media depict us as a bunch of people smiling amid the face of adversity. It somehow fosters a national image of a strong nation--the one that bounces back quickly with a joke and a smile as his or her weapon. Seeing this somehow uplifts the spirit and hopeful that life is normal, or shall resume normally soon.

However, this “resilience” has also been contentious. People tend to dismiss adversities as something we should deal with everyday. Since we’re “resilient,” we often brush off risks in an advent of another event, and would do the same thing “over and over.” If you complain about the inconveniences, people would often see you as negative and a pessimist—not helping uplift the spirit and the society needed in these tough times. The image of “resiliency” being played over and over again seems to border “insanity” — doing the same thing while expecting a different result.
