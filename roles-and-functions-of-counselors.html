26. What are the roles and functions of counselors?


Who are counselors and their roles?

Counselors are individuals that have been educated and experienced in learning human behavior, and their primary task is to assist individuals who need therapy in achieving an optimum degree of psychosocial functioning through applying ideas learned and, over time, learning the individual's way of life through counseling.

Counselors provide support to clients, partners, friends, and communities who are struggling with mental health and well-being problems. Often counselors take a holistic approach to their practice, using a health model (rather than a disease model) that emphasizes and supports clients' abilities.

What are the roles of counselors?

Improve mental wellbeing by working with people, organizations, and neighborhoods.

Encourage clients to talk about their feelings and perceptions.

Substance misuse, aging, bullying, conflict control, employment, loneliness, families, LGBTQ problems, self-image, tension, and suicide are all topics to consider.

Collaborate for relatives

Assist clients in defining their objectives, developing action plans, and gaining input.

Create therapeutic procedures.

Clients should be referred to psychiatrists and other programs.

Approach mental health treatment from a holistic (mind-body) perspective.
