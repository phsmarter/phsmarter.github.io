How does animals and humans differ in terms of language and communication?​

What is language and communication?

Language is a method of communication that uses both verbal and nonverbal codes to convey information. Communication is the process of two or more people exchanging messages or information while focused on the message. Language is a communication instrument.

The most significant distinction between people and animals is that humans speak and animals do not. Many creatures communicate in some way, but only humans have something as complex as language.

Signals are used in both animal and human communication. Sound or gestures that have meaning for individuals who use them are known as signals. Both signals and symbols are used in human communication. Sounds, gestures, material items, or written words that have special meaning to a group of people are referred to as symbols.
