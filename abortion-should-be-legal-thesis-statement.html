It should be legal, since regardless of the law women will get abortions, and it is better for all that women have access to safe abortions, than illegal and potentially deadly abortions.

The US failed experiment with banning alcohol during Prohibition shows that passing a law to ban something isn't going to work if a large percentage of the population isn't going to support the law. Alcohol is the most abused drug, but banning it created more problems than it solved. Same for abortion.

Back when abortions were illegal, there were cases of some young 16 and 15 year old dying due to an illegal abortion, and that is a tragedy that should not be repeated. For those morally opposed to abortion, the solution isn't banning it, but persuading people there are better options. Let a scared young girl who thinks her whole life will be ruined know that she has options, and things won't be as bad as she thinks.

Abortion should be legal because it’s the best way to make the abortion rate decrease.

Abortions have always existed. Women have been having abortions since before we became humans, indeed abortions in some form exist among apes and all mammals, while birds can simply expel an egg and ignore it to die. Making abortion illegal does nothing to decrease the abortion rate, it only makes abortions more dangerous and widespread. But being illegal abortion prices become very high, so women looking for abortions either wait too long in order to collect the money, or can only afford unsafe rudimentary abortion methods (like using knitting needles).

What happens when abortion is legal? It emerges, it becomes visible and accounted for. Prices drops (especially if there is access to legal free abortion through the single-payer healthcare system) so women in need of an abortion can start immediately to organize a solution. As it emerges, it becomes easier to identify the causes of the abortion requests and address them. If, for instance, it emerges a lack of sexual education in teens, it becomes easier to institute mandatory sex education classes in schools, or if a leading cause is identified in the fact that new mothers are at risk of being fired, it becomes possible to legiferate to prevent this (for instance, forbidding the firing of the new mothers for a period of 3 years unless there are severe reasons that prevent her to remain in the position). This decreases the need for abortions.

Another tool to decrease the abortion rates occurs because women who consider an abortion can get be given alternatives, which include economic support for the mother and child, protection programs for women who are being abused (family abuse is one of the causes of abortion), and also access to adoption programs. When abortion is illegal women are never offered alternatives the result is also that less cases of abuse are discovered.

As a matter of facts, a constant is that as the abortion gets legalized, the abortion rate explodes for the first 3–5 years, to then drop slowly and constantly. This occurs in pretty much all countries where abortion is legalized, a repetitive pattern that shows how legalization of the abortion is the best way to fight abortion, while making it illegal is actually a way to raise the abortion rates.

There is also another range of cases in which abortion is necessary, and that’s medical abortions. When the fetus is severely diseased, the likelihood of a healthy birth is low, and the likelihood of death for the mother is high. In this cases, especially when faced with a 100% probability of death at birth or miscarriage, the abortion is the safest option for the mother.

Is there a line to be drawn? I am partial to the way abortion is managed in most of Europe, with abortion being accessible on request until the 12th week (more or less), and medical abortion being legal at least until the 23rd week.

They point out that women have the right to control their own bodies, and that denying them the ability to have an abortion can have serious consequences for their health and well-being.

In terms of evidence, studies have shown that making abortion illegal does not actually reduce the number of abortions that take place.
