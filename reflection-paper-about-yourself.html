What activities or assignments helped you learn the best/most this quarter?

Reflect on the different activities or assignments you completed throughout the quarter and identify which ones you found most engaging, interesting, and helpful in your learning process. Be specific about what made these activities or assignments effective for you.

How did you know you were learning?

Think about the signs that indicated you were making progress in your learning, such as being able to apply new concepts or skills, seeing improvements in your grades or performance, or feeling more confident in your abilities.

Which concepts and skills do you feel the most confident with now?

Identify the specific concepts and skills that you feel the most comfortable with after completing the course. Reflect on what helped you develop this confidence, such as specific activities or assignments, feedback, or practice.

What type of activities or assignments were the most thought-provoking for you?

Think about the activities or assignments that challenged you to think deeply or critically, and reflect on what made them thought-provoking for you. This could be something that challenged your beliefs or assumptions, pushed you out of your comfort zone, or required you to make connections between different concepts.

Identify your effort level for this class. (10 is the highest level)

Be honest with yourself about how much time and energy you dedicated to the course throughout the quarter. Reflect on what factors influenced your effort level, such as interest in the subject matter, competing priorities, or challenges you faced.

What type of feedback was the most motivating to you over the quarter?

Think about the feedback you received throughout the quarter and identify what type of feedback was most helpful and motivating for you. Was it specific feedback that helped you improve your performance or more general encouragement that boosted your confidence?

How much time did you spend on English assignments and activities each day, approximately?

Reflect on how much time you devoted to English assignments and activities each day, and consider how you could have managed your time more effectively or efficiently.

Think about the ways in which you developed as a learner throughout the quarter, such as improving your study habits, developing new skills, or gaining a deeper understanding of the subject matter.

How does your learning connect with other content areas and with real life?
