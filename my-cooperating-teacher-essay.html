2. Technical and scientific cooperation -essay​


Answer:

The Technical and Scientific Cooperation (TSC) Portfolio under the Convention on Biological Diversity aims to encourage and facilitate action to enhance the implementation of Articles 16, 17 and 18, as well as related provisions of the Convention and its Protocols, through a coordinated and integrated approach.

The objective is to enable Parties to pursue their individual or collective biodiversity-related goals through matchmaking, cooperative exchange of knowledge and expertise, as well as by improving access to available data, resources and technologies.

Through a number of bespoke programmes, the CBD Secretariat:

Catalyzes cooperation through matchmaking.

Nurtures partnerships by leveraging a series of round tables and expert dialogues.

Facilitates access to innovative solutions to address biodiversity-related challenges through the organization of knowledge fairs.

Promotes the use of gaming tools to raise awareness and inspire action.

Tests out new ideas for transformative change through technology transfer and open innovation.

The CBD Secretariat also helps to support and accelerate the work of the Consortium of Scientific Partners on Biodiversity and its broader member organizations to contribute to the successful implementation of the Strategic Plan for Biodiversity 2011-2020 and the Aichi Biodiversity Targets.

16. what questions are ask by cooperating teacher in the course of discussion.​


Answer:

During a discussion with a cooperating teacher, there are several types of questions that may be asked, including:

1. Clarifying questions: These are used to help the cooperating teacher understand the student's perspective or to gather more information about a particular topic.

2. Probing questions: These are used to encourage the student to think more deeply about a topic, to explore different perspectives, or to draw connections between different pieces of information.

3. Critical thinking questions: These are designed to challenge the student's assumptions, to promote problem-solving and creative thinking, or to foster higher-order thinking skills.

4. Evaluation questions: These are used to assess the student's understanding or application of a particular concept or skill.

5. Summative questions: These are meant to help the cooperating teacher understand how well the student has learned the material, what still needs to be reviewed, and what future plans should be.

6. Reflective questions: These are aimed to help the student to think about what they have learned, how they have grown, and how they can improve in the future.

7. Questions about the student-teacher dynamic: These are related to the relationship between the student and teacher and the way the class is being executed.

It's important to note that the questions that the cooperating teacher ask can vary depending on the context of the discussion, the lesson plan, and the teaching method. The teacher may adjust the questions accordingly to make sure that it aligns with the goals of the discussion.
