2. essay comparing and contrasting past and present life​


Answer:

Life in the past and life in the present have many differences and similarities. One of the major differences is the advancements in technology and the convenience it brings to our daily lives. In the past, people had to rely on manual labor and simple tools to accomplish tasks, whereas today we have access to advanced machinery and gadgets that make our lives easier and more efficient.

Another significant difference is the increase in globalization and connectivity. In the past, people lived in isolated communities and had limited interactions with others outside their immediate surroundings. Today, with the help of the internet and social media, we are able to connect with people all over the world and access a vast array of information and resources.

Despite these differences, there are also many similarities between life in the past and present. For instance, both societies value family, community, and hard work. In the past, people relied on their families and communities for support and security, and today we still prioritize these connections and rely on them for support and guidance. Additionally, the importance of hard work and determination has not changed, as both past and present societies value individuals who are dedicated and dedicated to their goals.

In conclusion, while life in the past and present have many differences, there are also many similarities. Both societies value family, community, and hard work, and both have faced challenges and advancements in their respective time periods. However, the advancements in technology and globalization have greatly impacted the way we live and interact with the world around us.

Answer:

The use of active or passive voice is a fundamental distinction in English, and one that causes trouble for many writers – including native English speakers!

Growing up in American schools, students are often taught that they should avoid the passive voice because it is “weak.” However, the choice between active and passive is actually quite nuanced.

Depending on the ideas you are trying to express and the conventions of the discipline/journal in which you are writing, the passive voice can be an appropriate, sophisticated, and even preferable choice over the active voice. Nevertheless, the active voice is sometimes a far better choice, and you may use both in the same article depending on the context and content of your sentences and the section of your paper you are writing.
