24. How did Kodak's business model and competitive advantages change over time, and what impact did these changes have on the company's performance? â€‹


Kodak was once a leader in the photography industry, but the company's business model and competitive advantages changed significantly over time. At its peak, Kodak's business model was focused on film and cameras, with a strong emphasis on vertical integration, meaning that it owned the entire supply chain, from manufacturing film to developing and printing photographs.

However, with the advent of digital photography, Kodak's competitive advantages were eroded. Unlike film, digital photography did not require a physical product, and Kodak was unable to keep up with the technological changes. As a result, Kodak's business model shifted to focus on digital photography and printers, but this was not enough to keep the company competitive.

Kodak's business model and competitive advantages also suffered due to the company's inability to adapt to changes in consumer behavior. Instead of embracing the shift towards digital photography, Kodak clung to its film-based business model for too long, and was slow to develop digital products that could compete with its rivals.

The impact of these changes on Kodak's performance was significant. The company's revenue declined sharply, and it struggled to remain profitable. Kodak ultimately filed for bankruptcy in 2012, after years of declining sales and profits.

In conclusion, Kodak's inability to adapt to changes in technology and consumer behavior led to a decline in its business model and competitive advantages. The company's failure to keep up with the times ultimately had a negative impact on its performance and contributed to its bankruptcy.


25. How do companies achieve competitive advantage through business strategy?â€‹

Strategies for Competitive Advantage

There are three strategies for establishing a competitive advantage: Cost Leadership, Differentiation, and Focus (Cost-focus and Differentiation-focus).

Answer:

There are three strategies for establishing a competitive advantage: Cost Leadership, Differentiation, and Focus (Cost-focus and Differentiation-focus).

Explanation:

#1 Cost Leadership

In a cost leadership strategy, the objective is to become the lowest-cost producer. This is achieved through large-scale production where companies can exploit economies of scale.

#2 Differentiation

In a differentiation strategy, a companyâ€™s products or services are differentiated from that of its competitors. This can be done by delivering high-quality products or services to customers or innovating products or services.

#3 Focus

In a focus strategy, a company focuses on a narrow target market segment. This strategy is successful if the company is able to successfully create products/services that can cater to these customers. The focus strategy also has two variants;

Cost-focus: Lowest-cost producer in a narrow market segment

Differentiation-focus: Differentiated products/services in a narrow market segment
