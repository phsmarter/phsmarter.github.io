10. write an essay about multiple intellegences and observe the rules in academic writting.apply academic text,reading and language as much as possible.​


Answer:

Multiple Intelligences-Based Instructional Strategy of English Academic Writing Skill Instruction (MIB-IS of EAWSI)

p> This study is focused on the implementation of Multiple Intelligences-Based Instructional Strategy (MIB-IS) to improve students English Academic Writing Skill Instruction (EAWSI). It uses Classroom Action Research (CAR). There are four steps in this CAR: (1) plan, (2) action, (3) observation, and (4) reflection. Multiple Intelligences-Based Instructional Strategy (MIB-IS) can improve Senior High School students’ academic writing (argumentative essay writing) in eight students’ intelligences (linguistic, logical-mathematical, spatial, bodily-kinesthetic, musical, interpersonal, intrapersonal, and naturalist) following the writing process of creating, outlining, drafting, and polishing. The process of academic writing instruction through MIB-IS will be more effective if the students are able to be more active in following every single step of writing process to hit the big issues of content, organization, unity, coherence, logic and the smaller issues of grammar, sentence-structure, punctuation, and spelling. And by giving an academic writing exercise in every phase of instruction is really important to improve students’ argumentative essay writing skill for students to master the concepts comprehensively and integrated properly. And so, the school as an educational institution must prepare its students to go to the university by the capability of supporting MIB-IS implementation in academic writing instruction as an effective English instructional strategy. </p

25. Short essay about too much is taken from earth and too much is put into earth


Explanation:

Human beings have been exploiting natural resources for their survival and growth since the beginning of civilization. However, in recent times, this exploitation has reached alarming levels, leading to the depletion of resources and environmental degradation. On one hand, too much is taken from the earth, and on the other hand, too much is put into the earth.

The demand for natural resources like water, minerals, and fossil fuels has increased exponentially with the growth of population and urbanization. Human activities like deforestation, mining, and overfishing have resulted in the depletion of these resources. This has not only led to the loss of biodiversity but also affected the ecological balance of the planet. The overuse of water has led to water scarcity in many parts of the world, while the extraction of fossil fuels has led to greenhouse gas emissions and climate change.

On the other hand, human activities like industrialization and urbanization have led to the pollution of land, air, and water. The disposal of solid and liquid wastes has led to soil contamination, while the emission of pollutants has led to air and water pollution. This has not only affected human health but also the health of the planet. The use of chemical fertilizers and pesticides in agriculture has also led to soil degradation and the loss of soil fertility.

Therefore, it is important to strike a balance between the use and preservation of natural resources. Sustainable development that takes into account the needs of the present generation without compromising the ability of future generations to meet their own needs is the need of the hour. It is important to reduce the use of non-renewable resources and adopt renewable sources of energy. Efforts should be made to reduce waste generation, recycle and reuse resources, and adopt cleaner production methods.

In conclusion, the earth's resources are finite, and their depletion and degradation have far-reaching consequences. It is, therefore, important to strike a balance between the use and preservation of natural resources. The sustainable development approach is a step towards achieving this balance and ensuring a healthy planet for future generations.

Answer:

Simultaneously, our actions are burdening the Earth with excessive waste and pollutants. Plastic waste litters our oceans and landfills, taking centuries to decompose. Harmful chemicals from industries seep into soil and water bodies, contaminating ecosystems and posing risks to human health. Carbon emissions from burning fossil fuels trap heat in the atmosphere, leading to global warming and climate disruptions.

This imbalance between taking and giving back is unsustainable. It compromises the Earth's capacity to regenerate and support life as we know it. We must recognize the urgency of the situation and strive for a more harmonious relationship with the planet.

To address this issue, a paradigm shift is required. We need to embrace sustainable practices that prioritize conservation, regeneration, and circularity. It involves adopting renewable energy sources, reducing waste generation, promoting recycling and reusing, and restoring degraded ecosystems.

Moreover, we must foster a collective sense of responsibility towards the Earth. Governments, businesses, and individuals all play vital roles in mitigating this crisis. Governments must enforce stringent environmental regulations, invest in green technologies, and promote sustainable development. Businesses should adopt sustainable practices and prioritize social and environmental responsibility alongside profits. Individuals can make a difference through conscious consumption, reducing their ecological footprint, and advocating for change.

Educating future generations about the importance of environmental stewardship is also crucial. By instilling a deep respect for nature and understanding the consequences of our actions, we can cultivate a generation that values sustainability and actively works towards a healthier planet.

In conclusion, the imbalance between taking from and giving back to the Earth is an urgent concern. To ensure a sustainable future, we must shift towards practices that prioritize the preservation of natural resources, the reduction of waste and pollutants, and the restoration of ecosystems. By doing so, we can restore the Earth's equilibrium and secure a healthy planet for generations to come.
