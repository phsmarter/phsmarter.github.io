1. Summary of Qualitative Research Techniques

Qualitative research is an approach that is used to gain insight into people's thoughts, opinions, and experiences. It involves the use of interviews, observations, and focus groups to collect data. The data collected is analyzed and interpreted to draw conclusions. The main goal of qualitative research is to understand the underlying reasons and motivations behind human behavior.

2. Three Specific Techniques Used in Qualitative Research

a. Interviews: Interviews are conversations between the researcher and the participant. The goal is to gain an understanding of the participant's experiences, attitudes, and beliefs. Interviews can be conducted face-to-face, over the phone, or online.

b. Observations: Observations involve the researcher watching and recording the behavior of the participants. The goal is to understand how people interact with each other and their environment. Observations can be done in natural settings, such as homes or workplaces, or in controlled settings, such as a laboratory.

c. Focus groups: Focus groups involve a group of people discussing a particular topic. The goal is to gain insight into the opinions and attitudes of the group. Focus groups can be conducted in person or online.

3. Potential Application of Qualitative Research

In the field of education, qualitative research can be used to gain insight into the experiences and attitudes of students, teachers, and administrators. For example, a researcher might conduct interviews with students to understand their experiences with online learning during the COVID-19 pandemic. This information could be used to improve online learning programs and make them more effective.

4. Example of Qualitative Research in the Real World

A study conducted by Grol-Prokopczyk (2019) used qualitative research to understand the experience of women who did not have children. The study found that these women faced social stigma and pressure to conform to societal expectations. The implications of this research are that society needs to be more accepting of women who choose not to have children.

9. Choose five kinds of qualitative research and find one example of actual research studies/articles for each. Make a summary for each study and explain why it belongs to that type of qualitative research.​


Answer:

1. Phenomenology:

Study: “Life after burn: Understanding the experiences of burn survivors”, International Journal of Nursing Sciences, 2018.

Summary: This study explored the lived experiences of burn survivors in order to better understand the psychosocial impact of their injuries. Researchers conducted in-depth interviews with 10 burn survivors to gather information about their physical, emotional, and social experiences following their burns.

Explanation: This research belongs to phenomenology because it aims to understand the essence of the participants’ experiences as burn survivors, focusing on their subjective perceptions, meanings, and interpretations of their lived experiences.

2. Grounded theory:

Study: “Becoming a leader: A grounded theory study of nurse managers' experiences”, Journal of Nursing Management, 2019.

Summary: This study used grounded theory to explore the experiences of nurse managers who were new to their leadership roles. Researchers conducted semi-structured interviews with 20 nurse managers and used open, axial, and selective coding to develop a theoretical model of the leadership development process.

Explanation: This research study belongs to grounded theory because it aims to develop a theory or model that explains the essential processes, concepts, and relationships that underlie a particular phenomenon, in this case, the process of becoming a nurse manager.

3. Ethnography:

Study: “Soccer fandom and protest in Turkey: Insights from a football ultras fieldwork”, International Review for the Sociology of Sport, 2018.

Summary: This study used ethnographic methods to explore the role of soccer fandom in political protest movements in Turkey. Researchers conducted participant observation and semi-structured interviews with members of a football ultras group over a one-year period.

Explanation: This research belongs to ethnography because it aims to understand the social and cultural practices, values, beliefs, and meanings of a particular group or community, in this case, soccer fans in Turkey, within their natural setting and context.

4. Case study:

Study: “'Extreme Makeover': A Case Study of an Urban School Teacher's Experience with Disruptive Behavior and Accommodation”, Journal of Cases in Educational Leadership, 2019.

Summary: This case study examined a teacher’s experience of dealing with disruptive student behavior and her attempts to accommodate individual student needs in a challenging urban school setting. The data was collected through semi-structured interviews, observations, and document analysis.

Explanation: This research belongs to case study because it focuses on a particular individual, group, situation, or event in detail in order to understand its complexity, richness, and uniqueness.

5. Narrative analysis:

Study: “The Influence of Stories on Perceptions of Workplace Incivility”, Journal of Applied Communication Research, 2019.

Summary: This study used narrative analysis to investigate the role of storytelling in shaping employees’ perspectives on workplace incivility. The researchers conducted in-depth interviews with 17 participants and analyzed the narratives they shared about incivility at work.

Explanation: This research belongs to narrative analysis because it aims to understand the ways in which stories and narratives shape individuals' beliefs, attitudes, and behaviors around a particular topic or phenomenon (incivility in the workplace).

1. Ethnography - This type of research involves in-depth observation and analysis of a particular cultural group or community.

Example: "Body and soul: A phenomenological study of the lived experience of yoga" by Catherine Cook-Cottone. This study is an ethnographic exploration of the lived experience of practicing yoga among a group of women. The author conducted interviews and observations of the participants over several months to gain insight into their perceptions and experiences of yoga.

2. Case Study - A case study involves an in-depth analysis of a particular individual, group, or situation.

Example: "The impact of parental involvement on children's education" by Jessica Gadsden. This study is a case study of a particular school and its efforts to engage parents in their children's education. The author conducted interviews with parents, teachers, and administrators to gain insight into the impact of parental involvement on student achievement.

3. Phenomenology - This type of research involves exploring the subjective experiences and perceptions of individuals.

Example: "The meaning of mental illness for Chinese immigrants" by Jennifer Fung. This study is a phenomenological exploration of the lived experiences of mental illness among Chinese immigrants in the United States. The author conducted interviews with participants to gain insight into their perceptions and experiences of mental illness.

4. Grounded Theory - Grounded theory involves generating theories and hypotheses from data collected through interviews or observations.

Example: "The process of resilience among older adults living with chronic illness" by Taryn Lindhorst. This study is a grounded theory exploration of the process of resilience among older adults living with chronic illness. The author conducted interviews with participants to identify key themes and patterns that emerged during the data analysis.

5. Narrative Analysis - This type of research involves analyzing stories or narratives to gain insight into the experiences and perspectives of individuals.

Example: "Coming out as trans: A narrative analysis" by Laura McGuire. This study is a narrative analysis of the experiences of transgender individuals coming out to their families and friends. The author analyzed the stories shared by participants in online forums to gain insight into the challenges and opportunities of coming out as trans.

In summary, each of these studies represents a different type of qualitative research, and each employs different methods and techniques to explore a particular research question or phenomenon. Ethnography involves in-depth observation and analysis of a cultural group or community, case study involves an in-depth analysis of a particular individual, group, or situation, phenomenology explores the subjective experiences and perceptions of individuals, grounded theory generates theories and hypotheses from data, and narrative analysis involves analyzing stories or narratives to gain insight into the experiences and perspectives of individuals.
