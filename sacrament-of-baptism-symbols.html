13. ●•●what is the significance of each sacramental symbol in the rite of baptism? what does it contribute to your deeper understanding and application of the sacrament?​


Answer:

1. Water: Water is a symbol of purification and new life in baptism. It represents the washing away of original sin and the start of a new life in Christ. It also reminds us of the importance of living a life of service and humility, just as Jesus was baptized in the River Jordan.

2. Oil: Oil is a symbol of strength, healing, and anointing in baptism. It represents the strengthening of the individual's faith and the gifts of the Holy Spirit, such as wisdom, understanding, and courage. It also reminds us of the importance of being nourished by the word of God and the sacraments in order to grow in faith.

3. White Garment: The white garment symbolizes the individual's new life in Christ, and the putting on of the new self, made in God's image. It represents the individual's commitment to living a life of holiness and righteousness, and being clothed in the righteousness of Christ.

4. Lighted Candle: The lighted candle symbolizes the individual's entrance into the community of faith, and the light of Christ shining within them. It represents the individual's commitment to living as a child of the light, and the call to be a witness to the light of Christ in the world.

27. -In what sense is the sacrament of baptism an "unfinished sacrament"?​


Answer:

The sacrament of baptism is often referred to as an "unfinished sacrament" because it marks the beginning of a person's faith journey, and it is up to the individual to continue to grow and develop in their faith throughout their life. It is a sacrament that initiates a person into the Christian community and bestows grace, but it is not a one-time event, rather it requires a lifetime of effort and commitment to live out the promises made during the baptism. It is a foundation for the other sacraments and for the Christian life as a whole, and it is through ongoing formation, prayer, and sacramental practice that one is able to fully live out the graces received through baptism.
