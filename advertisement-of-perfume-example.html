19. perfume output cut the picture of advertisement form and old newspaper or a magazine and paste it on a sheet on paper below the picture write or identity the propaganda technique used.​


Answer:

Some common propaganda techniques used in advertising include:

Bandwagon: This technique tries to persuade the audience to do something because "everyone else is doing it."

Testimonial: This technique uses the endorsement of a celebrity or someone considered to be an expert to sell a product.

Transfer: This technique associates a product with positive symbols, values, or feelings in order to transfer these positive associations to the product being advertised.

Glittering generalities: This technique uses vague, emotional words and phrases to appeal to the audience's values and beliefs, without providing any specific information about the product.

Snob appeal: This technique targets the audience's desire to be seen as sophisticated or fashionable by using exclusivity and elitism to sell a product.

Plain folks: This technique uses ordinary people to sell a product, implying that the product is "down-to-earth" and suitable for everyday use.

These are just a few examples of propaganda techniques that may be used in an advertisement for a perfume. It's always important to critically evaluate the claims made in any advertisement and to consider the techniques that may be used to persuade you to buy a product.
