Relative pronoun introduces a clause, or part of a sentence, that describes a noun. The most common relative pronouns are who, whom, whose, which, and that. Sometimes when and where can be used as relative pronouns as well. Relative pronoun is placed directly after the noun it modifies. 7 Examples: ● The driver, who ran the stop sign. was careless. ● The children, whom we love dearly, need better educations. ● I have a friend whose cat is adorable. ● The book, which is now out of print, has all the information you need. ● This is the book that everyone is talking about.
