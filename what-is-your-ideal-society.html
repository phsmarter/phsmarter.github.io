18. What is your own opinion with regards to the society where Jesus had lived?Is it an ideal society or not? Why?​


Answer:

Recent discussions of Jesus' social class try to locate him within the social structures of Mediterranean society generally, or Galilean society, in the first century. And there seems to be a debate among many contemporary scholars of Jesus as to whether he was really a peasant or... somewhat higher in the socio-economic strata. We know in general he was low class, by the standards of the Roman imperial aristocracy or even of the ruling class of Palestine, the Herodian client kings. But he may have been an artisan. He doesn't seem to have been a peasant in the strict sense, someone who was working the land for a living. He was close, however, to peasant society; all of the images in his parables and his aphorisms are firmly rooted in peasant society and call upon everyday things like a sower, or sowing seed. But they also call upon images of land owners and relationships between slave owners and slaves, masters and servants. So Jesus seems to have been aware of that level of the socio-economic mix. And he may well have stood in some relationship to it. So an artisan of some sort is probably the best way of describing him.

An ideal society can be described as one that promotes the well-being and happiness of its citizens while upholding the principles of justice, equality, and individual freedom. In such a society, people are free to express their ideas, beliefs, and values without fear of persecution or discrimination. They are also encouraged to participate actively in the political process and have a voice in shaping the policies that affect their lives.

In an ideal society, education is accessible to all, and opportunities for personal growth and development are available to everyone regardless of their socio-economic background. This means that access to healthcare, employment, and affordable housing is guaranteed, and social safety nets are in place to support those who need it.

Another critical feature of an ideal society is the protection and preservation of the natural environment. This means that people are mindful of their ecological footprint and take measures to reduce their impact on the environment. Sustainability and conservation are prioritized, and policies are in place to promote the use of renewable resources and protect natural habitats and biodiversity.

Furthermore, an ideal society values diversity and celebrates cultural differences. People from different backgrounds and ethnicities are respected and treated equally, and discrimination on the basis of race, gender, sexual orientation, or religion is not tolerated.

In conclusion, an ideal society is one that promotes the well-being of its citizens while upholding the principles of justice, equality, and individual freedom. It is a society that values education, personal growth, and the protection of the natural environment. It is also one that values diversity and celebrates cultural differences. While such a society may not be perfect, striving towards these ideals can lead to a more just, equitable, and sustainable future for all.
