17. A police station has 25 vehicles of cars and motorcycles. the total number of wheels is 70.find the number of cars and motorcycles the station has


Answer:

There are 10 cars and 15 motorcycles.

Step-by-step explanation:

Let x be the number of cars

Let  y be the number of motorcycles

x + y = 25   -------equation 1

4x + 2y = 70  ------equation 2

From equation 1

x + y = 25

x = 25 - y   ----equation 1'

Substitute equation 1' to equation 2

4x + 2y = 70

4(25 - y) + 2y = 70

100 - 4y + 2y = 70

Transpose

4y - 2y = 100 -70

2y = 30

y = 30/2

y = 15

Substitute y = 15 to equation 1'

x = 25- y

x = 25-15

x = 10

28. The ratio of the number of cars to the number of motorcycles at a car park was 2 : 5. When 22 more cars entered the car park and 22 motorcycles left the car park, the ratio became 3 : 2. How many cars were there at the car park at first? *


Ratio and Proportion

The problem is an application of ratio and proportion. Let's define, ratio and proportion.

What is Ratio

A ratio is an ordered pair of numbers a and b, written as a / b or a : b, where a and b does not equal to zero. Remember that, ratio is always written in lowest term.

What is Proportion

A proportion is an equation in which two ratios are set equal to each other.

Example

2/3 = 4/6

1/3 = 3/9

4/7 = 8/14

You can identify if the set of ratio are proportion when the cross product is equal.

2/3 = 4/6

Cross Product → (2)(6) = (3)(4) → 12 = 12 ✓

In the given problem "The ratio of the number of cars to the number of motorcycles at a car park was 2 : 5. When 22 more cars entered the car park and 22 motorcycles left the car park, the ratio became 3 : 2. How many cars were there at the car park at first?"

Solution and DiscussionStep 1: List down all the given information and/or data

ratio of the number of cars to motorcycles was 2 : 5

when 22 more cars entered the car park and 22 motorcycles left the car park, the ratio became 3 : 2.

Step 2: Identify what is being asked

How many cars were there at the car park at first?

Step 3: Make a mathematical equation

2:5 can be written as 2/5

3:2 can be written as 3/2

let x is the number of cars

let y is the number of motorcycle

2/5 = x/y → at first

3/2 = (x+22)/(y-22)

Step 4: Solve for the unknown variables

2/5 = x/y

3/2 = (x+22)/(y-22)

Apply system of linear equation

2/5 = x/y → 5x = 2y

3/2 = (x+22)/(y-22) →2(x+22) = 3(y-22)

5x = 2y → y = 5x/2

Solve for x, when y = 5x/2

2(x+22) = 3(y-22) →2(x+22) = 3[(5x/2)-22)]

2(x+22) = 3[(5x/2)-22)]

2x+44 = 15x/2 - 66

2[2x+44] = 2[15x/2 - 66]

4x + 88 = 15x - 132

15x - 4x = 88 + 132

11x = 220

x = 220/11

x = 20

y = 5x/2, when x = 20

y = 5(20)/2

y = 100/2

y = 50

Final Answer20 cars

speed of car =(2×40-30)=50 miles/ hr

Step-by-step explanation:


Answer:

Motorcycle = 40mph, car = 50mph


Step-by-step explanation:

Define x:

Let the speed of the motorcycle be x mph

The speed of the car is (2x - 30) mph


Find the distance traveled by the motorcycle in two hours:

Speed = x mph

Time taken = 2 hours


Distance = Speed x Time

Distance = 2x miles


Find the distance traveled by the car in two hours:

Speed = (2x -30) mph

Time taken = 2 hours


Distance = Speed x Time

Distance = 2(2x - 30)  miles


Solve x:

They are 20 miles apart

2(2x - 30) - 2x = 20

4x - 60 - 2x = 20

2x - 60 = 20

2x = 80

x = 40


Find the speed:

Motorcycle = x = 40 miles per hour

Car = 2x = 30 = 2(40) - 30 = 50 miles per hour


Answer: Motorcycle = 40mph, car = 50mph
