Answer:

When you show someone how to make an omelette, change a tire, or set up an instant messaging account, you are using a pattern of development called "process analysis"; when you describe what your roomate looks like when she gets up in the morning you are using "description"; when you offer several reasons why you are late for an appointment, you are using cause and effect; and when you tell the story of your first date, you are using narration. Learning to use these patterns of development in writing is not difficult: you will need to familiarize yourself with key transitional expressions that send a signal to the reader that a particular pattern is being used, and you will need to make conscious decisions about which strategie best develops your ideas.

19. general to particular pattern of idea development examples​


Answer:

eneral to particular pattern of idea development is a way of organizing information that starts with broad or general statements and gradually narrows down to more specific details. Here are some examples of this pattern:

Animal Classification:

General: Animals are classified into different groups based on their characteristics.

Particular: Mammals are a type of animal that have hair or fur, produce milk, and give birth to live young.

Food:

General: Food is essential for survival and provides us with energy.

Particular: Fruits and vegetables are important sources of vitamins and minerals that help keep our bodies healthy.

Literature Analysis:

General: Literature is a form of artistic expression that reflects the values and beliefs of a culture.

Particular: In the novel "To Kill a Mockingbird," the character of Atticus Finch represents the ideals of justice and morality.

History:

General: The study of history helps us understand the past and how it has shaped the present.

Particular: The American Civil War was a pivotal moment in US history that led to the abolition of slavery and the expansion of civil rights.

Science:

General: Science is a method of inquiry that seeks to explain natural phenomena.

Particular: The theory of evolution is a scientific explanation for the diversity of life on Earth, based on the principles of natural selection and genetic variation.

Overall, the general to particular pattern is a useful way of organizing ideas and information in a logical and coherent manner. It allows us to start with broad concepts and gradually delve into more specific details, making complex topics more accessible and easier to understand.

26. five examples for each pattern of idea development using grammatical signals​


Answer:

Here are five examples for each pattern of idea development using grammatical signals:

1. Definition and Classification:

- Definition: The term "Big Data" refers to massive sets of structured and unstructured data gathered from various sources.

- Classification: The types of renewable energy include solar, wind, hydropower, biomass, and geothermal.

2. Listing:

- Examples: There are several examples of renewable energy, including solar power, wind turbines, hydropower stations, biomass energy, and geothermal power.

- Advantages: The advantages of renewable energy are numerous, such as reducing greenhouse gas emissions, generating local economic development, and decreasing reliance on fossil fuels.

3. Comparison and Contrast:

- Comparison: Both conventional and renewable energy sources have their benefits and drawbacks, with the former having a steady supply and higher initial costs, while the latter generating lesser environmental impacts and requiring greater investment in infrastructure.

- Contrast: While renewable energy sources are typically more environmentally friendly than conventional energy sources, they often have more significant upfront costs.

4. Cause and Effect:

- Cause: The continuous burning of fossil fuels releases carbon dioxide and other greenhouse gases into the atmosphere, which contributes to climate change.

- Effect: The effects of climate change include more frequent and intense natural disasters, such as hurricanes, heatwaves, and droughts.

5. Process:

- Steps: The scientific method has several key steps, including observation, hypothesis generation, experimental testing, data analysis, and conclusion.

- Procedure: The process of making a computer chip involves several stages, including designing a chip layout, fabricating a wafer, testing the chips, then packaging and shipping them.
