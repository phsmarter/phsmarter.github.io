Answer:

Depression can range from debilitating sadness to a constant, flat feeling. It can impact our appetite, sleeping habits, ability to enjoy activities, motivation, attention/focus, and even our willingness to live.

It can be situational (like after a break-up or the death of a loved one) or it can take up constant residency, offering little reprieve. It often leaves us feeling helpless as it impacts our sense of hope or belief in the possibility of change.

As we try to manage these feelings, we might find ourselves using coping strategies that work in the short-term but end up hurting us in the long run. Whether it is by isolating ourselves, drinking excessively, engaging in self-harm, or binge eating, we try to find ways that allow us to get past these painful moments.

While it can feel impossible to find our footing when in the grips of depression, counselling can offer the chance to put healthy coping strategies in place and address the root causes of these feelings.

If you are feeling the impacts of depression and would like to try counselling, feel free to reach out for further information.

25. reflection about depression years in 1930 to 1939?​


Explanation:

The Great Depression was a severe worldwide economic depression that took place mostly during the 1930s, beginning in the United States. The timing of the Great Depression varied across the world; in most countries, it started in 1929 and lasted until the late 1930s.[1] It was the longest, deepest, and most widespread depression of the 20th century.[2] The Great Depression is commonly used as an example of how intensely the global economy can decline.[3]

Dorothea Lange's Migrant Mother depicts destitute pea pickers in California, centering on Florence Owens Thompson, age 32, a mother of seven children, in Nipomo, California, March 1936.

US annual real GDP from 1910 to 1960, with the years of the Great Depression (1929–1939) highlighted

The unemployment rate in the US during 1910–60, with the years of the Great Depression (1929–39) highlighted

The Great Depression started in the United States after a major fall in stock prices that began around September 4, 1929, and became worldwide news with the stock market crash of October 29, 1929, (known as Black Tuesday). Between 1929 and 1932, worldwide gross domestic product (GDP) fell by an estimated 15%. By comparison, worldwide GDP fell by less than 1% from 2008 to 2009 during the Great Recession.[4] Some economies started to recover by the mid-1930s. However, in many countries, the negative effects of the Great Depression lasted until the beginning of World War II.[5]

The Great Depression had devastating effects in both rich and poor countries. Personal income, tax revenue, profits and prices dropped, while international trade fell by more than 50%. Unemployment in the U.S. rose to 23% and in some countries rose as high as 33%.[6]

Cities around the world were hit hard, especially those dependent on heavy industry. Construction was virtually halted in many countries. Farming communities and rural areas suffered as crop prices fell by about 60%.[7][8][9] Facing plummeting demand with few alternative sources of jobs, areas dependent on primary sector industries such as mining and logging suffered the most.[10]

See also: Timeline of the Great Depression

The Dow Jones Industrial Average, 1928–1930

Economic historians usually consider the catalyst of the Great Depression to be the sudden devastating collapse of U.S. stock market prices, starting on October 24, 1929. However,[11] some dispute this conclusion and see the stock crash as a symptom, rather than a cause, of the Great Depression.[6][12][full citation needed]

Even after the Wall Street Crash of 1929 optimism persisted for some time. John D. Rockefeller said "These are days when many are discouraged. In the 93 years of my life, depressions have come and gone. Prosperity has always returned and will again."[13] The stock market turned upward in early 1930, returning to early 1929 levels by April. This was still almost 30% below the peak of September 1929.[14]

Together, the government and business spent more in the first half of 1930 than in the corresponding period of the previous year. On the other hand, consumers, many of whom had suffered severe losses in the stock market the previous year, cut back their expenditures by 10%. In addition, beginning in the mid-1930s, a severe drought ravaged the agricultural heartland of the U.S.[15]

Interest rates had dropped to low levels by mid-1930, but expected deflation and the continuing reluctance of people to borrow meant that consumer spending and investment were depressed.[16] By May 1930, automobile sales had declined to below the levels of 1928. Prices, in general, began to decline, although wages held steady in 1930. Then a deflationary spiral started in 1931. Farmers faced a worse outlook; declining crop prices and a Great Plains drought crippled their economic outlook. At its peak, the Great Depression saw nearly 10% of all Great Plains farms change hands despite federal assistance.[17]

27. What is the meaning of depressions? and what are the types of depressions?


What is depression?

Depression - is marked especially by sadness, inactivity, difficulty in thinking and concentration, a significant increase or decrease in appetite and time spent sleeping, feelings of dejection and hopelessness, and sometimes suicidal tendencies.

○ Different Types of Depression ○

▪ Major depression

— it has severe symptoms lasting six months or more and has a profound effect on the patient's life.

▪ Bipolar disorder

— it's also known as manic depression . Those with this disorder may experience intense joy and deep sadness.

▪ Dysthymia

— although not as severe as major depression , has depressive symptoms that make it difficult for the patient to move normally. Others may also experience intense grief.

▪ Postpartum depression

— it is a debilitating mood disorder that affects many postpartum mothers.

▪ Seasonal affective disorder or SAD

— is more likely to occur due to lack of sunlight during the fall and winter. It usually disappears in spring and summer.
