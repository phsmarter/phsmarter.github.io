13. reasons why people communicate give the eight example​


Communication

Communication is an activity in which there is information between living things, whether they are ideas, ideas or messages, exchanged from one side to the other. You usually do this verbally or verbally in communication to achieve the ideal.

The most basic purpose of communication is to find, meaning to find something we need in communication. Whether finding identity, finding important information, and the like. Through communication we will learn to understand how we are and the people we are talking to.

Here are the reasons people communicate and examples :

1. Meeting needs

The first reason a person needs to communicate is so that he can fulfill his life needs. Communication is included in the social need to interact with other people. With communication, we can meet the needs of clothing, food, shelter and curiosity. Communication is a tool to convey the meaning of goods or services that we need.

Examples : Communications made by buyers and sellers in the market, Communications made by customer service to customers.

2. Exchange of information

Interaction is a relationship between two or more people who have mutual interests. Communication is a tool for interacting with each other. Maintain good relations with other people. With the interaction can be established cooperation, reciprocal relationships between humans.

Example: Communication between teachers and students at school and Communication between friends.

3. Deliver messages or information to others

Communication aims to provide information to others so that they are able to influence thoughts, change attitudes, and move others to do what is conveyed.

Example: In economic activities, communication is useful to state the quality of a product and then make an offer so that people want to buy or use the product.

4. Preventing Conflict

Lack of communication can lead to misunderstanding and frustration. This misunderstanding will lead to conflict. Good communication allows people to listen to each other, and this can reduce misunderstandings. Differences between individuals are unavoidable because everyone has a different character. That is an important value why we communicate, namely to prevent disputes in the family environment, workplace, with partners and friends.

Example: The existence of misunderstandings between families can be prevented by communicating in advance about an issue.

5. To get to know other people

Humans need to communicate so that everyone can know each other and understand what messages are conveyed. One's communication skills that need to be understood are listening, reading and interpreting. This tool to be able to communicate uses language, both verbal and spoken language.

For example: Introduction to new people and environments so communication is needed.

6. Express your opinion

There needs to be communication between humans so that when conveying opinions, ideas, ideas and input they have to others. With the hope that this opinion can be understood and finally accepted by others.

Example: Communication in meeting and debate activities to be able to express each person's opinion on these activities

7. Helps Maintain Relationships

Good communication can help build long term relationships with anyone. If you want to have a long-term good relationship with your partner, friend or business partner, it is very important to pay attention to the way of communication in everyday life.

Example: Communication between partners in a romantic relationship.

8. Decision making

Communication has a function as a decision maker, namely deciding whether or not to do certain activities. In this decision, it is influenced by 2 factors, namely it is decided by itself and decided after getting input from other people. The more important the decision of the parties who will be involved in communicating will be more and more.

Example: Communication between meeting members in making meeting decisions.
