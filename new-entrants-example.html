5. . Joe, s Superstore prevents competitors from enter the market by temporarily pricing its good below cost, thus driving new entrants out of business. This practice is known as ______________pricing.


Answer:

The practice that you described, in which a company temporarily prices its goods below cost in order to prevent competitors from entering the market and driving them out of business, is known as predatory pricing. Predatory pricing is a form of unfair competition in which a dominant firm intentionally sets prices below its own cost of production in order to drive competitors out of the market. Once the competitors are driven out, the dominant firm can then raise its prices and potentially profit from a lack of competition.

Predatory pricing is generally considered to be an unfair trade practice and is prohibited by competition laws in many countries. It can have negative effects on competition and can lead to higher prices for consumers in the long run.
