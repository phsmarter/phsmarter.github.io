24. a solution is prepared by dissolving 29 grams of sodium chloride in water to prepare 80 ml o solution what is the percentage by mass/volume of sodium chloride in the sodium PS HELP


Answer:

Given:

mass of solute (sodium chloride) - 29 g

volume of solution - 80 mL

Asked: % mass/volume

Formula: % mass/volume = [tex]\frac{mass of solute}{volume of solution}[/tex] x 100%

Solution:

% mass/volume = [tex]\frac{mass of solute}{volume of solution}[/tex] x 100%

                           = [tex]\frac{29g}{80 mL}[/tex] x 100%

                           = 0.3625 x 100%

                           = 36.25 %

Explanation:

The mass/volume percent of a solution refers to the ratio of the mass of solute that is present in a solution, relative to the volume of the solution, as a whole.  Because this type of concentration is expressed as a percentage, the indicated proportion must be multiplied by 100.

    % mass/volume = [tex]\frac{mass of solute}{volume of solution}[/tex] x 100%

A mass/volume percent concentration is most often used and calculated for solutions that are specifically prepared by dissolving solid solutes in liquid solvents. It is important to note that the mass of the solute must be expressed in grams, the volume of the solution must be provided in milliliters.

25. Chlorine gas can be prepared in the laboratory by the reaction of solid manganese dioxide with hydrochloric acid. (The other reaction products are aqueous manganese chloride and water.) How much MnO2 should be added to excess HCl to obtain 275 mL of chlorine gas at 5.0C and 650 mmHg?


SOLUTION:

Step 1: Calculate the number of moles of gas using ideal gas equation.

275 mL = 0.275 L

5.0°C = 278.15K

650 mmHg = 0.85526 atm

[tex]\begin{aligned} n & = \frac{PV}{\text{RT}} \\ & = \frac{(\text{0.85526 atm})(\text{0.275 L})}{\left(0.082057 \: \dfrac{\text{L}\cdot\text{atm}}{\text{mol}\cdot\text{K}}\right)(\text{278.15 K})} \\ & = \text{0.010305 mol} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 2: Write the balanced chemical equation.

MnO₂ + 4HCl → MnCl₂ + 2H₂O + Cl₂

Step 3: Calculate the mass of MnO₂ needed.

Based on the balanced chemical equation, 1 mol MnO₂ is stoichiometrically equivalent to 1 mol Cl₂.

The molar mass of MnO₂ is 86.937 g/mol.

[tex]\begin{aligned} \text{mass of} \: \text{MnO}_2 & = \text{0.010305 mol} \: \text{Cl}_2 \times \frac{\text{1 mol} \: \text{MnO}_2}{\text{1 mol} \: \text{Cl}_2} \times \frac{\text{86.937 g} \: \text{MnO}_2}{\text{1 mol} \: \text{MnO}_2} \\ & = \boxed{\text{0.896 g}} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, 0.896 g of MnO₂ should be added to excess HCl to obtain 275 mL of chlorine gas at 5.0°C and 650 mmHg.

27. To prepare a 1l of 1000 ppm solution of calcium chloride, how many grams of calcium chloride dihydrate should be weighed? solution.


The weight of calcium chloride is 147.02g

Explanation:

     The salt combining calcium and chlorine is called calcium chloride. Both anhydrous (CaCl2) and hydrated versions of calcium chloride are sold commercially (CaCl2 H2O, CaCl2 2H2O, CaCl2 4H2O, CaCl2 6H2O). To make a 1 M calcium chloride stock solution, any of them can be utilized. White powder that is solid at room temperature is anhydrous calcium chloride. It must be stored in containers that are tightly sealed and airtight because it is highly hygroscopic.

      When dissolved in water, calcium chloride that is anhydrous as well as the lower hydrates produce a lot of heat (exothermic reaction). When dissolving them in water, exercise caution. Due to its high hygroscopicity, anhydrous CaCl2 is challenging to weigh precisely (quickly absorbs water molecules from air). Dihydrate and tetrahydrate forms of calcium chloride are more stable in this aspect.

Prepare the following:

Solutions and reagentsCalcium chloride dihydrate (molecular weight: 147.02; CaCl2.2H2O)distilled waterCylinder for measuringBeaker or conical flaskStirrer with a magnetCalcium Chloride, 1 M

Procedure:

Use a magnetic stirrer to dissolve 147.02 g of CaCl2.2H2O (molecular weight: 147.02) in 800 ml of deionized or Milli-Q water to make 1000 ml of a 1 M calcium chloride (CaCl2) solution.With deionized water, adjust the volume to 1000 ml. Mix it once more.Fill an autoclavable bottle with the medium. The solution should be autoclaved for 20 minutes at 15 lb/sq.in. (psi) from 121–124°C on the liquid cycle to sterilize it.

The solution can be kept at ambient temperature or in a frigid environment.
