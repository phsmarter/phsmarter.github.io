18. In the passage “The Two Gifts,” one traveler gives money to a woman in need while another traveler gives no money but later brings her to his inn for dinner. Write an essay analyzing how the motives of the travelers relate to a theme in the passage. Use evidence from the passage to support your response. \


Answer:

In “The Two Gifts,” the story of two travelers, the first traveler’s generosity and the second traveler’s compassion serve to illustrate a theme of charity. Both travelers have a chance encounter with a poor woman in need and each of them respond in a different way. The first traveler gives the woman money, a tangible gift, while the second traveler provides her with a meal, an intangible gift.

The first traveler’s motivations are clear and straightforward; he seeks to help the woman by giving her money. He is aware that her need is great and his gift is immediate, but it is also limited. The traveler is “ashamed” of his own wealth while the woman is “poor and destitute” and he feels compelled to give her money to ease her suffering. His gift is an act of charity, a way of easing her burden and giving her a tangible benefit.

The second traveler’s motivations are more subtle. While he does not give the woman money directly, he does provide her with a meal and shows her kindness and compassion. He recognizes that the woman’s need is greater than his own, and he is moved by her plight

20. Write an essay about"Why Traveling Alone is Great Idea"with the proper use of opinion marking signal.Note:Write an ESSAY and with the use of OPINION MARKING SIGNAL, ASAP NA​


Answer:

Traveling alone can be a great idea for many reasons. First and foremost, it allows you to be in complete control of your itinerary and decisions. You can go where you want, when you want, and do what you want without having to consult anyone else. This can be especially liberating if you are someone who is used to always having to consider the preferences and needs of others.

Furthermore, traveling alone can be a great way to challenge yourself and build confidence. Navigating unfamiliar places, trying new activities, and meeting new people can all be intimidating, but also incredibly rewarding experiences. By doing these things on your own, you can push yourself out of your comfort zone and learn more about your own capabilities.

Additionally, traveling alone can be a great opportunity for self-reflection and personal growth. Without the distractions and obligations that come with traveling with others, you can have the time and space to truly immerse yourself in new experiences and think deeply about your life and goals.

In my opinion, one of the best things about traveling alone is the opportunity to meet new people. When you are by yourself, you are more likely to strike up conversations with locals and other travelers, which can lead to fascinating conversations and even lifelong friendships.

Of course, there are also potential drawbacks to traveling alone, such as feeling lonely or being more vulnerable to safety risks. However, these can be mitigated by careful planning and taking necessary precautions.

Overall, I believe that traveling alone can be a wonderful experience that offers many benefits. It allows you to be in control of your own adventure, challenge yourself, and grow as a person. It can also open up new social connections and provide a unique opportunity for self-discovery.

Light travels much faster than sound

Light travels at a speed of 299,792,458 m/s (that’s nearly 300,000 km/s!). The distance around the Earth is 40,000 km, so in 1 second, light could travel seven and a half times around the world.

Sound only travels at about 330 m/s through the air, so light is nearly a million times faster than sound.

If lightning flashes 1 kilometre away from you, the light reaches you in 3 millionths of a second, which is almost instantly. The sound of the thunder takes 3 seconds to travel 1 kilometre – to work out many kilometres away lightning is, count the seconds for the thunder to arrive and divide by 3.

Light takes about 8 minutes and 20 seconds to reach the Earth from the Sun. When we see the Sun, we are seeing what it looked like over 8 minutes ago.

26. Activity 1.3: My EssayDirections: Write a one-paragraph essay on how does light travel. Give somescientific evidences or proofs to support your answer. Use a separate sheet ofpaper for this activity.​


Answer:

Light travels in straight lines

Once light has been produced, it will keep travelling in a straight line until it hits something else.

Shadows are evidence of light travelling in straight lines. An object blocks light so that it can’t reach the surface where we see the shadow. Light fills up all of the space before it hits the object, but the whole region between the object and the surface is in shadow. Shadows don’t appear totally dark because there is still some light reaching the surface that has been reflected off other objects.

Once light has hit another surface or particles, it is then absorbed, reflected (bounces off), scattered (bounces off in all directions), refracted (direction and speed changes) or transmitted (passes straight through).
