13. what are the condition Lord capulet give for agreeing to paris marrying Juliet? ​


Answer:

In Shakespeare's play "Romeo and Juliet", Lord Capulet agrees to Paris marrying Juliet but he sets the following conditions:

Paris must wait two years before marrying Juliet.

Paris must win Juliet's heart and gain her consent before the marriage takes place.

Lord Capulet wants to keep the marriage a secret until Paris has won Juliet's heart.

The wedding must be a grand affair with a large guest list.

Lord Capulet insists on having the final say in all the wedding arrangements.

However, it's important to note that Juliet does not love Paris and is already secretly married to Romeo. The marriage to Paris is arranged by Lord Capulet without Juliet's consent, which ultimately leads to tragic consequences.

Answer:

Capulet speaks to Juliet about her marriage to Paris. He tells her that he has arranged for her to marry Paris, and that it is a good match for their family. Juliet is surprised and refuses, saying that she has not yet thought of marriage. Lord Capulet becomes angry and insists that she marry Paris, threatening to disown her if she does not comply. He tells her that she must marry Paris or never come back to his house again. Juliet, distressed and helpless, seeks help from the Nurse, who advises her to go along with the marriage to avoid further conflict with her father. This scene sets up the conflict between Juliet's desire for love and her duty to her family, leading to the tragic events that unfold later in the play.
