2. write your definition of research, Scope, Delimitation and beneficiary​


Answer:

Research-  The creation of new knowledge and/or the use of existing knowledge in a new and creative way so as to generate new concepts, methodologies and understandings. This could include synthesis and analysis of previous research to the extent that it leads to new and creative outcomes.

Scope- The scope of a study explains the extent to which the research area will be explored in the work and specifies the parameters within the study will be operating. Basically, this means that you will have to define what the study is going to cover and what it is focusing on.

Delimitation- Refer to the boundaries of the research study, based on the researcher's decision of what to include and what to exclude. They are characteristics of the research design or methodology that are out of your control but influence your research findings.

Beneficiary- Beneficiaries are those who are likely to be interested in or to benefit from the proposed research.  Researchers in other disciplines; Academic organisations; Companies, public sector bodies and others who may use the results to their advantage; or. Policy makers.

Answer:

Scope and Delimitation:

The School Enrollment System is a web-based platform designed to provide a convenient and efficient way for students to enroll in classes, manage their course schedules, and access their academic records. The scope of this study includes the development and implementation of the system, as well as the testing and evaluation of its usability and effectiveness.

However, the following aspects are outside the scope of this study:

- Financial transactions related to tuition and fees

- Student recruitment and admissions processes

- Academic advising and counseling services

- Campus facilities management and maintenance

Definition of Terms:

School Enrollment System: A web-based platform designed to automate and simplify the process of student enrollment, course scheduling, and academic record keeping.

Web-based platform: A software application that is accessible through a web browser, which allows users to interact with the system using a graphical user interface (GUI).

Course Schedule: A document that outlines the specific classes that a student is enrolled in for a given academic term, including the class name, section number, meeting time, and location.

Academic Records: A collection of documents and data that provide a comprehensive overview of a student's academic history, including transcripts, grades, and degree requirements.

Usability: The ease with which users can interact with the School Enrollment System, as measured by factors such as efficiency, effectiveness, and user satisfaction.

Effectiveness: The degree to which the School Enrollment System achieves its intended objectives, such as streamlining the enrollment process and reducing errors.

Tuition and Fees: The monetary costs associated with attending a school or university, including tuition, fees, and other expenses such as books and supplies.

Student Recruitment: The process of attracting and enrolling new students into a school or university.

Admissions Process: The set of procedures and requirements that must be fulfilled by students seeking admission to a school or university.

Academic Advising: The process of providing guidance and support to students to help them make informed decisions about their academic goals and career aspirations.

Counseling Services: Services provided by a school or university to help students address personal, social, or academic challenges that may be impacting their performance.

Campus Facilities Management and Maintenance: The processes and services involved in the upkeep and maintenance of a school or university's physical infrastructure, including buildings, grounds, and equipment.

Scope and Delimitation:

This study will focus on the effects of online learning on the academic performance of students during the COVID-19 pandemic. The study will be conducted among students in a selected school in the Philippines. The study will cover the period from the start of the COVID-19 pandemic in March 2020 until the end of the academic year in March 2021. The study will be limited to students from grades 7 to 12 who are enrolled in the selected school.

Significance of the Study:

The findings of this study will provide insights into the effects of online learning on students' academic performance during the COVID-19 pandemic. The study will help students, teachers, school administrators, parents, and the community understand the challenges and opportunities of online learning. The study will also provide recommendations for improving online learning and ensuring that students continue to receive quality education during the pandemic.

4. Students - refers to individuals who are enrolled in a school or educational institution.

5. Teachers - refers to individuals who are responsible for teaching and facilitating learning in a school or educational institution.

6. School administrators - refers to individuals who are responsible for managing and overseeing the operations of a school or educational institution.

7. Parents - refers to individuals who have legal responsibility for a student and are involved in their education.

8. Community - refers to a group of people living in a particular area or sharing common interests or characteristics.
