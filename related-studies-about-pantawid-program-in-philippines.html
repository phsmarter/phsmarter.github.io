7. pantawid pamilya program​


Ang Pantawid Pamilyang Pilipino Program (4Ps) ay isang hakbang ng pambansang pamahalaan para sa pagpapabuti ng kalagayang pantao ng ating mga kababayan. Nagbibigay ito ng kondisyonal na tulong-pinansiyal para sa pinakamahihirap na Pilipino upang pabutihin ang kalusugan, nutrisyon, at edukasyon ng mga batang may edad 0 hanggang 18 taong gulang. Kailangang matupad ng mga pamilya ang mga kondisyon ng pamahalaan upang maibigay sa kanila ang tulong-pinansiyal. Halaw ito sa programang Conditional Cash Transfer (CCT) ng mga bansa sa Latin Amerika at Aprika, na naialpas sa kahirapan ang milyon-milyong tao sa buong mundo.

Ang Department of Social Welfare and Development (DSWD) ang punong ahensiya ng pamahalaan na namamahala sa 4Ps.

14. give five primary objects of pantawid pamilyang pilipino program​


OBJECTIVES

The 4Ps has dual objectives as the flagship poverty alleviation program of the Aquino administration:

social assistance, giving monetary support to extremely poor families to respond to their immediate needs; and

social development, breaking the intergenerational poverty cycle by investing in the health and education of poor children through programs such as:

health check-ups for pregnant women and children aged 0 to 5;

deworming of schoolchildren aged 6 to 14;

enrollment of children in daycare, elementary, and secondary schools; and

family development sessions.

16. 10 government agencies that will help pantawid Ang what is the programs and services​


Answer:

10 government agencies that offer Pantawid or cash assistance programs and services:

1. Department of Social Welfare and Development (DSWD) - The DSWD offers the Pantawid Pamilyang Pilipino Program (4Ps), which provides conditional cash transfer to eligible families to help alleviate poverty.

2. Department of Labor and Employment (DOLE) - DOLE provides various programs such as the Tulong Panghanapbuhay sa Ating Disadvantaged/Displaced Workers (TUPAD) and COVID-19 Adjustment Measures Program (CAMP), which provide cash assistance to workers who have been displaced or affected by the pandemic.

3. Department of Agriculture (DA) - DA offers the Rice Farmers Financial Assistance (RFFA) and Cash Assistance for Rice Farmers (CARES) programs that provide financial assistance to Filipino farmers who have been affected by recent calamities and the current pandemic.

4. Department of Finance (DOF) - DOF offers the Small Business Wage Subsidy (SBWS) program that helps micro, small, and medium enterprises (MSMEs) affected by the pandemic to keep their employees and avoid layoffs.

5. Department of Transportation (DOTr) - DOTr provides assistance through the Service Contracting Program for Public Utility Vehicles (PUVs) which aims to help drivers and operators cope with the impact of the pandemic on their livelihood.

6. Department of Energy (DOE) - The DOE offers the lifeline rate subsidy program (LRSP) that provides electricity subsidies to the poorest households for their household consumption.

7. Technical Education and Skills Development Authority (TESDA) - TESDA provides programs such as the Tulong Panghanapbuhay sa Ating Disadvantaged/Displaced Workers – Training and Emergency Employment Program (TUPAD-TEEP) that aim to provide skills training and emergency employment opportunities to workers affected by the pandemic.

8. Philippine Health Insurance Corporation (PHIC) - PHIC offers the Point-of-Care Enrollment Program (POCEP) that provides immediate health insurance coverage to indigent families and other marginalized groups.

9. Department of Science and Technology (DOST) - DOST provides various programs such as the Small Enterprise Technology Upgrading Program (SETUP) that provides financial and technical assistance to MSMEs affected by the pandemic.

10. Department of Trade and Industry (DTI) - DTI provides programs such as the COVID-19 Assistance to Restart Enterprises (CARES) that provides zero-interest loans and grants to MSMEs affected by the pandemic to help them restart their businesses.

19. Does the pantawid pamilya program entail any effective redistribution of income?


Answer:

The conditional cash transfer (CCT) program locally known as Pantawid Pamilya Pilipino Program, or 4Ps, is a government program that provides conditional cash grants to the poorest of the poor in the Philippines. The program aims to break the cycle of poverty by keeping children aged 0-18 healthy and in school, so they can have a better future.
