11. write a reflective essay about,“Every high priest is taken from among men and made their representative before God, to offer gifts and sacrifice for sins”. Hebrews 5:1​


Answer:

The fourth chapter ended with a depiction of Jesus' relationship with humanity. He can understand human temptation because of His whole humanity. As the rest of this passage explains, He is capable of acting as a legitimate high priest. This job necessitates the priest's ability to interact with both God and men. This includes making sin offerings.

Earlier parts of Hebrews explained that Messiah had to be a human creature, not just an angel (Hebrews 2:17). The topic discussed between the end of Chapter 4 and the beginning of Chapter 5 is one explanation for this. Messiah had to be human in order to genuinely represent humanity as a priest. He wouldn't be able to sympathize with our failings (Hebrews 4:15), operate as a mediator between God and men (1 Timothy 2:5), or be someone we could approach in trust if He didn't have humanity (Hebrews 4:16).

They have provided us with food, shelter, and clothing, and have given us the opportunity to receive an education and pursue our dreams. They have also taught us important life lessons, such as the value of hard work, honesty, and respect for others.

In many religious traditions, parents are seen as a gift from God, as they are the ones who pass down their faith and values to their children. They are seen as the first teachers of the faith, and are responsible for instilling a love of God and a sense of morality in their children.

It is important to recognize the gift of parents, and to show them love, respect, and appreciation for all that they have done for us. We can do this by spending time with them, expressing gratitude for their hard work and sacrifices, and living our lives in a way that honors the values and lessons they have taught us.

In conclusion, parents are often seen as a gift of God, as they provide us with love, guidance, and support throughout our lives. They help shape our character and values, and are responsible for passing down important life lessons and faith traditions. We should recognize the gift of parents and show them love, respect, and appreciation for all that they have done for us.
