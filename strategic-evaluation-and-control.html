8. Compare and contrast the four job evaluation methods. Give an example of an organization in which each of the four methods might provide an optimal strategic fit.


Answer:

The four primary methods of job evaluation are point factor, factor comparison, job ranking, and job classification. The point factor method involves breaking down jobs into various factors, such as skill, effort, and responsibility, and assigning points to each factor based on its importance to the job. The factor comparison method involves comparing jobs based on a set of predetermined factors and assigning a relative value to each job. The job ranking method involves ranking jobs based on their importance to the organization, while the job classification method involves grouping jobs into predetermined classes based on their level of responsibility and skill.

An organization that values a highly structured and objective approach to job evaluation may find the point factor method to be the best fit. For example, a manufacturing company that has a large number of employees with similar job responsibilities may use the point factor method to ensure that compensation is based on objective criteria.

An organization that values a more subjective approach to job evaluation may find the factor comparison method to be the best fit. For example, a creative agency that has a small number of employees with unique job responsibilities may use the factor comparison method to ensure that compensation is based on subjective criteria.

An organization that values a simple and straightforward approach to job evaluation may find the job ranking method to be the best fit. For example, a small retail store that has a limited number of employees may use the job ranking method to ensure that compensation is based on the relative importance of each job.

An organization that values a more flexible approach to job evaluation may find the job classification method to be the best fit. For example, a consulting firm that has a diverse range of employees with varying levels of responsibility and skill may use the job classification method to ensure that compensation is based on the level of responsibility and skill required for each job.
