Let's assume that the total number of children is represented by the variable "x".

From the given information, we know that:

1/3 of the children are from Makati City, which means there are (1/3)x children from Makati.

0.5 of the children are from Quezon City, which means there are (0.5)x children from Quezon.

The rest of the children are from Manila City, which means there are 30 children from Manila.

To find the value of "x", we can set up an equation:

(1/3)x + (0.5)x + 30 = x

Simplifying the equation, we get:

(0.83)x = 30

Dividing both sides by 0.83, we get:

x = 36.14 (rounded to two decimal places)

Therefore, there are approximately 36 children altogether in the botanical garden.

16. what is the importance of having a botanical garden in our community​


What is the Importance of having a botanical garden in our community?

Having a botanical garden in a community can bring numerous benefits, including:

1. Education and Conservation: Botanical gardens serve as living museums that showcase a wide variety of plant species, providing an educational resource for learning about plant diversity, ecosystems, and conservation. They often have educational programs, exhibits, and guided tours that can help raise awareness about the importance of plants in our lives and the need to conserve them for future generations.

2. Recreation and Relaxation: Botanical gardens are often serene and peaceful places where people can escape the hustle and bustle of urban life and connect with nature. They provide opportunities for recreation and relaxation, such as walking trails, picnic areas, and gardens designed for meditation or contemplation. Botanical gardens can also be great places for community events, festivals, and gatherings that bring people together and foster a sense of community.

3. Research and Conservation Efforts: Botanical gardens often engage in research and conservation efforts to protect endangered plant species, conduct plant-related scientific studies, and conserve plant genetic diversity. They may also collaborate with other organizations and institutions to conduct research on plant conservation, plant breeding, and sustainable gardening practices, which can contribute to the advancement of scientific knowledge and the development of environmentally friendly practices.

4. Beautification and Environmental Sustainability: Botanical gardens are known for their stunning displays of plants and landscapes, which can enhance the aesthetic appeal of a community and contribute to its overall beautification. Botanical gardens often prioritize sustainability and use eco-friendly practices in their gardening and landscaping techniques, promoting environmentally responsible practices such as composting, water conservation, and organic gardening, which can serve as a model for the community.

5. Economic Benefits: Botanical gardens can contribute to the local economy by attracting visitors, tourists, and researchers, which can boost local businesses such as hotels, restaurants, and gift shops. They can also provide employment opportunities, both in the garden itself and in related fields such as horticulture, landscaping, education, and research.

6. Health and Well-being: Studies have shown that spending time in nature, including botanical gardens, can have numerous health benefits. Being surrounded by green spaces and engaging with nature has been linked to reduced stress, improved mental health, increased physical activity, and enhanced overall well-being. Botanical gardens can provide a place for people to connect with nature and promote healthy lifestyles.

In summary, having a botanical garden in a community can provide educational, recreational, research, economic, and health benefits. They contribute to the conservation of plant species, provide a place for people to connect with nature, and promote sustainable practices. Botanical gardens can enhance the overall well-being and quality of life of a community and serve as valuable resources for education, research, and community engagement.

28. how do zoological gardens ensure that plants that came from other countries do not become "invasive" species?


Answer:

Zoological gardens can take several measures to ensure that plants that come from other countries do not become invasive species:

Conducting risk assessments: Before introducing any new plant species, zoological gardens should conduct a risk assessment to determine the likelihood of the plant becoming invasive. This involves researching the plant's native range, its biology, and its potential impact on local ecosystems.

Quarantining plants: Zoological gardens can quarantine plants for a period to ensure that they are free from pests and diseases. During this time, staff can monitor the plants to ensure they are not displaying any invasive tendencies

30. There were 955 tourist visiting the national zoo last week. It was 5 times of the number of tourist visiting the botanic garden. What was the difference between the number of tourist visiting the national zoo and the botanic garden?


Answer:

764 tourists

Step-by-step explanation:

Let's find out the number of tourists that visited the botanic garden first. It is said that five times of that is the number of tourists that visited the national zoo which is 955 tourists.

So, the tourist that visited the botanical garden is a fifth of 955 tourists that went to the national zoo.

955 ÷ 5 = 191

Now, we know that 191 tourists visited the botanical garden. Let's get the difference between the number of tourist visiting the national zoo and the botanic garden.

955 - 191 = 764

Therefore, the difference between the number of tourist visiting the national zoo and the botanic garden is 764 tourists.
