Structuralism and formalism are two literary theories or literary criticisms that focus on the structure of a particular text. ... However, structuralism connects the work of a particular author with works of similar structures whereas formalism only analyses one particular work at a time.

16. Give the meaning of the 7 Approaches to Literary Criticism 1. Structuralist 2. Formalist 3. Moralist 4. Marxist 5. Feminist 6. Historical 7. Reader-Response


Answer:

1. Structuralist - is a method of interpreting and analysing such things as language, literature, and society, which focuses on contrasting ideas or elements of structure and attempts to show how they relate to the whole structure.

2. Formalist - 1. Rigorous or excessive adherence to recognized forms, as in religion or art.

                       2. An instance of rigorous or excessive adherence to recognized forms.

3. Moralist - 1. one who leads a moral life

                    2. a philosopher or writer concerned with moral principles and                                     problems

                    3. one concerned with regulating the morals of others

4. Marxist - is someone who strongly agrees with the political, economic, and philosophical ideas of Karl Marx and Friedrich Engels

5. Feminist - is someone who supports equal rights for women

6. Historical - is something that provides evidence to the facts of history or is based on people and events of the past

7. Reader-Response - is a school of literary theory that focuses on the reader (or "audience") and their experience of a literary work, in contrast to other schools and theories that focus attention primarily on the author or the content and form of the work.

22. What are the example of structuralist literary approach?​


pa brainliest po

Structuralism is a literary approach that seeks to understand the underlying structures and systems of a text, rather than focusing on its content or individual elements. Here are some examples of structuralist literary analysis:

Binary Opposition: This is a key concept in structuralism, which holds that all meaning is created through the opposition between two opposing concepts. For example, good vs. evil, male vs. female, or nature vs. civilization.

Narratology: This is the study of narrative structures and how they function within a text. Structuralist narratology seeks to understand the basic building blocks of narrative, such as plot, character, and setting, and how they work together to create meaning.

Semiotics: This is the study of signs and symbols, and how they are used to convey meaning in a text. Structuralist semiotics focuses on the underlying systems of signification within a text, such as language, imagery, and symbolism.

Intertextuality: This is the idea that all texts are interconnected, and that meaning is created through the relationships between different texts. Structuralist approaches to intertextuality seek to understand how a text is shaped by its relationships to other texts, and how it in turn shapes the meaning of those texts.

Formalism: This is an approach to literary analysis that focuses on the formal aspects of a text, such as its structure, language, and style. Structuralist formalism seeks to understand how these elements work together to create meaning, and how they are shaped by the underlying structures of the text.
