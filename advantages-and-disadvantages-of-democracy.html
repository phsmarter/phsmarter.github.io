1. Advantages and disadvantages of democracy


Answer:

Advantages of democracy:

⏩Democracy improves the quality of Decision Making.

⏩Democracy provides a method to deal with differences and conflicts.

⏩Democracy allows people to correct their own mistakes.

⏩In a democracy, people rule themselves as leaders are elected by them.

Disadvantages of democracy:

⏩Democracy is all about political competition and power play. ...

⏩Consultation in a democracy from many people leads to delays.

⏩Not knowing the best interest of the people by the elected leaders leads to bad decisions.

⏩Democracy leads to corruption for it is based on electoral competition.

Advantages of Democracy :

The government is constrained by an electoral mandate, following which it must contend with other parties for power once again. The monopoly of the ruling power is avoided by this structure. The ruling party must ensure that it serves the interests of the people because, without popular reelection, it cannot continue to hold power after its mandate has ended.Governmental reforms are possible under a democratic system without using force. Elections are the means through which power is transferred from one party to another in a democracy. The government of a country is decided by the votes of its people. The support of the populace powers the government. Therefore, democracy enables a reliable system of government.Democracy encourages equality and the defense of individual rights. Everyone is treated equally by the law because it has a robust legal system. Every citizen has the right to cast a ballot. They also have the same other social and political rights, which are not determined by their caste, creed, or any other characteristic. Contrary to monarchies, when the ruler may favor a particular group or community, there is very little potential of discrimination based on these criteria.

Disadvantages of Democracy :

Making and carrying out choices takes longer in a democracy. In a monarchy, only one person has the authority to make decisions and ensure that they are carried out promptly. Democracies are less quick to act than other forms of governance since majority voting is required for execution.The right to choose one's representatives and government is one that belongs to the people in a democracy. A general observation is that not all citizens are properly informed of their nation's political environment or its problems. People might elect the wrong candidate as a result of this.Representatives may not always be chosen based on qualifications. In a voting system, the majority decides, and votes cast by literate and illiterate people are counted equally. People might favor someone based on things other just talent. As a result, the person chosen by the general public may not necessarily be the best choice for the job.

Answer:

Explanation:

Forms of Democracy:

1. Representative democracy - a system of government where citizens elect representatives to make decisions on their behalf.

2. Direct Democracy - a system of government where citizens directly participate in decision-making.

Types of Democracy:

3. Classical Democracy - a type of democracy that originated in ancient Greece, where all citizens have an equal say in government decisions.

4. Liberal Democracy - a form of democracy that emphasizes individual freedoms, human rights, and the rule of law.

5. Social Democracy - a form of democracy that aims to create a balance between individual freedoms and social welfare through government intervention.

6. Radical Democracy - a form of democracy that seeks to challenge and change the existing political and social structures.

Elements of Democracy:

7. Competition for Power - a system of government where political power is contested through free and fair elections.

8. Participation in Civic and Political Life - a system of government where citizens have the right to participate in decision-making and express their views freely.

9. Respect for Individual Rights - a system of government that protects individual rights such as freedom of speech, religion, and assembly.

10. Rule of Law - a system of government where laws are applied equally to all citizens, and nobody is above the law.

26. what are the disadvantages of democracy​


1. Democracy is ineffective unless voters educate themselves on governing decisions.

A democracy allows an individual to cast a vote either directly or through a preferred representative on the issues that the government must manage. There is no direction as to how voters approach this responsibility. Although some people will educate themselves on each issue to offer an experienced opinion, there is no requirement to go through all of this work. Someone can turn in a ballot that is a straight-party ticket with no consideration about the individual views or needs.

President Teddy Roosevelt reportedly once said this: “A vote is like a rifle; its usefulness depends upon the character of the user.”

2. The structure of democracy depends upon the will of the majority.

History has taught us that the will of the majority is not always the ethical or moral position that one should take. We have dealt with issues like slavery, discrimination, and gender inequality in the past because the perspective of the those with the most votes say that society deserves to have those elements. If someone finds themselves outside of the will of the majority more often than not, then it will feel like their vote doesn’t really count for something.

“Majority rule only works if you’re also considering individual rights,” said Larry Flynt. “Because you can’t have five wolves and one sheep voting on what they should all be having for supper.”

3. Democracy can encourage mob rule.

People are migrating toward neighborhoods, employment opportunities, and even relationships based on how comfortable they are around other people. The prevalence is to have neighbors and friends who have a like-minded perspective because there is a fear present in democracy of being wrong. No one wants to be stuck on the outside looking in when it comes to governing. Some states in the U.S. are even becoming polarized as families keep moving to stay within their comfort zone. Democracies encourage mob thinking because every election becomes an “us vs. them” edict.
