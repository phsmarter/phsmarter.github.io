19. Read about ESSAY. Give the following: 1. Meaning 2. Structure 3. Characteristics of a Good Essay 4. Types of Essay Write your findings in your English notebook.​


Answer:

Meaning:

An essay is a piece of writing that presents an author's perspective or argument on a particular topic. It is a form of literary composition that aims to inform, persuade, or entertain the reader. An essay can be formal or informal and can vary in length and complexity, depending on the intended audience and purpose.

Structure:

A typical essay has three main parts: an introduction, a body, and a conclusion.

The introduction should grab the reader's attention and provide a thesis statement or main argument for the essay. The body should present evidence, examples, and analysis that support the thesis statement. The conclusion should summarize the main points of the essay and provide a final thought or call to action.

Characteristics of a Good Essay:

A good essay should have the following characteristics:

Clarity: The essay should be clear and concise, with a well-defined thesis statement and supporting arguments that are easy to follow.

Coherence: The essay should have a logical structure, with each paragraph building on the previous one and leading to a clear conclusion.

Relevance: The essay should be relevant to the topic and should address the main issues or questions related to the topic.

Evidence-based: The essay should be based on evidence, examples, and analysis that support the author's argument.

Originality: The essay should present a unique perspective or argument that adds to the existing body of knowledge on the topic.

Correctness: The essay should be free of grammatical, spelling, and punctuation errors, and should adhere to the conventions of academic writing.

Types of Essay:

There are several types of essays, including:

Descriptive Essay: A descriptive essay describes a person, place, or thing in detail.

Narrative Essay: A narrative essay tells a story or recounts an event.

Expository Essay: An expository essay explains a topic or idea in detail.

Persuasive Essay: A persuasive essay aims to persuade the reader to accept the author's point of view.

Argumentative Essay: An argumentative essay presents an argument and supports it with evidence and analysis.

Compare and Contrast Essay: A compare and contrast essay compares and contrasts two or more things.

Cause and Effect Essay: A cause and effect essay explains the causes and effects of a particular event or phenomenon.

Process Essay: A process essay explains how to do something or how something works.

20. Write a short essay about what is the best reading style or technique for reading great books and the best that suits you.


Answer:

Reading is a personal and subjective activity, and there is no one-size-fits-all approach to reading great books. However, there are certain reading styles and techniques that can enhance your reading experience and help you get the most out of the books you read.

One of the most effective reading techniques is active reading. This involves engaging with the text actively by asking questions, making connections, and drawing conclusions. Active reading encourages critical thinking and helps you retain information more effectively. To read actively, it is important to take notes, underline or highlight important passages, and ask yourself questions about the text as you read.

Another effective reading technique is slow reading. Slow reading involves taking your time with the text and reading it carefully and attentively. This technique is particularly useful for complex or dense texts that require a deeper understanding. Slow reading allows you to absorb the material more thoroughly and to notice details and nuances that might be missed in a faster read.

Furthermore, there is no one best reading style, and the best technique is the one that suits you the most. For example, some people prefer to read in silence, while others like to read with background noise. Some prefer to read in short bursts, while others like to read for long stretches at a time. Experiment with different styles and techniques to find what works best for you.

Personally, I find a combination of active reading and slow reading to be the most effective for me. I like to take notes, underline passages, and ask questions as I read, while also taking my time with the text to ensure I fully understand it. I also like to read in silence to minimize distractions and allow myself to focus on the material.

In conclusion, the best reading style or technique for reading great books varies from person to person. However, active reading and slow reading are two effective techniques that can enhance your reading experience and help you get the most out of the books you read. Experiment with different styles and techniques to find what works best for you and stick with it.

HISTORY OF NATIONAL READING MONTH

March was designated as National Reading Month to celebrate Dr. Suess. He was born on March 2, 1904. Interestingly enough, Dr. Suess was not a doctor at all, he was a writer and an illustrator of very popular children’s books including “The Cat in the Hat” and “How the Grinch Stole Christmas”. He also wrote several books for beginner readers of which “One Fish Two Fish Red Fish Blue Fish” (published in 1960) was the most notable one. He received a Pulitzer Prize in 1984 for his contribution to the education of America’s children and their parents.

29. Write an essay about the theme "fostering culture if reading in times of pandemic" i really need this ​


Answer:

Young children readingWith the start of the academic year well under way in the United States, schools and classrooms look very different from how they looked and operated before COVID-19. Rather than wait at school bus stops, many students begin their day by logging into their virtual classrooms. The students who are in schools—socially distanced, masked, and seated behind plexiglass shields—no longer peruse the shelves of their school and classroom libraries. In many cases, classroom libraries have been temporarily removed, and public libraries are either shuttered or available by appointment only. For nearly all of our students in today’s COVID-19 pandemic, book access remains severely restricted or largely digital. (Ed: For more information, watch ILA’s free digital event Book Access in the Post-COVID Era featuring Molly Ness, Susan B. Neuman, Allister Chang, and Karlos Marshall.)

Though today’s challenges are new and unique, book access has been an ongoing issue for too many children for too long. The central tenet in the ILA’s Children’s Rights to Read declares both that children have the basic human right to read and that children have the right to access texts in print and digital formats. Prior to the COVID-19 pandemic, these rights were jeopardized by the presence of book deserts, defined in ILA’s Literacy Glossary as “underresourced or underserved areas and homes with little access to written materials.”
