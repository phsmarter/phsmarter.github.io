9. Instruction: Identify if the underlined word is an adjective or an adverb. 1. Everyone must come early and meet in front of Luneta Grandstand. 2. The Earth Run will take place in the early morning of January 18. 3. The fast runners will be given simple tokens of recognition. 4. Some exercise, perhaps, can make old folks run fast. 5. The organizers have surely worked hard to prepare everything for this event.


Answer:

1. Everyone must come EARLY and meet in front of Luneta Grandstand.

ADVERB (modifies the verb come)

2. The Earth Run will take place in the EARLY morning of January 18.

ADJECTIVE (modifies the noun morning)

3. The FAST runners will be given simple tokens of recognition.

ADJECTIVE (modifies the noun runners)

4. Some exercise, perhaps, can make old folks run FAST.

ADVERB (modifies the verb run)

5. The organizers have surely worked HARD to prepare everything for this event.

ADVERB (modifies the verb worked)

Explanation:

An adjective is a part of speech used to describe or modify a noun or a pronoun.

An adverb s a part of speech used to describe a verb, an adjective or another adverb.
