2. 1. How will you differentiate news writing from feature writing? from opinion editorial essays? from sports article?2. What kind of information each text type provides?3. What is the purpose of the writer in each text type?​


Answer:

1. News writing focuses on reporting "hard news" stories that provide objective information about events, people, and issues. Feature writing, on the other hand, offers an in-depth analysis of a particular topic, often with a human interest angle. Opinion editorial essays represent the writer's personal view on a subject, and their goal is to persuade readers to agree with them. Sports articles report on recent games or events in the world of sports. They include scores, statistics, and commentary on the game.

2. News writing provides the most recent and important information, including the five W's (who, what, where, when, and why/how), as well as quotes from eyewitnesses and officials. Feature writing often explores a subject in depth, includes human-interest angles and takes a more creative approach to storytelling.

3. The writer of news articles aims to provide a neutral, factual account of a recent event without commentary or opinion. The goal of feature writing is to broaden readers' understanding of a particular topic with a unique angle or perspective.
