2. similarities of charles darwin and ferdinand de saussure


Answer:

Charles Darwin and Ferdinand de Saussure were both influential figures in their respective fields of study. Darwin was a naturalist and scientist who is best known for his theory of evolution, while de Saussure was a linguist who is considered one of the founders of modern linguistics.

One of the most notable similarities between the two is that both men were interested in understanding the underlying structures that govern complex systems. In Darwin's case, this meant studying the natural world and the way that living things evolve over time. For de Saussure, it meant studying the structure of language and how it functions as a system of communication.

Both men also made significant contributions to their fields through their work. Darwin's theory of evolution has had a profound impact on the way we understand the natural world and has influenced many different areas of science. De Saussure's work on linguistics laid the foundation for many of the concepts and theories that are still used in the field today.

In addition, both Darwin and de Saussure were able to generate new insights and ideas through their careful observations and analysis of the systems they studied. Their work has helped to shape our understanding of the world and has had a lasting impact on the way we think about and study complex systems.

5. 2. Sino ang gumawa ng Suez Canal? a. Prince Phillip c. Ferdinand de Lesseps b. Ferdinand Marcos d. Ferdinand Magellan​


Answer:

c

Explanation:

In 1858, Ferdinand de Lesseps formed the Suez Canal Company for the express purpose of building the canal. Construction of the canal lasted from 1859 to 1869. The canal officially opened on 17 November 1869. It offers vessels a direct route between the North Atlantic and northern Indian oceans via the Mediterranean Sea and the Red Sea, avoiding the South Atlantic and southern Indian oceans and reducing the journey distance from the Arabian Sea to London by approximately 8,900 kilometres (5,500 mi), or 10 days at 20 knots (37 km/h; 23 mph) to 8 days at 24 knots (44 km/h; 28 mph).[1] The canal extends from the northern terminus of Port Said to the southern terminus of Port Tewfik at the city of Suez. Its length is 193.30 km (120.11 mi) including its northern and southern access-channels. In 2020, more than 18,500 vessels traversed the canal (an average of 51.5 per day).[2]

The original canal featured a single-lane waterway with passing locations in the Ballah Bypass and the Great Bitter Lake.[3] It contained, according to Alois Negrelli's plans, no lock systems, with seawater flowing freely through it. In general, the water in the canal north of the Bitter Lakes flows north in winter and south in summer. South of the lakes, the current changes with the tide at Suez.[4]

While the canal was the property of the Egyptian government, European shareholders, mostly British and French, owned the concessionary company which operated it until July 1956, when President Gamal Abdel Nasser nationalized it—an event which led to the Suez Crisis of October–November 1956.[5] The canal is operated and maintained by the state-owned Suez Canal Authority[6] (SCA) of Egypt. Under the Convention of Constantinople, it may be used "in time of war as in time of peace, by every vessel of commerce or of war, without distinction of flag."[7] Nevertheless, the canal has played an important military strategic role as a naval short-cut and choke point. Navies with coastlines and bases on both the Mediterranean Sea and the Red Sea (Egypt and Israel) have a particular interest in the Suez Canal. After Egypt closed the Suez canal at the beginning of the Six-Day War on 5 June 1967, the canal remained closed for precisely eight years, reopening on 5 June 1975.[8]

The Egyptian government launched construction in 2014 to expand and widen the Ballah Bypass for 35 km (22 mi) to speed up the canal's transit-time. The expansion intended to nearly double the capacity of the Suez Canal, from 49 to 97 ships per day.[9] At a cost of 59.4 billion Egyptian pounds (US$9bn), this project was funded with interest-bearing investment certificates issued exclusively to Egyptian entities and individuals. The "New Suez Canal", as the expansion was dubbed, was opened in a ceremony on 6 August 2015.[10]

The Suez Canal Authority officially opened the new side channel in 2016. This side channel, located at the northern side of the east extension of the Suez Canal, serves the East Terminal for berthing and unberthing vessels from the terminal. As the East Container Terminal is located on the Canal itself, before the construction of the new side channel it was not possible to berth or unberth vessels at the terminal while a convoy was running.[11]

9. A short summary of "The First Voyage around the World of Ferdinand Magellan" by Antonio Pigafetta.​


Answer:

Antonio Pigafetta's account of "The First Voyage around the World of Ferdinand Magellan" is a journal of the famous Portuguese explorer's expedition to the Spice Islands. Pigafetta was one of the few crew members to survive the voyage and his journal provides a detailed account of the journey, including the crew's interactions with the native peoples they encountered.

The expedition left Spain in September 1519 and after a long journey, they arrived in Brazil in December. From there, they sailed down the coast of South America, discovering the Strait of Magellan and passing through it to reach the Pacific Ocean. They encountered various islands in the Pacific and made landfall in the Philippines, where Magellan was killed in a battle with local tribes.

Pigafetta describes the various cultures and customs of the people they met on their journey, including the native peoples of South America, the Pacific Islands, and the Philippines. He also documents the challenges the crew faced, including disease, hunger, and mutiny.

Despite the hardships, the expedition was a significant achievement in the history of exploration, as it was the first circumnavigation of the globe. Pigafetta's account provides a valuable historical record of the voyage and the cultures encountered along the way.

13. martial law of ferdinand marcos summary please


Answer:

Martial law in the Philippines (Filipino: Batas Militar sa Pilipinas) refers to the various historical instances in which the Philippine head of state placed all or part of the country under military control most prominently during the administration of Ferdinand Marcos,but also during the Philippines' colonial period, during the second world war, and more recently on the island of Mindanao during the administrations of Gloria Macapagal Arroyo and Rodrigo Duterte. The alternative term "Martial Law Era" as applied to the Philippines is typically used to describe the Marcos martial law period specifically.

Ang Pranses na diplomat na namamahala sa pagtatayo ng Suez Canal (1805-1894)

Pangkalahatang-ideya

Ferdinand Marie, Vicomte de Lesseps, GCSI (Pranses: [də lesɛps]; Nobyembre 19, 1805 - Disyembre 7, 1894) ay isang Pranses diplomat at mamaya nag-develop ng Suez Canal, na kung saan sa 1869 ay sumali sa Mediterranean at Red Seas, malaki pagbabawas ng sailing mga distansya at mga oras sa pagitan ng Europa at Silangang Asya.

Sinubukan niyang ulitin ang tagumpay na ito sa pagsisikap na bumuo ng isang Panama Canal sa antas ng dagat sa panahon ng 1880s, ngunit ang proyekto ay nasira ng mga epidemya ng malarya at dilaw na lagnat sa lugar, pati na rin sa mga suliranin sa pananalapi, at ang pinlanong de Lesseps Ang Panama Canal ay hindi pa nakumpleto. Sa kalaunan, ang proyekto ay binili ng Estados Unidos, na lutasin ang mga problema sa medisina at binago ang disenyo sa isang kanal sa antas ng hindi dagat na may mga kandado. Ito ay natapos noong 1914.

Isang Pranses diplomat, isang mahusay na kontribyutor sa pagtatayo ng Suez Canal . Consulates Lisbon, consular Cairo, ambasador ng Madrid. Noong 1832, pinasigla ang pagtatayo ng Suez Canal, na may pag-apruba ng Egyptian Taroku Mehmet Said noong 1854, itinayo ito noong 1859 at natapos noong 1869. Noong 1881, nagsimula kaming maghukay sa Panama Canal , ngunit naging sanhi ito ng pagbagsak sa pananalapi at ito ay isang hindi pagkakaunawaan ng kaso sa panloloko ng Panama, at ang kanyang mga huling taon ay malungkot. Bilang karagdagan, ang tiyuhin ng JBB Lesseps ay sumali sa paglalayag sa eksplorasyon ng La Perouse , at naglabas ng isang ulat tungkol sa paggalugad ng hilagang Japan.

Lesson 4 explores the works of two French Romantic painters, Ferdinand Eugene Delacroix and Jean Louis Andre Theodore Gericault. Gericault, on the other hand, was known for his dramatic and emotional works that often featured horses and other animals. His most famous work is "The Raft of the Medusa."
