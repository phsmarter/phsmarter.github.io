2. short description about clouds​


Answer:

A cloud is a hydrometeor consisting of minute particles of liquid water or ice, or of both, suspended in the atmosphere and usually not touching the ground

Answer:

Clouds are visible masses of water droplets or ice crystals that float in the Earth's atmosphere. They form through the process of condensation or freezing of water vapor in the air, and their shapes and sizes vary depending on the atmospheric conditions. Clouds play an important role in regulating the Earth's climate by reflecting sunlight and trapping heat, and they also have cultural significance in various societies and artistic representations.

24. Write a short essay explaining how “I Wandered Lonely as a Cloud” illustrates Wordsworth’s process of poetic composition.​


Answer:

I wandered lonely as a cloud

That floats on high o’er vales and hills,

When all at once I saw a crowd,

A host, of golden daffodils;

Beside the lake, beneath the trees,

Fluttering and dancing in the breeze.

Continuous as the stars that shine

And twinkle on the milky way,

They stretched in never-ending line

Along the margin of a bay:

Ten thousand saw I at a glance,

Tossing their heads in sprightly dance.

The waves beside them danced; but they

Out-did the sparkling waves in glee:

A poet could not but be gay,

In such a jocund company:

I gazed—and gazed—but little thought

What wealth the show to me had brought:

For oft, when on my couch I lie

In vacant or in pensive mood,

They flash upon that inward eye

Which is the bliss of solitude;

And then my heart with pleasure fills,

And dances with the daffodils.

29. write a story about water droplet taking the following journey lake - clouds - ocean - cloud - river - ocean - cloud - glacier relate the concept of condensation, freezing, and deposition in your story. ​


Answer:

Once upon a time, in a serene lake nestled amidst towering mountains, there was a tiny water droplet named Dew. Dew was born in the lake and loved the peacefulness of her surroundings. But one day, she felt a strong pull upward, and before she knew it, she was soaring high above the lake.

Dew soon found herself in a vast expanse of white fluffy clouds. She had never seen anything like it before, and it was a sight to behold. Up there in the clouds, Dew met many other water droplets like her, all of whom seemed to be having a great time playing and floating around.

But soon, Dew noticed something strange. The air around her was getting colder, and her fellow droplets started freezing into ice crystals. Dew herself started to freeze, and her body transformed into a beautiful snowflake. She drifted through the clouds, amazed by the beauty of her surroundings.

As time passed, Dew's snowflake form became heavier and heavier, and she began to fall back towards the earth. She landed in the ocean with a splash and was quickly absorbed into the water. But Dew's journey was far from over.

Once again, she was pulled upwards, and soon she found herself back in the clouds. The air was warmer this time, and Dew's snowflake form melted back into a water droplet. She stayed in the clouds for a while longer, enjoying the company of her fellow droplets.

But then, something incredible happened. Dew noticed that the air around her was changing, and her fellow droplets started turning into ice crystals once again. This time, however, they were not falling back to earth. Instead, they were sticking to the surface of a glacier, slowly building it up over time.

Dew couldn't believe what she was seeing. She had witnessed the process of deposition firsthand, and it was amazing. She stayed on the glacier for a while, but eventually, she was pulled back towards the ocean, starting her journey all over again.

As Dew traveled from the lake to the clouds, the ocean, the river, and back to the glacier, she learned many things about the water cycle. She learned about condensation, freezing, and deposition, and she marveled at the incredible power of nature.

In the end, Dew realized that her journey was not just a physical one, but a spiritual one too. She had grown and changed along the way, becoming wiser and more appreciative of the beauty of the world around her. And as she continued on her journey, she looked forward to all the amazing experiences that lay ahead
