4. What is the product of sodium hydroxide + hydrochloric acid --> sodium chloride + water?First to answer mark brainliest


Answer:

The products in the reaction are sodium chloride (NaCl) and water (H₂O).

Explanation:

The format for a chemical reaction is always:

reactant ⇒ products (the arrow is read as "yields")

Reactants are the initial form of the reaction. They are the "parents", to simplify. The reactants in this reaction are sodium hydroxide (NaOH) and hydrochloric acid (HCl).

The products, on the other hand, are the final form of the reactions after the reactants are mixed. These are the "children". The products of this reaction are sodium chloride and water.

The chemist's way to write this reaction is:

[tex]NaOH +HCl[/tex] ⇒ [tex]NaCl + H_2O[/tex]


5. When an acetic acid solution is titrated with sodium hydroxide, the slope of the titration curve (Ph vs volume of NaOH added) increases when sodium hydroxide is first added. This change shows that a. the pH is not affected until all the acetic acid is consumed. b. the reaction is very slow during this part of the titration c. acetic acid is being converted to sodium acetate. d. a more concentrated solution of NaOH needs to be present to initiate the reaction.


c. acetic acid is being converted to sodium acetate.

When an acetic acid solution is titrated with sodium hydroxide, the slope of the titration curve increases when sodium hydroxide is first added because this is when acetic acid is being converted to sodium acetate. This conversion causes the pH of the solution to change, resulting in an increase in the slope of the titration curve. Options a and b are not correct because the pH of the solution is affected as soon as sodium hydroxide is added, and the reaction is not necessarily slow during this part of the titration. Option d is not correct because the concentration of the sodium hydroxide solution does not affect the slope of the titration curve in this way.

10. 1.Titration reveals that 11.6 mL of 3.0 M sulfuric acid are required to neutralize the sodium hydroxide in 25.00 mL of NaOH solution. What is the molarity of the NaOH solution?(w/ solution)​


The molarity of the NaOH solution : Mb = 2.784 M

Further explanation  

Titration is a procedure for determining the concentration of a solution (analyte) by reacting with another solution whose known concentration (usually a standard solution) is called the titrant. Determination of the endpoint /equivalence point of the reaction can use indicators according to the appropriate pH range  

Titrations can be acid-base titration, depositional titration, and redox titration. An acid-base titration is the principle of neutralization of acids and bases  

Acid-base titration formula  

Ma Va. na = Mb. Vb. nb  

Ma, Mb = acid base concentration  

Va, Vb = acid base volume  

na, nb = acid base valence  

Given

Va = acid = 11.6 ml

Ma = 3 M

Vb = base = 25 ml

Required

Mb = the concentration of NaOH solution  

Solution

na = 2 (valence 2 because it releases 2 H⁺) ⇒H₂SO₄⇒2H⁺+SO₄²⁻

nb = 1 (valence 1 because it releases 1 OH⁻) ⇒NaOH⇒Na⁺+OH⁻

Input the value :

3 M x 11.6 ml x 2 = Mb x 25 ml x 1

Mb = 2.784 M

18. For 2.915 of khp, how many milliliters of 0.4 m sodium hydroxide is needed to reach the endpoint of the titration

Explanation:

First you need to write out the balanced equation to determine the mole ratio between C8H5KO4(aq) and NaOH(aq)

The chemical formula for KHP is C8H5KO4

NaOH + C8H5KO4-->NaC8H4KO4 + H2O

Because the ratio between C8H5KO4 and NaOH is one to one you will need the same number of moles of NaOH as KHP to reach the equivalence point. So the steps are grams KHP to moles KHP to mmol KHP to mmol NaOH to ml NaOH as your final answer:

20. 1.Titration reveals that 11.6 mL of 3.0 M sulfuric acid are required to neutralize the sodium hydroxide in 25.00 mL of NaOH solution. What is the molarity of the NaOH solution?​


Note: The acid is sulfuric acid and the base is sodium hydroxide. Sulfuric acid has two replaceable hydrogen ions and sodium hydroxide has one replaceable hydroxide ion.

Given:

[tex]M_{\text{acid}} = \text{3.0 M}[/tex]

[tex]V_{\text{acid}} = \text{11.6 mL}[/tex]

[tex]f_{\text{acid}} = 2[/tex]

[tex]V_{\text{base}} = \text{25.00 mL}[/tex]

[tex]f_{\text{base}} = 1[/tex]

Unknown:

[tex]M_{\text{base}}[/tex]

Solution:

[tex]f_{\text{acid}}M_{\text{acid}}V_{\text{acid}} = f_{\text{base}}M_{\text{base}}V_{\text{base}}[/tex]

[tex]M_{\text{base}} = M_{\text{acid}} × \frac{V_{\text{acid}}}{V_{\text{base}}} × \frac{f_{\text{acid}}}{f_{\text{base}}}[/tex]

[tex]M_{\text{base}} = \text{3.0 M} × \frac{\text{11.6 mL}}{\text{25.00 mL}} × \frac{2}{1}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{M_{\text{base}} = \text{2.8 M}}[/tex]
