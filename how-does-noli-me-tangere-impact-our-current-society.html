2. How does the celebration in The dinner affect our current time noli me tangere


Answer:

The celebration in the dinner scene of "Noli Me Tangere" affects the current time of the story in several ways:

Foreshadowing: The dinner scene is the first indication that there is a growing tension between the colonizers and the colonized. The Filipinos are treated with disrespect, and the Spaniards are shown to be condescending and patronizing towards them. This sets the stage for the larger conflict that develops throughout the novel.

Symbolism: The dinner scene is full of symbolism that reflects the power dynamic between the colonizers and the colonized. The Filipinos are seated at a separate table, and the Spanish guests are served first. This symbolizes the unequal treatment and the lack of respect shown towards the Filipinos.

Irony: The dinner scene is also ironic in that the Spaniards are celebrating their victory over the French in battle, while they continue to oppress the Filipinos. This underscores the hypocrisy and the double standards of the colonizers.

Character Development: The dinner scene also reveals the personalities of the different characters in the novel. It shows the arrogance and the sense of entitlement of the colonizers, as well as the subservience and the resignation of the colonized.

Overall, the dinner scene in "Noli Me Tangere" is a significant moment in the novel that sets the stage for the larger conflict between the colonizers and the colonized. It also serves as a reflection of the power dynamic between the two groups and reveals the personalities of the different characters in the story

It can serve as a reference for research on the socio-economic and political problems faced by the Philippines during the colonial period and their lasting impact on the country.Language and writing - Noli Me Tangere is written in the Spanish language, which was the official language during the Spanish colonial period. The novel's linguistic and stylistic features can be analyzed to gain a better understanding of Philippine literature and the evolution of the Filipino language.

13. What is the impact of noli me tangere during that era and our millennial era?​


Answer:

       Jose Rizal’s Noli Me Tangere was all about describing the Spanish colonization here in the Philippines and how the Spanish mistreated Filipinos. It followed the story of Crisostomo Ibarra who came from Europe to study and returned to the Philippines after his seven years of journey. He reunited with his acquaintances including his nemesis, Padre Damaso at Kapitan Tiago’s house. He also visited his love, Maria Clara whom he missed for so long. He was shocked to learn about his father’s death. Lieutenant Guevarra, his father’s friend, told the story behind his father’s death. He was told that Padre Damaso ordered the gravediggers to transfer Rafael Ibarra’s body to the Chinese Cemetery. Although the gravediggers did not follow Padre Damaso’s order, they threw the body into the river. This made Crisostomo Ibarra angry and started a revolution against Padre Damaso and the Spanish government.
