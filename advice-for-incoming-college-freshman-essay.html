12. The average height of females in the freshman class of a certain college has been 162.5 centimeters with a standard deviation of 6.9 centimeters. Is there a reason to believe that there has been a change in the average height if a random sample of 50 females in the present freshman class has average of 165.2 centimeters? Use 0.05 level of significance.


Answer:

To determine whether there is a reason to believe that there has been a change in the average height of females in the freshman class, you can conduct a hypothesis test using the sample mean of 165.2 centimeters and the known population mean of 162.5 centimeters.

The null hypothesis in this case would be that there has been no change in the average height, and the alternative hypothesis would be that there has been a change in the average height.

To test the hypothesis, you can use a z-test, which is a statistical test that compares the sample mean to the population mean, taking into account the standard deviation of the population and the sample size.

To conduct the z-test, you would first calculate the standard error of the mean, which is the standard deviation of the population divided by the square root of the sample size. In this case, the standard error of the mean is 6.9 / sqrt(50) = 1.07.

Next, you would calculate the z-score, which is the difference between the sample mean and the population mean, divided by the standard error of the mean. In this case, the z-score is (165.2 - 162.5) / 1.07 = 2.74.

Finally, you would compare the z-score to the critical value for the desired level of significance. At a significance level of 0.05, the critical value is 1.96.

Since the z-score is greater than the critical value, there is strong evidence to reject the null hypothesis and conclude that there has been a change in the average height of females in the freshman class. The sample mean of 165.2 centimeters is significantly different from the population mean of 162.5 centimeters at the 0.05 level of significance.

18. EAPP is grounded in four academic papers the,____paper, concept paper, position paper, and report paper, which are tradionally taught for college freshman​


EAPP is grouped in four academic papers. They are reaction paper, concept paper, position paper, and report paper.

Reaction paper

A reaction paper offers a thoughtful and well-informed view of the world, popular culture, and the arts as well as a rational and well-founded response to it.

Concept paper

The concept paper serves as the initial form of communication between the doctoral student and the doctoral committee, laying the groundwork for the practical dissertation process. The Concept Paper essentially serves as a proposal, giving the doctoral student the chance to specify a research area and get preliminary comments on the proposed study. A carefully thought-out Concept Paper will pique the dissertation committee's attention and lay out a concise strategy for the student's dissertation.

Position paper

A position paper is an essay that takes a stand on a particular topic. On the basis of the information gathered, it presents reasons in favor of the writer's viewpoint.

Report paper

It is a written account of a specific circumstance that includes both qualitative and quantitative information. An effective report has an impartial tone while also conveying humanity.
