6. What is the positive and negative effect of colonization


Answer:

Colonial powers introduced Western schools and healthcare, resources that often had a positive effect on the lives of the colonized people. Government schools typically focused on training for low-level civil service occupations. In many African colonies, missionaries shouldered much of the responsibility for educating the masses. Although fewer than half of the children in most colonies went to school, many of the leaders of national liberation movements who went on to lead post-colonial governments had been educated in colonial government and missionary schools. Where colonizers established medical centers, they succeeded in lowering infant mortality and promoted vaccination and disease prevention. While the colonizers did bring positive improvements and advancements, the inhabitants often lacked immunity to the pathogens the colonizers also brought from their home countries. Indigenous populations fell to plagues including smallpox, influenza and other viruses that hadn't existed within their county prior to colonization.

Political Strife

In many territories, colonizing powers created centralized governments where none existed before. The colonizers, however, created political borders that served their own needs rather than reflecting the historical territorial divisions of the people who lived there. Suddenly, many ethnic groups that had never worked together were forced to do so. At the same time, some groups found their traditional territory divided by a political border between two colonial countries, fracturing ties between extended family and kinsmen. As a result, colonialism created large, ethnically fragmented countries prone to civil wars both during and after the colonial era.

Plantation Mentality

Colonial powers conquered distant lands in part to exploit their natural resources, and exerted complete control of the extraction and export of cash crops and minerals -- often with little regard to helping the people native to the area. In agriculture-based colonies, a plantation economy developed, such as the coffee plantations of India and modern-day Sri Lanka. Plantation owners exerted strict control over the native people they hired to work there, often in deplorable conditions. In Southeast Asia, colonialism did have the positive effect of European investment and construction of canals and irrigation systems. African territories also saw roads and other infrastructure as a result of colonialism. However, such progress came at the expense of harsh treatment by the colonizers.

Traditions Lost

During the colonial era, many European colonizers, such as Great Britain, took a paternalistic view of the native culture. They saw themselves as acting in the best interests of these people, bringing them Christianity and civilization. This attitude destroyed traditional beliefs and social values, however, and had a negative effect on colonized populations. Some groups attempted to curry favor with the colonizers by converting to Christianity and renouncing their traditional beliefs. Converts often acquired special military positions or privileged tasks in colonial government administration. For example, the French preferred to employ Vietnamese clerks and government administrators in Cambodia and Laos. Over time, colonizers often came to prefer certain ethnic groups in a colony over others, reinforcing or even exacerbating ancient ethnic tensions.

Answer:

What are the positive influences of Spanish colonization of the Philippines?

1. Introduction of Catholicism: Spanish colonization brought Catholicism to the Philippines, which is still the dominant religion today.

2. Establishment of an organized government: The Spanish established an organized government in the Philippines, which provided a framework for the nation to develop further.

3. Improvement of infrastructure: The Spanish constructed roads and bridges, which improved communication and transportation throughout the islands.

4. Introduction of new foods: The Spanish introduced new foods and dishes, such as adobo and other popular Filipino dishes.

5. Promotion of education: The Spanish encouraged Filipino children to attend school, and established a number of educational institutions throughout the islands.

6. Expansion of trade: The Spanish opened the Philippines to the global economy, expanding trade and increasing the wealth of the nation.

Explanation:

The Spanish colonization of the Philippines had several positive influences on the country and its people, such as:

Christianity: The Spanish introduced Christianity to the Philippines, which led to the widespread conversion of the indigenous population to the Catholic faith. This had a significant impact on the cultural and spiritual lives of the people, and many of the country's religious traditions and practices today have their roots in Spanish Catholicism.

Education: The Spanish established a system of formal education in the Philippines, which led to the spread of literacy and the development of a educated class. They also established schools and universities, which helped to promote education and knowledge among the population.

Architecture and Engineering: Spanish colonization brought in a new style of architecture, engineering, and urban planning in the Philippines. Many of the country's oldest and most iconic buildings, including churches and government buildings, were built during this time.

Agriculture and Industry: The Spanish introduced new crops such as sugarcane, tobacco, coffee, and cotton, which led to the development of agriculture and industry in the Philippines.

Language: Spanish also introduced the Spanish language, which became one of the official languages of the Philippines and is still spoken by some people today.

It's important to note that colonization also brought negative consequences such as loss of land, forced labor, and suppression of indigenous cultures, traditions, and languages.
