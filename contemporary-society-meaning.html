2. Contemporary art as reflection of contemporary culture and society


Answer:

Contemporary art as a reflection of contemporary culture and society.

Artists are historians, and their work serves as a diary. Without a doubt, art is a mirror of the times as well as an expression of beauty and ingenuity. This is also one of the primary reasons why art is a dynamic medium. Art's evolution may be seen in all of its forms. From the worlds of music, dance, and stage performances to the visual arts, such as sculptures and paintings, there has been a shift in standards and culture.

A work of art is a reflection of the artist's personality. It depicts his thoughts, feelings, and passions. Religion, battle, and power were all major subjects for medieval artists, and they can be seen in almost every piece of medieval art. As it ushered in the twentieth century, Impressionism provided us with the calm, vibrancy, and color of the world, long before it was confronted with the terrible reality of inequality and conflict.

Many people consider the arts to be trivial and unimportant in comparison to the technologies that are revolutionizing the world. Artists aren't revered in the same way that engineers and doctors are.

Art is a social reflection. Because the arts encompass a wide range of disciplines such as painting, music, literature, dance, and other forms of creative expression, they have a substantial impact on society, religion, and education, and hence deserve the same respect accorded to the sciences.

Art evolves to represent society's new advances as it expands and grows. Art is a reflection of our history and a record of a significant aspect of our life.

If you've ever studied literature, you'll notice that it reflects the time period in which it was written. When writers write, they are impacted by whatever is going on in their culture at the time. For instance, Jeffrey Eugenides' novel "The Marriage Plot" was a meditation on how the concept of marriage as a driving storyline, which was popularized by authors such as Jane Austen, is no longer the same driving force today.

His writing reflects a society where divorce is all too common, and his personal perspective reflects our culture's marital values. People in the future will read Eugenides' story and gain an understanding of what society valued prior to the future generation.

23. The contemporary type of society nowadays


Answer:

Contemporary society, according to social and political scientists, is characterised by at least three fundamental directions:

increasing human interconnection through a network of relationships that is progressively covering the whole planet;

the pace and depth of the evolution of human ways of life determined by technological innovation represent an absolute novelty in human history;

the scale of anthropological and ecological transformation due to the interaction between evolutionary factors (social, cultural, economic, and technological) has no historical precedent.

These are some examples, but they are many more.

These presentations are the result of a number of fundamental changes that are irreversibly transforming our daily lives, our way of thinking and perceiving the world and our way of living together. Among these fundamental changes are: improvements in life conditions, life expectancy, literacy and gender equality; changes in domestic and international political institutions; and the breakdown of natural equilibria.
