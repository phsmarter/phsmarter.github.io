8. Synopsis of Marriage story Movie?


"Marriage Story" is a drama film released in 2019 and directed by Noah Baumbach. The movie follows the story of a married couple, Charlie and Nicole, who are going through a difficult divorce. The film takes the audience through their journey as they navigate the complicated and emotional process of separating their lives and family.

The film begins with a montage of Charlie and Nicole's happy memories together and their life in New York City. However, as the story progresses, it becomes clear that their marriage is falling apart due to their different priorities and ambitions. Nicole, an actress, feels neglected and unfulfilled in her marriage to Charlie, a successful theater director who is consumed by his work.

Their separation becomes more complicated when they involve lawyers, and the couple is forced to confront their unresolved issues and the impact their decisions have on their son, Henry. The movie portrays the emotional toll of the divorce on both Charlie and Nicole, as well as their struggles with custody battles and financial disagreements.

Throughout the film, the audience sees the couple's attempts to reconcile and come to terms with their differences, ultimately leading to a bittersweet conclusion. The movie features standout performances from Adam Driver and Scarlett Johansson, who portray Charlie and Nicole, respectively, with depth and vulnerability.

Overall, "Marriage Story" is a poignant and honest portrayal of the complexities and realities of marriage and divorce, exploring themes of love, loss, and forgiveness.

28. SYNOPSIS of the movie "Hidden Figures​


Answer:

"Hidden Figures" is a 2016 biographical drama film directed by Theodore Melfi, based on the non-fiction book of the same name by Margot Lee Shetterly. The film tells the true story of three African American women who worked at NASA during the 1960s space race and contributed to the successful launch of astronaut John Glenn into orbit.

The main characters are Katherine Goble Johnson (Taraji P. Henson), Dorothy Vaughan (Octavia Spencer), and Mary Jackson (Janelle Monáe). Katherine is a brilliant mathematician who is assigned to the Space Task Group to calculate the trajectories for the Mercury program. Dorothy is a supervisor in the West Area Computers division, where African American women are segregated and treated unfairly. Mary is a talented engineer who wants to become an engineer, but is held back by racial and gender discrimination.

The three women face numerous obstacles in their pursuit of success, including racist attitudes from their colleagues, lack of support from their families, and institutionalized discrimination. Despite these challenges, they persevere and make significant contributions to the space program.

The film also explores the broader cultural and political context of the 1960s, including the Civil Rights Movement and the Cold War. It highlights the role of African American women in the space program, and the importance of diversity and inclusion in science and technology.

"Hidden Figures" was a critical and commercial success, receiving numerous award nominations and grossing over $236 million worldwide. The film was praised for its powerful performances, inspiring story, and message of perseverance and equality.
