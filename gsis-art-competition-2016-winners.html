26. Give the differences between dance as an art and dance as a competition?​


Answer:

Dance as an art and dance as a competition

Explanation:

Dance as an art - Dance is a fully-fledged art form, belonging to the 'performing arts', a goal in itself and a means of allowing a human being to express his inner feelings, thoughts and experiences. Dance is a natural form of self-expression: the body expresses itself naturally and so therefore does the spirit.

Dance as a competition - Competitive dance is a sport where dancers compete by performing dances in a number of permitted dance styles like tap, lyrical, jazz, acro, ballet, hip-hop, contemporary, modern etc. The competitors compete by dancing and performing the style of their choice before a common group of judges.
