13. What are the considerations in writing a book review or an article critique​


Answer:

First and foremost, let’s define critique:

Critique is a detailed analysis and assessment of something. In college writing, it’s an essay type that analyzes and evaluates a book or an article.

Do not confuse book critique and book reviews! When assigned to write a critique essay, you need to analyze a book (or an article) rather than summarize and retell its contents.

In other words, it’s not about information but analysis and persuasion. When writing a critique paper, you enhance brain functions and critical thinking.

When writing a critique paper, you need to give an assessment of literary works. It’s a systematic analysis discussing a book’s validity and evaluating its worth. Also known as a critical response, this type of writing argues whether a book is worth reading and why.

A critique dead Stars by Paz Benitez

The story revolves around the idea of romanticism and the responsibility that comes with it. Dead Stars is a rather brief tale written by Par Marquez Benitez in 1925, a time when infidelity was deemed to be romantic in lieu of today's ideologies. The story follows Alfredo Salazar, a thirty-year-old bachelor who is trying to understand the complexities of his emotions while still retaining his dignity as a man with a bright future.

When I initially read the story, I was particularly drawn to the way it was written because the entire narrative was told in the third person, which surprised me given that the books I typically read are written in the first person.

When I first read the story, the way it was written particularly piqued my interest. From start to finish, the entire narrative was written in third person, which surprised me because most of the books I read usually tell stories through the eyes of a particular character. Finding it immensely appropriate that the novel was written in the early 1900s, when classic stories about lords and ladies—the Filipino equivalent of Don and Dona—often appeared, gives context to its writing. found the story to be captivating even though it did present a perfect illustration of infidelity, which is now frequently frowned upon.

The characters reminded me a lot of Romeo and Juliet, which is a story highly influenced by patriarchal attitudes, in my opinion. When I got down to the details, though, I felt that Alfredo Salazar was presented quite humanely because he was simply acting out of emotion at the time but gradually changed his mind toward the end.
