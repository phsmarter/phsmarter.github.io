Who is the famous literature in the Philippines? 4. Why is it important to study 21st century literature from the

There are many famous literature writers in the Philippines, but some of the most renowned include Jose Rizal, Nick Joaquin, F. Sionil Jose, and Lualhati Bautista.

Jose Rizal is considered the national hero of the Philippines and is known for his novels "Noli Me Tangere" and "El Filibusterismo," which exposed the injustices and corruption of Spanish colonial rule in the Philippines.

Nick Joaquin is known for his works that explore Filipino identity, culture, and history, such as "The Woman Who Had Two Navels" and "Cave and Shadows."

F. Sionil Jose is a prolific writer who has written numerous novels and short stories that tackle social and political issues in the Philippines, including poverty, corruption, and human rights.

Lualhati Bautista is known for her feminist writings that tackle issues of gender and class in Philippine society, such as "Dekada '70" and "Bata, Bata, Pa'no Ka Ginawa?"

Studying 21st-century literature is important because it allows us to understand the contemporary issues and concerns of our time. Literature reflects the social, cultural, and political realities of the world we live in, and studying 21st-century literature can help us gain a deeper understanding of the issues that affect us today. It also allows us to appreciate the diversity of voices and perspectives that exist in the world, and to engage with literature as a means of critical thinking and self-reflection.

24. Which of the following is considered as importance of studying Philippine Literature?a. We can learn the culture of people across time and space. b. We can understand not only the past life of a nation but also its present. c. To appreciate our cultural heritage. d. All of the above​


Answer:

The correct answer is d. all of above

Explanation:

Studying Philippine Literature is important for various reasons, including:

a. Learning about the culture and history of the Philippines: Philippine Literature provides insights into the lives, customs, beliefs, and values of the Filipinos across time and space. It allows us to understand how the culture and traditions of the country came to be, and how they have evolved over time.

b. Understanding the present of the nation: By studying Philippine Literature, we can also learn about the current issues, challenges, and opportunities faced by the country. Literature often reflects the social, political, and economic issues of the time, and it can help us understand the current state of the country.

c. Appreciating our cultural heritage: Philippine Literature showcases the creativity, artistry, and imagination of the Filipino people. It allows us to appreciate and celebrate our cultural heritage, and to recognize the diverse talents and contributions of Filipino writers.

Therefore, all options given (a, b, and c) are considered important reasons for studying Philippine Literature.
