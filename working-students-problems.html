9. What is the problem or issue of Time management of working student?​


Answer:

As a student, the key to success is recognising the importance of time management, so you can keep on top of your studies when work gets busy. Thankfully, there are lots of simple steps you can take to maximise the time you have.

Here are some time management tips for students that will help you be effective during busy periods at work.

Plan ahead

As soon as you’ve enrolled, make note of your assessment dates, webinars or online tutorials in a calendar. This will help you to manage competing commitments at work, stay on top of upcoming tasks, so you don’t fall behind. A visual reminder of these key dates is an effective way to complement reminders on your phone or work laptop, so invest in a big desk calendar or hang one up on your wall.

Create a schedule

Schedules and set study times per week will help you feel in control and maximise your preparedness. Create a weekly planner or timetable to outline when you do what activity, but allow for variation leading up to known busy and/or seasonal periods at work.

Be realistic

Unexpected things can happen at work, so you may miss a deadline due to other commitments. Don’t be hard on yourself, you can contact your tutor and student success advisor to explain your situation. They realise that the majority of online students are also balancing work, so they are understanding and there to support you. To avoid this, allow for extra time in your schedule for the unexpected and try not to commit to things when you know you will be stressed out.

Avoid studying at work

There is always a temptation to squeeze in a bit of study during work hours, or vice versa - working from home when you should be using that time to study. One or both will suffer as a consequence, so make sure that you keep work hours for work, and stick to your scheduled weekly study time commitments. This will ensure that you give each the attention they deserve.

Work Immersion

Examples of the issues or problems students might encounter in work immersion:

Unpredictable adaptability Work /travel hour adjustment Company Culture understanding Real workplace stress Pressure from keeping up

Work Immersion is a brand new substitute for the On the Job Training in College. This only applies to the Senior High School students from Grade 11 to Grade 12, just like the new curriculum work immersion tends to hone students’ skills and ability to be part of the vast modern society. Though, it isn’t that far from being compared to actually studying for College.

Just like how on the job training works, work immersion is when students are allowed to take internships from various companies that are willing to take them in and also with the school’s approval. Sometimes the company choosing is done by the school and is suggesting it to their students in particular fields. Though, these abilities are usually done if your school has a sponsor or backing from a specific company.

But otherwise, the students are the ones who will have to look for a place that they want to have a work immersion with. Work immersion allows students to:

Engage in a real work setting Experience the real way of working Expand their understanding Hone their skill and ability to adapt to work environments
