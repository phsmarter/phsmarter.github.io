We learned the Carinosa from the Spanish suite where the ladies holds the fan and the men holds the handkerchief. 19. similarities of cariñosa and tinikling​


Answer:

Cariñosa is a folk dance of the Philippines which was introduced in period of the Spaniards (Hispanic history), and later was made official dance of the Filipino. It uses a fan and handkerchief. It is innate because women is wearing a Maria Clarang dress (happy) and the men are naka-barong tagalog. Its compass is 3/4 like some dances of the Spaniards.

While Tiniklíng is one of the most popular folk dances in the Philippines. The dance originated in Leyte. Its music is fast and on a basic compass. The careful avoidance of the female and male dancers in the bamboo flute is derived from the vigorous but gentle avoidance of the tikling bird in the trap set by the farmers in their paddy fields.

The dance can be very hurtful — when the feet of the dancers who jump slowly are pinched to avoid a trap. The rhythm of the dance speeds up while the rubbing of the bamboos also speeds up. It takes courage and concentration for anyone to try to dance it.
