4. what are the 3 primary prevention of the disease dengue


Answer:

The three primary prevention measures for dengue are:

1. Eliminating breeding sites of mosquitoes: Dengue is transmitted through the bite of infected Aedes mosquitoes. Therefore, the best way to prevent dengue is to eliminate breeding sites of mosquitoes by removing standing water in and around homes, schools, and other public places.

2. Using mosquito repellent: Mosquito repellents containing DEET, picaridin, IR3535, or oil of lemon eucalyptus can help prevent mosquito bites and reduce the risk of dengue infection.

3. Wearing protective clothing: Covering the skin with long-sleeved shirts, pants, and socks can reduce the risk of mosquito bites and prevent dengue infection.

History

The first case of probable dengue fever (DF) was recorded in a Chinese medical encyclopedia from the Jin Dynasty (265–420 AD) which referred to as “water poison” associated with flying insects. There have been descriptions of epidemics in the 17th century, but the most plausible early reports of dengue epidemics occurred between 1779 and 1780, when an epidemic swept through Asia, Africa and North America. From that time until 1940, epidemics were infrequent (2, 3).

In 1906, transmission by the Aedes mosquitoes was confirmed, and in 1907 dengue was the second disease (after yellow fever) that was shown to be caused by a virus. Further investigations by John Burton Cleland and Joseph Franklin Siler completed the basic understanding of dengue transmission (2, 3).

Dengue fever in India was first reported in 1946 and many small outbreaks occurred after that in different parts of country, like in Vishakhapatnam in 1969 and Jalore, Rajasthan in 1985, but first dengue hemorrhagic fever (DHF) case was reported in Kolkata back in 1964, and Delhi experienced major outbreak in 1996 (22, 23). During an investigation of an outbreak of unknown fever in siliguri, district Darjeeling, in oct-nov 2005 dengue fever cases were confirmed (24).
