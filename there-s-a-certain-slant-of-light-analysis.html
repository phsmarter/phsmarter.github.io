 lateral surface area   A   of the cone   =   πrl    /    l = slant height

  120π   =  π r 16     /   area  =  120π,    l  = 16

    16 r  =   120    /  π is cancelled out

         r  =    120/16=  7.5

         the base of the right triangle    b  =   7.5/2 =  3.75

          h²  =   l² -  b²    /   Pythagoras theorem of a right triangle

           h²  =   16² -  3.75²

                   =  256 - 14

             h²   =  242

              h    =   √242

13. An ice cream cone with r= 3cm s= 12cm (slant, height )​


Answer:

To find the surface area of an ice cream cone, we use the formula:

SA = πr^2 + πrs

where SA is the surface area, r is the radius of the base, and s is the slant height of the cone.

Given:

- Radius of the ice cream cone (r) = 3 cm

- Slant height of the cone (s) = 12 cm

To find:

- Surface area of the ice cream cone

Solution:

- Using the given values, we can now find the surface area of the ice cream cone:

SA = πr^2 + πrs

= π(3)^2 + π(3)(12)

= 9π + 36π

= 45π

≈ 141.37 sq. cm (rounded to two decimal places)

- Therefore, the surface area of the ice cream cone is approximately 141.37 sq. cm.

So, the surface area of the ice cream cone is approximately 141.37 square centimeters.
