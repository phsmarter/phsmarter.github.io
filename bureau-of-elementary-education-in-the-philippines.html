1. without science and technology, what would be the status of elementary education in the philippines? ​


Answer:

Without science and technology, elementary education in the Philippines would likely be very different than it is today. Science and technology have played a significant role in shaping and improving the quality of education in the country.

One significant impact of science and technology on education is the availability of educational resources and tools. For example, the availability of textbooks, educational videos, and online resources have made learning more accessible and engaging for students. Without these resources, students may have limited access to learning materials, making it more challenging for them to acquire new knowledge and skills.

Additionally, science and technology have facilitated communication and collaboration among educators and students. The use of online platforms for discussions, group projects, and remote learning has become increasingly prevalent, especially during the pandemic. Without these tools, communication and collaboration may be limited to in-person interactions, which could hinder students' learning and development.

In summary, the absence of science and technology would likely have a detrimental effect on elementary education in the Philippines. Access to learning materials and resources would be limited, and communication and collaboration among educators and students would be more challenging.

Secondary education - it is the final and second basic phase of the education which comes before tertiary education.

•Tertiary education - it is commonly referred to as post Secondary Education referred to academic persuits undertaken after High School.

•Vocational education - it is a kind of education that prepares people to work in various jobs, such as trade ,craft or as technician.
