5. Campaign speech for mayor


Explanation:

Good afternoon. I am here today as a candidate running for mayor of Middleton. As your mayor, I will help protect flooded areas, benefit elderly society, and provide meeting places for teens. With my political experience as City Council Chairman for Middleton in 2007, I come to you prepared and ready to go to work.

Flooding is an issue that we, together can fix. It is enormously affecting citizens along the shoreline of the Mississippi River. We can install gutter systems on buildings along the river. We can build an underground pipe system to divert water flow. These are only some of the ideas when it comes to flooding. We should always be preparing for our next flood when we aren’t experiencing one.

We also have citizens worrying about teens, as they are grouping together downtown at night. We can create a teen center in the Middleton Library. Provide a teen center, where there is a small fee according to what families can afford. We can increase police patrol around former “hangout” areas, to ensure safety. Experience how the change positively affects our teens as a whole.

Another issue we in Middleton face is taking care of our elderly. They are having a hard time affording the material that they need. One way to help is give our senior citizens some tax breaks. We should establish a senior center that provides transportation to and from it. We should also give them free transportation to places around Middleton, like shopping centers and medical buildings. These steps will greatly improve the quality of life for our seniors.

There is a reason I’m here. When I was born in Washington DC, I found out I didn’t fit in. When I moved to Middleton I started participating with the community. I created the Wright foundation, a charity that really meant something to me. As I moved to politics, I thought, Middleton is an amazing place! It was a great opportunity to be elected City Council chairman, and continue to serve Middleton. We can control the issues such as the flooding, help the teens finding positive recreation, and support the elderly money issues. Middleton is already a great place, but with a few small tweaks we can make it even better! If I am elected your mayor you will Experience the Change!

8. example of speech campaign for P.I.O or public information officer... help po​


Speech Campaign: Public Information Officer

Good morning to all of our professors, classmates, fellow students, and candidates. One thought immediately sprang to mind when I was selected to run for Public Information Officer in Supreme Student Government. "Great responsibility goes along with great power." "Spiderman did cross my thoughts, yes. That's correct! Possession of a position entails both tremendous privilege and considerable responsibility. Being a leader is not an easy undertaking, nor does it make you a well-known person, as it requires additional effort outside of coursework and other school-related tasks.

I am, nevertheless, really fortunate since I was chosen. Trust is meant by this. Being your SPG officer for the upcoming academic year as your Public Information Officer fills me with great joy in a world where individuals take advantage of anything, especially trusts.

As the work suggests, accurate information should be spread from us to your end, advocating true, just, and fair information and denouncing false information.

If given the opportunity, I will support my fellow officers in all their undertakings as your public information officer and stand by their side. Additionally, two-way communication will also be implemented through listening to the voices of you, my classmates, and fellow students.

By doing this, I hope to earn your support as your SPG Public Information Officer for the 2019–2020 academic year and to demonstrate my ability to uphold the faith you have placed in me. As I see it, let's work together to create goodness within each other and find the silver lining.

Good morning once again!

14. sample campaign speech​


Answer:

Good morning/afternoon fellow students. Before anything else, allow me to sincerely thank you for acknowledging my candidacy for senator in our beloved university. Since freshman year, I have been an advocate for our student body. Although I have not held a position similar to what I am running for now, I have always been actively helping our student government in my humble capacity as First Year representative. Through my efforts, the freshmen have earned two seats in the school council, empowering them more by making their voices heard. The constant complain of first year students about not having a place for them to hang out during their free time because most study areas and even libraries are dominantly occupied by older students, was finally solved when I made representations in the school board to ask for funds for the construction of at least two study sheds exclusively for the use of freshmen. The construction was done before the end of the first semester, and the facilities are now serving their purpose to the delight of our youngest students. In the second semester, I batted for transparency by demanding the student council’s treasurer to give a quarterly financial report which was to be published in our school publication. This measure was carried out and as a result, we now have a good knowledge on how the funds derived from our school fees are being used by our student government. Towards the end of the fourth quarter, I crafted a petition, demanding better services from our food providers through the sale of nutritious food and banning unhealthy snack and drinks. The petition is still being heard and evaluated by the school administration. If given your trust this year as senator, I pledge to finish the programs that I started last year, and pursue other endeavors that would make the lives of every student in this school better. I will continue to advocate for transparency and clean governance. I will continue to be your voice in the student council, and I shall not tire of listening to your sentiments about issues directly affecting your studies. I will find ways to make the enrollment process easier and more efficient so that students don’t have to wear themselves out for one day only to be told to come back the next day. The enrollment should not be long and tedious so students don’t have to spend more for their extended stay in the school. My self-driven advocacy for all of us is what drives me forward. On Election Day, I ask for your support so in return, I can continue making your voices heard. My dream has always been to serve others, and this is something I have been able to do in the past. I pray that this school year will be another time for me to improve myself as a leader and another opportunity to live my dream once more. I promise not to betray the trust that you have given me in the past. I enjoin you all to vote for me and the rest of my team not only because you know many of us but because you know deep in your hearts that we can deliver the services that you truly deserve. My record speaks for my capacity to serve. I care about your interests and your voice. I care about providing equal opportunities to every student who come to study in this university. I care about making your student life as enjoyable and as fulfilling as possible. Let us make this journey together, and let us all reach for our dreams and aspirations. Come share your ideas, and I will make them happen. Thank you and have a great day.

Answer:

Ladies and Gentlemen,

Thank you very much for inviting me to your wonderful state here in the great heartland of America but also pretty near the coast. It is an honor to speak from this podium, in this storied courthouse, where so many profound legal judgments were handed down that they more or less canceled each other out.

Baricco traces the trajectory of a revolution in the way we think, feel, and communicate—and seeks to discover what it might actually mean for our future.

I am a simple man with a Harvard degree and a solid understanding of tractors. When I was a young boy, my father took me on his knee and told me the principle that has guided my life ever since: “Agree with some things, disagree with other things.” His was truly the Greatest Generation, along with a few that came before and a couple that have come since.

Why am I running for president of the United States of America? That’s a good question, and perhaps there’s no good way to answer it. Or perhaps there is a good way to answer it. Either way, it’s a good question and I’m glad it was raised.

I want to talk a little bit about our nation’s children. Before this speech, a young man named Stanley Exley came up to me. Stanley is a mechanic, a noble profession if there ever was one. Or perhaps he is a chemistry professor, also noble. He was holding his small daughter, Emily, an adorable 3-year-old who was recently diagnosed with leukemia. Stanley is a straight shooter, and he came up to me and asked me a question I’ll never forget: “Do you like children?” And I looked him straight in the eye and said, “Yes, Stanley, I very much like children.” Maybe that’s not a popular opinion, but it’s what I believe. And to those of you who would say, “I don’t think children are our future,” I must reply in the strongest of terms: “I disagree.” Sometimes you have to take a stand for what you believe in.

Our country is sharply divided over a war that is being waged in a distant land. My views on this war are clear: it is happening, it is happening in Iraq, and it will continue to happen until it stops. Some people believe we should withdraw all the troops now. Some people believe we should stay and fight until we’ve established a stable nation. Some people believe we should gradually hand over control to the Iraqi government. I feel blessed to live in a country with so many beliefs.

On the subject of South African apartheid: I strongly, strongly, strongly oppose it. I’m glad it’s gone, and I hope it never comes back.

The state of our economy is in flux. Every single day, the stock market goes up or goes down or stays the same. If elected president, I will ask the Federal Reserve to take a good long look at the interest rate and decide whether or not to change it. If elected president, I will create jobs where there are none, and where there are jobs, I will create internships.

Let us take actions that will make people happy.

Let us take actions that will make people healthy or perhaps have the private sector do it.

“This is the kind of magazine you keep on your bookshelves with your favorite books.”

— Cece Bell, author of El Deafo

Let us take actions regarding taxes.

And let us move boldly so that our children and our children’s children can look back and say, “I’m glad they moved boldly on this, this, and that.”

Thank you.

27. example of a simple campaign speech for presidential.​


A Presidential campaign speech example.

In making a campaign speech text for the president, the important parts that we must include are:

In the opening sentence, greet the audience.Talking about things that concern you as a presidential candidateExpress your vision, mission, and plans to lead the country in the future.

Good morning everyone.

I thank God for allowing us to meet today and I also want to thank everyone who is willing to come today.

Recently, I can feel that economic and technological changes require us to always be able to adapt quickly in order to remain a country that is able to compete on an international scale. And this certainly requires a lot of improvements and also cooperation with various parties. In this regard, my main vision in running the government is to make our country a developed country and able to compete in the international world by implementing the mission of working with all member countries in implementing constructive policies, improving the quality of human resources, creating a clean, effective and trusted government, and keep maintain the noble values and culture that we have.

Why do I want to be president of the Philippines? I think there is no best answer other than that I really want this country to be a developed and safe country for all people. Therefore, I hope that we can all work together, hand in hand, to build a better country in the future. Because I believe that a good country can be realized with good cooperation between the government and the people, and I really appreciate the voice of every people.

Thank you.
