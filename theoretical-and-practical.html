METHODS, THEORIES, AND TOOLS

Various tools are commonly used to aid designers, and several additional theories offer more analytically rigorous support to engineering designers. Concurrent engineering may be the most practical method to improve the design process, and other common tools are used to obtain input from stakeholders in the design process (the Pugh Method, Quality Function Deployment, Decision Matrix techniques, and the Analytical Hierarchy Process). These tools incorporate relatively high levels of subjective judgment. An additional set of tools address variability, quality, and uncertainty in the design process (Projected Latent Structure, the Taguchi method, and Six Sigma). These tools are more analytical and are typically coupled to the processes used to produce products. Still other tools are used to generate alternatives for designers (artificial intelligence and TRIZ). Design theories also exist (Dym’s, Suh’s Axiomatic, Yoshikawa’s, and a Mathematical Framework) that are less widely used but offer more rigorous analytical bases. Finally, certain other tools are used primarily in the fields of management science and economics, and are being explored in current research for applicability to decision making in engineering design

Answer:

The theoretical framework for this study is centered around the Social Ecological Model (SEM). The SEM is a framework that identifies multiple levels of influence on individual health behaviors, including intrapersonal factors, interpersonal relationships, community and societal factors, and policy and environmental factors.

Intrapersonal factors refer to individual factors such as knowledge, attitudes, and beliefs that influence health behaviors. In this study, intrapersonal factors include students’ current knowledge, attitudes, and beliefs regarding food choices and chronic disease prevention.

Interpersonal relationships include social and cultural norms, family and peer influence, and other relationships that influence an individual’s behavior. The study will examine how social and cultural norms and peer influence affect the food choices of students in Agusan National High School.

Community and societal factors refer to the larger social and cultural environment that influences individual health behaviors. These factors might include access to healthy food options and food marketing strategies, such as advertisements for unhealthy foods. The study will investigate how the availability and accessibility of healthy food options in the school community and surrounding environment influence food choices of students.

Finally, policy and environmental factors refer to structural and systemic factors that affect health behavior. These factors may include government policies and regulations that impact food access and affordability, as well as environmental factors such as the availability of safe public spaces for physical activity. This study will examine how local policies and regulations impact food choices of students in the Agusan National High School.

Overall, this study will utilize the social ecological model to examine various factors that influence food choices and how these factors can be used to encourage healthier food choices among students in Agusan National High School.

17. PRACTICAL RESEARCH: PART OF CHAPTER 1: THE PROBLEM, THEORETICAL FRAMEWORK ABOUT PERCEPTION OF STUDENTS TO E-COMMERCE. ​


Answer:

The Problem

The surge in e-commerce has revolutionized the way businesses operate, and it has become increasingly popular among consumers globally. In recent times, the world has experienced a significant shift towards online shopping, and this trend has been accelerated by the COVID-19 pandemic. The emergence of e-commerce has brought about many benefits, including convenience, accessibility, and affordability. However, despite these benefits, there are still some reservations about using e-commerce platforms, particularly among students.

Theoretical Framework

The study is anchored on the theory of planned behavior, which posits that attitudes, subjective norms, and perceived behavioral control are the main predictors of an individual's intentions to perform a specific behavior. Attitudes refer to an individual's overall evaluation of a behavior, while subjective norms refer to the perceived social pressure that an individual receives from significant others to perform a behavior. Perceived behavioral control refers to the individual's perception of the ease or difficulty of performing the behavior.

In the context of e-commerce, the theory of planned behavior suggests that students' perceptions of e-commerce are influenced by their attitudes towards the behavior, subjective norms from their peers and family, and perceived behavioral control, such as their level of computer literacy and internet access. These factors may either promote or hinder students' intentions to use e-commerce platforms, and ultimately affect their actual behavior.

The study aims to investigate the perceptions of students towards e-commerce, considering the factors that influence their attitudes, subjective norms, and perceived behavioral control. By understanding these factors, the study can provide insights into how e-commerce platforms can be designed to better meet the needs and preferences of students, ultimately leading to increased adoption and usage of e-commerce among this demographic.
