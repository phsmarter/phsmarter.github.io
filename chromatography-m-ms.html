23. Examples of chromatography


Paper chromatography is a method used to separate colored chemicals. In two-dimensional chromatography, a type of paper chromatography, is carried out using two solvents and rotating the paper 90Â° in between. This is useful for separating complex mixtures of compounds having similar polarity. The setup has three components. The mobile phase( which is a solution that travels up the stationary phase because of capillary action), the stationary phase (which is usually water), and paper (which supports the stationary phase). Difference between TLC and paper chromatography is that stationary phase in TLC is a layer of adsorbent (usually silica gel, or aluminium oxide), and stationary phase in paper chromatography is water.

Other examples of chromatography are: column chromatography, ion-exchange chromatography, gel-permeation chromatography, affinity chromatography, thin-layer chromatography, and gas chromatography.

30. What is chromatography?


Answer:

Chromatography, technique for separating the components, or solutes, of a mixture on the basis of the relative amounts of each solute distributed between a moving fluid stream, called the mobile phase, and a contiguous stationary phase. The mobile phase may be either a liquid or a gas, while the stationary phase is either a solid or a liquid.
