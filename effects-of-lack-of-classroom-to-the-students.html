12. - List down all the reason why school are not allowing cellphone inside the classroom including it's effect if students will not comply.​


Here are some reasons why schools are not allowing cellphones inside the classroom and their potential effects if students do not comply:

Distraction: Cellphones can be a major distraction in the classroom. Students may be tempted to check their notifications or messages during class, which can affect their learning and performance.

Cheating: Cellphones can be used for cheating during tests or exams. Students may access online resources or messaging apps to get answers from their classmates.

Cyberbullying: Cellphones can also be a tool for cyberbullying. Students may use their phones to send hurtful messages or post embarrassing photos or videos of their classmates.

Disruptive behavior: Students may use their phones to play games or watch videos during class, which can disrupt the learning environment and distract other students.

Health concerns: Some studies suggest that prolonged exposure to cellphone radiation may have negative health effects. Schools may want to limit students' exposure to radiation by prohibiting cellphone use in the classroom.

Socialization: Allowing cellphones in the classroom may discourage face-to-face interaction between students and teachers. Students may be more focused on their phones than on their peers or instructors, which can lead to a lack of socialization and communication skills.

Effects of non-compliance:

Lower grades: Students who are easily distracted by their phones may have lower grades and struggle to keep up with the class.

Punishments: Schools may impose penalties or punishments for students who do not comply with the cellphone policy, such as detention, suspension, or confiscation of the device.

Negative impact on learning: Students who are constantly on their phones during class may miss important information or instructions, leading to a negative impact on their learning and performance.

Safety concerns: Allowing cellphones in the classroom may pose safety concerns, as students may use their phones to access inappropriate content or engage in unsafe behaviors.

Cyberbullying and harassment: Students who use their phones to engage in cyberbullying or harassment may face disciplinary action or legal consequences.

Lack of self-confidence can have a significant impact on the academic performance of grade 10 students. Self-confidence is an important factor that influences how students approach their studies, interact with their peers, and engage with their teachers. When students lack self-confidence, they may struggle to participate in class discussions, ask questions, and seek help when they need it. This can lead to a lack of engagement with the material, which can ultimately result in poor academic performance.

Furthermore, a lack of self-confidence can lead to negative self-talk and feelings of inadequacy, which can create a self-fulfilling prophecy where the student begins to believe that they are not capable of succeeding academically. This can lead to a cycle of negative thoughts and behaviors that can be difficult to break.

In addition, a lack of self-confidence can also affect the student's social interactions, which can further impact their academic performance. Students who lack self-confidence may struggle to form positive relationships with their peers and may avoid participating in group work or other collaborative activities.

Overall, the lack of self-confidence can significantly impact the academic performance of grade 10 students, and it is important for educators and parents to be aware of this issue and take steps to support students in building their self-confidence.
