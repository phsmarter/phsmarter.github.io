13. Write an argumentative essay about “Mandatory Military Service for all Filipinos upon turning 18 years old”​


MANDATORY MILITARY SERVICE IN THE PHILIPPINES

Answer:

Military conscription, often known as mandatory force service, is a tactic used by governments to build a large and powerful military that is ready to be deployed in times of war or when the state's sovereignty is endangered. Many governments have used it throughout history, including China's Qin Empire in 221 BC and France during the French Revolution in the 1790s. Even now, several countries need military duty. North Korea, which expanded its 10-year military conscription in 2014; Myanmar, which drafts men and women into its armed forces; and South Korea, which requires all inhabitants to serve in the military.

Military service contributes to national cohesion in a variety of ways. For starters, it allows civilians to study and train alongside military personnel, resulting in the shared experience of having served in the military. Then there's the general idea of what it's like to be in the army, what the job requires, and what must be done to protect the nation. Citizens understand and respect the sacrifices made by military personnel for their country. All of these elements have the potential to bring people together, especially when confronted with a cultural or political threat from another nation. Maintaining mandatory military conscription entails maintaining an active reserve of large armies ready to respond quickly and efficiently to any threats to national security. People will be more conscious and watchful of the government's policies, particularly those impacting national security and the like, if every citizen is required to join the armed forces when called upon. People will want to learn more about the threats to their country and have a greater voice in how their government addresses problems if their lives are at jeopardy or sacrificed. Service in the military may teach people more than simply how to salute and shoot straight. They provide training that goes well beyond the technical skills necessary to do the job. Many military volunteers who went on to work in the civilian sector reported a range of new skills and job-related mindsets that assisted them in their employment. These include teamwork, responsibility, initiative, stress management, diversity, and global awareness. Others learn discipline and healthy living skills, as well as self-defense methods.

Mandatory military duty, on the other hand, constitutes a hazard for the following reasons. Because it is a required obligation that is implemented throughout the country, no one has the final say on whether or not to participate in military training and join the army. When young men (and women) are at the pinnacle of their intellectual ability, they are frequently recruited into the military (18 years old). This postpones people's pursuit of additional education and entry into the civilian labor market, decreasing the returns on human capital investments. Unlike voluntary troops who are ready to go through rigorous training and serve their country for a lengthy period of time in the military, draft soldiers may lack the necessary experience and preparedness, resulting in poor combat skill quality when deployed to war. This might lead to a substantial number of casualties among personnel recruited for mandatory military service. Under mandatory military duty, every citizen is compelled to join in and serve in the armed forces, however not everyone is cut out for it. Not everyone is physically, mentally, and emotionally fit to meet the demands of the job, whether due to a mental, physiological, or psychological ailment. Anxiety, depression, and other such issues should be thoroughly assessed. Everyone who is drafted into the military

Mandatory military service comes with a variety of benefits and has proven effective in maintaining the state's sovereignty as well as extending its borders in places where violence is rife. However, the implications for young people who enlist, the quality of military service, the labor market, future generations, and so on must be carefully considered.

Pros and cons of the mandatory use of facemasks:

Pros:

Facemasks can help reduce the spread of respiratory viruses, such as COVID-19, when used properly. This is because they can block droplets that may contain the virus from entering the mouth or nose of the person wearing the mask.Facemasks may help protect the wearer from respiratory viruses, especially if the person is in a crowded place or in close contact with others who may be infected.Mandating the use of facemasks can help promote a culture of public health and encourage people to take personal responsibility for preventing the spread of illness.

Cons:

Some people may find facemasks uncomfortable to wear, especially for long periods of time.Facemasks may make it more difficult for people to communicate with one another, especially if they rely on lip reading or facial expressions to understand what others are saying.There may be some financial costs associated with the mandatory use of facemasks, as people may need to purchase masks or other personal protective equipment.Some people may object to the mandatory use of facemasks on philosophical or political grounds, arguing that it infringes on their personal freedom.
