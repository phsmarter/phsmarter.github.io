Biologically, there may be a reason women cry more than men: Testosterone may inhibit crying, while the hormone prolactin (seen in higher levels in women) may promote it.

Hormones may play a role in women having more pain sensitivity. In addition, women have greater nerve density (more nerves in a given area of the body)—which may cause women to feel pain more severely than men

13. Make a poem or essay about "The cry of the dead whale"​


Answer:

As tons of plastics pollute our oceans every day, marine animals do their best to keep swimming.  Even in Marine Protected Areas and the deepest of the deep sea, plastic waste proliferates.  And marine mammals can’t escape it. Just two months ago, a dead sperm whale washed ashore in the Philippines…with 88 pounds of plastic in its stomach. A month later, a dead, pregnant sperm whale washed up on a beach in Italy with almost 50 pounds of plastic in her stomach.  These disturbing tragedies are beginning to occur often, and UNESCO reports that more than 100,000 marine mammals die from plastic pollution every year.

Last month, Philippines artist Biboy Royong sent a powerful message about plastic pollution to his country and to the world. He revealed his latest work of art, a 78-foot-long whale sculpture made entirely of plastics recovered from the ocean. Royong built the sculpture using materials such as single-use grocery bags, water bottles, cups, straws, cutlery, and ghost fishing gear. Royong named this piece “the Cry of the Dead Whale,” which he installed in front of the Cultural Center of the Philippines in Manila Bay to demonstrate the heartbreaking impact of human plastic consumption.  

"The Philippines is the 3rd largest contributor to plastic waste in our oceans. Sightings of different sea creatures washing ashore in our coastlines with tons of plastics inside are no longer news. As the problem grew bigger, people no longer bothered to ask why it happens and what can be done to stop it. The challenge was to make people see the effects of plastic wastes in our oceans. To achieve this, we needed to effectively visualize the worsening case of plastic pollution," said artist, Biboy Royong in an interview with Earth Island Institute Philippines staff member, Karl Ramirez.

15. 2. CRY OF BALINTAWAK (minimum of 3 paragraphs) 1. What is the importance of the Cry of Rebellion to the Philippine Independence? 2. What is the desire of the Filipinos who participated in the Cry of Balintawak? 3. What lesson can be learned from the event Cry of Balintawak? Explain.​


Answer:

The Cry of Balintawak, also known as the Cry of Pugad Lawin, was a significant event in Philippine history that marked the beginning of the Philippine Revolution against Spanish colonization. It occurred on August 23, 1896, when a group of Filipino revolutionaries, led by Andres Bonifacio, tore their cedulas or tax certificates and raised the Philippine flag in Balintawak, a town in present-day Caloocan City.

The Cry of Balintawak is important because it symbolized the Filipinos' desire for freedom and independence from Spanish colonial rule. It sparked a wave of revolution and resistance across the country, with Filipinos from different regions joining the cause and fighting for their rights and sovereignty. It also led to the formation of the Katipunan, a secret revolutionary society that aimed to overthrow Spanish colonial rule and establish an independent Philippine republic.

The Filipinos who participated in the Cry of Balintawak desired to end the centuries-long oppression and exploitation under Spanish colonialism. They wanted to reclaim their rights, culture, and identity, and establish a government that would represent their interests and aspirations. They were willing to sacrifice their lives and engage in armed struggle to achieve their goals and gain freedom from foreign domination.

The Cry of Balintawak teaches us the importance of unity, courage, and perseverance in the face of adversity. It reminds us that freedom and independence are not easily won and require sacrifice and struggle. It also highlights the role of ordinary people in shaping history and fighting for their rights and dignity. As we commemorate the Cry of Balintawak, we honor the bravery and patriotism of our ancestors and recommit ourselves to the pursuit of justice, democracy, and national sovereignty.
