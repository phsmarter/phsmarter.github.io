5. Ano ano ang pagkakaiba ng apat na bangko, tulad ng COMMERCIAL BANK,RURAL BANK,THRIFT BANK at SPECIAL BANK?


Pagkakaiba ng Apat na Bangko: Commercial Bank, Rural Bank, Thrift Bank at Special Bank

Ang bangko ay isang institusyon na namamahala ng salapi. Ito ang pangunahing inaasahan ng pamahalaan sa pagsusuplay at pagsasalin-salin ng salapi sa ekonomiya.

Mga Uri Ng Bangko

Ang bangko ay may apat na uri. Narito ang mga uri at pagkakaiba ng mga ito:

Commercial Bank

Bangkong KomersyalIto ay tumatanggap ng lahat ng uri ng deposito ng publiko gaya na lang ng savings deposit, time deposit at deman deposit sa pamamagitan ng paggamit ng tseke. Ito rin ay nagpapautang ng puhunan, housing loan, auto loan at iba pa. Tinatanggap din dito ang letter of credit, bill of exchange, at iba pang instrumento ng kredit mula sa mga negosyante.

Rural Bank

Bangkong RuralIto naman ang bangko na ang layunin ay tulungan ang mga magsasaka upang magkaroon ng puhunan. 2 million ang kapital na kinakailangan sa pagtatayo ng ng ganitong uri ng bangko.

Thrift Bank

Bangko ng PagtitipidIto ay kilala sa tawag na savings bank. Ito ang humihikayat sa mamamayan na mag-impok. Ito ay may tatlong sekta: savings at loan association - ang gawain nito ay magpahiram ng salapi; private development bank - tumatanggap ng deposito; savings and mortgage bank - nanghihikayat din na mag-impok at tumatanggap ng sangla.

Special Bank

Espesyal na BangkoIto naman ang tumutulong sa pamahalaan na mapaunlad ang ekonomiya.Halimbawa nito ay Land Bank at Development Bank.

13. Define and differentiate commercial banks from investment banks (mention their roles as "banks").​


Answer:

Explanation:

The term commercial bank refers to a financial institution that accepts deposits, offers checking account services, makes various loans, and offers basic financial products like certificates of deposit (CDs) and savings accounts to individuals and small businesses. while investment bank is a financial services company or corporate division that engages in advisory-based financial transactions on behalf of individuals, corporations, and governments. Traditionally associated with corporate finance, such a bank might assist in raising financial capital by underwriting or acting as the client's agent in the issuance of securities. An investment bank may also assist companies involved in mergers and acquisitions (M&A) and provide ancillary services such as market making, trading of derivatives and equity securities, and FICC services (fixed income instruments, currencies, and commodities).

15. What are the present powers of the commercial bank?​


Answer:

Commercial banks have several powers in the present day, including:

Accepting deposits: Commercial banks are authorized to accept various types of deposits, such as savings deposits, current deposits, and fixed deposits, from individuals, businesses, and other organizations.

Lending money: Commercial banks are authorized to lend money to individuals, businesses, and other organizations in the form of loans, overdrafts, and credit facilities. This is a key function of commercial banks and generates income for the bank in the form of interest on the loans.

Issuing credit and debit cards: Commercial banks issue credit and debit cards to their customers, which can be used for various transactions, both online and offline.

Providing investment and insurance products: Many commercial banks offer investment and insurance products, such as mutual funds, stocks, bonds, and life insurance policies, to their customers.

Offering foreign exchange services: Commercial banks provide foreign exchange services, such as currency exchange and remittance services, to their customers.

Providing online and mobile banking services: Commercial banks offer online and mobile banking services that allow customers to access their accounts, transfer money, pay bills, and perform other transactions using their computer or mobile device.

Serving as custodian and trustee: Commercial banks serve as custodians and trustees for various assets, such as securities, mutual funds, and pension funds, on behalf of their customers.

Overall, commercial banks play a vital role in the economy by mobilizing savings and providing credit to individuals and businesses, while also offering a range of other financial services.

Similarities between Rural bank and Commercial bank​

A commercial bank is a bigger bank, mostly with wide scope (from national to international scope). They also wide range of services like deposits, ATM's, withdrawals, bills payment, loans (small businesses, enterprises, corporations etc.) , credit cards, money remittance, insurance and other banking and financing services.  

A rural bank is a smaller bank which services people in remote and small areas. Most of the rural banks has limited services such as loan products (for small businesses), deposits, ATM's, bills payment and money remittance services.
