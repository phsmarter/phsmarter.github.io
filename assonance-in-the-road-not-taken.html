22. I blew the whistle. he threw the ball. (ASSONANCE OR NOT ASSONANCE.)The blue bird flew into the new coop in the school. (ASSONANCE OR NOT ASSONANCE.)​


ASSONANCE

Assocance is the recurrent sound of a vowel or diphothong (combination of vowels) in words of a poem or prose to give it a better poetic effect. The soumd of the vowel needs to be repeated in the same sentence in order for it to quality as assonance.

Not Assonance: I blew the whistle. he threw the ball.

(In this text, you will see that the sound 'oo' is repeated. However, it is not repeated within the same sentence. This does not qualify it to be assonance.)

Assonance: The blue bird flew into the new coop in the school.

(You can see that the sound 'oo' is repeated more than thrice, within the same sentence. As you read it, you get a sense of rhythm to it.)

The thing to keep in mimd is that assonance is different from a rhyme, in the sense that you need ti enunciate the sound of the vowel or diphthong that is repeated to get the desired effect.

26. what is an assonance


Answer:

Assonance is a resemblance in the sounds of words/syllables either between their vowels (e.g., meat, bean) or between their consonants (e.g., keep, cape).

Explanation:

However, assonance between consonants is generally called consonance in American usage. The two types are often combined, as between the words six and switch, in which the vowels are identical, and the consonants are similar but not completely identical. If there is repetition of the same vowel or some similar vowels in literary work, especially in stressed syllables, this may be termed "vowel harmony" in poetry (though linguists have a different definition of "vowel harmony").

A special case of assonance is rhyme, in which the endings of words (generally beginning with the vowel sound of the last stressed syllable) are identical—as in fog and dog or history and mystery. Vocalic assonance is an important element in verse. Assonance occurs more often in verse than in prose; it is used in English-language poetry and is particularly important in Old French, Spanish, and the Celtic languages.
