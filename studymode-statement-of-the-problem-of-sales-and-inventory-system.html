3. What is the difference between the journal entry of sale for cash in periodic and perpetual inventory system?


Answer:

While management of a company may consider whether using periodic or perpetual inventory system—in the aim of getting inventory system that fits into their operation, accountants would need to be familiar with inventory journal entries in both periodic and perpetual system. The journal entries for the perpetual inventory system should seem familiar to all of you—as perpetual system has been assumed in all other posts in this site (except a discussion in the periodic system).

The only downside of using perpetual inventory system is the costs and investments (dollar and time) that are required to operate and maintain the system—to updating the inventory records each time a purchase and sale are made.

26. Problem 3YVONE Trading provides the following information:December 312020:Net credit sales - P930,750Cost of sales - 576,000Trade receivables - 52,500Inventory - 144,000December 312019:Trade receivables - P48,000Inventory - 117,000


To analyze the financial performance of YVONE Trading, we can calculate some key ratios based on the information provided:

1. Gross Profit Margin = (Net Credit Sales - Cost of Sales) / Net Credit Sales

Gross Profit Margin = (P930,750 - P576,000) / P930,750

Gross Profit Margin = 38%

2. Inventory Turnover Ratio = Cost of Sales / Average Inventory

Average Inventory = (Inventory at Dec 31, 2020 + Inventory at Dec 31, 2019) / 2

Average Inventory = (P144,000 + P117,000) / 2

Average Inventory = P130,500

Inventory Turnover Ratio = P576,000 / P130,500

Inventory Turnover Ratio = 4.41 times

3. Days Sales Outstanding (DSO) = (Trade Receivables / Net Credit Sales) x 365

DSO = (P52,500 / P930,750) x 365

DSO = 20.6 days

The Gross Profit Margin of 38% indicates that the company is able to generate a reasonable profit on its sales. The Inventory Turnover Ratio of 4.41 times suggests that the company is able to sell its inventory relatively quickly, which is a positive sign. The DSO of 20.6 days indicates that the company is able to collect its receivables in a reasonable amount of time.

However, it is difficult to draw any definitive conclusions about the financial performance of YVONE Trading without additional information, such as its operating expenses, net income, and cash flow.
