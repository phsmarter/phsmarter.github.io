Arranged marriage

Claim of Fact: Arranged marriages are a common practice in some cultures and countries.

Claims of Value: Some people believe that arranged marriages can strengthen family and community ties, while others believe that they are a form of coercion and lack individual freedom and agency.

Claims of Policy: Policymakers could consider whether to allow or restrict arranged marriages, or whether to provide support and resources for individuals entering into arranged marriages.

School Uniform

Claim of Fact: Many schools require students to wear uniforms.

Claims of Value: Some people believe that school uniforms promote a sense of unity and equality among students, while others believe that they restrict self-expression and individuality.

Claims of Policy: Policymakers could consider whether to implement or abolish school uniform policies, or whether to allow for flexibility and choice within the uniform guidelines.

Child labor

Claim of Fact: Child labor refers to the employment of children in work that is inappropriate for their age or that may interfere with their education.

Claims of Value: Some people believe that child labor is necessary for the survival of families in poverty, while others believe that it is a form of exploitation and abuse that undermines children's rights and well-being.

Claims of Policy: Policymakers could consider measures to eliminate child labor, such as setting minimum age requirements for employment and enforcing labor laws, or providing support and resources for families to lift themselves out of poverty.

War on Drugs

Claim of Fact: The War on Drugs is a term used to describe the efforts by governments and law enforcement agencies to combat drug trafficking and drug abuse.

Claims of Value: Some people believe that the War on Drugs is necessary to protect public health and safety, while others believe that it has led to mass incarceration and disproportionately affected marginalized communities.

Claims of Policy: Policymakers could consider alternative approaches to drug policy, such as decriminalization or legalization, or implementing harm reduction measures to address drug use as a public health issue rather than a criminal justice issue.

Minor Curfew

Claim of Fact: A minor curfew is a law that prohibits minors (people under the age of 18) from being in public or certain places during certain hours.

Claims of Value: Some people believe that minor curfews are necessary to protect minors from danger and to promote responsible behavior, while others believe that they unfairly restrict the freedom and autonomy of minors and disproportionately affect marginalized communities.

Claims of Policy: Policymakers could consider whether to implement or abolish minor curfew laws, or whether to provide exceptions or alternatives for minors who have legitimate reasons to be out during curfew hours.

25. ebate topic DISAGREE OF WEARING SCHOOL UNIFORMS CLAIM (1MIN)EVIDENCE (1 MINUTES​


Answer:

CLAIM: I disagree with the idea of wearing school uniforms because it restricts students' freedom of expression and can create financial burdens for families.

EVIDENCE: The first reason I disagree with school uniforms is that they limit students' freedom of expression. Students should be able to express themselves through their clothing, and uniforms do not allow for this. It's important for young people to have the ability to express their individuality and creativity, which can help to develop their personalities and boost their self-esteem.

Additionally, school uniforms can create financial burdens for families, especially those with multiple children. Purchasing multiple sets of uniforms for each child can be expensive, and some families may struggle to afford them. This can create a sense of inequality between students, as those who cannot afford the uniforms may feel excluded or ashamed.

Furthermore, enforcing school uniforms can lead to a lack of diversity and inclusivity within the school community. It can stifle the expression of cultural or religious identities, as uniforms may not allow for certain clothing choices that are important to some families.

In summary, while some may argue that school uniforms promote a sense of unity and discipline, I believe that they ultimately limit students' freedom of expression, create financial burdens, and can negatively impact inclusivity within the school community.
