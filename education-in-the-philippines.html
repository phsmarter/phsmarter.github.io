3. PHILIPPINE EDUCATION SYSTEM -- HISTORY --PRIMARY EDUCATION --SECONDARY EDUCATION --TERTIARY EDUCATION​


Answer:

The Philippine education system has a long and unique history, beginning with the primary, secondary, and tertiary levels. From early colonization to the present day, numerous factors have influenced the development of education in the country. At the primary level, compulsory education was introduced by Spanish colonizers in 1863. This laid the foundation for a system designed to create literate citizens, who could then progress to higher levels of education. Secondary schooling soon followed with technical and vocational programs being offered as well as academic curricula. This led to increased enrolment in universities and colleges across the nation at the tertiary level, where students can further specialize their studies and pursue professional careers.The Philippine education system is divided into three parts: primary, secondary, and tertiary education.

Primary education in the Philippines starts at the age of six and lasts for six years. Students spend three years in elementary school and three years in junior high school. During this time, they learn basic academic subjects such as math, science, English, and Filipino.

Secondary education in the Philippines begins at the age of 12 and lasts for four years. Students spend two years in high school and two years in senior high school. During this time, they focus on more specialized academic subjects and prepare for college entrance exams.

Tertiary education in the Philippines typically begins at the age of 18 and lasts for four years. Students can either attend a university or a vocational/technical school. At a university, they can choose to study a specific academic discipline or pursue a general course of study. Vocational/technical schools provide training in specific trades such as carpentry, plumbing, or welding.

Answer:

1. Effects of Educational Social Problems in the Philippines: - High drop-out rates among students - Poor quality of education due to lack of resources and facilities - Limited access to education, especially for children from poor families - Inadequate training and support for teachers - Unequal distribution of educational opportunities across regions and social classes - Limited job opportunities for graduates, leading to unemployment and underemployment - Brain drain as highly skilled professionals seek better opportunities abroad - Limited innovation and economic growth due to lack of skilled workers

2. Solutions of Educational Social Problems in the Philippines: - Increase government funding for education to improve resources and facilities - Provide scholarships and financial assistance to students from poor families - Improve teacher training and support to enhance teaching quality - Address regional and social inequalities in educational opportunities - Encourage private sector investment in education - Promote technical and vocational education to address unemployment and underemployment - Enhance research and development in education to promote innovation and economic growth

3. Causes of Drugs in the Philippines: - Poverty and lack of economic opportunities - Weak law enforcement and corruption - Accessibility of drugs due to porous borders and inadequate border control - Influence of drug syndicates and organized crime - Peer pressure and social acceptance of drug use - Lack of awareness and education on the dangers of drug use - Mental health issues and trauma

4. Effects of Drugs in the Philippines: - Increased crime rates and violence - Health problems and addiction - Social disintegration and breakdown of families - Economic losses due to decreased productivity and increased healthcare costs - Stigmatization and discrimination against drug users - Undermining of national security and sovereignty

5. Solutions of Drugs in the Philippines: - Strengthen law enforcement and border control measures - Address poverty and provide economic opportunities for vulnerable communities - Increase awareness and education on the dangers of drug use - Provide access to mental health services and trauma support - Enhance rehabilitation and treatment programs for drug users - Address corruption and organized crime - Promote community-based approaches to drug prevention and rehabilitation.

21. How will science education help the development of education in the Philippines?


Answer:

Science education can play a crucial role in the development of education in the Philippines in several ways:

Improving critical thinking skills: Science education focuses on teaching students how to ask questions, gather evidence, and form conclusions. This approach helps students develop critical thinking skills that can be applied in other subjects as well.

Preparing students for future careers: Science is a rapidly evolving field that is increasingly becoming more important in our technological world. By providing students with a strong foundation in science, we can prepare them for careers in fields such as medicine, engineering, and technology.

Promoting technological advancements: Science education can foster technological advancements by providing students with the knowledge and skills to create new technologies and solve problems. This can lead to improved living standards and economic growth.

Encouraging environmental awareness: Science education can help students understand the impact of human activities on the environment and promote environmental awareness. This can lead to more environmentally conscious decisions and behaviors.

Improving scientific literacy: Science education can improve scientific literacy among the general population, which is essential for informed decision making and active participation in the development of science and technology.

In conclusion, science education has the potential to play a major role in the development of education in the Philippines by promoting critical thinking skills, preparing students for future careers, fostering technological advancements, encouraging environmental awareness, and improving scientific literacy.
