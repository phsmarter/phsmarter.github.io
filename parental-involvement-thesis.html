1. Identifying the Impacts of Parental Involvement in the Academic AchievementIntroduction for thesisplease help​


Answer:

Parental involvement in the academic achievement of children has been a topic of interest for researchers and educators for many years. The role of parents in their child's education has been found to be crucial in shaping their academic success. Parental involvement can take many forms, such as helping with homework, attending parent-teacher conferences, volunteering at school, and providing emotional support.

The purpose of this thesis is to identify the impacts of parental involvement on the academic achievement of children. The study will examine the relationship between parental involvement and academic achievement, as well as the different types of parental involvement and their effects on academic performance.

The research will be conducted through a literature review of previous studies on parental involvement and academic achievement. The review will analyze the findings of various studies and identify the common themes and patterns that emerge.

The results of this study will provide valuable insights into the importance of parental involvement in the academic success of children. The findings will be useful for educators, parents, and policymakers in developing strategies to improve parental involvement in education and enhance the academic achievement of children.

21. Difference between thesis and thesis statement


Answer:

Your thesis is the main idea, point, incident, or argument of your paper. That is in your mind.

Your thesis statement is that idea expressed in a sentence. An important caveat: this sentence must conform to the class or the reviewer’s (professor’s) requirement. If there is a specific form required, that should have been explained to you and taught to you before you have to write it. In one of my classes, stdents were required to create thesis statements, bring them to class, and have the critiqued in class discussions. We also studied examples of both good and bad thesis statements from published papers.

Thesis — idea.

Thesis statement — a sentence giving the thesis (idea) which conforms to the established form stating that idea.
