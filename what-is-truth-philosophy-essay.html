19. example of personal domain of truth in Philosophy​


Answer:

Here’s a list of personal domains of truth:

Your body: in any given moment, it cannot be anything other than exactly what it is.

Your moment: only you can inhabit each moment within which you exist.

Your emotions: no one can have exactly the same emotions as you, and your emotions, though they are ever-changing, are undeniably and irrefutably present as you live.

Your thoughts: whatever you’re thinking is a real event unique to you.

Your sensory experiences: no one else can experience exactly what you see, hear, feel, touch, and smell, and whatever they are, your sensory opportunities present themselves to you in some form whenever you pay attention to them, unless you’ve suffered the loss or absence of one of them.

Your memories: they may not be accurate, or even real, but you have them, and they’re uniquely and truthfully yours.

Your relationships: you interact with other objects and beings, real and imagined, and each interaction occurs uniquely for you in a unique intersection of time and space.

Your ancestors’ circumstances and choices: you’re here because of those who came before you and who lived and procreated in exactly the way that led to your creation through your mother and father.

The universe: it takes the entire universe to create and sustain the conditions that allow your unique existence.

Your death: it’ll be yours and yours alone, and it will happen.

29. 3 domains of truth in philosophy


Domains of truth in Philosophy

The following are the three main domains of truths that we have in Philosophy:

First one is the Objective domain - this refers to the truths that are established based on abstracted theories. This includes scientific theories or mathematical theories that are currently used as a fundamental basis in establishing other theories The second one is the Social domain - this may be the result of the agreement between a society that has been accepted over time. This does not necessarily mean that something is true but rather, this is about a truth that has been socially agreed upon as time passed by.  The last one is the Personal domain - this refers to the truths that are biased towards the person who established it. It may be based in the experience or theories that a person has. The authenticity and validity of this truth relies on the person themselves
