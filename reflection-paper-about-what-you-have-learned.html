Explanation:

Based on the information I know, the evolution of media has played a significant role in shaping the values and norms of society. Media has the power to influence people's way of thinking, behavior, and beliefs, which ultimately affects the culture and society as a whole.

In the Philippines, the influence of media has been prevalent since the introduction of television and radio in the 1950s. It has rapidly changed the way Filipinos consume information, entertainment, and news. With the rise of social media and the internet, the influence of media has become more pervasive and directly affects how Filipinos view different issues.

The values and norms of Filipinos have been shaped by media in ways we don't often realize. For instance, media has portrayed an ideal standard of beauty, which promotes westernized standards, and has led to the normalization of skin-whitening and plastic surgery. Media also reinforces gender roles and stereotypes, perpetuating the notion that women should be submissive and inferior to men.

Moreover, media has the power to influence people's political views and opinions. The recent 2016 Philippine presidential election is an excellent example of how media can sway public opinion through fake news that influences people's choices.

In conclusion, media plays a significant role in shaping the values and norms of a society. It has the power to influence people's behavior, thoughts, and beliefs which ultimately reflects cultural patterns. It is essential to be critical of what we consume and the effects it has on our society. We should strive to be informed and critical thinkers to avoid being unwittingly influenced by media.

The importance of food preservation and some common learnings, feelings, and difficulties associated with the experience.

a. Food preservation can be an important learning experience as it teaches us the value of preserving food for long-term storage and consumption. Through food preservation, we can reduce food waste and save money by extending the shelf life of perishable items. One important learning from food preservation is the different methods that can be used to preserve food, such as canning, freezing, dehydrating, and pickling, and how each method works to prevent spoilage and decay.

b. Food preservation can be a rewarding and satisfying experience as it allows us to take control of our food supply and reduce our reliance on store-bought, processed foods. 19. Reflection Paper - What did you learned about the E-Government & E-Governance lesson- Any flashes of inspiration you have had;What you find puzzling, difficult or contradictory; andWhat new knowledge, skills or understanding have you gained during the process of writing your learning journal​


Answer:

E-government and e-governance are essential components of digital transformation in public administration. E-government refers to the use of information and communication technologies (ICT) to deliver government services, disseminate information, and engage citizens. E-governance, on the other hand, refers to the broader concept of using ICT to transform government structures, processes, and relationships with citizens to enhance transparency, accountability, and participation.

During the lesson, I learned that the adoption of e-government and e-governance has several benefits, including improved efficiency, transparency, and accountability. For instance, e-government can reduce administrative costs, enhance service delivery, and promote citizen engagement. E-governance, on the other hand, can enhance transparency and accountability by enabling citizens to access government information and participate in decision-making processes.

However, the implementation of e-government and e-governance also poses some challenges, such as privacy and security concerns, digital divide, and limited institutional capacity. Privacy and security concerns arise from the collection and storage of personal data, which can be misused if not adequately protected. The digital divide is a concern because not all citizens have equal access to digital technologies, which can limit their ability to participate in e-government and e-governance initiatives. Finally, limited institutional capacity can affect the success of e-government and e-governance initiatives, as it requires skilled personnel, appropriate infrastructure, and strong political will.

In summary, e-government and e-governance are crucial components of digital transformation in public administration. They have the potential to enhance efficiency, transparency, and accountability. However, their implementation poses several challenges that must be addressed to ensure their success.
