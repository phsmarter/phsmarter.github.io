8. Students from GTMNHS had a field trip. From the school ground, they travel 47 km East going to Alabang. Afterwards, they travel 75 km South going to Tagaytay. a. What was the total distance travelled by the students?b. Determine the total displacement from the starting point.​


Answer:

To determine the total distance traveled by the students, we need to add the distances traveled in each leg of the trip:

Distance traveled from the school ground to Alabang = 47 km

Distance traveled from Alabang to Tagaytay = 75 km

Total distance traveled = 47 km + 75 km = 122 km

Therefore, the total distance traveled by the students on their field trip is 122 kilometers.

To determine the total displacement from the starting point, we need to consider the straight-line distance between the starting point and the final destination. We can use the Pythagorean theorem to calculate the displacement.

Using the distances traveled in the East and South directions as the two sides of a right triangle, the displacement (D) can be calculated as follows:

D^2 = (47 km)^2 + (75 km)^2

D^2 = 2209 km^2 + 5625 km^2

D^2 = 7834 km^2

Taking the square root of both sides, we get:

D = √7834 km^2

D ≈ 88.47 km

Therefore, the total displacement from the starting point is approximately 88.47 kilometers.

Answer:

Explanation:

In order to maintain balanced sodium levels, the body naturally bonds excess salt with water and excretes it with urine. So the logical conclusion: More salt means the body needs more water, so salty food makes you thirsty. Salt may also temporarily increase your body weight by causing you to retain water to balance out said sodium levels, showing up on the scale as extra pounds.
