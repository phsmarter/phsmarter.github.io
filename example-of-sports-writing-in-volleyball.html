Basic Volleyball Rules for Playing the Game

6 players on a team, 3 on the front row and 3 on the back row

Maximum of three hits per side

Player may not hit the ball twice in succession (A block is not considered a hit)

Ball may be played off the net during a volley and on a serve

A ball hitting a boundary line is "in"

A ball is "out" if it hits... an antennae, the floor completely outside the court, any of the net or cables outside the antennae, the referee stand or pole, the ceiling above a non-playable area

It is legal to contact the ball with any part of a players body

It is illegal to catch, hold, or throw the ball

If two or more players contact the ball at the same time, it is considered one play and either player involved may make the next contact (provided the next contact isn't the teams 4th hit)

A player can not block or attack a serve from on or inside the 10 foot line

After the serve, front line players may switch positions at the net

At higher competition, the officiating crew may be made up of two refs, line judges, scorer, and an assistant scorer.

Creamline Cool Smashers Sweeps Choco Mucho Flying Titans in PVL Action

In a highly anticipated matchup, the Creamline Cool Smashers emerged victorious over the Choco Mucho Flying Titans in a thrilling straight-set win in the Premier Volleyball League (PVL) on Saturday.

The Cool Smashers' firepower was on full display as they dominated the match from start to finish, outscoring their opponents 25-20, 25-18, and 25-22.

Alyssa Valdez led the way for Creamline with 21 points, while Michelle Gumabao and Jia Morado chipped in with 13 and 10 points, respectively. The Cool Smashers' defense was also rock-solid, limiting Choco Mucho's top scorer Kat Tolentino to just nine points.

On the other hand, Choco Mucho struggled to find their rhythm, committing several unforced errors that cost them crucial points throughout the game. Despite the efforts of Maddie Madayag and Gyra Barroga, who each scored 11 points, the Flying Titans failed to mount a comeback and suffered their second loss in the tournament.

The win moves the Cool Smashers to the top of the standings with a perfect 2-0 record, while Choco Mucho falls to 1-2.

Creamline head coach Tai Bundit expressed satisfaction with his team's performance but also highlighted the need for improvement in their upcoming games. "We played well today, but there's always room for improvement. We'll continue to work hard and prepare for our next game," he said in a post-game interview.

Choco Mucho coach Oliver Almadro, meanwhile, rued his team's missed opportunities and vowed to bounce back in their next game. "We have to learn from this loss and come back stronger next time. We still have a long way to go in this tournament, and we'll continue to work hard," he said.
