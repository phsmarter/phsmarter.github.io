Answer:

The importance of free education for me is that it allows individuals to access higher education regardless of their socio-economic status. This can lead to increased opportunities for personal and professional growth and can help to reduce inequality within our society.

The advantages of free education include increased access to education for individuals who may not have been able to afford it otherwise, as well as the potential for a more educated and skilled workforce. Additionally, free education can help to reduce the burden of student debt and improve social mobility.

However, there are also drawbacks to free education. One potential disadvantage is that it may lead to overcrowding in classrooms and a decrease in the quality of education. Additionally, free education may put a strain on public funds and could result in a decrease in funding for other public services.

In my opinion, free education in college does not necessarily decrease the quality of education. However, it is important for schools and universities to ensure that they have sufficient resources and support to provide high-quality education to all students.

Free education can benefit our society by increasing access to higher education and providing individuals with the skills and knowledge they need to succeed in the workforce. This can lead to a more educated and skilled workforce, which can in turn drive economic growth and development.

20. compose an argumentative essay about the advantages and disadvantages of technology in education today.​


Advantages

Modern technology is mostly seen as a blessing for learners. The widespread use of mobile devices and the internet have made learning easier.

Here are a few advantages of modern technology in education:

• Independent learning has become easier and more effective

The internet can provide you with information in any field. No matter what you need to know, you can easily find that online. You can not always trust the data and the source, but you can still use them as educational resources. Students can use the internet without the assistance of their teachers or parents.

• Good preparations for the future

It is pretty obvious that learning in the future will be technology-based. Students who are using technology now will find it easy to compete in the future. That is why students should be encouraged to collaborate and communicate using digital technology.

• Modern technology has lowered tuition and textbook prices

More resources are available now, and those resources are easily accessible. Students are now less dependent on textbooks, and as a result, the cost of textbooks has decreased. Many learners are not buying physical textbooks. Rather, they are now using e-books.

and etc.

Disadvantages

It is also important to remember that modern technology is not an unmixed blessing for students. The main allegation against digital devices is that these devices are making students lazy. Now we are going to discuss the disadvantages of modern technology.

• Lack of interest in hard work

Due to the availability of all types of information online, many students are now becoming lazy. They no longer want to study the hard way. Some students even do not attend classes, mistakenly thinking that everything can be found online. They think that teachers are no longer necessary.

• Misuse of technology

Mobile devices, computers, and other technologies are usually seen as tools of entertainment, not of education. Textbooks are known as just tools for learning. This is the teaching of consumerism, and it has a negative effect on students.

• Instructional challenges

Professors and teacher, if not trained regularly, often fail to instruct their students. Teachers who are used to teach in traditional methods sometimes cannot stay abreast with technology. Some teachers shun technology because they do not find it convenient to use technology.

22. advantages of informal education​


Answer:

Advantages of Informal Education:

Learning is more focused, as it is fully driven by the learner. Learning is more fulfilling –As adult learners, we want autonomy and control. With informal learning, we are in full control of what we are exploring and learning. Unlike formal learning, which can often become a bitter pill, informal learning is more fun and happens at our pace, based on our interest. We can begin without any significant learning plan. Today, we have access to so much information, particularly online, that can be used to continuously acquire new learning. Without the pressure of having to clear a test, get a decent score, and perform (show its application on the job), we find it easier to dive into and sustain the momentum of Informal learning. On the other hand, a colleague or a mentor would also find it a lot easier to participate in an impromptu discussion of knowledge share. In the case of formal training, they will need to prepare in advance, schedule the session, ensure the target profile turns up, and so on.
