3. How is phenolphthalein used as an indicator?


Answer:

Phenolphthalein is a colourless, weak acid that is widely used as an indicator in titration experiments to indicate the endpoint of the titration. The endpoint is indicated by the formation of a pink colour since this compound dissociates to form pink anions when dissolved in water.

Therefore, the answer is (a) Acid.

Further explanation

Indicator is a compound used as a marker that the solution is acidic, basic or neutral. In the titration can be divided into references that the titration process has reached the end point / equivalent point.

Each indicator has a specific pH range except the litmus indicator to determine the acid-base solution or universal indicator that has a pH range of 0-14.

Red and blue litmus, including indicators that can show the acidic nature of the solution

Red litmus in acidic solutions is red and bases are blue and neutral are red.

Blue litmus in acidic solutions are red and bases are blue and neutral are blue.

The phenolphthalein indicator changes color from colorless in acidic solutions to pink in alkaline solutions. The pH range is between 8.3 - 10. under 8.3 colorless, above that , the color becomes pink-red.

Litmus paper is not able to determine the pH but only distinguishes acidic or basic solutions

Litmus cannot distinguish pH 1.2 or 3 because everything is red.

To be able to estimate the pH value, several indicators / combination of indicators can be used.

pH 11 means that the pH is a base solution (alkaline) and above 8.3 (above the phenolphtalein pH range) the indicator will show pink-red
