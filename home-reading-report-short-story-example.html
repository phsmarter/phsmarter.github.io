Author: J.D. Salinger

Setting: The novel is set in the 1950s, primarily in New York City and briefly in California.

Characters:

The protagonist of the novel is Holden Caulfield, a sixteen-year-old boy who has been expelled from his prep school for failing grades. He is an unreliable narrator, as he often lies or exaggerates to make himself look better. Other characters include Holden's younger sister Phoebe, his friend Ackley, and his love interest Sally Hayes.

Plot: The novel follows Holden Caulfield as he leaves his prep school and embarks on a journey through New York City. Along the way, he meets various people and experiences a range of emotions, from loneliness to joy. He visits museums, bars, and other places of interest in the city before eventually deciding to go home to his family. On the way home, he stops at a carousel and reflects on life before finally returning home to face his parents' disappointment.

30. Read an English short story ang make a story report about it.Pa help po​


Title of the Story

The Cask of Amontillado

Author of the Story

Edgar Allan Poe

Setting of the Story

The Cask of Amontillado takes place in Italy during Carnevale: a festive time in the country similar to Mardi Gras in the United States.

Characters

The Protagonist is Montresor and the Antagonist is Fortunato, the motive is Montresor does not like Fortunato and wants to kill him. The Antagonist (Fortunato) is a cool guy and wanted to have fun in the carnival, The Protagonist (Montresor) seeked for revenge.

Theme

The major theme of “The Cask of Amontillado” is revenge.

Thematic Statement

Montreseur is motivated by his hate to take revenge on Fortunato, after Fortunato greatly insulted his family and caused him “a thousand injuries” (Poe)

Point of view of the story

The short story “The Cask of Amontillado” by Edgar Allan Poe is a first-person narration, told from the perspective of the main character, Montresor.

Plot of the Story

Beginning

''The Cask of Amontillado'' begins with the narrator Montresor explaining that a man called Fortunato has wronged him a thousand times over, but his insult is the final blow that has provoked his vow to revenge.

Climax

The climax of the story is when Montresor chains Fortunato to the wall of the catacombs. Montresor then ignores the screams of Fortunato for mercy. This is a picture of Fortunato being chained to the wall, and a wall being built in front of him.

Ending

At the end of the story, the narrator reveals that 50 years have passed since he took revenge and Fortunato's body has not been disturbed. Scholars have noted that Montresor's reasons for revenge are unclear and that he may simply be insane.

Lesson

Revenge is a central theme in "The Cask of Amontillado". Montresor feels very disrespected when Fortunato insults him and, as a result of that, Montresor vows revenge: “The thousand injuries of Fortunato I had borned as best I could; but when he ventured upon insult, I vowed revenge.”
