3. "What are some of the practices of the Filipino youth during their period that is not observed now a days (already a taboo)?​


1. Wearing of the traditional Baro't Saya. This traditional dress was worn by young women during special occasions such as weddings and festivals. It is no longer commonly seen today.

2. Playing traditional Filipino games such as Patintero, Tumbang Preso, and Luksong Tinik. These games were popular among Filipino youth in the past but are not as widely played today.

3. Visiting relatives during the summer months to celebrate fiestas and other special occasions. This was a common practice in the past but is not as common today due to increased mobility and work commitments of modern Filipino youth.

4. Participating in religious activities such as novenas and processions during Holy Week or Christmas season. This was a popular practice among Filipino youth in the past but is not observed as much today due to changing religious beliefs and practices among younger generations.

8. How do you describe the Filipino Youth of today the way Jose Rizal described them before?​


Rizal saw the many injustices suffered by his fellow Filipinos: they depended on the religious corporations or on big landowners, for land to till, or for their living; people were afraid of airing their grievances or of talking or protesting against the friars or the government, in short, there was no real freedom of the press or speech. Most Filipinos lacked the privilege of education, and its resultant benefits, or if they did have education, this was the obscurantist kind generally propagated by the colonialist policy, which not only kept Filipinos in the dark about their rights, but worse, had molded them into an abject, submissive people ignorant or worse, ashamed of their own proud heritage, a heritage that existed even before the arrival of the Spaniards. Finally, Rizal realized that the Philippines had not been consistently represented in the Spanish parliament. For Rizal, this was the root of the absence of justice in the country, or of their being deprived of basic rights.

Answer:

It is difficult to make a blanket statement about the attitudes of all Filipino youth, as people of different ages, backgrounds, and experiences may have varying perspectives. However, it is important to consider the factors that may influence the attitudes and behaviors of young people in the Philippines.

One factor that may contribute to a perception of apathy among some Filipino youth is the lack of opportunities and resources. For example, limited access to education and employment opportunities may leave some young people feeling disheartened and disconnected from the world around them.

Another factor to consider is the level of political and social engagement in the country. In some cases, young people may feel disillusioned with the political system or feel that their voices are not heard, leading to a sense of apathy.

On the other hand, it is also important to recognize that there are many Filipino youth who are actively engaged in their communities and are working towards positive change. It is important not to stereotype or generalize the attitudes of all young people in the Philippines.
