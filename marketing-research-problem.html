1. example of research problem in marketing​


Companies and other organizations use marketing research to manage the risks associated with offering new products and services. These organizations don't want to spend too much money developing a product line that research indicates will be unsuccessful. A well-designed and well-executed marketing study can go a long way towards identifying consumer tastes and demographic preferences to help with a product launch. However, several common types of problems occur with market research that can make it overly costly and produce results of questionable value for the organization.

2. Learning Task 1: Being a future entrepreneur, explain the meaning of the following con- cepts. 1. Market Problem 2. Problem Identification Research 3. Market Segmentation 4. Pricing Research 5. Market Share​


Answer:

Market Problem: A market problem refers to a challenge or opportunity that exists in the market that can be addressed by a product or service. It is a gap between what customers want and what is currently available in the market. A market problem can be identified through research, customer feedback, or by observing trends in the market.

Problem Identification Research: Problem identification research is a process of identifying market problems by conducting research to gather information about customers' needs, preferences, and behaviors. It involves analyzing data from various sources such as customer surveys, focus groups, and market trends to identify gaps or opportunities in the market.

Market Segmentation: Market segmentation is the process of dividing a market into distinct groups of consumers who have similar needs, characteristics, or behaviors. It is a critical step in developing a marketing strategy as it helps to identify the target market for a product or service and tailor the marketing efforts accordingly.

Pricing Research: Pricing research is a process of determining the optimal price for a product or service. It involves gathering information about customers' willingness to pay, competitors' pricing strategies, and the costs associated with producing and delivering the product or service.

Market Share: Market share refers to the percentage of total sales in a particular market that a company or a product/service has. It is a measure of a company's competitive position in the market and is often used to compare the performance of different companies or products in the same industry. A higher market share indicates a stronger position in the market.

1. Poor product quality: Obviously, a product, which is of poor quality, cannot be sold in the market.

2. Higher price: Another reason for the failure of certain products is the price factor. Higher production and distribution costs may lead to higher price. Such a product cannot be sold in a market consisting of middle and lower income buyers.

25. Why is problem definition the most important step in the marketing research process?​


Answer:

Problem definition is the most important step in the marketing research process because it sets the direction and scope of the research. It involves clearly identifying the problem or opportunity that the research is intended to address, and defining the research objectives and questions that will guide the research process. If the problem is not clearly defined or if the research objectives are not well-defined, the research may not yield accurate or useful results. Additionally, a well-defined problem helps researchers to identify the appropriate research methods, data collection techniques, and analysis tools to use to gather and interpret data. Therefore, problem definition is the foundation of effective marketing research and critical to making informed business decisions.

The problem definition stage is critical to the success of the research process because "a problem well defined is a problem half-solved." If we are able to clearly identify the research problem, we increase the chances of collecting the necessary information to solve the problem.
