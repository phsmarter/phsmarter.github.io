2. concept buliding essay writing example‚Äã


answer:Concept essays provide a chance to explore ideas you might previously have taken for granted. Writing a concept essay requires careful exploration of a concept, a concise and interesting thesis and a strong overall structure. Before you begin to write, it may be helpful to engage in some prewriting. Word webs, outlines and free writing can help you uncover insights about a topic you might not realize at first. After prewriting, develop a working thesis, an interesting introduction, body paragraphs and a conclusion.

Thesis

Your working thesis should define a concept you will develop throughout the essay. Allow your working thesis to change as you explore the concept instead of trying to build an essay around a definition that you no longer believe. Though a bit persuasive by nature, a concept essay thesis should not be an argument. Instead, a concept essay thesis should provide one well-reasoned definition among many, because the meaning of a concept can be difficult, if not impossible, to define through objective reasoning. A clear definition of a concept also can allow you to explore it in various contexts, whereas a vague definition might leave you unsure of why the concept matters. Finalize your thesis once most of the paper has been written

Introduction

Strong introductions should show why a concept matters in real life and how your essay will explore the connection between the concept and human experiences. If the concept is ‚Äúsuccess,‚Äù an introduction might provide an anecdote in which success was an important part of your life or some world event, such as the Olympics. Narrative introductions often work for positive concepts such as ‚Äúhappiness,‚Äù while more objective introductions work best for somber concepts such as ‚Äúwar.‚Äù

Body

Develop body paragraphs that explore important aspects of the concept. Because concepts often are more complex than short college essays can fully account for, choose the most illuminating aspects or those with the most relevance to you and your audience. For example, if the concept of an essay is ‚Äúsuccess,‚Äù body paragraphs might discuss the differences between professional, cultural and personal success. Use a strong topic sentence to indicate the purpose of each body paragraph, and connect all of your body paragraphs to expand your definition of the concept.

One function of education is to promote socialization, which refers to the process of learning social norms, values, beliefs, and behaviors necessary for individuals to function effectively in society. Through socialization, individuals learn to interact with others, develop a sense of identity, and acquire the necessary skills and knowledge to participate in social institutions.

An example of socialization in education is the school system. In school, students learn to interact with their peers and teachers, follow rules and procedures, and develop a sense of responsibility and accountability. They also learn about the values and beliefs of their society, such as honesty, respect, and cooperation.

Moreover, socialization through education helps individuals develop critical thinking skills and the ability to make informed decisions. By learning about different perspectives and viewpoints, individuals can understand and appreciate diversity, which is crucial in today's globalized world.

In summary, the function of education in promoting socialization is important in shaping individuals' personal and social development. It helps them to become productive members of society and prepares them to face the challenges of the future.
