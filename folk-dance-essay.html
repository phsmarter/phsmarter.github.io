1. Essay about folk dancing


A folk dance is developed by people that reflect the life of the people of a certain country or region. Not all ethnic dances are folk dances. For example, ritual dances or dances of ritual origin are not considered to be folk dances. Ritual dances are usually called "Religious dances" because of their purpose. The terms "ethnic" and "traditional" are used when it is required to emphasize the cultural roots of the dance. In this sense, nearly all folk dances are ethnic ones. If some dances, such as polka, cross ethnic boundaries and even cross the boundary between "folk" and "ballroom dance", ethnic differences are often considerable enough to mention.

13. create an ESSAY of Why is it important to learn or to study the Philippine Folk dances? pls answer correctly thankyou! ​


Studying Philippine folk dances is important for various reasons. First and foremost, it is part of preserving the country's rich cultural heritage. The unique characteristics of each folk dance are rooted in Filipino history, customs, and traditions, which have been passed down from generation to generation. As Filipinos, it is essential to have an understanding and appreciation of our cultural identity, and studying folk dances is one way of achieving this.

Furthermore, learning Philippine folk dances can help strengthen physical activity and enhance health and wellness. Traditional dances require a person to engage in physical activity, which can improve balance, coordination, flexibility, and endurance. Moreover, folk dances are often performed in a group setting, which fosters socialization and interaction, leading to increased self-confidence and emotional well-being.

Moreover, knowing these dances can also promote tourism in the country. Foreigners can appreciate and recognize the country's culture when they witness traditional Filipino dance performances. As a result, it can attract more tourists to visit the Philippines, positively impacting the country's economy.

Lastly, Philippine folk dances showcase the depth and beauty of the country's soul and express its diverse cultures, which can promote national pride and unity. Studying these dances can also help in understanding one's community and the shared experiences of one's ancestors.

In summary, learning Philippine folk dances is important because it preserves the country's cultural heritage, promotes physical activity and enhances health and wellness, contributes to tourism, and fosters national pride and unity. Moreover, it also provides opportunities for people to gain a deeper appreciation of Filipino culture and history, allowing them to have a better understanding of their identity as Filipinos.

Give the 3 special classification of folk danceESSAYAs a student, how will you empower and nurture traditional folk dance and ethnic dances in the Philippines.​


Answer:

1. Philippine Folk Dance is a traditional dance form that reflects the distinct cultural heritage and identity of the Filipino people. It encompasses various indigenous artistic expressions, including music, literature, and visual arts.

2. The primary objectives of folk dance are to preserve cultural heritage, promote social cohesion and solidarity, and provide entertainment for the community. Furthermore, it aims to foster national pride and identity, promote physical fitness and well-being, and enhance individuals' self-esteem.

3. The three values of folk dance are cultural identity, diversity, and creativity. It reflects the unique cultural identity of the people of the Philippines, celebrates the country's diversity and promotes creative expressions of the art form.

4. The four factors affecting folk dance are historical context, cultural diversity, socio-economic conditions, and technological advancements.

5. The four classifications of Philippine Folk Dance are tribal or ethnolinguistic dances, rural or country dances, court or palace dances, and religious or ceremonial dances.

6. The two geographical extents of origin are Luzon and Mindanao.

7. The eight nature of folk dance are communal, traditional, expressive, symbolic, celebratory, rhythmic, creative, and participatory. Examples of communal dances are Pandango sa Ilaw and Tinikling. Examples of celebratory dances are Ati-Atihan and Sinulog.

8. The four movements of folk dance are turning, hopping, leaping, and sliding.

9. The three formations of folk dance are circular, square, and straight line.

10. The three special classifications of folk dance are ancient or pre-colonial dances, fusion or modernized dances, and revival or recreation dances.

As a student, I understand the importance of preserving and nurturing traditional folk dance and ethnic dances in the Philippines. These dances are essential in promoting our cultural identity, celebrating our diversity, and enhancing our sense of pride as Filipinos.

To empower and nurture traditional folk dance and ethnic dances, we need to promote and support the preservation of our cultural heritage. This can be done by organizing folk dance festivals and competitions and by providing opportunities for local dancers to showcase their talents.

Moreover, we must also integrate folk dance and ethnic dances in our school curricula and extra-curricular activities. This will expose younger generations to these traditional art forms and promote cultural awareness and understanding.

Finally, we should use technology to digitize traditional folk dance and ethnic dance performances and make them accessible to a broader audience, especially those who are unable to attend traditional performances in person. This can help promote the art form worldwide and create more opportunities for our traditional dancers and artists.

In conclusion, as a student, I recognize the value and importance of promoting and preserving traditional folk dance and ethnic dances in the Philippines. By empowering and nurturing these traditional art forms, we can promote our cultural identity, celebrate our diversity, and enhance our sense of pride as Filipinos.
