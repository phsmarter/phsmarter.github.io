1. Write an Argumentative speech Here is the topic for your argumentative Speech... Title: Compulsory military service is good for society and the country.


Answer:

Ladies and gentlemen,

I stand before you today to make the case for compulsory military service. I firmly believe that such a system is not only good for society and the country, but also for the individuals who serve.

First of all, let's consider the benefits for society and the country. Military service plays a crucial role in the defense of a nation. By requiring all able-bodied citizens to serve, a country can ensure that it has a strong and ready military force. This not only protects the country from external threats, but also helps to maintain peace and stability within its borders.

In addition to its role in defense, military service can also have a positive impact on society as a whole. It can instill a sense of national pride and unity, as well as promoting values such as discipline, teamwork, and leadership. These qualities are essential for the functioning of any society, and military service provides an opportunity for individuals to develop and hone them.

But what about the individuals who serve? Some may argue that compulsory military service is a burden, but I disagree. Military service can be a truly rewarding experience, both personally and professionally. It provides individuals with the opportunity to challenge themselves and push their limits, as well as to learn new skills and gain valuable life experience.

Furthermore, military service can also provide important benefits for those who serve. These can include education opportunities, job training, and financial assistance. These benefits can help individuals to better their lives and achieve their goals, both during and after their service.

In conclusion, compulsory military service is a valuable institution that is good for society, the country, and the individuals who serve. It is a necessary and important part of any nation's defense, and it also provides individuals with the opportunity to develop valuable skills and achieve personal and professional growth. I urge you to support compulsory military service as a vital and necessary part of our society. Thank you.

HERE ARE THE ANSWER;

Ladies and gentlemen,

Today, I want to argue that compulsory military service is good for society and the country.

First and foremost, military service helps to promote national unity and cohesion. By requiring all citizens, regardless of their background or circumstances, to serve in the military, we can create a sense of shared experience and common purpose. This can foster a greater sense of belonging and loyalty to the nation, as well as a stronger sense of national identity.

Furthermore, military service helps to develop important skills and virtues in individuals. Through their training and service, young people learn discipline, responsibility, teamwork, and leadership. These skills and virtues are valuable not just in the military, but in all aspects of life. By requiring military service, we can help young people to develop these important qualities and set them on a path towards success and fulfillment in their personal and professional lives.

Additionally, military service helps to defend the nation and maintain peace. By having a strong military, we can deter potential threats and protect our citizens and our national interests. Military service also provides an opportunity for young people to serve their country and make a positive contribution to society.

Finally, military service can be an affordable and efficient way to ensure that the country has the personnel it needs to defend itself and maintain peace. Instead of relying on a professional military, which can be expensive to maintain, compulsory military service allows the country to tap into the talent and resources of its entire population.

In conclusion, compulsory military service is good for society and the country. It promotes national unity and cohesion, develops important skills and virtues in individuals, helps to defend the nation and maintain peace, and is an affordable and efficient way to ensure that the country has the personnel it needs. I urge you to support compulsory military service as a crucial aspect of our national security and well-being. Thank you.

26. Deliveringinspirationalspeech for theaspiringsoldiers in thePhilippineMilitaryAcademy​


Answer:

THOSE who have been conferred their commissions today have just passed an exacting course in leadership. Their physical, mental, and moral qualifications to assume command have been tested and found adequate. As we congratulate them, we also pray that the qualities which have sustained them through the rigorous regime of this academy will develop and mature under the sharper challenges of the field, especially of combat. I have every confidence that the training they have received here from the able and competent faculty of this academy will not fail them in the more difficult tests ahead.

For many years now, the Philippine Military Academy has been the principal source of professional officer material for our army. From this institution have come the men who occupy today some of the highest and most important commands and positions in our military establishment. Year after year in the future, commencement exercises such as this one will continue to augment and replenish the supply of professionally-trained officers for our army.

The nature and quality of an organization are determined to a large extent by the type and manner of men who lead it. To know what kind of an army we have, we must find out what manner of men are the officers who command it.

What kind of training do they receive here at this academy? What is its orientation? What are the traditions that influence and mould the character, the outlook, the perspective of the men who prepare here for military leadership?

The curriculum of this institution is largely concerned, of course, with the art and science of soldiery. Students are trained in the basic skills of their profession, and are imbued with those habits and qualities of character which are so essential to a successful soldier’s career. These are courage, loyalty, discipline, and sound judgment.

This is not to say, however, that the education they receive is one-sided, exaggerating the specialized skills of soldiery at the expense and sacrifice of a well-rounded education. I feel that this academy can justly take pride in the achievement that it turns out not merely competent soldiers, but educated men.

More important, however, than the substance and contents of the training that our future officers obtain here is its basic orientation. What are the various aspects of this orientation? While this academy trains its students for a soldier’s career, it also teaches them to accept and recognize the superiority of civilian over military authority in our system of government. While it drills them in the Manual of War, it takes even greater pains and effort to indoctrinate them in principles and substance of our Constitution. It affirms the rule of law and justifies the use of arms and force only when it is sanctioned by law.

In brief, the orientation of their education and training is democratic. Similarly, the traditions that have clustered around this institution and our military organization have reflected only the democratic spirit of our way of life. I do not know of any instance in the history of our military when men in uniform plotted or attempted to overthrow civilian authority. One will search our history-books vainly for a tradition of military usurpation of authority.

The sum of all these facts, my friends, is that our officers—and through them our soldiers—are trained and indoctrinated according to the fundamental democratic concept of the supremacy of civilian authority. They are Filipinos first, soldiers second, owing loyalty first to the Constitution and only secondly to the Manual of War. They are raised and steeped in the ethics of a democratic citizen’s army, not in the arrogant traditions of an all-powerful elite corps.
