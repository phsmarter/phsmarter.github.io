17. why is there such a thing as world hunger?​


Answer:

The main cause of hunger worldwide is poverty. Millions of people around the world are simply too poor to be able to buy food. They also lack the resources to grow their own food, such as arable land and the means to harvest, process, and store food.

GLOBAL HUNGER - A PROBLEMATIC CYCLE

Answer:

Global hunger creates an impenetrable circle from which individuals are unable to escape. It makes individuals less productive and more susceptible to illness, limiting their ability to improve their livelihoods or earn a greater pay. But, if the world produces enough food to feed everyone, how come there are so many hungry people? Hunger is more than just a lack of food; it is also a lack of nutritious food to eat. Poor nutrition is responsible for 45 percent of child mortality under the age of five, and one out of every six children in developing countries is underweight. Micronutrient deficiencies — a shortage of vitamins and minerals necessary for good health – affect a large portion of the world's population.

POVERTY

Poverty is the primary source of global hunger. Millions of individuals in developing countries simply cannot afford the land or agricultural supplies needed to farm or otherwise have access to healthy food due to unequal income distribution and a paucity of resources. It's also a cyclical problem: if you're constantly hungry, you'll have low energy and poor mental and physical performance, making it difficult to work or study. As a result, persistent poverty and hunger result.

CHANGE IN THE CLIMATE

Unpredictability and volatility in the climate are rapidly becoming a driving force behind world hunger. The number of climate-related calamities – drought, starvation, floods, and high heat – has more than doubled since the early 1990s. In only one year, almost 124 million individuals in 51 countries suffered "crisis" levels of acute food insecurity or worse, demanding immediate emergency action to defend their lives and livelihoods. Temperatures are rising, and rainfall patterns are changing, which has a detrimental impact on crops and animals, with major ramifications for food security and nutrition.

CONFLICT AND INSTABILITY

Political conflict may have a severe influence on market infrastructure, transportation services, and land availability on a communal level, while also causing income loss, housing displacement, and food price inflation on an individual level. As places become more turbulent and violent, crucial services and supplies become even more unavailable to the most vulnerable. In fact, the UN believes that conflict is responsible for 80% of its humanitarian financial needs. It is apparent that future efforts to combat global hunger must coexist with those to maintain global peace.

ECONOMIC SLOWDOWN

Even in calm contexts, the current downturn in global economic development has resulted in rising food insecurity. Rising expenses and reduced expenditure have significantly impacted the ability of individuals in many nations to feed themselves, from import capacity and food access to commodity pricing and subsidies for social security programs.

22. Figthing hunger essay based on module


Answer & Explanation:

Fighting hunger is a crucial issue that affects millions of people around the world. Hunger is a problem that not only affects a person's physical health but also has a profound impact on their emotional, social, and cognitive well-being. In this essay, I will discuss the causes of hunger, the impact it has on individuals and communities, and what can be done to fight it.

Hunger is caused by a variety of factors, including poverty, lack of access to food, and natural disasters such as drought and famine. In many developing countries, people do not have the resources to grow or purchase enough food to feed themselves and their families. This is often due to economic and political factors that limit access to resources and markets. In addition, natural disasters can destroy crops, disrupt food systems, and increase the risk of hunger.

The impact of hunger on individuals and communities is far-reaching and devastating. Hunger can lead to malnutrition, which can cause a range of health problems, including weakened immunity, increased risk of disease, and even death. Hunger also has a negative impact on cognitive development, affecting a person's ability to learn and perform tasks. On a larger scale, hunger can lead to poverty and social unrest, as people struggle to meet their basic needs.

Fighting hunger requires a multi-faceted approach that addresses its root causes. One solution is to improve food security by increasing access to resources and markets, and promoting sustainable agriculture practices. Another approach is to provide emergency food aid to communities affected by natural disasters or conflict. In addition, improving access to education, healthcare, and job opportunities can help people lift themselves out of poverty and achieve food security.

In conclusion, hunger is a major global issue that affects the well-being of millions of people around the world. It is caused by a range of factors, including poverty, lack of access to food, and natural disasters. The impact of hunger on individuals and communities is far-reaching and devastating, and requires a comprehensive approach to fight it. By addressing the root causes of hunger, promoting sustainable agriculture practices, and providing emergency aid and support, we can work towards a world where everyone has enough to eat.
