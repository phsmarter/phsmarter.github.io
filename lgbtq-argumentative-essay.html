Improve the health, safety, and well-being of lesbian, gay, bisexual, and transgender (LGBT) individuals.

Explanation:

LGBT individuals encompass all races and ethnicities, religions, and social classes. Sexual orientation and gender identity questions are not asked on most national or State surveys, making it difficult to estimate the number of LGBT individuals and their health needs.

Research suggests that LGBT individuals face health disparities linked to societal stigma, discrimination, and denial of their civil and human rights. Discrimination against LGBT persons has been associated with high rates of psychiatric disorders,1 substance abuse,2, 3 and suicide.4 Experiences of violence and victimization are frequent for LGBT individuals, and have long-lasting effects on the individual and the community.5 Personal, family, and social acceptance of sexual orientation and gender identity affects the mental health and personal safety of LGBT individuals.6

The LGBT companion document to Healthy People 20107 highlighted the need for more research to document, understand, and address the environmental factors that contribute to health disparities in the LGBT community. As part of this work, we need to increase the number of nationally-representative health-related surveys that collect information on sexual orientation and gender identity (SOGI).

At the beginning of the decade, the following 6 national data systems collected sexual orientation data:

Behavioral Risk Factor Surveillance Survey (BRFSS)

National Health and Nutrition and Examination Survey (NHANES)

National Survey of Family Growth (NSFG)

Youth Risk Behavior Survey (YRBS)8

National Crime Victimization Survey (NCVS)

American Community Survey (ACS)

Since the beginning of the decade, 2 additional national surveys have begun collecting sexual orientation data:

National Health Interview Survey (NHIS) – added in 20139

Equality among the LGBTQ+ community is a trend towards greater social acceptance and legal recognition of the rights of lesbian, gay, bisexual, transgender, and queer people. This trend is driven by a growing understanding and acceptance of diversity, as well as advocacy by LGBTQ+ individuals and allies.

The push for equality has been ongoing for decades, and progress has been made in many areas. For example, many countries have legalized same-sex marriage, and discrimination on the basis of sexual orientation or gender identity is increasingly being recognized as a violation of human rights. Additionally, more LGBTQ+ people are represented in politics, media, and other prominent positions, which helps to raise awareness and reduce stigma.

However, there is still much work to be done to achieve full equality for the LGBTQ+ community. Many LGBTQ+ individuals still face discrimination and prejudice in various aspects of their lives, including in employment, housing, healthcare, and education. Transgender individuals, in particular, face high rates of violence and harassment.

To continue the trend towards greater equality, it is important to continue advocating for LGBTQ+ rights and raising awareness of the issues that the community faces. This can be done through political action, community organizing, and education. It is also important for allies to support the LGBTQ+ community and speak out against discrimination and prejudice.

It's important to raise awareness and take steps to create a society where everyone is treated with respect and fairness, regardless of their sexual orientation or gender identity.

Another important aspect to consider is the process of "coming out". This is when someone publicly shares their sexual orientation or gender identity with others. It can be a difficult and emotional process for LGBTQ individuals, and it's important to support them through it.

The LGBTQ community also fights for their rights, LGBTQ individuals deserve the same rights and protections as everyone else. This includes things like marriage equality and laws that protect LGBTQ individuals from discrimination.

It is crucial to remember that everyone is unique and should be treated with respect and empathy, regardless of their sexual orientation or gender identity. Being an ally means being supportive and educating oneself about LGBTQ issues.

It's important to be sensitive when discussing this topic and keep in mind that there is a lot more to learn and understand about the LGBTQ community and their experiences.
