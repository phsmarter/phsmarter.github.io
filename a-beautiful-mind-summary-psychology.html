5. TRUE or FALSE: Psychological barrier is when your communicative actions are affected by your state of mind.


Psychological Barrier

It is also called as the emotional barriers. This refers to the mental state of a person that will have a great effect on the ability to properly communicate to people. This can hinder the following:

Opinions Behavior Attitudes Status consciousness Emotions

In order to properly communicate to another person, it always depends on the mental condition of a certain person. If the person that you are talking to is mentally challenged or emotionally sound, communication will be defective and most likely the receiver would not understand the person talking and will often misunderstood.

Here are the main kinds of Psychological Barrier

Premature Evaluation

People who tend to jump into conclusions and forms judgement without considering the aspect of the information. This can commonly be seen to people who are impatient and with selective listening. This premature evaluation can be a barrier to proper communication and it can also lower the morale of the speaker or sender.

Poor Retention

Retention is the ability of the brain to store things in the memory. Our brain cannot store all information that come across but it has a capacity to remember important stuff. If a person cannot recall anything specially if the topic has just recently been discussed and when being asked, person does not remember the topic at all can be a barrier to effective communication too.

Lack of Attention

If the person is pre-occupied with other stuff and does not pay attention to what you are saying then it can be a barrier to communication as the person would not be able to understand you fully. Specially if you are talking about a certain process that is linked together. One missed information, the rest of the information that your audience who does not pay attention will most likely be lost of the topic and they would not be able to follow through.

Loss By Transmission

Improper and lack of information that is being said or handed to others can be a great hindrance of communication. You will lose contact and will often bore the audience as they can no longer get where you are going.

Distrust

To be able to have a proper and effective communication, speaker and receiver must establish trust with each other. If there is no trust between the two, all information that either the speaker or receiver receives, it would just turn out to be negative and higher chance of arguing with each other.  

Emotions

Every communication is being influenced by emotions. If the speaker has a bad temper, most likely the receiver would not listen or worst walk out on you. If the speaker is a positive type of person and can handle criticism or judgement most likely the receivers would be more engaged to the speaker, trust the speaker and would be more interested in listening.

So, for the question:

TRUE or FALSE: Psychological barrier is when your communicative actions are affected by your state of mind.

My answer would be TRUE.

6. Psychological barrier is when your communicative actions are affected by your state of mind. Select one: True False


Psychological Barrier

Answer:

Psychological Barrier is when your communicative actions are affected by your state of mind, True. When it comes to our way of communicating we often think of our words first before we speak. We use our minds first but it is indeed also possible when we just blurt out words without minding what words or how will affect the person we are conversing to.

Psychological Barriers:

Premature Evaluation- the person is in a hurry to come up with something, either he jumps to conclusions or just plainly selective to information Lack of Attention- people tend to get lost in thoughts most of the time when their mind is unable to keep focus. Either he gets easily distracted or unable to grasp the situation entirely. Emotions- emotions has a huge impact to how and what will our communication will be. It can cloud your judgment and purely drive with passion or it will be beneficial to you since your words are true. Distrust- a psychological barrier that makes the person wary of every words the person he is talking to saying. He can either trust him and take his words as true or distrust him and disregard what he is saying.

Solution for Psychological Barriers from the examples:

Premature Evaluation/ Complete Understanding- to fully read the text, to practice yourself hearing all the side of the story. Lack of Attention/ Find your focus of interest- when you lose attention try finding something that will appear to you as interesting, when you do you will focus on the subject unconsciously since your brain will find more about that point of interest of yours. Emotions/ Emotional Control or Management- sometimes emotions can get out of place or we misunderstand the thought, with this we need to control our emotions to provide a more clear and open-minded thought. Distrust/ Trust- to simplify it you just need to trust the person you are talking to, or just trust his words at that certain point of time.
