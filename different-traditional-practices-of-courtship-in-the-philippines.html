Answer:

Sure! Here are three traditional courtship practices from different regions in the Philippines:

1. Ilocos Region: "Pamamanhikan" - This practice involves the man's family visiting the woman's family to ask for her hand in marriage. During the visit, the families discuss the terms and conditions of the marriage, including the dowry that the man's family will pay to the woman's family.

2. Bicol Region: "Pamamanhikan sa ilog" - This practice involves the man's family visiting the woman's family by boat. The families then have a meal together, and the man's family will present the woman's family with gifts as a way of showing their sincere intentions and respect.

3. Mindanao Region: "Paninilbihan" - In this practice, the man serves the woman's family for a certain period of time, usually a few months, as a way of proving his love and commitment. The man may be asked to do household chores or farm work, and he is expected to be respectful and obedient to the woman's family during this time. This practice is also known as "bunót" or "magpakasalá."
