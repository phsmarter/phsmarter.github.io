18. description/uses of saya english ​


Answer:

An ankle-length outer skirt tied at the waist that is worn by women in the Philippines and Spanish America.

Baro't saya is an ensemble that traditionally consists of four parts: the blouse (baro or camisa), a long skirt (saya or falda), a kerchief worn over the shoulders (pañuelo, fichu, or alampay), and a short rectangular cloth worn over the skirt (the tapis or patadyong).

19. Differences for Barong Tagalog/Baro't Saya


Barong Tagalog

The Barong Tagalog is an embroidered high -waisted baro and is the recognized national men's clothing in the Philippines. First called the baro of Tagalog, this garment has also been used in the country for four centuries and is constantly being beautified to keep pace with social change.

Baro't Saya

Baro't Saya is the national women's clothing of the Philippines which consists of a thin and embroidered top, and a skirt or saya that is colorful and often striped. Using the clog as a footwear, women who wear it also hold a fan to cover their face or chest. Its wearing was in accordance with the Spanish command to cover and clothe the naked bodies of the natives, especially of the women, upon their landing in the country.

Ang baro't saya ay mahalaga sa kulturang Pilipino sapagkat ito ang nagbibigay ng ala-ala kung anong klase ang pamumuhay ng mga Pilipino. Sa pamamagitan ng kasuotang baro't saya nagkakaroon ng pagkakilanlan ang mga babaeng Pilipino, nagkakaroon din ng kaalaman sa kasaysayan ang mga tao at bumubuo ito sa pagkatao ng mga Pilipino.
