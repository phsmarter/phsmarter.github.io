Answer:

Noli Me Tángere, Latin for "Touch me not", is an 1887 novel by José Rizal during the colonization of the Philippines by Spain to describe perceived inequities of the Spanish Catholic friars and the ruling government.

Originally written in Spanish, the book is more commonly published and read in the Philippines in either Tagalog or English. Together with its sequel, El filibusterismo (Grade 10), the reading of Noli is obligatory for high school students (Grade 9) throughout the country. The two novels are widely considered the national epic of the Philippines and are adapted in many forms, such as operas, musicals, plays, and other forms of art.

11. System during spanish colonial era while rizal is writing noli me tangere​


During the Spanish colonial era in the Philippines, the system of government was controlled by the Spanish colonizers. The Philippines was ruled by the Governor-General, who was appointed by the Spanish monarch and had complete control over the colony. The Spanish colonial government was divided into three branches: the executive, legislative, and judicial.

The executive branch was headed by the Governor-General and his staff, who were responsible for enforcing laws and maintaining order. The legislative branch was composed of the Spanish-controlled Philippines Assembly and the Spanish Cortes. The judicial branch was made up of Spanish-controlled courts, which had the power to try and punish criminals and hear civil cases.

During this time, the Philippines was also divided into provinces and municipalities, which were governed by local officials appointed by the Spanish colonial government. The Catholic Church also played a significant role in the colony, with the Spanish friars controlling much of the land and having significant influence over the local population.

During the time when Rizal was writing "Noli Me Tangere," the Philippines was experiencing a growing sense of nationalism and dissatisfaction with the Spanish colonial rule. Rizal's novel, which exposed the injustices and abuses of the Spanish colonizers, was a powerful catalyst for the Philippine Revolution, which eventually led to the country's independence.

18. Why did Jose Rizal write his novel Noli Me Tangere?​


Answer:

JOSÉ Rizal wrote the Noli Me Tangere for the benefit of the indios. But it was not meant for indios to read. The mere fact that he wrote it in the Spanish language and not Tagalog tells us that he had a different audience in mind: The Spaniards and their government, so that they could grant the necessary reforms to the indios, which included representation, the granting of equal rights and the distinction of being a province.
