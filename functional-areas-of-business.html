2. Why is it important for the various functional areas of a business organization to collaborate?


It is important for the various functional areas to collaborate because collaboration will lead to improved communication among the departments (functions) that in turn will improve the performance of the firm. Collaboration will reduce the chance of sub-optimization by a functional area due to the possibility that a particular functional area does not have enough information about the other areas and their constraints or decisions.

23. Identify the three major functional areas of business organizations and briefly describe how they interrelate


The following are the three major functional areas of a business:Management Production/Operations Marketing

One motivation for segmenting business processes into functional areas is to enable each to operate within its area of competence, increasing overall efficiency and effectiveness.

Management's significant responsibility in business is to supervise the performance of others. The majority of managerial activities can be classified as follows:

Planning: Managers plan by establishing long-term goals for the firm and the short-term strategies necessary to achieve those goals.Arranging: Managers are responsible for organizing an organization's operations in the most efficient manner possible, allowing the business to make the best use of its resources.Managing:  A considerable portion of a manager's time is spent monitoring the business's actions to ensure that it is on the path to meeting its objectives. When individuals or systems deviate from the course, managers are frequently the first to detect and correct.Leading: Managers serve as organizational leaders in both practical and symbolic ways. The manager may be responsible for leading work teams or groups by implementing a new process or developing a new product.

Operations are converting inputs (factors of production) to outputs (goods and services). Processes function similarly to the heart of a firm, pumping forth goods and services in sufficient numbers and of a high enough quality to suit clients' expectations. The operations manager is in charge of the business's day-to-day operations, which might range from ordering raw supplies to scheduling personnel to generate tangible things.

Marketing encompasses everything a business does to ascertain its consumers' needs and produce products and services to fulfill those demands. Additionally, marketing is responsible for advertising goods and services, establishing how goods and services will be delivered, and developing a price strategy to acquire market share while remaining competitive.

1. What is purpose of an intermation system from a business perspective? What role does it play in

the business information value chains.

Answer:

What is purpose of an intermation system from a business perspective?

- Business Information System, eases the process of decision making and simplifies the process of delivering the required information and hence assists in taking better decisions instantly. Business Information System can be effectively implemented to help communication better between the employers and the employees.

What role does it play in the business information value chains?

- Information systems help businesses in developing a larger number of value added systems in the company. For example, a company can integrate information systems with the manufacturing cycle to ensure that the output it produces complies with the requirements of the various quality management standards.

From a business perspective, an information system is an important instrument for creating value for the firm. Information systems enable the firm to increase its revenue or decrease its costs by providing information that helps managers make better decisions or that improves the execution of business processes.

information systems today?

Answer:

There are three key management challenges in building and using information systems:

1. designing systems that are competitive and efficient;

2. understanding the system requirements of a global businesses environment;

3. creating an information architecture that supports the organization's goals.
