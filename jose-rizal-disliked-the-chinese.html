29. Refer to the " Hierarchy structure of the people in the society " during Rizal time . Describe the following group of people listed below . PENINSULARIES , INSULARIES , SPANISH MESTIZOS ,PRINCIPALIA , CHINESE MESTIZO , CHINESE AND INDIO . In your own words . ​


Peninsulares- They are the highest class and entrusted with the offices of high rank. They are pure blooded Spaniards born from Spain and sent to Spanish colonies to govern. They are the most powerful people governing the government and church.

The insulares or criollos- they are European descent but born in the colonies of Spain. A son or daughter of a Spanish couple is an insulares.  Insulares have government and church positions but when economics and power shifted, they changed to capitalist driven entrepreneurs owning large lands.

Spanish mestizos- They are off springs of Spanish-Filipino couples. Mestizo is a term given to individuals inheriting foreign ancestry. The term was used in Latin America but was later adopted in the Philippines to children of racial inter-marriage.  Parents of mestizos may have been an Alcalde or another important position in the government.

The principalía or noble class- was the ruling and usually educated upper class in the pueblos of the Spanish Philippines, comprising the Gobernadorcillo, who was later referred to as Capitan Municipal , El Teniente de Justicia (Lieutenants of Justice), and the Cabezas de Barangay (heads of the barangays) who governed the districts, former Gobernadorcillos or Municipal Captains, and Municipal lieutenants in good standing during their term of office. They are wealthy pure-blooded natives said to have descended from the kadatoan class

Chinese mestizos- Chinese that have married with inhabitants of the Philippines, results in a creolized community of Chinese mestizos under the Spanish colonial regime.

Chinese- They are also called Sangley. A term that used in the Spanish Philippines to refer to people of pure Chinese descent; came from the Hokkien word “seng-li” meaning business.

Indio- The Malayan, native born inhabitants of the Philippine islands were called “indio” or “indigenta.” This class or group occupied the lowest level in a highly stratified class society.
