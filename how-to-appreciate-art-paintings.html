1. A visual artwork has movement, of course. The dance itself is an artistic expression primarily based on movement. Movement is also used in visual art but in a unique way. The illusion of action in a work of art is achieved through the artistic principle of visible movement. Any part of a composition, or the complete thing, can undergo motion.

The visual aspects of art, including colour, line, texture, shape, form, value, and space, can convey the sense of motion in a work of art. Incorporating these components in various configurations allows for a wide range of possible motion.

The artist uses the principle of movement to lead the viewer's eye into, around, and out of work.

2. Harmony in art generally is created when an artwork's elements merge coherently. Some details can be reused without losing their cohesiveness overall. Neither monotony nor anarchy characterizes harmony. The two go together so well.

There are various ways of expressing harmony. Use colour as a medium, and create a balance between the marks and the shapes you paint.

3. Carlos Francisco created several works, one of which is titled Bayanihan. The artwork titled Bayanihan, which translates to "becoming a bayan," is often used to symbolize a strong sense of solidarity and collaboration among members of a community.

The Tagalog word "bayan," which means "nation," "town," or "community," is the source of the Filipino custom known as Bayanihan (buy-uh-nee-hun). The Filipino concept of "bayanihan," or "being in a bayan," is central to Filipino identity since it emphasizes the importance of community and cooperation.
