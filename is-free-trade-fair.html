2. Ano ang pagkakaiba ng Free Trade at fair trade


Free trade is about profits because it gives countries unrestricted sale and purchase of services without the imposition of constraints such as tariff, duties and quotas. On the other hand, Fair trade is about people because it aims to pay producers, such as farmers and tailors, a living wage that will enable them to live comfortably. 

3. Ano ang pagkakaiba ng Free Trade at fair trade​


Fair trade places restrictions on farmers and producers. It forces them to pay minimum wages, adopt safe working conditions and pay lip service to planetary protection.

Free trade removes all boundaries for all parties. It affords unfettered international export and import, free from taxes, tariffs, worker protections or pesky minimum wages.

Globally: fair trade makes things more expensive, free trade makes things cheaper; fair trade means workers earn more, free trade means workers earn less. So while free does mean cheap, it also means we earn collectively less money with which to buy all that cheap stuff.

18. Is there a difference between fair trade and free trade? What is it and which is of greater importance?


Answer:

Fair trade and free trade are two different approaches to international trade. Free trade refers to a system of international trade where goods, services, and capital flow freely across borders without any barriers or restrictions. On the other hand, fair trade is a movement that aims to promote social and environmental sustainability by ensuring that producers in developing countries receive fair prices for their products.

The main difference between fair trade and free trade is the focus of each approach. Free trade focuses on promoting economic growth by removing barriers to trade, such as tariffs and quotas. The idea behind free trade is that when countries can specialize in producing goods that they are most efficient at producing, and then trade with other countries for goods that they are less efficient at producing, everyone benefits.

Fair trade, on the other hand, focuses on promoting social and environmental sustainability by ensuring that producers in developing countries receive fair prices for their products. Fair trade organizations work with producers in developing countries to help them improve their production methods, pay fair wages to workers, and protect the environment.

Which approach is of greater importance is a matter of debate. Supporters of free trade argue that it promotes economic growth and helps to reduce poverty by creating jobs and increasing access to goods and services. However, critics of free trade argue that it can lead to exploitation of workers in developing countries and can harm the environment.

Supporters of fair trade argue that it promotes social and environmental sustainability by ensuring that producers in developing countries receive fair prices for their products. They also argue that fair trade helps to empower small-scale farmers and workers in developing countries by giving them access to markets that they might not otherwise have access to. However, critics of fair trade argue that it can be expensive for consumers and may not always be effective at improving the lives of producers in developing countries.

In conclusion, both fair trade and free trade are important approaches to international trade. While free trade focuses on promoting economic growth by removing barriers to trade, fair trade focuses on promoting social and environmental sustainability by ensuring that producers in developing countries receive fair prices for their products.
