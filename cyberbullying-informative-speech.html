4. Performance Output #3 Write a prepared speech 'Why cyberbullying about must be stopped?'. Follow the tips given previously to deliver your speech successfully.​


Good evening everyone,

I am here today to talk to you about an issue that is becoming increasingly prevalent in our society: cyberbullying.

For those who may not be familiar with the term, cyberbullying refers to the use of technology, such as the internet and social media, to harass, intimidate, or harm others. It can take many forms, such as sending threatening messages, spreading rumors or false information, or sharing embarrassing or private photos without permission.

Cyberbullying is a serious problem that can have devastating consequences for those who are targeted. It can lead to feelings of isolation, depression, and even suicide. In fact, a recent study found that teenagers who are cyberbullied are more than twice as likely to attempt suicide as those who are not.

But cyberbullying is not just a problem for the victims. It also affects those who engage in this behavior. Bullying others can lead to criminal charges and damage one's reputation and future opportunities.

So why do people engage in cyberbullying? There are many reasons, but some common ones include a lack of empathy, a desire for power and control, or a need to fit in with a certain group. Whatever the reason, it is never justified.

So what can we do to stop cyberbullying? First and foremost, it is important for all of us to be aware of the issue and to speak out against it when we see it happening. We can also support those who have been bullied and help them to feel safe and supported.

Furthermore, we can educate ourselves and others about the effects of cyberbullying and the importance of treating others with kindness and respect. This can be done through schools, community organizations, and even through social media campaigns.

In conclusion, cyberbullying is a serious problem that must be stopped. By raising awareness, supporting those who have been affected, and educating ourselves and others, we can make a difference and create a safer and more compassionate online community.

Thank you for listening.

6. informative essay about cyberbullying.​


Answer:

Cyberbullying is a type of bullying that involves the use of technology such as social media, messaging apps, and other digital platforms to harass, intimidate, or harm others. It can take many forms, including spreading rumors, posting hurtful comments, sharing embarrassing photos or videos, or making threats online. Cyberbullying can have serious consequences for both the victim and the perpetrator, and it is important to understand its effects and how to prevent it.

One of the most harmful effects of cyberbullying is the impact it can have on the victim's mental health. Victims of cyberbullying often experience anxiety, depression, low self-esteem, and other negative emotions. This can lead to poor academic performance, social isolation, and even suicidal thoughts or attempts. It is crucial for parents, teachers, and other adults to recognize the signs of cyberbullying and provide support and resources for those who are affected.

Another consequence of cyberbullying is the potential for it to escalate into real-world violence. In some cases, cyberbullying has led to physical assaults, school shootings, and other acts of violence. It is important to take threats seriously and report them to the appropriate authorities. Parents and educators can also work to promote a positive and inclusive school environment that discourages bullying of any kind.

Preventing cyberbullying requires a multifaceted approach. Parents can teach their children about responsible online behavior, including how to use social media and messaging apps safely and respectfully. They can also monitor their children's online activity and intervene if they see signs of cyberbullying. Educators can incorporate lessons on digital citizenship and online safety into their curriculum, and work to create a safe and welcoming school environment for all students.

Technology companies also have a role to play in preventing cyberbullying. They can develop tools and algorithms that detect and remove harmful content from their platforms, and implement policies that prohibit cyberbullying and other forms of online harassment. They can also work with researchers, educators, and other experts to develop effective strategies for preventing and addressing cyberbullying.

In conclusion, cyberbullying is a serious issue that can have devastating consequences for those who are affected. It is important for parents, educators, and technology companies to work together to prevent cyberbullying and promote a safe and positive online environment. By teaching responsible online behavior, monitoring online activity, and creating a culture of kindness and inclusion, we can help prevent cyberbullying and protect the mental health and well-being of young people.
