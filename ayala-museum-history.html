19. characteristic of national museum of natural history​


Answer:

Characteristic of national museum of natural history​.

The National Museum of Natural History is a type of museum that focuses on the natural world and the scientific study of it. It typically has exhibits on subjects such as geology, paleontology, zoology, botany, and ecology. Some of the characteristics of a National Museum of Natural History may include:

Collections of specimens and artifacts: The museum may have a wide variety of natural specimens, such as rocks, minerals, fossils, plants, and animals, as well as artifacts related to the natural world, such as tools and equipment used by naturalists and scientists.Interactive exhibits: Many National Museums of Natural History have interactive exhibits that allow visitors to learn about natural phenomena and the scientific methods used to study them in a hands-on way.Educational programming: Many National Museum of Natural History offer educational programs for school groups, families, and the general public. These programs may include lectures, workshops, and guided tours.Scientific research: Some National Museums of Natural History conduct their own scientific research and may have research staff and facilities on site.Conservation and preservation: Many National Museums of Natural History also have a role in conserving and preserving natural resources and endangered species.Permanent and Temporary exhibitions: National Museum of Natural History usually has both permanent and temporary exhibitions, which showcases different aspects of natural science and history.

25. Medical Museum, history?​

A medical museum is an institution that stores and exhibits objects of historical, scientific, artistic, or cultural interest that have a link to medicine or health. Displays often include models, instruments, books and manuscripts, as well as medical images and the technologies used to capture them (such as X-ray machines).
