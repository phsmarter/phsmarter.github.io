2. k-12 new issue essay​


Answer:

The K-12 program in the Philippines has been a topic of debate for several years now. While the program aims to improve the quality of education in the country by lengthening the basic education cycle and aligning it with international standards, it has also faced various challenges and criticisms.

One of the main issues with the K-12 program is the financial burden it places on families, particularly those who are struggling to make ends meet. The additional two years of senior high school means more expenses for tuition, books, and other school fees. This has led to concerns about the accessibility of education, especially for those in low-income households.

Another issue is the readiness of schools and teachers to implement the K-12 program. Many schools lack the necessary resources and infrastructure to provide quality education, and teachers need proper training to effectively teach the new curriculum. This has led to uneven implementation and quality of education across the country.

Furthermore, the K-12 program has been criticized for its focus on producing graduates who are employable, rather than well-rounded individuals who are critical thinkers and problem solvers. The emphasis on technical skills and vocational education has been seen as a move away from the liberal arts and humanities, which are essential for developing a well-informed and engaged citizenry.

Despite these challenges, the K-12 program also has its benefits. The additional two years of senior high school provides students with more time to explore their interests and strengths, and to prepare for college or the workforce. The program also aims to provide students with skills that are relevant to the needs of the 21st century, such as communication, digital literacy, and global awareness.

To address the issues surrounding the K-12 program, the government should provide more funding for education and ensure that schools are properly equipped to implement the new curriculum. Teachers should also be given adequate training and support to effectively deliver the new curriculum. Moreover, the government should ensure that the K-12 program provides a well-rounded education that emphasizes critical thinking, creativity, and citizenship, alongside technical skills and vocational education.

In conclusion, while the K-12 program in the Philippines has faced various challenges and criticisms, it also provides opportunities for students to develop their skills and prepare for college or the workforce. Addressing the issues surrounding the program requires a concerted effort from the government, schools, teachers, and other stakeholders to ensure that every student can access quality education and reach their full potential.

3. depend on K-12 revision definition essay​


Answer:

Revision, in the context of K-12 education, refers to the process of revisiting and improving upon previous work or ideas. This process helps students to refine their understanding and expression of a particular topic or subject.

Revision is an important aspect of the writing process and can greatly enhance the quality and clarity of a student's work. It involves re-reading and evaluating previous work, making corrections, and making improvements to overall structure, content, and style. This process allows students to reflect on their work and make necessary changes to better convey their ideas and insights.

Revision also helps students to develop critical thinking skills and improve their ability to analyze and evaluate their own work. By revisiting their previous ideas, students can identify areas where they need to further clarify their arguments, add supporting evidence, or improve the overall flow of their writing.

In K-12 education, revision is typically incorporated into writing assignments and projects, allowing students to practice this valuable skill in a structured and supportive environment. Teachers may also provide guidance and feedback to help students with the revision process, pointing out areas where they can improve and suggesting strategies for improvement.

In summary, revision is an essential part of the K-12 educational experience and is crucial for students to develop their writing and critical thinking skills. By revisiting and improving upon their previous work, students are able to produce high-quality writing that effectively communicates their ideas and insights.

7. essay about amendment on the K-12 curriculum ​


Answer:The K-12 curriculum refers to the educational program for primary and secondary education in the United States. In recent years, there have been discussions and debates about the need to amend the K-12 curriculum to better prepare students for the challenges of the modern world. While the current curriculum covers a wide range of subjects, some argue that it needs to be updated to reflect the changing needs of society.

One proposed amendment to the K-12 curriculum is to increase the emphasis on STEM subjects, which stands for science, technology, engineering, and mathematics. This is because there is a growing demand for workers in STEM fields, and it is important that students are prepared for these careers. Additionally, STEM education can foster critical thinking, problem-solving, and creativity, which are essential skills for success in any field.

Another amendment to the K-12 curriculum is to integrate more real-world experiences and practical skills. This can include courses in financial literacy, entrepreneurship, and vocational education. These skills are important for students who may not pursue traditional academic paths and can provide them with the skills and knowledge needed to succeed in the workforce.

Furthermore, there is a growing movement to incorporate social and emotional learning (SEL) into the K-12 curriculum. SEL can help students develop self-awareness, social awareness, and responsible decision-making skills. This can help students become more resilient and better equipped to deal with challenges they may face in their personal and professional lives.

Lastly, the K-12 curriculum could also be amended to include a greater focus on diversity, equity, and inclusion. This can include courses on multiculturalism, gender and sexuality, and racial justice. By promoting inclusivity and cultural understanding, students can develop a deeper appreciation for different perspectives and learn how to work effectively in diverse environments.

Overall, amending the K-12 curriculum can provide students with a more relevant and comprehensive education that prepares them for success in the 21st century. By incorporating STEM education, practical skills, social and emotional learning, and diversity and inclusion, students can develop the skills, knowledge, and values needed to succeed in an increasingly complex and interconnected world.

15. argumentative essay about terminating of the k-12 program​


The implementation of the K-12 program in the Philippines has been a controversial topic since it was first introduced in 2011. While some people argue that it is necessary for the country's educational system to compete globally, others believe that it should be terminated due to several issues.

One of the main arguments against the K-12 program is the financial burden it places on families. The program requires additional two years of education, which means more expenses for parents who already struggle to pay for their children's basic needs. Some families may have to sacrifice other important expenses such as food and healthcare just to keep their children in school.

Another issue with the K-12 program is the lack of facilities and teachers to accommodate the increased number of students. This has led to overcrowded classrooms, inadequate teaching materials, and a shortage of qualified teachers. As a result, students are not getting the quality education they deserve, and the program is failing to achieve its goals.

Furthermore, the K-12 program has been criticized for its inability to address the country's job market needs. Despite the additional two years of education, many students still struggle to find employment opportunities after graduation. This is because the program has not focused on developing the necessary skills and knowledge that are relevant to the country's job market.

In conclusion, the K-12 program has not lived up to its promises of providing quality education and better job opportunities. Instead, it has placed a financial burden on families, failed to provide adequate facilities and teachers, and failed to address the country's job market needs. Therefore, it is time to consider terminating the program and finding alternative solutions that will truly benefit the students and the country as a whole.

Ang Pilipinas ay itinuturing na isa sa mga bansang mahihirap, pausbong at papaunlad pa lamang. Ngunit ang mga katagang ito ay halos ilang dekada na nating naririnig, na para bang hindi na natin makakamit ang pag unlad na mukhang mararanasan nalang natin kapag ang mga mata'y nakapikit.

Sa gitna ng mga pang araw-araw na krisis na hinaharap ng ating bansa, isa sa mga pang matagalang solusyon sa matagal ng palaisipang ito ay ang pag lulunsad ng programang K-12, o ang tinatawag na dagdag na dalawang taon sa paaralang sekondarya. Ilan sa mga positibong bagay na maaaring idulot nito ay ang pagkakaroon ng trabaho ng mga kapos-palad na mag aaral na walang kakayahang magpatuloy pa sa kolehiyo, pagkatapos nilang dumaan sa programang ito. Pagiging handa ng mga mag aaral sa pagsalubong sa kolehiyo at pagiging globally competitive ng Pilipinas sa malawakang aspeto. Ngunit sa kabila ng mga positibong bagay ay ang mga negatibong naidulot ng programang ito matapos ang dalawang taong pagpapatupad nito sa mga paaralan. Ang mga mag aaral ay lalo lamang nasanay sa sekondarya dahil sa dalawang taong dagdag, at sa halip na maging handa sa kolehiyo ay lalo lamang nagulat sa biglaang pagbabago sa klase ng sistemang pampaaralan pagkatapos nila sa K-12. Higit sa lahat, nahihirapang makakuha ng trabaho ang mga mag aaral na galing dito, sapagkat higit na nais parin ng mga kompanya ay mga manggagawa na nakapag tapos ng kurso sa kolehiyo.

Positibo man o negatibo, ang sarili lamang natin ang may hawak ng ating mga kapalaran. Tayo ang nagdedesisyon, nag iisip, kumikilos at nagaaral. Hindi man makamit ng Pilipinas ang mabilis na pag unlad, nararapat lamang na tayo, bilang mga mag aaral at tinatawag na pag asa ng bayan, ay mag aral ng mabuti para sa ikauunlad natin at upang matulungan ang kapwa natin na umunlad din.

30. Handa sa hamon ng buhay k-12 essay


Answer:

Handa sa Hamon ng Buhay

Ang buhay ng isang tao ay napakahirap intindihin. May mga oras na masaya, oras na malungkot at may mga oras na parang wala kang maramdaman. Bawat isa, bata o matanda, mahirap o mayaman, maraming kaibigan o kakaunti, lahat ay may kanya-kanyang kulay ng buhay na nangangahulugan lamang na iba-iba rin tayo ng mga kinakaharap na buhay.

Maraming hamon ang buhay. Maaaring hamon tungkol sa pamilya, kaibigan, paaralan o buhay pag-ibig. Ngunit kahit anong hamon ito, kailangan nating pagdaanan lahat yan at wala tayong ibang pagpipilian kung hindi ang maging handa. Hindi nagsisimula ang isang tao na matatag, kailangan mong maging buo upang maging matatag. Ibig sabihin, kilalanin mo ang sarili mo, pansinin mo ang mga maliliit na detalye. Punan mo kung ano yung kulang at bawasan mo yung sobra. Sa ganyang paraan, mas magiging handa ka.

Ang mga problema sa buhay ay nangyayari at dumarating hindi lang para maghirap tayo. Instrumento ito upang mas maging malakas tayo, upang matuto tayo para mas maging handa tayo sa kahit anumang ibigay ng buhay na pagsubok. Lahat ay may dahilan, ganoon din ang pagkakaroon ng problema.
