Answer:

"The Wedding Dance" by Amador Daguio is a story about Awiyao, a man from the Kalinga tribe in the Philippines, who is trying to have a child with his wife, Lumnay. The story is divided into three parts: the beginning, middle, and end.

The Beginning:

The story begins with Awiyao leaving his wife, Lumnay, in their small hut to attend a tribal dance. The dance is a traditional courtship ritual where men and women dance around a fire, and the women try to catch the men they want as their husbands. Awiyao is hoping to be caught by Madulimay, a young woman he has been admiring for some time. However, he is also aware that he is leaving behind his wife, Lumnay, who has been unable to bear him a child.

The Middle:

As the dance goes on, Awiyao realizes that Madulimay is not interested in him. He decides to leave the dance and return home to Lumnay. However, when he arrives, he finds that Lumnay has already left their hut to attend the dance. Awiyao goes after her, hoping to convince her to come back home with him. When he finds her, she is dancing with the other women, and Awiyao tries to join in. However, Lumnay is angry and refuses to dance with him. She tells him that she is tired of being neglected and forgotten, and that she wants to have a child with him. Awiyao tries to explain that he loves her, but Lumnay is too hurt to listen. In the end, Awiyao leaves the dance alone, and Lumnay stays behind to dance with the other women.

The End:

The story ends with Lumnay returning to their hut alone. She realizes that Awiyao will never leave his desire for a child, and that she will never be able to give him one. She is heartbroken but knows that she must move on with her life. As she looks up at the stars, she remembers the story of Kabunyan, the god of the Kalinga tribe, who had to sacrifice his own child to save his people. Lumnay realizes that she too must make a sacrifice for the sake of her tribe and her husband, and she decides to let Awiyao go.

Characterization:

Awiyao is the main character of the story. He is a strong and respected member of the Kalinga tribe, but he is also torn between his desire for a child and his love for his wife, Lumnay. He is portrayed as a traditional man who values his tribe's customs and traditions.

Lumnay is Awiyao's wife. She is a strong and independent woman who wants to have a child with her husband. She is also hurt by Awiyao's neglect and feels forgotten. Lumnay is characterized as a woman who is willing to make sacrifices for the sake of her tribe and her husband.

Madulimay is the young woman that Awiyao is hoping to be caught by. She is portrayed as a beautiful and desirable woman who is sought after by many men at the dance.

Kabunyan is the god of the Kalinga tribe. He is a central figure in the story, and his story of sacrifice is used as a metaphor for Lumnay's own sacrifice.

The Chief of the tribe and the Elders are minor characters in the story. They are portrayed as traditional men who value their tribe's customs and traditions. The Dancing Women are also minor characters who participate in the dance.
