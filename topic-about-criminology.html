Criminology is an interdisciplinary field in both the behavioural and social sciences, which draws primarily upon the research of sociologists, political scientists, economists, psychologists, philosophers, psychiatrists, biologists, social anthropologists, as well as scholars of law.

13. INTRODUCTION TO CRIMINOLOGYwhat did you learn about birth of criminology ​


(Apologies if my provided answer is incorrect)

Criminology is the scientific study of crime, criminal behavior, and the criminal justice system. It is a multidisciplinary field that draws on knowledge from sociology, psychology, law, and other disciplines to understand the causes, consequences, and control of criminal behavior.

The origins of criminology can be traced back to the 18th and 19th centuries, when a number of social and political changes led to the development of the modern criminal justice system. In particular, the Enlightenment period saw a shift towards rationalism and a belief in the power of reason to understand and solve social problems. This, along with the growth of the urban population and the increasing complexity of society, led to the development of new theories and approaches to crime and punishment.

One of the early pioneers of criminology was the Italian philosopher and statesman Cesare Beccaria, who wrote the influential treatise "On Crimes and Punishments" in 1764. In this work, Beccaria argued against the use of torture and the death penalty, and called for a system of punishment that was fair, proportionate, and focused on rehabilitation.

Other important figures in the early history of criminology include the French legal scholar François-Etienne de Saint-Cyran, the English philosopher and jurist Jeremy Bentham, and the Italian philosopher and economist Vilfredo Pareto.

Today, criminology is a thriving field with a wide range of sub-disciplines, including forensic criminology, environmental criminology, critical criminology, and criminal psychology. It continues to evolve and adapt to new developments in society, technology, and the criminal justice system.

17. INTRODUCTION TO CRIMINOLOGYWhat did you learn about this topicFACTORS CONTRIBUTING AND AFFECTING THE DEVELOPMENT AND EXISTENCE OF CRIMINALITY (GIVE 2 PRAGRAPH)GEOGRAPHICAL FACTORS. ​


Answer:

Criminologists have long studied the role of geographical factors in the development and existence of criminal behavior. Some of the key geographical factors that have been found to contribute to criminal activity include population density, urbanization, and access to resources.

For example, research has shown that higher population density can lead to increased competition for resources, which can in turn lead to a higher prevalence of criminal activity. Urbanization, or the process of people moving from rural areas to urban centers, can also lead to increased competition for resources and higher levels of crime.

In addition, access to resources such as education, employment opportunities, and social services can play a role in the development of criminal behavior. Those who have limited access to these resources may be more likely to turn to criminal activity as a means of survival or to gain access to the resources they need.

SOCIAL AND ECONOMIC FACTORS

In addition to geographical factors, social and economic factors can also play a role in the development and existence of criminal behavior. For example, poverty and social inequality can create conditions that foster criminal activity. Those who live in poverty may be more likely to engage in criminal behavior as a means of survival or to gain access to the resources they need to improve their lives.

Furthermore, social and economic disparities can create feelings of frustration and resentment among individuals, leading some to engage in criminal behavior as a way to express their discontent. In addition, the lack of access to education and employment opportunities can lead to a lack of positive outlets for individuals to channel their energy and talents, making them more likely to engage in criminal activity.

Overall, the development and existence of criminal behavior is influenced by a complex interplay of factors, including geographical, social, and economic factors. By understanding these factors, criminologists can better understand the causes of criminal behavior and work to develop effective strategies for preventing and reducing crime.

Biological factors refer to the physical, genetic, and chemical characteristics that are innate to living organisms. These factors can play a role in determining an individual’s physical characteristics, behavior, health, and overall wellbeing. Biological factors can include genetics, hormones, immunity, and environmental conditions, among many others. Many of these factors are interconnected and can affect one another, making it difficult to determine the causal relationship between them. Understanding biological factors is important for helping to diagnose and treat a variety of diseases, as well as to understand the complex nature of individual differences.

I learned that in criminology, the biological factor refers to the role of genetics and other biological factors in determining an individual's likelihood of committing a crime. Research has shown that certain genetic variations may be associated with an increased risk of criminal behavior, and that individuals with certain neurological conditions or brain abnormalities may also be more likely to engage in criminal behavior. However, the relationship between biology and crime is complex and not fully understood, and many other factors, such as social and environmental influences, also play a role in determining an individual's likelihood of committing a crime.
