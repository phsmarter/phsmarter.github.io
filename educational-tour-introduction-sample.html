23. In an educational tour 96 of the pupils were from the primary level this was 4 10 of the 240pupils who joined the tour which would tell us the rate the rate of the pupils in the primary level who joined the tour explain you answer


Step-by-step explanation:

To find the rate of pupils in the primary level who joined the tour, we can use the following formula:

Rate = (Number of pupils in primary level who joined the tour / Total number of pupils who joined the tour) x 100%

From the given information, we know that 96 pupils were from the primary level and this represents 410 out of the total 240 pupils who joined the tour. Therefore:

Number of pupils in primary level who joined the tour = 96

Total number of pupils who joined the tour = 240

Number of pupils in primary level who joined the tour / Total number of pupils who joined the tour = 410/240

Number of pupils in primary level who joined the tour / Total number of pupils who joined the tour = 1.7083 (rounded to four decimal places)

To convert this to a percentage, we multiply by 100:

Rate = 1.7083 x 100% = 170.83%

Therefore, the rate of pupils in the primary level who joined the tour is 170.83%. This result indicates that the number of pupils from the primary level who joined the tour is more than the total number of pupils who joined the tour, which is not possible. This suggests that there may be an error in the given information or calculations.
