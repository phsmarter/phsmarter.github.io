2. environmental problem in zambales essay ​


Answer:

Environmental problems in Zambales, and in many parts of the world, are a major concern. Some of the major environmental issues in Zambales include deforestation, soil erosion, water pollution, and the loss of biodiversity.

Deforestation is a major issue in Zambales. Trees are being cut down at an alarming rate to make way for agriculture, mining, and other forms of development. This has led to soil erosion, which can cause landslides and other natural disasters. It has also contributed to the loss of habitat for many species of plants and animals, which can have negative effects on the overall biodiversity of the region.

Water pollution is another major environmental problem in Zambales. Industrial waste and sewage are often dumped into rivers and other water sources, contaminating them and making them unsafe for human consumption. This can have serious health consequences for the people who rely on these water sources for their daily needs.

The loss of biodiversity is also a major concern in Zambales. Many species of plants and animals are threatened or endangered due to habitat loss, poaching, and other forms of human activity. This can have negative impacts on the overall health of the ecosystem and can also lead to the extinction of some species.

Overall, environmental problems in Zambales are a serious concern and require urgent attention and action. It is important for government officials, local communities, and individuals to work together to find solutions and protect the environment for future generations.

Zambales, a province in the Philippines, is facing a number of environmental problems. The most pressing of these is the deforestation and degradation of the province's forests. The province has lost a significant portion of its forest cover due to illegal logging, slash-and-burn agriculture, and residential and commercial development. This has resulted in soil erosion, loss of biodiversity, and a decrease in the amount of water available for local communities.

Illegal mining is also a major environmental concern in Zambales. The province is home to numerous mining operations, and the mining activities have caused severe water and air pollution. The mining operations have also disturbed the province's fragile ecosystems, causing further loss of biodiversity.

The increase in population in Zambales has also had a negative impact on the environment. The increasing population has caused a rise in waste generation, leading to water and air pollution. The lack of proper waste management systems has led to the dumping of waste in rivers and oceans, which further exacerbates water pollution.

In addition, climate change is also a major concern in Zambales. The province is particularly vulnerable to extreme weather events, such as floods and droughts, due to its location in the Pacific Ring of Fire. As a result, Zambales is also at risk of experiencing more frequent and intense weather-related disasters.

Given the environmental problems facing Zambales, it is essential that the local government takes action to protect the province's environment. answer

1. Justify the choice of the problem.

Apply the criteria we’ve listed above – frequency, duration, range, severity, equity, perception – as well as asking yourself whether your organization or another can address it effectively, in order to decide whether the problem is one that you should focus on.

Let’s take the problem we used as an example earlier: The percentage of overweight and obese children in the community has been steadily increasing, and now approaches 25%. Since we know that childhood obesity tends to lead to adult obesity, and that obesity and being overweight are linked to chronic conditions – diabetes, heart disease, stroke – this is a problem that needs to be addressed now. Our organization has the will and the ability to do it.

2. Frame the problem.

State the problem without implying a solution or blaming anyone, so that you can analyze it without any assumptions and build consensus around whatever solution you arrive at. One way is to state it in terms of a lack of a positive behavior, condition, or other factor, or the presence or size of a negative behavior, condition, or other factor.

There are too many children in the community who are overweight or obese. The problem is particularly serious among low-income families.

3. Identify whose behavior and/or what and how environmental factors need to change for the problem to begin to be solved.

This can be as straightforward as individuals changing their behavior from smoking to not smoking, or as complex as persuading legislators to change laws and policies (e.g., non-smoking ordinances) in order to change others’ behavior (smokers don’t smoke in buildings or enclosed spaces used by the public) in order to benefit yet another group by changing the environment (children are protected from secondhand smoke in public.)

All, and particularly low-income, children should have the opportunity and the motivation to eat more healthily and exercise more. Parents may need to change their children’s – and perhaps their own – diets, and schools may need to adjust their lunch programs and exercise schedules. In low-income neighborhoods, there needs to be greater access to healthy food and more safe places for children to play or participate in sports, both outdoors and indoors.

“Social determinants” refer to broader social factors, such as income inequality or social exclusion, that lead to or influence health and development outcomes.

Income inequality. Once a country has reached the point of development where most deaths come not from infectious diseases (tuberculosis, dysentery, cholera, malaria, flu, pneumonia, etc.), but from chronic diseases (heart disease, diabetes, cancer), the economic and social equality within the society is a greater determinant of death rates and average lifespan than the country’s position with regard to others.

Social connectedness. Many studies indicate that “belonging” – whether to a large extended family, a network of friends, a social or volunteer organization, or a faith community – is related to longer life and better health, as well as to community participation.

Sense of personal or collective efficacy. This refers to people’s sense of control over their lives. People with a higher sense or stronger history of efficacy tend to live longer, maintain better health, and participate more vigorously in civic life.
