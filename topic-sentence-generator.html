WHY SHOULD THE THREE BASIC ELEMENTS OF PARAGRAPH (THE TOPIC, TOPIC SENTENCE/GENERAL STATEMENT, AND SUPPORTING DETAILS) NEEDS TO BE CONSISTENT WITH THE IDEA?

The building components of papers are graphs. Without a proper written paragraphs that enumerates ideas from one topic to the next and that provide support the fundamental topic being studied or discussed in any understandable way, your reader might not understand what you are trying to convey. A good written paragraph should be well understood by the reader.

3 BASIC ELEMENTS OF  A PARAGRAPHTopic Sentence or a subject sentence states the topic sentence or the main idea of the paragraph. The most important sentence in the paragraph, the subject sentence, caught the reader's attention to the significant concepts provided in depth in the following lines (called supporting sentences).The supporting sentences that go along with the theme. The supporting sentences in a paragraph elaborate on the main idea given by the topic phrase. This development can take the form of examples, reasons, explanation, and so on, depending on the purpose of your paragraph. For example, if you're writing an opinion paragraph, your supporting sentences will include logic and specifics. If you're writing a description, your supporting sentences will give specific details to help the reader develop a visual.The concluding sentence either reminds the reader of the paragraph's theme and core notion or presents the primary idea in a new way.
