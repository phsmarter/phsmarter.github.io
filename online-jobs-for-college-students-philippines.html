7. what is the importance of the study in investigating academic performance in online learning of the college students, through qualitative research?​


Answer:

Qualitative research investigating academic performance in online learning of college students is important as it provides valuable insights into student experiences, informs the design of effective online learning practices, enhances student support services, and contributes to the academic literature on the topic.

Explanation:

The investigation of academic performance in online learning of college students through qualitative research is important for several reasons:

1. Understanding student experiences: Qualitative research allows researchers to explore and understand students' experiences with online learning in-depth. By analyzing students' perceptions, attitudes, and behaviors, researchers can identify factors that affect academic performance in online learning, such as motivation, engagement, and self-regulated learning.

2. Improving online learning practices: The findings from qualitative research can inform the design and implementation of online learning practices that support student success. For example, the research may reveal effective instructional strategies, such as providing clear instructions, fostering interaction and collaboration, and using multimedia materials.

3. Enhancing student support: The study can also identify factors that can hinder students' academic performance, such as technological barriers, lack of social support, and academic stress. The results can inform the development of support services and interventions that address these barriers and promote student success in online learning.

4. Contributing to the literature: Qualitative research can contribute to the academic literature on online learning by providing rich, detailed descriptions of student experiences and perspectives. This can help to build theory and inform future research on the topic.

Overall, investigating academic performance in online learning of college students through qualitative research can provide valuable insights that can help to enhance the effectiveness of online learning and support student success.

Explanation:

This study sought to identify predictors of suicidal behavior among college students who are psychotherapy clients, as well as to determine underlying classes of clients with suicidal ideation. Data were gathered from 101,570 clients, 391 of whom engaged in suicide behavior during treatment. Regression analyses revealed that suicide behavior was positively associated with 3 pretreatment variables: depression, prior suicide behavior, and prior nonsuicidal self-injury. Four latent classes of clients with suicidal ideation were identified that were named "prior ideation," "extensive risk," "prior treatment," and "circumscribed depression." The number of clients in each class varied widely, as did the relative risk of suicide behavior. Implications for treatment, suicide assessment, and suicide prevention are discussed.
