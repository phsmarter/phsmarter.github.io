1. Asian literature vs Philippines literature


One cannot make a direct comparison between Asian and Philippine literature as the latter is actually an example of Asian literature, specifically Southeast Asian literature. We can, however, discuss common themes where famous Philippine literary works were rooted from. 
During the pre-colonial period, stories were passed on by word of mouth, which eventually became written works. Nature and beauty were the focal points of these stories, and these connect its audience to the world of gods and goddesses. Think of the famous Philippine epics and legends, for example, where superhuman beings overpower the mortals.
Then came the Spaniards who introduced Christianity to Filipinos. In the beginning, most of the published works were religious and secular articles used to propagate Catholicism and filial piety. Books about morality and etiquette emerged. Years later, narratives and essays about politics and social structure surfaced. Nationalism then became the central theme in Philippine literature.
Post-colonial literature surfaced after the war. These mostly talked about human experiences while the country was under siege--being marginalized, tortured, and how the war completely changed peoples' lives. These works continued to circulate years after the Martial Law has ended. These literary works carried so much angst just so the writer can strike back at the so-called center of power.

5. explain the difference of JPEG vs. GIF vs. PNG vs. BMP vs. TIFF


JPEG

(Joint Photographic Experts Group)

is a File Format with a lossy compression. It allows some data loss for a smaller file size. It compresses grayscale and color information but allows for images to be displayed in 16,000,000 colors.

GIF

(Graphics Interchange Format)

is a File Format with a lossless compression. Meaning, there is no data lost in the compression. GIF supports animation and is preferred for images with only a few colors. (GIF only allows for 256 colors)

PNG

(Portable Network Graphics)

is a File Format that takes off from the transparency capabilities of GIF, primarily developed due to GIF's licensing requirement.

TIFF

(Tagged Image File Format)

is a File Format that is a flexible bitmap image that is supported by many software. It has a much larger file size compared to JPEG and GIF and is commonly recommended by professional printers since it is supported by most press machines.

BMP

(Bitmap)

The standrad Windows file format on DOS and Windows–compatible platforms. It has a lossless compression.

16. RP1 ACT2B COMPARING AND CONTRASTING EXPRESSIONS. Without going back to the lessons in this module, compare and contrast each given pair of expression. Words in Pairs Comparison Contrast 1. Research vs. Report Writing 2. Author-date system Humanities VS. system 3. MLA vs. APA 4. One-line quotation vs. Five- line quotation 5. Conclusion vs. Recommendation Left-Right 6. margin Top-Bottom margin 7. Abstract vs. Introduction 8. language Literary Language VS. Academic VS. 9. Passive voice vs. Active voice 10. Research report VS. Literature report​


Answer:

1.) Research vs. Report Writing

Comparison: Both research and report writing involve gathering and presenting information.

Contrast: Research is the process of investigating a topic or problem, while report writing is the act of presenting the findings and conclusions of the research.

2.) Author-date system vs. Humanities VS. system

Comparison: Both systems are used to cite sources in academic writing.

Contrast: The Author-date system is commonly used in the social sciences and sciences, while the Humanities VS. system is commonly used in the humanities.

3.) MLA vs. APA

Comparison: Both are citation styles used in academic writing.

Contrast: MLA is primarily used in the humanities, while APA is primarily used in the social sciences.

4.) One-line quotation vs. Five-line quotation

Comparison: Both types of quotations are used to incorporate the ideas of others into one's own writing.

Contrast: One-line quotations are shorter and are generally used to support smaller points, while five-line quotations are longer and are generally used to support larger points.

5.) Conclusion vs. Recommendation

Comparison: Both conclusion and recommendation provide an end to the discussion and offer a course of action.

Contrast: Conclusions are drawn from the results of a study, while recommendations are suggestions for future actions.

6.) Margin Top-Bottom margin

Comparison: Both margins serve a similar purpose, to provide space between the text and the edge of the page.

Contrast: The top and bottom margins determine the distance between the text and the page edges at the top and bottom of the page, respectively.

7.) Abstract vs. Introduction

Comparison: Both abstracts and introductions provide an overview of the paper.

Contrast: Abstracts are brief summaries of the paper's main points, while introductions provide background information, context, and thesis statements.

8.) Literary Language VS. Academic Language

Comparison: Both types of language are used in writing.

Contrast: Literary language is used in literature and creative writing, while academic language is used in scholarly research and writing.

9.) Passive voice vs. Active voice

Comparison: Both passive and active voice are grammatical constructions used to convey meaning in writing.

Contrast: Passive voice emphasizes the object or recipient of the action, while active voice emphasizes the subject or doer of the action.

10.) Research report VS. Literature report

Comparison: Both types of reports present information on a topic.

Contrast: Research reports present findings from research studies, while literature reports analyze existing literature on a topic.
