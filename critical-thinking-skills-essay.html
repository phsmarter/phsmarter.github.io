Ecosystem: A community of living and non-living things interacting in a specific environment.

Predation: The relationship between two species where one organism (the predator) hunts and kills the other organism (the prey) for food.

Symbiosis: A close and prolonged relationship between two different species in which both benefit from the interaction.

Commensalism: A relationship between two species in which one benefits and the other is neither helped nor harmed.

Mutualism: A relationship between two species in which both benefit from the interaction.

C.ESSAY

The ecosystem is a complex system of living organisms and their physical environment, and it greatly affects the environment in several ways. The first way is through the balance of species in the ecosystem. Each species plays a unique role in maintaining the balance of the environment, and the loss of a species can have a chain reaction that alters the entire system.

The second way the ecosystem affects the environment is through the cycling of materials and energy. In a healthy ecosystem, materials and energy are cyclically moved from one organism to another, but if the ecosystem is disrupted, this cycle can be disrupted, leading to changes in the availability of materials and energy and potential environmental harm.

The ecosystem plays a role in maintaining soil and water quality. Healthy ecosystems help to maintain soil fertility and water quality through the actions of soil-building organisms, such as earthworms and bacteria, and by filtering pollutants from water. However, human activities such as deforestation and overgrazing that damage ecosystems can result in soil erosion, nutrient depletion, and water pollution.

In conclusion, the ecosystem has a significant impact on the environment, and it is important to understand and protect the relationships between living organisms and the environment to maintain a healthy and sustainable ecosystem and preserve the environment for future generations.
