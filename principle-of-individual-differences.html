Describe how the different elements and principles were used by expressionist artist to convey their individual style.Discuss their three examples from works in this learner's material.

Remember the visual elements and design principles ... this  involves looking at the line,shape,color,space,point of the piece.

If an artist produces two-dimensional or three dimensional art, works in a traditional medium such as painting, or makes art using the latest technology, both artists use the same basic visual form building blocks (elements) and visual structure techniques (principles) to achieve

        visual unity.

Identify and define the five elements of design

1. The Point

A point is the visual dimension which is the foundation of all others.  

It can be defined as a spatially singularity or, in geometrical terms, the region where two coordinates meet. If an artist marks a simple point on a surface (also referred to as the ground), they establish an immediate  

connection between figure and ground.

This is, they split the job between its surface and all that has been applied to it.  

Our eyes discriminate between the two and their arrangement has much to do with how we perceive a final composition. The point itself can be used as a shape making way. Pointillism, for example, is a form of painting made  

popular by the late nineteenth-century French artist Georges Seurat.

2. Definitions and Qualities of Line

Essentially, you build a line when you bring two or more dots together.  

A line can be described as a point in motion, lyrically. There are several  

different types of lines all distinguished by greater length than width.  

Depending on how the artist wants to use them, lines may be static or  

dynamic. They aid in deciding the movement, direction and energy of an art  

work.

3. Shapes: Positive, Negative and Planar Issues

A shape is known as a two dimensional, enclosed space.  By nature shapes are often suggested by design and square. They can be generated in many ways, the simplest  

of which is to enclose an region with an outline. They can also be  

created by covering an area with other types or by putting various  

textures next to each other — for example, the shape of an island surrounded by water.Forms do much of the heavy lifting in arranging works,

since they are more complex than lines.

4. Space

Space is the empty region surrounding objects which are actual or

implied. Human beings categorize space: outer space, the boundless

vacuum we enter beyond our sky; inner space, which exists in the

minds and imaginations of men, and personal space;An significant but intangible area surrounding each person and violated if someone else gets too close to each other. Pictorial space is flat, and cyberspace is  

where the digital world exists. Art is reacting to all those kinds of space.

5. Color

Due to the combinations and variations inherent in its use color is the  

most complex artistic feature. Humans react differently to color  

combinations and artists partly research and use color to give their work the desired direction.In several types of art color is important. Within a given work its importance, use and purpose depends on the medium of that work. Although some color definitions are commonly common in the media, others aren't. Newton discovered that by moving it through a  

prism, white light could be separated into a spectra. Color study in art  

and design often begins with the theory of colors. The theory of color  

breaks down colors into three categories: primary, secondary and

tertiary.
