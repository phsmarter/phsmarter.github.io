13. How to apply forCHEDscholarship?​


Answer:

For those who would like to apply for Commission on Higher Education scholarship (Student Financial Assistance Programs - StuFAPs), read the details below.

download and fill-out the CHED StuFAPs Application Formsubmit the fully-accomplished application form together with pertinent documentary requirements directly to the CHED Regional Office where the schools that you graduated from are basedsubmission of application is from March 1, 2020 to May 31, 2020

Scholarship Qualifications

Applicants must meet ALL the qualifications to qualify for CHED scholarship program.

Must be an incoming freshman or a college-level studentMust be a Filipino citizenMust be a senior high school graduate and/or candidate for graduationThe family gross income must not exceed three hundred thousand pesos (Php 400,000.00)*Must avail of only one CHED scholarship or financial assistance programMust not be a graduate of any degree program

* In highly exceptional cases where income exceeds PhP400,000.00, an applicant must present a written certification or medical findings of illness of a family member, or school certifications of two or more dependents enrolled in college.

CHED's Types of Scholarships

1. Full Scholarship

High school graduates must have a GWA of at least 90% or its equivalentMust have at least 90% GWA in 3rd year and in the first 3 grading periods of the fourth year

2. Partial Scholarship

High school graduates must have a GWA of at least 85% or its equivalentMust have at least 85% GWA in 3rd year and in the first 3 grading periods of the fourth year

High school report card for incoming freshmen students eligible for college.Candidate for Graduation in Senior High SchoolDuly certified true copy of Photocopy of grades in Grade 11 and 1st semester of Grade 12.Certified true copy of the birth certificateProof of Income (any of the following)

14. essay about scholarship,why do you deserve to be a scholar?​


Essay about scholarship

You can answer why do you deserve to be a scholar by example below:
I deserve this scholarship not because I deserve anything, but because I may provide a great return on your investment. I work hard, am motivated by myself, have achievable goals, and want to contribute to the advancement of society as a whole. Fundamentally, it is about others rather than myself. What can I do to make the world a better place? How can I improve things for others in my own way? Your scholarship will assist me in achieving my goals. My dream is to grow up to be a valuable person who makes a significant contribution to society. Through this scholarship, I will be able to continue my studies and expand my attitude for the improvement of the society.

I have so far gained knowledge about community involvement through volunteer work. My desire to make a greater social contribution in the future with my expertise is stimulated by these activities.

Here are some guidelines for preparing an essay on why you should award you a scholarship:

-Describe how the scholarship funds would help you achieve your long-term objectives.

-Concentrate on the scholarship's purpose. Consider the purpose of the scholarship and its funding source when you write your essay. By demonstrating your prior accomplishment, you might inspire hope for your future success.

-Use a thesis statement in the same way that you would in any other essay. All throughout the essay, you should make references to your argument and weave it into your summary.

-Focus on how you overcame challenges in your life when sharing them. Show your ability to solve problems

-Avoid making generalizations; statements like "I deserve this because I work hard" are insufficient. What distinguishes you as unique, noteworthy, and special?

-Support your argument with a personal success from your life and upbringing.
