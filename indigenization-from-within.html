26. In what way the rights of Indigenous people differ from the right of non-Indigenous people ? Is there stated in Indigenous people that doesn't exist to non-indigenous people Yes, cite some examples and explain briefly your answer.​


Answer:

Indigenous peoples, also known as First Nations, Aboriginal peoples, or Tribal peoples, are groups of people who are the original inhabitants of a specific region or country, and who have a distinct culture, language, and identity. Indigenous peoples often have rights that are recognized and protected by national and international laws, and these rights may differ from those of non-Indigenous peoples.

One example of a right that is specifically recognized for Indigenous peoples is the right to self-determination. This means that Indigenous peoples have the right to determine their own political status and pursue their own economic, social, and cultural development. This may include the right to control and manage their own lands, resources, and institutions.

Other examples of rights that may be recognized for Indigenous peoples include:

The right to maintain and revitalize their cultural practices and traditions.The right to education in their own language and in a manner that reflects their culture and traditions.The right to participate in decisions that affect their lands, resources, and rights.The right to receive fair compensation for the use of their lands or resources.These rights may not be recognized or protected for non-Indigenous peoples in the same way. It is important to note that the rights of Indigenous peoples can vary depending on the specific laws and policies of a given country or region.

28. five indigenous instruments from Latin American music​


Answer:

1. Pandeiro

This instrument resembles a tambourine. It is held by the hand and comes with small cymbals around its side. It’s widely popular in Brazilian music especially capoeira and samba. This instrument is considered versatile as it can be tuned to your liking. It can also be played in different ways including using the fingers and the whole palm.

2. Conga

With its origins from Cuba, this single-headed drum commonly comes in a set of two. Each of the drums is tuned to a specific pitch, giving the drums its rhythmic beat. You can use various sounds with the conga by striking the instrument on its head using your hand.

3. Güiro

This handheld musical instrument is also believed to originate from Cuba. It is made with a piece of metal or wood. It is hollowed out and comes with grooves that run horizontally along its side. The güiro is played similar to a washboard – it’s held in one hand and using a stick, it’s scraped, which creates a rattling sound. Another way to play it is to strike it using a stick.

4. Timbale

This instrument is an essential part of variousLatin music including meringue, salsa, and mambo. It has a resemblance to the conga, in such a way that it is played in pairs. However, the timbale comes with metal rims and is shallower than the conga. The timbale heads give a resonant and high-pitched tone because the drum heads are tuned high. It can be played by striking a stick to its head or metal casing.

5. Maracas

The maracas are believed to originate from various places including Venezuela and Puerto Rico. It is not only popular in Latin American music but also all over the world as a back-up instrument. Probably the major reason behind its popularity is its simplicity to play. The maracas are made of a hollowed-out shell with a handle. Inside the shell are dried seeds, so that when the instrument is shaken, the maracas produce a rattling sound.
