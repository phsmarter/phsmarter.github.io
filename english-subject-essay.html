3. essay example for english subject


Answer:

The Five Paragraph Essay

Though more advanced academic papers are a category all their own, the basic high school or college essay has the following standardized, five paragraph structure:

Paragraph 1: Introduction

Paragraph 2: Body 1

Paragraph 3: Body 2

Paragraph 4: Body 3

Paragraph 5: Conclusion

Though it may seem formulaic – and, well, it is - the idea behind this structure is to make it easier for the reader to navigate the ideas put forth in an essay. You see, if your essay has the same structure as every other one, any reader should be able to quickly and easily find the information most relevant to them.


4. Essay about integration of music across learning discipline like in mathematics, Science English Mapeh and Filipino Subjects.​


Answer:

Music is a universal language that has the power to connect people from different cultures, backgrounds, and ages. It is also a versatile tool that can be integrated into various learning disciplines, including mathematics, science, English, MAPEH, and Filipino subjects. The integration of music across these disciplines can enhance learning outcomes, promote creativity, and improve student engagement.

In mathematics, music can be used to teach concepts such as rhythm, patterns, and fractions. For example, students can learn about time signatures and beats in music, which can help them understand fractions and division in mathematics. Moreover, music can also help students develop their problem-solving skills by analyzing and interpreting musical compositions.

In science, music can be used to teach concepts such as sound waves, frequency, and resonance. Students can learn about the physics of sound by exploring how different instruments produce different sounds and how sound travels through different mediums. Additionally, music can also be used to teach biology by exploring the anatomy and physiology of the human ear.

In English, music can be used to teach literary elements such as theme, symbolism, and imagery. Students can analyze song lyrics and explore how they relate to literary works. Moreover, music can also be used to teach language skills such as vocabulary, grammar, and pronunciation.

In MAPEH, music is already an integral part of the curriculum. However, it can be further integrated into other areas such as physical education, arts, and health. For example, students can learn about the history and culture of different music genres and how they relate to different dance styles. Additionally, music can also be used to promote mental and emotional health by exploring the therapeutic benefits of music.

In Filipino subjects, music can be used to teach cultural and historical concepts such as folk songs, traditional dances, and national anthems. Students can learn about the different regions of the Philippines and how their music and dance styles reflect their unique culture and traditions.

In conclusion, the integration of music across different learning disciplines can enhance learning outcomes, promote creativity, and improve student engagement. By incorporating music into various subjects, educators can create a more holistic and dynamic learning environment that meets the diverse needs of students.
