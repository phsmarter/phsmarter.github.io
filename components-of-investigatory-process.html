1. what are the components of the investigatory process?


Before I enumerate the components of the investigatory process, let us define first scientific investigation. Scientific investigation is the orderly way that scientists {and other people} use to solve different problems. Hence, from this definition we could identify the first component which is stating the problem. Without the proper identification of the problem, the investigation process will not continue because it serves as the goal of the study. From there the next component could be applied which is observing. Some consider this as the first step, however, I go with others that this has to be the second. It is because after having a specific problem, one has to research and look for possible ways to find an answer. This part usually takes time in order to prevent drastic mistakes along the whole process. Third is formulating the hypothesis. From observation, one can have an intelligent presumption on how to solve the problem. And this has to be tested which leads us to the fourth component, experimenting. Applying the possible way to answer the stated inquiry could also take so much time. Example of investigatory project that could take years is regarding the growth of some living things. Since growth cannot be manipulated, one has to wait patiently for the result. Moreover, after gathering the data from the experiment, the fifth component is drawing a conclusion. It is the discussion whether the hypothesis was correct in achieving the solution to the problem or it has to be changed in order to find a better result. I hope these five components which are also related to the scientific method would help you.

The scientific method attempts to minimize the influence of bias or prejudice in the experimenter. Even the best-intentioned scientists can't escape bias. It results from personal beliefs, as well as cultural beliefs, which means any human filters information based on his or her own experience. Unfortunately, this filtering process can cause a scientist to prefer one outcome over another. For someone trying to solve a problem around the house, succumbing to these kinds of biases is not such a big deal. But in the scientific community, where results have to be reviewed and duplicated, bias must be avoided at all costs.
