1. The picture below a summation of what qualitative research is. Write an essay about your understanding of qualitative research and its importance in our lives. Include the different types of qualitative research in your essay​


Qualitative research is a type of research that focuses on understanding the meaning, experience, and interpretation of phenomena from the perspectives of those who are experiencing them. It is often used to gain a deeper understanding of complex social and human behavior, and is particularly useful for studying topics that are difficult to measure quantitatively.

One of the key features of qualitative research is that it is grounded in the participants' own experiences and perspectives. It typically involves the use of techniques such as in-depth interviews, focus groups, and observations to collect data, which is then analyzed and interpreted to uncover patterns, themes, and insights.

The importance of qualitative research in our lives is that it helps us to understand the complexity and diversity of human experiences and social phenomena. It can provide valuable insights into the beliefs, attitudes, and behaviors of individuals and groups, and can help us to understand the social and cultural context in which they occur. For example, qualitative research has been used to study topics such as mental health, substance abuse, education, and poverty.

There are several different types of qualitative research, each with its own set of methods and techniques. Some of the most common types include:

Ethnography: This type of research involves immersing oneself in a particular culture or community to gain an in-depth understanding of its customs, beliefs, and practices. It typically involves the use of ethnographic techniques such as participant observation, interviewing, and the analysis of artifacts and documents.

Case study research: This type of research involves in-depth examination of a particular individual, group, or organization. It is often used to understand the context and dynamics of a specific situation or problem.

Narrative research: This type of research involves the collection and analysis of personal stories and experiences. It is used to understand how individuals make sense of their lives and experiences, and can provide insights into the social and cultural factors that shape their perspectives.

Grounded theory: This type of research involves the development of a theory that emerges from data collected from the research participants. It is often used to study complex social phenomena and seeks to identify patterns and themes in the data.

Qualitative research provides a unique and valuable perspective on the complexities of human experience, and has important implications for policy, practice, and future research. It allows us to understand the meaning and experience of social phenomena in ways that quantitative research cannot, and provides a valuable tool for addressing pressing social and policy issues.

9. Distinguish Qualitative Research from Qualitative Research


QUALITATIVE

- interpretation-based, descriptive, and relating to language

- focuses more on personal accounts or documents that illustrate in detail how people think or respond within society

- categorical variable

- involve “open-ended” research verbs such as explore, understand, narrate, articulate [meanings], discover, or develop.

- depends on the verbal and written data

- collected using questionnaires, interviews, or observation, and frequently appears in narrative form

QUANTITATIVE

- numbers-based, countable, or measurable

- "what" or "how many" aspects of a research question

- numeric variable

- involve “closed-ended” research verbs such as determine, measure, correlate, explain, compare, validate, identify, or examine

- utilizes statistical and analytical statements to get a conclusion

- gathered using instruments, such as a questionnaire/surveys which includes a ratings scale or a thermometer to collect weather data

1. Summary of Qualitative Research Techniques

Qualitative research is an approach that is used to gain insight into people's thoughts, opinions, and experiences. It involves the use of interviews, observations, and focus groups to collect data. The data collected is analyzed and interpreted to draw conclusions. The main goal of qualitative research is to understand the underlying reasons and motivations behind human behavior.

2. Three Specific Techniques Used in Qualitative Research

a. Interviews: Interviews are conversations between the researcher and the participant. The goal is to gain an understanding of the participant's experiences, attitudes, and beliefs. Interviews can be conducted face-to-face, over the phone, or online.

b. Observations: Observations involve the researcher watching and recording the behavior of the participants. The goal is to understand how people interact with each other and their environment. Observations can be done in natural settings, such as homes or workplaces, or in controlled settings, such as a laboratory.

c. Focus groups: Focus groups involve a group of people discussing a particular topic. The goal is to gain insight into the opinions and attitudes of the group. Focus groups can be conducted in person or online.

3. Potential Application of Qualitative Research

In the field of education, qualitative research can be used to gain insight into the experiences and attitudes of students, teachers, and administrators. For example, a researcher might conduct interviews with students to understand their experiences with online learning during the COVID-19 pandemic. This information could be used to improve online learning programs and make them more effective.

4. Example of Qualitative Research in the Real World

A study conducted by Grol-Prokopczyk (2019) used qualitative research to understand the experience of women who did not have children. The study found that these women faced social stigma and pressure to conform to societal expectations. The implications of this research are that society needs to be more accepting of women who choose not to have children.
