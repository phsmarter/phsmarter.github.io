8. Essay State the reasons why schedule I and II are risky and not prescribed by the doctor?​


Answer:

Schedule I drugs are those prescription drugs that have a high potential for drug abuse and overuse. Currently these drugs, which include heroin and marijuana, have no accepted medical use. Schedule II drugs are narcotics such as morphine, opium, and fentanyl may lead to severe psychological or physical dependence.

It's the negative effects of the drug to the person that makes doctors shy away from prescribing these medications, except in scenarios when the benefit of having the patients take them clearly outweigh the potential or abuse and psychological dependence. The human body can only do so much in processing and detoxifying these harmful substances and that's the reason why doctors do everything they can to veer away from prescribing them.
