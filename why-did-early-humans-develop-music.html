1. Why did the music of the early Christians practically use no musical instruments, but only the human voice?​


Answer:

The music of the first Christians practically did not use musical instruments, but only the human voice, because it led to the distortion of the individual.

In this way, the first Christians believed that the sounds of the instruments would distort the citizens, therefore, they did not allow the people to practice it.

Thus, the songs were performed in a single voice, called monodic, with a very simple music structure and little flexibility.

They preferred sculpture and painting to music. Where they had the habit of making sculptures in tombs, and painting in frescoes (paintings made on the ceiling, with the base made in plaster or mortar).

Therefore, this is the reason why the music of the first Christians practically did not use musical instruments, but only the human voice.

5. 1. How was Culture developed through the physical changes that occured in early humans? 2.Why is bipedalism an important development in the physical capacity of humans? 3. Why is it important to study Human Evolution ? 4. Explain why should the early humans adapt to their environment?​


Answer:

Culture began/begins with a child being born to a mother. The mother brings her “expectations” to the baby and we could say that the baby brings their evolution (and temperament) determined expectations to the mother. So there are initial cultural conflicts between the mother and child.

Some mothers adapt well in this process (to the child rearing/training process) as they make efforts to have the child conform to their own cultural expectations while persisting while the child expresses their in-born culture.

So we can go all the way back to this initial mother+child cultural adaptation process. You can see how one mother could mold their child into ongoing dependence and another into increasing independence. Later, the influence of the father can help to further transition the child into greater independence.

This does not always transition smoothly (mother to father) and of course their are wide variations in how much dependence and risk-aversion the child and adults will have.

Societies generally homogenize the local/regional family cultures into a general common social culture. There might also be some more utopian/ideal goal that the family and/or society might promote to increase “social progress” in the culture.

27. 2. Describe the stages of early human development.​


Answer:

Development of the human body is the process of growth to maturity. The process begins with fertilization, where an egg released from the ovary of a female is penetrated by a sperm cell from a male. The resulting zygote develops through mitosis and cell differentiation, and the resulting embryo then implants in the uterus, where the embryo continues development through a fetal stage until birth. Further growth and development continues after birth, and includes both physical and psychological development, influenced by genetic, hormonal, environmental and other factors.
