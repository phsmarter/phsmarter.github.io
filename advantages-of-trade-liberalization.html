4. pagpatupad nang trade liberalization​


Answer:Ano ang ibig sabihin ng pagpapatupad ng "trade liberalization"? Ang ibig sabihin ng pagpapatupad ng "trade liberalization" ay ang pagtanggal o pagbawas ng mga restriksyon sa pakikipag-kalakal ng mga bansa. Kabilang sa mga restriksyon na ito ay mga taripa, kota, at iba pa. Kaugnay nito, ang iba pang detalye tungkol sa pagpapatupad ng "trade liberalization" ay narito.

I. Ano ang iba pang mahalagang detalye tungkol sa "trade liberalization"?

Sa ilalim ng "trade liberalization", inaalis o binabawasan ang mga restriksyon o harang sa pagkakaroon ng malayang kalakalan sa pagitan ng mga bansa.

Naniniwala ang mga ekonomista na ang "trade liberalization" ay nakakapagsulong ng isang malayang kalakalan ng mga produkto at serbisyo.

Bukod dito, naniniwala ang mga ekonomista na ang "trade liberalization" ay nakakapagpaganda sa ekonomiya ng mga bansa.

II. Ano ang halimbawa ng mga restriksyon sa kalakalan?

Narito ang ilang halimbawa ng mga restriksyon sa kalakalan:

taripa - Ang taripa ay buwis o bayarin na binabayaran sa mga partikular na "import" o "export".

kota - Ang kota ay tumutukoy sa takdang dami na maaaring angkatin mula sa ibang bansa o iluwas sa ibang bansa.

mga lisensya - Ang mga lisensya ay mga dokumento na kinakailangang makuha bago makipagkalakal.

Iyan ang mga detalye tungkol sa pagpapatupad ng "trade liberalization". Narito ang iba pang mga links tungkol sa nasabing paksa.

Liberalism is an understanding related to the freedom and equality of each individual to create a free society with the characteristics of freedom of thought. These are several advantages and disadvantages of Liberalism

The Advantages of Liberalism

-There is a desire and initiative from the community to develop for the better.

-Everyone has the same rights and freedoms in society.

-There is positive competition in society so that everyone wants to produce high quality products.

Individual freedom to have political parties without intervention from other parties.

-The press has the right and freedom to give sharp criticism of the government, of course within the limits and applicable press ethics.

-The emergence of a profit-seeking motive in the community so that economic activity becomes more effective and efficient.

Disadvantages of Liberalism

-Parties who have these resources tend to exploit workers, causing social inequality in society.

-The occurrence of a monopoly on the people of small or poor groups

-Press freedom is often used by certain parties to achieve profit.

-The emergence of free competition means that an equitable distribution of income in society will be very difficult to achieve.

-The emergence of community groups that consider themselves to be higher in rank than other communities, or vice versa,

 

Definition of Liberalism

Liberalism is an ideology or understanding that upholds the freedom and equality of individual rights in various aspects of life, be it in economic, political, social, religious, or other matters concerning the dignity of life.

This understanding of liberalism is an understanding and political tradition that promotes freedom and equal rights for every individual in society. That is, a country and its government must respect and protect the freedom and rights of each of its citizens in various aspects of human life.

This understanding of liberalism generally grows in a democratic system because both have the concept of freedom. However, even though this understanding promotes individual freedom, that freedom is not unlimited freedom; there is still order, and, of course, it must be accounted for.

6. 1. Trade Liberalizationans​


Answer:

Trade liberalization is the removal or reduction of restrictions or barriers on the free exchange of goods between nations. These barriers include tariffs, such as duties and surcharges, and nontariff barriers, such as licensing rules and quotas.

Explanation:

First things first: The primary objective of trade liberalization is to extract the most value possible from the world's collective assets. The removal of protectionist obstacles like duty fees, surcharges, and quotas encourages the efficient creation of goods and execution of services that have real value.

8. Advantages and disadvantages of trade


The advantages of trade

Trade increases competition and lowers world prices, which provides benefits to consumers by raising the purchasing power of their own income, and leads a rise in consumer surplus. Trade also breaks down domestic monopolies, which face competition from more efficient foreign firms.

The disadvantage of trade

is that sometimes developed countries export harmful products to other countries (generally developing) leading to damage to the environment of importing country and hence international trade poses an environmental hazard for nations doing international trade.


9. Enumerate the positive and negative effects of trade liberalization


Answer:

The following are some of the advantages and disadvantages of liberalisation.

Advantages:

Increase in foreign direct investment

Abolishing of licensing system in the country

Reducing the monopoly of public sector

Increase in the employment opportunities

Economic development of the nation

Reduction in rates of interest and tariffs

Development in technology due to use of foreign technology in industrial applications

Disadvantages:

Due to liberalisation there will be an increased dependence on other nations for forex, technology etc.

There will be economic instability as any changes in the currency in foreign markets will result in a significant impact on the economy.

Due to the introduction of new technology, there will be less skilled workers who can implement the technology.

The domestic sector will take a hit as industries will become dependent on cheap raw materials from foreign countries.
