Your self-image is a conceptual picture of yourself, both as a physical body and an individual. A healthy body image means that you know who you are, feel confident in your skin, and understand your full potential.

Elements and Dimensions of Self-Image

These are the proposed elements and dimensions of self-image (Oltmann, 2014):

The three elements of a person’s self-image are:

The way a person perceives oneself. The way a person interprets another person’s perceptions of him or her. The perception of the ideal self.

The six dimensions of a person’s self-image are:

Physical dimension Psychological dimension Intellectual dimension Skills dimension Moral dimension Sexual dimension  

Examples of Positive and Negative Self-Image

A positive self-image is having a good judgment of yourself; for example:

Seeing yourself as a good-looking and desirable person. Having an image of yourself as a smart and intellectual person. Seeing a joyful, fit, and healthy person when you look in the mirror. Believing that you are at best to your ideal self. Thinking that others recognize you as a person as well as yourself.

On the other hand, negative self-image is the reverse of the above; it looks like:

Seeing yourself as unappealing and undesirable. Having an image of yourself having below-average intelligence. Seeing an unhappy and pale-looking when you look in the mirror. Believing that you are not your ideal version of yourself. Thinking that others do not recognize you as a person as well as yourself.
