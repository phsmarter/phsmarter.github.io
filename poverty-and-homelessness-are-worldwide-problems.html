6 Possible causes of poverty and homeless

Sure, here are six possible causes of poverty and homelessness:

1. Lack of affordable healthcare: Without access to healthcare, individuals and families may struggle with medical debt and ongoing health problems, which can lead to financial instability and poverty.

2. Discrimination: Marginalized communities, such as people of color, LGBTQ+ individuals, and people with disabilities, may face discrimination in employment, education, and housing, which can contribute to poverty and homelessness.

3. Natural disasters: Natural disasters, such as hurricanes, floods, and wildfires, can destroy homes and disrupt communities, leading to homelessness and financial hardship.

4. Low wages: Many jobs, particularly those in the service industry, do not pay a living wage, making it difficult for workers to support themselves and their families.

5.Lack of access to affordable childcare: Without affordable and accessible childcare, parents may struggle to maintain employment or education, leading to financial instability and poverty.

6.Criminal justice system: People who have been incarcerated or have criminal records may face difficulty finding employment, housing, and accessing resources, contributing to poverty and homelessness.

20. make an outline about worldwide povertymatinong sagot po pls​


Answer:

Poverty goes beyond lack of income. It encompasses economic, social, and governance dimensions. Economically, the poor are not only deprived of income and resources, but of opportunities. Markets and jobs are often difficult to access, because of low capabilities and geographical and social exclusion. Limited access to education affects the ability of the poor to get jobs and to obtain information that could improve the quality of their lives. Poor health due to inadequate nutrition, hygiene and health services further limits their prospects for work and from realizing their mental and physical potential. This fragile position is exacerbated by insecurity. Living in marginal conditions with no resources to fall back on, shocks become hard or impossible to offset. The situation is made worse by the structure of societies and institutions that tends to exclude the poor from participating in decision-making over the direction of social and economic development.x

Answer:

SOCIAL

Explanation:

Poverty entails more than the lack of income and productive resources to ensure sustainable livelihoods. Its manifestations include hunger and malnutrition, limited access to education and other basic services, social discrimination and exclusion as well as the lack of participation in decision-making.

Loneliness seems to be a growing problem in the modern West. Wider social trends, like the decline of the traditional family and institutions such as churches and social clubs, are experienced all too personally by individuals who find themselves with no close family or friends, especially in older age.

Homelessness is a complex social problem with a variety of underlying economic and social factors such as poverty, lack of affordable housing, uncertain physical and mental health, addictions, and community and family breakdown.
