5. What is the relevance of understanding literary genre in the ANALYSIS of the piece​


Answer:

the importance of genre in writing is the key to success. Get it wrong and it will cost us, as writers, a small fortune in book sales and advertising dollars. Get it right, and readers will tell their friends about us. Because genre is all about reader expectations, and if a Story doesn’t meet them, it’s finished. Even the most beautiful turn of phrase can’t save it.

Explanation:

What does Genre mean?

We were readers before we were writers. Some of us are also literary scholars and hold English degrees and MFAs. While this experience helps to inform our writing, it means that we all have a different perception of genre in writing. We’re used to looking at genre as a reader does, or as a scholar does. Now we have to look at it as a writer does.

Think of it this way. You’re craving a hamburger – a half pound of thick, juicy, flame-broiled, prime beef topped with sharp cheddar and maple smoked bacon. You’ve been working out at the gym all week and watching your diet. It’s Friday night, and you’ve earned your treat. You go to the best restaurant in town and ask for a burger. Twenty minutes later the waiter brings you a tofu burger. This is award-winning tofu, prepared by a top chef. The waiter assures you that it’s delicious and although it may well be, it’s not what you were expecting. It’s not your idea of a burger. It’s not what you were in the mood for and so you feel disappointed. You won’t be recommending the restaurant because they didn’t give you what you wanted.

More Learning:

Every genre (type of writing/writing style) has a set of conventions that allow that particular genre to be unique. These conventions include the following components:

O Tone: tone of voice, i.e. serious, humorous, scholarly, informal.

Diction: word usage - formal or informal, i.e. “disoriented” (formal) versus “spaced out” (informal or colloquial). Content: what is being discussed/demonstrated in the piece? What information is included or needs to be included?

O  Style / Format (the way it looks): long or short sentences? Bulleted list? Paragraphs? Short-hand? Abbreviations? Does punctuation and grammar matter? How detailed do you need to be? Single-spaced or double-spaced? Can pictures / should pictures be included? How long does it need to be / should be? What kind of organizational requirements are there?

O  Expected Medium of Genre: where does the genre appear? Where is it created? i.e. can be it be online (digital) or does it need to be in print (computer paper, magazine, etc)? Where does this genre occur? i.e. flyers (mostly) occur in the hallways of our school, and letters of recommendation (mostly) occur in professors’ offices.

Audience:  What audience is this piece of writing trying to reach?

Genre creates an expectation in the minds of its audience and may fail or succeed depending on if that expectation is met or not.

Many genres have built-in audiences and corresponding publications that support them, such as magazines and websites.

Purpose:  What is the goal of the piece of writing? What is its purpose?

The goal of the piece that is written, i.e. a newspaper entry is meant to inform and/or persuade, and a movie script is meant to entertain.

Basically, each genre has a specific task or a specific goal that it is created to attain.

Charlson Ong's award-winning novel An Embarrassment of Riches (2000) creatively narrates the history of the Chinese diaspora as an overdetermined event in the Southeast Asian Region. The novel explores the politics of belonging, displacement, identity, and territory through a/n (re)imagined nation built primarily on Philippine historical and cultural contingencies. This essay seeks to explore the junctures and vicissitudes of the Chinese diaspora in the region through the following questions: How is hybridity manifested by selected characters and other cultural elements in the narrative? Second, how do the perceptions of homeland, marked by ambivalence and contradiction, operate in the narrative? And third, how does the memory of the characters' homeland or the lack of it contribute to identity formation? Furthermore, an analysis of the novel's form has been conducted, possibly asserting the notion that the genre is also a hybridized entity. This paper illustrates the complexity of the Chinese diaspora in the region and its bearing to identity formation.

29. Connect all the elements of film analysis to another & give its importance.Elements of film analysis. 1. Film facts2. Genre3. Setting4. Plot and Structure5. Conflict 6. Characterization 7. Narrator 8. Imagery9. Themepa help po salamat po​


Answer:

Film analysis is the process of examining a movie in detail to better understand its components and their impact on the overall experience of the viewer. Each element of film analysis is interconnected and has its own importance in conveying the filmmaker's message.

Film facts - This includes information about the director, actors, production team, and other behind-the-scenes details that can help contextualize the movie and provide insights into the filmmaking process.

Genre - The genre of the film sets the expectations for the viewer and can influence how the film is interpreted. A horror film, for example, is expected to create a sense of fear and suspense.

Setting - The setting of the film includes the physical location, time period, and cultural context. This element can affect the tone and mood of the film, as well as its historical and cultural significance.

Plot and Structure - The plot and structure of the film refer to the way the story is told and the order in which events unfold. This element can affect the viewer's emotional response to the film and can influence the film's overall impact.

Conflict - The conflict refers to the central struggle in the film, whether it is between characters or within a character's psyche. This element is essential in driving the story forward and creating tension.

Characterization - This refers to the way in which the characters are developed and portrayed in the film. It can include their backstory, motivations, and personality traits. This element is essential in creating empathy for the characters and in driving the story forward.

Narrator - The narrator is the voice that guides the viewer through the story. It can be a character in the film or an omniscient voiceover. The narrator can affect the viewer's emotional response and understanding of the story.

Imagery - The imagery in a film refers to the visual elements that create meaning and convey emotion. This can include lighting, color, camera angles, and special effects. Imagery is essential in creating the overall atmosphere and mood of the film.

Theme - The theme is the underlying message or meaning of the film. It can be overt or subtle and can be expressed through the story, characters, or imagery. The theme is essential in creating a cohesive and impactful film.
