1. why is it essential to practice self discipline -Essay


Answer:

Self-discipline is an essential trait that helps individuals achieve their goals and live a fulfilling life. It involves regulating one's behavior and actions to ensure that they align with their values and objectives. In this essay, we will explore why self-discipline is crucial and how it can benefit individuals in various aspects of their lives.

Firstly, practicing self-discipline helps individuals develop a sense of responsibility and accountability. When we exercise self-discipline, we take ownership of our actions and their consequences. This means that we become more aware of the impact our behavior has on ourselves and others. For instance, if we are trying to lose weight, we must discipline ourselves to eat healthily and exercise regularly. By doing so, we acknowledge our responsibility to take care of our bodies and ensure that we remain healthy and fit.

Moreover, self-discipline helps individuals overcome procrastination and laziness. Often, we find ourselves putting off tasks that we know are important or necessary. This can lead to stress, anxiety, and a sense of overwhelm. However, by cultivating self-discipline, we can push ourselves to take action and complete tasks even when we don't feel like it. This not only helps us become more productive but also boosts our confidence and self-esteem.

In addition, practicing self-discipline is essential for achieving long-term success in various areas of life. Whether it's in academics, career, or personal relationships, discipline is crucial for achieving our goals. For instance, if we want to excel in our studies or advance in our careers, we must discipline ourselves to put in the necessary effort, time, and resources. This means sacrificing short-term pleasures and distractions for long-term gains and benefits.

Furthermore, self-discipline helps individuals develop resilience and perseverance. Life is full of challenges, setbacks, and obstacles, and it's easy to give up when things get tough. However, with self-discipline, we can train ourselves to keep going even when the going gets tough. This means staying committed to our goals and values, even in the face of adversity. This not only helps us overcome obstacles but also strengthens our character and builds our resilience.

Finally, practicing self-discipline is essential for maintaining good mental health and well-being. When we lack self-discipline, we are more likely to engage in unhealthy behaviors and habits that can harm our mental and physical health. For instance, we may indulge in excessive drinking or drug use, engage in risky sexual behavior, or engage in other self-destructive behaviors. However, by cultivating self-discipline, we can avoid such behaviors and make choices that promote our health and well-being.

In conclusion, self-discipline is a crucial trait that helps individuals achieve their goals, develop responsibility and accountability, overcome procrastination and laziness, achieve long-term success, develop resilience and perseverance, and maintain good mental health and well-being. By practicing self-discipline, we can become more self-aware, confident, and successful in various aspects of our lives.

16. essay Compare and contrast how the self has been represented across different disciplines and perspectives. ​


Depending on the context of the question, answering who you are can require as simple, or a complex, answer. In part, this multi-faceted question has led to a wide body of literature from multiple fields of study, all trying to figure out what exactly is an identity.

If you think about it, do you really know or understand your identity? Are you a single, coherent expression of yourself in every situation, or do you change, and act differently, with different people? If you wake up in a bad mood, can you really say you're the same person you are on a good day?

Even popular music gets involved with the debate with the lyrics of a Lardpony song titled You Vary as a Function of Time.

And now, let's take a look at some of the scholarly theories of identity, the field of study they originated from, and the theorist involved.

Erik H. Erikson (1902-1994)

Field: Psychoanalysis and Psychology

Theory: Psychosocial Development Theory

Summary of Theory

In Erikson's model of development, introduced during the 1950s, the formation of identity by an adolescent was key to the total development of the person into adulthood. It moves through a linear series of stages in which the person incorporates, or rejects attitudes, beliefs, and behaviors. These are learned from what Erikson called ''agents of socialization,'' also known as family, co-workers, school teachers, classmates, and more. The focus of this development is the eventual formation of a singular, coherent sense of self, accompanied by a confidence that one can maintain this inner oneness.

25. Discipline and self control


DISCIPLINE AND SELF-CONTROL

Definitions

Discipline

Discipline refers to a way of acting or behaving that shows a great desire or urge to obey rules or regulations or the behavior that is evaluated or judged by how well it follows a set of rules or orders.

Self-control

Self-control refers to the inhibition and restraint applied over one's own actions, tendencies, or emotions.

It refers to the control over your own desires or actions.
