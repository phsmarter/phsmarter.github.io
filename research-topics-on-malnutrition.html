11. Topic 2Controlling Malnutrition and Micronutrient Deficiencies ​


Answer:

Three major intervention strategies are available for the control of micronutrient malnutrition: supplementation of the specific micronutrients; fortification of foods with micronutrients; and horticulture intervention to increase production and nutrition education to ensure regular consumption of micronutrient rich foods. In India currently the national nutrition programmes being implemented for preventing these deficiencies are based on short term supplementation like periodic mega dosing of vitamin A, distribution of iron and folic acid tablets, and salt iodisation. Though these have been in operation for over two decades, there has been no perceptible biological impact on the prevalence of micronutrient malnutrition. Among the constraints, the most important are: lack of coordination, shortage of resources and manpower, inadequate and irregular supplies, lack of proper orientation and training to the functionaries, poor monitoring and supervision and absence of nutrition education. Integrated and multi-sectoral approaches are required to achieve the goals set under the National Nutrition Policy. These should include community-friendly nutrition education to increase awareness and motivation; active people's participation; food fortification; nutrient supplementation; nutrition oriented horticulture programmes; orientation of functionaries, and establishment of integrated micronutrient surveillance. Concerted and focussed efforts are needed to combat micronutrient malnutrition by the 2000 AD.

Scenario 1: Define Research Problem:

The research problem is to investigate the prevalence and causes of malnutrition in Sorsogon, a province in the Philippines. The study aims to identify the factors that contribute to malnutrition in the area and develop strategies to address the issue.

Scenario 2: Review the Literature:

The literature review will focus on previous studies that have investigated malnutrition in the Philippines and other developing countries. It will also explore the socio-economic and cultural factors that contribute to malnutrition in the region, as well as the existing interventions and programs that aim to address the issue.

Scenario 3: Formulate the Hypothesis:

Based on the literature review, the following hypothesis can be formulated: The high prevalence of malnutrition in Sorsogon can be attributed to a combination of socio-economic and cultural factors, such as poverty, lack of education, and unhealthy food practices.

Scenario 4: Design Research:

The research will employ a mixed-methods approach, combining quantitative and qualitative methods. The quantitative phase will involve a survey of households in Sorsogon to determine the prevalence of malnutrition and its correlates, while the qualitative phase will involve in-depth interviews and focus group discussions with key informants, such as mothers, health workers, and community leaders, to gain a deeper understanding of the socio-economic and cultural factors that contribute to malnutrition in the area.

Scenario 5: Collect Data:

The data collection process will involve recruiting and training survey enumerators and qualitative data collectors, as well as obtaining ethical clearance from the relevant authorities. The survey will be conducted using a standardized questionnaire, and the qualitative data will be collected using semi-structured interview guides and focus group discussion outlines.

Scenario 6: Analyze Data:

The quantitative data will be analyzed using descriptive and inferential statistics to determine the prevalence of malnutrition and its correlates. The qualitative data will be transcribed, coded, and analyzed using thematic analysis to identify the key themes and patterns that emerge from the data.

Scenario 7: Interpret and Report:

The findings of the study will be interpreted and reported in a research report that will be disseminated to key stakeholders, including government agencies, non-governmental organizations, and the community. The report will provide recommendations for addressing the issue of malnutrition in Sorsogon, based on the findings of the study. Effective interventions to address malnutrition require a multi-sectoral approach that involves government, community, and individual efforts.

The report will provide recommendations for interventions to address malnutrition in Sorsogon, based on the study's findings. The report will also be disseminated to relevant stakeholders, including government agencies, NGOs, and community-based organizations, to inform policies and programs aimed at reducing malnutrition in the region
