13. Which will help the student nurse joshua determine whether a patients confusion is cause by delirium?


Determine it by looking at the patient's condition, delirium is temporary and usually goes away completely.


Delirium is a condition of acute and fluctuating loss of consciousness. The suffer severe confusion and reduced awareness of the surrounding environment. This mental condition can worsen or occur suddenly over a day or two. People who experience this condition can feel confused, or even more than usual. If people with this problem don’t get treatment right away, those around them can find it difficult, especially when the cause is unknown.


The doctor will diagnose delirium by conducting medical interviews with the sufferer's family who understands the course of the symptoms experienced by the patient, performs a physical and neurological examination, and examines mental conditions. Delirium is treated first by addressing the cause. For example, someone who has low oxygen or sugar levels needs to be treated to prevent this from occurring. If it was caused by an infection, antibiotics will be given. The doctor make sure all the things that cause it will be treated properly. Delirium will usually improven if the cause was found and treated correctly. In addition, a supportive environment will also needed so that the sufferer can recover faster.
