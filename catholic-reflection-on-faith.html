FOUR PILLARS OF THE CATHOLIC FAITH

Answer:

The Catholic Church's Catechism is split into four portions or divisions. The four portions are known as the Church's Pillars.

Creed. Every week, as we say the Nicene or Apostles Creed, it reminds us of all of our convictions. God is the creator, Jesus Christ is our Savior, and the Holy Spirit empowers us.

Sacraments. We experience God's grace via the Sacraments. We are made more holy through the Sacraments of Initiation (Baptism, Confirmation, Eucharist), Healing (Penance and the Anointing of the Sick), and the Sacraments at the Service of Communion (Marriage and Holy Orders).

Morality. Living the moral life to which we are called is the only way to enter God's kingdom. In addition to the Ten Commandments, Catholic Social Teaching provides us with instruction on how to love God and our neighbor.

Prayer. Prayer can take numerous forms, including adoration, supplication, intercession, thankfulness, and praise, and it can be vocal, meditative, or contemplative. Prayer connects us to the liturgy and allows us to maintain a vital relationship with our Lord.
