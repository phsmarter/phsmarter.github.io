20. 2. Better late than never. (Explain in your own words. Give a particular example of a situation/ or experience when this already happened to you.) Type your answer below​


Answer:

"Better late than never" is an idiomatic expression that means it is better to do something late than to never do it at all. It implies that it is still valuable to complete a task or fulfill a commitment, even if it's done after the expected or desired time.

An example of a situation where "better late than never" applies is in meeting a deadline. Imagine you were given a project to finish by a certain date, but due to unforeseen circumstances, you weren't able to complete it on time. Instead of giving up and not submitting the project at all, you continue working on it and submit it after the deadline. Although it's late, it's still better to submit it than not at all, especially if it's required for your work or studies.

I have also experienced this in my personal life, such as when I missed the opportunity to attend a friend's important event because of a conflict in my schedule. I was disappointed that I wasn't able to attend and show my support, but I made it a point to reach out and congratulate them afterwards. It may not have been the same as being there in person, but it was still better than not acknowledging their achievement at all.

ANSWER:

"Better late than never" is a common phrase that suggests that it's better to do something late than to never do it at all. This means that it's better to take action, even if it's delayed, rather than not doing anything. This phrase can apply to various situations such as completing a task, apologizing, or pursuing a goal.

For example, if a student misses a deadline for submitting an assignment, they can still submit it late rather than not submitting at all. The late submission may result in a lower grade, but it's better than receiving a zero for not submitting the assignment. Another example is when someone apologizes for their mistake or hurtful behavior, even if it's late, it's still better than not apologizing at all.

Overall, the phrase "Better late than never" encourages people to take action, even if it's not perfect or timely, because it's still better than not doing anything at all.
