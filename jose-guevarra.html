23. kahalagahan ni tenyente guevarra sa noli me tangere


Answer:

Tenyente Guevara is a minor character in the novel "Noli Me Tangere" by Jose Rizal, but his role is significant in exposing the corruption and injustices prevalent in the Spanish colonial government during that time.

Tenyente Guevara is a fair and just officer who sympathizes with the oppressed Filipinos and recognizes the flaws of the colonial system. He is in charge of investigating the death of Don Rafael Ibarra, the father of the novel's protagonist, Crisostomo Ibarra. Unlike his corrupt and abusive superiors, Tenyente Guevara conducts a thorough and impartial investigation to uncover the truth behind Don Rafael's death.

Through his actions, Tenyente Guevara represents the possibility of a just and fair colonial administration, which is in stark contrast to the oppressive and exploitative regime of the Spanish colonizers. He also serves as a symbol of hope and justice for the oppressed Filipinos who are struggling under the colonial system.

Overall, the importance of Tenyente Guevara's character in the novel lies in his representation of the potential for a fair and just system of governance, which was unfortunately rare during the Spanish colonial period in the Philippines.
