8. Research about 1974 rape of the singer/actress Connie Francis in Westbury, N.Y. hotel and make a brief summary regarding the incident and the case. Write also short Insights about the incident and how this can be a lesson and can help hotels and companies impose tense security.​


Answer:

The rape of Connie Francis, a singer and actress, occurred in a Westbury, N.Y. hotel in 1974. On the night of the incident, Francis was in the hotel bar, when she was approached by a man who asked her to dance. Francis accepted and they went to a secluded corner of the bar. The man then proceeded to rape her.

The perpetrator was apprehended and sentenced to 3-10 years in prison. Francis sued the hotel for negligence, claiming that she had been left in an unsecured area of the hotel and that the hotel had failed to provide adequate security. The judge ruled in favor of Francis, finding the hotel liable for negligence and awarding her $2.5 million in damages.

The incident is a lesson for hotels and companies to pay attention to their security protocols and ensure that areas within their premises are adequately monitored and secured. They must also ensure that their staff is properly trained to respond to potential threats and safety concerns. Additionally, companies should also ensure that their guests are aware of the security measures in place, in order to ensure their safety.

28. continental-continental​


Answer:

Continent-continent convergence creates some of the world’s largest mountains ranges. Magma cannot penetrate this thick crust, so there are no volcanoes, although the magma stays in the crust. Metamorphic rocks are common because of the stress the continental crust experiences. With enormous slabs of crust smashing together, continent-continent collisions bring on numerous and large earthquakes.
