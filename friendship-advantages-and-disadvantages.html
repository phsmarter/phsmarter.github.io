Answer:

Learning Task No. 6: Essay.  

Explanation:

1. Compare and contrast our time to the 70s and 80s in terms of the following:  

a. What are the advantages and disadvantages of living in a post- industrial/technological society?  

Advantages:

Post-industrial societies focus on theoretical knowledge, creating new scientific disciplines and technological advances. Some of the effects of post-industrialization are outsourcing manufacturing jobs to other countries, working from home, global communities, and global networking

Disadvantages: disparities between social classes becomes wider.  

b. What are the advantages and disadvantages of living in BEFORE post-industrial/technological society?  

Advantages: privacy is not sacrificed, less costs

Disadvantages: limited jobs

c. What are the differences of social relationships between those living 70s 80s and to our contemporary era?  

In the 70s and 80s, if you wanted to socialize, you had to make the effort to leave the comfort and security of your home and drive to a place where you would be likely to see other people of about your own age. Depending on who you were and what your goals were, that might mean spending a considerable amount of time on your appearance. The idea was to impress people.

d. Concept of love and friendship  

Love and friendship are strict boundaries that doesn’t usually encompass each other. Friendship can turn to love but love cannot turn to friendships.  

2. Is it better to go back to the tribal and feudalistic forms of society where the sense of community is stronger? Explain your answer.  

No. Because feudalism is all about having a mastery and a system of slavery.  

3. Applying everything you learned about digital society and citizenship as well as with the disembodied subject, do you think an examined life can be compatible with these societies?

I believe they are compatible and mutually intelligible given the right resources and reintroduction.
