29. Compare the movie in clash of titans and gorgons head


Answer:

The Greek gods and prayers

In the movie, the gods require the prayers of mortals to fuel themselves. No such concept exists in the myths. Rather, the gods are petty and violent and screw with the lives of mortals with no consequence. God of War captures this aspect particularly well.

Hades is not a villain

In the movie, Hades is antagonistic to the other gods as well as humankind. He seeks to usurp Zeus, dresses in all black, and is pretty much Voldemort with a beard.

In the myths, Hades is the most fair, just, and politically neutral of all the gods. He never actively tries to kill people, as he is just a ruler of the dead, not its messenger. Death is personified as Thanatos. As for Hades’ appearance, the ancient Greeks believed gems and minerals come from Hades’ domain (the underground). So the god was often depicted as very wealthy. Also, in the myth of Perseus, Hades actually lets him borrow his helmet of invisibility.


30. similarities of gorgon's head and Daedalus and icarus??


GREEK STORIES

The Gorgon's head

According to the story of the Gorgon's head, Perseus's mother is imprisoned and held captive by King Polydectes.

In order to free his mother, the king gave Perseus a condition; he should embark to a journey to kill Medusa and bring Medusa's head back in exchange of his mother's freedom. By his ingenuity, intelligence, and help from the gods, Perseus accomplished his mission, freed his mother, and saved Andromeda, which later became his wife.

Daedalus and Icarus

The story of Daedalus and Icarus is all about a man named Daedalus who is a genius, and a great inventor that made the impossible possible to free his imprisoned son Icarus from the maze of King Minos where the fearsome Minotaur lives.

The famous Daedalus created wings made of feathers and wax for him and Icarus to escape from Crete, however, Icarus (his son) ignored his father's warnings and flew too close to the sun; which caused his wings to melt that eventually made him fell into the sea where he perished soon after.

Similarities

1. There were both Kings involved in the story. King Minos (from Daedalus and Icarus) and then King Polydectes (from The Gorgon's head).

2. Someone is held captive in both stories. The mother of Perseus and Daedalus's son Icarus.

3. Both main characters had to deal with a particular "monster". Perseus had to behead Medusa and Daedalus had to escape from the Minotaur's wrath.

4. Both main characters are children of gods. Perseus is a son of Zeus, and Daedalus's mother is Athena.
