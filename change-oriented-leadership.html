27. How was governance and leadership changed in Filipino society from Spanish Era to present?


Answer:

The Spanish Era in the Philippines lasted for more than three centuries and left a significant impact on governance and leadership in Filipino society. During this time, the Philippines was under the colonial rule of Spain, and the Spanish government implemented a centralized system of governance. The country was divided into provinces, each headed by a governor, and each province was further divided into municipalities or towns. The local government was run by a gobernadorcillo or town mayor, who was appointed by the governor.

The Spanish government also introduced the encomienda system, where the Spanish crown granted a person a certain number of natives to work for them. This system gave rise to the social hierarchy in the Philippines, with the Spanish occupying the topmost rung and the natives at the bottom. The natives were treated as second-class citizens and were subjected to forced labor and tribute.

However, the Philippines eventually gained independence from Spain in 1898 and went through a series of changes in governance and leadership. The country transitioned from a colonial government to a democratic republic with the adoption of the Malolos Constitution in 1899. The new government consisted of a president, a vice president, and a cabinet of ministers.

Since then, the Philippines has undergone several changes in governance and leadership, including the adoption of a parliamentary form of government under the 1935 Constitution, a shift to a presidential form of government under the 1987 Constitution, and the current system of governance that features three branches of government: the executive, legislative, and judiciary.

In terms of leadership, the Philippines has seen a mix of leaders who have brought significant changes to the country, including Ramon Magsaysay, who focused on eradicating corruption and promoting economic progress, and Corazon Aquino, who restored democracy after years of martial law under Ferdinand Marcos. However, the country has also faced leadership challenges.

Overall, the governance and leadership of Filipino society have undergone significant changes from the Spanish Era to the present, with the country transitioning from a colonial government to a democratic republic and experiencing a mix of leaders who have brought both progress and challenges to the country.
