10. The exporter or importer has a perfect right to delegate the business of ysical distribution, and documentation, to a freight forwarder.


As a responsible private importer of goods from foreign countries, you want to be sure that your shipment is well taken care of. That means following the letter of the law and making well-informed decisions at every opportunity. From regulations you need to comply with, to costs and benefits of choosing one shipping option over another, there's a lot to consider. And even more so if you're a business owner or seasoned importer who needs to oversee multiple international shipments every year.

If you're an exporter or importer, you're no stranger to the importance of documentation and shipping. You also don't want to be burdened with these activities, which is why shipping and documentation are prime targets for outsourcing. You'll have an efficient system in place in no time if you start outsourcing these processes to a freight forwarder.

" A freight forwarder is a professional who specializes in the supply chain. This includes anything from warehousing your goods to arranging them on the ship and including their distribution once they reach their destination.

Shipment is an integral part of international trade and the global supply chain. The exporter or importer has a perfect right to delegate the business of physical distribution, and documentation, to a freight forwarder.

Answer:

When an organisation has made a decision to enter an overseas market, there are a variety of options open to it. These options vary with cost, risk and the degree of control which can be exercised over them. The simplest form of entry strategy is exporting using either a direct or indirect method such as an agent, in the case of the former, or countertrade, in the case of the latter. More complex forms include truly global operations which may involve joint ventures, or export processing zones. Having decided on the form of export strategy, decisions have to be made on the specific channels. Many agricultural products of a raw or commodity nature use agents, distributors or involve Government, whereas processed materials, whilst not excluding these, rely more heavily on more sophisticated forms of access.
