One of the must-visit destinations for eco-tourists is the Binucot Beach in San Andres, Romblon. Aside from its natural beauty, Binucot Beach is also a sanctuary for turtles, which lay their eggs on its shores. Visitors can also experience its colorful coral reefs by snorkeling or diving.

For those who love mountain climbing, the Gomotong Twin Peaks in Banton are a must-see destination. This trek offers a scenic view of the lush mountain forest and panoramic views of the surrounding islands. At the top, you'll see the twin rock formations that are a sight to behold.

Another popular destination for eco-tourism in Romblon is the Mount Guiting-guiting Natural Park in the town of San Fernando. The reserve is home to endemic and endangered species of flora and fauna. The unique ecosystem of the park includes a variety of tree species, including the rare Rafflesia plant, one of the world's largest flowers.

Moreover, Romblon is well-known for its heritage sites and old churches that are worth visiting, such as the St. Joseph Cathedral in Romblon town and Simara Church in the town of Corcuera. These structures date back to the Spanish colonial period, bearing the rich cultural heritage of the province.

To experience the best of all these natural, cultural and historical wonders, you can join a local ecotourism group. These are composed of trained local guides who can take you on an adventure, share the province's history and culture, and promote the value of protecting its environment.

13. what is the cultural treasure of the philippines


Answer:

UST BAYBAYIN

Explanation:

The UST Baybayin Documents are two 17th century land deeds written in baybayin, an ancient Philippine syllabary or suyat. The first document was written in 1613, while the second was written in 1625. It is the first document to be declared a national cultural treasure.

Answer:

- The UST Baybayin Documents are two 17th century land deeds written in baybayin, an ancient Philippine syllabary or suyat.

The first document was written in 1613, while the second was written in 1625. It is the first document to be declared a national cultural treasure.

Answer:

Day 1:

Welcome to Romblon, a hidden gem in the Philippines that offers breathtaking scenery, crystal clear waters, and a diverse ecosystem. Our ecotourism journey begins with a visit to the Binucot Beach, located in the Municipality of San Andres. Here, you can witness the beautiful coral reefs and swim with the colorful fishes. Take a stroll along the beach and appreciate the stunning rock formations that are unique to Romblon.

Day 2:

On the second day, we will visit the Sibuyan Sea, a protected marine sanctuary located in the Municipality of Alcantara. The sanctuary offers a variety of activities such as snorkeling, diving, and island hopping. You will be amazed by the vibrant coral reefs, colorful fishes, and sea turtles that call this place their home.

Day 3:

We will explore the Mount Guiting-Guiting National Park, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, and Biosphere Reserve located in the Municipality of San Fernando. The park is home to diverse flora and fauna, including the Philippine eagle, the tarsier, and the flying lemur. Trek up to the summit and witness the stunning panoramic views of the surrounding islands.

Day 4:

On the fourth day, we will visit the Bonbon Beach, located in the Municipality of Romblon. The beach is famous for its fine white sand and crystal clear waters. It is also a nesting ground for sea turtles, and you can witness the hatching of the eggs during the nesting season. You can also go kayaking or stand-up paddleboarding along the shore.

Day 5:

Our final day in Romblon will be spent at the Looc Fish Sanctuary, located in the Municipality of Looc. The sanctuary is home to different species of fishes and other marine creatures. You can go snorkeling and witness the vibrant underwater world that is just a few meters away from the shore.

In conclusion, Romblon offers a unique ecotourism experience that is perfect for nature lovers, adventure seekers, and those who want to escape the hustle and bustle of the city. Come and visit Romblon and witness the beauty of its natural wonders while promoting sustainable tourism practices.
