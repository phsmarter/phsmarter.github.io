9. formalist approach on "invictus" ​


Answer:

The formalist approach to literary analysis focuses on the form, structure, language, and other elements of a text as they exist within the text itself, rather than looking at the author's life or social or historical context. In this approach, the meaning of the text arises from the way these elements work together.

In the poem "Invictus" by William Ernest Henley, the form and language of the poem are notable for their directness and simplicity. The poem consists of four stanzas, each with four lines. The rhyme scheme is ABAB, and the meter is mostly iambic tetrameter, with occasional variations. The poem also makes use of repetition, with the phrase "I am" repeated three times in the first two stanzas.

The poem's language is straightforward and declarative, with short, simple sentences. The speaker asserts his own strength and independence, despite the challenges he has faced, and emphasizes his own willpower and determination. The language is also highly visual, with vivid images such as "the fell clutch of circumstance" and "the bludgeonings of chance."

One key element of the poem is its use of the first person point of view. The speaker asserts his own agency and autonomy, claiming that he is the master of his fate and the captain of his soul. This emphasis on the individual's power and control is in keeping with the formalist emphasis on the text itself, rather than on external factors such as history or biography.

Overall, the formalist approach to "Invictus" focuses on the poem's form, structure, and language as they contribute to its meaning. The poem's simple, direct style and emphasis on the individual's strength and willpower contribute to its enduring appeal and resonance.

26. What is the importance of the Formalist Approach and Marxist Approach? explain.​


The Formalist and Marxist approaches are two literary theories that provide different lenses through which to analyze and interpret literature. Both approaches have significant importance in the field of literary criticism.

The Formalist approach, also known as New Criticism, emphasizes the text itself as the primary object of study. It focuses on the formal elements of the text, such as structure, language, and imagery, and seeks to understand how these elements contribute to the overall meaning and effect of the work. The Formalist approach is important because it encourages close reading and analysis of the text, helping readers to appreciate the complexity and richness of literary works.

The Marxist approach, on the other hand, examines literature in the context of its historical and social setting, with a focus on how the work reflects and reinforces power structures and class relations. It emphasizes the role of economic and social forces in shaping literature and argues that literature is a product of its time and place. The Marxist approach is important because it helps readers to understand the political and social implications of literary works, and to recognize the ways in which literature can both reflect and challenge dominant ideologies.

In combination, the Formalist and Marxist approaches offer a complementary and comprehensive framework for literary analysis. While the Formalist approach provides a deep understanding of the formal elements of the text, the Marxist approach provides insight into the social and historical context in which the text was produced. Together, these approaches help readers to gain a nuanced and multifaceted understanding of literature, and to appreciate the ways in which it reflects and shapes the world around us.
