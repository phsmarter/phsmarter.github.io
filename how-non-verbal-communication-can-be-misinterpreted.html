11. 2. Provide some examples (atleast 5) of how people misinterpret or misuse media. How can their media analysis be improved?5 to7 sentence ​


1.Confirmation bias: People often consume media that confirms their pre-existing beliefs or values, and disregard or reject media that challenges those beliefs. To improve their media analysis, people should strive to expose themselves to diverse viewpoints and sources of information, and critically evaluate the credibility and accuracy of the information presented.

2.Misleading headlines: Media outlets often use attention-grabbing or sensational headlines to attract readers, but the content of the article may not actually support the headline's claims. To avoid being misled by headlines, people should read the full article and evaluate the evidence presented.

3.Out-of-context information: Media consumers may encounter information that has been taken out of context or distorted to fit a particular narrative or agenda. To avoid being misled, people should seek out the full context of the information and consider multiple perspectives.

4.Fake news: The proliferation of false information and conspiracy theories online can make it difficult for people to discern what is real and what is not. To improve their media analysis, people should fact-check information from multiple reliable sources before accepting it as true.

5.Echo chambers: Social media algorithms can create "echo chambers" where people are only exposed to viewpoints and information that align with their pre-existing beliefs. To broaden their perspectives and improve their media analysis, people should seek out diverse voices and viewpoints, and engage in respectful dialogue with those who hold different opinions.

14. 1 communication happens when there is understanding between the sender and receiver of the message in what way can you make sure that the message that you are trying to relay will not be misinterpreted?​


Answer:

To be an effective communicator, you need to make sure that your messages are clear, concise, accurate, and well planned . This will avoid misunderstanding and ensure that they get through each stage of the Communications Process without a hitch.

WAY TO MAKE SURE THAT THE MESSAGE WILL NOT BE MISINTERPRETED:

》Always try to simplify your message. Bring down your sentences to the simplest degree to avoid ambiguity in your messages. If the words and sentences are simple the probability of understanding the message is higher. There is no point in highfalutin sentences, if the receiver doesn’t decodes the message correctly.

》Listening is another important aspect of being a good communicator. You cannot deliver a perfect message until you are a good listener. Without listening you will not know what are you being asked for.

》Make sure you do not include vocabulary that can be difficult for the receiver to understand. Using jargons and technical terms can create misunderstanding if the decoder is not familiar with those words.
