Additional Example of Using Polya's Four-Step Problem-Solving Process

In the following example, use Polya's Four-Step Problem-Solving Process as outlined in the video lesson. Be sure to execute each step of the process and to state what that step involves.

Example:

Example:Farmer Brown has many animals on his farm. He has 72 chickens, which make up 60% of his total animals, and the rest of his animals are sheep. How many legs in total do his animals have?

Solution

1) Step One of Polya's Process is to understand the problem. We are trying to count how many legs the animals have in total. The animals are chickens (which have 2 legs each) and sheep (which have 4 legs each).

2) Step Two of Polya's Process is to devise a plan. We will work with an equation. An example of an equation to use to solve the problem is (72 chickens * 2 legs) + (number of sheep * 4 legs) = total legs. However, we do not know the number of sheep. We know that 60% of the total number of animals is equal to 72, so if n is the total number of animals, we have 0.60n = 72 so the total number of animals is 72/0.6 = 120. Then the number of sheep is the remaining amount of animals. A revised equation to use to solve the problem is (72 chickens * 2 legs) + ((120-72) sheep * 4 legs) = total legs.

3) Step Three is to carry out the plan. We will solve our equation. 120 - 72 = 48 sheep, and so we have (72 * 2) + (48 * 4) = total legs. 72 * 2 = 144 and 48 * 4 = 192, so the total number of legs is 144+192 = 336 legs.

4) Step Four is to look back. Does this answer make sense? There should be more legs than animals and the number should be an even number (the animals each have an even number of legs) and 336 fits this. We can check that 0.6(120) = 72 chickens and that 0.4(120) = 48 to make sure the number of animals is correct. Our answer checks out.

10. What is the first step in polya’s four step problem solving strategy?


"Polya's Method"

Attached is a picture of four phases in problem solving.

Polya developed his well-known four-step method for overcoming problems, which is applied worldwide to help people:

First, Comprehend the problem.Second, Create a plan in step two (translate).Third, Put the strategy into action (solve).Fourth, Take a look behind you (check and interpret)

First step is Understanding the Problem

This seems so apparent that it is frequently overlooked, yet students are frequently asking because they don't comprehend it, they are hindered in their attempts to solve difficulties. Totally, or even partially among the questions Polya instructed teachers to pose to their pupils were:

Are all the words used to describe the issue clear to you?What are you required to look for or display?Would you please restate the issue in your own words? Can you imagine an image or diagram that could assist you in understanding them problem?Do you have access to enough data to solve the problem?

To address any problem, George Polya created a four-step process that included understanding the issue, creating a plan, carrying it out, and looking back and reflecting. The method has gained a reputation as a traditional approach to issue resolution due to how straightforward and general it is.

The "Four-Step Problem Solving" method teaches primary arithmetic students to solve problems in four steps while using solid reasoning and developing their mathematical vocabulary. The four steps in this problem-solving technique are specifics, primary concept, strategy, and how.

As you can see, there is a cycle to problem solving: prepare, plan, execute, and perfect. Use your actions carefully, and keep the problem and its solution at the forefront of everything you do.

26. What is the second step in polya’s four step problem solving strategy?


Devising a plan or drawing up a plan is the second step in polya’s four step problem solving strategy.

About Polya's step problem solving strategy.

Polya (1973) states four stages when using problem solving skills, namely: understanding the problem ; devising a plan or drawing up a plan; carrying out the plan; and looking back or checking back.

At the stage of understanding, students must be able to know the problem available to be able to write down all the elements or data given in the problem and the data asked for in the question.

Furthermore, in the stage of preparing a problem-solving plan, students are required to do mathematical modeling of the problems contained in the questions and must find any relationship between the data contained in the questions and the unknown data.

After that, students can consider possible problems and must decide what solutions can be given to solve the problem. Then, in the stage of implementing the completion plan of problems, it is important for students to maintain the plans that have been made previously, However, if the plan or solution cannot be implemented, then an election can be carried out in other ways or plans or solutions so that the problem can be resolved.

The last stage, i.e., re-checking, checking the results of the answers is done to re-check whether the answers obtained are correct or if there are errors. This is important because if the student's answer is found to be an error, the student can correct the answer again.
