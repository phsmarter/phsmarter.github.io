[Answer]✍(◔◡◔)  

The Philippines is known for its pristine beaches and other picturesque tourist attractions that surely take one’s breath away. But there’s one thing that people usually forget about even if it’s worth looking at—Architecture Philippines.

The Filipino architecture is an interesting child of varied influences, both local and foreign. Before the modern architecture in the Philippines were a mix of American and Spanish colonial architecture in the Philippines that resulted in rich architectural feats we have today.

While the Philippines is popular for its tropical climate and getaway destinations, those are not valid reasons to undervalue the architectural designs in the Philippines and ignore its beauty and story. You see, the history and culture of the Philippines are reflected not just in the traditions and values of the people but also in its architectural heritage that has risen in response to the demands of progress and the aspirations of the people.  

All the examples of architecture in the Philippines today is the result of the growth, time, and varied influences of our neighboring countries and colonizers. It developed the pre colonial architecture in the Philippines leaving us with the Spanish architecture in the Philippines that you can see across the country especially in Manila architecture, Vigan architecture, and Cebu architecture. As a result, the country has become an architectural melting pot, featuring both uniquely Filipino and pre colonial architecture in the Philippines
