21. Cora is 60 years old. She is four years older than twice the age of her youngest child. How old is Cora's youngest child? ​


Let's call Cora's youngest child's age "x". We know that Cora is 60 years old and that she is four years older than twice her youngest child's age. We can write this information as an equation:

60 = 2x + 4

To solve for x, we can first subtract 4 from both sides of the equation:

56 = 2x

Then we can divide both sides by 2:

x = 28

Therefore, we can solve for Cora's youngest child's age by first setting up the equation 60 = 2x + 4, where "x" is the youngest child's age. Simplifying the equation, we get 56 = 2x. Dividing both sides by 2, we find that x = 28. Hence, Cora's youngest child is 28 years old.

30. make a conclusion about being the youngest child ​

Being the youngest child in a family can come with its own unique set of advantages and disadvantages. On the one hand, younger siblings may benefit from having older siblings to look up to and learn from, as well as the opportunity to receive more attention and support from their parents. However, younger siblings may also experience feelings of neglect or insecurity, as they may feel overshadowed by their older siblings and struggle to establish their own identity.

Despite the challenges that being the youngest child can bring, it's important to remember that every family dynamic is different and each individual's experience will vary.
