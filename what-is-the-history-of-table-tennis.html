16. history equipment basic skills of badminton,table tennis and lawn tennis?​


ANSWER:

Badminton, table tennis, and lawn tennis are all popular racquet sports that require a combination of physical skill and strategic thinking. In each of these sports, players use a racquet to hit a ball back and forth over a net, trying to score points by making it difficult for their opponent to return the ball.

In badminton, players use a lightweight racquet and a shuttlecock, which is a feathered object that is struck back and forth across the net. The game is played indoors or outdoors on a rectangular court, and players must use a combination of power and control to keep the shuttlecock in play.

In table tennis, players use a small, lightweight racquet and a hollow celluloid ball that is struck back and forth across a table. The game is played indoors on a table that is divided by a net, and players must use quick reflexes and precise placement to keep the ball in play.

In lawn tennis, players use a larger racquet and a pressurized rubber ball that is struck back and forth across a net on a rectangular court. The game is played outdoors on a grass, clay, or hard court surface, and players must use a combination of power and spin to keep the ball in play and score points.

Overall, while the specific equipment and rules of badminton, table tennis, and lawn tennis may vary, all of these sports require a combination of physical skill and strategic thinking to be successful.

Answer:

1. a game in which two or four players strike a ball with rackets over a net stretched across a court. The usual form (originally called lawn tennis ) is played with a felt-covered hollow rubber ball on a grass, clay, or artificial surface.

2. The origins of the game can be traced to a 12th–13th-century French handball game called jeu de paume (“game of the palm”), from which was derived a complex indoor racket-and-ball game: real tennis.

3. A competitive table tennis table should measure 2.74 m (9 ft) long, 1.525 m (5 ft) wide and be 76 cm (2 ft 6 in) high. The surface of a table tennis table must be the same dark colour across the court and be of a matt appearance. The net is 15.25 cm (6 in) high and extends 15.25 cm (6 in) past the end of the table.

4. A game is won by being the first player to win 11 points, and be at least 2 points ahead of his or her opponent. If both players have won 10 points, then the first player to get a 2 point lead wins the game. A match is the best of 5 games. The score begins at 0-0, and the server will serve first.

All About Table Tennis. Like most other sports, table tennis had humble beginnings as a “parlor game,” open to anyone with access to a table, paddle, and ball. The game began in the 1880s, when lawn tennis players adapted their game to play indoors during the winter.

Equipment used: Table tennis equipment, consisting of racket, ball, net, and table.

Rules: The ball must rest on an open hand palm. Then it must be tossed up at least 6 inches and struck so the ball first bounces on the server's side and then the opponent's side.
