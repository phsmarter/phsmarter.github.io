6. LITERARY JOURNALISM ASSIGNMENT Your task is to investigate a current or historical event, person, place, or thing and write about it in a creative way. The requirements are as follows: 1. Investigate a person, place, thing, or event through real-world, actionable strategies such as interviewing or under cover tactics, in addition to traditional research methods. 2. Document your investigation. 3. Tell the story. 4. Use literary techniques in your telling. ​


For my literary journalism assignment, I decided to investigate the rise of plant-based diets and the impact they are having on the food industry. I began by conducting interviews with individuals who have adopted plant-based diets, as well as chefs and food industry experts who are incorporating more plant-based options into their menus.

Through my investigation, I discovered that the reasons for adopting plant-based diets vary widely, from concerns about animal welfare to environmental sustainability and health benefits. I also found that the food industry is responding to this trend by offering more plant-based options, both in restaurants and in grocery stores.

To tell the story of this trend, I used literary techniques such as imagery and metaphor. For example, I described the variety of colorful fruits and vegetables available in a farmer's market as a "rainbow of flavors," and likened the plant-based movement to a "green revolution" in the food industry.

Overall, my investigation revealed that the rise of plant-based diets is not just a passing trend, but a growing movement with far-reaching implications for the food industry and our society as a whole. By using literary techniques to tell this story, I hope to engage readers and inspire them to think more critically about their own food choices.
