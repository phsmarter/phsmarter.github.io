Communication etiquette

According to Abdul Samad Arief, et al in the book Basics of Business Communication (2021), ethics is a principle to regulate behavior in society. While communication is a relationship between human interactions, in the form of sending and receiving messages.

It can be concluded that communication ethics is the principle that regulates the interaction relationship in the community in the form of sending and receiving messages.

Functions of Communicating Ethics

Quoted from the book Communication Ethics in Social Media: Filter Before Sharing (2021) by Rahmanita Ginting, et al, here are some functions of communication ethics:

Serves as a moral foundation between humans. For example with good language, polite, clear and concise.Simplify the process of delivering messages. If the process of delivering the message is good, the way it is conveyed will be good.As a human guide in communicating. By applying good communication ethics, humans will receive messages with a good response because ethics will produce polite delivery.

Effective and attractive way of communication etiquette

According to pintek.id there are 9 ways to convey messages properly, including:

Speak well. Deliver messages clearly, not too fast, and speak with bullet points.Be still, listen, and listen carefully. You are digesting the information conveyed by the other person. The response you make will be greatly influenced by how effectively you listen.Adjust to the context. Make sure when you are a beginner to learn to communicate, deliver the material according to the context of the discussion so that you are not confused with what is being presented.Body gestures. Gestures also include the language of how to convey messages, with body style also very effective in attracting the attention of the audience.Insert humor. To keep your presentation from looking so stilted that it's boring, insert a joke for ice-breaking.

*he central doctrine of Schopenhauer's ethics is that morality is based on the everyday phenomenon of compassion, … the immediate participation, independent of all ulterior considerations, primarily in the suffering of another, and thus in the prevention or elimination of it … Only insofar as an action has sprung from compassion does it have moral value; and every action resulting from any other motives has none. Compassion is not egoistic because the compassionate person does not feel different from the suffering person or animal that is seen. Even though the sufferer is experienced as an external being, I nevertheless feel it with him, feel it as my own, and not within me, but in another person… But this presupposes that to a certain extent I have identified myself with the other man, and in consequence the barrier between the ego and the non–ego is for the moment abolished…. Were they followed or violated?What can be done to improve communication processes, principles, and ethics in the media? ​


1. Communication processes used in media content may vary depending on the type of media, audience, and purpose. Some common communication processes used in media content include storytelling, visual communication, persuasive communication, and interactive communication. Whether these processes are effective or not depends on various factors, such as the target audience, the message being conveyed, and the context in which it is presented.

2. Ethical and unethical communication practices in media content can be observed in various forms, such as biased reporting, sensationalism, invasion of privacy, plagiarism, and deception, among others. Ethical communication in media content should be truthful, accurate, and fair, while unethical communication can be manipulative, misleading, or harmful.

3. The ethical standards of communication in the media are crucial in shaping the content that is presented to the audience. These standards can be influenced by factors such as media ownership, government regulations, and professional codes of conduct. Ethical standards require that media content is truthful, accurate, and fair.

4. Whether ethical standards were followed or violated in media content depends on the specific cases being analyzed. Violations can occur due to various reasons, such as pressure from media owners, political influence, or individual biases. To improve communication processes, principles, and ethics in the media, media organizations can implement ethical guidelines, provide training to journalists and staff, and encourage transparency and accountability. Additionally, the government can regulate media content to ensure that it adheres to ethical standards.
