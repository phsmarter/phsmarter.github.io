1. My personal values essay​


Personal values are the beliefs and principles that guide an individual's decision-making and behavior in life. They are the foundational principles that shape our lives and help us to find meaning and purpose in what we do. In this essay, I will discuss my personal values and the importance they have in shaping my beliefs and actions.

Honesty is one of the most important values for me. I believe that being honest with myself and others builds trust, respect, and helps to create harmonious relationships. In my personal and professional life, I have always strived to be honest in my interactions with others, even if it may be difficult or uncomfortable.

Integrity is another value that is important to me. Integrity involves having strong moral principles and being able to stand by them when faced with ethical dilemmas. I believe that integrity is what defines who we are and what we stand for. It is important that I act in a way that reflects honesty, respect, and fairness in all that I do.

Respect is another value that I hold dear. I believe that everyone should be treated equally, regardless of their background, culture, or lifestyle. Respecting others' opinions and values is an essential aspect of building a strong and harmonious community. It is important to me that I respect others and that I am respected in return.

Compassion is a value that helps me to connect with others on a deeper level. I believe that showing compassion towards others helps build relationships and shows that we care about one another.

19. Write an essay about the topic:"My Journey towards Personal Growth”​


Answer:

Mas Mabuti Basahin muna ang nasa picture

This is to be continew

Then it happened. Burnout; Nervous Breakdown; Major Depressive Episode. The health professionals all had different labels. The truth was, I was broken. Pride comes before the fall. Here was the inevitable fall. In the matter of a single moment sitting in front of my computer screen, my entire life changed course. Nothing would ever be the same again. I did not realize it at the time and thought that a day off was all that was required. It was not to be. The psychologist had a serious talk to me and I realized the seriousness of the situation I found myself in. I kept wondering how I had missed all the signs? It was plain to see, constant high blood pressure, inability to sleep, growing anxiety, lack of boundaries, stress, the list was almost endless.

My journey had begun.

For the first time that I can recall I had no destination in mind. I was broken. I did not want to be anywhere or do anything. I had lost the will to live and I didn’t care. Looking back at that time I don’t even recall having thoughts. I either lay in bed or sat on a chair under a blanket. I simply existed, as if in some strange comatose state, whether this was brought on by the breakdown or the handful of tablets that I was required to take every day I am not sure. None the less I was little more than an inanimate object. My fiancé took great care of me, prepared meals, handled whatever business fallouts were taking place, encouraging me to rest and give it time, inviting me to try to make tiny steps towards recovery.

This was not how I imagined the journey would be. After all, when they say that it is about the journey, not the destination, one would think that the journey should at least be enjoyable. I could find absolutely no joy in anything.

There is also a saying that: “every cloud has a silver lining.” Fortunately, that has also proven to be true for me. My brother in law bought me a return ticket to go visit them in India, and I accepted the gift as I needed to just get away from the world. In March this year, I nervously embarked on a journey to go spend some time with them in India. I did not have any plans to do anything in India, and the main reason for me going was just to get away from everything for a while. Even if the only thing I did whilst I was in India was stay in their house for 6 weeks that would have worked for me.

Something inside me shifted though. The moment I said goodbye to my fiancé and stepdaughter and walked through to departures I knew that I was on my own from here. I knew that it was now up to me to create the context for how this journey would unfold.

My childhood memories (personal recount)

Childhood is a precious time that shapes the person we become. Looking back, I can vividly recall many fond memories from my early years. The carefree days of playing with friends, exploring the outdoors, and enjoying the simple pleasures of life.

One of my fondest memories is the times I spent with my grandfather. He was a kind and gentle man, who always had a twinkle in his eye and a story to tell. Whenever I visited him, he would take me on long walks in the countryside, pointing out all the plants and animals we came across.

Another treasured memory is the summer vacations spent at my aunt's farm. We would spend the days picking fresh fruits and vegetables, feeding the animals, and playing in the open fields. At night, we would gather around the campfire and listen to my uncle's stories about his travels around the world.

I also have memories of my school days, the excitement of learning new things, the thrill of participating in sports, and the joy of making new friends. There were times when I struggled with my studies, but my parents and teachers were always there to support and guide me.

As I grew older, life became more complex, and I began to appreciate the simplicity of those childhood days. The memories of those carefree days have stayed with me, providing a source of comfort and inspiration during the challenges of adult life.

In conclusion, childhood memories are an integral part of our lives, shaping our identity and providing a foundation for the future. I cherish the memories of my childhood and the people who helped make it such a special time.
