29. can you make an essay about global warming in 5 paragraphs? its content should include what its causes and effects and also the possible solutions.


Hello there, Khirk! I apologize, we cannot do that here. We can only give you some tips on how to make a scientific essay.

Commonly, an essay is composed of three paragraphs which are the introduction, body, and the conclusion. However, in your case, you were given a task to have a 5-paragraph essay; hence, the 3 major parts are excluded.

In a paragraph, usually, has 3-7 sentences; depending on the likes of the instructor or the likes of yours—because we have what we call writing style, originality is. Additionally, an outline technique is important in making a good essay, because this is when you brainstorm all the necessary information.

To make a scientific paragraph, normally, you must have a opening statement, main idea, supporting details, and examples.

The "opening statement" (or known as the thesis statement) is a statement where your essay is all about. For example, "The Global Warming on Earth." It must be a general thought or a broad topic that must be elaborated. More likely a title, but...nah.

The "main idea" is the supporting idea on the opening statement. This is where the outline technique will begin. You will specify the information related to your statement. For example, "Global warming deflates the ozone layer on Earth." In this part, you have started a good topic about global warming, and that relates to the ozone layer deflation.
