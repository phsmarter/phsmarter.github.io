17. what are the condition Lord capulet give for agreeing to paris marrying Juliet? ​


Answer:

In Shakespeare's play "Romeo and Juliet", Lord Capulet agrees to Paris marrying Juliet but he sets the following conditions:

Paris must wait two years before marrying Juliet.

Paris must win Juliet's heart and gain her consent before the marriage takes place.

Lord Capulet wants to keep the marriage a secret until Paris has won Juliet's heart.

The wedding must be a grand affair with a large guest list.

Lord Capulet insists on having the final say in all the wedding arrangements.

However, it's important to note that Juliet does not love Paris and is already secretly married to Romeo. The marriage to Paris is arranged by Lord Capulet without Juliet's consent, which ultimately leads to tragic consequences.

20. Duty"" is told from Lady Capulet’s point of view. What is her opinion of Romeo and Juliet?


Answer:

Like many other mothers of teens, Lady Capulet has her hands full with Juliet. Sure, Lady Capulet does make an effort to reach out to her daughter now that she's of an age to be married. But it's obvious that Juliet's closest bond is with the Nurse; Lady Capulet never even comes close to challenging that.

As a result, Lady Capulet doesn't come across as a particularly great mom. But why? Why isn't she close to her daughter? Why isn't she supportive when Juliet needs her most? Just when Juliet needs her mom's support, Lady Capulet coldly ignore her daughter's pleas to help her avoid marrying Paris. After Lord Capulet storms out, Juliet turns to her mother to soften her father's punishment. Juliet begs her even to delay the marriage. Lady Capulet responds, "Talk not to me, for I'll not say a word / Do as thou wilt, for I have done with thee"

There could be a few different things going on here. It seems very likely that Lady Capulet herself had an arranged marriage with Juliet's father, and it seems she went along with it obediently. So, when Juliet rebels against the planned marriage with Paris, she's rebelling against her mother's way of life, and against the kind of marriage that Lady Capulet learned to suffer through. In general, moms don't like that—either in the 21st century or in the 16th century.

There's also the more general issue that Lady Capulet is just being a good, obedient wife by going along with what her husband wants—and the really kooky interpretation that she might be pursuing an affair of her own, given her excessive praise of Paris's pretty face.

23. How does Lord Capulet react to Juliet's refusal? What ultimatum does he give her?​


Answer:

How does Lord Capulet react to Juliet's refusal?

While at first Lord Capulet says he will not approve Juliet's marriage to Paris without her consenting to it, when she refuses to marry Paris, Capulet becomes furious with her. He resorts to name-calling, cursing, and a threat of disowning Juliet as he yells in rage against her disobedience.

What ultimatum does he give her?

There are really a couple of ultimatums here. First, Lord Capulet tells Juliet that he will drag her to the church on a hurdle (something that was done to criminals) if she did not go on her own to marry Paris. Juliet's reaction to that is to beg her father to listen to her.

Later on, her father gives another ultimatum. If she does not marry Paris he will throw her out of the house and she will need to go live in the street. When he says this, she begs her mother to put the wedding off a while. Otherwise, she says, they may as well put her bed in the tomb with Tybalt because she will die.
