29. 1. How did dagmay affect the culture of the Mandayas in terms of textiles and clothing?2. How did T’nalak show the significance of the T’boli’s traditions and belief in the realm of dreams?3. How did kulintang show the importance of music in one’s community?​


1. Dagmay is a traditional textile art form of the Mandayas, which has greatly impacted their culture in terms of textiles and clothing. The intricate and colorful designs of Dagmay reflect the Mandayas' rich cultural heritage and have become an important symbol of their identity. The Mandayas use Dagmay not only for clothing but also for ritual purposes, including weddings, births, and other important occasions.

2. T'nalak is a traditional handwoven fabric of the T'boli people in Southern Mindanao. It is made from abaca fibers and is believed to be a product of dreams. The significance of T'nalak lies in the belief that it is a manifestation of the dreamweaver's spiritual connection to the divine. Through T'nalak, the T'boli's traditions and beliefs are preserved and passed down to future generations. It also represents their respect for nature and their way of life.

3. Kulintang is a traditional musical instrument of the Maranao people in Mindanao. It is a set of small, horizontally laid gongs that produce a melodious sound. The importance of kulintang in the Maranao community is evident in its use in various rituals, including weddings, funerals, and other important occasions. The music produced by kulintang is believed to bring harmony and balance to the community, and it is also used to communicate with spirits and ancestors. Thus, kulintang is an integral part of the Maranao culture and identity.
