8. What is poetry? And what makes a poem, a poem?​


Answer:

Poetry is a type of literature based on the interplay of words and rhythm. It often employs rhyme and meter (a set of rules governing the number and arrangement of syllables in each line). In poetry, words are strung together to form sounds, images, and ideas that might be too complex or abstract to describe directly.

Most of my poetry was lost through years of running, using and abusing - it was burned up, snorted up or shot up.

I probably had a verse for every hit I took at some point. With addiction, it doesn't matter what or how much you have when you get clean - it can all disappear in an instant. Flat screen TV's, fancy cars, tropical vacations - I can cook each one of those material things up in a spoon and shoot it in heroin form.

​

Poetry, however, can't be replaced if its lost - poems are not as trivial as a vacation. I can't revive the rhythmic order or linguistic flow of each piece.

Some things were meant to run their course and not be replaced or recreated, I suppose.

But everything happens for a reason, so I give what I have and I'll keep adding more.

29. example poem with elements of poetry​


Answer:

Stanzas are a series of lines grouped together and separated by an empty line from other stanzas. They are the equivalent of a paragraph in an essay. One way to identify a stanza is to count the number of lines. Thus:

couplet (2 lines)

tercet (3 lines)

quatrain (4 lines)

cinquain (5 lines)

sestet (6 lines) (sometimes it's called a sexain)

septet (7 lines)
