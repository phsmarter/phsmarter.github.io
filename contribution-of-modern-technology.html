In simple terms, the phrase "mathematics as the language of nature" means that maths is the key to understanding how the natural world works. The laws of mathematics can be used to explain and predict the behavior of physical phenomena, from the smallest subatomic particles to the largest galaxies. This understanding of nature in mathematical terms is fundamental to the development of technology and engineering, as it enables people to manipulate and control their environment with precision and accuracy. The fact that the human brain can comprehend this language is a testament to the power and universality of mathematical concepts. However, despite its importance, this fact is not always fully appreciated, and many people fail to recognize the crucial role of mathematics in shaping our understanding of the natural world.
