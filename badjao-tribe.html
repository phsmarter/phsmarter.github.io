Answer:

Manobo - The Manobo people are an indigenous group in Mindanao known for their complex social structure, traditional beliefs, and cultural practices. They are subdivided into various subgroups with distinct dialects, customs, and traditions. The Manobo are skilled in agriculture, hunting, and fishing, and they have a deep respect for nature and the spirits. They have faced many challenges in preserving their culture and way of life due to external influences and development projects.

Badjao - The Badjao, also known as sea gypsies, are a nomadic indigenous group that inhabit the coastal areas of Mindanao. They are known for their exceptional swimming and diving skills, and their traditional way of life revolves around the sea. The Badjao have a distinct language, culture, and religious beliefs, and they have faced many challenges in preserving their unique way of life due to modernization, poverty, and conflict.

Maranao - The Maranao people are an indigenous group in Mindanao known for their rich cultural heritage, arts, and crafts. They have a strong Islamic faith and are proud of their heritage and traditions. The Maranao are skilled in weaving, metalworking, and woodcarving, and they have a rich tradition of storytelling and poetry. They have faced many challenges in preserving their cultural identity due to historical conflicts, modernization, and development projects.

Yakan - The Yakan people are an indigenous group in Mindanao known for their colorful and intricate weaving traditions. They have a distinct language, culture, and religious beliefs that are deeply rooted in their history and traditions. The Yakan are skilled in weaving, carving, and metalworking, and they have a deep respect for nature and the environment. They have faced many challenges in preserving their cultural heritage due to modernization, poverty, and conflict.
