What is the main argument of strain theory?

Strain Theory argues that crime occurs when there aren't enough legitimate opportunities for people to achieve the normal success goals of a society. In such a situation there is a 'strain' between the goals and the means to achieve those goals, and some people turn to crime in order to achieve success.

28. Explain the two types of deviance associated with labeling theory.


Deviance has been defined as "a violation of social norms" Labeling Theory explains this concept by stating that individuals will treat those groups which differ from them differently than other groups. The main focus of this theory is on how labels influence our behavior towards different groups. It focuses specifically on how we label ourselves and others based on their group membership. For example, if someone were born into an ethnic minority group, they would likely feel more comfortable associating themselves with members of their own group rather than with members of another group. This is referred to as internalized discrimination. If someone was born into an ethnic majority group, however, they may feel uncomfortable around people outside of their group. This is called externalized discrimination.
