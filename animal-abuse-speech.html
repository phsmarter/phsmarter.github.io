2. Emotional harm: Animals that are subjected to abuse may suffer from emotional trauma, such as fear, anxiety, and depression. This can result in behavior problems, such as aggression or withdrawal, making it more difficult for them to be adopted or rehomed.

19. Speech about child and women abuse


Answer:

Here is an example of a student speech about child and women abuse:

"Hello fellow students,

Today, I want to talk to you about a serious issue that affects children and women all around the world: abuse. Child abuse is the physical, sexual, emotional, or neglectful treatment of a child by a parent, caregiver, or someone else in a position of power or trust. Women abuse, also known as domestic violence, is the use of physical, sexual, emotional, or financial abuse by a partner or spouse to control and dominate the other person.

These forms of abuse are not only morally wrong, but they also have severe and long-lasting effects on the victims. Children who experience abuse may suffer from physical injuries, developmental delays, and mental health issues. Women who experience abuse may also suffer from physical injuries, as well as emotional and psychological trauma.

It's important that we all do our part to prevent child and women abuse. We can do this by speaking up if we see or hear about abuse happening, and by being supportive and nonjudgmental of those who have experienced abuse. We can also educate ourselves about the signs of abuse and how to report it.

Let's work together to create a safer and more loving world for children and women. Thank you."
