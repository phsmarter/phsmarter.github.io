4. Suppose a gardener learns that the soil in a garden has low nitrogen content. Describe two ways to increase the nitrogen available for plants in the garden.


Answer:

Nitrogen is a plant fertilizer necessary for the proper growth of plants. There are two ways or methods to increase the nitrogen available for plants in the soil: organic and non-organic.

The organic method includes adding composted manure or coffee grounds to the soil, planting a green manure crop such as borage or planting nitrogen fixing plants such as peas or beans. This method gives better results but requires time.

The non-organic method includes adding chemical fertilizers. This method gives quick results, but short-term.

[tex]l = - w + 12 \\ \\ 27 = lw \\ 27 = ( - w + 12)(w) \\ 27 = - {w}^{2} + 12w \\ {w}^{2} - 12w + 27 = 0 \\ (w - 9)(w -3) = 0 \\ \\ w - 9 = 0 \\ w = 9 \\ \\ w - 3 = 0 \\ w = 3[/tex]

22. A rectangular garden has an area of 132 m^2 and a perimeter of 46 m. What equation would describe the area of the garden? Write the equation in terms of the width of the garden.


Sum and Product of Roots of Quadratic Equations:Area of the Rectangle:

Question:

A rectangular garden has an area of 132 m² and a perimeter of 46 m. What equation would describe the area of the garden? Write the equation in terms of the width of the garden.

Answer:

The area of the garden is defined by the equation x(23 - x) = 132.

Solution:

Using the formula P = 2l + 2w then 46 = 2l + 2w.Solve for w in terms of the length then 2w = 46 - 2l.Dividing both sides of the equation by 2 it will give: 2w/2 = 46 - 2l/2 = w = 23 - l.Allow x to represent the width then x = 23 - x where 23 - x is the length of the rectangle.Now, using the formula A = l x w, where A is the area, l is the length, and w is the width then, 132 = (x)(23 - x).
