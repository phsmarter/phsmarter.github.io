4. Give 2 advocacy were they use ict for social change and explain


Answer:

Digital Rights Advocacy: This advocacy uses ICT to promote and protect digital rights such as privacy, freedom of expression, and access to information. 18. how to plan and promote campaign advocacy for social change​?


Answer:

Define your goals and objectives: Clearly define the problem or issue you want to address, and what specific change you hope to achieve. Identify your target audience, and define your key message.

Research your audience and the issue: Conduct research on your target audience, including demographics and key issues. Research the issue you want to address, including current policies and legislation, and identify existing campaigns and organizations working on the issue.

Develop a plan: Create a detailed plan that includes specific goals, strategies, and tactics to achieve your objectives. Identify key milestones and timelines for your campaign, and assign specific tasks and responsibilities to team members.

Build a coalition: Build a coalition of organizations, community leaders, and individuals who share your goals and can help you achieve them. Identify potential allies and partners, and work together to create a unified message and strategy.

Create a communication plan: Develop a communication plan that includes targeted messaging, social media, and traditional media outreach. Identify key influencers and media outlets that can help amplify your message.

Measure and evaluate your progress: Regularly measure and evaluate your progress, and adjust your strategies as needed. Use metrics to track your progress, and use feedback from your target audience to improve your campaign.

Celebrate and sustain your successes: Celebrate your successes, and work to sustain the momentum of your campaign. Use your successes to build momentum and support for future campaigns.

22. What are some sample advocacies for social change in the community?


Community Advocacy

Community advocacy can help you over the phone or in person with issues including self-advocacy, making difficult decisions about your care and treatment, getting access to social, medical, and healthcare resources, planning for your end of life, making future care plans, and more. In the end, community advocacy supports you in living the life you want to live by enabling you to access information, explore your alternatives and choices in life, understand your human rights, and be more actively involved in life decisions.

1. Commit to performing one little activity related to your passions, interests, and values each day or each week.

For instance, if environmental concerns are important to you, you could help out in the neighborhood community garden. You might build a tiny free pantry if you are concerned about food justice.

2. Participate in mutual help initiatives or volunteer with nearby groups.

Look for neighborhood organizations to donate your time and effort to. Keep an eye out for regional mutual help initiatives that you might join.

3. Post information on social media and retweet it.

We can now quickly communicate problems and solutions thanks to the internet. Both online and offline, hashtags have made it easier for people to connect, and posting or retweeting content that supports movements, news, or leaders is one method to do so.

4. Include donation choices when you host or attend events.

Invite attendees to bring items like canned food or period care supplies to events like book clubs, neighborhood meetings, and celebrations. Then give the collection to a neighborhood charity. Since the majority of people usually already have these goods on hand, this is a fantastic method to involve your community in action.

Advocacy and social change

Advocacy and social change are at the heart of what we do, whether working to achieve gender equality and social inclusion or to ensure universal health care. We know that creating long-term social transformation within societies requires investment in strong advocacy and social movements. We also know that progressive social policies are influenced by mobilising civil society, changing the political process to include previously marginalised groups, challenging existing gender norms and promoting alternative social norms.

We are creating change by:

Working with multiple partners to manage complex, multi-country and global social change initiatives

Developing and managing branded campaigns for social change and advocacy that bring together civil society organisations

Partnering with civil society and governments to develop strategic and operational plans, advocacy plans and messaging frameworks based on strong theories of change

Building the capacity of civil society organisations and the media to better use and package data to influence political will

Conducting innovative research to understand the perspectives of marginalised people and to identify opportunities for social change

Monitoring and evaluating advocacy and social change initiatives to draw lessons from complex processes of change.
