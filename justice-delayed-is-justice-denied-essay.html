12. "Justice delayed is Justice denied" explain this statement​


Answer:

This statement means that if justice is not carried out promptly or efficiently, it is effectively denied to the person seeking it. The idea is that justice should be administered in a timely manner, so that the wronged person can receive the remedy or compensation that they are due. If justice is delayed, it may no longer be relevant or effective in addressing the situation, and the person seeking justice may be left without recourse. Therefore, the saying emphasizes the importance of ensuring that justice is administered swiftly and fairly.

27. Justice is tarried when the filing of cases is delayed. What word in the sentence is synonymous is with tarried?


Answer:

The word that is synonymous with tarried is delayed.

Explanation:

Justice is tarried when the filing of cases is delayed.

The given sentence above tells that the time for justice is dependent with the filing of case. For this reason, when the filing of case is delayed, justice will be delayed as well. Therefore, the verb tarried should be similar to the word delayed.

Tarry verb \ˈta-rē\

To delay an action or an activity being done.

Examples:

The teachers tarry the examination because of the weather.

Nelson’s bank transactions got tarried.

Delay noun \ di-ˈlā\

To slow down or inhibit an action, event, or process.

Example:

The medication delayed the spread of infection.

I have to delay our meeting today.
