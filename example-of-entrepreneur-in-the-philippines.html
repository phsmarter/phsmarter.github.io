2 Kinds of Weathering

1. Mechanical weathering (Physical Weathering)

 breaking of rocks or minerals into smaller pieces without altering its composition.

 Examples: abrasion (by waterand wind), Frost action/ frost wedging, Thermal expansion and contraction, Root pry or Root action

2. Chemical Weathering

 involves change in the composition of rocks that allows them to break down into pieces

 Examples: Oxidation, Organic compounds, Carbonation

Agents of Weathering

1. Water 4. Plants

2. Wind 5. Animals

3. Temperature 6. Humans

Soil Erosion

 The process by which wind and water transport soil particles from one place to another.

Sediments

Agents of Soil Erosion
