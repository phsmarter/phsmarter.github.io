10. Help me pleaseee...observe how students learn in a learner-centered curriculum.


Answer:

In a learner-centered curriculum, the focus is on the needs and interests of the student, rather than solely on the teacher or the material being taught. In this type of learning environment, students take an active role in their own education and are encouraged to explore and discover new ideas and concepts on their own.

Observing students in a learner-centered curriculum can involve several methods, including:

Classroom observations: Observing students as they participate in class discussions, group work, and independent projects can provide insight into their learning styles and engagement levels.

Student self-reflection: Encouraging students to reflect on their own learning experiences can provide valuable insight into their thought processes, motivations, and challenges.

Assessment of student work: Evaluating student projects, essays, and other forms of assessed work can provide evidence of their learning and understanding of the material.

Student-teacher conferences: One-on-one conversations between students and teachers can help to identify areas where students may need additional support or challenges.

By observing students in a learner-centered curriculum, educators can gain a better understanding of the individual needs and interests of each student, and adjust their teaching strategies accordingly. This can lead to a more engaging and effective learning experience for students, and can help to foster a love of learning that will benefit them throughout their lives.

13. What are the strengths and weaknesses of each of the different types of curriculum operating in schools? taught curriculum supported curriculum recommended curriculum written curriculum learned curriculum assessed curriculum hidden curriculum I need it now. Nonsense report​


Answer:

Taught curriculum refers to the curriculum that is actively taught to students by teachers. The strengths of this type of curriculum include its ability to provide a clear structure and direction for teaching and learning, as well as the ability to ensure that key knowledge and skills are covered. However, a weakness of taught curriculum could be that it may be too rigid and not allow for flexibility or student-centered learning.

Supported curriculum refers to the resources and materials that are provided to teachers to assist them in delivering the taught curriculum. The strengths of this type of curriculum include the ability to provide teachers with the necessary resources to effectively teach the curriculum. A weakness may be that it might not be up to date with the latest research, or might not align with the needs and interests of the students.

Recommended curriculum refers to curriculum that is suggested or recommended by educational authorities or organizations, but is not necessarily mandatory. The strength of this type of curriculum is that it can provide guidance and recommendations for teaching and learning, while also allowing for flexibility and local decision-making. A weakness may be that it might not be properly enforced or implemented.

Written curriculum refers to the official documents that outline the goals, objectives, and content of the curriculum. The strengths of this type of curriculum include the ability to provide a clear and measurable framework for teaching and learning, as well as the ability to ensure that key knowledge and skills are covered. A weakness may be that it might not be flexible enough to meet the needs of all students.

Learned curriculum refers to the curriculum that is actually learned by students, which may differ from the taught or written curriculum. The strength of this type of curriculum is that it can provide insight into what students are actually learning and understanding. A weakness may be that it might not align with the goals and objectives of the taught or written curriculum.

Assessed curriculum refers to the curriculum that is evaluated or measured to determine student progress and achievement. The strength of this type of curriculum is that it can provide a means of determining whether students are meeting the goals and objectives of the curriculum. A weakness may be that it might not be aligned with the goals of the taught or written curriculum or might not be fair or accurately measuring student learning.

Hidden curriculum refers to the implicit values, beliefs, and attitudes that are conveyed through the education system. The strength of this type of curriculum is that it can provide insight into the underlying values and beliefs of the education system. A weakness may be that it might be unconsciously conveying harmful or biased messages.

Restructuring is the process of re-organizing something to make it more efficient and effective. It involves changing the structure of something to make it more suitable for a particular purpose. Restructuring can involve changing the way something works, such as introducing new policies, procedures, or systems. It can also involve changing the way an organization is structured, such as re-organizing departments and changing reporting lines. Restructuring can also involve introducing new roles, changing job titles, and altering the way people work together. Restructuring can be used to make an organization more efficient, to create new opportunities for growth, and to reduce costs. When it comes to education, restructuring could involve changing the curriculum, introducing new technologies, or altering the way teachers and students interact. It could also involve introducing new policies and procedures, such as assessment and reporting methods, or it could involve changing the way schools are managed and governed. Whatever the changes, the goal of restructuring is to make the educational system more effective and efficient.
