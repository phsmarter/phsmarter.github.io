Writing employment instruments is essential to ensure that the terms and conditions of an employee's work are clearly defined and understood. Employment instruments provide the foundation for the relationship between employer and employee. These instruments may include job descriptions, job postings, offer letters, employment contracts, confidentiality agreements, and non-disclosure agreements. It is important to have a clear and comprehensive understanding of these documents in order to create an equitable and mutually beneficial agreement for both parties.

Learning how to write employment instruments, such as resumes, cover letters, and job applications, is essential because these documents are often the first impression that a potential employer has of a job candidate.
