11. Make a agrumentative essay about "students are becoming wise and intelligent but have less manners"​


Answer:

The debate around whether or not students are becoming wise and intelligent but lacking in manners has been going on for years. Some argue that students are receiving an education and thereby growing in knowledge and understanding, while others point out that the lack of politeness and manners has been on the decline. While an argument can be made for both sides, it is clear that students are indeed becoming wiser and more intelligent, but they are also increasingly lacking in manners.

To begin with, education has made an undeniably positive impact on the knowledge and wisdom of students around the world. People in their twenties today know more than their grandparents at their age, and in most countries, higher education is becoming more accessible than ever, allowing more students to have access to the wisdom and expertise of university-level coursework. With this access comes increased understanding and the ability to think critically about the world around them.

Despite this increase in wisdom and knowledge, students are also becoming increasingly lacking in manners. A recent survey of high schoolers found that 78 percent of them admitted to using inappropriate language in public places, and nearly three quarters of them said that using rude language was common among their peers. This is a worrying trend, and it's likely linked to a decline in individual respect among students, as well as to the tendency of millennials and younger generations to use the internet and social media to be rude and disrespectful to each other.

Overall, it can be seen that while students are becoming wiser and more intelligent, they are also increasingly lacking in politeness and manners. This worrying trend can be attributed to a number of factors, including the rise of instant messaging, social media, and the decline of respect among young people. To curb this trend, educators should place an emphasis on teaching respect and manners in their classrooms and prioritize teaching students to treat each other with kindness. Doing so would help ensure that the next generation of students is both intelligent and polite.

Answer:

Education is undoubtedly one of the most crucial aspects of an individual's life. It helps students to acquire knowledge and develop critical thinking skills that are essential for their personal and professional growth. However, the question of whether students are becoming wiser and more intelligent but have less manners is a matter of debate.

On one hand, it is true that students are becoming more intelligent due to the advancements in education technology and the increasing accessibility of information. With the availability of the internet, students can easily access a wealth of knowledge, allowing them to broaden their horizons and gain a better understanding of the world. Moreover, schools are now emphasizing critical thinking skills over rote memorization, encouraging students to analyze problems and develop creative solutions.

However, this increasing focus on academic excellence has led to a decline in manners and social etiquette among students. Many students prioritize academic success over interpersonal skills, leading to a lack of respect for others and an inability to communicate effectively. In today's world, it is not uncommon to see students using their phones or other devices during class, ignoring their peers and teachers. This not only shows a lack of respect for others but also undermines the learning environment.

Moreover, the pressure to perform academically can sometimes lead to a disregard for social norms and values. Students may feel that they need to cheat or engage in unethical behavior to achieve academic success, which can lead to a lack of integrity and a disregard for the consequences of their actions. This can ultimately harm their relationships with others and their ability to succeed in the long term.

In conclusion, while it is true that students are becoming more intelligent and wise, they are also becoming increasingly disconnected from social norms and manners. It is important for students to recognize that academic excellence is not the only measure of success, and that interpersonal skills and respect for others are equally important. Teachers and parents can play a crucial role in instilling these values in students, by emphasizing the importance of manners, social etiquette, and integrity in all aspects of life. By doing so, we can ensure that students not only achieve academic success but also become well-rounded individuals with strong moral character.

21. History of intelligence during World War ll ,Post War at present time?Explain essay?​


Answer:

During the Revolutionary War, General George Washington was an avid user of intelligence as well as a consummate practitioner of the intelligence craft. Records show that shortly after taking command of the Continental Army in 1775, Washington paid an unidentified agent to live in Boston and surreptitiously report by use of "secret correspondence" on the movements of British forces. Indeed, Washington recruited and ran a number of agents, set up spy rings, devised secret methods of reporting, analyzed the raw intelligence gathered by his agents, and mounted an extensive campaign to deceive the British armies. Historians cite these activities as having played a major role in the victory at Yorktown and in the ability of the Continental Army to evade the British during the winters at Valley Forge.
