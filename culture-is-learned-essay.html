From a philosophical perspective, the self is seen as a complex and subjective concept that is composed of different parts. Philosophers such as Descartes, Hume and Kant have argued that there is a fundamental essence of the self that is both unique and immutable. This essence is often seen as being composed of the mind and body, and is thought to be the basis of our sense of identity.
