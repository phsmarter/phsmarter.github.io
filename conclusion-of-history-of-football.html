1. history of association of football​


Answer:

The modern game of association football originated with mid-nineteenth century efforts between local football clubs to standardize the varying sets of rules, culminating in formation of The Football Association in London, England in 1863.Rugby football, named after an English boarding school, was a variation that allowed players to carry and run with the ball to advance it toward the goal. The game played under the Football Association's rules thus became known as association football.The rules drafted by the association allowed clubs to play each other without dispute, and specifically banned both handling of the ball (except by goalkeepers) and hacking during open field play. After the fifth meeting of the association a schism emerged between association football and the rules played by the Rugby school, later to be called rugby football. Football has been an Olympic sport ever since the second modern Summer Olympic Games in 1900.

24. if steve throws the football 50 meters in 3 seconds, what is the average speed of the football?​


To calculate the average speed of the football, we need to use the formula:

Average speed = distance / time

In this case, the distance is 50 meters and the time is 3 seconds. So, we can plug these values into the formula:

Average speed = 50 meters / 3 seconds

Simplifying this expression gives us:

Average speed = 16.67 meters/second (rounded to two decimal places)

Therefore, the average speed of the football is 16.67 meters per second.

To expand on this answer, we can think of average speed as the total distance traveled by an object over a certain period of time. In this case, Steve threw the football a total distance of 50 meters in 3 seconds. The average speed tells us how fast the football was moving on average throughout this period of time.

It's important to note that average speed is different from instantaneous speed. Instantaneous speed refers to the speed of an object at any given moment in time, while average speed calculates the overall speed over a certain period of time.

In practical terms, knowing the average speed of an object can be useful in a number of contexts. For example, in sports like football, knowing how fast a player can throw or kick the ball can be important for making strategic decisions during a game. In physics, average speed is often used to calculate other important properties like velocity and acceleration.

A player kick 0.40 kg football with a force of 25 N. what is the acceleration of the football?

m ( mass ) = 0.4 kg

Solution:

We can use the formula ( F = m × a ) in order to solve the problem: to find (a), we will its derivation formula  (a = F ÷ m)

a = F ÷ m

  = 25 kg·m/s² ÷ 0.4 kg

  = 62.5 m/s²
