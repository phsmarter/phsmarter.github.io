22. Why isn't it recommended to settle private dispute on social media?​


Answer:

It is not recommended to settle private disputes on social media because it can cause more harm than good. Social media platforms are public spaces where everyone can see what is being said, and the conversation can quickly escalate and get out of control.

When people argue on social media, there is a high risk of misinterpretation and misunderstandings. This is because written communication can be easily misinterpreted, and people may not be able to convey their tone or intention accurately. Moreover, people often react quickly on social media without fully considering the consequences of their words.

Settling private disputes on social media can also have long-lasting negative consequences. It can damage relationships, lead to legal problems, and harm reputations. Moreover, once something is posted on social media, it can be difficult to take it back or remove it completely, and it may stay online indefinitely.

In summary, it is best to settle private disputes through private communication channels, such as in-person conversations, phone calls, or emails, where the parties involved can have a constructive and respectful discussion without the added pressure and public scrutiny of social media.
