4. make a persuasive essay about" Should we have to do Homework? "​


Answer:

In modern education, homework has been a longstanding practice that has been the subject of debate for decades. While some educators argue that homework is an essential part of a student's education, others believe that it is an unnecessary burden on students' time and mental health. In this essay, I will argue that students should not be burdened with homework.

Firstly, homework often results in an overwhelming amount of stress for students. When students are given a substantial amount of homework, they become anxious and overwhelmed, which can result in feelings of burnout and exhaustion. This stress not only negatively impacts their mental health but can also affect their performance in class, leading to poor academic performance and low grades.

Secondly, the time spent doing homework could be better used for other important activities. After spending six to eight hours at school, students should be able to spend their free time engaging in activities they enjoy, such as sports, hobbies, or spending time with family and friends. These activities are important for a student's mental and physical health and help to promote a balanced lifestyle.

Furthermore, homework can also create a significant imbalance in the time and workload between students. Some students may have the resources, support, and time to complete their homework while others may not. This imbalance creates a disadvantage for students who cannot afford to dedicate extra time and resources to their homework, thus widening the achievement gap and creating an unfair playing field.

In conclusion, homework should not be a requirement in modern education. Homework often results in an overwhelming amount of stress, reduces time for other important activities, and can create an imbalance between students. Instead, educators should focus on in-class activities, group projects, and other innovative teaching strategies that promote a healthy and balanced learning environment. It is time to reconsider the value of homework and place students' mental and physical health as a top priority.

Answer:

As a student, it is important to have a balance between academic responsibilities and personal life. One way to achieve this balance is by eliminating homework on weekends. Homework is an essential part of learning, but it can be overwhelming and stressful when it is assigned during weekends. Therefore, I firmly believe that students should not have homework on weekends.

First and foremost, weekends are a time for rest and relaxation. After a long week of classes, students deserve a break from the academic workload. A homework-free weekend allows students to recharge their batteries and spend time with their family and friends. Moreover, students who have hobbies or engage in extracurricular activities have the opportunity to pursue their interests without the added stress of homework.

Secondly, homework can be a significant source of stress and anxiety for students. When homework is assigned on weekends, students may feel like they are constantly under pressure to complete their work. This can lead to a lack of motivation, which can ultimately harm academic performance. In addition, excessive homework can also affect the physical and mental health of students, leading to fatigue and burnout.

Lastly, it is essential to remember that homework is not the only measure of a student's academic success. While it is necessary to complete homework assignments to a satisfactory standard, a student's ability to learn and retain knowledge should not be solely based on homework completion. Teachers can provide alternative methods of assessing students' understanding of the material, such as class discussions, group projects, or in-class assignments.

In conclusion, the weekend is a time for relaxation, family, friends, and personal interests. Therefore, I strongly believe that students should not have homework on weekends. A break from the academic workload allows students to recharge, destress, and engage in other activities that contribute to their overall well-being. By eliminating homework on weekends, students can achieve a healthy balance between academic responsibilities and personal life.

17. "does homework help you learn?" argumentative essay​


Answer:

Research has shown that homework can improve the achievement of students in terms of exam results, grades, and the likelihood of attending college. Studies have also indicated that students who spend time each day on their homework scored better on exams compared to students who didn't do homework. One can develop a better memory and incorporate new skills with repetition. Homework is based on classwork and focuses on integrating the new skill through its practice. This helps in boosting memory and retaining the acquired knowledge for exams and future tests. Homework can provide an increased understanding of concepts taught at school. Giving students extra practice, allows those concepts to be committed to each student's long term memory. Good homework reinforces what is taught at school.

Homework has been a controversial topic for many years, and the question of whether it actually helps students learn is one that is still debated today. While some argue that homework is an essential part of the learning process, I believe that it is not an effective tool for helping students learn. In fact, homework can often be a waste of time for students who have responsibilities beyond their homes, and it can even lead to cheating and academic dishonesty.

One of the main arguments against homework is that it can be copied or found online. With the internet at our fingertips, students can easily search for answers to homework questions and copy them without actually learning anything. This can lead to a false sense of understanding and can even be detrimental to a student's learning in the long run. Additionally, students who copy homework may not actually understand the material and may struggle with future assignments or tests.

Furthermore, homework can be a waste of time for students who have responsibilities beyond their homes. Many students have part-time jobs, extracurricular activities, or family obligations that take up much of their time outside of school. For these students, spending hours on homework each night can be a burden and can even detract from their ability to focus on other important tasks. This can lead to stress and burnout, which can ultimately harm a student's academic performance and well-being.

In conclusion, I do not support the argument that homework helps students learn. While it may be a useful tool for some students, it can also lead to cheating and academic dishonesty, and it can be a waste of time for those with responsibilities beyond their homes. Instead of relying on homework as the sole means of learning, schools should focus on creating engaging and interactive classrooms that allow students to learn in a variety of ways. By doing so, we can help all students succeed and reach their full potential.

21. Write a persuasive essay on the topic Homework is necessary giving two reasons against the topic


Homework is a topic that has been heavily debated for decades. Some argue that it is necessary for students to succeed, while others believe it is an unnecessary burden. In this essay, we will explore two reasons why homework is not necessary.

Firstly, homework can be harmful to a student's mental health. With the increasing amount of homework being assigned, students are being put under immense pressure to perform. This can lead to anxiety, stress, and even depression. 26. Disagreeing the "No homework policy" argumentative essay for debate.Introduction:Argument1:Argument2:Argument3:Conclusion:​


Answer:

Introduction:

As students, we are accustomed to receiving homework as part of our academic life. However, the debate over the effectiveness of homework has been ongoing for years, with some advocating for the abolishment of homework altogether. While the idea of no homework policy may sound attractive to students, it is not a feasible solution to improve the educational system. In fact, homework is an essential component of learning, and it helps students develop crucial skills that they will need in the future.

Argument 1: Homework Helps Students Learn and Retain Information

One of the primary reasons why homework is essential is that it reinforces what students have learned in the classroom. By completing homework assignments, students are more likely to remember the information they have learned and retain it for a more extended period. Moreover, homework provides an opportunity for students to apply what they have learned in the classroom to real-life situations. This helps them develop a better understanding of the subject matter, and it enables them to see the practical applications of the knowledge they have gained.

Argument 2: Homework Develops Key Skills

Homework also helps students develop key skills that are essential for success in both their academic and personal lives. By completing homework assignments, students learn how to manage their time effectively, prioritize tasks, and meet deadlines. These are skills that will serve them well in college and beyond, as well as in their careers. Additionally, homework encourages students to think critically and independently, as they must solve problems on their own without the help of a teacher.

Argument 3: Homework Prepares Students for the Future

Homework is not just about learning subject matter; it is also about preparing students for the future. In college and the workforce, students will be required to complete assignments independently and manage their time effectively. Homework is an excellent way to develop these skills, and it provides students with a taste of what they can expect in the future. Additionally, homework teaches students responsibility and accountability, as they are expected to complete assignments on time and to the best of their abilities.

Conclusion:

While it may be tempting to do away with homework altogether, this is not a practical or effective solution. Homework is a critical component of learning, and it provides numerous benefits to students. It reinforces classroom learning, helps students develop key skills, and prepares them for the future. Rather than abolishing homework, we should focus on improving the quality of homework assignments and finding ways to make them more engaging and relevant to students' lives. Homework should be seen as an opportunity for students to continue their learning outside of the classroom, and as a way to develop essential skills that will serve them well in the future
