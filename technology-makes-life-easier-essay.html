27. how computer technology helps people to make life easier.​


PA BRAINLIEST PO

There are many ways in which computer technology helps people to make life easier. Some of the most significant ways include:

Communication: Computers and the internet have made it easier for people to communicate with each other, regardless of where they are located. People can use email, messaging apps, video conferencing, and social media to stay in touch with friends, family, and colleagues.

Work and productivity: Computers and related technology have greatly improved the efficiency and productivity of many different types of work. For example, word processing and spreadsheet software make it easy to create and edit documents and data, while project management tools help teams to collaborate and coordinate their work.

Education and learning: The internet and online learning platforms have made education more accessible to people around the world. It is now possible to take classes, earn degrees, and learn new skills online, which can be especially useful for those who may not have the opportunity to attend traditional in-person schools or institutions.

Entertainment: Computers and the internet provide an almost endless supply of entertainment options, including movies, TV shows, music, games, and more. People can access this content from their computers, tablets, or smartphones, making it easy to stay entertained on the go.

Convenience: In general, computers and related technology have made many tasks and activities more convenient. For example, people can use online shopping and delivery services to purchase goods and have them delivered to their homes, and they can use ride-sharing apps to get around without owning a car.

29. Give at least two technological innovations. How do these inventions make life easier or comfortable for us?


Answer:

Technology

Electronic Digital Computer: John Atanasoff and Clifford Berry - 1937

John Atanasoff and Clifford Berry constructed the first electronic computer with vacuum tubes while working for Iowa State College. The Atanasoff-Berry computer was the first digital computer. It introduced the concepts of binary arithmetic, regenerative memory, and logic circuits. The Atanasoff-Berry machine never reached the production stage and remained a prototype.

Energy

Modern Alternating Current Electrical Supply System: Nikola Tesla - 1891

Nikola Tesla filed for seven U.S. patents in the field of polyphase alternating current motors and power transmission. Tesla's patents comprised a complete system of generators, transformers, transmission lines, motors, and lighting. Tesla is also credited with the invention of the radio, although a patent dispute with the Marconi Company that was ultimately decided by the U.S. Supreme Court brings into question if he should receive the sole credit for the radio invention.

Antibiotics: Alexander Fleming - 1928

Alexander Fleming discovered the active substance that he termed "penicillin" while working on the influenza virus. Fleming made this discovery by observing that mold had developed accidentally on a staphylococcus culture plate and that the mold had created a bacteria-free circle around itself. He was inspired to further experiment and he found that a mold culture prevented the growth of staphylococci, which verified his discovery. To this day, penicillin is used to treat a host of bacterial infection
