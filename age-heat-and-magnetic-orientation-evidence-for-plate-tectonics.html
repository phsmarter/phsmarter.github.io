Magnetic patterns are important evidence for plate tectonics because we can use the magnetic signatures of rock to identify the movement of large.

Modern continents hold clues to their distant past. Evidence from fossils, glaciers, and complementary coastlines helps reveal how the plates once fit together. Fossils tell us when and where plants and animals once existed.

Plate tectonics consists of two words that have different meanings. Plates are the parts that make up the material in the uppermost area of ​​the earth.

Most of the earth's plates will have the resilience to reach 100 km. Meanwhile, the word tectonic has the meaning of a movement process in the area of ​​the earth's crust that can cause indentations, folds, faults that can result in high and low conditions on the earth's surface.

There are three types of tectonic plate movements. Starting from divergent movements, convergent movements and also transform movements.

Divergent Movement

Divergent movements or boundaries can also be referred to as accretion zones or the process of forming new plates. Where the divergent boundary is also a zone that undergoes a process of plate movement away from each other. Therefore, the empty space caused by the movement of the plates away will be filled by the Earth's mantle in the lithosphere layer.

Convergent Plate

Next there is the movement or convergent boundary which is also a zone of destruction or consumption. In this condition, later the plates on the earth's surface will approach each other. Where there will be a plate that will enter and penetrate the mantle so that the plate can experience high temperature melting or destruction.

Transform Movement

The movement or transform boundary can also be referred to as a shear boundary. This is because at the transform boundary there is no destroyed lithosphere or no newly created lithosphere. The plates will tend to move laterally or land against each other.

16. magnetic orientation evidence of plate movement


Answer:

Just as similar age and fossil bands exist on either side of a divergent boundary, studies of the magnetic orientations of rocks reveal bands of similar magnetic orientation that were equidistant and on both sides of divergent boundaries.Tremendously persuasive evidence of plate tectonics is also derived from correlation of studies of the magnetic orientation of the rocks to known changes in Earth's magnetic field as predicted by electromagnetic theory. Paleomagnetic studies and discovery of polar wandering, a magnetic orientation of rocks to the historical location and polarity of the magnetic poles as opposed to the present location and polarity, provided a coherent map of continental movement that fit well with the present distribution of the continents.

Evidence of Continental Drift

Here are some of the evidences supporting the continental drift theory:

Fossil – fossils of Mesosaurus, Cynognathus, Lystrosaurus, and the plant Glossopteris, were distributed equally among the southern continents. Coastline matching – the coastlines of South America and Africa looks like a perfect fit, just like a jigsaw puzzle. Paleomagnetism – it shows the orientation of magnetic minerals, like magnetite. These minerals change their orientation if the magnetic north moves in an event called the geomagnetic reversal. Age, Heat and Magnetic orientation – additional evidences that support the continental drift theory is the age of the sampled materials, their magnetic orientation, and their heat sources. Earthquakes and Volcanoes – the areas near subduction zones, rift, mid-oceanic ridges, collision zones, and transition zones experiences a lot of earthquakes and volcanic eruptions.
