Since death is inevitable, you know that you are going to die someday. How would you like to be remembered by the people in your life?​

Grief is our emotional reaction to the death or loss of someone we love.

Grief has an impact on our bodies, thoughts, emotions, and spirits.

People may notice or demonstrate grief in a variety of ways:

Physical reactions to mourning include changes in diet or sleep, an upset stomach, a tight chest, crying, tense muscles, trouble relaxing, low energy, restlessness, or problems focusing.

Good recollections of the person who died, grief or regrets, or imaginations about how life will be without them are all examples of frequent thoughts.

Sadness, rage, remorse, despair, relief, love, or hope are examples of strong emotions of grief.

Spiritual reactions could include discovering spiritual meaning and connections, doubting religious views, or finding strength in faith.

We call persons who experience these reactions and emotions "crazy."
