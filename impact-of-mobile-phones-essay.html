15. should mobile phones be allowed in schools argumentative essay 4 paragraphs​


Our day-to-day lives are increasingly dependent on our mobile phones. They are used for entertainment, information, and communication. However, educators, parents, and students have begun to disagree regarding the use of mobile phones in schools. While some contend that they ought to be permitted, others are of the assessment that they ought to be restricted. We will examine the arguments in favor of and against allowing mobile phones in schools in this essay.

First, proponents of allowing students to use mobile phones in schools argue that they are useful learning tools. Through the use of their phones, students can access educational resources, conduct topical research, and collaborate with other students. Additionally, mobile phones can be used to access digital textbooks, record lectures, and take notes. Students' academic performance can improve if they are able to stay organized and engaged in their studies as a result.

However, those who oppose the use of mobile phones in schools claim that they cause distractions. Social media, messaging, and gaming are examples of non-educational uses for mobile phones that can hinder learning. Additionally, cell phones can be utilized to cheat during tests, which sabotages the trustworthiness of the schooling system. Additionally, concerns have been raised regarding the detrimental effects of excessive screen time on students' mental and physical health.

Concerns about safety are another reason why mobile phones shouldn't be allowed in schools. Especially during activities like sports and practical classes, mobile phones can act as a distraction that can cause accidents. Additionally, students' mental health and well-being may be seriously impacted by the use of mobile phones for cyberbullying.

The use of mobile phones in schools is, in conclusion, a contentious issue. While those who support them argue that they are necessary for learning, those who oppose them are worried that they could distract students, encourage cheating, and pose safety risks. In the end, each school's unique requirements and circumstances should be taken into account when deciding whether or not to allow mobile phones in the classroom.

19. write a 5- paragraph essay in the Mobile phone(should & should not) be allowed ​


Answer:

Mobile phones have become an essential part of our daily lives. However, there is an ongoing debate on whether or not mobile phones should be allowed in schools. This essay will discuss the pros and cons of allowing mobile phones in schools.

On one hand, allowing mobile phones in schools can be beneficial for students. They can use their phones to access online educational resources, such as videos and articles, that can enhance their learning experience. Additionally, mobile phones can serve as a tool for communication between students and teachers, especially during emergencies or sudden schedule changes.

On the other hand, mobile phones can also be a source of distraction for students. Students may be tempted to use their phones for non-academic purposes, such as browsing social media or playing games, which can negatively impact their academic performance. Furthermore, mobile phones can be a form of cheating during exams, with students using their phones to look up answers or communicate with others.

Another argument against allowing mobile phones in schools is the potential health risks associated with their use. Some studies suggest that prolonged exposure to mobile phone radiation can lead to health problems, such as cancer and neurological disorders. While there is no conclusive evidence to support this claim, it is a concern for many parents and educators.

In conclusion, the decision to allow mobile phones in schools should be carefully considered. While there are potential benefits, such as access to online educational resources and communication with teachers, there are also potential drawbacks, such as distraction and cheating. It is important for schools to have clear policies in place regarding the use of mobile phones in classrooms, and for students to be aware of the responsibilities that come with using their phones in school. Ultimately, the decision should prioritize the safety and academic success of the students.
