26. essay about nutrition 2018


as the times flies by more foods are made but lot of sickness also develop. it is not important that we eat 3 times a day 7 days a week but the right nutrition to become healthy individual. this will help us to think brightly to achieve our goal.

“Ugaliing magtanim, sapat na nutrisyon ay aanihin.” This is the theme of this year’s nutrition month. This catchy phrase intends to promote planting at home, which can help a Filipino family save money and have a healthier lifestyle. But, the major question is, is it really possible?  


With the advent of technology and a faster pace in life, do filipino people have the time to plant their own fruits and vegetables in their backyard? People these days mostly spend their free time clinging to their gadgets, as if it would kill them not to be online. They probably consider it as their rest and relaxation, when in fact, the truth is it only adds up to their stresses. If you would take some time to think and compare, you can see the difference between a family who relies in takeouts and supermarkets, and the ones who take time with their food choices.


Some families in some areas in our country opt to grow their own food in their backyards. This is beneficial not only in the family’s budget, but also for their health. If we try to analyze, home-grown food is definitely free from chemicals and fertilizers and they are fresher. The ones that we buy from commercial establishments have been processed for God knows how long. I know there are organic ones but they are really expensive. Fruits coated in wax to preserve freshness is really common. I kid you not, it’s true. It actually is food-grade wax, but still, you have to wash it off properly and not everyone knows or does that. It is quite funny as what we thought is safe and clean, ends up being more dangerous to our health.


 We have a saying that if there’s a will, there’s a way. Some people may have a lot of reasons like, “I can’t plant”, “I have no time” or “We don’t have any space in our house”. Well, every good thing requires a bit of sacrifice, right? If we want to improve our health, maybe we should try a little harder. Planting a few vegetables in a box wouldn’t hurt. It will surely be beneficial in the long run. My friend, it surely wouldn’t be easy, but it’s not impossible.
