11. A. Summarize the events in the poem Footprints in the Sand in 5 sentences. 1. 2. 3. 4. 5.


Answer:

The poem "Footprints in the Sand" is a spiritual poem that describes a person's journey through life and their relationship with God. The poem begins by describing a dream in which the person sees two sets of footprints in the sand, one belonging to the person and the other belonging to God. The person notices that during the difficult times in their life, there is only one set of footprints, and they wonder why God abandoned them. God then replies that during those times, God was carrying the person and helping them through their struggles. The poem concludes by encouraging the reader to trust in God and have faith that God will always be there for them, even during the most difficult times.

Republic Act 7610

The Philippines has a law protecting children against child abuse. This is the "Special Protection of Children Against Abuse, Exploitation and Discrimination Act." In this law specific penalties are to be imposed for those who commit crimes against children such as child trafficking, sexual and physical abuse are subject to incarceration and fine. What is sad is that even with this law, there is still a growing number of children who are exploited.  

Child Abuse at Home

Recent data indicate that most children who are abused are abused by someone they know at home or in the neighborhood. As much as 60% of physical abuses (beating, etc) happen where they should be safest, their homes. The only way this can be stopped is to ensure that we report the cases--but as seen in the poem--it is really difficult to stand up to abuse, because you also fear for your own safety.
