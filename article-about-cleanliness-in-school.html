7. reason why it's hard to maintain the cleanliness of the restroom in the school​


Answer:

Maintaining the cleanliness of restrooms in schools is essential for maintaining good hygiene and preventing the spread of germs and diseases. However, it can be challenging to keep restrooms clean in a school setting for several reasons.

Firstly, the number of students using the restrooms in schools is often very high, which means that the restrooms are used frequently and become dirty quickly. As a result, it can be difficult for school janitorial staff to keep up with the high volume of cleaning required to maintain cleanliness.

Secondly, many students may not have good hygiene habits and may not clean up after themselves. They may leave paper towels, toilet paper, or other items on the floor or may not flush the toilet after use. This lack of cleanliness can make it challenging for janitorial staff to keep the restrooms clean.

Thirdly, vandalism is another issue that can make it difficult to maintain restroom cleanliness in schools. Some students may intentionally damage restroom facilities, such as breaking toilets or sinks, which can make it challenging for janitorial staff to keep the restrooms clean and functioning properly.

Lastly, limited resources and budget constraints can also contribute to the challenge of maintaining restroom cleanliness in schools. The limited budget may not be enough to hire enough staff or purchase cleaning supplies and equipment needed for maintaining cleanliness.

In conclusion, maintaining the cleanliness of restrooms in schools is essential for the health and safety of students and staff. However, it can be challenging due to the high number of students using the restrooms, poor hygiene habits, vandalism, and limited resources. It is important for schools to address these issues and provide adequate resources to maintain cleanliness in their restrooms.
