3. k-12 new issue essay​


Answer:

The K-12 program in the Philippines has been a topic of debate for several years now. While the program aims to improve the quality of education in the country by lengthening the basic education cycle and aligning it with international standards, it has also faced various challenges and criticisms.

One of the main issues with the K-12 program is the financial burden it places on families, particularly those who are struggling to make ends meet. The additional two years of senior high school means more expenses for tuition, books, and other school fees. This has led to concerns about the accessibility of education, especially for those in low-income households.

Another issue is the readiness of schools and teachers to implement the K-12 program. Many schools lack the necessary resources and infrastructure to provide quality education, and teachers need proper training to effectively teach the new curriculum. This has led to uneven implementation and quality of education across the country.

Furthermore, the K-12 program has been criticized for its focus on producing graduates who are employable, rather than well-rounded individuals who are critical thinkers and problem solvers. The emphasis on technical skills and vocational education has been seen as a move away from the liberal arts and humanities, which are essential for developing a well-informed and engaged citizenry.

Despite these challenges, the K-12 program also has its benefits. The additional two years of senior high school provides students with more time to explore their interests and strengths, and to prepare for college or the workforce. The program also aims to provide students with skills that are relevant to the needs of the 21st century, such as communication, digital literacy, and global awareness.

To address the issues surrounding the K-12 program, the government should provide more funding for education and ensure that schools are properly equipped to implement the new curriculum. Teachers should also be given adequate training and support to effectively deliver the new curriculum. Moreover, the government should ensure that the K-12 program provides a well-rounded education that emphasizes critical thinking, creativity, and citizenship, alongside technical skills and vocational education.

In conclusion, while the K-12 program in the Philippines has faced various challenges and criticisms, it also provides opportunities for students to develop their skills and prepare for college or the workforce. Addressing the issues surrounding the program requires a concerted effort from the government, schools, teachers, and other stakeholders to ensure that every student can access quality education and reach their full potential.

4. Should K to 12 be abolished? 1. issue 2.claim 3.argument 4. evidence ( at least at 3) 5 counter argument 6 rebuttal pa help Po​


Answer:

1. Issue: The K to 12 education system has been a controversial topic in many countries, with some arguing that it is unnecessary and should be abolished.

2. Claim: K to 12 should not be abolished.

3. Argument: The K to 12 education system is important because it provides students with a more comprehensive education, better prepares them for college and the workforce, and promotes a more equitable and inclusive education system. The additional years of schooling in K to 12 allow students to develop critical thinking and problem-solving skills, as well as knowledge in specialized areas such as technical-vocational education. Moreover, the K to 12 system provides students with a smoother transition to college and the workforce, as they are better prepared for the demands and challenges of higher education and the job market. Lastly, the K to 12 system promotes a more equitable and inclusive education system by providing opportunities for all students, regardless of their socio-economic background, to receive a quality education.

4.Evidence:

According to the Department of Education in the Philippines, the K to 12 system has been successful in improving the quality of education, with more students passing the college entrance exams and being employed after graduation.

A study conducted by the World Bank showed that the K to 12 system has led to a more equitable education system, with increased access to education for students in rural and remote areas.

A survey conducted by the Philippine Statistics Authority showed that 71.2% of K to 12 graduates are employed, compared to 59.7% of high school graduates.

5. Counter argument: Some argue that the K to 12 system is unnecessary and places an additional burden on families, as they have to pay for an additional two years of schooling.

6. Rebuttal: While it is true that the K to 12 system may be an added expense for some families, the benefits of the additional years of schooling outweigh the costs. Moreover, the government provides financial assistance and scholarship programs for students who are unable to afford the additional years of schooling. Ultimately, the K to 12 system is an investment in the future of students and the country, as it provides a more comprehensive and equitable education system that better prepares students for success in college and the workforce.
