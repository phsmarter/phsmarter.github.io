16. Compare and Contrast Fliptop battle and Old Balagtasan


OLD BALAGTASAN VS. FLIPTOP BATTLE RAP

Old Balgtasan- Balagtsan is an old form of debate among filipinos, where the two poet exchanges poetic verses. According to researchers, balagtasan consists of three speakers: a narrator, a male, and a woman. The three interact with each other in a sort of a “debate.” Each of the speaker takes turns in sharing and reciting lines in poetic rhyme in which the next speaker will try to respond in a form of rebuttal. Topics are primarily political, including mostly with Filipino heroism in a formal manner. Sometimes the speaker also applies comedy in their poetic lines.

Fliptop Battle Rap- better known as Fliptop, is a Battle Rap league recently established by a group of flipino rappers that aims to upheave and revive the filipino hip-hop in the Philippines.

The Fliptop consists of multiple judges that ranges from five to rarely ten, and three speakers: an arbiter, and two male/female emcees also known as rappers, sometimes it is a battle between a female and a male emcee.

The three interacts with each other in a form of informal debate. The two rappers takes turns in reciting their written bars and verses, mostly about each other's life and their deeds as a rapper. Usually Fliptop lasts for about 3 rounds and is a battle of creativity, in a form of insulting, criticizing, dissing, bragging, joking, rapping and showing off. Mostly, rapper tends to joke about the appearance of their opponents, and blustering words about how and why they are worthy of winning the ongoing battle between them. The winners are usually the ones who pulled off an exceptional extravaganza, and creatively knocked out their opponents using, figures of speech, double meanings, wordplays, rap bars and hilarious jokes. In Fliptop you should convince the crowd and judges by effectively using your own words in order to win. At least in my own opinion as a guy who watched Fliptop Battles a thousand times now.
