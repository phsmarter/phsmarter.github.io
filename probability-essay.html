1. Write a essay focused on probability


Answer:

Probability is the branch of mathematics concerning numerical descriptions of how likely an event is to occur, or how likely it is that a proposition is true. The probability of an event is a number between 0 and 1, where, roughly speaking, 0 indicates impossibility of the event and 1 indicates certainty.[note 1][1][2] The higher the probability of an event, the more likely it is that the event will occur. A simple example is the tossing of a fair (unbiased) coin. Since the coin is fair, the two outcomes ("heads" and "tails") are both equally probable; the probability of "heads" equals the probability of "tails"; and since no other outcomes are possible, the probability of either "heads" or "tails" is 1/2 (which could also be written as 0.5 or 50%).

22. write an essay about application of probability & real life application of statistics. 5 sentences.(KINDLY NEED IT RIGHT NOW).​


Probability theory is a branch of mathematics that deals with the analysis of random phenomena. It is used extensively in a variety of fields such as finance, economics, engineering, and science. One of the most important applications of probability theory is in statistical inference, where probabilities are used to make predictions about a population based on a sample.

Statistics is the science of collecting, analyzing, and interpreting data. It plays a crucial role in many real-life applications such as quality control, medical research, and public policy. For example, in quality control, statistical process control is used to monitor and improve the quality of products by analyzing data from production processes.

In medical research, statistical methods are used to test hypotheses about the effectiveness of new drugs or treatments. This involves collecting data from clinical trials and analyzing it to determine whether there is a statistically significant difference between the treatment and control groups.

In public policy, statistics is used to inform decisions about important issues such as education, healthcare, and environmental policy. For example, statistical analyses of the effectiveness of different educational programs can help policymakers make informed decisions about how to allocate resources.

In conclusion, probability theory and statistics have numerous real-life applications that are essential for decision-making in a wide range of fields. Whether it is predicting the likelihood of a future event or analyzing data to make informed decisions, the applications of these two fields are vast and varied.
