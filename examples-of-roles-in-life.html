13. in a marine life give 2 examples of biotic and abiotic and their roles and survival


Answer:

Biotic:

1. Fish: Fish are an important part of the marine ecosystem, providing food for other animals and helping to balance the food web. They also help control the number of smaller organisms in the water, such as plankton, by consuming them.

2. Seaweed: Seaweed is a keystone species in the marine ecosystem, providing shelter and food for many species. It also helps in nutrient cycling, as it absorbs carbon dioxide and other nutrients from the water and then releases them back into the environment.

Abiotic:

1. Salinity: Salinity is the measure of the amount of salt in the water and is important for the survival of many marine species. It affects the water temperature, nutrient availability, and the ability of organisms to move through the water.

2. Temperature: Temperature can affect the behavior and development of marine species, as many species have narrow temperature ranges in which they can survive. Temperature also affects the solubility of oxygen and other gases in the water, which can be critical for the survival of many species.
