2. Give me an example of personal Obstacle and how to overcome it!


Answer:

1. Self-Doubt

Some people are born with a seemingly endless supply of self-confidence. They believe in themselves and know their worth.

Others fake it until they make it, pretending to be confident to fool themselves and others. Lastly, there are those who doubt themselves every step of the way and make no effort to change it.

The problem is, self-doubt is one of the most common examples of obstacles many can’t overcome. It leads to the inability to make a decision, taking far too long on projects leading to success, and a lot of second-guessing.

Overcome this obstacle by learning to trust your intuition. This also means learning how to love yourself, including both your good qualities and your faults. You are unique and have something only you can offer this world, remember that.

11. What is the relevance of journalism in overcoming obstacles this pandemic?


Answer:In the midst of the COVID-19 pandemic, journalists have the challenging task of gathering and distributing accurate information. Journalists exist as a part of an ecology in which their work influences and is influenced by the environment that surrounds it. Using the framework of disaster communication ecology, this study explores the discursive construction of journalism during the COVID-19 crisis. To understand this process in the field of journalism, we unpacked discourses concerning the coronavirus pandemic collected from interviews with journalists during the pandemic and from the U.S. journalism trade press using the Discourses of Journalism Database. Through discourse analysis, we discovered that during COVID-19 journalists discursively placed themselves in a responsible but vulnerable position within the communication ecology—not solely as a result of the pandemic but also from environmental conditions that long preceded it. Journalists found their reporting difficult during the pandemic and sought to mitigate the forces challenging their work as they sought to reverse the flow of misinformation.

Understand communication style

It is important for you to understand the communication style of the people in your work environment, but sometimes this is not easy. So it takes time to understand someone's communication style. Basically there are 4 communication styles that you should know, which are as follows:

Passive

People with this style usually look ignorant of something. They usually often fail to express feelings or needs.

Aggressive

It's easy enough to see the intent of someone who is communicating aggressively. However, they often issue orders, ask questions harshly and fail to listen to others.

Passive-Aggressive

The passive-aggressive communication style is quite difficult to understand. Because usually people with this communication style appear passive on the surface, but inside they are very active.

Assertive

People with this communication style are criteria for co-workers that you can work well with. Communicating with them will be easier. Because the assertive communication style can express their ideas and feelings well, without sidestepping the interests of others.

Convey the message clearly

This can make the message conveyed well received by your colleagues. If you feel you can't communicate well or are usually in a hurry, try to calm down first. This can be done when listening to what the other person is saying.
