25. direction:infer how gravity affects movements of different objects.read and answer on a separate sheet of paper.​


Answer:

Gravity affects the movement of different objects in different ways.

For example, the force of gravity on an object on Earth is constant and pulls the object toward the center of the planet. This force affects the movement of objects in different ways, depending on their mass, shape, and location.

For objects with a low mass, such as a feather or a piece of paper, gravity has a relatively weak effect on their movement. They are easily affected by other forces, such as wind or air resistance, and might be blown around by these forces instead of being pulled by gravity.

For objects with a larger mass, such as a car or a building, gravity has a much stronger effect on their movement. These objects are harder to move because of their weight, and they are more likely to be pulled by gravity than affected by other forces.

The shape and location of an object also affect how gravity affects its movement. For example, a ball that is tossed into the air will rise and fall in a predictable pattern because of gravity, while a plane can fly because its engines generate enough lift to overcome the force of gravity and stay in the air.

Overall, it can be said that gravity affects the movement of different objects by pulling them toward the center of the planet and influencing the way they move in relation to other objects and forces.


26. om's.assess yourself how well you done the activity by reflecting on the following statement i can now explain how gravity and friction keep things in order and what would happen if there is no gravity and friction i can now discuss how we live and cope with the presence of gravity and friction​


Answer:

Gravity and friction are two fundamental forces that play a crucial role in keeping things in order in the universe. Gravity is the force of attraction between two objects, and it depends on their masses and the distance between them. Friction is the force that opposes motion between two surfaces in contact. Both of these forces are essential for the functioning of many natural and human-made systems.

Without gravity, objects would not be held to the surface of the earth, and everything would float away into space. There would be no planets or stars, and the universe as we know it would not exist. Similarly, without friction, it would be challenging to walk, drive, or even hold objects in our hands, as everything would slide around uncontrollably.

We live and cope with the presence of gravity and friction by adapting to their effects. For example, we use friction to our advantage by wearing shoes with rubber soles, which provide better traction and grip on surfaces. We also use gravity to our advantage by using it to generate energy through hydroelectric power or to keep our bones healthy through weight-bearing exercises.

In conclusion, understanding the role of gravity and friction is crucial for explaining many natural phenomena and designing systems that work in harmony with these forces. Coping with their presence requires us to adapt to their effects and use them to our advantage.
