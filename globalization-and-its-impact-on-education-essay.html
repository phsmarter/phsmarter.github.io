23. impact of foreign education on children's succeed(Essay)​


Answer:

Foreign education can have a significant impact on a child's success in several ways. Firstly, children who receive a foreign education often develop a broader perspective and understanding of different cultures, languages, and ways of thinking. This exposure can help them develop a more open-minded and tolerant attitude towards others, which is beneficial in today's globalized world.

Secondly, foreign education can provide access to a higher quality of education and specialized fields of study, which may not be available in the child's home country. This exposure can enable children to develop unique skills and knowledge, making them more competitive in the global job market.

However, foreign education can also present some challenges for children, such as language barriers, cultural adjustment issues, and homesickness. These challenges can be significant, and they may affect a child's academic performance and overall well-being.

Overall, the impact of foreign education on a child's success depends on several factors, including the quality of education, the child's willingness to adapt, and the support provided by the child's family and school. When given the right support and resources, foreign education can provide children with valuable skills and experiences that can help them succeed in their future endeavors.

30. the effects of global pandemic to education essay with a minimum of three paragraphs ​


GLOBAL PANDEMIC

School closures due to COVID-19 have brought significant disruptions to education. Emerging evidence from some of the region’s highest-income countries indicate that the pandemic is giving rise to learning losses and increases in inequality.

The current coronavirus (COVID-19) pandemic is having a profound impact, not only on people’s health, but also on how they learn, work and live. The COVID-19 pandemic has created the largest disruption of education systems in human history, affecting nearly 1.6 billion learners in more than 200 countries. Closures of schools, institutions and other learning spaces have impacted more than 94% of the world's student population. The pandemic and school closures not only jeopardized children’s health and safety with domestic violence and child labor increasing, but also impacted student learning substantially.

The online learning, distance and continuing education have become a panacea for this unprecedented global pandemic, despite the challenges posed to both educators and the learners. Transitioning from traditional face-to-face learning to online learning can be an entirely different experience for the learners and the educators, which they must adapt to with little or no other alternatives available. The education system and the educators have adopted “Education in Emergency” through various online platforms and are compelled to adopt a system that they are not prepared for.

The lesson learnt from the COVID-19 pandemic is that teachers and students/learners should be oriented on use of different online educational tools. After the COVID-19 pandemic when the normal classes resume, teachers and learners should be encouraged to continue using such online tools to enhance teaching and learning.
