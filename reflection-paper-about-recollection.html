Ang recollection ay misang ginagawa sa paggunita ng mga ginawa sa nakalipas at sa layuning pagsisihan o maituwid ang mga bagay na ito.  Dito ay inaalala natin ang mga ginawa nating mali o kung may tao tayong nagawan ng masama. Sa pamamagitan nito ay nagkakaroon tayo ng pagkakataong huminging tawad sa Diyos at sa mga taong ating naagrabyado.

Explanation:

"Recollections of My Children" is a poem written by Yamagami-no Okura, a Japanese poet from the Asuka period (538-710). The poem expresses the deep love and affection a parent has for their children, and the sadness that comes with the realization that they will eventually grow up and leave the family home.

In the poem, Yamagami-no Okura reflects on the past when his children were young, and he remembers the happy memories they shared together. He reminisces about the times when they played and laughed, and how they would cuddle up to him when they were scared or tired. However, as his children grew up, they became more independent, and he realized that they would eventually leave him and start their own lives.

The poet expresses his deep emotions of love and pride for his children, while also acknowledging the inevitability of change and the fleeting nature of life. He ultimately comes to accept that his children will grow and change, and that he must cherish the time they have together while they are still young.

Overall, the poem is a beautiful tribute to the joys and challenges of parenthood, and it highlights the deep bond that exists between a parent and their children.
