History as a discipline is heavily reliant on subjective knowledge, but that does not mean historians ignore objective knowledge. A historian's lens is used to interpret history, but primary sources can always be used to gain a better understanding of the past. Historian is obligated to present their points of view with their understanding, but they do not follow the rule of facts.

As a result, the answer given as true is correct.

True 2. We make sense of the past through historical interpretation.

Historical interpretations are based on historical knowledge; thus, a sense of the past can be maintained through one's understanding. It is largely dependent on the reader's perception of these events, and thus historical interpretations make sense of the past to some extent.

As a result, the answer given as true is correct.

True 3. Multiperspectivity is a quality of historical writing attributed to a variety of lenses that may be used to view the past.

Several historians present their perspectives on historical events; for example, different historians interpret Aurangzeb's role in India differently. Some regard him as an unjust ruler, while others regard him as an effective administrator. In some cases, it is unavoidable for the Historian to express their bias toward a particular historical event; thus, it is viewed through different lenses by different readers.

As a result, the answer given as true is correct.
