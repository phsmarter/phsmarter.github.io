"The Yellow Shawl" is a feminist approach because it explores the experiences and struggles of women in a patriarchal society. The story delves into the character's inner thoughts and feelings, highlighting their oppression and the limitations imposed on them by societal norms and expectations. The protagonist's desire for freedom and self-expression is a central theme, and her struggle against the constraints of her society can be seen as a feminist act. The story also touches on the themes of motherhood, domesticity, and the conflict between personal desires and societal expectations, all of which are important issues in feminist discourse. Overall, "The Yellow Shawl" can be viewed as a feminist work because it highlights the experiences and struggles of women and challenges patriarchal norms and values.

Initially a commercial failure, it came to be considered one of the most important American operas of the twentieth century and an American cultural classic.
