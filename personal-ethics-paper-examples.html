19. Answer the following in 5 sentences: Differentiate between Personal Ethics and Professional Ethics​


Personal ethics refer to an individual's personal beliefs, values, and principles that guide their behavior and decision-making in their personal life. Personal ethics are influenced by an individual's upbringing, culture, religion, and personal experiences. Professional ethics, on the other hand, are a set of moral principles and values that guide behavior and decision-making in a professional context. Professional ethics are typically defined by industry standards, codes of conduct, and legal requirements. While personal ethics are subjective and vary from person to person, professional ethics are objective and apply to all members of a particular profession. Personal ethics may sometimes conflict with professional ethics, and it is important for individuals to manage these conflicts to avoid ethical dilemmas.

22. 1. What is the impact of ethics in a person's values and attitude? 2. Give example or scenario that may apply in each ethical principle. 3. Differentiate the forms of ethical theory.​


Answer:

1. Ethics play a significant role in shaping a person's values and attitude. Ethics provide a framework for determining right and wrong behavior, which in turn influences a person's behavior and decision-making. When individuals adopt ethical principles as part of their values, they tend to exhibit more ethical behavior in their personal and professional lives, leading to a positive impact on society.

2. Examples of ethical principles and their application in various scenarios include:

a. Utilitarianism: In a hospital, a doctor has to decide which of two patients, who are both in critical condition, to save first. Utilitarianism suggests that the doctor should save the patient who can be saved with the greatest ease, even if it means sacrificing the other patient. This decision is based on the principle of maximizing overall happiness or minimizing overall harm.

b. Deontology: An employee at a company is faced with a dilemma where they can either be honest with a client about the company's mistakes or lie to protect the company's reputation. Deontology suggests that the employee should tell the truth and not lie, as it is their moral duty to be truthful.

c. Virtue Ethics: A police officer is offered a bribe to let a criminal go free. Virtue ethics suggest that the police officer should act with honesty and integrity, upholding the virtues of justice and fairness. They should refuse the bribe and uphold the law.

d. Care Ethics: A nurse has to make a difficult decision regarding the treatment of a patient who is nearing the end of their life. Care ethics suggests that the nurse should prioritize the patient's comfort and quality of life, ensuring that they are treated with dignity and compassion.

3. There are three main forms of ethical theory:

a. Consequentialism: This theory holds that the morality of an action is determined by its consequences. Utilitarianism is a form of consequentialism.

b. Deontological Ethics: This theory suggests that the morality of an action is determined by its adherence to a set of moral rules or duties. Immanuel Kant's ethics is an example of deontological ethics.

c. Virtue Ethics: This theory suggests that the morality of an action is determined by the character of the person who performs it. Virtue ethics emphasizes the importance of developing virtues, such as honesty, compassion, and fairness, to guide moral decision-making.

Answer:

1. Ethics has a significant impact on a person's values and attitude. Ethics is concerned with determining what is right or wrong, good or bad, just or unjust, and moral or immoral. It provides a framework for individuals to make decisions and take actions that align with their values and beliefs. Ethics can shape a person's character and behavior, influencing their actions and decisions in various personal and professional situations.

2. Examples or scenarios for each ethical principle are:

a. Autonomy - A patient has the right to refuse medical treatment even if it may be in their best interest, and the physician should respect their decision.

b. Beneficence - A physician provides the best possible treatment to a patient, taking into account the patient's preferences and circumstances, to maximize the patient's benefits and minimize harm.

c. Non-maleficence - A researcher ensures that the participants in a study are not harmed physically or psychologically during the course of the study.

d. Justice - A school administrator ensures that all students have access to the same educational opportunities regardless of their socioeconomic status.

e. Fidelity - A lawyer keeps client information confidential and acts in the best interest of their client.

3. There are different forms of ethical theory, including:

a. Deontological ethics - This theory is based on rules and duties. It emphasizes the importance of following moral rules and principles, regardless of the consequences. In deontological ethics, the rightness or wrongness of an action is determined by whether or not it follows a moral rule or principle.

b. Consequentialist ethics - This theory is based on the consequences of an action. It emphasizes the importance of maximizing the positive consequences and minimizing the negative consequences of an action. In consequentialist ethics, the rightness or wrongness of an action is determined by its outcome or consequences.

c. Virtue ethics - This theory is based on character and virtues. It emphasizes the importance of developing good habits and character traits, such as honesty, courage, and compassion, to guide one's actions and decisions. In virtue ethics, the rightness or wrongness of an action is determined by whether it reflects the virtues or character traits of the person performing the action.
