26. Task 1: Essay (100 Words each Number) 1. the different hazards caused by geological processes (earthquakes, volcanic eruptions, and landslides) 2. describe the various hazards that may happen in the event of earthquakes, volcanic eruptions, and landslides


1. Geological processes such as earthquakes, volcanic eruptions, and landslides can cause a range of hazards. Earthquakes can cause ground shaking, ground rupture, and liquefaction, which can damage buildings, roads, and other structures. Volcanic eruptions can result in lava flows, ashfall, pyroclastic flows, and lahars, which can destroy homes and buildings, and disrupt air and sea travel. Landslides can cause rockfalls, debris flows, and earth flows, which can damage homes, roads, and other infrastructure. These hazards can result in loss of life, economic losses, and long-term environmental damage.

2. In the event of an earthquake, hazards can include ground shaking, which can damage buildings and other infrastructure, and trigger landslides. In volcanic eruptions, hazards can include lava flows, ashfall, pyroclastic flows, and lahars, which can bury buildings and destroy infrastructure, and pose risks to air and sea travel. Landslides can occur in response to earthquakes or heavy rain, resulting in rockfalls, debris flows, and earth flows that can destroy homes and infrastructure. These hazards can cause loss of life, economic losses, and long-term environmental damage, highlighting the importance of preparedness and disaster risk reduction measures.

Answer:

1.

Geological processes such as earthquakes, volcanic eruptions, and landslides pose various hazards to human life and infrastructure. Earthquakes can cause ground shaking, ground rupture, liquefaction, and tsunamis, resulting in structural damage, injuries, and fatalities. Volcanic eruptions can cause lava flows, ashfall, pyroclastic flows, and volcanic gases, leading to property damage, crop failure, and respiratory problems. Landslides can be triggered by heavy rain, earthquakes, or volcanic eruptions, causing soil erosion, property damage, and fatalities. They can also result in secondary hazards such as flooding and damming of rivers. These hazards can have devastating impacts on communities and require effective mitigation measures to minimize their risks.

2.

Earthquakes can cause a wide range of hazards, including ground shaking, ground rupture, liquefaction, landslides, and tsunamis. Ground shaking can damage buildings, bridges, and other infrastructure, leading to injuries and fatalities. Ground rupture can cause fissures or cracks in the ground, disrupting roads and utilities. Liquefaction can cause soil to lose its strength and stability, resulting in the sinking or settling of buildings and structures. Volcanic eruptions can produce various hazards, such as lava flows, ashfall, pyroclastic flows, and volcanic gases. These can damage buildings, disrupt transportation, and impact air quality, posing health risks to people and animals. Landslides can cause property damage, destroy infrastructure, and block roads, rivers, and other waterways, leading to flooding and other secondary hazards.
