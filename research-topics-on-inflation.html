1. Theoretical framework of inflation in research


Answer:

Inflation is a key economic indicator that measures the rate at which the general level of prices for goods and services is rising over time. Inflation has significant impacts on various economic variables, including interest rates, employment rates, purchasing power, and the overall health of an economy. Therefore, understanding the theoretical framework of inflation is essential for conducting research on this topic.

The theoretical framework of inflation is based on the quantity theory of money. According to this theory, the general level of prices is directly proportional to the amount of money in circulation in an economy. This means that when the supply of money in an economy increases, prices rise, and when the supply of money decreases, prices fall.

Inflation is also affected by various factors such as demand-pull inflation and cost-push inflation. Demand-pull inflation occurs when the demand for goods and services in an economy exceeds the supply, leading to an increase in prices. Cost-push inflation, on the other hand, occurs when the cost of production increases, leading to an increase in the prices of goods and services.

Additionally, inflation can be measured using various indicators such as the consumer price index (CPI) and the producer price index (PPI). The CPI measures the average price of a basket of goods and services consumed by households, while the PPI measures the average price of goods and services sold by producers.

Conclusion, the theoretical framework of inflation is based on the quantity theory of money and is affected by various factors such as demand-pull inflation and cost-push inflation. Measuring inflation is important in understanding the health of an economy, and various indicators such as CPI and PPI are used for this purpose. Understanding this theoretical framework is crucial for conducting research on inflation and its impact on various economic variables.

Answer:

The theoretical framework of inflation in research provides a conceptual framework for understanding inflation and its impact on the economy. Inflation refers to the general increase in prices of goods and services over time, leading to a decline in purchasing power. Inflation is a complex phenomenon affected by several factors, including monetary policy, fiscal policy, supply and demand, and external factors such as international trade.

One of the key theories that explain inflation is the Quantity Theory of Money. According to this theory, the level of prices in an economy is directly proportional to the amount of money in circulation. In other words, if the money supply increases faster than the production of goods and services, prices will rise, leading to inflation. Conversely, if the production of goods and services increases faster than the money supply, prices will fall, leading to deflation.

Another theory that explains inflation is the Phillips Curve. The Phillips Curve posits that there is an inverse relationship between unemployment and inflation. When unemployment is high, there is less demand for goods and services, leading to lower prices and inflation. Conversely, when unemployment is low, there is more demand for goods and services, leading to higher prices and inflation.

The rational expectations theory is another framework that explains inflation. According to this theory, people's expectations of future inflation affect their behavior and actions, such as their saving and spending patterns. If people expect inflation to rise, they may adjust their behavior by reducing their spending and increasing their saving, leading to a decrease in demand and lower inflation.

In conclusion, the theoretical framework of inflation provides a useful guide for understanding the causes and effects of inflation. It helps researchers to identify the key factors that drive inflation and to develop policies to manage inflation and its impact on the economy.

2. Theoretical framework of inflation in research?


Answer:

The theoretical framework for researching inflation typically falls within the realm of economics, in particular the Keynesian, classical and post-Keynesian theories.

Keynesian theory suggests that prices are determined by aggregate demand and argues that the primary impact of inflation is cost-push. This is because when the cost of inputs rises, businesses pass these on to consumers in the form of higher prices.

Classical economic theory takes a different stance, arguing that prices are determined by the relative supplies of and demands for goods. Classical theory suggests that inflation is a result of too much money chasing too few goods and services.

Finally, post-Keynesian theory suggests that money supply (for example quantitative easing) can impact long-term inflation, possibly through indirect channels such as changes in the labor market. Post-Keynesian economists argue that inflation is caused by unbalanced money supply and credit expansion in the economy.

PHILIPPINES FOOD INFLATION

INPUT

One of the primary sources of inflation in the Philippines has been rising food prices, including the recent surge in sugar prices. Food inflation peaked at 7.1% in July before falling to 6.5% in August.

Sugar, confectionery, and desserts (30.2%); corn (26.2%); and oils and fats are the leading contributors to increasing food inflation among food categories (20.1%). In addition, meat and fish costs have increased for some time and are already adding to our food inflation figures.

Consequently, it raises income inequality by widening the gap between high and low-income groups.

PROCESS

When domestic institutional constraints, the international agricultural trade environment, and weather and climate circumstances impede the utilization of current high-yield farming practices, rising food prices can increase poverty over the long run.

High inflation exacerbates inequality and poverty because it significantly impacts the disposable income and savings of low and middle-income households more than those of wealthy families. Moreover, by increasing inflation, residents that have just left poverty may be forced back into it.

OUTPUT

Food inflation causes a food shortage. Food scarcity reduces the poor's access to high-quality food, resulting in malnutrition among the poor. Undernourishment increases by 0.5% for each percentage point of food inflation.

Malnutrition causes poverty by lowering the economic capacity of the population, and poverty exacerbates malnutrition by increasing the likelihood of food insecurity.

11. Ano ang pagkakaiba ng headline inflation sa core inflation​

What Is Headline Inflation?

Headline inflation is the raw inflation figure reported through the Consumer Price Index (CPI) that is released monthly by the Bureau of Labor Statistics. The CPI calculates the cost to purchase a fixed basket of goods, as a way of determining how much inflation is occurring in the broad economy. The CPI uses a base year and indexes the current year's prices according to the base year'score inflation

Core inflation is the change in the costs of goods and services but does not include those from the food and energy sectors. This measure of inflation excludes these items because their prices are much more volatile.

26. Ano ang mga dahilan ng pagkakaroon ng inflation? a. Cost push inflation b. Core inflation c. Headline inflation d. Demand pull inflation e. Inflation rate


Explanation:

Ang mga dahilan ng pagkakaroon ng inflation ay ang mga sumusunod:

a. Cost push inflation - Ito ay nagaganap kapag tumaas ang mga gastos sa produksyon tulad ng mga raw materials at labor costs, na nagdudulot ng pagtaas ng presyo ng mga produkto at serbisyo.

b. Core inflation - Ito ay tumutukoy sa pagtaas ng presyo ng mga bilihin at serbisyo na hindi kasama ang volatile components tulad ng pagkain at enerhiya. Ito ay nagsisilbing indikasyon ng pangkalahatang pagtaas ng presyo.

c. Headline inflation - Ito ay tumutukoy sa pangkalahatang pagtaas ng presyo ng mga bilihin at serbisyo, kabilang ang mga volatile components tulad ng pagkain at enerhiya.

d. Demand pull inflation - Ito ay nagaganap kapag tumataas ang demand sa mga produkto at serbisyo, na nagdudulot ng pagtaas ng presyo. Ito ay kadalasang nagaganap sa panahon ng mataas na ekonomikong aktibidad at low unemployment rates.

e. Inflation rate - Ito ay tumutukoy sa rate ng pagbabago ng pangkalahatang presyo ng mga bilihin at serbisyo sa loob ng isang panahon, karaniwang binabatay sa consumer price index (CPI) o producer price index (PPI).

28. Make a research introduction to this title "The impact of the inflation rate to the income of the small business owners."​


Answer:

Introduction:

Inflation is a persistent rise in the prices of goods and services in an economy, leading to a decrease in the purchasing power of money. It is a complex economic phenomenon that affects different sectors of the economy in various ways. Small business owners are among the groups most vulnerable to the impact of inflation, as they often operate on a tight budget and have limited resources to cushion against rising costs. The purpose of this research study is to explore the impact of inflation on the income of small business owners and identify strategies that can help mitigate its negative effects.

Small businesses play a significant role in the economy, as they create jobs, foster innovation, and contribute to economic growth. However, they often face numerous challenges in their daily operations, including financial constraints, market competition, and changing economic conditions. Inflation is one of the key economic conditions that small business owners have to contend with, as it affects the cost of raw materials, labor, and other business inputs, and ultimately impacts the profitability and sustainability of their enterprises.

The objective of this research study is to provide insights into how inflation affects the income of small business owners, and to identify the coping strategies that they use to manage its impact. The study will use a mixed-methods approach, combining quantitative analysis of secondary data on inflation rates and small business income, and qualitative interviews with small business owners in selected industries. The research findings are expected to inform policy and practice by highlighting the need for targeted support to small businesses facing inflationary pressures, and by identifying best practices for managing inflation in small business operations.

In summary, this research study aims to contribute to the understanding of the impact of inflation on the income of small business owners and to identify strategies that can help mitigate its negative effects. By providing insights into the challenges faced by small businesses and the coping mechanisms they use, this study has the potential to inform policy and practice and promote the sustainability and growth of small businesses in the face of economic uncertainties.
