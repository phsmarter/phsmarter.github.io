2. Compose a three-paragraph descriptive essay about your Family.​


My family is the most important part of my life. We are a close-knit family, consisting of my parents, my two siblings, and myself. Our family is known for our strong bond, unwavering support, and unconditional love for one another.

Growing up, my family was always there for me, through the good times and the bad. We would often spend our weekends bonding over our shared love of sports, playing board games, and enjoying each other's company. My parents always made sure that we had everything we needed, and they never hesitated to put our needs before their own.

One thing that I admire most about my family is their resilience. We have been through some tough times, but we have always come out stronger on the other side. We support each other through our successes and failures, and we are always there to pick each other up when we fall.

In summary, my family is the backbone of my life. They have shaped me into the person I am today, and I am forever grateful for their love and support. They are my biggest fans, my closest confidants, and my source of strength.

25. Explain what is Sequence Informational Text and Description Informational Text. Write an example of Sequence Informational Text(Write the things you do before your online Class) and Description Informational Text( Ex. In a paragraph, tell something about your family. Describe how you work and help each other or any topic of your choice


Answer:

Sequence is a form of writing that is used if the author wishes to inform readers about certain topics by presenting this information by listing events or steps in a sequence or in presenting information in chronological order using time. Words that signal this type of text structure are first, next, before, and after.

Example: Eating cereal is easy.  First, get out your materials.  Next, pour your cereal in the bowl, add milk, and enjoy.

A descriptive text usually focuses on describing a single location, object, event, person, or place. It endeavors to engage all five of the reader's senses to evoke the sights, sounds, smells, tastes, and feel of the text's subject.

Example: When you walk into my bedroom there is a window facing you.  To the right of that is a dresser and television and on the other side of the window is my bed.
