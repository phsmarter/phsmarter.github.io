It is the shared concept, idea, or view between two or more people. Intersubjectivity shows that even though individuals are unique and have their unique experiences, they can still share or agree on a subject.

Let us take a look at the next question for an example.

How intersubjectivity present in respect?

Since there are more than thousands of different cultures in the world, the definition and views surrounding respect may differ from one person to another.

A Filipino child may be expected to look straight into his parents' eyes as a way to show respect and sincerity while a Japanese child is taught that looking straight into the eyes of an elder is disrespectful and a show of scorn. These two acts are opposites but are considered acceptable in their own cultures.

If we take away the act, we are left with the concept of respect. In both cultures, they do agree that respect is due to elders and parents. So while they both have a different way of doing things, they still have something in agreement. This is the same for many other things in the world.

Another way of seeing intersubjectivity in relation to respect is that by keeping an open mind and accepting that people will always be different from one another due to their own experiences and learned behavior, people can still get along by agreeing to respect one another and agreeing to disagree on certain things.
