Answer:

Here are the answers to the questions you provided:

Which is not true about critical thinking?

а. It carefully weighs any idea.

b. It gives evaluation and judgements.

Answer: D. None of the above. Both statements are true about critical thinking. Critical thinking involves carefully weighing and evaluating ideas, and making judgements based on evidence and reasoning.

What is the difference between a reaction and critique paper?

a. Critique paper is based on facts while Reaction paper is based on opinions.

b. Critique paper presents negative judgements while Reaction paper presents positive judgements.

c. Critique paper gives judgements based on criteria while reaction paper gives judgements based on emotions, feelings, and experiences.

d. Critique paper is subjective while Reaction paper is objective.

Answer: C. Critique paper gives judgements based on criteria while reaction paper gives judgements based on emotions, feelings, and experiences. A critique paper is a formal evaluation of a work, in which the writer makes judgements about the work based on specific criteria. A reaction paper, on the other hand, is a personal response to a work, in which the writer shares their emotions, feelings, and experiences in relation to the work.

Which is true about the roles of facts and opinions in critical writing?

a. Facts and opinions have to work together in order to present a convincing evaluation of an idea.

b. Facts must be supported with relevant opinions.

C. Evaluation and judgement are factual statements that needs to be explained.

d. Our opinions should be supported by irrelevant factual details.

Answer: A. Facts and opinions have to work together in order to present a convincing evaluation of an idea. In critical writing, it is important to consider both facts and opinions in order to present a well-rounded and persuasive evaluation of an idea. Facts provide the foundation for an argument, while opinions help to support and explain the reasoning behind the argument.

Which of the following describe a good critique?

a Accurate

b. Balanced

c. Evaluative

d. All of these

Answer: D. All of these. A good critique should be accurate, balanced, and evaluative. It should present a thorough and fair evaluation of the work being critiqued, based on clear and well-defined criteria.

What is contained in the introduction of your critique and reaction paper?

a. background information

c. final thoughts of critic

b. Restatement of the overall judgement

d. supporting details

Answer: A. background information. The introduction of a critique or reaction paper typically includes background information about the work being critiqued or reacted to, including the title, author, and any relevant context. This helps to provide a foundation for the rest of the paper and helps the reader to understand the focus of the critique or reaction.
