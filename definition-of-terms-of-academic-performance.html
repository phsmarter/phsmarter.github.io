Answer:

G-11 learners have a preference and motivation in choosing a strand in SHS, which has implications for their academic performance. Generally, students tend to prefer strands that align with their interests, strengths, and career goals. For instance, students who are interested in science may choose the Science, Technology, Engineering, and Mathematics (STEM) strand, while those who are more creative may opt for the Arts and Design strand.

However, the implications of a student’s strand choice on their academic performance can be significant. Students who opt for a strand that aligns with their interests and strengths tend to perform better in their classes, as they are more motivated to learn and apply their knowledge. On the other hand, students who select a strand that does not suit their interests may struggle to keep up with the curriculum and may not perform as well as those who have chosen more relevant strands.

Overall, G-11 learners should carefully consider their preferences and motivations when choosing a strand in SHS, as this can have a major impact on their academic performance.
