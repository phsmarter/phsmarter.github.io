1. differences of round character and flat character​


Answer:

Round character is a significant player who is often the star of the story. This type of character encounters conflict and is changed by it.

Flat characters are two-dimensional in that they are relatively uncomplicated and do not change throughout the course of a work.

Flat characters are two-dimensional in that they are relatively uncomplicated and do not change throughout the course of a work. By contrast, round characters are complex and undergo development, sometimes sufficiently to surprise the reader.

13. settings Characters protagonist and antagonist round and flat characters, description characters, the case book of Sherlock Holmes​


Answer:

"The Casebook of Sherlock Holmes" is a collection of twelve short stories featuring the famous detective Sherlock Holmes and his assistant Dr. John Watson, written by Sir Arthur Conan Doyle.

Characters:

- Protagonist: Sherlock Holmes, a brilliant detective known for his deductive reasoning and keen observation skills. He is often aloof and detached from emotions, and is obsessed with solving complex cases. He is described as having sharp features, piercing eyes, and a hawk-like nose.

- Antagonist: In each story, there is a different antagonist or culprit who is causing trouble or committing a crime. These characters are typically rounded, with their own motivations and backstories that contribute to their actions in the story.

- Round characters: Some of the characters in the stories are rounded, meaning that they have complex personalities and motivations that are explored in depth. Examples include the villains in "The Adventure of the Illustrious Client" and "The Adventure of the Three Garridebs."

- Flat characters: Other characters are relatively flat, meaning that they are not fully fleshed out and exist mainly to serve a specific purpose in the story. Examples include some of the witnesses and minor characters that appear in the stories.

Description of Characters:

- Sherlock Holmes: As mentioned above, Holmes is described as having sharp features, piercing eyes, and a hawk-like nose. He is often depicted wearing a deerstalker cap and smoking a pipe. He is also known for his exceptional memory and attention to detail.

- Dr. John Watson: Watson is Holmes' loyal friend and assistant, and often serves as the narrator of the stories. He is described as being a military veteran and a skilled doctor. He is also depicted as being more emotionally expressive and empathetic than Holmes.

- Antagonists: The antagonists in the stories are described in varying degrees of detail, but they are all portrayed as being cunning, manipulative, and dangerous. Some are motivated by greed, while others are seeking revenge or trying to cover up a crime. Their physical appearances are often used to highlight their sinister nature, with descriptions of sharp features, dark eyes, and cruel smiles.
