contemporary art allows students to broaden their understanding of themselves, their community, and the world. It helps them become critical thinkers as they consider the influences that led to the creation of each work of art, such as sociopolitical climate, the artist's training, and cultural influence.

Working in a wide range of mediums, contemporary artists often reflect and comment on modern-day society. ... Contemporary artists working within the postmodern movement reject the concept of mainstream art and embrace the notion of "artistic pluralism," the acceptance of a variety of artistic intentions and styles

Contemporary Art is its aesthetic value. This means that the art possesses some capacity to elicit a sense of pleasure in the viewer. Although, what is considered pleasing to the eye may vary depending on the viewer, given the wide variety of mediums and methods for this style of art there is likely to be some form that will appeal to each person.

Given its aesthetic value, contemporary artwork can also be used to decorate many different venues. Even outdoor sculpture parks can contain art and make it easily accessible for anyone to view, while it livens up the city landscape. 10. choose a visual artwork that you have (on hand) .Write a one paragraph essay answering this question to what extent can art (referring to your visual artwork) help in the information of your values and a society culture?​


Answer:

One example of a visual artwork that can inform our values and understanding of a society's culture is the painting "The Starry Night" by Vincent van Gogh. This post-impressionist painting, created in 1889, is widely regarded as one of van Gogh's most famous works and is a great example of how art can reflect the values and cultural context of a society.

In "The Starry Night," van Gogh employs a vibrant and bold use of color, brushwork, and symbolism to depict a starry night sky over a small town. The swirling stars, cypress trees, and the night sky convey a sense of movement and dynamism, while the structured houses and church tower suggest stability and order. The painting reflects van Gogh's emotional state at the time, as well as the larger cultural movements of his time, such as the rise of industrialization and the growth of urban centers. By studying "The Starry Night," we can gain a deeper understanding of the cultural and emotional climate of the time, as well as van Gogh's individual values and perspectives.
