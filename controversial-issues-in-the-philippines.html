30. Why , the execution of gomburza remains as one of the most controversial issues deeply embedded in philippine history?


Answer:

Gomburza, alternatively stylized as GOMBURZA or GomBurZa, refers to three Filipino Catholic priests, Mariano Gomez, José Burgos, and Jacinto Zamora, who were executed by garrote in 17 February 1872 in Bagumbayan, Philippines by Spanish colonial authorities on charges of subversion arising from the 1872 Cavite mutiny. The name is a portmanteau of the priests' surnames.

Answer:

The execution of Gomburza remains as one of the most controversial issues deeply embedded in Philippine history.

Their deaths were facilitated in a public execution at Bagumbayan (Luzon) using a garrote due to false accusations charged against them by Spanish authorities.

The execution has since been labeled the Terror of 1872, and is recognized as a pivotal event contributing to the later Philippine Revolution
