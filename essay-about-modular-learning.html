A Primer on Course Design

Using a Modular Approach to Course Design

Using a Modular Approach to Course Design

This section of “A Primer on Course Design” introduces a fundamental concept: modular course design or, put simply, building courses out of smaller, discrete units known as modules.

Modules Defined

modules_overview

A module can be defined as a unit, chapter, topic, or segment of instruction. It is a standard unit or instructional section of your course that is a “self-contained” chunk of instruction. A week is a common module length, but it can be shorter or longer depending upon content and your teaching style.

A module structure is especially important in online learning environments, as it provides an aid in the presentation and application of the online teaching and learning process. When students are aware of the structure of the course, they spend less time guessing about what is expected of them and more time focusing on the content and activities.

KEY: Essentially, the module structure, as portrayed in the folder structure in your Blackboard course site design and in your schedule, provides students with a “road map” and can help keep them on track.

The structure, including the elements and how they are sequenced, is one you define. The structure of each individual module may be unique, or it may be a consistent pattern that is repeated throughout the course, in which case, the structure of the first module you create can serve as a pattern or template for other modules in your course. Keep in mind a possible module for your own course as we consider what could be a part of a module.

25. expository essay about online digital distance learning is better than online modular distance learning ​


Online digital distance learning refers to a type of education in which students learn through digital platforms and materials, such as online lectures and videos, and can access course content at any time. Online modular distance learning, on the other hand, refers to a type of education in which students work through self-contained modules or units at their own pace, rather than following a set schedule.

There are several reasons why online digital distance learning may be better than online modular distance learning. One reason is that online digital distance learning is often more interactive and engaging. With online lectures and videos, students can see and hear the instructor and have the opportunity to ask questions and participate in discussions. This can help to create a sense of community and facilitate learning.

Another reason is that online digital distance learning may be more efficient. Rather than working through modules on their own, students can progress through the course material at their own pace with the guidance of the instructor. This can help to ensure that they are learning the most important information and not getting bogged down in unnecessary details.

Finally, online digital distance learning may be more flexible than online modular distance learning. With a set schedule and live lectures, students can easily fit their education into their busy lives and schedule their coursework around their other commitments.

Overall, while both online digital distance learning and online modular distance learning have their own strengths and benefits, online digital distance learning may be the better option for students who value interactivity, efficiency, and flexibility in their education.
