1. What causes temporary hardness in water? permanent hardness? How can they be removed?


Answer:

Temporary hardness is mainly caused by the carbonates and bicarbonates of calcium and magnesium. It can be easily removed by boiling of water. Permanent hardness is due to presence of sulfates and chlorides of calcium and magnesium in water.

Answer:

Temporary hardness is a type of water hardness caused by the presence of dissolved bicarbonate minerals (calcium bicarbonate and magnesium bicarbonate). Boiling promotes the formation of carbonate from the bicarbonate and precipitates calcium carbonate out of solution, leaving water that is softer upon cooling.

Permanent hardness is hardness (mineral content) that cannot be removed by boiling. When this is the case, it is usually caused by the presence of calcium sulfate and/or magnesium sulfates in the water, which do not precipitate out as the temperature increases.
