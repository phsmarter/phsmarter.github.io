Analogy about Family

A family is analogous to a pile of stones. If one is removed, the entire structure may collapse. My father resembles a tree because he is as strong as a tree. My mother is like a plant because it can survive the longest droughts or the most violent storms and still bloom magnificent flowers. Our mothers are as strong and beautiful as plants. My brother is like a monkey because he constantly imitates me. My older brother is like a lion in that he is always sleeping, but he has complete control over the house. While you are like a star that shines brightly in the darkest moments of our lives, I am like a star that shines brightly in the darkest moments of our lives.

What is Analogy?

An analogy is something that shows how two things are similar, but the ultimate goal is to make a point about this comparison.

An analogy's purpose is not only to demonstrate but also to explain. As a result, an analogy is more complex than a simile or metaphor, which is only shown without explaining.
