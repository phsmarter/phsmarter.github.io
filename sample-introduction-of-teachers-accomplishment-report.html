23. What is the meaning of Introduction, Body and Conclusion and sample​


Answer:

Introduction, body, and conclusion are the three main parts of an essay or any piece of academic writing.

Introduction: The introduction is the first part of an essay or academic paper that provides background information and sets the stage for the reader. It should grab the reader's attention, provide context for the topic, and end with a clear thesis statement that summarizes the main points of the paper.

Example: In an essay about the impact of social media on mental health, the introduction might begin with a statistic about the rising use of social media among young people and then explain why this is a relevant and important topic. The thesis statement might be, "This essay will argue that social media use is contributing to increased rates of anxiety and depression among teenagers and young adults."



Body: The body is the main section of an essay or academic paper that presents the evidence and arguments to support the thesis statement. It should be divided into several paragraphs, each focusing on a specific point or aspect of the topic. Each paragraph should begin with a topic sentence that connects to the thesis statement and is supported by evidence and examples.

Example: In an essay about the impact of social media on mental health, the body paragraphs might focus on different ways in which social media use can contribute to anxiety and depression, such as cyberbullying, social comparison, and information overload. Each paragraph would provide evidence and examples to support the argument, and would end with a transition sentence connecting to the next paragraph.



Conclusion: The conclusion is the final part of an essay or academic paper that summarizes the main points and restates the thesis statement in a new way. It should also provide some final thoughts or recommendations related to the topic, and leave the reader with a sense of closure.

Example: In an essay about the impact of social media on mental health, the conclusion might summarize the main points made in the body paragraphs, restate the thesis statement in a new way, and provide some recommendations for how individuals and society can address the negative effects of social media use on mental health. It might also end with a thought-provoking question or call to action to encourage further reflection or action on the topic.

Introduction: The first paragraph of your essay. It is the first impression your readers recieve, and explain the main point of your paper. Your introduction contains your thesis statement. Body: The paragraphs in between your introduction and conclusion. The body of your paper supports the main point of your paper.
