This is because of the Coriolis effect, which is the apparent deflection of objects (such as air mass and water) moving in a straight path relative to the rotating Earth.

The Coriolis effect causes objects to appear to curve to the right in the Northern Hemisphere and to the left in the Southern Hemisphere. Since the pen cap was dropped on the North Pole, it will appear to curve to the right or towards the west, as viewed from above the North Pole.
