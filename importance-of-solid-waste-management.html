19. Answer the following:1. What is the role of the student in waste management?2. Why is it important to be aware of the practices of solid waste management in school?​


Answer:

1. The role of the student in waste management is to be responsible and conscious about the waste they produce and to follow the school's waste management practices. This can include properly sorting and disposing of waste, reducing the amount of waste generated, and participating in waste reduction and recycling initiatives

2. It is important to be aware of the practices of solid waste management in school because waste has an impact on the environment, public health, and the local community. By being mindful of these practices, students can help to reduce waste and its negative effects, and promote sustainable and responsible waste management in their school and community. Additionally, being aware of waste management practices can help students to understand the importance of reducing, reusing, and recycling resources, and to develop habits that support these efforts
