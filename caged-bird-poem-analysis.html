1. write your analysis of the poem i know why the caged bird sings.


Answer:

"I Know Why the Caged Bird Sings" is a powerful poem written by Maya Angelou that explores themes of oppression, racism, and the longing for freedom. The poem draws its inspiration from the caged bird, which serves as a metaphor for the African American experience during a time of segregation and discrimination.

The poem is divided into two stanzas, with the first stanza describing the free bird's life and the second stanza describing the caged bird's life. The free bird is portrayed as living a carefree and joyful life, enjoying the beauty of nature and the freedom to fly wherever it wishes. In contrast, the caged bird is described as being confined and oppressed, with its wings clipped and its ability to fly restricted.

The metaphor of the caged bird is used to illustrate the struggles of African Americans during a time when they were denied their basic human rights and treated as second-class citizens. The caged bird's song is described as being "a prayer back to heaven" and a symbol of hope and resilience in the face of adversity.

The poem is also a powerful commentary on the human desire for freedom and the will to survive in the face of oppression. Despite being trapped and confined, the caged bird continues to sing, a testament to its determination to overcome its circumstances.

Overall, "I Know Why the Caged Bird Sings" is a deeply moving and thought-provoking poem that speaks to the human condition and the struggle for freedom and equality. Through the use of vivid imagery and powerful metaphor, Maya Angelou captures the essence of the African American experience and the resilience of the human spirit in the face of adversity.

5. Write a critique of the poem "Caged Bird"â€‹


Answer:

"Caged Bird" was published in Maya Angelou's 1983 poetry collection Shaker, Why Don't You Sing? The poem describes the opposing experiences between two birds: one bird is able to live in nature as it pleases, while a different caged bird suffers in captivity. Due to its profound suffering, the caged bird sings, both to cope with its circumstances and to express its own longing for freedom. Using the extended metaphor of the two birds, Angelou paints a critical portrait of oppression in which she illuminates the privilege and entitlement of the un-oppressed, and conveys the simultaneous experience of suffering and emotional resilience. In particular, the poem's extended metaphor can be seen as portraying the experience of being a member of the African American communit.

23. who are the characters in the poem caged bird?â€‹


Answer:

Explanation:

Caged Bird Character List

The Free Bird

The free bird is an uncaged bird who revels in his freedom to glide on wind currents and hunt juicy worms waiting on lawns. The speaker depicts the free bird as feeling entitled to the world it inhabits and oblivious to its privilege as a free creature.

The Caged Bird

The caged bird, unlike the free bird, lives its life captive in a cage. He has clipped wings and his legs are tied. To express his frustration at being held in a cage and his longing for experiences he has never known, the caged bird sings a song of freedom that reaches far beyond his cage.

The Speaker

The speaker of the poem is an unidentified omniscient voice that relates the perspectives of the free bird and the caged bird to the listener. The speaker's depiction of the caged bird's suffering and the free bird's oblivious entitlement suggests that the speaker sympathizes with the caged bird.
