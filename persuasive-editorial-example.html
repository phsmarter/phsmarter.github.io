13. Compare the 4 types of Editorial Articles (interpretative/interpretive, critical, persuasive, & praising)


Answer:

Editorial articles are opinion pieces that are written by the editorial staff or columnists of a newspaper, magazine, or website. There are four main types of editorial articles, each with its own purpose and style.

1. Interpretative/Interpretive Editorial: This type of editorial article aims to interpret and analyze a particular news event or issue. It explains the significance of the issue and its impact on society. Interpretative editorials are usually well-researched and provide a balanced analysis of the topic.

2. Critical Editorial: This type of editorial article is written to criticize or condemn a particular policy, decision, or action taken by a person, organization, or government. It highlights the flaws or negative consequences of the issue and calls for corrective action.

3. Persuasive Editorial: This type of editorial article aims to persuade the readers to take a particular stance on a topic. It presents arguments and evidence to support the author's point of view and may provide a call to action for the readers.

4. Praising Editorial: This type of editorial article is written to praise and celebrate a particular person, organization, or event. It highlights the positive aspects and achievements of the subject and may inspire readers to follow their example.

In summary, interpretative editorials aim to provide analysis and interpretation, critical editorials aim to critique and condemn, persuasive editorials aim to persuade readers to take a particular stance, and praising editorials aim to celebrate and inspire. Each type of editorial article serves a different purpose and has a unique style of writing.

30. How does editorial of argument of persuasion set it self apart from the other types of editorial?​


Answer:

An editorial is an article that presents the newspaper's opinion on an issue. It reflects the majority vote of the editorial board, the governing body of the newspaper made up of editors and business managers. It is usually unsigned. Much in the same manner of a lawyer, editorial writers build on an argument and try to persuade readers to think the same way they do. Editorials are meant to influence public opinion, promote critical thinking, and sometimes cause people to take action on an issue. In essence, an editorial is an opinionated news story.
