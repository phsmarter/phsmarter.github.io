7. what is scarcity? cite example on how scarcity can be observed in real life situation​


Answer:

Scarcity as an economic concept "refers to the basic fact of life that there exists only a finite amount of human and nonhuman resources which the best technical knowledge is capable of using to produce only limited maximum amounts of each economic good." If the conditions of scarcity didn't exist and an "infinite amount of every good could be produced or human wants fully satisfied... there would be no economic goods, i.e. goods that are relatively scarce..." Scarcity is the limited availability of a commodity, which may be in demand in the market or by the commons. Scarcity also includes an individual's lack of resources to buy commodities. The opposite of scarcity is abundance.

Example:

Scarcity, or the lack of sufficient resources, affects virtually all aspects of life, as people must constantly acquire wealth to pay for needs that are in short supply.

Answer:

Scarcity is a problem that many societies face due to limited resources and unlimited wants. As an agent of the economy, there are several options for dealing with scarcity: economic growth, improving the use of available resources, and reducing expectations.

Economic Growth:

One solution to scarcity is to focus on economic growth. Economic growth can be achieved by increasing the production of goods and services, creating more job opportunities, and increasing investment in research and development. This will help to increase the supply of goods and services, which can help to reduce the scarcity problem. Governments can encourage economic growth by implementing policies that promote investment and innovation, and by reducing barriers to trade.

Improve the Use of Available Resources:

Another option for dealing with scarcity is to improve the use of available resources. This can be achieved by implementing more efficient production methods, reducing waste, and promoting recycling. By using resources more efficiently, we can reduce the amount of resources needed to produce goods and services, which can help to reduce scarcity. Governments can promote resource efficiency by implementing regulations and incentives that encourage companies to adopt more sustainable practices.

Reduce Expectations:

Reducing expectations is another way to deal with scarcity. When people have unrealistic expectations about what they can consume, it can lead to a situation where demand exceeds supply, which can exacerbate scarcity. To reduce expectations, governments can implement education campaigns that promote more sustainable and responsible consumption patterns. This can be done through public awareness campaigns and by working with businesses to promote sustainable production and consumption practices.

In conclusion, dealing with scarcity requires a multifaceted approach that includes a focus on economic growth, improving the use of available resources, and reducing expectations. Governments can play an important role in addressing scarcity by implementing policies and incentives that promote sustainable production and consumption practices. By working together, we can help to reduce scarcity and ensure a more sustainable future.

Why does scarcity create social problems?

Almost every good we consume as individuals, or as a society, costs something and is scarce. By consuming one good, another good is foregone. Therefore, scarcity creates a need for decisions and trade-offs to be made.

Societies can deal with scarcity by increasing supply. The more goods and services available to all, the less scarcity there will be. Of course, increasing supply comes with limitations, such as production capacity, land available for use, time, and so on.
