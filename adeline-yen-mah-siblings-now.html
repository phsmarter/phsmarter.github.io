18. Maria and her siblings _studying their lessons now. a.am b.is c.are d.were​


Answer:

LINKING VERB

Maria and her siblings _studying their lessons now.

a. am b. is c. are d. were​

Explanation:

In this item, what is being asked is the linking verb that is appropriate to the sentence. A linking verb is used in a sentence as a way of connecting the subject of the sentence into the verb being mentioned or described in the sentence. In this case, we can see how the subject of the sentence is Maria and her siblings. Since it is in plural form, we are bound to use [are] rather than [is]. On the other hand, the sentence is written in present form for they used the word [now] therefore, insinuating that the sentence is written in present form; that is why we used [are] instead of [were]. We must be careful in choosing the correct form of linking verb in the sentence for if we were to choose wrong, it will affect the whole state of the sentence.
