Physiological Changes that occur in the aging process

The aging process that occurs with age can affect all organs of the human body. Not only physical, here are some physiological changes in the elderly along with the aging process.

1. At risk for cardiovascular disorders

As you age, your blood vessels and arteries become stiffer. This condition can be caused by aging or an unhealthy lifestyle that was carried out during youth.

2. The size and strength of bones and teeth is decreasing

As you age, your bone size and density will shrink. This makes them weaker and more prone to bone problems.

That's why some older people are more prone to injuries when they fall, such as broken bones. This condition can also be caused by osteoporosis which is common in older people.

3. Muscles and joints are often problematic

Parents also often complain of muscle and joint problems as they get older. This is because, along with increasing time, muscles and joints will also experience a decrease in endurance, strength, and flexibility.

4. Digestive system decreases

Aging can also cause structural changes in the colon. It is not uncommon that the elderly often experience constipation or difficulty defecating.

5. Having problems with the urinary tract and bladder

The aging process also causes the pelvic floor and bladder muscles to weaken and become less elastic.

As a result, many elderly people often urinate. Not infrequently, some of them also have difficulty holding back urination, or urinary incontinence.

6. Decreased memory and thinking ability

One of the most affected body systems during the aging process is memory and thinking skills.

Dementia and Alzheimer's are the most common problems of cognitive decline in the elderly.
