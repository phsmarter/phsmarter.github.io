A folk dance is a dance developed by people that reflect the life of the people of a certain country or region. Not all ethnic dances are folk dances. 3. Give the 8 Nature of Philippines folk dances. ​


The 8 nature of Philippine folk dances are the following:

1.OCCUPATIONAL DANCES - depict actions of a particular occupation. (e.g. Planting, Punding)

2.RELIGIOUS/CEREMONIAL - associated with religion, vows and ceremonies. (e.g. Dugsu, Sua-sua)

3.COMIC DANCES - depict funny movements for entertainment. (e.g. Kinotan, Makonggo)

4.GAME DANCES - done with play elements. (e.g. Lubi-lubi, Pavo)

5.WEDDING DANCES - performed during wedding feasts. (e.g. Panasahan)

6.COURTSHIP DANCES - depict the art of courtship. (e.g. Hele-hele, Tadek, Pantomina)

7.FESTIVAL DANCES - suitable for special occasions. (e.g. Pandanggo, Habanera)

8.WAR DANCES - show imagery combat. (e.g. Sagayan, Palu-palo)

Answer:

1. Philippine Folk Dance is a traditional dance form that reflects the distinct cultural heritage and identity of the Filipino people. It encompasses various indigenous artistic expressions, including music, literature, and visual arts.

2. The primary objectives of folk dance are to preserve cultural heritage, promote social cohesion and solidarity, and provide entertainment for the community. Furthermore, it aims to foster national pride and identity, promote physical fitness and well-being, and enhance individuals' self-esteem.

3. The three values of folk dance are cultural identity, diversity, and creativity. It reflects the unique cultural identity of the people of the Philippines, celebrates the country's diversity and promotes creative expressions of the art form.

4. The four factors affecting folk dance are historical context, cultural diversity, socio-economic conditions, and technological advancements.

5. The four classifications of Philippine Folk Dance are tribal or ethnolinguistic dances, rural or country dances, court or palace dances, and religious or ceremonial dances.

6. The two geographical extents of origin are Luzon and Mindanao.

7. The eight nature of folk dance are communal, traditional, expressive, symbolic, celebratory, rhythmic, creative, and participatory. Examples of communal dances are Pandango sa Ilaw and Tinikling. Examples of celebratory dances are Ati-Atihan and Sinulog.

8. The four movements of folk dance are turning, hopping, leaping, and sliding.

9. The three formations of folk dance are circular, square, and straight line.

10. The three special classifications of folk dance are ancient or pre-colonial dances, fusion or modernized dances, and revival or recreation dances.

The word “binasuan” means “with the use of drinking glasses.” It is one of the most challenging Filipino dances as the dancers need to balance glasses on their heads and in their hands as they move.pandanggo sa ilaw- is a Philippine folk dance which has become popular in the rural areas of the Philippines. The dance evolved from Fandango, a Spanish folk dance, which arrived in the Philippines during the Hispanic period. The dance is accompanied by castanets. This dance, together with the Jota, became popular among the illustrados or the upper class and later adapted among the local communities. In the early 18th century, any dance that is considered jovial and lively was called Pandanggo.maglalatik-It originated in the town of Binan in the Laguna province of the Filipino Islands and is also performed as homage to the town saint, San Isidro de Labrador. ... Maglalatik is an original native dance of the Philippines in which the coconut halves are attached to the torso of the dancer.carinosa-The dance originated in Panay Island in the Visayan Islands and was introduced by the Spaniards during their colonization of the Philippines. It is related to some of the Spanish dances like the bolero and the Mexican dance Jarabe Tapatio or the Mexican Hat Dance.
