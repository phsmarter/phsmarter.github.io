Philosophy is not necessarily a primarily skill-oriented field of study, but it is the specialized field of study for critical thinking, and it can help us improve our critical thinking skills.

29. Importance of Philosophy in your life as a student


Here are the importance of Philosophy in my life as a student.

Let's be honest. Philosophy is often seen as a field of knowledge which is considered as useless. People don't find it interesting, and even find it boring, and they can't get anything out of it (pragmatically).

This myth has given birth to countless arguments on why it is wrong, and as students, we have to help people in promoting Philosophy as a subject of value rather than believing what people think of Philosophy.

I am going to list three reasons why Philosophy is important in my life as a student:

Engaging in Philosophy will train your critical thinking. It is a common secret that engaging in Philosophy will enhance our ability to think critically. Next, all we feel is that ideas sprung up easier than the time when we are not interested in Philosophy.It helps our brain to think systematically. Systematic thinking allows one to follow the pattern of thinking according to the proper order. We will get fewer number of biases and confusion when we deal with our regular school subjects.It will help us in forming a proper arguments. When doing homework that requires us to write arguments or explain something, Philosophy, I think, will help a lot. This is because when we read books that deal with Philosophy, we are studying how to form a good arguments at the same time.

In conclusion, never think of Philosophy as useless, especially if you are still in school. You might feel that you get nothing out of it right now, but the benefits will surely follow you through in long term. So, go to your library and read some books talking about Philosophy.
