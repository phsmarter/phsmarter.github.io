2. Explain the film technique called " montage " ?


Answer:

Montage is combining different pictures or designs in a single run. In film, this technique is editing shots and combining them in a quick succession to present a chunk of information in a short amount of time.

Montage technique is used when the film needs to convey some background information that do not need individual scenes.  However, this technique is not used to convey emotions, rather it is used to inform the audience of what they should know by now as the film progresses.

Often included in montages are narrations, fast music, and quick shots that have many cuts. These are done to convey that a certain time has passed or this kind of information is needed by the audience to make sense of the film.

12. explain the technique called "montage"​


Answer:

Montage is a film editing technique in which series of short shots are sequenced to condense space, time, and information. With montage, portions of motion pictures can be carefully built up piece by piece by the director, film editor, and visual and sound technicians, who cut and fit each part with the others. This allows to conveys a lot of information in a relatively short period. One example is from 2002 "The Rules of Attraction", which narrates a journey across many countries in Europe.

Visual montage may combine shots to tell a story chronologically or may juxtapose images to produce an impression or to illustrate an association of ideas. An example of the latter occurs in Strike (1924), by the Russian director Sergey Eisenstein, when the scene of workers being cut down by cavalry is followed by a shot of cattle being slaughtered.

This is done by cutting out unnecessary scenes and arranging them in chronological order according to the storyboard. It is also important to choose the right transition from Scene A to Scene B. 13. enumerate five techniques you did in producing a quality photo montage​


Answer

Photomontage itself is a technique and a style in visual art. It is the editing of various types of images in which different photographs are cropped/cut and combined to create a new image.

1) Experiment with different visual art style

Techniques may vary depending on the style you're aiming for. For example, you can make an impressionist like photomontage by cutting photos into impressionist brushstroke sizes.

2) Planning your artwork

The involvement of different photos might make the production disorganized. Thus, planning helps save time in producing a photomontage by avoiding mistakes.

3) Consistency in lighting

Sharing the same angle of a light source, makes different images merge well creating the illusion of being one image.

4) Create Harmony

Depending on the concept, it is best for the variation of photos to share the same quality of imagery. After all, a quality photomontage should look as if all cut-outs belong to a single picture.

5) Study the composition of your final output

A Photomontage is similar to a painting because it involves creating a composition to produce pleasing visuals. For example, look for lines that can guide the eyes to the focus of the photomontage.
