3. Make an Extemporaneous speech about Defining failure in Academic Achievements​


Answer:

Defining Failure in Academics

Make an Extemporaneous speech about Defining failure in Academic Achievements

Good afternoon everyone. Today, I would like to talk about the topic of defining failure in academic achievements.

Failure is a subjective term and it is often perceived differently by individuals. For some, it may mean getting a lower grade than they expected, while for others it may mean not getting accepted into their desired school or program.

However, in the context of academic achievements, failure can be defined as the inability to reach a desired goal or outcome. It doesn't mean a lack of intelligence or ability, but rather the experience of learning and growth that helps individuals improve and succeed in the future.

Furthermore, it is important to understand that failure is not permanent and is not a reflection of one's self-worth. Rather, it is a learning opportunity to reflect on what went wrong, what can be improved and how to move forward.

In conclusion, failure in academic achievements should not be viewed as the end, but rather as a stepping stone towards success. It is through our failures that we develop resilience, determination, and a growth mindset. So, the next time you face a setback in your academic pursuits, remember to embrace it as a valuable learning experience. Thank you.

22. Positive Externality ​sa Market Failure and Negative Externality sa Market Failure


Answer:

Positive Externality-With positive externalities, the buyer does not get all the benefits of the good, resulting in decreased production. ... In this case, the market failure would be too much production and a price that didn't match the true cost of production, as well as high levels of pollution.

Negative Externality-When negative externalities are present, it means the producer does not bear all costs, which results in excess production. ... In this case, the market failure would be too much production and a price that didn't match the true cost of production, as well as high levels of pollution.
