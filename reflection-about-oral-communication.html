29. what is your suggestion about oral communication​


tips/suggestions on doing oral communication

• Be prepared to listen. Concentrate on the speaker, and not on how you are going to reply.

• Keep an open mind and avoid making judgements about the speaker.

• Concentrate on the main direction of the speaker’s message. Try to understand broadly what they are trying to say overall, as well as the detail of the words that they are using.

• Avoid distractions if at all possible. For example, if there is a lot of background noise, you might suggest that you go somewhere else to talk.

• Be objective.

• Do not be trying to think of your next question while the other person is giving information.

In reflecting on my experience with local and oral research, I have come to appreciate the value of listening to and learning from the perspectives and experiences of those who have been historically marginalized or underrepresented.

I have also realized the importance of building trust and relationships with community members, as this can greatly enhance the quality and depth of the information gathered. Furthermore, I have learned the importance of being flexible and responsive to the needs and concerns of the community.
