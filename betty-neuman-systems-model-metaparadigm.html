3. What is the metaparadigm or the components of Nursing?​


What is the metaparadigm or the components of Nursing?​

A metaparadigm is a set of concepts or beliefs that define how a field should operate. These ideas address the patient as a whole, the patient's health and well-being, the patient's surroundings, and the nursing obligations in a nursing discipline. These four core nursing metaparadigms indicate to a holistic vision of care where a person's well-being and medical health are linked to four interconnected components, despite the fact that there are multiple distinct nursing theories. Person, environment, health, and nursing are the four metaparadigms of nursing.

Person Component: The metaparadigm's person component focuses on the individual who receives the care. Family members and other essential groups for the patient, on the other hand, are included in the person connection. The assumption is that the individual is empowered to manage his or her own health and well-being in a dignified and self-sufficient manner while maintaining strong personal relationships.

Environment Component: The nursing metaparadigms' environmental aspect focuses on the patient's environment, according to the theory, how a person interacts with her surroundings on a daily basis has an impact on her health and well-being. Physical and social variables such as economic situations, geographic locations, culture, social ties, and technology, as well as interactions with family, friends, and other people, are all part of the environment. According to this metaparadigm component, a person's health can be improved by changing his or her surroundings.

Health Component: The four metaparadigms' health component relates to a patient's level of health and access to health treatment. In a continual state of mobility, this health component is defined as one with various dimensions. In order to maximize health advantages, health and wellness include a person's lifetime and genetic composition, as well as how a person's physical, emotional, intellectual, social, and spiritual well-being is incorporated into health treatment. According to the hypothesis, these elements have an impact on the patient's well-being.

Nursing Component: The nursing component of the metaparadigm entails providing the patient with the best possible health outcomes through establishing a trusting and loving connection. To carry out tasks and obligations for obtaining the best possible scenario in patient health result, the nursing component utilizes concepts of knowledge, skills, technology, partnerships, professional judgment, and communication. It connects with other metaparadigm components for patient well-being and values a high level of service.

19. Alvin, Betty and Cheryl scored a total of 2014 points during a competition. Betty scored 271 points less than Alvin. Betty scored 3 times as many points as Cheryl. How many points did Betty score? with explanation po pls


Answer:Let's start by assigning variables to the unknown quantities in the problem. We can let "A" be the number of points that Alvin scored, "B" be the number of points that Betty scored, and "C" be the number of points that Cheryl scored. Then we can set up a system of equations to represent the information given in the problem:

A + B + C = 2014 (the total number of points scored is 2014)B = A - 271 (Betty scored 271 points less than Alvin)B = 3C (Betty scored 3 times as many points as Cheryl)

Now we can use the third equation to substitute for "B" in the first two equations:

A + 3C + C = 2014 (substitute B = 3C into the first equation)A - 271 = 3C (substitute B = 3C into the second equation)

We can simplify the first equation by combining like terms:

A + 4C = 2014

Next, we can solve the second equation for "A" by adding 271 to both sides:

A = 3C + 271

Now we can substitute this expression for "A" into the first equation:

(3C + 271) + 4C = 2014

Simplifying, we get:

7C + 271 = 2014

Subtracting 271 from both sides:

7C = 1743

Dividing both sides by 7:

C = 249

So Cheryl scored 249 points. Now we can use the equation B = 3C to find out how many points Betty scored:

B = 3(249)

Simplifying, we get:

B = 747

Therefore, Betty scored 747 points.
