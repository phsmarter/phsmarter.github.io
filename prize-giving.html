25. A. Don Juan has 30 prizes for parlor games. He decided to give 2/3 to the winnersof the relay game. How many of these prizes are for the other games?​


Answer:

Ten (10) prizes are reserved for the other games.

Step-by-step explanation:

Don Juan has 30 prizes for parlor games. He decided to give 2/3 to the winners  of the relay game. How many of these prizes are for the other games?

If [tex]\frac{2}{3}[/tex] of the prizes are for the winners of the relay game, then the remaining [tex]\frac{1}{3}[/tex] are for the other games. Find the [tex]\frac{1}{3}[/tex] of 30.

[tex]30 \times \frac{1}{3} = \frac{30}{3} = \boxed{10}[/tex]

Therefore, 10 prizes are for the other games.

You can also solve this in another way.

Find the [tex]\frac{2}{3}[/tex] of 30 to determine the number of prizes for the winners of the relay game.

[tex]30 \times \frac{2}{3}=\frac{60}{3}\\=20[/tex]

Subtract the result, 20, from 30 to determine the number of prizes there are for the other games.

[tex]Total\ number\ of\ prizes\ - Prizes\ for\ the\ relay\ game = Prizes\ for\ the\ other\ games\\30 - 20 = \Large \boxed{10}[/tex]
