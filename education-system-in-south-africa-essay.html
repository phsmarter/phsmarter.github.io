Is education Relevant in the Philippines system?

To bring its education system in line with that of other Asian countries, the Philippines recently undertook a substantial makeover, extending basic education from 10 to 13 years. As a result, young graduates in the Philippines becoming more competitive in the employment market, both domestically and internationally. From pre-colonial periods to the present day, we look at education in the Philippines.

There was little formal schooling in the Philippines before the arrival of the Spanish in 1521. Fathers taught their sons how to get food, while moms taught their daughters how to take care of the house.

With the entrance of the Spaniards, education became more formalized; Spaniards, mestizos (mixed Spanish/Filipino people), and rich Filipinos of the Philippines were given priority, forming an educational system.
