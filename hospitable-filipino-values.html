13. hard work

A hard worker person does not easily give up even when trials are along his or her way.  Filipinos are known to be hard workers because a lot of businessmen, including the local vendors, are known to have various ways to earn money.

14. hospitality

Hospitality is shown when a family accommodates their visitors warmly and offer them everything the family can provide.

15. compassionate

Being compassionate is the ability to sympathize with others and to show deep kindness especially to those who are in great need.  

16. camaraderie

Camaraderie is sharing good fellowship with people or a community.

17. religiosity

Filipinos are known for their religiosity.  There may be a variety of religions and beliefs in the Philippines, but this diversity connects every person anyhow.

18. resiliency

It is evident how resilient Filipino people are during calamities and tragic events.

HospitalityThis is the most frequently used term to describe how Filipinos greet foreign visitors or tourists who visit the nation. This characteristic of Filipinos regarding their tourists is undoubtedly unique.14.) Utang na LoobThe nature of utang na loob is an obligation to repay someone who has adequately done one a favor. Favors that elicit the Filipino's sense of utang na loob are often those whose value is intangible or, if quantitative, entail a very personal internal dimension. This interior dimension, loob, distinguishes utang na loob from conventional utang (debt); as an inner spectacle, utang na loob thus goes much deeper than traditional debt or even the western sense of owing a favor. According to Filipino psychology, this reflects the kapwa orientation of shared personhood or shared self that is central to the Filipino value system.15.) A. Bayanihan In the Philippines, the Bayanihan spirit is communal solidarity, assisting others without expecting compensation to accomplish a purpose. Historically, when dwellings were constructed of lighter materials such as coconut leaves, Bayanihan also meant assisting one's neighbors in relocating their home. The Bayanihan is a Filipino ceremony derived from the word "bayan," which translates as nation, town, or community in Filipino. Bayanihan translates as "being in a bayan" and relates to the spirit of communal cooperation, work, and cooperation toward a common purpose.
