Understanding the risks and disaster perception, factors and impact1. personal experience2. cultural and social factors3. media and communication4. socioeconomic statusGive your own understanding about the understanding the risks and disaster perception, factors and impact with example of each factors.​


Understanding the risks and disaster perception involves recognizing and assessing potential hazards and their likelihood of occurrence, as well as understanding how individuals and communities perceive and respond to these risks. There are several factors that can influence risk perception and disaster impact, including:

1. Personal experience: People's personal experiences can shape their perception of risks and disasters. For example, someone who has lived through a hurricane may be more likely to take precautions and evacuate during a future storm, while someone who has never experienced a hurricane may underestimate the potential danger.

2. Cultural and social factors: Cultural and social factors can play a role in how people perceive and respond to risks and disasters. For example, in some cultures, there may be a strong sense of community and cooperation during times of crisis, while in others, there may be a greater emphasis on individualism and self-reliance.

3. Media and communication: The way that risks and disasters are communicated in the media can also have an impact on how people perceive them. For example, sensational or exaggerated news coverage may create a sense of panic or fear, while accurate and informative reporting can help people make informed decisions.

4. Socioeconomic status: Socioeconomic factors such as income, education, and access to resources can also influence risk perception and disaster impact. For example, people with limited financial resources may be less able to prepare for and recover from disasters, while those with more resources may have greater access to information and resources that can help them mitigate risks.

Overall, understanding the factors that influence risk perception and disaster impact can help individuals and communities better prepare for and respond to potential hazards. By taking into account personal experiences, cultural and social factors, media and communication, and socioeconomic status, people can make more informed decisions that can help protect themselves and their communities during times of crisis.

30. In the Globalization of Media Culture, which has more significant impact 'traditional media' (oral communication, script, printing) or 'modern media' (electronic and digital)? Explain. Elaborate. Give examples.​


The role of modern media has more influence on the globalization of media culture.

Explanation :

Traditional media is a form of local media in a cultural community. The existence of this media has long been used by the cultural community to establish interaction and communication among members of that community before the presence of modern mass media.

Meanwhile, digital media is a content format that can be accessed by digital devices. This digital media can be in the form of websites, social media, digital images and videos, digital audio and others.

In globalization, digital media has a more significant role. This is because globalization is the process of entering cultures from all over the world into a region. The culture of other countries will certainly enter more easily through modern communication systems that make more use of digital technology such as social media.
