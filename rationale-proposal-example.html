13. what are the guidelines in writing the recommendation? what is the rationale for each guideline? Give examples.​


Answer:

The Conclusions and Recommendations may be combined or, in long reports, presented in separate sections. If there are no recommendations to be made as a result of the project, just call this section Conclusions.

Explanation:

The Conclusions section sums up the key points of your discussion, the essential features of your design, or the significant outcomes of your investigation. As its function is to round off the story of your project, it should:

be written to relate directly to the aims of the project as stated in the Introduction

indicate the extent to which the aims have been achieved

summarise the key findings, outcomes or information in your report

acknowledge limitations and make recommendations for future work (where applicable)

highlight the significance or usefulness of your work.

The conclusions should relate to the aims of the work:

Example:

Aim  

This investigation will review the volume of passengers using the Monash University Clayton-Caulfield inter-campus bus in order to recommend adjustments to the service if needed.

Conclusions

Analysis of the survey data obtained in this project shows that use of the Clayton-Caulfield inter-campus bus has increased by 24% in the past five years. It is recommended that frequency be increased during peak usage.

20. As a public official, Propose an ordinance which you think is applicable in your area? just write down the rationale of your proposal. please help ​


Answer:

As a public official, I propose an ordinance mandating the installation of solar panels on all new residential and commercial construction in the area.

The rationale for this proposal is to reduce the community's dependence on fossil fuels and decrease the carbon footprint of new buildings. Solar energy is a clean and renewable energy source that can reduce the need for electricity generated by fossil fuels. By mandating solar panels on new construction, we can decrease the amount of greenhouse gas emissions produced by the community and combat climate change.

Additionally, the ordinance would have economic benefits. The installation of solar panels can help reduce electricity costs for the building's occupants and make new construction more energy-efficient. This would benefit both the building owners and the community as a whole.

It's worth noting that before proposing a law, a lot of things should be considered such as feasibility, cost, benefits, legal and legislative process, community feedback and Impact on the existing regulations. And also to assess the possible impact on the local economy and environment.
