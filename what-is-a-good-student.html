22. What makes a good student​


Answer:

A good student is someone who demonstrates certain qualities and habits that promote academic success and personal growth. Here are some traits that make a good student:

Positive Attitude: A good student has a positive attitude towards learning and is willing to put in the effort to succeed.

Responsibility: A good student takes responsibility for their own learning and their own mistakes, and is willing to seek help when needed.

Good Study Habits: A good student develops and maintains good study habits such as time management, organization, and note-taking skills.

Curiosity: A good student has a curious and inquisitive mind, always seeking to learn more and ask questions.

Active Listening: A good student listens actively and pays attention in class, and is engaged in discussions and activities.

Persistence: A good student is persistent in the face of challenges and setbacks, and keeps working towards their goals.

Respectful: A good student is respectful towards their teachers, classmates, and school community.

Overall, a good student is someone who is dedicated, hardworking, and motivated to learn and grow both academically and personally.

27. What are the good effects of oral recitation for students?


Answer:

Oral recitation can have several positive effects on students, including:

Improved Memory Retention: Oral recitation helps to enhance memory retention as students have to recall information from memory and recite it. The process of speaking the information aloud helps students to remember it better than if they had just read or listened to it.Enhanced Understanding: Oral recitation can also help students to better understand the material they are studying. When students recite information out loud, they engage with it more actively and can identify areas of confusion or misunderstanding.Increased Confidence: Reciting information aloud can help students build confidence in their ability to communicate effectively. By practicing oral recitation, students can develop their public speaking skills and become more comfortable speaking in front of others.Active Learning: Oral recitation is a form of active learning that encourages students to participate actively in their own learning process. By reciting information aloud, students are more engaged with the material and are better able to connect with it on a deeper level.Improved Pronunciation and Speaking Skills: Oral recitation can also help students to improve their pronunciation and speaking skills, particularly for those who are learning a second language or have a speech impediment. By practicing oral recitation, students can become more fluent and confident in their speech.

Overall, oral recitation can be a powerful tool for enhancing learning and academic performance, as well as developing important skills such as memory retention, active learning, confidence, and communication skills.
