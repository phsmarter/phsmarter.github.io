1. Summary for Ernest Hemingway's “Hills Like White Elephants” need this ASAP


Answer:

Ernest Hemingway's “Hills Like White Elephants” is a short story about a couple's conversation at a train station in Spain. The story follows the couple as they discuss an operation that the woman, Jig, is considering having. The man, who is never named, is trying to convince her to get the operation, while Jig is ambivalent and uncertain. Throughout their conversation, they discuss the implications of the operation, the relationship between them, and the views of their society. Ultimately, the story ends with the couple's decision left unresolved, with the man saying, "It's all right. We'll be all right."


2. Summary for Ernest Hemingway’s “Hills Like White Elephants” need it ASAP


Answer:

Ernest Hemingway’s “Hills Like White Elephants” is a short story that depicts a conversation between an American man and a woman, who is referred to as Jig. The two are waiting at a train station in Spain and discussing the possibility of Jig having an abortion. The tension between the two characters is palpable, as they struggle to communicate their feelings and desires. Jig is torn between her love for the American man and her desire to keep the baby, while the American man is focused on his own desires and unwilling to commit to anything. Through the dialogue, Hemingway explores themes of communication, decision-making, and the complexities of human relationships. The story ends with the train arriving, and the two characters making their decision as they prepare to embark on a new journey.

15. HELP PLEASE! Insights on the literature entitled hills like white elephants.


This story revolves around the idioms " elephant in the room" and " white elephant". Jig repeatedly brings up the topic of how the hills looked like white elephants. This was her way of subtly asking the american man about what they were going to do about the fact that Jig was carrying his child. The american man tries his best to ignore the silent pleas because not only does he not want to face the issue but also because the child itself was a white elephant or an unwanted gift. Perhaps it was because he did not want to disturb the superficial, lighthearted, and free flowing nature of their relationship. The phrase in the story, from all the hotels where they had spent the night." implies that the couple had no plans of pursuing a stable relationship. Additionally, love was never mentioned between the couple in the story. In the end it was unclear whether the couple kept the child or proceeded to go through the process of abortion. 

This story made excellent use of the figures of speech.

Nakapagsulat sya ng pitong nobela, anim na koleksyon ng maiikling kwento at tatlong non-fiction. Ang mga sinulat nya ay isa sa mga sinasabing classical American literature. Kilala din sya sa style ng panulat na tinatawag na iceberg theory. Sa istilong ito, kakaunting interpretasyon lamang ang isinusulat nya sa mga report nya bilang journalist.  

Nadala nya ang istilong ito maging sa pagsusulat nya ng mga nobela at maiikling kwento. Naniwala si Hemingyway na mas makakatulong sa mga mambabasa kung direkta ang lenguwaheng ginagamit sa pagsusulat at ang pagsasabi lamang ng mga pangyayari na walang halong bias o interpretasyon.   Si Hemingway ay nabuhay mula July 21, 1899 hanggang July 2, 1961.
