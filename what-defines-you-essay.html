Answer:

1. An informative essay is a paper that provides information on a specific topic. In other words, it educates the reader about a particular thing, be it a term, event, natural phenomenon, etc. An informative essay doesn't contain the author's evaluation or analysis. Instead, it aims to describe a subject.

2. An analytical essay is a piece of writing that provides substantive analysis of a topic. Analysis papers can be written about art, music, literary works, current events, historical events, politics, scientific research, and philosophy, among other topics.

3. Persuasive writing, also known as the argument essay, uses logic and reason to show that one idea is more legitimate than another. It attempts to persuade a reader to adopt a certain point of view or to take a particular action.

4. An argumentative essay is a piece of writing that takes a stance on an issue. In a good argumentative essay, a writer attempts to persuade readers to understand and support their point of view about a topic by stating their reasoning and providing evidence to back it up

24. ne example of an outline will help you understand to write an informative essay. The topic is about the influence of smoking on humans. Given the outline on how to start your ideas all you need is to provide more information about the topic for you to be able to come up with a complete informative essay. Write your answer on the blanks provided. Introduction Define the topic (what smoking is).​


Answer:

I'd be happy to help you with this outline for an informative essay on the influence of smoking on humans. Here's what you can include in each section:

I. Introduction

- Definition of smoking (e.g. the act of inhaling and exhaling smoke produced by burning tobacco or other substances)

- Brief history of smoking and its prevalence in society

- Importance of understanding the influence of smoking on human health

II. Health Effects of Smoking

- Overview of the negative health effects associated with smoking (e.g. lung cancer, heart disease, stroke, respiratory disease, etc.)

- Explanation of how smoking affects different parts of the body (e.g. lungs, heart, blood vessels, brain)

- Statistics on the prevalence of smoking-related diseases and deaths

III. Addiction and Smoking Cessation

- Explanation of how nicotine addiction works

- Overview of the challenges of quitting smoking and the withdrawal symptoms that can occur

- Discussion of smoking cessation methods (e.g. nicotine replacement therapy, medication, counseling)

IV. Secondhand Smoke

- Explanation of what secondhand smoke is and how it can affect non-smokers

- Overview of the health risks associated with exposure to secondhand smoke (e.g. lung cancer, heart disease, respiratory illnesses)

- Discussion of policies and laws aimed at reducing exposure to secondhand smoke (e.g. smoke-free workplaces, public smoking bans)

V. Conclusion

- Recap of the negative influence of smoking on human health

- Call to action for individuals to quit smoking or never start, and for policymakers to implement measures to reduce smoking rates and protect non-smokers from secondhand smoke.
