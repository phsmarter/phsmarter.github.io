In philosophy, psychology, sociology, and anthropology, intersubjectivity is the relation or intersection between people's cognitive perspectives. It is often used in contrast to solipsistic individual existence and experience, emphasizing the importance to any person of other people's subjectivity, such as their perceptions, thoughts, feelings, and intentions. In phenomenology, intersubjectivity has been called a bridge between subjectivity and objectivity.
