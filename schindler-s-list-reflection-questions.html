Answer:

1. The title of the movie is Immortal Beloved.

2. The main characters in the movie are Ludwig van Beethoven, played by Gary Oldman, and his Immortal Beloved, Antonie Brentano, played by Isabella Rossellini. Ludwig is a famous musician and composer in the late 18th and early 19th centuries, and is struggling with his increasing deafness. Antonie is the mysterious woman he loves, and the movie follows their tumultuous relationship. The supporting characters are Ludwig’s friends, Karl Beethoven and Johann van Beethoven, and his publishers, Anton Schindler and Franz Oliva.

3. Schindler visits Vienna, Prague, Berlin, Leipzig, and Dresden to find the Immortal Beloved. In Vienna, Schindler visits Antonie Brentano’s home, as well as the residences of other women who may have been the Immortal Beloved. In Prague, he visits the home of Josephine Brunsvik. In Berlin, he visits the home of Amalie Sebald. In Leipzig, he visits the home of Julie Guicciardi. And in Dresden, he visits the home of Therese Malfatti.

4. Karl Beethoven is Ludwig van Beethoven’s older brother. He is the executor of Beethoven’s estate and is responsible for delivering the Immortal Beloved letter to Schindler after Beethoven’s death. Karl was also a great influence in Beethoven’s life, providing financial support, and motivating Beethoven to continue writing music. He is the one who pushed Beethoven to become the great composer he is today.

5. The compositions by Ludwig Van Beethoven included in the movie are The Moonlight Sonata, The Appassionata, Emperor Concerto, and The Ninth Symphony. The inspirations for these pieces were personal experiences such as his unrequited love for Antonie Brentano and his struggles with deafness. His motivations were to express his emotions and to prove to the world that he can still create great music despite his deafness.

6. The probable reasons of Beethoven's deafness are hereditary, exposure to loud noises, illnesses, and a medical condition known as otosclerosis.

7. The Immortal Beloved is Antonie Brentano, a married woman whom Beethoven had an unrequited love for. She is called such because Beethoven never revealed her identity in the letter he wrote to her, and her identity remained a mystery until Schindler identified her through his investigation.

8. My favorite part of the movie is the scene in which Beethoven is conducting his Ninth Symphony in Vienna. I love this scene because it is the climax of the movie as Beethoven has overcome his struggles with deafness and is able to showcase his brilliance as a composer. It is also a moment of triumph for him as he is able to prove that he can still create beautiful music despite his disability.

9. My least favorite part of the movie is the scene when Beethoven is dying. I do not like this scene because it is a sad moment when Beethoven passes away, and it is heartbreaking to watch.

10. My learnings/realizations after watching Immortal Beloved are:

• Love can be powerful, and it can drive people to do extraordinary things.

• Music can transcend language and communicate emotions.

• It is possible to work through disability and adversity.

• It is important to appreciate the people in our lives.

• Even though we may never know who the Immortal Beloved is, her story is a reminder of the power of love.
