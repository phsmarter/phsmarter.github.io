One of the most pressing issues in our country today is poverty. Poverty is a complex and multifaceted problem that affects millions of people across the country, and it can have profound social, moral, and economic implications.

To address the issue of poverty, there are a number of steps that can be taken at various levels. At the individual level, people can work to reduce their own personal consumption and increase their savings, which can help to create a more stable financial foundation. This may involve making lifestyle changes, such as cutting back on unnecessary expenses, finding ways to earn additional income, or developing new skills and knowledge that can lead to better job opportunities.

At the community level, people can work together to create programs and initiatives that help to address poverty. This might involve setting up local food banks or other assistance programs to help provide basic necessities for those in need, or organizing job training and education programs that help people acquire the skills they need to succeed in the workforce.

At the national level, governments can take a number of steps to address poverty, such as implementing social welfare programs, investing in education and training programs, and promoting economic growth and job creation. It is also important to ensure that policies and regulations are in place to protect the most vulnerable members of society, such as children, the elderly, and those with disabilities.

Ultimately, addressing poverty requires a multi-faceted approach that involves individuals, communities, and governments working together to create sustainable solutions. While there is no single solution to this complex problem, by working together and focusing our efforts on creating positive change, we can make progress towards a more equitable and just society for all

Answer:

1. Poverty is a complex and multifaceted issue that affects individuals, families, and communities. It is a socio-economic problem that belongs to the larger issue of social inequality. Poverty is often linked to various issues such as unemployment, lack of education, and inadequate access to basic needs like food, shelter, and healthcare. It is a systemic issue that affects millions of people around the world, and it requires a comprehensive and sustained effort to address it.

2. Growing up in a developing country, I have seen firsthand how poverty can impact families. I have seen families struggle to put food on the table, send their children to school, and provide basic healthcare. 23. resolved the poverty injustice issues​


Answer:

1. Increase employment  

There’s a lot of work to be done in the U.S. but much of it won’t generate a profit. That’s where government can step in. Investments in infrastructure fixing old bridges, building mass transit, converting to clean energy sources and investments in vital services such as schools, childcare and elder care generate both public benefits and jobs. So do local-hiring ordinances for large employers in low-income communities.  Building low-cost housing provides jobs as it increases disposable income by lowering housing costs. Free community college could train more people. And if you believe anyone willing to work should have a job, then government can be the employer of last resort.

2. Raise America’s pay

The 2015 federal minimum wage of $7.25 / hour comes to $15,080 annually for full time work, 52 weeks a year. The federal poverty threshold for a family of three is $20,090 (and try living on that!).  If the minimum wage were increased to $10.10/hour (lower in real dollars, btw, than the minimum wage of 1968), 20% of America’s children would benefit.  Cities and some states are taking the lead raising the minimum wage to a living wage.  Many studies suggest that strengthening unions and collective bargaining rights would also bring upward pressure on wages across the board.

3. Sustain not cut the social safety net

Strengthening existing programs like unemployment insurance, food stamps (SNAP), cash assistance, and the earned income tax credit (EITC),  along with new initiatives like child allowances and a guaranteed income, can raise household income and protect children.
