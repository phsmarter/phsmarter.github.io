Answer:

How Recycling can help save Mother Earth?

When we recycle plastic, we reduce the need for new plastic to be made. Through recycling paper, we help to reduce deforestation and save trees from being felled. Separating cans and other metals helps to minimize toxic extraction and our ever-increasing need for raw materials. (summary:  Recycling Conserves Resources)

It takes much more resources to produce industrial-grade products from scratch than it does to reform and reuse old materials. Recycling conserves energy because recycled products do not require manufacturers to emit as much greenhouse gas emissions as they would if the same object were made from scratch using raw materials.(summary: Recycling Saves Energy)

When we reduce the amount of new materials and natural resources we need to harvest from the earth, whether by planting, mining, forestry, or other means, we protect fragile habitats and animals from damage or extinction and ensure their survival for future generations. Recycling products releases far less greenhouse gases into the atmosphere than primary processing.

(summary:  Recycling Protects the Environment)

There are 2,000 operating landfills in the United States alone, all of which are full of industrial waste that release greenhouse gases into the environment as it eventually decomposes. This does not include the many that have been closed due to storage, or the potential landfills that will be needed as the current landfills run out of space. (summary: Recycling Slows the Spread of Landfills)

That's right: believe it or not, one of the advantages of recycling is that it helps to build work! According to a Friends of the Earth report, achieving a 70 percent home recycling rate will generate 51,400 jobs in the United Kingdom alone. In the United States, the Natural Resources Defense Council (NRDC) discovered that achieving a national recycling rate of 75% would result in the production of 1.5 million new jobs. (summary:  Recycling Creates Jobs)

Our sustainability, as well as the futures of future generations, is dependent on sustainable development and consumption. As a result, recycling is one of the most important strategies to contribute to the United Nations Sustainable Development Goals. (summary: Recycling Supports the SDGs)

12. write an informative essay about "Save Mother Earth"​


Answer:

Mother Earth, or also known as our planet, is the only known planet in the universe that is capable of sustaining life. With a diverse range of ecosystems, resources, and inhabitants, it is a magnificent and complex system that we have the privilege of inhabiting. However, over the past few decades, human activity has caused significant damage to our planet, and we are now facing the consequences of our actions. Global warming, deforestation, pollution, and loss of biodiversity are just some of the pressing issues that are threatening the well-being of our planet.

Fortunately, there are many ways that we can help save Mother Earth. One of the most important actions that we can take is to reduce our carbon footprint. This can be achieved by adopting more sustainable and eco-friendly lifestyles, such as using renewable energy sources, reducing waste, and conserving water. We can also make conscious decisions when it comes to our daily activities, such as using public transportation or carpooling instead of driving alone, and choosing to purchase products that are sustainably produced and packaged.

Another essential step in saving our planet is to protect our natural resources. This includes preserving forests, oceans, and other ecosystems that are home to a vast array of plant and animal species. We can support conservation efforts by supporting organizations that work to protect and preserve these resources, volunteering for environmental causes, and educating ourselves and others about the importance of sustainability.

Reducing pollution is crucial in helping to save our planet. We can reduce pollution by reducing our use of single-use plastics, properly disposing of hazardous waste, and advocating for policies that promote clean energy and reduce emissions from industries and transportation.

We can also help to save Mother Earth by being mindful of our daily habits and behaviors. This includes avoiding excessive consumption of resources, practicing eco-friendly habits at home, and encouraging others to join the movement to protect our planet.

In conclusion, the survival of our planet and the well-being of all its inhabitants depend on our actions today. By taking small, everyday actions to reduce our carbon footprint, protect our natural resources, reduce pollution, and practice sustainable habits, we can all help to save Mother Earth. It is up to us to take responsibility and make a positive impact on our planet, and in doing so, ensure that future generations will have the same opportunity to enjoy the wonders and benefits of our beautiful planet.

28. Importance of Netiquette. (Essay Type 100-150 words)​


Answer:

Netiquette is the etiquette or good manners that one observes in cyberspace. It is a set of rules that people who are online have to follow. In cyberspace we don’t meet people face to face and so new users may not know how to conduct themselves properly when interacting with others. This may give rise to mistakes and misunderstandings. Virginia Shea’s book, ‘Netiquette’ explains the rules of conduct in cyberspace. She identifies 10 important rules to be a good netizen or net citizen.

Rule No. 1 is ‘Remember the Human’. People who are online should remember that they are interacting with real people. They should not say things which offend others. Some people, emboldened by the fact that they are not speaking face to face, may say things that hurt the other person. This is bad netiquette.

Rule No. 2 says that we should display ethical behavior when we go online. One should not transgress the laws of society and cyberspace, thinking that one will not get caught.

30. create an essay containing atleast 150 words earth is only habitable planet ​


A planet’s habitability, or ability to harbor life, results from a complex network of interactions between the planet itself, the system it’s a part of, and the star it orbits. The standard definition for a habitable planet is one that can sustain life for a significant period of time. As far as researchers know, this requires a planet to have liquid water. To detect this water from space, it must be on the planet’s surface. The region around a star where liquid surface water can exist on a planet’s surface is called the “habitable zone.” However, this definition is confined to our understanding of current and past life on Earth and the environments present on other planets. As researchers learn more and discover new environments in which life can sustain itself, the requirements for life on other planets may be redefined.

What makes the Earth habitable? It is the right distance from the Sun, it is protected from harmful solar radiation by its magnetic field, it is kept warm by an insulating atmosphere, and it has the right chemical ingredients for life, including water and carbon. The processes that shape the Earth and its environment constantly cycle elements through the planet. This cycling sustains life and leads to the formation of the mineral and energy resources that are the foundation of modern technological society.
