1. According to the poet, how can one die nobly? (If We Must Die By Claude McKay)


One can die nobly by "dying but fighting back." The author is trying to convince the reader to keep fighting for whatever it is you want. He says that giving up and giving in to things without ever trying it like dying like a hog.

The author wants the reader to live with pride and courage and fight for things they want. 

2. What do the first four lines establish? (If We Must Die By Claude McKay)


If we must die, let it not be like hogs
Hunted and penned in an inglorious spot,
While round us bark the mad and hungry dogs,
Making their mock at our accursèd lot.

The poem, which talks about soldiers being pinned into a difficult position, calls for audience to take arms and not to die like animals being hunted. He implores that the men take up their pride and face death with the courage of man not a cornered animal. 

3. How does the persona describe his enemies? (If We Must Die By Claude McKay)


the persona first describes his enemies as vicious and vile. The persona relates his enemies to monsters that are cruel and cold blooded. Mocking their victims and treating them as animal. At the end of the poem they are then called a “cowardly pack.” Even though at the beginning and at the end of his poem his enemies are evil and murderous the persona calls them cowards and implies that they are only brave in numbers. Ultimately the author then likens them to an animal as to why they are called a pack and calls himself as a man and the enemy an animal even though the enemy at first treats them as animals.

What do the first four lines establish?

========

Lines 1 through 4 establish that the speaker and his allies are under attack. The speaker urges his allies not to give up without a fight. The next four lines draw on the emotions of the allies to die honorably. Lines 9 through 12 contain the speaker's rallying cry to his allies.

2.What message does the persona forward to his allies?

==========

His poem's speaker encourages his allies to not stand by while in battle, but to fight back. Knowing that this would lead to death, the speaker tells those with him that their deaths will be noble, and that they must be men and face those cowards taunting and fighting them.

3. What message does the persona forward to his allies?

========

The persona is a black American who is oppressed, humiliated and persecuted. We know this from the history of the poet and the fact that blacks were hunted, penned (locked in small cells) and killed like hogs (pigs) in America. Hunted and penned in an inglorious spot.

12. Critique the poem ( title: If we must die ) using FORMALIST ( focusing on forms )​​


The poem "If We Must Die" by Claude McKay is a powerful and emotive poetic work that explores themes of endurance, strength, and courage in the face of adversity. Through its use of free verse and metaphor, McKay illustrates the bravery of the unnamed protagonist in his refusal to surrender in the face of an unknown enemy. The poem's subject is universal and timeless, and the author's use of enjambment and prophetic diction creates a sense of urgency and import that captures the poem's solemn message. The poem culminates in a powerful rhetorical question, a chilling yet inspirational call to arms, that demands resolution of the impending conflict and serves to emphasize the strength of the protagonist’s conviction at the poem’s climax. All in all, McKay’s “If We Must Die" is an impressive work of formalist poetry that speaks powerfully to its timeless themes of courage and tenacity.
