Answer:

The advantage of using emotional appeals to reach customers is that it can be effective in creating a connection with the audience and making a lasting impression on them. By tapping into the emotions of the audience, a company can create a sense of relatability and resonance that can persuade them to buy the product or service. In the case of the Subaru commercial, the company was able to appeal to the emotions of parents by showing a realistic portrayal of a father handing over the car keys to his daughter for the first time. This creates a sense of identification for parents who can relate to this experience, and it highlights the safety and reliability of the Subaru brand, which are key factors for many parents when choosing a car for their teenager.

Another advantage of using emotional appeals is that it can help to differentiate the company and its products from competitors. By creating an emotional connection with the audience, the company can stand out in a crowded marketplace and create a sense of loyalty among its customers. This can be especially important for companies that sell products that are seen as similar to those of their competitors, as it can help to establish a unique selling proposition and build a strong brand identity.

However, there are also some potential disadvantages to using emotional appeals to reach customers. One disadvantage is that it can be difficult to predict how the audience will respond to the appeal. Some people may be moved by the emotional appeal, while others may be resistant to it or even turned off by it. This can make it hard for companies to gauge the effectiveness of their emotional appeals and to determine whether they are reaching their target audience effectively.

Another disadvantage of using emotional appeals is that it can be seen as manipulative or insincere if not done correctly. If the company does not genuinely care about the issues it is highlighting or does not have a strong track record of addressing those issues, the emotional appeal may come across as contrived or insincere, which can damage the company's reputation and credibility.

Finally, emotional appeals can be expensive to create and may require significant resources to produce and disseminate. This can be a disadvantage for companies with limited budgets or resources, as it can be difficult to compete with larger companies that have the resources to produce high-quality emotional appeals.
