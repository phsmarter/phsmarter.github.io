8. Which interest you the most, a 2D art forms or 3D art forms? Why?


When it comes to 2d art for me, there is a sense of story telling. What events happened to lead up to the moment the picture captures.

When it comes to 3d art( for me) it’s more about the character’s backstory( since I mostly do characters in 3d), whether I am doing traditional clay sculpting or digital.

I also find that a piece of 2d art I can usually finish in 1 hr- 4 hrs, however when I start a 3d art project I usually spend about 6–25 hrs to finish the piece.

And to be honest I would say I like both. I enjoy switching to a drawing ( traditional paper) after staring at a computer sculpting for a while, or standing up and sculpting in clay for awhile. Sometimes its nice to pick up a pencil and get my story out on a piece of paper, and sometimes I want to lose myself into the anatomy and workings of a 3d character I am creating.

Finally for my final verdict on which I prefer……..

3D art!

For me I prefer 3d just a bit more. I like the complexity of working from all angles, and visualizing how the bones/muscles/fat/skin all come together to form the anatomy of my character. I also get a better feeling of satisfaction after looking at a 3d model I created and the time I spent to create the character. The feeling of working with clay in my hands is also pretty rewarding, and 3d printing my digital models is also a nice bonus if I am working digital.
