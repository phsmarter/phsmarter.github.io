1. what can you say about children who shows rebel to their parents?​


Answer and Explanation:

Children who rebel against their parents often do so when they do not fully understand their parents' reasoning for setting limits and boundaries. Children also rebel to test boundaries, because they are tired of being told what to do all the time, or simply because it is a passing phase. Here are some ways to deal with children who struggle with rebellious behavior.

- Keep calm and be patient: children need time and space to grow up, make mistakes, and learn from them as well. Children learn more and better by doing, not by listening.

- Do not allow children to talk back. This is a classic rebellion tactic. When the child starts to talk back it is because the parent has said "No" or "You must do such and such". Just say "Yes" or "That is correct." If you have tried talking and the child does not respond, try calling for a time out, if necessary: do both for about one minute each.

- Limit what your child does at home as much as possible.

Although it may not seem like it at the time, this sort of behavior is normal and healthy. The key task for young people in their teenage years is to find out who they are and what they stand for. It is almost impossible for a teenager to do this successfully if they just uncritically accept all of their parent's values and ways of thinking.
