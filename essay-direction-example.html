**Essay on the importance of statistics in our daily life:**

Statistics is the study of the collection, organization, analysis, interpretation, and presentation of data. It is a powerful tool that can be used to make sense of the world around us. Statistics can be used to answer questions about everything from the weather to the stock market to the effectiveness of new medical treatments.

In our daily lives, we are constantly bombarded with statistics. We see them in the news, on the internet, and even on the back of cereal boxes. But what do all these numbers mean? And how can we use them to make better decisions?

Statistics can help us to understand the world around us by providing us with a way to quantify information. For example, if we want to know how many people in our city support a particular candidate for mayor, we can use statistics to conduct a poll. This will give us a more accurate picture of public opinion than if we simply asked our friends and family.

Statistics can also help us to make better decisions. For example, if we are trying to decide which car to buy, we can use statistics to compare the fuel efficiency, safety ratings, and price of different models. This will help us to make a more informed decision.

In conclusion, statistics is a powerful tool that can be used to make sense of the world around us and to make better decisions. By understanding statistics, we can become more informed citizens and make better choices in our lives.

Answer:

"The Untouchable Trees" is a selection that provides insight into the intertwined relationship between culture, history, and the environment. The author describes the ancient trees in the redwood forest of California, highlighting their significance in Native American culture and history.

The trees are referred to as "untouchable" because they are considered sacred by the Yurok tribe, who have a long-standing connection with the forest. The author explains how the Yurok people view the forest as a source of spiritual power and the trees as living beings with their own consciousness. This belief system is a significant aspect of their culture and history, dating back centuries.

Moreover, the author portrays the environment as an essential element in the Yurok way of life. The forest is a vital source of food, medicine, and building materials for the tribe, and they have developed sustainable methods of harvesting to preserve the forest's resources.

The author also touches on the destructive impact of European settlement and the exploitation of the forest's resources for commercial gain. This has led to the destruction of many ancient redwood trees and irreparable damage to the environment. This aspect of history is a reminder of the consequences of ignoring the cultural and environmental significance of natural resources.

In conclusion, "The Untouchable Trees" illustrates the intricate connection between culture, history, and the environment. The selection provides a glimpse into the Yurok people's beliefs and practices, highlighting the importance of preserving cultural and natural heritage. The author also sheds light on the consequences of disregarding such heritage, underscoring the need for sustainable practices that benefit both people and the environment.
