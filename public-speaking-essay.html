Answer:B

Explanation:

An editorial, leading article (US) or leader (UK), is an article written by the senior editorial people or publisher of a newspaper, magazine, or any other written document, often unsigned. Australian and major United States newspapers, such as The New York Times[1] and The Boston Globe,[2] often classify editorials under the heading "opinion".

Illustrated editorials may appear in the form of editorial cartoons.[3]

Typically, a newspaper's editorial board evaluates which issues are important for their readership to know the newspaper's opinion on.[4]

Editorials are typically published on a dedicated page, called the editorial page, which often features letters to the editor from members of the public; the page opposite this page is called the op-ed page and frequently contains opinion pieces (hence the name think pieces) by writers not directly affiliated with the publication. However, a newspaper may choose to publish an editorial on the front page. In the English-language press, this occurs rarely and only on topics considered especially important; it is more common, however, in some European countries such as Denmark, Spain, Italy, and France.[5]

Many newspapers publish their editorials without the name of the leader writer. Tom Clark, leader-writer for The Guardian, says that it ensures readers discuss the issue at hand rather than the author.[6] On the other hand, an editorial does reflect the position of a newspaper and the head of the newspaper, the editor, is known by name. Whilst the editor will often not write the editorial themselves, they maintain oversight and retain responsibility.[7]

In the field of fashion publishing, the term is often used to refer to photo-editorials – features with often full-page photographs on a particular theme, designer, model or other single topic, with or (as in a photo-essay) without accompanying text.[8]

1.The argumentative essay is a genre of writing that requires the student to investigate a topic; collect, generate, and evaluate evidence; and establish a position on the topic in a concise manner. Please note: Some confusion may occur between the argumentative essay and the expository essay.

2.made, done, or formed

1 : made, done, or formed on or as if on the spur of the moment : improvised. 2 : composed or uttered without previous preparation : extemporaneous. impromptu.

3.A conclusion is the last part of something, its end or result. ... The phrase in conclusion means "finally, to sum up," and is used to introduce some final comments at the end of a speech or piece of writing.

4.1 : the physical whole of a live or dead person or animal the human body. 2 : the main part of a person, animal, or plant She held her arms tightly against her body. 3 : a human being The resort offered everything a body could want. 4 : the main or central part the body of a letter. 5 : the main part of a motor vehicle.

23. Make a 4-5 essay about the topic: Public Speaking.


Public speaking is the act of addressing a group of people with the intention of conveying information, presenting a point of view, or persuading an audience. Public speaking is an integral part of communication and plays a significant role in various walks of life, including academics, politics, sports, and business. A good public speaker is one who can communicate with clarity, conviction, and authority, and is capable of engaging the audience and leaving a lasting impression.

Public speaking is often considered to be one of the most challenging activities for many people. Fear of public speaking, also known as glossophobia, is a common phenomenon, with many people experiencing anxiety, nervousness, and stress when they are required to speak in front of a crowd. Research shows that the fear of public speaking is more prevalent than the fear of death, height, or spiders. However, with the right skills, strategies, and practice, anyone can become a proficient public speaker and overcome this fear.

A key element of effective public speaking is effective communication. This involves delivering a message that is clear, concise, and impactful. A good public speaker uses a variety of techniques to deliver their message, such as pauses, tone of voice, body language, and eye contact. They also adapt their communication style to suit the needs and preferences of their audience. For instance, when addressing a group of young children, the speaker might use simple language and use visual aids to illustrate their points. However, when addressing a group of business executives, the speaker might use more technical language and incorporate data and statistics to support their message.

Another vital aspect of public speaking is the ability to engage, inspire, and persuade an audience. A good public speaker knows how to capture the attention of the audience and create interest in their message. They use techniques such as storytelling, humor, and analogies to make their message more relatable and memorable. They also use persuasive language and rhetoric to convince the audience to take action, change their behavior, or adopt a new idea.

To become an effective public speaker, individuals need to invest time and effort in developing their skills. The first step is to understand the basics of public speaking, such as preparing and organizing their message, choosing appropriate language, and practicing their delivery. They can also improve their skills by participating in public speaking competitions, attending workshops and training courses, and seeking feedback from mentors or peers.

In conclusion, public speaking is an essential communication skill that is needed in various aspects of life. While the fear of public speaking can be a significant obstacle, with the right mindset, strategies, and practice, anyone can become a proficient public speaker. Effective public speaking requires a combination of effective communication, engagement, inspiration, and persuasion, and the ability to adapt to different audiences and situations. By developing these skills, individuals can enhance their confidence, credibility, and influence and make a lasting impact in any field they choose to pursue.
