10. Magma rises toward Earth's surface due to:a.It is hotter than the surroundings rocksb.It is less dense than the surrounding rocks c.It is more fluid than the surrounding rockd. It is more mafic than the surrounding rocks.​


Answer:

Magma can rise when pieces of Earth's crust called tectonic plates slowly move away from each other. ... Magma also rises when these tectonic plates move toward each other. When this happens, part of Earth's crust can be forced deep into its interior. The high heat and pressure cause the crust to melt and rise as magma.

Explanation:

As they rise, gas molecules in the magma come out of solution and form bubbles and as the bubbles rise they expand. Eventually the pressure from these bubbles is stronger than the surrounding solid rock and this surrounding rock fractures, allowing the magma to get to the surface.
