22. What is the author's thesis in the essay "Letter from Tawi- tawi"?​


"Letter from Tawi-Tawi" is an essay written by Filipino journalist Jose Burgos Jr. that reflects on the conflict and violence in the Southern Philippines. The essay does not have a clear and explicit thesis statement, but rather presents a series of observations and reflections on the author's experiences in Tawi-Tawi.

However, we can discern an implied thesis from the author's writing. The author seems to argue that the conflict in the Southern Philippines is a result of historical, political, and economic factors that have led to widespread poverty, corruption, and violence. The author also suggests that the situation in Tawi-Tawi is representative of larger issues facing the Philippines as a whole.

Throughout the essay, the author describes the daily struggles and challenges faced by the people of Tawi-Tawi, such as the lack of basic resources, poor infrastructure, and rampant corruption. The author also highlights the impact of the conflict on the local population, including displacement, trauma, and loss of life.

Well for me, the essay talks about the author's experience of visiting Tawi-tawi, a small island in the southernmost part of the Philippines. The author describes the beauty of Tawi-tawi's natural environment, the richness of its culture and traditions, and the hospitality of its people. The essay also touches on some of the challenges faced by the island, such as poverty, lack of infrastructure, and the threat of terrorism. Overall, the essay offers a glimpse into the struggles and triumphs of the people who call Tawi-tawi their home, and serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of our global community. While the essay does not provide a solution to the challenges faced by Tawi-tawi, it presents a compassionate and empathetic portrayal of the island and its people, and encourages readers to reflect on their own place in the world.
