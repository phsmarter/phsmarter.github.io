3. what is the potential energy of a 1-kg cat sleeping on the top of 5-m high roof​


[tex]\tt{\huge{\blue{Explanation:}}}[/tex]

The potential energy of an object is given by

[tex]\boxed{PE = mgh}[/tex]

where:

PE = potential energy

m = mass

g = acceleration due to gravity = 9.8 m/s²

h = height

[tex]\tt{\huge{\red{Solution:}}}[/tex]

Solving for PE

PE = mgh

PE = (1 kg)(9.8 m/s²)(5 m)

[tex]\boxed{PE = \text{49 J}}[/tex]

Therefore, the potential energy of the cat is 49 J.

Essentially the glass is cracking to relieve the stress created by that shrinkage. ... Glass expands when hot, contracts when cold.
