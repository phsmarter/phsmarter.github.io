What is a research concept paper?

 

The Concept Paper lays the foundation for the applied dissertation process, providing an introductory form of communication between the doctoral student and the doctoral committee. Essentially, the Concept Paper acts as a tentative proposal; it allows the doctoral student the opportunity to define a research focus and obtain early feedback on the research idea. A well-planned Concept Paper will capture the interest of the dissertation committee and establish a clear plan for the student’s dissertation.

When is the research concept paper written?

 

The Research Concept Paper is completed prior to the dissertation proposal and serves as a development tool and summary of the planned dissertation. The Concept paper is a brief document. Depending upon the requirements of the specific school or academic program, the Concept Paper may range from as few as 2-3 pages to as many as 10-20 pages. The essential point of the Concept Paper is to explain the importance of a particular research project.

29. . What useful information can you get in a subject syllabus that can help you to plan effective lessons? List at least 5 aspects and briefly explain why these aspects are important.


Answer:

A subject syllabus provides valuable information that can help teachers to plan effective lessons. Here are five aspects that can be useful:

1. Course objectives: The course objectives outline the expected outcomes of the subject. By understanding these objectives, teachers can plan lessons that are aligned with the goals of the course.

2. Course outline and schedule: The course outline provides an overview of the topics that will be covered throughout the term or semester, while the schedule indicates when each topic will be taught. This information helps teachers to plan their lessons in a logical and organized way, ensuring that they cover all the necessary material within the allotted time.

3. Required resources and materials: The syllabus may list required textbooks, readings, or other materials that students will need to use during the course. By knowing these requirements in advance, teachers can plan their lessons to make the best use of these resources.

4. Grading criteria: The syllabus often includes information on how students will be graded and the weight of each assessment task. This information can be helpful for teachers to plan lessons that align with the assessment tasks and prepare students for the types of questions they will be asked.

5. Policies and procedures: The syllabus may include information on policies and procedures such as attendance, late work, and academic integrity. By understanding these policies, teachers can plan lessons that encourage students to adhere to these expectations, which can contribute to a positive learning environment.
