5. what is normal policing period​ vs modern policing period


Answer:

°The concept of “police” in the early modern period was far removed from our present understanding. From the end of the Middle Ages as used in legislative texts, the term was used to mean the maintenance of good order, at first frequently in a local or regional context, extending later to a territory or kingdom. It entailed practices of authority notionally described as “police,” associated with the concept of sovereignty and attempting to regulate the relations between individuals. In an era in which kings began to lay down laws defining social norms, this legislation extended to everything but the affairs of the court, the justice system, taxes, and the military. In practice, however, irrespective of the laws passed and contrary to their intention, social order long continued to be established and maintained at local or regional level.

12. Activity 3: Physical Property VS Chemical Property: Is there a difference?PHYSICAL PROPERTYVSCHEMICAL PROPERTY​


A physical property is any property that is measurable, whose value describes a state of a physical system. The changes in the physical properties of a system can be used to describe its changes between momentary states. Physical properties are often referred to as observables. They are not modal properties.A chemical property is any of a material's properties that becomes evident during, or after, a chemical reaction; that is, any quality that can be established only by changing a substance's chemical identity.

30. contemporary art vs modern art


Answer:

Let us differentiate Contemporary from Modern Art:

TIMELINE

Modern art is mostly used for art being created today but that is wrong. Modern art refers to art created at the beginning of the 1880s up to the 1960s. Artworks created after the 1960s to the present time are called Contemporary Art.

ARTISTS

Famous artists during the Modern Art movement were Van Gogh, Salvador Dalí, and Manet.

STYLE

Modern Art focuses on the subjective representation of an artwork's subject. This means it doesn't focus on interpreting realism but rather surrealism. An example of this is Salvador Dalí's" The Persistence of Memory" 1931.

Contemporary art uses both traditional and technological methods. It applies and mixes styles from any art period. Contemporary art has more freedom and liberty in expression and experimenting in styles and techniques. Contemporary art also has more social impact than Modern art.
