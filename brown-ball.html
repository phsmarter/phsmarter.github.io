13. 1. A box contains 4 red balls, 6 blue balls and 8 brown balls. Two balls are drawn at random, what is the probability that the first ball is red, and the second ball is brown without replacement of the ball? ​


Answer:

The odds are 5 in 9.

What may seem strange to you is that, at the time just before you drew the first ball, the odds of drawing a blue ball would have been 6 in 10.

Since the second ball is blue and you haven’t looked at the first ball yet, the odds will have changed after the fact.

To prove it to yourself, immagine that you have drawn 7 balls instead of 2. If the last 6 were blue, what are the odds that the first was blue?

Certainly not 6 in 10.
