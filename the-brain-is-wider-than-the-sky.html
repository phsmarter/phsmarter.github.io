Answer:

The dam. You can see from the diagram that the dam is much thicker at the bottom than it is at the top. This is because the pressure of the water is much greater deeper down and the dam needs to be thick at the bottom so that it is strong enough to withstand this larger pressure.

The reason is that the pressure increases as we go deeper and deeper into the water. So, the pressure is greatest at the bottom of the water. So, to withstand the pressure, dams are made broader at the bottom.

A dam is thicker at the bottom than at the top because the forces exerted are strongest close to the ground. Water pressure increases proportionally with depth, so the lower portion of the dam has greater water pressure exerted upon it. The increasing thickness of the lower dam also helps it support its own weight.
