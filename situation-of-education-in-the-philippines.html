12. Provide situational examples on The Historical Foundation of Education in The Philippines On New Society And The 5th Republic Era


Answer:

The historical foundation of education in the Philippines during the New Society and the 5th Republic era (1972-1986) was marked by significant changes in the educational system. Here are some situational examples:

The implementation of the New Society Education Program: In 1973, President Ferdinand Marcos introduced the New Society Education Program (NSEP), which aimed to provide a more relevant and responsive education system that is geared towards national development. The NSEP emphasized the integration of vocational and technical skills in the curriculum to address the needs of the workforce.

The establishment of the Ministry of Education, Culture and Sports (MECS): In 1978, the Ministry of Education, Culture and Sports was established to oversee the implementation of education policies and programs. This move centralized the management of education in the country and provided a more streamlined approach to education.

The introduction of the Integrated Reorganization Plan: In 1972, the Integrated Reorganization Plan was introduced to restructure the government bureaucracy. Under this plan, the Department of Education, Culture, and Sports was created, and it was tasked to manage the educational system in the country.

The adoption of the 1973 Constitution: The 1973 Constitution provided for free and compulsory elementary education for all Filipinos. This constitutional provision paved the way for the government to prioritize the education sector and provide access to basic education for all.

The implementation of the Bilingual Education Policy: In 1974, the Bilingual Education Policy was introduced to promote the use of Filipino as the medium of instruction in schools. This policy aimed to foster a sense of national identity and pride among Filipinos by promoting the use of the national language

17. Reflect on the current situation of education in the Philippines, in 5 to 7 sentences, write the various ways media technologies are shaping the country's educational system?​


The current situation of education in the Philippines is marked by challenges such as inadequate resources, underfunding, and the digital divide. However, media technologies are playing an increasingly important role in shaping the country's educational system. Online platforms and digital tools are being used to facilitate distance learning and provide access to educational resources. This has become especially crucial during the COVID-19 pandemic, which has forced schools to rely on remote learning. Social media and messaging apps are also being used to communicate with students and parents, and to provide updates and announcements. However, these technological advancements are not without their own set of challenges, such as internet connectivity issues and the need for digital literacy skills among both teachers and students. Nonetheless, the integration of media technologies in education has opened up new possibilities and opportunities for learning in the Philippines.
