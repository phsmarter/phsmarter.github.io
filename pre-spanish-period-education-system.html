24. write an essay discussing the monetary system of the philippines from the pre-spanish regime to the post-war period​


Answer:

The monetary system of the Philippines has undergone significant changes throughout its history, starting from the pre-Spanish regime up to the post-war period. Prior to the arrival of the Spanish colonizers, the Philippines had a barter system where goods and services were exchanged for other goods and services. The barter system was replaced by the use of gold, silver and copper coins known as piloncitos, which were used by the pre-colonial Filipinos in their trade and economic activities.

When the Spanish colonizers arrived in the Philippines in the 16th century, they introduced the use of the Spanish peso, which became the official currency of the Philippines. The Spanish peso was divided into 8 reales, which was further divided into 100 centavos. The Spanish peso was used for more than 300 years, until the United States of America took control of the Philippines in 1898.

After the Philippines became a colony of the United States, the US dollar was introduced as the official currency. The US dollar was divided into 100 cents, and was used in the Philippines until the end of World War II.

After the war, the Philippines became an independent nation and in 1949, the Philippine peso was re-introduced as the official currency. The Philippine peso was divided into 100 centavos, and was pegged to the US dollar at a rate of 2 pesos to 1 dollar. However, the value of the Philippine peso gradually declined due to economic and political instability, resulting in the government's decision to float the peso in the early 1990s.

Today, the monetary system of the Philippines is based on the Philippine peso, which is divided into 100 centavos. The Central Bank of the Philippines, Bangko Sentral ng Pilipinas, is responsible for the issuance, regulation and control of the country's money supply. The monetary system of the Philippines is supported by a robust banking system and a stable economy.

In conclusion, the monetary system of the Philippines has undergone significant changes throughout its history, from the pre-colonial barter system to the use of various currencies such as the Spanish peso, US dollar, and the Philippine peso. The current monetary system is supported by a stable economy and a robust banking system, which helps to maintain economic stability and growth in the country.
