27. Deep desire to master technology is what causes it to slip out of hand.


TECHNOLOGY

  Technology itself, in Heidegger's view, is neither good nor bad; rather, the issue is that technological thinking, or calculative thinking, has come to dominate all other forms of thought. Heidegger observed that modern technology's primary function is frame, or Ge-stell, which literally translates to mean that everything in nature is "standing-reserve" (Bestand). Reality is "exposed" in a particular way by entering into a certain relation with it. And this is where technology enters the picture, as it is the method of revelation that best represents our period. Technology represents a particular mode of world-revealing in which people exert control over reality.

There are two risks associated with contemporary technology:

A threat to human existence whose ontological relationship to reality is reduced to exploitationA threat to human thought because it loses the ability to consider an alternative ontological relationship to the universe.

When Heidegger claims that "the essence of technology is by no means anything technological," he means that neither the machines themselves nor even the various human activities connected to contemporary modes of production are where technology's driving force lies.

29. what are the good effect/causes of disadvantage of technology. Need sagot asap!​


Answer:

Technology has had many positive effects on society, such as improving communication, increasing access to information, and making certain tasks more efficient.

Some specific examples of the benefits of technology include:

1) Improved communication: Technology has made it easier for people to communicate with each other from different parts of the world. Video conferencing, messaging apps, and social media have all made it possible for people to stay in touch with friends, family, and colleagues, no matter where they are.

2) Increased access to information: The internet has made it possible for people to access a wealth of information on any topic imaginable. This has opened up opportunities for education, research, and innovation, as people can easily find the information they need to pursue their goals.

3) Increased efficiency: Technology has made many tasks faster and more efficient. For example, automated manufacturing processes can produce goods more quickly and with fewer errors than manual processes. Similarly, online shopping has made it possible to buy and sell goods without leaving the house, saving time and reducing transportation costs.

However, technology also has its disadvantages, which can include:

1) Dependence: People can become overly reliant on technology and may struggle to complete tasks without it. This can lead to a loss of skills and a reduced ability to problem-solve.

2) Isolation: Technology can also contribute to feelings of isolation, as people spend more time online and less time interacting with others in person.

3) Security risks: As technology becomes more prevalent, there are also increased security risks, such as hacking, identity theft, and cyberbullying.

4) Environmental impact: The production and disposal of technology can have negative environmental impacts, including the use of non-renewable resources and the generation of electronic waste.
