3. essay about scholarship,why do you deserve to be a scholar?​


Essay about scholarship

You can answer why do you deserve to be a scholar by example below:
I deserve this scholarship not because I deserve anything, but because I may provide a great return on your investment. I work hard, am motivated by myself, have achievable goals, and want to contribute to the advancement of society as a whole. Fundamentally, it is about others rather than myself. What can I do to make the world a better place? How can I improve things for others in my own way? Your scholarship will assist me in achieving my goals. My dream is to grow up to be a valuable person who makes a significant contribution to society. Through this scholarship, I will be able to continue my studies and expand my attitude for the improvement of the society.

I have so far gained knowledge about community involvement through volunteer work. My desire to make a greater social contribution in the future with my expertise is stimulated by these activities.

Here are some guidelines for preparing an essay on why you should award you a scholarship:

-Describe how the scholarship funds would help you achieve your long-term objectives.

-Concentrate on the scholarship's purpose. Consider the purpose of the scholarship and its funding source when you write your essay. By demonstrating your prior accomplishment, you might inspire hope for your future success.

-Use a thesis statement in the same way that you would in any other essay. All throughout the essay, you should make references to your argument and weave it into your summary.

-Focus on how you overcame challenges in your life when sharing them. Show your ability to solve problems

-Avoid making generalizations; statements like "I deserve this because I work hard" are insufficient. What distinguishes you as unique, noteworthy, and special?

-Support your argument with a personal success from your life and upbringing.

REASONS ON WHY RIZAL DESERVE TO BE A NATIONAL HERO OF THE PHIPILLINES.

1.) Rizal deserve to be a national hero of the Philippines because he fought for freedom in a silent but effective manner. Rather than using force or violence, he communicated his affection for the Philippines through his novels, essays, and posts.

2.) Rizal deserve to be a national hero of the Philippines because he became the voice of a Filipinos who had been oppressed by Spanish ruler for decades. He was subjected to some of the abuses perpetrated by Spanish friars and government officials as an educated individual. He advocated for reforms of Spanish colonial rule being the founder and member of the La Liga Filipina.

3.) Rizal deserve to be a national hero of the Philippines because he was a very amazing person. He was humble, preferring to fight for reforms through his writings rather than through revolution. Instead of being aggressive, he used his intelligence, talents, and skills in a more peaceful manner.

4.) Rizal deserve to be a national hero of the Philippines because he is a multifaceted person with abilities in both the mental and manipulative realms, not to mention his innate character and love for people, especially to the poor. As evidenced by his sincere services to the people as a doctor, engineer, teacher, artist, farmer, and other professions, his sensitivity to the needs of others was overwhelming.

5.) Rizal deserve to be a national hero of the Philippines because he love the country unselfishly. He took the risk of fighting for freedom even if he knew from the very start that it could lead to his own death.

Historian Ambeth Ocampo writes (often): “Rizal’s greatest misfortune was becoming the National Hero of the Philippines. He is everywhere and therefore nowhere.”

Explanation:

As words or statements lose their meaning when repeated, the same is true for Rizal. Or at least, it seems, to be so on the surface. We know him, what he did, and how he died for it—but do we really know the man behind the myth?

Since we normal, non-academics have no direct access to what he left behind, it can be quite difficult to understand Rizal the man. Based on some secondary and tertiary sources though, I have come to the conclusion that Jose Rizal is not entirely the hero we often think he is.
