14. what are the effects of computer hacking


The common stance on hacking with the average person is that it is morally wrong. There have been several instances where hacking has proven to have caused problems. Hacking can create a variety of damages to people, groups and systems of broad spectrum. Negative Hacking Interactions:

Identity Theft – Some hackers can gain access to sensitive information which could be used to fuel identity theft. This identity theft can cause damages to credit ratings from consumer agencies, run-ins with the law because the person who stole the identity committed a crime, or other damages which may not be repairable at all.

E-mail Access – Hackers have the ability to gain access to personal e-mail accounts. These can have a variety of information and other private files which most people would regard as important. This information could also hold sensitive data which could be used against someone or simply cause ruin for those who are involved in the breach of privacy.

Website Security – Many websites have been victims of hackers. Usually the hackers would simply destroy data and leave the websites in an inoperable state which would leave website owners with the task of rebuilding their sites from scratch if they did not have a backup. This could also pose risks for companies who had their consumer's payment information hosted on their websites. Defacing the websites by leaving tags or "calling cards" stating the unknown group's signature was not uncommon in the early days of hacking websites.

20. effects of use of tablets and computers ​


effects of use of tablets and computers.

[tex] {\huge{\boxed{\tt{{{ANSWER}}}}}}[/tex]

_____________________________________

The use of tablets and computers has become a common phenomenon in the modern age. The rise of technology has made life easier and efficient, improving productivity in people’s lives, which is beneficial. However, tablets and computers have negative effects on the human body and psyche. One of the significant effects of using tablets and computers is eye strain. Prolonged use of these devices can cause eye strain, leading to dry eyes, blurred vision, headaches, and fatigue.

The second effect of using tablets and computers is poor posture, leading to back and neck pain. Most people use these devices for an extended period, and the position of the neck and back tends to be hunched over, which leads to poor posture. The poor posture over time can lead to back pain, neck pain, and stiffness in the spine.

Another critical effect of using tablets and computers is addiction. The internet is full of information, and thus, people tend to spend a lot of time on these devices. The risk of addiction is high, and this addiction can lead to various problems such as sleep disturbance, decreased physical activities, and social isolation. Children are the most vulnerable to addiction since their use of digital devices at a young age is rampant.

Lastly, the use of tablets and computers affects sleep, which is essential for overall health. The blue light emitted from the devices interferes with sleep patterns, making it harder to fall asleep. Exposure to devices before bedtime can lead to sleep disturbance and insomnia, which results in decreased productivity the following day. Therefore, it is advisable to avoid using these devices before bedtime.

In conclusion, while the use of tablets and computers improves productivity and makes life easier, it has negative effects on the human body and psyche. These effects range from eye strain to addiction and sleep disturbance. It is essential to practice moderation and take breaks when using these devices to mitigate the risks.
