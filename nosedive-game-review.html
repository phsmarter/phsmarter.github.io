2. a game review essay.​


Title: "Fortnite Review"

Fortnite has taken the gaming world by storm since its release in 2017. It's a battle royale style game that has players fighting to be the last one standing on an island filled with weapons and resources. The game is free to play, with in-game purchases available for players who want to customize their character or buy in-game currency.

One of the things that sets Fortnite apart from other games is its building mechanic. Players can collect resources from the environment and use them to build structures, which can be used for protection or as a way to gain an advantage in combat. The building aspect adds an extra layer of strategy to the game, as players have to constantly consider their surroundings and how they can use their resources to their advantage.

Another feature that makes Fortnite stand out is its regular updates and events. The developers are constantly adding new weapons, skins, and game modes to keep things fresh and interesting. The events, which often have unique challenges and rewards, are a great way to mix up the gameplay and provide players with new content to keep them coming back.

One of the biggest criticisms of Fortnite is its reliance on microtransactions. While the game is free to play, many players feel that the in-game purchases give an unfair advantage to those who are willing to spend real money. While it's true that players can progress and be competitive without spending any money, the option to buy items and currency can be tempting for some.

Overall, Fortnite is a fun and addicting game that has a lot to offer players of all skill levels. Its building mechanic and regular updates keep things interesting, and the battle royale style of play adds an element of tension and excitement. While the microtransactions can be a point of contention for some players, they are not necessary to enjoy the game.

VIRTUAL WORLD

A virtual world (also called a virtual space) is a computer-simulated environment which may be populated by many users who can create a personal avatar, and simultaneously and independently explore the virtual world, participate in its activities and communicate with others. These avatars can be textual, graphical representations, or live video avatars with auditory and touch sensations. Virtual worlds are closely related to mirror worlds.

VIDEO GAMES

Video games, also known as computer games, are electronic games that involves interaction with a user interface or input device – such as a joystick, controller, keyboard, or motion sensing device – to generate visual feedback. This feedback mostly commonly is shown on a video display device, such as a TV set, monitor, touchscreen, or virtual reality headset. Some computer games do not always depend on a graphics display, for example text adventure games and computer chess can be played through teletype printers. Video games are often augmented with audio feedback delivered through speakers or headphones, and sometimes with other types of feedback, including haptic technology.

27. Print preview allows a review of the document and the implementation of necessary changes before printing. True or False?


Answer:

True.

Explanation:

Print preview is a feature in some software applications that allows users to view their document in the format it will appear when printed. This feature allows users to review the document layout, font styles, images, and other elements before printing, which can help them identify any errors or formatting issues that need to be corrected. Additionally, print preview can also provide users with the option to make adjustments to the document, such as adjusting margins or page orientation, before sending it to the printer. Overall, print preview is useful for ensuring that the document is printed correctly and is ready for distribution or dissemination.

30. THE IMITATION GAME (MOVIE REVIEW)


Answer:

THE IMITATION GAME (MOVIE REVIEW)

The Imitation Game is a 2014 biographical drama film directed by Morten Tyldum and starring Benedict Cumberbatch as Alan Turing, the British mathematician, logician, cryptanalyst and computer scientist who helped crack the Enigma code during World War II. The film follows Turing's life from his school days to his eventual arrest for homosexuality in 1952.

The Imitation Game is an excellent movie that captures the struggles of Alan Turing as he works to crack the Enigma code. Cumberbatch gives a powerful performance as Turing, conveying both his genius and vulnerability. The supporting cast also does an excellent job in their roles, particularly Keira Knightley as Joan Clarke and Mark Strong as Stewart Menzies. The script is well-written and engaging throughout, with plenty of suspenseful moments that keep you on the edge of your seat.

Overall, The Imitation Game is a great movie that tells an important story about one of history's most influential figures. It's well-acted and beautifully shot with some great period detail that brings it all to life. Highly recommended!
