3. my journey in life as a grade 7 scienceplease i need it right now ​


As a grade 7 science student, my journey in life has been one filled with curiosity, discovery, and a passion for understanding the world around me. Science has been an exciting and challenging subject that has opened my mind to the wonders of nature and the laws that govern the universe. Here's a glimpse into my journey:

1. Exploring the Basics: In the early days of grade 7, I began by building a strong foundation in scientific concepts such as the scientific method, matter and energy, cells and their functions, and basic chemistry. I eagerly participated in hands-on experiments, observed the natural world, and learned to ask critical questions about the world around me.

2. Diving into Earth and Space Sciences: I delved into the mysteries of Earth and space, learning about geology, meteorology, astronomy, and ecology. I studied the rock cycle, weather patterns, celestial bodies, and the delicate balance of ecosystems. I was fascinated by the diversity of life on our planet and the vastness of the universe.

3. Unleashing my Inner Chemist: Chemistry became a new fascination as I learned about atoms, elements, compounds, and chemical reactions. I conducted experiments to observe and analyze the properties and behavior of different substances. I marveled at the power of chemical reactions and their impact on our daily lives, from the food we eat to the materials we use.

4. Getting Hands-on with Physics: I also had the opportunity to explore the fundamental principles of physics, such as motion, forces, energy, and electricity. I conducted experiments to understand concepts like velocity, acceleration, and simple machines. I was intrigued by the laws that govern the behavior of objects in motion and the fundamental forces of the universe.

5. Embracing the Scientific Method: Throughout my journey in grade 7 science, I learned the importance of the scientific method – the systematic process of observation, hypothesis formation, experimentation, data analysis, and drawing conclusions. I learned to think critically, analyze data, and communicate my findings effectively.

6. Real-world Applications: I discovered the practical applications of science in everyday life, such as technology, medicine, environmental conservation, and more. I gained an appreciation for the role of science in addressing real-world problems and making a positive impact on society.

7. Building Skills: Along with scientific knowledge, I honed my skills in observation, data collection, analysis, and critical thinking. I learned to work independently and in groups, to collaborate, and to communicate my ideas and findings effectively.

8. Overcoming Challenges: I also faced challenges in my science journey, such as complex concepts, challenging experiments, and problem-solving tasks. But with perseverance, determination, and the support of my teachers and peers, I learned to overcome these challenges and develop resilience.

9. Inspiring Future Adventures: My journey in grade 7 science has ignited a passion for lifelong learning and curiosity about the world around me. It has inspired me to continue exploring the wonders of science and pursue further studies in fields like biology, chemistry, physics, or environmental science.

In conclusion, my journey in life as a grade 7 science student has been an exciting and enriching experience, filled with curiosity, discovery, challenges, and a growing love for the wonders of science. I look forward to continuing my scientific exploration and discovering new frontiers in my future adventures in science.

20. my journey in making my als portfolio​


Answer:

Making an ALS portfolio is an important part of the Alternative Learning System program. It allows you to showcase your learning progress and achievements to potential employers, schools, or other organizations. Here are some steps you can take to make your ALS portfolio:

1. Gather your materials: Start by collecting all of your ALS materials, such as your class notes, handouts, and assignments. You should also include any certificates, awards, or recognition you have received.

2. Organize your materials: Sort your materials by subject or topic and arrange them in a logical order. This will make it easier to find and showcase your work.

3. Create a cover page: Your cover page should include your name, contact information, and a brief introduction about yourself.

4. Include a table of contents: A table of contents will help your reader navigate through your portfolio easily. List all the materials included in your portfolio along with the corresponding page numbers.

5. Write a reflection: Write a reflection about your learning journey in the ALS program. Discuss your challenges, achievements, and how the program has helped you in your personal and professional life.

6. Format your portfolio: Choose a format for your portfolio that suits your needs. You can create a physical or digital portfolio, depending on your preference.

7. Review and edit: Before submitting your portfolio, review and edit it for grammar and spelling errors. Make sure that all the materials are in order and that your reflection is well-written and thought-provoking.

Remember, your ALS portfolio is a reflection of your learning progress and achievements. Take your time in creating it, and make sure that it showcases your best work. Good luck!

26. essay for my journey as a junior highschool​


Answer:

My journey as a junior high school student has been a time of growth, learning, and discovery. During these years, I have had the opportunity to explore new subjects, meet new people, and develop new skills that will serve me well throughout my life.

One of the most significant changes I experienced during my junior high school years was the shift from a more general education to a more specialized curriculum. As I moved from grade school to junior high school, I had the opportunity to study subjects in greater depth, such as science, math, and English. This allowed me to develop a deeper understanding of these subjects and prepare for the more advanced coursework that awaits me in high school and beyond.

Another important aspect of my junior high school journey has been the opportunity to make new friends and expand my social circle. As I entered a new school with new classmates, I had the chance to meet people from different backgrounds and with different interests. This allowed me to broaden my perspective, learn from others, and develop important social skills that will serve me well throughout my life.

Finally, my journey as a junior high school student has been characterized by a commitment to personal growth and development. I have learned the importance of hard work, dedication, and perseverance in achieving my goals, whether that be acing a test or mastering a new skill. I have also learned the value of seeking help when needed, whether that be from teachers, mentors, or peers, and the importance of taking care of my physical and mental health.

Overall, my journey as a junior high school student has been a time of growth, learning, and development. I am grateful for the experiences and opportunities that have come my way during these years and look forward to continuing my journey as I move on to high school and beyond
