1. What is your reflection (introduction, body and conclusion) of the poem "A poem that has no Title" by Jose rizal​


Answer:

Introduction:

"A Poem that has no Title" is a poem written by Jose Rizal, the Philippine national hero. It is a melancholic poem that expresses Rizal's emotions of sadness and loneliness. The poem is a reflection of the author's feelings towards life and how he is searching for something that will bring him true happiness.

Body:

The poem "A Poem that has no Title" is composed of four stanzas, each containing four lines. The first stanza talks about the author's search for a happiness that seems elusive. He expresses his longing for a place where he can find peace and joy.

In the second stanza, Rizal describes how he feels trapped and helpless in his current situation. He wishes to break free from the constraints of life and find a way to be happy. The third stanza talks about Rizal's disillusionment with love and how it has failed to bring him the happiness he seeks.

In the final stanza, Rizal reflects on his own mortality and the fleeting nature of life. He acknowledges that all things must come to an end and that happiness is something that is transient.

Conclusion:

"A Poem that has no Title" by Jose Rizal is a poignant poem that reflects the author's feelings of sadness and despair. It is a reflection of the struggles of life and the search for happiness. The poem is a reminder that life is fleeting, and that happiness is something that we must find within ourselves. Jose Rizal's poem speaks to the human experience of searching for meaning in life, and the emotions that come with that search.
