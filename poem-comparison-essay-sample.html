13. An extended metaphor is a feature of a lyric poem that presents comparison over several lines throughout the poem.​


Answer:

While an extended metaphor is commonly used in lyric poetry, it is not exclusive to that genre. An extended metaphor is a literary device that is used to compare two unlike things in greater depth and detail over multiple lines, stanzas, or even an entire work. It is an extended version of a simple metaphor that continues throughout a poem or a story to create a more complex and layered meaning.

The purpose of an extended metaphor is to provide a deeper understanding of the subject being described by drawing comparisons between it and something else. It allows the writer to explore complex ideas or emotions by using a tangible and relatable comparison.

While extended metaphors are commonly used in lyric poetry, they can also be found in other forms of literature, such as novels, plays, and essays.

18. make an essay about social comparison​


Social comparison is a natural human tendency to evaluate one's own abilities, opinions, and worth based on the perceived characteristics of others. This behavior can lead to feelings of insecurity and inadequacy, especially when comparing oneself to others who appear to be more successful or talented. It is important to remember that comparisons are often based on limited information and can be distorted by personal biases, making them an unreliable source of self-evaluation.

Answer:

Youth Yesterday:

In the past, youth was often associated with innocence and carefree living. The youth of yesterday was more focused on living in the present moment and enjoying their youth. They were less exposed to technology and social media, so they spent more time outdoors, playing sports, and engaging in social activities with their peers. Young people of yesterday had a more traditional outlook on life and tended to follow the path set by their parents or society.

Youth Today:

The youth of today are more tech-savvy and connected than ever before. They have access to a vast range of information and opportunities through the internet and social media. With the advent of smartphones, social media, and instant messaging, young people today are more connected than ever before. They are more likely to have a diverse set of interests and a more individualistic outlook on life. Youth today are more likely to question authority and challenge traditional norms.

Comparison:

Both youth yesterday and today have a strong desire for social interaction and forming connections with their peers.The youth of yesterday and today have a desire to explore and learn new things.

Contrast:

The youth of today are more tech-savvy and have access to more information and opportunities than youth yesterday.Youth today have a more individualistic outlook on life and are more likely to question authority and challenge traditional norms than youth yesterday.

Alliteration is the repetition of the beginning sounds of neighboring words.
