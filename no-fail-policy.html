Answer: In recent years, there has been a growing trend in educational institutions towards implementing a pass, no fail policy. Under this system, students are no longer given letter grades, but instead are simply assessed as passing or failing the course. While some have criticized this approach as "coddling" students, there are a number of compelling arguments in favor of the pass, no fail policy.

One of the key benefits of a pass, no fail policy is that it reduces stress and anxiety among students. When students are graded on a letter scale, they often become fixated on achieving the highest grade possible, even at the expense of their mental health and wellbeing. By removing the pressure to achieve top marks, the pass, no fail policy allows students to focus on their learning and development, rather than on simply getting good grades.

Another benefit of a pass, no fail policy is that it encourages risk-taking and experimentation. When students are not penalized for making mistakes, they are more likely to try new things and take risks in their learning. This can lead to greater creativity and innovation, as students are able to explore new ideas and approaches without fear of failure.

Furthermore, the pass, no fail policy promotes a more equitable and inclusive educational environment. Letter grades can often reflect socioeconomic factors or other privileges that are outside of a student's control, rather than their actual performance in the course. By eliminating letter grades, the pass, no fail policy levels the playing field and allows students from all backgrounds to succeed based on their own efforts and abilities.

Finally, the pass, no fail policy recognizes that learning is a lifelong process, and that grades are not the only measure of success. Under this system, students are able to focus on their own personal growth and development, rather than on competing with their peers for top grades.

In conclusion, the pass, no fail policy offers a number of compelling advantages over traditional grading systems. By reducing stress and anxiety, encouraging risk-taking, promoting equity and inclusivity, and emphasizing lifelong learning, this policy can help to create a more positive and supportive educational environment for students.

14. argumentative essay for No fail policy​


ARGUMENTATIVE ESSAY FOR NO FAIL POLICY

Every civilization needs a strong educational system since it helps to mold the next generation. Giving pupils the information, skills, and attitudes necessary for success in their personal and professional life is a core aim of the educational system.

However, the emphasis on grades in the conventional educational model frequently creates a competitive and stressful environment for pupils. Some educational institutions have put in place a no-fail policy to address this problem.

The no-fail rule is advantageous for pupils' overall growth, according to this argument.

Grades are heavily emphasized in the old educational system, which has created a culture of rivalry among pupils. As a result, adolescents frequently experience extreme pressure to perform well in school, which is bad for their mental health and general wellbeing.

This strain will be lessened by the no-fail policy, which also intends to foster a helpful and cooperative learning atmosphere.

Eliminating the fear of failure encourages pupils to attempt new things and take chances, which can foster increased ingenuity and creativity.

The no-fail rule also gives pupils a second chance to grow academically. Students who fail a topic are frequently put behind in the traditional educational system, which halts their study.

The no-fail policy makes sure that students have the assistance and materials they need to succeed. Students may concentrate on their study and progress rather than the worry of failing thanks to this strategy.

A more inclusive and equal educational system cannot be established without the no-fail rule. Systemic impediments frequently prevent students from underprivileged groups from achieving academic success.

The no-fail policy reduces these obstacles by fostering a more encouraging and welcoming learning environment. Academically challenged students are given the same chance to thrive and reach their full potential as other students.

The no-fail policy is criticized for failing to adequately educate students for life in the real world, where failure is a common occurrence. This argument, however, disregards the fact that students are nonetheless held accountable for their work despite the no-fail policy.

23. balita tungkol sa (NO FAIL POLICY)​


DepEd, 'di pabor sa panukalang "no fail policy" ngayong school year

- Hindi sang-ayon ang Department of Education sa panukalang "No fail policy" para sa kasalukuyang panuruang taon

- Sinasabing ang naturang panukala ay upang mabawasan ang stress ng mga estudyante habang nag-aaral ngayong pandemya

- Ayon sa DepEd, kinailangang ipaunawa sa mga bata na kinakailangang seryosohin pa rin ang pag-aaral

- Sinabi rin ng naturang kagawaran na mas mainam na bigyan pa rin ng marka ang mga mag-aaral upang malaman nilang ito ay ayon sa kanilang ginagawa

Hindi pabor ang Department of Education sa panukalang "no fail policy" para sa panuruang taong 2020-2021.

Nalaman ng sila nang nasabing rekomendasyon ay naglalayong mabawasan ang stress ng mga estudyante na patuloy na nag-aaral ngayong panahon ng pandemya.

Subalit sa panayam kay DepEd Undersecretary Diosdado San Antonio ng GMA News, sinabi nitong hindi patas kung lahat ng mga estudyante ay ipapasa na lamang.

"Kailangang maunawaan ng mga bata na ang pag-aaral ay kailangan ng effort at sineseryoso kaya ang paniniwala namin ay kailangan ng marka, numerical pa rin ang grading,” ayon kay San Antonio.

Dagdag pa niya, mayroong mga mag-aaral na todo ang effort sa pag-aaral, pagsasagot ng mga learning modules at pagdalo ng online classes habang mayroon ding mga hindi ito sineseryoso.

Nilinaw din ng DepEd na nauunawaan nila ang motibo ng naturang panukala subalit mas mainam na motibasyon umano sa mga bata kung bibigyan ng grado ang kaukulang ginawa ng mga mag-aaral para sa kasalukuyang panuruang taon.

“Hindi naman pwedeng lahat ay papasa. 'Yung talagang nagpapakita ng interes at ginagawa ang lahat pero nahihirapan ay bibigyan ng tulong at atensyon ng mga guro,” paliwanag pa ng DepEd official.

Hindi pa rin binuksan ang mga paaralan para sa face-to-face classes kaya naman naghanda ang Kagawaran ng Edukasyon ng iba't ibang pamamaraan ng pagkatuto tulad ng online classes at modular learning.
