3. what is the five native american music?​


Answer:

Native American Musical Styles And Genres

Aspects of style

The following discussion of styles and genres by region addresses a number of characteristics of music and how they are produced.

North America

North American Indians (i.e., those in present-day Canada and the United States) emphasize singing, accompanied by percussion instruments such as rattles or drums, rather than purely instrumental music.

Eastern Woodlands

In terms of musical characteristics, the Eastern Woodlands area stretches from New Brunswick, Canada, south to the Gulf of Mexico and from the Mississippi River east to the Atlantic Ocean.

Plains

The Plains area extends from Texas north to south-central Canada and from the Rocky Mountains east to the Mississippi River.

Cheyenne River Sioux

A Cheyenne River Sioux troupe in traditional dress singing and dancing at the Native Nations Procession, Washington, D.C., 2004.

Pablo Martinez Monsivais/AP

Great Basin

Tribes such as the Shoshone, Paiute, Washo, and Ute live in the Great Basin area, which reaches from the Colorado River Basin north to the Fraser River in British Columbia, Canada, and from the Rocky Mountains west to the Sierra Nevada and Cascade Range.

Southwest

The Southwest region, which includes New Mexico, Arizona, and southern California, is home to traditionally sedentary Pueblo Indians, such as the Hopi and Zuni, as well as to tribes that were traditionally transhumant (seasonally moving), such as the Navajo.

Northwest Coast

The Northwest Coast area covers a thin strip about 100 miles (160 km) wide between the Pacific Ocean and the coastal mountains of the United States and Canada, extending from northern California to the Alaska panhandle.

Arctic

Many independent but related communities occupy the Arctic region, which reaches from Alaska across northern Canada to Greenland. Inuit or Eskimo peoples such as the Netsilik, Copper, Iglulik, and Baffin Islanders inhabit the Arctic area.

21. 5 example of native American indian music​


Answer:

Native American Indian music is diverse and rich in tradition, with many different styles and instruments used across the various Indigenous cultures in North America. Here are five examples of Native American Indian music:

Explanation:

1.) Powwow music: Powwows are traditional Native American gatherings that often feature singing, drumming, and dancing. Powwow music is typically performed by drum groups, who play a variety of drums and sing in Native languages.

2.) Flute music: The Native American flute is a traditional instrument that is used in many Indigenous cultures. Flute music is often played as a solo instrument or in combination with other instruments, and it is often used in spiritual and ceremonial contexts.

3.) Round dance music: Round dances are traditional Native American social dances that feature singing and drumming. Round dance music is typically upbeat and energetic, and it is played on a variety of instruments, including drums, rattles, and shakers.

4.) Traditional music of the Hopi: The Hopi people of the Southwest United States have a rich tradition of music that includes vocal and instrumental pieces, as well as traditional dances. Hopi music often features the use of flutes, drums, and rattles, and it is often used in ceremonial and spiritual contexts.

5.) Traditional music of the Lakota: The Lakota people of the Great Plains region have a rich tradition of music that includes vocal and instrumental pieces, as well as traditional dances. Lakota music often features the use of drums, rattles, and flutes, and it is often used in ceremonial and spiritual contexts.
