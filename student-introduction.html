Here are some ideas for an introduction about working students in a PowerPoint presentation:

Start with a quote or a startling fact that captures the attention of your audience. For example, you could use a quote such as "I have never let my schooling interfere with my education" by Mark Twain to highlight the challenges faced by working students.

Give an overview of the topic and its significance. Introduce the idea of working students, and why it is an important topic to discuss. Explain that many students have to balance work and school, and this can be a significant challenge that affects their academic performance and well-being.

By starting with a strong introduction, you can capture the attention of your audience and set the stage for an engaging and informative presentation.

Because these needed requirements for effectiveness have been largely absent for many, remote education during the pandemic has impeded teaching and learning.

Research on home schooling shows that it works well for students for whom intentional, personalized, and sufficient resources are available. The crisis-induced delivery of home schooling without time for planning around children’s learning styles and circumstances means that many children home schooled during the pandemic are not replicating such model and thus not reaping the associated benefits.

Reduced learning time has likely impeded student learning and also affected the development of the whole child. Once the pandemic allows it, we will need to make up for this time by increasing both the amount and quality of learning time—through extended schedules, summer enrichment and after-school activities, more personalized instruction, and staffing strategies that reduce class sizes and staff schools with sufficient and highly credentialed educators.

Research on chronic absenteeism and on remote learning reinforces the urgency of providing appropriate support to children who are least prepared and especially to those at risk of becoming disengaged and eventually dropping out.

Research on summer learning (loss or gain) points to the importance of personalized instruction. The research shows that learning styles and outcomes vary greatly, and that the outcomes are a function of the educational resources that families and systems provide to children across the year and of a large number of factors and circumstances that shape children’s learning and development.

Research shows that a lack of contingency planning exacerbates the negative impacts of recessions, natural disasters, and pandemics on learning. Contingency planning thus needs to be institutionalized and include emergency funding to replenish the resources drained during emergencies.

26. related study introduction body of psychological needs of student​


Answer:

Introduction:

The psychological needs of students have been the subject of significant research in the field of psychology. Understanding these needs is crucial for educators and parents to provide an environment that fosters learning and growth. This study aims to explore the psychological needs of students and their impact on academic performance, mental health, and overall well-being.

Body:

Psychological needs are the basic requirements that individuals must fulfill to achieve optimal psychological functioning. In the context of students, psychological needs refer to the fundamental needs that students require to feel motivated, engaged, and successful in their academic pursuits. The three primary psychological needs identified by Self-Determination Theory (SDT) are autonomy, competence, and relatedness.

Autonomy refers to the need for students to feel in control of their learning and to have a sense of choice and volition. Competence refers to the need for students to feel capable and effective in their academic pursuits. Relatedness refers to the need for students to feel connected and valued by others, particularly peers and teachers.

Research has shown that satisfying these psychological needs is essential for students to thrive academically and psychologically. Students who feel a sense of autonomy, competence, and relatedness are more likely to be intrinsically motivated and engaged in their learning, leading to higher academic performance and better mental health outcomes.

Furthermore, when students' psychological needs are not met, they may experience negative outcomes such as anxiety, depression, and disengagement from school. For example, students who feel a lack of autonomy may feel disengaged and unmotivated, while those who feel a lack of competence may experience anxiety and self-doubt.

In conclusion, understanding the psychological needs of students is essential for creating a positive learning environment that promotes academic success and overall well-being. By satisfying students' autonomy, competence, and relatedness needs, educators and parents can help students achieve their full potential and lead fulfilling lives.
