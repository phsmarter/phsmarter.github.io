SEPARATION OF STATE AND CHURCH EXPLAINED

The separation of church and state is not only understood, it is also very important. That is perhaps one of the few points where everyone on all sides of the debate can easily agree that their reasons for agreeing may be different, but they agree that the separation of church and state is one of the fundamental constitutional principles in Philippine History.

This is important because it emphasizes the fact that, in church and state diversity, we cannot talk about a single, literal church and a single, literal state. These words are metaphorical, meaning pointing to something larger. The church should be represented as any organized religious body with its doctrines / dogmas, and the state should be described as any governmental body, any government -run organization, or any government -supported event.
