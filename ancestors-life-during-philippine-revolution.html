Explanation:

ELITE (ILUSTRADOS) DURING SPANISH COLONIALIZATION:

Theoretically, apart from death, education is the "great social equalizer." As a result, the rich and the poor have equal opportunities in society. There is also a belief that in a conscious and educated society, education is also the best advocate of democracy.

The occupier belittled local knowledge while spreading conservative and pseudo-scientific knowledge in church-run schools. The teaching of metaphysics continues even when the laws of motion of matter are fully understood. The teaching of science is also not complete like the example shown in Rizal's El Filibusterismo which makes the experimental equipment only decoration.

The education implemented by the Spaniards was elitist. Education was denied to the indios. The majority of Filipinos were nailed to the ground and not given the opportunity to study. According to Jose Rizal, "the root of ignorance and poverty in the Philippines is the lack of education and knowledge, which is a disease from birth to the end." He criticized the Spaniards and the friars for not meeting the needs of the Filipinos because there were not even books on Philippine history, geography, morality based on its experience in a feudal system. The student became detached from his or her study.

Instead of arms, the elites used propaganda and established La Solidaridad.

Rizal symbolised the elite but he was not originally elite (like rich person).

MASSES DURING THE SPANISH COLONISATION

The mass were the arms of the Ilustrados. They took the fight through weapon when the elite's powers are not enough.

Through armed struggle, the Spanish government did not underestimate Filipinos.

But they were badly led. One of the most highlighted revolutions is the Cavite Revolt. In particular, rebels in Cavite led by Mariano Álvarez and Baldomero Aguinaldo (who were leaders from two different factions of the Katipunan) won early major victories.

Then there's the establishment of KKK ( Kataastaasan, Kagalanggalangang Katipunan ng mga Anak ng Bayan).

In the course of our history, women have played significant roles. Before the coming of the colonizers, they made their presence felt by becoming leaders in society or the barangay.
