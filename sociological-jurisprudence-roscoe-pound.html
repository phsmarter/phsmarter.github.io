2. what is jurisprudence


Answer:

Jurisprudence or legal theory is the theoretical study of law. Scholars of jurisprudence seek to explain the nature of law in its most general form and provide a deeper understanding of legal reasoning, legal systems, legal institutions, and the role of law in society.

4. During an experiment, Roscoe tossed a fair coin 10 times and got 6 "heads". What is the probability the coin will land showing heads on Roscoe's next toss?


Answer:

The coin is fair, which means that the probability of getting heads or tails is the same for each toss. Therefore, the probability of getting heads on any given toss is 1/2 or 0.5.

Step-by-step explanation:

The outcome of previous coin tosses does not affect the probability of the next toss. Each toss is an independent event, so the probability of getting heads on the next toss is still 1/2 or 0.5.

Therefore, the probability of Roscoe getting heads on his next toss is 0.5 or 50%.
