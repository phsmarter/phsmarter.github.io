Answer:

The poetic persona of the poem is not explicitly stated, but it is likely the author herself, Edith Tiempo.The line that is repeated in the first and second stanza is "All that I love". The difference in meaning between the two is that in the first stanza, it seems to be a rhetorical question, while in the second stanza, it is being affirmed that all the things mentioned are indeed things that the speaker loves.The line "Till seashells are broken pieces/From God's own bright teeth" implies that even the smallest and seemingly insignificant things in life are evidence of God's great love. This is seen in the beauty and intricacy of seashells, which are created by God. Examples of stories that affirm God's love could include the story of Jesus sacrificing himself for humanity or the story of the prodigal son in the Bible.Love is being portrayed as something that is cherished and valued, and that can be kept and treasured in small, physical objects.In the third stanza, the speaker mentions scaling down love to emphasize the importance of keeping memories and cherished objects in a compact and tangible form. Scaling down love allows the speaker to keep all that she loves with her at all times, making it easier to remember and cherish the memories associated with those objects. Scaling down love can be done in different ways depending on the individual. For example, keeping sentimental items in a small box or container, carrying a locket or other small keepsake, or even just taking photographs of special moments or objects can help scale down love into something tangible and manageable.
