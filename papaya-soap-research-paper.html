17. Write the steps in making Pickled Papaya using transition/signal words. Do it in a whole sheet of paper.​


Answer:

First, combine water and 4 tablespoons of salt in a mixing bowl. Stir.

Add the shredded papaya. Cover and place inside the refrigerator. Soak for 2 days.

Second, put the papaya in cheesecloth in a way that it is trapped in the center. Gently rotate the cheesecloth while tightly holding one end. Continue to rotate until the papaya completely releases liquid. Do this more than once for better results.

Then arrange the papaya in a mixing bowl. Add all the vegetables and raisins. Toss and set aside.

Next is make the syrup by heating the vinegar in a cooking pot. Once it starts to boil, add sugar and 1 teaspoon of salt. Mix well and cook for 1 to 3 minutes.

After it put the papaya mixture in a mason jar. Do not completely fill the jar, leave a little space for the syrup.

Gently pour the hot syrup into each jar. Quickly close the lid. This will somewhat cook the papaya and veggies and will make it a bit softer.

FInally let it cool and then refrigerate, or simply store on a shelf at room temperature, Lastly, serve with Lechon Kawali and Crispy Pata.

20. rrls research topic papaya 2010 ​


I will provide you...3 RRLs about papaya in APA format citations:

1. According to a study by Siddiqui et al. (2017) investigated the potential health benefits of papaya leaf extract. The study found that the extract had antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties, and may have potential as a treatment for conditions such as dengue fever and cancer. Siddiqui, M. J., Siddiqui, A. A., Ahmad, S., Ahsan, H., & Arshad, M. (2017). Papaya leaf extract: A potent anticancer agent. Journal of Ethnopharmacology, 210, 80-85. https://doi.org/10.1016/j.jep.2017.07.025

2. A study by Silva et al. (2014) investigated the potential of papaya seed extract as a natural alternative to chemical preservatives in food products. The study found that the extract had antimicrobial activity against a range of foodborne pathogens, and could be used to extend the shelf life of certain food products. Silva, T. F., Costa, E. M., Melo, E. A., & Silva, M. V. (2014). Natural antimicrobial agents for preservation of food products: Papaya seeds extract as a case study. Food Research International, 56, 13-20. https://doi.org/10.1016/j.foodres.2013.11.025

3. Another study by Choudhary et al. (2017) investigated the potential of papaya leaf extract as a natural remedy for dengue fever. The study found that the extract had antiviral properties against dengue virus and could reduce the severity of symptoms in patients with dengue fever. Choudhary, N., Sekhon, B. S., & Sachdeva, K. (2017). Antiviral activity of Carica papaya (Linn.) against dengue virus type 2 in vitro. International Journal of Current Microbiology and Applied Sciences, 6(3), 2145-2154. https://doi.org/10.20546/ijcmas.2017.603.245
