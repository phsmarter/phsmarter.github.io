11. How the argumentative essay differs from the other types?​


Answer:

An argumentative essay contains a combination of facts and writer's personal ideasThe main difference between argumentative and informative essays is that an informative essay contains only information to explain a topic, whereas an argumentative essay contains statistics, facts and writer's personal ideas.

Explanation:

An argumentative essay tends to be a longer essay involving independent research, and aims to make an original argument about a topic. Its thesis statement makes a contentious claim that must be supported in an objective, evidence-based way.

An expository essay also aims to be objective, but it doesn’t have to make an original argument. Rather, it aims to explain something (e.g., a process or idea) in a clear, concise way. Expository essays are often shorter assignments and rely less on research.

24. III. ESSAY TYPE. Directions: Define the ff. 1. PERSUASION 2. ARGUMENT 3. ESSAY ​


Answer:

Explanation:

1. Persuasion refers to the act of convincing or influencing someone to believe or act in a certain way. It involves presenting compelling arguments, evidence, and appeals to emotions or logic to sway the opinion or behavior of the person or audience. The goal of persuasion is to get someone to adopt a specific viewpoint, take a particular action, or change their behavior or attitude.

2. An argument is a logical and coherent series of statements or claims used to support or refute a proposition or point of view. It involves presenting evidence and reasoning to convince someone that a particular claim is true or false. Arguments can be expressed in various forms such as debates, essays, speeches, and discussions.

3. An essay is a piece of writing that presents and defends a particular point of view, idea, or argument. It is a structured and formal piece of writing that typically includes an introduction, body paragraphs, and a conclusion. Essays can be persuasive, informative, descriptive, or narrative in nature. They are often used in academic contexts to assess students' knowledge, critical thinking, and writing skills. Essays can cover a wide range of topics and may involve research, analysis, and evaluation of sources.
