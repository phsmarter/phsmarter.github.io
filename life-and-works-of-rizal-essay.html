3. ESSAY ABOUT HOW DID OUR SUBJECT ON LIFE AND WORKS OF RIZAL CHANGED YOU? ​


Explanation:

The study of the life and works of Jose Rizal has had a profound impact on my understanding of Philippine history and my own identity as a Filipino.

Before studying Rizal, I had a limited understanding of the events that led to the Philippines becoming a colony of Spain, and the subsequent struggle for independence. Through reading Rizal's writings, such as "Noli Me Tangere" and "El Filibusterismo", I gained a deeper understanding of the injustices and oppression that the Filipino people faced during that time. Rizal's writings not only exposed the injustices and corruption of the Spanish colonial government, but also highlighted the resilience and determination of the Filipino people to fight for their rights.

Rizal's writings also helped me to better understand the Philippines' cultural identity. Rizal was a true patriot, and his works were infused with pride in the Philippines' unique culture and history. Through his writings, I learned about the customs, traditions, and beliefs of the Philippines, which helped me to appreciate my own heritage.

In addition, Rizal's writings also helped me to develop a sense of social responsibility. Rizal was not only a writer, but also a reformer who advocated for the rights of the Filipino people. His example of standing up for what is right, even in the face of oppression, has inspired me to become more engaged in the issues that affect my community and to take action to make a difference.

In conclusion, the study of the life and works of Jose Rizal has changed me in many ways. It has broadened my understanding of Philippine history and culture, and has inspired me to become more socially responsible. Rizal's legacy continues to be a source of inspiration for all Filipinos, and I am proud to be a part of that legacy.

5. CHOSEN CAREER the Rizal course Considering that tertiary curriculum, try to relate the is mandatory Rizal's life, works and writings to your importance chosen program component of studying or career. ESSAY.​


Jose P. Rizal

BECAUSE IT IS MANDATED BY LAW The teaching of Jose Rizal's life, works, and writings is mandated by Republic Act 1425, otherwise known as the Rizal Law. To gain an inspiring source of patriotism through the study of Rizal's life, works, and writings.

Jose P. Rizal or better known as Pepe was a Filipino reformist for a free society and not a revolutionary seeking independence. As head of the Movement for Change in the Philippines founded by Filipinos in Barcelona, ​​Spain, he contributed to La Solidaridad.

The goals:that the Philippines become a part-province of Spain;to have representatives in the Spanish Legislature (Parliament);to have Filipino priests in charge or to have secularization;freedom of assembly and press;equal rights before the law, whether Filipino or Spanish.

The powerful officials could not accept those changes, because it meant the loss of Spanish dominance. So when Rizal returned to Manila from Spain, he was accused of sowing trouble for the establishment of La Liga Filipina, tried and exiled to the City of Dapitan/Dapitan, Zamboanga del Norte/Zamboanga in 1892. There, he built a school children's, and a hospital. In addition, he also implemented a municipal drainage system.
