17. What are some injustices caused by war today and where in the world is this injustice still happening in?


Answer:

War is a devastating event that can cause numerous injustices, both in the short and long term. Some of the injustices caused by war today include the following:

1. Displacement: War often results in the displacement of millions of people who are forced to leave their homes and communities behind. These displaced individuals often have to leave everything they own and rely on humanitarian aid to survive. The situation is made worse by the fact that many governments do not have the resources or the willingness to provide adequate support and protection for these displaced populations.

2. Human Rights Violations: War can lead to severe human rights violations, including extrajudicial killings, torture, and forced disappearances. Civilians and non-combatants are often the primary victims of these abuses, and they are seldom held accountable.

3. Destruction of Infrastructure: War can cause vast destruction of infrastructure, including hospitals, schools, and homes. This destruction can have long-term consequences, including the loss of access to essential services and the displacement of people from their homes.

4. Environmental Damage: War can cause significant environmental damage, including the destruction of ecosystems and the contamination of soil and water sources. This damage can have long-term consequences for both the environment and the health of the people living in affected areas.

Explanation:

Currently, there are several regions of the world where these injustices are still happening, including Syria, Yemen, Afghanistan, and Myanmar. In Syria, the ongoing civil war has resulted in massive displacement, severe human rights violations, and the destruction of infrastructure. In Yemen, the ongoing conflict has caused a humanitarian crisis, with millions of people facing starvation and limited access to healthcare. In Afghanistan, the ongoing war has resulted in the displacement of millions of people and severe human rights violations, including the targeting of civilians by armed groups. In Myanmar, the military coup and subsequent crackdown on pro-democracy protesters have resulted in human rights violations, including extrajudicial killings and the displacement of hundreds of thousands of people.
