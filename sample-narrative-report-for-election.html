Situation:A population of PWD learners in 5 schools in a certain mınicipality of Quezon Province are (r)9, 5, 6, 12, and 15. Suppose that two scho as samples, determine the mean and variance of the sampling distri mean. elected sample​


Answer:

Step-by-step explanation:

To compute for the mean and variance of the sampling distribution of the sample mean, we need to follow these steps:

Step 1: Compute the population mean (μ) and population standard deviation (σ) using the given population data.

μ = (9 + 5 + 6 + 12 + 15)/5 = 9.4

To compute for the population standard deviation, we first need to compute for the deviations of each data point from the population mean, square each deviation, sum up the squared deviations, divide by the population size, and then take the square root of the result:

Deviation from the mean: (9 - 9.4), (5 - 9.4), (6 - 9.4), (12 - 9.4), (15 - 9.4)

Squared deviations: 0.16, 18.36, 11.56, 7.84, 31.36

Sum of squared deviations: 69.28

Variance: 69.28/5 = 13.856

Standard deviation: sqrt(13.856) = 3.719

Therefore, the population mean (μ) is 9.4 and the population standard deviation (σ) is 3.719.

Step 2: Randomly select two samples from the population and compute their sample means.

Suppose we randomly select two samples of size 2 from the population. Let's say we get the following samples:

Sample 1: 5, 9

Sample 2: 12, 15

To compute the sample means, we simply add up the values in each sample and divide by the sample size:

Sample 1 mean: (5 + 9)/2 = 7

Sample 2 mean: (12 + 15)/2 = 13.5

Step 3: Compute the mean and variance of the sampling distribution of the sample mean.

To compute the mean (μx̄) and variance (σx̄^2) of the sampling distribution of the sample mean, we use the following formulas:

μx̄ = μ

σx̄^2 = σ^2/n

where n is the sample size.

Substituting the values, we get:

μx̄ = 9.4

σx̄^2 = 3.719^2/2 = 6.925

Therefore, the mean (μx̄) of the sampling distribution of the sample mean is 9.4 and the variance (σx̄^2) is 6.925.
