3. Social, political, economic and cultural issues in the Philippine history Mandated topic: Agrarian Reform Policies


Agrarian reform has been a contentious issue in the Philippines for much of its history. The country's largely agricultural economy has been characterized by a small group of wealthy landowners controlling the majority of fertile land, while the vast majority of farmers have been left with little or no land of their own. This has led to widespread poverty and social unrest, as well as conflict between farmers and landowners.

The Philippine government has attempted to address this issue through various agrarian reform policies over the years. One of the first such policies was the Land Reform Act of 1955, which aimed to distribute land to tenant farmers and small landholders. However, this policy was largely ineffective and was later replaced by the Comprehensive Agrarian Reform Program (CARP) in 1988.

CARP was intended to be a comprehensive program that would provide land to millions of landless farmers, as well as support for land acquisition and development. However, the implementation of CARP has been slow and has faced numerous challenges, including resistance from landowners and a lack of resources.

Despite these challenges, agrarian reform remains an important issue in the Philippines, as it is closely tied to issues of social justice, economic development, and political stability. In recent years, the government has continued to try to address the issue through various initiatives, such as the Comprehensive Agrarian Reform Program Extension with Reforms (CARPER) and the Philippine Development Plan 2017-2022. However, much work remains to be done to ensure that all farmers in the country have access to the land and resources they need to thrive.

Answer:

Introduction:

A. Statement of the Topic:

What are the current economic issues in the Philippines and how are they affecting the country's development?

B. Rationale:

The Philippines is a developing country with a population of over 100 million people, and its economy has been experiencing both progress and setbacks. Some of the current issues facing the country include a high poverty rate, income inequality, unemployment, and underemployment, among others. These issues have the potential to hinder the country's progress and development, making it an interesting topic to explore.

C. Organization:

This paper will first review the relevant literature and identify the gaps that exist in current research. Then, economic theory will be applied to the issues at hand. The paper will also analyze the latest data and statistics, summarize the results, and present the findings. Finally, the conclusion will discuss the objectives of the paper.

Body of the Paper:

A. Related Literature:

Previous studies have highlighted the economic challenges in the Philippines, such as poverty, income inequality, and unemployment. However, there is a need to update the literature and explore the current state of these issues. For example, the COVID-19 pandemic has exacerbated the poverty rate, causing more people to lose their jobs and income. Another gap in the literature is the lack of attention to underemployment, which is a prevalent issue in the country.

B. Application of Economic Theory:

The economic theory can be applied to explain the issues facing the Philippines.
