14. cursive writing is an important skill​


Answer:

Research suggests that printing letters and writing in cursive activate different parts of the brain. Learning cursive is good for children's fine motor skills, and writing in longhand generally helps students retain more information and generate more ideas.

In fact, learning to write in cursive is shown to improve brain development in the areas of thinking, language and working memory. Cursive handwriting stimulates brain synapses and synchronicity between the left and right hemispheres, something absent from printing and typing.

In recent years, the issue of allowing cellular phones in schools has been a hotly debated topic. Some argue that the presence of phones in classrooms is a distraction that hinders learning, while others believe that they can be used as valuable educational tools. While it is true that cell phones can be a source of distraction, the benefits of allowing them in school far outweigh the negatives.

Firstly, cell phones can be a powerful tool for learning. Students can use them to access educational resources and online materials, research topics, and communicate with teachers and peers. Smartphones have become an essential part of our daily lives, and they offer a wealth of information and opportunities for learning that should not be overlooked. By allowing cell phones in school, educators can harness the power of this technology and create a more engaging and effective learning environment.

Secondly, allowing cell phones in schools can also promote safety and security. In the event of an emergency, students can use their phones to call for help or alert authorities. This can be particularly important in situations where traditional communication channels are unavailable or overloaded. Additionally, parents can reach their children more easily in case of an emergency, providing peace of mind for both parents and students.

In conclusion, the benefits of allowing cell phones in schools far outweigh the potential negatives. By using them as educational tools and promoting safety and security, educators can create a more effective and engaging learning environment. While it is important to establish clear guidelines for phone use and monitor their use in the classroom, the benefits of allowing cell phones in schools cannot be ignored.
