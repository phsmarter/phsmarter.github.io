4. what type of Philippine folk dance that use 2/4 time signature.​


Answer:

Tinikling

It is a traditional dance from the Philippines that originated in the Visayan islands. The dance is named after the tikling bird, which is known for its quick and graceful movements.

The dance involves two people who use bamboo poles to create a rhythmic beat while dancers move in between and outside the poles, imitating the bird's movements as it jumps over grass stems, runs over tree branches, or ducks under bamboo traps set by rice farmers.

The Tinikling dance is typically performed during festive occasions, such as weddings and town fiestas. It is considered a national dance of the Philippines and is often performed by dance troupes during cultural festivals and events.
