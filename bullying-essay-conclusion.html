3. Bullying essay Isang law Conclusion end


Answer:

Bullying is a pervasive issue that affects many individuals, especially children and teenagers. It is defined as repeated aggressive behavior intended to harm or intimidate another person. Bullying can take many forms, including physical, verbal, and emotional abuse. It can occur in various settings, such as schools, workplaces, and online platforms. In the Philippines, bullying is a serious concern that has prompted the government to enact laws to protect victims.

The Anti-Bullying Act of 2013 (Republic Act No. 10627) is the primary law in the Philippines that addresses the issue of bullying. The law defines bullying as any severe or repeated use by one or more students of a written, verbal, or electronic expression, or a physical act or gesture, or any combination thereof, directed at another student that has the effect of actually causing or placing the latter in reasonable fear of physical or emotional harm or damage to his property; creating a hostile environment at school for the other student; infringing on the rights of the other student at school; or materially and substantially disrupting the education process or the orderly operation of a school.

Under this law, schools are required to establish policies and procedures to prevent and address bullying incidents. These policies should include measures for reporting and investigating incidents of bullying, providing support for victims and their families, and imposing appropriate sanctions for offenders. Schools are also mandated to conduct programs that promote awareness and understanding of bullying among students, teachers, and parents.

In conclusion, bullying is a serious issue that can have long-lasting effects on its victims. The Philippines recognizes this problem and has taken steps to address it through the Anti-Bullying Act of 2013. This law provides a framework for schools to prevent and respond to incidents of bullying effectively. However, it is essential to continue promoting awareness and education about this issue to prevent it from happening in the first place.

19. BULLYING ISSUES ESSAY Introduction,body and conclusion (make own essay)​


Answer:

Introduction:

Bullying is a pervasive issue that affects people of all ages and backgrounds. It can take many forms, including physical, verbal, and psychological abuse. The negative impact of bullying can be long-lasting and far-reaching, leading to a range of physical, emotional, and social problems for both victims and perpetrators. In this essay, we will explore the issues surrounding bullying, including its causes, effects, and potential solutions.

Body:

Bullying can have a significant impact on the mental and emotional health of its victims. Those who experience bullying may suffer from anxiety, depression, and other mental health issues. They may also have difficulty forming and maintaining healthy relationships with others, leading to feelings of isolation and loneliness. In extreme cases, bullying can even lead to self-harm or suicide.

While victims are clearly the primary targets of bullying, perpetrators can also suffer from a range of negative consequences. Those who engage in bullying behavior may struggle with feelings of guilt, shame, and regret, which can impact their self-esteem and sense of self-worth. They may also experience social ostracism and exclusion from their peers, as others may view them as aggressive or mean-spirited.

The causes of bullying are complex and multifaceted, but often stem from a range of personal, social, and environmental factors. These can include a lack of empathy, a desire for power and control, a learned behavior, and personal issues like low self-esteem. Addressing these underlying factors is key to developing effective solutions to the issue of bullying.

There are several potential solutions to the problem of bullying, including prevention programs, increased education and awareness, and stronger disciplinary measures for those who engage in bullying behavior. Prevention programs can help to create a culture of empathy, respect, and inclusivity, while education and awareness campaigns can help to reduce the stigma surrounding bullying and increase understanding of its impact. Stronger disciplinary measures can also serve as a deterrent for those who engage in bullying behavior, making it clear that this type of behavior will not be tolerated.

Conclusion:

In conclusion, bullying is a pervasive issue that has a far-reaching impact on individuals and communities. While the causes of bullying are complex and multifaceted, addressing the underlying factors is essential for developing effective solutions.
