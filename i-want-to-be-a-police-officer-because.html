The Reasons To Be A Police

I want to be police because:

1. Police serve the community

2. Police have a variety in job duties

3. Police have to use problem-solving skills

4. Police have the potential to work with animals

5. Police have physically fit

Let's elaborate on the answer above!

1. Police Serves Community

I want to be police because I can serve the community. A police officer has to protect the community or certain neighborhoods and try to reduce crime rates. A police officer keeps the community safe by arresting suspects who are committing crimes.

2. Police Has Variety of Job Duties

I want to be a police officer because a police officer will experience much variety of assignments. Such as issuing a citation for traffic violations and motor vehicle accidents. furtherly, a police officer may be responsible for taking over a criminal case, such as gathering evidence and interviewing various people.

3. Police have to use problem-solving skills

I want to be a police officer because police have to use problem skills to solve problems. A police officer has to act and think quickly to keep civilians safe. For example, police use footage from a body camera and testimonies from witnesses to connect elements of a case.

4. Police have the potential to work with animals

I want to be police because I want to work with animals. A police officer always trains dogs to detect illegal things and participate in rescue operations. A police officer with an affinity and command of animals can succeed in preparing them for fieldwork.

5. Police Has Physically Fit

I want to be police because I can stay physically fit. It is because a police officer has to chase and arrest suspects and it requires so much strength and energy. A police officer has to pass a physical fitness test before receiving a job offer and of course, they have to maintain a fit body.

27. why is there's a need for a police officer to render a police report?​


Explanation:

A police report is a crucial document in the law enforcement process as it serves as an official record of an incident, crime, or investigation. The need for a police officer to render a police report is to provide a clear and concise account of the events that took place, to preserve evidence, and to serve as the basis for further investigation and potential legal proceedings.
