11. Population of eagle in Philippines decreases because of deforestation(Density-Dependent or Density-independent)​


Answer:

The decrease of eagle population in the Philippines due to deforestation is an example of a density-independent factor. Density-independent factors are those that affect a population regardless of its size or density. In this case, deforestation is a factor that affects the overall habitat of the eagles, reducing the availability of resources such as food and nesting sites. The decrease in the eagle population is not dependent on the density of the population, but rather on the availability of suitable habitat.
