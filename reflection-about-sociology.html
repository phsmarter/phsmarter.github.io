11. Reflection in anthropology sociology and political science


Reflection In Anthropology Sociology and Political Science

Political science is the sociological study of how power is distributed and transferred during decision-making, the functions, and structures of government, including national and international institutions, as well as political behavior and public policies. The study of what makes humans human is called anthropology. Sociology is the study of how human societies form, are organized and operate.

Political science, anthropology, and sociology all contribute to the understanding of the various dimensions of how societies are organized by humans. Political science focuses on certain facets of society, such as laws, regulations, governments, and other similar practices, whereas sociology examines people's typical social activities. Some of the above subjects are covered under the broad area of anthropology, along with human biology and psychology. These three disciplines can show how human civilizations function both independently and cooperatively when taken together.

By using these disciplines, we can build stronger social structures and communities that will benefit society as a whole. Additionally, it aids in closing social gaps within the society itself and improves knowledge of how various facets of society operate and interact.

Anthropologists investigate a variety of subjects in order to achieve this goal, including evolutionary biology, linguistics, cultural studies, history, economics, and political structure, to name a few. The subject of anthropology can range from the familiar (like the anatomy of the foot) to the entirely foreign to most of us (like the star lore of the Australian aborigines). Its scope is both broad (such as the development of skin tone) and narrowly focused (e.g., the use-wear of pottery in the Andean highlands of Peru). Everything from ancient Mayan hieroglyphics to the music of African Pygmies to the corporate culture of a U.S. automaker could be included in the wide range of research topics.

Sociology and anthropology have enormous significance because they recognize that societies around the world differ and set out to research and comprehend these variations. Understanding these societal distinctions and being aware of them might promote tolerance in society.

Sociology and anthropology involve the systematic study of social life and culture in order to understand the causes and consequences of human action. Sociologists and anthropologists study the structure and processes of traditional cultures and modern, industrial societies in both Western and non-Western cultures. They examine how culture, social structures (groups, organizations and communities) and social institutions (family, education, religion, etc.) affect human attitudes, actions and life-chances.

Sociology and anthropology combine scientific and humanistic perspectives in the study of society. Drawing upon various theoretical perspectives, sociologists and anthropologists study areas such as culture, socialization, deviance, inequality, health and illness, family patterns, social change and race and ethnic relations. Combining theoretical perspectives with empirical research allows students an opportunity to develop new insights and a different perspective on their own lives. This combination also helps students to understand everyday social life as a blend of both stable patterns of interaction and ubiquitous sources of social change.

29. Which of the following is NOT true about the nature of sociology? Select one: a. Sociology is science. b. Sociology is a social science. c. Sociology is a physical science. d. Sociology is a categorical discipline.


Answer:

c. Sociology is a physical science.

Explanation:

Sociology is a not a physical science, but instead it is a social science.

Sociology belongs to the family of social sciences which concentrates its’ attention on man, his social behaviors, social activities and social life. As a member of the family of social sciences, it is very well connected to other social science like history, political science, economic, philosophy, anthropology and other similar subjects. While physical sciences are focused on the study of inanimate natural objects, such as physics, chemistry, astronomy and other related topics. With the statement that sociology is concerning with the social universe, it differentiate it from astronomy, physics, Chemistry, Geology, Mathematics and others physical sciences.

Nevertheless, it is also a point that sociology cannot be directly modeled on the patterns of natural sciences, because human action is different from the discipline of nature.

Sociology and its Nature

Sociology is defined as the branch of knowledge and it has its own characteristics. Sociology has different nature in society. It is dissimilar from other sciences in certain aspects. The list below are the other main characteristics of sociology as enumerated by Robert Bierstedt in his book ” The Social Order”:

Sociology is science. Sociology is a scientific field of study. It is a science since it involves objective and systematic methods of investigation and evaluation of social reality in the context of empirical evidence and interpretation. It is clear that sociology is a science for the reason that it fulfils the basic general requirements of objective and rational knowledge of social reality.  Sociology is an independent science. Sociology has now evolved into an independent science. It is not studied under a branch of any other science, like philosophy or political philosophy, or history. As a stand-alone science it has its own field of study, boundary and method. Sociology is a social science. The originating fathers of sociology are interested with the study of social order and change. They also yearned to model the science of society or sociology as an exact discipline as natural sciences. Sociology is a general Science and not a special Social Science. The area of information of sociology is general and not specialized. It relates with human connections and human life in general. It also studies man and human interactions, but note that not all about human interaction. It concentrates its intention on certain aspects of human interaction and activities. Accordingly, if economics specializes itself in the study of economic activities, political science concentrates on political activities and so on, sociology follows that it investigate not economics, religion, political, legal, moral or any other special kind of phenomenal in relation to human life and activities. It only studies human activities in a general way.   Among other dissimilarities is the subject matter of natural science. It is relatively static and unchanging whereas human behavior, the subject matter of sociology, is flexible and dynamic. Sociology is a categorical and not a normative discipline. Sociology “restricts itself to statements about “what is”, and not about “what should be” or “ought to be”. As a science, sociology is necessary silent about question of value. It does not make any kind of value-judgment. Its method is neither moral nor immoral, but amoral. It is ethically neutral, but does not mean that sociological knowledge is useless or serves no purpose. It only means that sociology as a discipline which does not deal with what is good and evil, what is right and wrong, and what is moral or immoral.
