1. What is research?

—Research is "creative and systematic work undertaken to increase the stock of knowledge". It involves the collection, organization and analysis of information to increase understanding of a topic or issue. A research project may be an expansion on past work in the field.

2. Why do we need to repeat doing research? What is IPO?

— It is very important that research can be replicated, because it means that other researchers can test the findings of the research. Replicability keeps researchers honest and can give readers confidence in research. If the research is replicable, then any false conclusions can eventually be shown to be wrong. An initial public offering or stock launch is a public offering in which shares of a company are sold to institutional investors and usually also retail investors. An IPO is typically underwritten by one or more investment banks, who also arrange for the shares to be listed on one or more stock exchanges.

3. What are the two types of research? What is the difference of quantitative and qualitative?

— There are two main categories of research methods: qualitative research methods and quantitative research methods. Quantitative research methods involve using numbers to measure data. Researchers can use statistical analysis to find connections and meaning in the data. Quantitative research deals with numbers and statistics, while qualitative research deals with words and meanings.

4. What are the data collection method that is used in quantitative research? What are the data collection method that is used in qualitative research?

— Although there are many other methods to collect quantitative data, those mentioned above probability sampling, interviews, questionnaire observation, and document review are the most common and widely used methods either offline or for online data collection. 17. Chapter 1: The Problem and Its Background Introduction, Background​


Explanation:

In Chapter 1 of a research paper, the problem and its background are introduced to provide readers with an understanding of the research topic. The introduction should start with a general statement about the topic and gradually narrow down to the specific problem being addressed. This section should also include a brief overview of the purpose and significance of the study.

The background section provides context for the problem by discussing previous research on the topic. This section should highlight gaps in existing knowledge that the current study aims to address. Overall, Chapter 1 sets the stage for the rest of the research paper by clearly defining the problem, explaining its significance, and providing context for further investigation.

22. when making the chapter one for a qualitative research, do you need to include a solution to your introduction?​


In qualitative research, the purpose of the introduction is to provide background information on the research topic, explain the importance of the research, and identify the research questions or objectives. It is not necessary to include a solution to the research problem in the introduction.

Qualitative research often focuses on exploring and understanding complex phenomena and experiences, rather than providing a specific solution to a problem. Therefore, the research questions or objectives are typically framed to guide the research process and to help the researcher gain a deeper understanding of the topic under investigation.

However, in some cases, qualitative research may aim to develop or test a theoretical framework, or to inform the development of interventions or policies. In these cases, the research may have a more practical or applied focus, and the introduction may include some discussion of potential solutions or implications of the research.

Overall, the content and structure of the introduction in a qualitative research study will depend on the specific research questions, objectives, and context of the study.

Introduction

In this chapter, we will discuss the materials required for conducting research on the role of music as a memory enhancer.

Participants: The study will require participants who are willing to participate in the research. The participants should be healthy adults aged between 18 to 50 years, with no hearing or cognitive impairments. The participants should be recruited from various social and cultural backgrounds to ensure the generalizability of the findings.

Music: The study will require a selection of music that is known to have a positive impact on memory retention and recall. The music selected should be from various genres and should be appealing to a wide range of participants.

Memory tasks: The study will require various memory tasks to assess the impact of music on memory retention and recall. The tasks will include both verbal and visual memory tasks. The verbal memory tasks will include recall of word lists and stories, while the visual memory tasks will include recall of pictures and videos.

Questionnaires: The study will require the use of questionnaires to collect demographic information from the participants. The questionnaires will also be used to assess the participants' mood and motivation levels.

Computer and software: The study will require a computer with software to present the memory tasks and questionnaires to the participants. The software should be able to record the participants' responses accurately and store them securely.

Headphones: The study will require headphones to ensure that the participants hear the music clearly without any external noise interference.

Consent forms: The study will require informed consent forms to be signed by the participants before they can participate in the research. The consent forms will provide information about the study, its purpose, and the procedures involved. The forms will also assure the participants of their confidentiality and right to withdraw from the study at any time.

Ethics approval: The study will require approval from an ethics committee to ensure that the research is conducted ethically and that the participants are not subjected to any harm or discomfort. The ethics approval will also ensure that the data collected is used only for research purposes and is kept confidential.

In summary, the materials required for conducting the study on the role of music as a memory enhancer will include participants, music, memory tasks, questionnaires, computer and software, headphones, consent forms, and ethics approval. These materials will ensure that the study is conducted systematically and ethically, and the data collected is valid and reliable.
