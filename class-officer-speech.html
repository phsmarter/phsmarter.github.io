This past year has been one of the most challenging times in our lives; the stress,

anxiety, and loneliness we have all faced would be unimaginable to our former selves. However,

during this mentally taxing period, I feel the interests of the student body have not been fully

met. In particular, the communication between our school administration and the students has

been lacking, making people uneasy about what the future holds. As a Class Officer, I would be

in a position to make adjustments according to whatever the students of our grade desire. I

would endeavor to listen to people's opinions and strive to make alterations based on those

viewpoints. Overall, I wish to be a Class Officer in order to shorten the divide between the

student body and the administration, as well as make sure the interests of that student body are

represented.

What are the difference between Direct Speech and Indirect speech?

Direct Speech

It is used to describe what someone said exactly. We use direct speech when we simply repeat what someone says and putting the phrase between quotation marks.

It is very common to use the verb ‘to say’ (‘said’ in the past) in writing direct speech. But others used verbs such as ‘ask’, ‘reply’, and ‘shout’.

Indirect Speech

It is used  to report what someone said without quotation marks and without writing the exact words.

When we report what someone says in the present simple we don’t change the tense, instead we change the subject. However, when we write in indirect speech in the past, we usually change the tense.
