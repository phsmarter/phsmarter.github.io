4. i am a real hero 300 words essay​


I am a Real Hero

A hero does not always have to wear a cape and be big for his super powers.  It is not always someone who risks his life and saves other people.  It is not always the good fighting against the bad.  It is not always a man or a woman who moves the Earth, holds the bullet in his hand, or eats fire and throws them out like a volcano.  A real hero is more than those.

I am a real hero – a hero guaranteed of tiny victories and good deeds.  I helped an elder cross the street and stopped the cars when the sign went red and the elder has not reached the other side of the street yet.  I shared my food with my classmates in school if they did not have their own lunch or snack.  I helped my mom do the chores and patted her on the back when we were done.  I hugged her so tightly after a long tiring day.  I knew it soothed her.  I smiled at the stranger who seem to be on the verge of crying and signaled him a thumbs up because it may be what he needed to get through the day.  I reported my classmates who were bullying the shy kid in class and they stopped bugging him.  I then befriended him and now, we are close friends.

Heroes do not always fly nor turn invisible.  Real heroes are those who do good to others and help them without asking anything in return.  They are an advocate of good deeds and generous acts without having the need to be known by all. The small things matter more than the big things which are not done in humility and sincerity.  I am a real hero because I empower people.

7. nation arts hero essay​


Answer:

A national hero of the Philippines is a Filipino who has been recognized as a national hero for his or her role in the history of the Philippines. Loosely, the term may refer to all Filipino historical figures recognized as heroes, but the term more strictly refers to those officially designated as such. In 1995 the Philippine National Heroes Committee officially recommended several people for the designation, but this was not acted upon. As of 2007, no one had ever been officially recognized as a Philippine national hero.
