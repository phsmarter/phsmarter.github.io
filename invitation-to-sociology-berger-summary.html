2. summary of sociology of deviant of behavior​


Answer:

#CarryOnLearning

Explanation:

In sociology, deviance defines an event or conduct that violates social norms, including a formal law ( e.g., crime), as well as an informal breach of social norms (e.g., rejection of folk and mores).

Structural functionalism argues that deviant behavior plays an active , constructive role in society by ultimately helping to bring together different populations within society. Conflict theory implies that deviant behavior is the product of social, political, or material differences within a social group.

26. Which of the following is NOT true about the nature of sociology? Select one: a. Sociology is science. b. Sociology is a social science. c. Sociology is a physical science. d. Sociology is a categorical discipline.


Answer:

c. Sociology is a physical science.

Explanation:

Sociology is a not a physical science, but instead it is a social science.

Sociology belongs to the family of social sciences which concentrates its’ attention on man, his social behaviors, social activities and social life. As a member of the family of social sciences, it is very well connected to other social science like history, political science, economic, philosophy, anthropology and other similar subjects. While physical sciences are focused on the study of inanimate natural objects, such as physics, chemistry, astronomy and other related topics. With the statement that sociology is concerning with the social universe, it differentiate it from astronomy, physics, Chemistry, Geology, Mathematics and others physical sciences.

Nevertheless, it is also a point that sociology cannot be directly modeled on the patterns of natural sciences, because human action is different from the discipline of nature.

Sociology and its Nature

Sociology is defined as the branch of knowledge and it has its own characteristics. Sociology has different nature in society. It is dissimilar from other sciences in certain aspects. The list below are the other main characteristics of sociology as enumerated by Robert Bierstedt in his book ” The Social Order”:

Sociology is science. Sociology is a scientific field of study. It is a science since it involves objective and systematic methods of investigation and evaluation of social reality in the context of empirical evidence and interpretation. It is clear that sociology is a science for the reason that it fulfils the basic general requirements of objective and rational knowledge of social reality.  Sociology is an independent science. Sociology has now evolved into an independent science. It is not studied under a branch of any other science, like philosophy or political philosophy, or history. As a stand-alone science it has its own field of study, boundary and method. Sociology is a social science. The originating fathers of sociology are interested with the study of social order and change. They also yearned to model the science of society or sociology as an exact discipline as natural sciences. Sociology is a general Science and not a special Social Science. The area of information of sociology is general and not specialized. It relates with human connections and human life in general. It also studies man and human interactions, but note that not all about human interaction. It concentrates its intention on certain aspects of human interaction and activities. Accordingly, if economics specializes itself in the study of economic activities, political science concentrates on political activities and so on, sociology follows that it investigate not economics, religion, political, legal, moral or any other special kind of phenomenal in relation to human life and activities. It only studies human activities in a general way.   Among other dissimilarities is the subject matter of natural science. It is relatively static and unchanging whereas human behavior, the subject matter of sociology, is flexible and dynamic. Sociology is a categorical and not a normative discipline. Sociology “restricts itself to statements about “what is”, and not about “what should be” or “ought to be”. As a science, sociology is necessary silent about question of value. It does not make any kind of value-judgment. Its method is neither moral nor immoral, but amoral. It is ethically neutral, but does not mean that sociological knowledge is useless or serves no purpose. It only means that sociology as a discipline which does not deal with what is good and evil, what is right and wrong, and what is moral or immoral.
