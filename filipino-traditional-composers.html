9. what was the role of western musical tradition to filipino composers ​


ANSWER

Religious or Christian issues occupied a significant portion of the people's cultural experience. The Spanish friars, who taught Gregorian Chant for masses and other Christian rituals, were the first to introduce Western music. Fray Juan de Santa Marta, for example, assembled about 400 boys from various locales in Lumbang, Laguna, in 1606 and instructed them in singing and instrumental playing. In addition, the Manila Cathedral founded a singing school in 1742. Baroque pipe organs were built during this time, and the one at San Agustin Church in Manila (restored in 1998) and the famed Bamboo Organ of Las Pias are still in use today.

14. How the filipino traditional composer significant effect to the modern music now a days​


[tex]\purple{Answer}[/tex]

Filipino traditional composers have had a significant effect on modern music in the Philippines and beyond. Their music has been influential in shaping the sound of Filipino music and has been used as inspiration by contemporary musicians and composers.

One way in which traditional Filipino music has influenced modern music is through the use of indigenous instruments. Many modern Filipino musicians incorporate instruments such as the kulintang (a set of brass gongs), the kudyapi (a type of lute), and the banduria (a small, plucked string instrument) into their compositions, adding a unique and distinct sound to their music.

In addition to instrumentation, traditional Filipino music has also influenced modern music through its use of rhythms, melodies, and song structures. The rhythmic patterns found in traditional music, such as the popular 2/4 or 4/4 time signature, have become staples in contemporary Filipino music. Melodies and chord progressions found in traditional Filipino songs have also been used in modern music, often being sampled or adapted to fit new genres.

Furthermore, Filipino traditional music has had a cultural impact on modern music in the Philippines. It has been used as a way to connect with Filipino heritage and identity, and has been a source of inspiration for musicians who seek to incorporate their cultural roots into their music.

Overall, Filipino traditional composers have had a significant effect on modern music in the Philippines and beyond, influencing the sound of contemporary Filipino music and adding a unique cultural flavor to the global music scene.


15. Fill in the chart to show the similarities and differences between different Filipino Traditional composers. ​


Answer:

Francisco Buencamino Sr.:

Music Education:

- Buencamino taught at the Ateneo de Manila in the early 1900s. He also taught at the Centro Escolar de Señoritas and headed its Music Department for 30 years. He founded the Buencamino Music Academy in 1930, and had Nicanor Abelardo as one of his students.

Style/Genre:

- His musical styles are Kundimans and Sarzuela.

Awards Recieved:

- Francisco Buencamino died on the 16th of October, 1952. in the same year, he was given a posthumous Outstanding Composer Award by the Manila Music Lovers Society.

Career As A Musician:

- He founded the Buencamino Music Academy in 1930, and had Nicanor Abelardo as one of his students. He also became a musical director for sarswela productions, and composer of music for films produced by Sampaguita Pictures, LVN, and Excelsior.

Francisco Santiago:

Music Education:

- American Conservatory of Music and University of the Phili[ppines System.

Style/Genre:

- Kundiman, Classical Music

Awards Recieved:

- Dr. Francisco Santiago died on the 28th of September, 1947. He was posthumously given the Patnubay ng Sining at Kalinangan Award by the City of Manila in 1968.

Career As A Musician:

- He is the first Filipino musician to attain a doctorate degree. He became the director of the UP Conservatory of Music in 1930, after the entire music faculty and students of the conservatory protested for the removal of the previous director, Alexander Lippay, for alleged harassment of students and musicians.

Nicanor Abelardo:

Music Education:

- In 1916, Nicanor began studying at the University of the Philippines Conservatory of Music, and by 1924, was appointed head of the composition department at the Conservatory. Later, he ran a school for young musicians, where he molded future National Artists Antonino Buenaventura, Alfredo Lozano, and Lucino Sacramento.

Style/Genre:

- Abelardo is best known for elevating the Philippine genre of the Kundiman into a western art-song form.

Awards Recieved:

- Awit Award for Best Traditional Recording

Career As A Musician:

- Nicanor Abelardo (1893 - 1934) was a composer who emerged from post-colonial Philippines to create a national musical identity. A composer of over 140 works that included sonatas, concerti and chamber music, Abelardo is best known for elevating the Philippine genre of the Kundiman into a western art-song form.

Col. Antonio Buenaventura:

Music Education:

- He studied under Nicanor Abelardo at the University of the Philippines Diliman Conservatory of Music and graduated in 1932 with a Teacher's Diploma in Music, major in Science and Composition and became an assistant instructor at the Conservatory. He also studied composition for a post-graduate degree under Jenő Takács.

Style/Genre:

- Buenaventura composed songs, compositions, for solo instruments as well as symphonic and orchestral works based on the folksongs of various Philippine ethnic groups.

Awards Received:

- Colonel Buenaventura is also a distinguished composer and educator. He composed the Symphony of C that won him the Cultural Heritage Award in 1966. He was a pioneer in the research on Philippine Ethnic and Folk Music. He founded several schools and became Director of the UST Conservatory of Music.

Career As A Musician:

- Colonel Buenaventura is a distinguished composer and educator.

Lucio San Pedro:

Music Education:

- The Julliard School and University of The Philippines System

Style/Genre:

- He wrote symphonic music, band, vocal, choral, music-theater, chamber, movie music. He is known for the glorification of the Filipino soul in his compositions.

Awards Received:

- He received the Patnubay ng Sining at Kalinangan from the City of Manila in 1984 and the Asean Award for Music and a Papal award in 1990. San Pedro was named national artist in 1991.

Career As A Musician:

- San Pedro came from a family with musical roots and he began his career early. When he was still in his late teens, he succeeded his deceased grandfather as the local church organist. By then, he had already composed songs, hymns and two complete masses for voices and orchestra.

Cipriano Cayabyab:

Music Education:

-  University of The Philippines System, University of the Philippines College Of Music, and University of the Philippines Diliman

Style/Genre:

- OPM, chorale, and musical theater

Awards Received:

- He was named as National Artist of the Philippines for Music in 2018. His works range from commissioned full-length ballets, theater musicals, choral pieces, a Mass set to unaccompanied chorus, and orchestral pieces, to commercial recordings of popular music, film scores and television specials.

Career As A Musician:

- He was the Executive and Artistic Director for several years for the defunct San Miguel Foundation for the Performing Arts.
