How do bacteria in the soil become beneficial to trees in the tropical rainforest?

c. there recycle to nutrients in the soil through decomposition

Tropical rainforests are a perfect home for bacteria and other microorganisms due to their hot and humid climate. Because these creatures are active all year, they decompose materials on the forest floor swiftly. In other biomes, such as the deciduous forest, leaf litter decomposition contributes nutrients to the soil. Plants in the tropical rainforest, on the other hand, grow so quickly that they quickly eat the nutrients from decomposing leaf litter. As a result, the majority of nutrients are found in trees in the forest and other plants rather than in the soil. The majority of nutrients taken into the soil in the forest are leached out by the plentiful rains, leaving the soil barren and acidic.
