a.) The Principle of the Greatest Number:

I have learned two realizations from the Principle of the Greatest Number. First, I have realized that this principle is based on the idea of utilitarianism, which states that the greatest good for the greatest number of people should be the determining factor for making ethical decisions. Second, I have realized that this principle does not necessarily prioritize the interests of the individual over the interests of the collective. Instead, it seeks to find a balance between individual and collective interests.

b.) Deontology:

I have learned two realizations from deontology. First, I have realized that this ethical theory is based on the idea of duty, which states that individuals have an obligation to act according to certain moral principles regardless of the consequences. Second, I have realized that deontology is focused on the intrinsic value of an action rather than the outcome. In other words, it emphasizes the importance of taking action in accordance with moral principles rather than seeking the best outcome for everyone involved.

12. III. Essay. As a student of ethics, cite two realizations you have learned from each of the ff. topics: 20 pts.a.) The Principle of the Greatest Number b.) Deontology pahelp plssss ​


Answer:

a.) The Principle of the Greatest Number

One realization I have learned from the Principle of the Greatest Number is that it is often used to justify actions that are meant to benefit the majority at the expense of the minority. For example, a government may choose to implement a policy that benefits the majority of its citizens, but may negatively impact a minority group.

Another realization I have learned from the Principle of the Greatest Number is that it is not always the best ethical principle to follow. While it may be appealing to try to maximize the overall happiness or well-being of the greatest number of people, it can sometimes lead to a lack of consideration for the needs and rights of individuals or smaller groups.

b.) Deontology

One realization I have learned from deontology is that it emphasizes the importance of following moral rules and duties, regardless of the consequences. Deontologists believe that some actions, such as telling the truth or keeping a promise, are always morally right or wrong, regardless of their consequences.

Another realization I have learned from deontology is that it can sometimes conflict with other ethical theories, such as utilitarianism, which holds that the right action is the one that maximizes overall happiness or well-being.
