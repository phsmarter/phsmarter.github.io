4. When MgSO4 with molar mass of 120g/mole are required to make 100.0mL of a solution that is 0.532M? How many grams of the Magnesium Sulfate will be needed to achieved the required solution?


Mass of MgSO₄ needed = 6.384 g

Further explanation  

The concentration of a substance can be expressed in several quantities such as moles, percent (%) weight/volume,), molarity, molality, parts per million (ppm) or mole fraction. The concentration shows the amount of solute in a unit of the amount of solvent.  

Molarity shows the number of moles of solute in every 1 liter of solution or mmol in each ml of solution  

[tex]\boxed{\bold{M=\dfrac{n}{V}}}[/tex]

Where  

M = Molarity , M  

n = number of moles of solute, mol  

V = Volume of a solution, L  

Given

molar mass of  MgSO₄ : 120 g/mole

100 mL of a 0.532M solution

Required

mass of MgSO₄

Solution

mol MgSO₄ :

= M x V

= 0.532 M x 0.1 L

= 0.0532

mass MgSO₄ :

= mol x molar mass

= 0.0532 x 120 g/mol

= 6.384 g

12. Determine the number of grams magnesium nitrate produced from the reaction of 65 g of AgNO3 with Mg: Note: For mass - mass calculation, convert mole into mass by multiplying the number of moles by the molar mass of the substance.


Solution:

Step 1: Write the balanced chemical equation.

Mg + 2AgNO₃ → Mg(NO₃)₂ + 2Ag

Step 2: Calculate the molar mass of substances needed.

For AgNO₃

molar mass = (107.9 g/mol × 1) + (14.01 g/mol × 1) + (16.00 g/mol × 3)

molar mass = 169.9 g/mol

For Mg(NO₃)₂

molar mass = (24.31 g/mol × 1) + (14.01 g/mol × 2) + (16 g/mol × 6)

molar mass = 148.33 g/mol

Step 3: Determine the mole ratio needed.

mole ratio = 2 mol AgNO₃ : 1 mol Mg(NO₃)₂

Step 4: Calculate the mass of Mg(NO₃)₂ produced by using the mole ratio.

[tex]\text{mass of Mg(NO₃)₂ = 65 g AgNO₃} × \frac{\text{1 mol AgNO₃}}{\text{169.9 g AgNO₃}} × \frac{\text{1 mol Mg(NO₃)₂}}{\text{2 mol AgNO₃}} × \frac{\text{148.33 g Mg(NO₃)₂}}{\text{1 mol Mg(NO₃)₂}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{\text{mass of Mg(NO₃)₂ = 28.4 g}}[/tex]

23. In a reaction with hydrochloric acid, why does powdered magnesium reacts faster than the same mass of magnesium ribbon?a. The powdered magnesium contains more atoms than the magnesium ribbon.b. The powdered magnesium is hotter than the magnesium ribbon.c. The powdered magnesium has a bigger surface area than the magnesium ribbon.d. The powdered magnesium has a smaller surface area than the magnesium ribbon.


Answer:

The powdered magnesium reacts faster than the magnesium ribbon because the powdered magnesium has a bigger surface area than the magnesium ribbon.

Explanation:

Kinetics is the study of the speed of chemical reaction. Some chemical reactions proceeds very fast while others are slow. Depending on what is being observed or needed, Chemists tend to slow down or speed up a chemical reaction. There are  several factors that affect chemical reactions.

Some factors are listed below:

Concentration of reactantsPressure of gaseous reactantsNature of reactantsParticle size of reactantsTemperatureCatalyst

For the situation given, the magnesium compounds in the reaction contain the same mass but differ in particle size. One is powdered and the other is in the form of a ribbon. Particle size will be our basis of our analysis.

Powdered substances have bigger surface areas. Increase in surface area of a solid reactant exposes more of its particle to come in contact with the other reactant. This will result to more collision between reactant particles, hence, speeding up the reaction.
