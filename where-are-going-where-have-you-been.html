1. The key success factors for Disney include:

Strong brand recognition and loyalty

Diversification of revenue streams through a variety of business units such as theme parks, movies, television, and consumer products

High-quality content creation and management

Strategic acquisitions of complementary companies such as Marvel, Lucasfilm, and Pixar

Effective marketing and advertising campaigns to drive brand awareness and engagement

Focus on innovation and new technologies to enhance the customer experience

Disney may be vulnerable in a few areas such as:

Dependence on a few key franchises and characters for revenue, which could lead to over-reliance and potential declines if those franchises experience decreased popularity

2. Vulnerability to economic downturns that may lead to lower consumer spending on non-essential items such as entertainment and travel

Increased competition in the entertainment industry, including from streaming services that offer lower-priced alternatives to traditional cable and satellite TV packages

Disney should watch out for potential disruption from new technologies and changing consumer preferences, such as the growing trend towards cord-cutting and increased adoption of streaming services.

3. Some recommendations for Disney's senior marketing executives going forward include:

Continuously monitor and analyze consumer trends and preferences to identify new opportunities and adjust marketing strategies accordingly

Expand the company's presence on digital and social media platforms to reach younger and more tech-savvy audiences

Continue to invest in the development and acquisition of high-quality content across various platforms

Emphasize and communicate the company's commitment to diversity and inclusion, which is becoming increasingly important to consumers

Consider strategic partnerships with other companies or brands that align with Disney's values and mission to expand the company's reach and appeal.

26. Have you ever been to a hot spring? There are a lot of hot springs in countries that belong to the Pacific ring of fire, this includes the Philippines and Japan. In fact, hot springs are such a fad in the Philippines that a lot of tourists, both local and foreign, go out of their way to visit them. How would you describe a hot spring? Can you name places where there are hot springs present? What do these areas have in common?


Answer:

Have you ever been to a hot spring?No, not yet.

How would you describe a hot spring?Hot spring, also called thermal spring, spring with water at temperatures substantially higher than the air temperature of the surrounding region. Most hot springs discharge groundwater that is heated by shallow intrusions of magma (molten rock) in volcanic areas.

Can you name places where there are hot springs present?Countries that are renowned for their hot springs include Turkey, Honduras, Canada, Chile, Hungary, Iceland, Israel, Japan, Taiwan, New Zealand, India, Romania, Fiji, and the United States, but there are interesting and unique hot springs in many other places as well.

What do these areas have in common?These are all volcanic areas. Hot springs are generally found in regions of recent volcanic activity. It is because the surface water percolates downward through the rocks below the Earth's surface into high-temperature regions surrounding a magma reservoir, either active or recently solidified but still hot.

30. Your supervisor has asked you to organize a vacation for your colleagues. A set amount has been allocated for this purpose.  You have shortlisted two destinations: Fancy Land and Heaven Island. How would you decide where to go?


I would consider the following before deciding where to go on a vacation with my colleagues. First, the length of our vacation. Most employees work from Monday to Friday, so the vacation granted with the colleagues would probably be only applied on a weekend, could be overnight. Second, the distance of the place from us. Time travel should also be considered if only given limited days of vacation. Lastly, it is proper to ask my colleagues what they would also prefer. Since I already have two choices, I’d give them some ideas about each place and what activities we could do, as well as the accommodation and food we may experience.


Based on the shortlisted destinations, personally, I think I would prefer the Fancy Land. Because, when we say fancy, it sounds decorated and structured. The place could be very attractive to visitors. With the day(s) allotted to the vacationers, I think Fancy Land would be more manageable. However, on the other hand, Heaven Island sounds like a very convincing place to relax and unwind, far from the hustle and bustle of the city. In this case, you really need to have a longer days of vacation, unless approved by your supervisor, then this will be chosen.
