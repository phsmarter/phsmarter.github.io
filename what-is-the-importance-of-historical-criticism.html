Originally, Manist critics focused on literary representations of workers and working classes. For later Manxists, however, literature became a document of a kind of knowledge and a record of the historical conditions that produced that knowfedge. Like cultural criticism, Marxist literary criticism offers critiques of the "canon and focuses on the ways in which culture and power intersect; for a Marxist critic, literature both reproduces existing power relations and offers a space where they can be contested and redefined. Important 20th-century Marxist literary critics include Georg Lucaks. Antonio Gramsci, Louis Althusser, Teny Eagleton, Raymond Williams, and Frederic Jameson. Some of the key concepts of Marxism include worker's uprising, materialism, class struggle, effect of capitalism, classless society, ideology, and working class​
