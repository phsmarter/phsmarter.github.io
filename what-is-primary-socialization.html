7. What is the primary concern of social control theory? pa answer naman​


Answer:

The primary concern of social control theory is to explain why individuals conform to social norms and rules, and what factors influence their decision to engage or not engage in deviant behavior. This theory assumes that humans are naturally inclined towards deviance, and that it is social bonds and relationships that prevent them from engaging in such behavior. Social control theorists believe that when individuals have strong ties to their family, peers, and community, they are more likely to follow societal norms and laws. On the other hand, when these bonds are weak or broken, individuals may turn to deviant behavior. Therefore, social control theory focuses on the ways in which socialization, social learning, and social bonds influence individuals' behavior and their adherence to societal norms.

Answer:

Control theory argues that deviance is primarily prevented by an individual's strong internal moral compass, and the approval or disapproval of other people has little effect.

11. What is Advantages and Disadvantages of having a PRIMARY SOCIAL GROUP in an Organization?​


Answer:

1. Contagiousness

Groups are a means of spreading epidemics. However, in modern societies, belonging to a group in which appropriate prevention measures are taken minimizes this risk, assuming that one may not be part of a group and yet relate to others.

2. Effect of social pressure

Another disadvantage of belonging to a group is that the simple fact of being part of one exposes us more to group pressure , given that the rest of its members share certain characteristics with us and through this identification we can legitimize initiatives to create uniformity among individuals.

For example, using as an excuse that all members of the group share a language, an attempt can be made to associate clothing and symbols that express that identity, making those who do not adopt this aesthetic stand out

3. Criminalization of dissent

It is part of the group dynamics to use exemplary punishment against those who were previously part of the group and later adopted behaviours that are considered to be contrary to group identity.

4. Appearance of default leaderships

Another disadvantage of belonging to the groups is that if we do not make an effort to avoid it by using democratic mechanisms, a leader appears who has more power than the rest in decision making and role distribution, regardless of his or her degree of ability in the task to be addressed collectively.

5. Outward facing

The feeling of belonging has various degrees of intensity, and in the most extreme it can legitimize hostility towards those who are not part of the group , just by being outside its limits.

But even when the feeling of belonging is not very strong, the simple fact of being part of one usually predisposes us to not be so supportive of those outside it.

21. method 2) what are the three (3) primary method of social work and explain each of the method.​


PRIMARY METHOD OF SOCIAL WORK

The primary or direct methods are the first three procedures discussed below. These are direct and primary in the sense that they are used directly in the field to help people solve their problems. These primary strategies are used to solve problems at the grassroots level through direct intervention by a social worker.

Explanation of three primary methods;

1. Social case work

It is the most common type of social work. It addresses individual issues through a one-on-one connection guided by the social case worker's professional skills. The social case worker uses this strategy to try to heal the client's damaged relationship with his social environment and to help the client to adapt to it through guided contact. Using this method, the case worker discovers various aspects of the client's problem, develops an appropriate treatment plan, and finally, using professional social relationship knowledge, attempts to bring about necessary changes in the client's attitude and behavior in order to promote his own growth and development.

2. Social group work

It is a process in which individuals in a social group are assisted by a professionally qualified worker who facilitates their interaction through planned program activities so that they can relate to others and find growth opportunities that are appropriate for their needs and capacities, all with the noble goal of individual group and community development in mind. The group worker in this method uses the group as a potential tool for individual positive transformation and personality development. Individuals in the group are most important in group work, and all program activities center on their development needs. They are helped to strengthen their relationships and personality qualities through the group work process, which may help them develop themselves and their community as a whole.

3. Community Organization

As a practice of social work, community organization is a process in which efforts are focused toward addressing community needs by organizing the community's human and material resources. Some of the specific activities in the community organization technique include identifying problems, discovering resources related to their requirements, building and organizing inter-personal and inter-group relationships, and planning and implementing effective program activities. The fundamental concern of this strategy is the organized and coordinated work of community members for their own development.
