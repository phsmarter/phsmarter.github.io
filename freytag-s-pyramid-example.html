15. Ask your parents or any elder in your neighborhood about a popular tall tale or legend. Write it down and identify the parts of its plot using Freytag's Pyramid as guide.​


ANSWER:

Juan Tamad - THE LAZY PERSON.

One popular tall tale in the Philippines is "Juan Tamad," which is about a lazy boy named Juan who always finds a way to avoid work.

Freytag's Pyramid is a narrative structure that consists of five parts: exposition, rising action, climax, falling action, and resolution. Let's analyze the plot of "Juan Tamad" using this pyramid.

Exposition:

The story starts by introducing the protagonist, Juan Tamad, and his character flaw of being incredibly lazy. Juan Tamad is notorious for sleeping all day and not doing any work.

Rising Action:

One day, Juan Tamad's mother asks him to do some chores like fetching water from the well and cooking rice. Instead of doing the tasks, Juan Tamad decides to find a way to avoid them. He lays down under a tree and asks the wind to do the chores for him. Surprisingly, the wind obliges and does the chores.

Emboldened by this success, Juan Tamad decides to ask other animals to do his work for him. He asks the ants to pick the rice grains and the chickens to lay eggs in his basket. All of them do his bidding, and Juan Tamad becomes more and more confident in his laziness.

Climax:

One day, Juan Tamad decides to go to the river to take a bath. He leaves his clothes by the bank and falls asleep. When he wakes up, he discovers that his clothes are gone. A group of thieves has stolen them. Juan Tamad panics and realizes that he cannot go home naked.

Falling Action:

Desperate for clothes, Juan Tamad decides to make a suit out of banana leaves. He carefully weaves the leaves together and makes a suit that he wears home.

Resolution:

When Juan Tamad arrives home wearing the banana leaf suit, his mother scolds him for being lazy. She tells him that he could have easily fetched water and cooked rice if he had just tried. Juan Tamad realizes the error of his ways and promises to change his lazy habits.

In conclusion, "Juan Tamad" is a classic tall tale in the Philippines that uses humor to teach a valuable lesson about the importance of hard work. The story follows Freytag's Pyramid, with the exposition introducing the protagonist, the rising action building up to the climax where Juan Tamad faces a challenge, and the falling action and resolution showing the consequences of his actions and his change of heart
