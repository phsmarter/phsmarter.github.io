1. Disagreeing the "No homework policy" argumentative essay for debate.Introduction:Argument1:Argument2:Argument3:Conclusion:​


Answer:

Introduction:

As students, we are accustomed to receiving homework as part of our academic life. However, the debate over the effectiveness of homework has been ongoing for years, with some advocating for the abolishment of homework altogether. While the idea of no homework policy may sound attractive to students, it is not a feasible solution to improve the educational system. In fact, homework is an essential component of learning, and it helps students develop crucial skills that they will need in the future.

Argument 1: Homework Helps Students Learn and Retain Information

One of the primary reasons why homework is essential is that it reinforces what students have learned in the classroom. By completing homework assignments, students are more likely to remember the information they have learned and retain it for a more extended period. Moreover, homework provides an opportunity for students to apply what they have learned in the classroom to real-life situations. This helps them develop a better understanding of the subject matter, and it enables them to see the practical applications of the knowledge they have gained.

Argument 2: Homework Develops Key Skills

Homework also helps students develop key skills that are essential for success in both their academic and personal lives. By completing homework assignments, students learn how to manage their time effectively, prioritize tasks, and meet deadlines. These are skills that will serve them well in college and beyond, as well as in their careers. Additionally, homework encourages students to think critically and independently, as they must solve problems on their own without the help of a teacher.

Argument 3: Homework Prepares Students for the Future

Homework is not just about learning subject matter; it is also about preparing students for the future. In college and the workforce, students will be required to complete assignments independently and manage their time effectively. Homework is an excellent way to develop these skills, and it provides students with a taste of what they can expect in the future. Additionally, homework teaches students responsibility and accountability, as they are expected to complete assignments on time and to the best of their abilities.

Conclusion:

While it may be tempting to do away with homework altogether, this is not a practical or effective solution. Homework is a critical component of learning, and it provides numerous benefits to students. It reinforces classroom learning, helps students develop key skills, and prepares them for the future. Rather than abolishing homework, we should focus on improving the quality of homework assignments and finding ways to make them more engaging and relevant to students' lives. Homework should be seen as an opportunity for students to continue their learning outside of the classroom, and as a way to develop essential skills that will serve them well in the future

3. essay about argumentative essay about technology. advantage and disadvantages of having a technology in the campus​


Technology has become an indispensable part of our daily lives, including in the field of education. With the rapid advancement of technology, schools and universities have integrated technology into their curriculum and campus infrastructure. However, the use of technology in the campus has been the subject of debate, as it has both advantages and disadvantages. In this essay, I will argue for and against the use of technology in the campus.

One of the major advantages of technology in the campus is its ability to enhance the learning experience. With the help of technology, students can access a vast array of educational resources, including online libraries, educational videos, and interactive software. This allows for a more personalized and interactive learning experience. Moreover, technology enables collaboration between students and teachers through online discussion forums and video conferencing.

Another advantage of technology in the campus is its ability to increase efficiency. With the use of technology, students can submit their assignments online, eliminating the need for printing and physically submitting papers. This saves time and resources for both students and teachers. In addition, technology can be used to automate administrative tasks, such as attendance tracking and grade recording, reducing the workload of teachers.

However, the use of technology in the campus also has its disadvantages. One of the major drawbacks is its potential to distract students from their studies. With the widespread use of social media and other entertainment applications, students may become more interested in browsing the internet than focusing on their studies. Moreover, the over-reliance on technology may lead to a decline in traditional skills, such as handwriting and critical thinking.

Another disadvantage of technology in the campus is its cost. The integration of technology requires a significant financial investment, including the purchase of hardware and software, as well as the cost of maintenance and upgrades. This may not be feasible for all educational institutions, particularly those in developing countries or those with limited resources.

In conclusion, while technology has brought significant advantages to the campus, it is not without its drawbacks. Therefore, it is important for educational institutions to strike a balance between the use of technology and traditional teaching methods, and to be mindful of its potential impact on students and the learning process. The proper integration of technology in the campus can enhance the learning experience and increase efficiency, but it must be done in a way that is responsible and sustainable.
