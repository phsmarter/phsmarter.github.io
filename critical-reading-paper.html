2. Choose a journal on critical thinking and reading topics. Make an Insight Paper from a reading digest (research journal).​


Journal: Thinking and Reading Skills (TARS) Journal

Reading Digest: "The Role of Critical Thinking in Effective Reading" by Emily Lai, published in TARS Journal, Volume 8, Issue 2, 2021.

Insight Paper:

Emily Lai's article "The Role of Critical Thinking in Effective Reading" highlights the importance of critical thinking in improving reading comprehension. Lai argues that critical thinking skills are necessary for readers to make meaning of the text they encounter. In the article, Lai provides an overview of critical thinking and its role in effective reading.

Lai begins the article by defining critical thinking as "the ability to analyze, interpret, evaluate, and synthesize information" (p. 4). She points out that critical thinking is a complex and multifaceted skill that involves cognitive processes such as reasoning, problem-solving, and decision-making. Lai stresses the importance of critical thinking in reading, as it enables readers to engage with the text, question assumptions, and evaluate the author's arguments.

Lai identifies several strategies that readers can use to improve their critical thinking skills. These strategies include identifying the author's purpose and point of view, distinguishing between fact and opinion, recognizing bias and stereotypes, and evaluating evidence and arguments. According to Lai, effective critical thinking in reading requires the application of these skills in a systematic and reflective manner.

The article also highlights the importance of metacognition, or thinking about one's own thinking, in developing critical thinking skills. Lai suggests that readers should be aware of their own cognitive processes and consciously monitor their thinking while reading. This kind of metacognitive awareness can help readers identify areas where they need to improve their critical thinking skills and develop strategies for doing so.

Overall, Lai's article provides a useful overview of critical thinking and its role in effective reading. The strategies and techniques she outlines can serve as a valuable guide for readers looking to improve their critical thinking skills. By incorporating critical thinking into their reading practices, readers can engage more deeply with the text and develop a more nuanced understanding of the author's arguments and ideas.
