1.The context of the story of The Little Prince, written by Antoine de Saint-Exupéry, is primarily set in the Sahara desert. The story revolves around a pilot who has crashed his plane and is stranded in the desert, where he meets the little prince. The prince comes from a small asteroid and is on a journey to explore the universe, visiting different planets and meeting various creatures along the way.

The Little Prince is not only a children's story but also a philosophical tale that explores themes such as loneliness, love, friendship, and the meaning of life. Through the characters and their experiences, the author reflects on the complexities of human nature and the importance of cherishing the simple things in life.

2.The author Antoine de Saint-Exupéry's intention behind "The Little Prince" is to convey philosophical and moral lessons through a simple, yet enchanting story.

Overall, the story of "The Little Prince" aims to inspire readers to value the simple things in life and to appreciate the beauty of the world around us. It encourages us to look beyond the surface and to see the essence of things, and to cherish the relationships we have with others.

4.Some of the literary devices used in the story are:

Allegory, Personification, Metaphor, Symbolism and Irony.

5.The story of "The Little Prince" by Antoine de Saint-Exupéry makes use of various literary techniques to convey its themes and ideas. Some of the most prominent literary techniques used in the story include:Allegory,Imagery, Foreshadowing, Metaphor and Personification.

6.At the end of "The Little Prince" by Antoine de Saint-Exupéry, the little prince tells the narrator that he is ready to return home to his asteroid, even though he will miss their conversations. The little prince says that he will be able to hear the sound of the narrator's laughter from his planet, and that this will be like having stars that can laugh. The narrator is sad to see the little prince go, but he understands that the little prince has to return home.

Before the little prince leaves, he allows a snake to bite him so that he can return to his planet. The narrator is heartbroken, but he sees the little prince's body disappear, leaving only his clothing behind. The narrator realizes that the little prince has returned to his own planet, and he feels comforted by the knowledge that the little prince is happy at home. The story ends with the narrator describing how he continues to look up at the stars and think of the little prince, and how he hopes that others will see the little prince's star and be inspired to seek out their own adventures.

20. what is the plot of the story the little prince


Answer:

The narrator, a pilot, crash-lands his plane in the Sahara desert. While he tries to repair his engine and monitor his dwindling supply of water and food, a little boy appears out of nowhere and simply asks him to draw a sheep. The author then learns that this "little prince" comes from the far away Asteroid B-612, where he left a rose and three volcanoes.

The prince’s most prized possession was the rose, but her tempestuous mien and fickleness tired him and he decided to leave his tiny planet. To his surprise, the flower was visibly sad to see him go, but she urged him on nonetheless.

Before arriving on Earth, the prince visited other planets and met with strange individuals: a king, a vain man, a drunkard, a lamplighter, and a geographer. At the geographer’s suggestion, he visited Earth but dropped down into the Sahara Desert. He found no friends there, but a snake told him that if he ever needed to return to his home planet, he could take advantage of the snake’s bite. He met a fox that taught him to realize that to know others we must “tame” them; this is what makes things and people unique. "The essential is invisible to the eye," says the fox.

The narrator grows to love and cherish the small boy, marveling at how fragile he seems though he adopts a serious air. He and the boy find a well and drink from it, which saves the narrator’s life, but he later, right as he is about to joyfully tell the prince he's fixed his engine, happens upon the prince talking to a yellow snake about poison. The plan is for the prince to reunite with his rose, but this is utterly devastating to the narrator. Nevertheless, the boy lets the snake bite him and falls over into the sand. The narrator cannot find his body the next day so he hopes that the boy is not dead.

The narrator returns to his life but always wonders about the prince and hopes he returns. He asks readers to let him know if they ever meet the prince.

27. what happened in the story of the little prince


What happened in the story of 'The Little Prince'?

The Little Prince or Le Petit Prince is a book written by Antoine de Saint-Exupéry. It is a story that contains reflections on the themes of childhood, friendship, love, adventure and imagination. It also points out the significance of an individual in the world.

In the story, the narrator recalls a memory he had when he was stranded in the Sahara Desert after a plane crash. There, he met a young boy, whom he referred to as the Little Prince. They became drawn to each other. The Little Prince spoke of his story and said that he is from a place called asteroid B612. He spent his days weeding his home of baobab trees, tending to his volcanoes and taking care of his rose. The Prince's rose was a mysterious and a delicate flower. He took care of her lovingly, until one day, he caught her telling lies at him and he felt disappointed about it. So, he decided to leave and start an expedition.

The Little Prince visited six asteroid-planets which are inhabited by foolish adults. First, he encountered a king who believes that he rules the movement of the stars. Next, he met a selfish man who yearns for attention but lives in vanity for himself. Then, a geographer who draws maps but does not leave to go out and explore the places he needs to go to to finish his maps. These visits made the Prince sad and disappointed because of the complexity and way of thinking of these foolish adults.

The Prince then decided to travel to the Earth. He first arrived at the desert where he met a snake who said that he can send the Prince back home only if he is to be bitten and is poisoned by his venom. The Prince declined and went on his way and discovered a rose garden only to find out that his rose wasn't unique and not one of a kind after all. Once again, deeply disappointed, the Prince lied down in the grass and cried. But then finally, he met a fox. The fox persuaded the Prince to tame him and eventually taught him the value of friendship. He taught the Prince that it is through friendship that two separate and different beings become unique to one another and that through friendship, life discovers its true meaning.

Contented and full of wisdom, the Little Prince went to and allowed the desert snake to bite him so that he can return to his planet. At his very last day, he met the pilot (the narrator) and told him the greatest lesson he learned. He expressed: It is only in the heart that one can see rightly. What is essential is invisible to the eye.

The two parted and the Little Prince returned to his planet. The story ended with the narrator requesting for the readers to look at the skies and to remember the Little Prince and the things that he taught him dearly.
