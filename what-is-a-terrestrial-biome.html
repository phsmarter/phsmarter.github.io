5. Compare and contrast the climates and communities of two terrestrial biomes. Include a basic description of how their locations on the planet affect their temperature and rainfall.​


Answer:

RAINFOREST

&

TUNDRA

Explanation:

Rainforests:

Tropical rain forests have a year-round hot and humid climate. Most rain forests receive a lot of rain, roughly 210-450 millimeters each year on average.

A diverse diversity of plant communities can be found in the Wet Tropics. Certain indigenous people like the Kayapo and the Yanomamo communities have lived in the Amazon rainforest for several years.

The rainforests affect rainfall, as dense and dark forests fully cover the area; the tree's transpiration process causes the rainfall. Plants release water as a result of photosynthesis, and thus rainforest has rainfall almost throughout the year. Since they have seen limited temperature change, rainforest species have been reported to be very susceptible to heat.

Tundra:

Tundra regions often have less than 25 cm of rainfall each year, making these areas deserts. They suffer lengthy, cold winters with wind gusts and surface temperatures under zero for six to ten months.

The Tundra is a region that supports grassland and marsh communities and small plants like shrubs. The vegetation is normally disbursed, but it can be uneven due to soil and moisture changes.

The Tundra region receives only about 15-25 cm rainfall, which includes both rain and snowfall. When the sky is clear, the temperature in the Tundra region is cooler in the winter and warm when the sky is foggy.

12. Which do you think of the two biomes (Terrestrial and Aquatic Biomes) is more damaged and is more prone to destruction? Why? ​


Answer:

Terrestrial Biomes is more prone to destruction than the Aquatic Biomes. Logging has depleted many old-growth temperate forests. The increased demand for homes, paper, and other wood products have not allowed for much conservation. More recently, people have begun to realize that logging has cleared much of these forests. Wiser use of the forests and efforts to replant trees have helped to slow down the depletion of these communities.

Tropical forests have fallen victim to timber exploitation, slash and burn farming, and clearfelling for industrial use or cattle ranching, particularly in Latin America. Our increasing demand for meat products has spurred these events. For years, this destruction was occuring at a rapid rate. Over half of the world’s original tropical forests are already gone. Public attention to this exploitation have helped to alleviate the problem somewhat, though many challenges are still to be faced.

29. what are biomes?describe several biomes​


Answer:

A biome /ˈbaɪoʊm/ is a community of plants and animals that have common characteristics for the environment they exist in. They can be found over a range of continents. Biomes are distinct biological communities that have formed in response to a shared physical climate.[1][2] Biome is a broader term than habitat; any biome can comprise a variety of habitats.

Explanation:

There are five major types of biomes: aquatic, grassland, forest, desert, and tundra, though some of these biomes can be further divided into more specific categories, such as freshwater, marine, savanna, tropical rainforest, temperate rainforest, and taiga. Aquatic biomes include both freshwater and marine biomes.
