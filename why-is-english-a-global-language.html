Answer:

1. English as a lingua franca is the concept of using the English language as a way to communicate globally. This means that even people whose first language is something else will be able to understand and speak the same language, which is English.

2. Right now, English is considered to be the best way to communicate with people from other countries and cultures across the globe. There are other languages that are more commonly spoken, but the difference is the number of resources one can use to study the English language. It makes the most sense to use it as the lingua franca.

As for the second question, I think that different English varieties should be recognized. It should not be considered wrong simply because they do not exactly resemble American English or UK English. Philippine English is different because it has been influenced by our local languages and dialects.

3. In my opinion, it is better to stay with different world Englishes. For one thing, even the more acknowledged versions of the language like American English or British English is different. It would take a lot of effort to build a standard version. On top of that, the different versions came about due to cultural differences and contribute to a better understanding of the world in general. In the end, important thing is that language is intelligible and could be used to communicate with other speakers regardless of the version of English they are using.

2.Language is intrinsic to the expression of culture. Language is a fundamental aspect of cultural identity. It is the means by which we convey our innermost self from generation to generation. It is through language that we transmit and express our culture and its values.

3.Society however controls our language by giving us preferences as what are acceptable and not, because each one of us has our own perception or point of view. ... Social changes produce changes in language. This affects values in ways that have not been accurately understood. Language incorporates social values.

4.Sociolinguistics is the descriptive study of the effect of any and all aspects of society, including cultural norms, expectations, and context, on the way language is used, and society's effect on language. It differs from sociology of language, which focuses on the effect of language on society.
