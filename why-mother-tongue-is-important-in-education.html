30. The use of the mother tongue as the primary language in education​.


Answer:

The Use of the mother tongue as the primary language of instruction in education has several potential benefits, including:

   Improved student engagement and motivation, as students are more likely to understand and connect with material taught in their native language.

   Enhanced cognitive development and language acquisition, as students are better able to process information and learn new concepts when taught in their mother tongue.

   Reduced feelings of exclusion and cultural marginalization, as students are able to connect with their own linguistic and cultural heritage.

However, there are also some potential drawbacks to this approach, including:

   Limited access to subjects taught in other languages, which may restrict career and academic opportunities for students.

   Difficulty transitioning to instruction in other languages, which may hinder development of multilingualism.

   Potential reduction in language proficiency and literacy in the dominant language of the country, which may impact future educational and career opportunities.

Ultimately, the decision to use the mother tongue as the primary language of instruction should be based on a careful consideration of the specific context, including the students, teachers, and educational resources available.

Answer:

The use of the mother tongue as the primary language in education refers to the teaching of academic subjects in the native language of students. The idea is that students learn best when they are taught in a language that they are most comfortable and familiar with. This approach has been shown to improve students' understanding of the material and their motivation to learn.

It also helps preserve their cultural identity and values, which can be lost when they are taught in a foreign language. Additionally, students are able to transfer the knowledge and skills they learn in their mother tongue to other languages, including the national or official language.

The use of the mother tongue as the primary language in education is therefore seen as a way to improve students' education outcomes, as well as to promote linguistic and cultural diversity.
