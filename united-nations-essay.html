During the Second World War, the need for an agency that could act as a successor to the League of Nations was felt and so, the United Nations Charter was drafted at a conference in April-June 1945. This charter came into effect on 24 October 1945, and the UN began its operations.

The general purpose of the United Nations was in homogeneity with that of the League of Nations. Its structure and functions too are in congruity with its predecessor. Same goes with its principal organs and related agencies. In some respects, however, the UN constituted a very different organization, especially with regard to its objective of maintaining international peace and security and its commitment to economic and social development.

The UN’s mission to preserve world peace was convoluted in its early decades by the presence of Cold War between the US and Soviet Union and their respective allies. The organization participated in major actions in Korea and Congo as well as approving the creation of the state of Israel in 1947. The organization’s membership grew significantly following widespread decolonization in the 1960s, and by the 1970s its budget for economic and social development programmes far outstripped its exenditure on peacekeeping. After the end of the Cold War, the UN took on major military and peacekeeping missions across the world with varying degrees of success.

15. Essay. Why are the Universal Declaration of Human Rights are important for our country and other members of the United Nations?


Answer:

The Universal Declaration of Human Rights (UDHR) is a critical document that outlines the basic rights and freedoms that every human being is entitled to, regardless of race, gender, religion, or any other status. The UDHR serves as a foundation for many international human rights treaties and national constitutions and is considered to be one of the most important human rights instruments in the world.

The importance of the UDHR for countries and members of the United Nations (UN) cannot be overstated. Firstly, the UDHR helps to protect human rights and ensure that all individuals are treated with dignity and respect. This document outlines the rights that everyone should enjoy, such as the right to life, liberty, and security, the right to freedom from discrimination, and the right to participate in government.

Secondly, the UDHR promotes equality by recognizing the inherent dignity and equal worth of all human beings. This helps to ensure that everyone is treated fairly and without discrimination, regardless of their background or status. By promoting equality, the UDHR helps to create a more just and equitable world.

Thirdly, the UDHR helps to ensure accountability by establishing a set of universal standards for human rights. By holding governments and other actors accountable for their actions, the UDHR helps to protect the rights of individuals and groups that might otherwise be vulnerable.

Finally, the UDHR promotes a culture of human rights, which helps to build a more peaceful world. By recognizing the rights and freedoms of all human beings, the UDHR encourages cooperation and understanding among nations, leading to greater stability and security.

In conclusion, the UDHR is an essential document that provides a foundation for human rights protection and promotion. By establishing a set of universal standards, the UDHR helps to ensure that everyone is treated with dignity and respect, and it plays a critical role in promoting equality, accountability, and peace in the world.
