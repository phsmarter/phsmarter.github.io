14. Recommendation: (in terms of the design, propaganda technique and language used in the taglines) nike and adidas​


In terms of design, Nike and Adidas have both established their own unique and recognizable branding, with Nike known for their "swoosh" logo and Adidas for their iconic three stripes. Both brands also use bold and eye-catching designs in their advertising campaigns, with Nike often featuring action shots of athletes in motion and Adidas incorporating bright colors and geometric shapes.

In terms of propaganda technique, Nike and Adidas both use a combination of emotional appeals and aspirational messaging to connect with their target audience. Nike often emphasizes the "Just Do It" slogan, which encourages consumers to push past their limits and strive for excellence. Meanwhile, Adidas often emphasizes their "Impossible Is Nothing" slogan, which emphasizes the idea that anything is possible with hard work and determination.

In terms of language, both Nike and Adidas use simple and straightforward language that is easy for consumers to understand and remember. They both use short and memorable taglines, such as Nike's "Just Do It" and Adidas' "Impossible Is Nothing," which have become ingrained in popular culture.

Overall, Nike and Adidas are both highly successful brands with strong and effective advertising strategies. While they may differ in their design and propaganda techniques, they both share a commitment to inspiring consumers to be their best selves and push past their limits.
