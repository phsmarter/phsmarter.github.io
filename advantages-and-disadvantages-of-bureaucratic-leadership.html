16. 13. What leadership style is currently practiced by the Philippine governmentbureaucratichistorical​


The bureaucratic leadership style is the form of leadership currently practiced by the Philippine government in which the government follows a certain line of authority rather than having a single leader that rules and decides. It was first introduced by Max Weber as an answer to the prevalent “favoritism” in society during the period of industrialization. Bureaucratic leadership has 6 core tenets: (1) hierarchy of authority, (2) division of labor, (3) formal selection, (4) career orientation, (5) rules and regulations, and (6) impersonality.

Hierarchy of authority. The government has a line of command, with tasks and responsibilities for each level of authority being clearly defined.Division of labor/task specialization. This kind of leadership style values the appropriate placement of a person that should be appropriate and qualified in each position with specific tasks. Formal selection. Values the merit system in which people are promoted, hired and fired based on their performance. Career orientation. Provides an opportunity for the employee to have a long-term career based on their performance. Rules and regulations. The system needs a clearly defined set of guidelines, procedures, and rules to keep the organization's structure intact.Impersonality. Focuses on the performance of the entire organization rather than individual contributions.

These tenets can be seen in the three branches of the Philippine government - the executive, legislative, and judiciary with different positions, institutions, and agencies that have different defined expectations and roles. Under the executive branch, we have the president, vice-president, and the cabinets that consist of the secretaries of the 22 executive departments or agencies. Under the legislative branch, we have the senators and representatives or the congressmen and congresswomen. Under the judiciary, we have the Chief justice, secretary of justice, and judges. For instance, the legislative branch will make laws and it is up to the executive branch to implement them. While the law cannot always stand and explain itself to the public, it is the judiciary branch that will explain it, and give justice, whenever the Filipinos need it.

Aside from Bureaucratic leadership, the Philippine government also practices democratic leadership in which they value the participation of the masses in policy-making.

What Is a Bureaucracy?

The term bureaucracy refers to a complex organization that has multilayered systems and processes. The systems and processes that are put in place effectively make decision-making slow. They are designed to maintain uniformity and control within the organization.

A bureaucracy describes the methods that are commonly established in governments and large organizations, such as corporations. A bureaucracy is pivotal in the administration of the entity's rules and regulations.

1

KEY TAKEAWAYS

The word bureaucracy implies a complex structure with multiple layers and procedures.

The systems that are put in place under a bureaucracy make decision-making slow.

Bureaucracies can render systems formal and rigid, which is needed when following safety procedures is critical.

The term bureaucracy is often criticized and deemed negative because of the implication that procedures are more important than efficiency.

The Glass-Steagall Act is a good example of effective bureaucracy in place in the United States.

Advantages of democratic leadership include creating employee job satisfaction and encouraging innovation and creative solutions to organizational issues and problems. Disadvantages can include reduction of efficiency and problems with accountability.

AUTOCRATIC LEADERSHIP

Disadvantages of Autocratic Leadership

Low Employee Morale. One of the biggest disadvantages of exercising autocratic leadership is that it can result in low employee morale. ...

Leads To Resentment. ...

Develops A System Of Dependence.

Decisions can be made very quickly

In this leadership style, there is no need to gather consensus from your team or from other parts of the organisation when making decisions. Your say goes. Because of this, autocratic leadership lends itself particularly well to high-stress situations where quick decisions are vital.

LAISSEZ-FAIRE LEADERSHIP

advantage of laissez-faire leadership?

Laissez-faire leaders have an attitude of trust and reliance on their employees. They don't micromanage or get too involved, they don't give too much instruction or guidance. Instead laissez-faire leaders let their employees use their creativity, resources, and experience to help them meet their goals.

Disadvantages of laissez-faire style include:

Confusion. Since the team receives very little guidance, they might get confused about the clarity of the role and job they must perform.

Isolation. Too much independence can lead to isolation and affect team-building opportunities.
