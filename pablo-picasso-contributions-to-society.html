12. describe the sculptor painting by Pablo picasso​


Answer:

Pablo Picasso was primarily known for his contributions to the art movement of Cubism, which sought to depict objects and people from multiple perspectives simultaneously. However, he was also a talented sculptor and created many works in this medium as well.

One of his most famous sculptural works is "Head of a Woman", which he created in 1909-1910. This work is a bronze sculpture of a woman's head, with sharp angles and jagged lines that are characteristic of Cubist art. The face is split into geometric shapes, with a sharp nose and jutting chin, and the eyes are deeply set and almost abstract in their appearance.

Picasso's sculpture is a departure from traditional representations of the human form, which often emphasized smooth lines and curves. Instead, "Head of a Woman" is characterized by its sharp, angular forms and rough, textured surface. It is a powerful example of how Picasso's innovative style transformed traditional ideas of sculpture and helped to redefine what was considered "art" in the early 20th century.

14. description of the artwork of pablo picasso​


Answer:

The Soup (1902-03)

La Soupe is characteristic of the somber melancholy of Picasso's Blue Period, and it was produced at the same time as a series of other pictures devoted to themes of destitution, old age, and blindness. The picture conveys something of Picasso's concern with the miserable conditions he witnessed while coming of age in Spain, and it is no doubt influenced by the religious painting he grew up with, and perhaps specifically by El Greco. But the picture is also typical of the wider Symbolist movement of the period. In later years Picasso dismissed his Blue Period works as "nothing but sentiment"; critics have often agreed with him, even though many of these pictures are iconic, and of course, now unbelievably expensive.
