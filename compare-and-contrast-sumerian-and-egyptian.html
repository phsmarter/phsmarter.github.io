2. compare & contrast: sumerian astronomy and Egyptian astronomy​


Answer:

- Sumerian and Egyptian civilizations developed complex astronomical systems that were crucial to their societies.

- However, there are significant differences in the way they approached astronomy.

- Sumerians were more focused on predicting celestial events, such as eclipses and planetary movements, while Egyptians used their astronomical knowledge to develop a calendar system that could predict the flooding of the Nile River.

- The Sumerians also believed that the planets had divine powers and were associated with different gods, while Egyptians saw their gods as being connected to specific constellations.

- Despite these differences, both civilizations made significant contributions to astronomy and helped lay the foundation for modern astronomy today.

8. Compare the neolithic and Egyptian arts


Neolithic art and Egyptian art developed in different regions and at different times, so there are some notable differences between the two.

Neolithic art refers to the art produced by hunter-gatherer societies and early agricultural communities from approximately 10,000 BCE to 2,000 BCE. Neolithic art is characterized by its simplicity and use of natural materials such as stone, bone, and clay. Neolithic art often takes the form of small figurines, pottery, and rock paintings, and is often associated with ritualistic or spiritual practices.

In contrast, Egyptian art developed during the Old, Middle, and New Kingdoms of Ancient Egypt, from approximately 3000 BCE to 30 BCE. Egyptian art is characterized by its formal, stylized representations of people and animals, often in profile, as well as its use of hieroglyphs and other symbolic imagery. Egyptian art served many purposes, including religious, political, and funerary functions, and was often executed in stone or painted on walls.

While both Neolithic and Egyptian art were often used for ritualistic or spiritual purposes, Egyptian art was much more sophisticated than Neolithic art, both in terms of its materials and techniques and its conceptual complexity. Egyptian art was much more concerned with the representation of the human form and with conveying specific meanings and messages through symbolic imagery. Neolithic art, on the other hand, was much simpler and more focused on the natural world and the rhythms of daily life.

Answer: Neolithic art and Egyptian art are two distinct styles of art that emerged in different time periods and regions. Neolithic art, which occurred during the New Stone Age, was primarily functional and created for practical purposes. It mainly consisted of pottery, terracotta sculptures, statuettes, and megalithic structures. On the other hand, Egyptian art emerged during the dynastic periods of the first three millennia BCE in the Nile valley regions of Egypt and Nubia. It consisted of architectural monuments, sculptures, paintings, and applied crafts and was primarily created for religious and political purposes. Egyptian art was highly stylized and symbolic, focusing on representing the human form and the gods. While both art styles were influenced by their respective cultures and time periods, they differed in their purpose, materials, and techniques.

These civilizations appear to have developed in response to their environment and in reaction to their human need for survival and security.

The first human civilizations of Mesopotamia, Egypt, Indus River Valley, and China (but not Mesoamerica) all developed around rivers; as such they were called alluvial, or located in the plains surrounding a river. Irrigation allowed water to be used farther away from the river itself, thereby allowing more people to use the river water. Plus, controlling the flood water was a necessity. 27. compare and contrast the Angono Petroglyphs and Egyptian Heiroglyphs


Answer:

Petroglyphs are drawings on rocks, often found in caves. They are a representation of an image and only that image, such as the cave paintings like those found in the Lascaux Paleolithic renderings, found in a complex of caves in the Dordogne region of southwestern France, because of their exceptional quality, size, sophistication and antiquity. Estimated to be up to 20,000 years old, the paintings consist primarily of large animals, once native to the region. Hieroglyphics are carvings, more often, in rock that have phonetic elements, approaching a written language. Egyptian and Maya hieroglyphs are examples of this phenomenon.
