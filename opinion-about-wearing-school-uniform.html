8. wearing of school uniform opinion​


The wearing of a school uniform is important in fostering student pride, self-confidence, and a sense of community. These elements support students' wellbeing by relieving them of the burden of choosing what to wear and the stress of living up to peer expectations.

The benefits for students in wearing uniform

Student self-esteem will increase and their individuality will be enhanced by wearing school uniforms. First, pupils may feel more equal when they wear matching uniforms. Because they do not dress in name-brand clothing like the wealthier children, poor students would no longer feel excluded.

They assist children in dressing appropriately for school, which will boost their self-assurance. Students understand that they are all treated equally by the educational institution. Children who properly wear their school uniforms will quickly learn the value of treating others with respect.

When uniforms are mandated, students experience a diminished sense of identity. According to a new nationwide survey, school uniforms don't appear to have any impact on young kids' behavior or overall attendance, in contrast to what many parents and instructors believe.

Wearing a uniform to work has many benefits, one of which is that it makes it simpler for clients and customers to recognize and spot employees of various businesses and organizations, allowing us to determine which business or organization a particular employee works for as well as the fact that the majority of customers will instantly recognize your brand.

Schools remove the stress caused by name brands and fashion trends by mandating pupils to dress in a manner akin to a uniform. According to studies, uniforms improve the learning environment for developing brains by fostering greater focus and fewer distractions, as well as by reducing bullying incidents, on-campus violence, and discipline referrals.

10. editorial article about wearing of school uniform and id​


In the Philippines, wearing school uniforms and IDs are important aspects of daily life for students. Wearing a uniform provides a sense of identity and pride among students, as well as an assurance that all members of the school community can be easily identified by faculty and staff.Moreover, having standardized dress codes eliminates any sort of economic or social class distinction between peers since everyone looks alike in their uniforms. This helps to foster unity within the student body while also promoting discipline in educational settings by emphasizing focus on academic performance rather than fashion trends or material possessions.Furthermore, uniforms can provide protection against harsh weather conditions. By wearing the same type of clothing and fabrics as their peers, students are able to stay warm or cool depending on the season without having to worry about buying new clothes all the time.Lastly, wearing IDs is important for security purposes. With ID cards, school administrators can easily identify who should and shouldn't be in the premises at any given time.This helps to ensure the safety of students and staff alike as it limits access to unauthorized personnel.In conclusion, wearing school uniforms and IDs are important for many reasons. From fostering unity among students to providing protection against weather conditions and ensuring safety on campus, the advantages of having a standardized dress code far outweigh any disadvantages.

13. convictions about wearing school uniform and explanation


Convictions about wearing school uniforms are opinions or beliefs held by different individuals or groups about the benefits or drawbacks of wearing uniforms in school. These convictions can be varied and often debated among educators, parents, and students.

Some people believe that school uniforms promote a sense of unity, equality, and professionalism among students, while reducing distractions and clothing-related bullying. Others argue that uniforms limit freedom of expression and individuality, do not address underlying issues, and can be uncomfortable and an additional cost burden for families.

In summary, there are both pros and cons to wearing school uniforms, and the decision to implement them should be based on a comprehensive evaluation of the potential benefits and drawbacks. Ultimately, the goal should be to create a positive and inclusive learning environment for all students

27. Essay about agree or disagree to wearing a school uniform in pandemic?


Coming from someone who has worn a school uniform for most of her life, I would say that it is necessary, but only to a certain extent.

Sure, uniforms help prevent disparities in the way students dress, inappropriate attire, and prevent poorer kids from being left out or laughed at.

However, children (mostly teens) will always find a way to show off what they have, whether it be in the form of a LV bag or expensive stationary. Honestly, uniforms do not really help to to level the playing field, it just makes the disparity less obvious at a first glance (extremely poor kids could not even afford uniforms and theirs were often faded and too big so uniforms did not really help them at all).

But when schools are too strict about the uniform, it can not only make school a less pleasant experience, but also hinder learning. In my old school, every thing you wore was regulated, from your shoes to the colour of your hair tie (that tiny elastic had to be dark blue or black. No exceptions). They seemed to be under the impression that my having a purple hair tie was a sign of my rebellion and that I was on the way to becoming a gangster. When the level of scrutiny reaches that level (common in Singapore), we must really ask, does this really benefit students?

I would suggest a happy medium. A school shirt has to be worn, but students are free to wear anything they would like with it (within reason of course). This saves them some time during decision making, but they do not need to go to great lengths to adhere to ridiculous and meaningless rules.
