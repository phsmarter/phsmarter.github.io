WHAT CAUSES GLOBAL WARMING?

Generating power

Generating electricity and heat by burning fossil fuels such as coal, oil and natural gas causes a large chunk of global emissions. Most electricity is still produced from fossil fuels; only about a quarter comes from wind, solar and other renewable sources.

Manufacturing goods

Manufacturing and industry produce emissions, mostly from burning fossil fuels to produce energy for making things like cement, iron, steel, electronics, plastics, clothes and other goods. Mining and other industrial processes also release gases.

Cutting down forests

Cutting down forests to create farms or pastures, or for other reasons, causes emissions, since trees, when they are cut, release the carbon they have been storing. Since forests absorb carbon dioxide, destroying them also limits nature‚Äôs ability to keep emissions out of the atmosphere.

Consuming too much

Your home and use of power, how you move around, what you eat and how much you throw away all contribute to greenhouse gas emissions. So does the consumption of goods such as clothing, electronics and plastics.

16. Give A Theory How To Solve The Problem About Parental Pressure On Teenagers‚Äã


Answer:

There are several theories and strategies that can be used to address the problem of parental pressure on teenagers. Here are a few:

Communication Theory: Communication is key in any relationship, including the parent-teenager relationship. Using communication theory, parents can learn effective communication skills and strategies to effectively communicate with their teenagers. This includes active listening, acknowledging their feelings and concerns, expressing empathy and understanding, and working towards a mutually acceptable solution.

Self-Determination Theory: According to Self-Determination Theory, people are motivated by three basic needs: autonomy, competence, and relatedness. Parents can apply this theory by allowing their teenagers to make decisions and take responsibility for their own lives. This includes giving them the freedom to pursue their own interests and passions, and supporting them in their efforts.

Cognitive Behavioral Theory: Cognitive Behavioral Theory suggests that our thoughts, emotions, and behaviors are interconnected. Parents can use this theory to help their teenagers identify negative thought patterns and develop positive coping strategies. This includes encouraging positive self-talk, developing problem-solving skills, and promoting healthy lifestyle habits.

Family Systems Theory: Family Systems Theory focuses on the dynamics of the family unit as a whole. Parents can apply this theory by recognizing the role they play in their teenager's life and the impact of their behavior on the entire family. This includes fostering a positive and supportive family environment, setting clear expectations and boundaries, and modeling healthy behaviors.

Ultimately, the key to addressing parental pressure on teenagers is to understand that every teenager is unique, and there is no one-size-fits-all solution. By applying these theories and strategies, parents can work towards fostering healthy and positive relationships with their teenagers while allowing them the space to grow and develop into their own individuals.
