23. what evidence is there that these changes have had a positive influence on organizational performance ​


There is a growing body of evidence that suggests that changes in organizational practices and culture can have a positive influence on organizational performance. Some examples of evidence include:

1. Increased employee satisfaction and engagement: Studies have shown that organizations that prioritize employee satisfaction and engagement tend to have higher levels of productivity, innovation, and customer satisfaction. For example, a study by Gallup found that companies with high levels of employee engagement had 21% higher profitability than those with low levels of engagement.

2. Improved financial performance: Organizations that implement changes in their management practices and culture often see improvements in their financial performance. For example, a study by McKinsey & Company found that companies with diverse leadership teams were 33% more likely to have above-average profitability than those with less diverse teams.

3. Higher levels of innovation: Organizations that foster a culture of innovation and creativity tend to be more successful in developing new products and services. According to a study by the Boston Consulting Group, companies that prioritize innovation have 2.7 times higher revenue growth than those that do not.

4. Increased customer satisfaction: Organizations that prioritize customer satisfaction tend to have higher levels of customer loyalty and retention. A study by the Harvard Business Review found that companies that prioritize customer satisfaction have higher levels of customer loyalty and are more likely to retain customers over the long term.

Overall, the evidence suggests that changes in organizational practices and culture can have a positive influence on organizational performance. By prioritizing employee satisfaction and engagement, financial performance, innovation, and customer satisfaction, organizations can create a culture that is conducive to success and growth.

26. 1. HOW IS ORGANIZATIONAL CULTURE CREATED AND COMMUNICATED?ESSAY (MINIMUM WORDS 500)​


Answer:

Organizational culture refers to the shared values, beliefs, customs, behaviors, and attitudes that characterize an organization. It is an intangible aspect of an organization that is shaped by its history, leadership, and employees. Organizational culture is created through a variety of processes and is communicated through various channels.

One way that organizational culture is created is through the actions and behaviors of its leaders. Leaders play a crucial role in shaping organizational culture, as their behaviors and attitudes are often emulated by employees. Leaders who prioritize transparency, collaboration, and respect will often create a culture that reflects these values.

Another way that organizational culture is created is through the hiring and selection of employees. Organizations often look for candidates who share their values and beliefs, and who will fit in with their culture. This can be done through a variety of methods, such as behavioral interviews, personality assessments, and reference checks.

Organizational culture is also communicated through a variety of channels. One of the most important ways that culture is communicated is through the stories and legends that are shared among employees. These stories often reinforce the values and beliefs of the organization and help to create a shared identity among employees.

Another way that organizational culture is communicated is through the symbols and artifacts that are present in the workplace. These can include physical objects, such as logos, trophies, and awards, as well as more intangible symbols, such as dress codes, language, and rituals.

Finally, organizational culture is communicated through the behavior of employees. Employees who consistently act in ways that are consistent with the values and beliefs of the organization help to reinforce the culture and create a sense of shared identity among colleagues.

In conclusion, organizational culture is a complex and multifaceted aspect of an organization that is created and communicated through a variety of processes and channels. By prioritizing transparency, collaboration, and respect, and by hiring employees who share their values and beliefs, organizations can create a culture that reflects their identity and mission. By communicating this culture through stories, symbols, and employee behavior, organizations can create a sense of shared identity and purpose that helps to drive success and foster a positive work environment.
