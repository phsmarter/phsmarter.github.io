1. informative speech about tree planting


Answer:

Tree planting means planting new seedlings to replace the old trees that have been cut down. Replacing the cut-down trees is needed because growing trees takes time. These newly planted seedlings that will soon grow and become mature trees will help in the prevention of soil erosion, flood and even landslide. Tree planting preserves the beauty of our natural environment, conserves our natural resources and prevents the extinction of some wild animals as trees become their source of food and also the place where they can live. People also need trees as source of wood for building a shelter, a source of food and of herbal medicine.

Explanation:

The Department of Environment and Natural Resources is a government agency in the Philippines that advocates the preservation and conservation of natural resources. This government body conducts environmental awareness seminar informing the people the importance of tree planting.

Tree planting is important because it prevents the forest from becoming bald. Trees hold water from the soil and hence prevents flood and landslide whenever there is typhoon. Trees also serves as source of food for the insects as butterflies feed on their leaves and animals eat the fruits. Trees also serves as a place for refuge for both human beings and animals.

The steps in tree planting are the following.

1. Coordinate with the Department of Environment and Natural Resources regarding the need for planting trees in the affected area of your town.

2. Encourage some volunteers who are interested to do tree planting.

3. Ask for seedlings and some fertilizers.

4. Go to the area that needs tree planting, bring your seedlings, shovel and some fertilizers and water.

4. Start planting trees but make sure to plant trees not so close to each other and not too far away, just enough distance.

5. Make a small fence to protect the seedlings.
