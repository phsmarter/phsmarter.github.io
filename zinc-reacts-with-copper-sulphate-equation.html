Further explanation

There are several possible reactions to chemical reactions

1. Synthesis 2. Decomposition 3. Single replacement 4. Double-Replacement

Fe reacts with CuSO₄ and form FeSO₄ and Cu

From The metal activity series (voltaic series)

Li-K-Ba-Ca-Na-Mg-Al-Mn- (H2O) -Zn-Cr-Fe-Cd-Co-Ni-Sn-Pb- (H) -Cu-Hg-Ag-Pt-Au

we can see that Fe is to the left of Cu so that Fe is a strong reducing agent and can push the Cu metal so that reactions occur

And this reaction is classified as a Single replacement reaction:

A + BC ---> AC + B

in which one element replaces the other elements of a compound to produce new elements and compounds

Substances involved in chemical reactions are reactants and products

The reactants in this reaction:

Fe and CuSO₄

While the reaction product :

FeSO₄ and Cu

13. chemical formula of Copper (II) sulphate?


Answer:

Copper(II) sulfate, also known as copper sulphate, are the inorganic compounds with the chemical formula CuSO4(H2O)x, where x can range from 0 to 5. The pentahydrate (x = 5) is the most common form. Older names for this compound include blue vitriol, bluestone, vitriol of copper, and Roman vitriol.
