11. what are the planning materials handling system?


Answer:

PLANNING PRINCIPLE

Be clear objectives objectives and functional specifications of the proposed methods.

STANDARDIZATION PRINCIPLE.

Equipment, controls and software must be standardized within the limits that achieve global performance objectives without sacrificing flexibility, modularity and production.

WORK PRINCIPLE.

Material handling work should be minimized without sacrificing productivity or the required level of operation.

ERGONOMIC PRINCIPLE.

Human capabilities and limitations must be recognized to ensure safe and effective operations

UNIT LOAD PRINCIPLE.

The unit loads must be of the appropriate size and be configured according to which they achieve a material flow and the inventory objectives at each stage of the supply chain.

SPACE UTILIZATION PRINCIPLE.

Effective and efficient use of available space must be made.

SYSTEM PRINCIPLE.

Material movement and storage activities must be fully integrated to form an operating system that encompasses reception, inspection, storage, production, assembly, packaging, unification, order selection, shipping, transportation, and claims handling.

AUTOMATION PRINCIPLE.

Material handling operations should be automated where possible in order to improve operational efficiency, increase responses, improve consistency and predictability, and lower operating costs.

If we pay attention to how the industry has changed in the last 20 years, the number of people involved in each of the stages of the process is less and less with new technologies, if it is automated, it gains in reliability, efficiency, safety , time, standardization and transportation, since there is better coordination.

Currently, among the main technologies and solutions that are being used to improve the end of line process, stand out robotized arms systems, automatic palletizing, AGV’s among others.

ENVIRONMENTAL PRINCIPLE.

The environmental impact and energy consumption must be considered criteria when selecting equipment for handling materials.

LIFE CYCLE COST PRINCIPLE.

Develop a comprehensive economic analysis that takes into account the entire life cycle of material handling systems.

17. For your understanding what is materials planning?​


Answer:

Material requirements planning (MRP) is a system for calculating the materials and components needed to manufacture a product. It consists of three primary steps: taking inventory of the materials and components on hand, identifying which additional ones are needed and then scheduling their production or purchase.

18. The lesson plan is a pedagogical tool for planning the teaching activity that materializes?a. The teaching planb. The disciplinary contentc. The pedagogical political projectd. The course plan​


Answer:

The lesson plan materializes the disciplinary content, as it is characterized as a pedagogical instrument for planning the teaching activity.

Therefore, the correct answer is b. The disciplinary content.

The lesson plan and the organization of disciplinary content:

The lesson plan includes not only the step by step of the classes to be taught in a given period of a week or a month, depending on the stage of education, as in early childhood education, where this planning can be daily but the delimitation of the content to be experienced in the classroom.

The themes worked are usually linked to skills and abilities in development, such as the exhibition of board games, which allow, in addition to a playful teaching, the improvement of student logical reasoning, especially within the exact areas, proving to be excellent methodologies of teaching in the most elementary grades.

In the absence of a lesson plan, the teacher can feel lost, consequently, the lessons lose their meaning.

Project Title: DIY Wooden Bench

Project Goal: To construct a wooden bench for outdoor use.

Materials:

8 wooden boards (2 x 6 x 8 feet)

Wood screws (2.5 inches)

Wood glue

Wood stain

Sandpaper

Tools:

Circular saw

Drill

Drill bits

Screwdriver

Clamps

Tape measure

Pencil

Safety glasses

Procedure:

Cut the wooden boards to the following lengths using a circular saw:

4 boards at 36 inches for the seat

4 boards at 17 inches for the legs

4 boards at 14 inches for the braces

2 boards at 30 inches for the armrests

Sand all of the wooden boards with sandpaper to ensure a smooth surface.

Lay out two of the 36-inch seat boards on a flat surface, with the edges flush. Apply wood glue to the ends of the two remaining seat boards and place them between the first two boards. Clamp the boards together and allow the glue to dry for at least two hours.

Attach the legs to the seat using wood screws. Place two legs at each end of the bench seat, making sure they are flush with the edge of the seat. Use a drill to make pilot holes for the screws, and then screw the legs into place.

Attach the braces to the legs using wood screws. Place one brace on each side of the bench, with the top edge of the brace 14 inches from the ground. Use a drill to make pilot holes for the screws, and then screw the braces into place.

Attach the armrests to the top of the braces using wood screws. Place one armrest on each side of the bench, with the top edge of the armrest 27 inches from the ground. Use a drill to make pilot holes for the screws, and then screw the armrests into place.

Sand the entire bench with sandpaper to smooth out any rough edges or surfaces.

Apply a coat of wood stain to the bench to protect it from the elements and give it a finished look.

Allow the bench to dry completely before using it.

Project Title: DIY Picture Frame

Project Goal: To create a wooden picture frame for a standard 8 x 10 inch photo.

Materials:

2 wooden boards (1 x 2 x 8 feet)

Wood glue

Wood screws (1 inch)

Wood filler

Sandpaper

Picture hanging hardware

Tools:

Miter saw or hand saw

Drill

Drill bits

Screwdriver

Clamps

Tape measure

Pencil

Procedure:

Cut two pieces of wood to 12 inches in length using a miter saw or hand saw. These will be the top and bottom pieces of the frame.

Cut two more pieces of wood to 10 inches in length using a miter saw or hand saw. These will be the left and right pieces of the frame.

Sand all of the wooden pieces with sandpaper to ensure a smooth surface.

Apply wood glue to the ends of the top and bottom pieces, and then attach them to the left and right pieces to form a rectangle. Use clamps to hold the pieces together while the glue dries.

Once the glue is dry, reinforce the joints by drilling pilot holes and screwing the pieces together using wood screws.

Use wood filler to fill any gaps or holes in the joints, and then sand the entire frame again to ensure a smooth surface.

Apply a coat of paint or stain to the frame, if desired.

Once the paint or stain is dry, attach picture hanging hardware to the back of the frame.

Insert the photo into the frame, and hang it on the wall
