12. create an argument about the "Proposed Mandatory Implementation of ROTC in Senior High School"


The proposed mandatory implementation of ROTC in senior high school is a controversial issue that has sparked debates among educators, parents, and students. Supporters argue that ROTC provides numerous benefits, including promoting patriotism, discipline, physical fitness, and leadership skills. However, opponents argue that mandatory ROTC violates human rights, promotes militarism, and puts students at risk of physical and mental harm.

On the one hand, proponents of mandatory ROTC argue that it instills a sense of pride and loyalty to the country. The program also teaches discipline and respect for authority, which can benefit students in their future careers and personal lives. Moreover, ROTC can provide students with physical fitness training, which is essential to maintaining a healthy lifestyle.

On the other hand, opponents argue that mandatory ROTC violates students' human rights, as it forces them to participate in a program that may conflict with their beliefs and values. They also argue that mandatory ROTC promotes militarism and glorifies war, which is not appropriate for a civilian educational institution. Moreover, students may be at risk of physical and mental harm, as ROTC involves military-style training that can be physically and emotionally demanding.

In conclusion, while the proposed mandatory implementation of ROTC in senior high school may have some benefits, it also raises significant concerns about human rights, militarism, and the safety of students. The decision to implement ROTC should be made with careful consideration of these issues, and alternative programs that promote discipline, physical fitness, and leadership skills should also be considered. Ultimately, the goal should be to provide students with a well-rounded education that prepares them for success in their future careers and personal lives, without compromising their safety and well-being.

22. What are the disadvantages of ROTC?​


Answer:

Joining ROTC—especially if you receive a scholarship—is a serious commitment. You must sign a legally-binding contract that could last up to 12 years. Under some circumstances, you must make this decision before even beginning the program.

 

If you drop out or are expelled from the program, you will face serious consequences, including potential legal action. You will also be required to pay back your scholarship if you have received one. (However, keep in mind that many of these consequences only apply to students who do receive ROTC scholarships.)

 

If you commit any violations of ROTC’s standards or your school’s, or drop below ROTC’s academic requirements, you may be asked to leave the program, and you will still face consequences and potential legal action.

 

While individual branch requirements vary, federal law requires that ROTC members must commit to a four-year active duty, usually directly after college, and an additional 8-year military service. These requirements may be slightly lower for non-scholarship students. Ultimately, this means you need to be certain that serving in the military is something you really want to do. Remember that this also means you’ll need to delay other career or education aspirations until your active service is complete, though you can pursue them while you are fulfilling non-active service requirements.

These skills are crucial not only for success in military careers but also for success in various other fields and aspects of life. ROTC programs also provide students with opportunities to engage in physical fitness activities, which promote healthy habits and wellbeing.

Moreover, ROTC programs are a great way for students to learn about the military, its role in society, and the history of our country. By exposing students to the military, they will gain a deeper understanding of the sacrifices made by the men and women who serve in our armed forces. This knowledge will also help them to become more informed citizens who can make informed decisions about military policies and their impact on society.

Another advantage of including ROTC in the SHS curriculum is that it provides students with a clear path to a military career. The program prepares students for the rigors of military life and provides them with the tools and skills they need to succeed in this demanding field. Furthermore, ROTC programs offer students the opportunity to earn scholarships and other financial benefits, which can make a military career more accessible and affordable.

In conclusion, the inclusion of ROTC in the SHS curriculum is not only necessary but also highly beneficial for students.
