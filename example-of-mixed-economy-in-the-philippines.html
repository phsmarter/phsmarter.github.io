17. who led the Philippines to become a mixed economy?


Answer:

The Philippines has undergone a series of economic reforms over the years that have transformed it into a mixed economy. There is no single individual who can be credited with leading the country to this status.

The mixed economy system in the Philippines developed through a combination of government policies, foreign investments, and the growth of private businesses. The country has a long history of government intervention in the economy, dating back to the post-World War II period when the government implemented import substitution industrialization policies to promote domestic industries.

In the 1980s and 1990s, the Philippines began to liberalize its economy, opening up to foreign investment and implementing market-oriented reforms. This led to increased economic growth, although poverty and income inequality remained persistent challenges.

In recent years, the government has continued to pursue market-oriented reforms, with the goal of further liberalizing the economy and promoting sustainable and inclusive growth. While no one individual can be credited with leading the Philippines to its status as a mixed economy, the journey has been shaped by the efforts of policymakers, businesses, and the people of the Philippines.
