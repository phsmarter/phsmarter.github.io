ColloquialismWhat is a colloquialism?

Colloquialism is a  style of language used to describe informal communication. Colloquial is the most commonly used functional form of speech. Used in everyday conversation and in many informal contexts.

Interjections and expressions are common in colloquial speech. Many colloquial expressions contain “slang”, but some do not. When used colloquially, slang is usually associated with, and sometimes restricted to, a particular social group.

When is slang used in literature?

Slang words and expressions include informal words, phrases, and slang. They are used by writers to create a sense of community and society. Often the author's way of speaking translates into his writing.

Writers use slang intentionally, even if they are not familiar with it. They do this to add a sense of realism to their writing and make it more believable. For example, in a fictional story set in America, authors can use slang to make the reader feel like they are with the characters.

 

Colloquialisms and colloquial language examples

There are many informal versions of the standard form language. Here are some examples:  

'Wanna' → Want to

'Y'all' → You all

'Gonna' → Going to
