1. "Justice delayed is Justice denied" explain this statement​


Answer:

This statement means that if justice is not carried out promptly or efficiently, it is effectively denied to the person seeking it. The idea is that justice should be administered in a timely manner, so that the wronged person can receive the remedy or compensation that they are due. If justice is delayed, it may no longer be relevant or effective in addressing the situation, and the person seeking justice may be left without recourse. Therefore, the saying emphasizes the importance of ensuring that justice is administered swiftly and fairly.

"justice delayed is justice denied" a legal maximum meaning that if legal redress is available for a party that has suffered some injury, but is not forthcoming in a timely fashion, it is effectively the same as having no redress at all. this principle is the basis for the right to a speedy trial and similar rights which are meant to expedite the legal system, becauaebit is unfair for the injured party to have to sustain the injury with little hope for resolution. the phrase has become a rallying cry for legal reformers who view courts or government as acting too slowly in resolving legal issues either bcos the existing system is too complex or overburdened, or bcoz the issue or party in question lacks political favour.
