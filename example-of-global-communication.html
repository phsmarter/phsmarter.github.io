16. What are the types of global/workplace communicator? Mention example for each type.​


Answer:

Communication Types and How to Improve Each One

Verbal (In-Person) Communication. Whenever possible, use face-to-face communication in the workplace to eliminate many of the misunderstandings that can occur. Speaking directly with someone in person allows both of you to see and hear the bigger picture. As you or your team member speak, you can see each other’s facial expressions and body language. The emphasis on focusing on one another and the conversation promotes a feeling of value and credibility. You may think it will take longer for you to leave your office and walk to the other end of the building to speak face-to-face with someone. But having one quick conversation rather than a multitude of emails can actually make it more efficient in the end.

Body Language & Facial Expressions. As mentioned above, your body language and facial expressions play a vital role in how effective or ineffective you are at communicating with staff members. Eye contact makes people feel acknowledged as they talk and listen. A relaxed stance with your arms by your sides encourages employees to feel comfortable in a meeting. If you rehearse what you’re going to say before a meeting, do so in front of a full-length mirror so you can see if what your body language says matches up with your words. Employees will receive your words more positively if your facial expressions and body language don’t put them on edge or the defensive.

Phone Conversations. Use of the phone is a common part of most every business day. Some people enjoy talking to team members by phone. Others dislike it so much that they put it off until the last minute. If the staff you lead is remote and spread out throughout the state, country or world, the phone will be a vital communication tool. You can positively impact the effectiveness of your phone communication by doing three simple things. Smile while you talk (in an appropriate conversation, of course); it sounds silly, but it gives your voice a friendlier tone. Take notes before the conversation and use them to ensure you cover all points. Finally, speak clearly and slowly so your words are understood.

Written Communication. Emails, memos and notes are common forms of written communication. Of all four communication styles, this is the one that can lead to the most misunderstandings. People often read between the lines or feel that there are implied messages or emotions in written forms of communication. In a workplace situation, this should and can be avoided. Before you send an email to your employees, distribute a memo or post an important notice on the staff bulletin board, have an objective pair of eyes read it first. A business coach or mentor can look over your written communication and and provide constructive criticism and helpful suggestions.

Communication between you and your team will always be a work in progress. The tips in this blog post can assist you in becoming more mindful about your written communication while providing you with strategies for improvement. If communication between you and your staff has been problematic, you can zone in on the trouble spots and learn how to change them by working with a business trainer or coach

22. Responding to the needs of a culturally diverse global community. pls po pa help.. pahinge explanation tapos example.. pls pi​


Answer:

Responding to the needs of a culturally diverse global community refers to the ability to address and cater to the needs of individuals from different cultures, religions, and backgrounds, while maintaining respect for their beliefs and values. It involves understanding and acknowledging cultural differences and adapting to these differences to ensure that everyone is included and their needs are met.

An example of this would be a multinational company that has employees from different countries and cultures. To respond to the needs of this culturally diverse community, the company may need to provide language training to its employees who are not fluent in the local language. Additionally, the company may need to consider cultural differences in work practices, religious beliefs, and holidays, and adjust work schedules or policies accordingly. By doing so, the company can create a welcoming and inclusive workplace for all employees, regardless of their cultural background.

A culturally diverse global community would need someone who understands and can communicate with people from a variety of cultures. A nurse who is culturally competent would be a good choice for this type of position. They would be able to understand the needs of the patients and be able to communicate effectively with them.

Answer:

1. Global health initiatives are collective efforts aimed at improving health outcomes worldwide. These initiatives are often spearheaded by international organizations, governments, non-profit organizations, and private sector entities. They focus on addressing health challenges such as communicable and non-communicable diseases, maternal and child health, vaccination programs, health systems strengthening, and access to essential medicines and technologies.

2. Global health initiatives can help sustain health by promoting a collaborative approach to addressing health challenges. They leverage resources, expertise, and technical support to improve health outcomes in different countries. Their activities often involve research, training, and advocacy to influence policies that promote health equity and improve access to essential health services.

3. In my community, one health problem that requires attention through global health initiatives is malnutrition. Many people, especially children, suffer from malnutrition due to inadequate access to nutritious foods. Malnutrition can lead to stunted growth, weakened immune systems, and cognitive impairments. Addressing malnutrition through initiatives such as promoting healthy diets, improving access to clean water, and sanitation can improve the health of the community.

4. As a student, one can contribute to solving global health problems by learning and disseminating information on health challenges and contributing to advocacy efforts aimed at improving access to health services globally. Students can also volunteer in health-related projects and participate in research aimed at finding solutions to health challenges. Additionally, studying health-related fields and pursuing careers in health can contribute to addressing global health problems in various ways.
