12. How mathematics has numerous applications in the world making it indispensable?


Mathematics can be seen everywhere in day-to-day life, in a variety of scenarios. Arithmetic is in the daily transactions, from riding a jeepney to buying in groceries. More complicated, but related, is how companies price their goods to ensure that they earn a profit.

In businesses, quick Math is essential, thus the art of estimating must be learnt by everyone from CEOs to the frontline. Increasingly in businesses as well, decisions are driven by data. Much of what is used in analyzing these data is Math and Statistics.

It's not only businesses, but also the economists and insurance or realty agents who use Mathematics to make sure that everything is financially viable.

Aside from Math in money, mathematical modelling (more on this here: https://brainly.ph/question/1653272 ) can be used to understand or predict natural phenomena such as typhoons and earthquakes.

Physicists and Engineers also use Math to make sure that buildings are stable and satellites work. Software developers use elements of finite mathematics in their codes. Even graphic designers use the Math of ratio and proportion to design their graphics and websites. Not to mention, their image-editing software was made using mathematics as well. Basically, everything on computers is from mathematics. It is no surprise that the father of Computer Science, Alan Turing, was a mathematician.

Even fields that were once thought to be "Math purely for Math's sake" such as Number Theory have found application in cryptography, which is used in encrypting and keeping data safe online.

Math is indeed indispensable, with applications ranging from economics, sciences, businesses, engineering, even arts. The better question, really, is, what field /doesn't/ use Math. Some suggested "religious life", well, even priests and nuns must organize outreach programs and budget for their parishes. It still involves Math.

I'd like to end this by quoting an xkcd comic strip I saw awhile ago. It says:

"Sociology is just applied Psychology.

Psychology is just applied Biology.

Biology is just applied Chemistry.

Which is applied Physics. ..."

Which is applied Math.

Really, most fields have applications of Math in one way or another. I guess the only fields that are /not/ are maybe logic and philosophy.

23. mathematics help numerous applications in the world making it indispensable


Pure abstract formal mathematics is dispensable, means you don’t need to know axioms and theorems to survive in the real world. Just imagine how the human started to live in this planet. Happens that you are not member of the tribe in the middle of nowhere living without computers.

On the other hand, our rational part of the brain uses somehow implicit mathematics. To be precise mathematics is a good way to describe how we process information. For example, when reasoning we do Boolean operations (AND, OR and NOT). Language can also be described mathematically. Also our psychomotor system, our vision, etc. Every aspect of the body could have a mathematical model.

So it’s not easy to define when you are using or not using mathematics. Does counting up to 5 is knowing mathematics? Adding? Subtraction? You definitely want to know those when getting your paycheck.

But again if we rephrase your question as “How powerful is someone knowing mathematics in current society/civilization?”. In general de answer is “very”. What do I mean? I refer to very big corporations doing a lot of money thanks to good resources planning, thanks to mathematics; strong encryption algorithms that decide if you win or lose a war; amazing mathematical models that make nuclear plants run; and so on… so on… Every mass production system has a mathematical models behind. Let’s name some cool products: cars, aircraft, motorbikes, bikes, shoes, shirts, TVs, phones etc. Most of the products you would like to buy were possible because of mathematical models.

Why mathematics are so powerful and or useful? Because they are precise reasoning building blocks. So that allows us to “predict the future” in many aspects. If you don’t use mathematics then you do human estimation, which tends to be good “within known values”. But the problem is when the estimation needs to be done far beyond known values, that’s when we tend to fail. We need precise building blocks that allows us to scale our reasoning and prediction limits. This doesn’t mean we do maths all the time, but we combine maths with all other reasoning techniques to find more and better solutions.
