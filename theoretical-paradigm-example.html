1. What is theoretical paradigm?


In sociology, a few theories provide broad perspectives that help explain many different aspects of social life, and these are called paradigms. Paradigms are philosophical and theoretical frameworks used within a discipline to formulate theories, generalizations, and the experiments performed in support of them.

8. Social Science Theoretical Approaches Under Positivist Paradigm


The positivist framework operates from the assumption that society can and should be studied empirically and scientifically. Positivism also calls for a value-free science, one in which researchers aim to abandon their biases and values in a quest for objective, empirical, and knowable truth.

Positivist thinkers lean strongly on determinism, empiricism, parsimony and generality. 'Determinism' means that events are caused by other circumstances; and hence, understanding such causal links is necessary for prediction and control.

30. example a paradigm shifting in your life following the five steps of kuhn cycle​


Answer:

Here's The Five Steps of Kuhn Cycle

Step 1 - Normal Science: The current paradigm is accepted and widely practiced in the scientific community.

For example, in the field of physics, the widely accepted paradigm for hundreds of years was Newtonian mechanics. This paradigm was used to explain the motion of objects in everyday life, as well as the motion of planets and stars.

Step 2 - Anomaly: Anomalies that cannot be explained by the current paradigm are observed.

However, in the late 1800s and early 1900s, physicists began to observe anomalies in the behavior of light and subatomic particles that could not be explained by Newtonian mechanics. These anomalies include the photoelectric effect, which showed that light behaves like particles, and the double-slit experiment, which showed that subatomic particles exhibit wave-like behavior.

Step 3 - Crisis: The anomalies lead to a crisis in the scientific community as scientists try to reconcile the observations with the current paradigm.

Physicists were unable to reconcile these observations with the current Newtonian paradigm. This led to a crisis in the scientific community, as scientists tried to come up with a new paradigm that could explain these observations.

Step 4 - Revolution: A new paradigm is proposed and accepted by the scientific community.

Eventually, a new paradigm was proposed that could explain these anomalies: quantum mechanics. This new paradigm replaced Newtonian mechanics as the dominant paradigm in physics, and it has since been used to explain a wide range of phenomena, from the behavior of atoms and molecules to the behavior of stars and galaxies.

Step 5 - Normal Science: The new paradigm is widely accepted and becomes the basis for normal scientific research.

Today, quantum mechanics is widely accepted in the scientific community and is the basis for much of modern physics research. It has led to a revolution in technology, including the development of computers and other electronic devices that rely on quantum mechanics to function
