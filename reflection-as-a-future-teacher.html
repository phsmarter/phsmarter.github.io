Answer:

Improving teacher quality is at the heart of our national effort to achieve excellence in the classroom. This comes at a time when the very structure of education is going through a profound change. With knowledge all around us, available anytime and anywhere, the role of the teacher is going to be fundamentally transformed in the 21st century.

In the future, schools will be more fluid, teachers more adaptable and flexible, and students will be more accountable as the task of learning becomes theirs. The challenge of the modern classroom is its increasing diversity and the skills that this diversity requires of teachers. 27. write a reflection Write your reflection on the topic, The Lexicon in at least three paragraphs. Expound your realizations and position as a future English teacher.​


The lexicon refers to the vocabulary of a language, including words, phrases, and expressions. It is through the lexicon that individuals can express their thoughts, feelings, and ideas. The study of the lexicon is important because it enables individuals to understand the meaning and usage of words in different contexts.

As a future English teacher, I realize the importance of the lexicon in language acquisition and the challenges that students may face in expanding their vocabulary.

One of the challenges in expanding vocabulary is the vastness of the English language. According to Oxford English Dictionary, the English language has over 170,000 words in current use, with an additional 47,000 obsolete words.

As an English teacher, I would need to guide my students in understanding the importance of vocabulary acquisition and the use of context clues to understand new words. I would also need to teach strategies such as word families, prefixes, and suffixes to enable students to learn new words more efficiently.

Another realization I have is the importance of using authentic language in teaching. Authentic language refers to language that is used by native speakers in real-life situations. It is important to use authentic language in teaching because it exposes students to the different nuances of language use, including idioms, phrasal verbs, and slang. As an English teacher, I would need to create opportunities for my students to interact with native speakers and engage in activities that involve authentic language use.

In conclusion, the lexicon is an essential aspect of language acquisition, and as a future English teacher, I realize the importance of teaching vocabulary effectively. I would need to create opportunities for my students to expand their vocabulary through various strategies, including word families, prefixes, and suffixes. I would also need to use authentic language in my teaching to expose my students to the different nuances of language use.


28. Having mastered the developmental tasks of early childhood, middle and late childhood, and adolescence, reflect on what you should do as a future teacher to facilitate your student’s acquisition of these developmental tasks.


As a future teacher, understanding the different development tasks across the life stages of the learners is essential. It guides the teacher in designing lessons and activities that are appropriate to the developmental tasks of the learners.

Teacher’s Role and Learner’s Developmental Tasks

Developmental tasks are the behaviors and activities that develop at or around a certain stage in life. Each life stage has corresponding developmental tasks that need to be done by the learners. It covers their developmental tasks (Havighurst), cognitive development (Piaget), psychosexual development (Freud), psychosocial development (Erikson), and moral development (Kohlberg). Knowledge of various developmental tasks helps teachers respond to the needs of their learners. Understanding the stages of growth is essential for providing teachers with the support they need as they work to improve their effectiveness in the classroom.Teaching styles and activities and their level of complexity should be based on the developmental stages of the learners and the expected skills that they are supposed to develop in the life stage where they are in. Teachers also should motivate their learners in mastering, coping, and learning the developmental tasks expected of them. Having sufficient knowledge of these would help them to understand and respond to their learner’s individual needs.

Future teachers should be equipped with sufficient knowledge and understanding of the development theories, learning theories, and their application in the teaching and learning process. These theories are crucial in developing learning activities and in addressing the needs of the learners.
