Answer:

Why is it important to monitor feed intake in farm animals?

Individual feed monitoring helps to monitor the productivity of each cow versus the quantity of feed being consumed. This aids in farm budgeting, milk yield forecasting, and feed nutrition rationingExplanation:Feeding animals is a vital husbandry skill when caring for all types of animals. ... With animal nutrition being at the forefront of media concerns over welfare, for example issues such as obesity, it is very important for those working in the animal care industry to know the correct way to feed a variety of animals.

Answer:

It is important to monitor the feeding of animals because:

1. Feed conversion efficiency can be determined based on the documented amount of feeds given to the animals.

2. The health of animals is directly affected by the feeding system so it is a must that feeding is monitored.

3. Through feed monitoring, the desired development of the animal can be slowed or shortened. For example, pullets in layer production will be given feeds that is high in calcium as to prepare their body for egg laying.

4. The sick ones can be easily determined during feeding hours and they can be separated right away for medication or rehabilitation. Sick ones are those who showed less interest in eating compared to healthy ones.

5. The acceptance of animals to the feeds given to them can be observed if monitoring during feeding is done.
