Moreover, research findings have shown that cyberbullying causes emotional and physiological damage to defenseless victims (Faryadi, 2011) as well as psychosocial problems including inappropriate behaviors, drinking alcohol, smoking, depression, and low commitment to academics (Walker et al., 2011).

Bullying research has traditionally been dominated by largescale cohort studies focusing on the personality traits of bullies and victims. 20. Make an essay about school bullying​


Answer:

Bullying in School

Bullying in the schools has negative effects on individual students and on the school climate as a whole. Bullying can cause long-term problems for both the victims of bullying and the bullies themselves. To explore the effects of bullying on adolescents, we will define bullying, identify the characteristics of bullies and victims, outline the extent and consequences of bullying, and present resources for further information and assistance.

Bullying is any behavior that is initiated by one or more students against a victim or victims that causes physical or psychological intimidation. Bullying behaviors can be classified as either direct (such as teasing, threatening, hitting, or stealing) or indirect (such as rumor spreading or social isolation). Boys typically employed direct methods of bullying, while girls tend to use in direct methods. Either way, behaviors must occur repeatedly overtime to be classified as bullying.

Characteristics of Bullies and Victims

There are specific behaviors that bullies tend to exhibit. The bullies often need to feel powerful and in control. They may feel no remorse when they inflict injury and suffering on others. Bullies tend to defy authority and are likely to break school rules. They seem to have little anxiety and appear to possess high self-esteem. Students who come from homes characterized by physical punishment tend to be more likely to exhibit these types of behaviors.

Victims also tend to exhibit specific characteristics. They are often anxious, insecure, cautious, and have low self-esteem. Victims tend to be socially isolated, and may lack social skills and friends. Because they tend to be weaker than their peers, either physically or socially, victims rarely retaliate against bullies. Students who have close ties with their parents/guardians or who have overprotective parents/guardians are more likely to be victimized by bullies.

Topic 1: Bullying in Schools

Research Title: Understanding the Prevalence and Effects of Bullying in Schools

Research Objective: This research aims to investigate the prevalence and effects of bullying in schools, determine potential risk factors, and identify effective intervention strategies.

Research Questions:

1. What is the prevalence of bullying in schools, and are there differences in bullying rates across age, gender, and socioeconomic groups?

2. What are the effects of bullying on the victims, bullies, and bystanders, and are there long-term consequences?

3. What risk factors contribute to bullying, such as school environment, family background, and individual factors?

4. What intervention strategies are effective in preventing and reducing bullying in schools?

Topic 2: Covid-19 and the Community in the New Normal

Research Title: Adapting to the "New Normal": Examining Community Responses to Covid-19

Research Objective: This research aims to examine how communities are adapting to the challenges brought by the Covid-19 pandemic, such as changes in social behavior, economic impacts, and mental health concerns, and identify strategies for building resilience and improving community well-being.

Research Problem: The Covid-19 pandemic has disrupted communities worldwide, posing significant challenges to people's daily lives and well-being. This research seeks to understand the ways in which communities are responding to the Covid-19 crisis and identify effective strategies to support community resilience and recovery.

Research Questions:

1. How has the Covid-19 pandemic affected community social behavior, such as social distancing, mask-wearing, and community support?

2. What are the economic impacts of the Covid-19 pandemic on communities, such as job loss, business closures, and financial stress?

3. How has the Covid-19 pandemic affected community mental health, including anxiety, depression, and social isolation?

4. What are effective strategies for building resilience, promoting community well-being, and adapting to the "new normal" in the post-pandemic era?
