16. mrs de chavez use the chart which she bought from the local bookshop in explaining the water cycle.print or non print material ​


Answer:

The chart that Mrs. De Chavez used to explain the water cycle is an example of a non-print material. Non-print materials are instructional materials that are not in written or printed form, but are used to convey information visually or through other means. Examples of non-print materials include charts, graphs, diagrams, models, videos, and audio recordings.

In the case of Mrs. De Chavez, the chart she used to explain the water cycle likely contained visual representations of the different stages of the cycle, such as evaporation, condensation, precipitation, and runoff. These visuals can help students better understand the concept of the water cycle and how water moves through the environment.

Non-print materials like charts are often used in educational settings to enhance teaching and learning. They can provide a visual aid that helps students better understand complex concepts, and can also serve as a reference tool that students can use to review and reinforce their learning.
