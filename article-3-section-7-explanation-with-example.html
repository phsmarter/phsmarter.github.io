15. what is the explaination of article 3 section 19 ?


In Article III, Section 19, the Constitution explicitly forbids the imposition of the death penalty. But lacking the courage of their convictions, the Charter framers also provided an exception.

Section 19 reads: “(1) Excessive fines shall not be imposed, nor cruel, degrading or inhuman punishment inflicted. Neither shall [the] death penalty be imposed, unless, for compelling reasons, involving heinous crimes, the Congress hereafter provides for it. Any death penalty already imposed shall be reduced to reclusion perpetua.”

23. Article 3 section 18 philippine constitution explanation


Section 18 of Article 3 of the Philippine Constitution sheds light on two items.The first item states that no one should be held in custody for questioning just because of the person's political beliefs and aspirations. For example, if a person is a member of a certain political party and he demonstrates his political beliefs and aspirations by blogging, he cannot be called and be detained. For as long as he does not encroach on someone else's rights and he does not violate any laws, he can follow his political beliefs and follow his political aspirations. 
The second item states that no forced services can be imposed or enforced on any one, except if it is part of a criminal punishment. For example, a daughter is compelled to deliver voluntary work in payment of her mother's debts. This is considered illegal.
