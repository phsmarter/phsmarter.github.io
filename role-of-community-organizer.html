1. What is a community? What are the roles of community organization?


A community is a social group consisting of several organisms that make up the environment, usually having the same interests and places of origin.

Community can be interpreted in the social or biological fields depending on the context of the conversation. There are also different types of community found around us.

Community among citizens is called community organizations. It is organizations founded and created by citizens voluntarily from the tendency of aspirations, desires, needs, interests, activities, and goals to participate in the development of a region or country. The role of community organizations is to become a manifestation of the ongoing civil society that functions to bridge, fight for, and defend the interests of citizens according to the control of the interests of capital and practical politics.

Community Types

The following discusses the types of communities and their types from exclusive criteria such as location, interest, or communion.

1. Community Based on Location or Place

The area or location of a community can be seen as a location where a group of people have something in common geographically. And getting to know each other, as a result, creates a relationship and contributes to the environment.

2.Community-Based Interest

A group of people who form a community because they have the same interests and interests. The community of interest has the largest number because it covers various aspects, for example, the gaming community, the arts community, the film community, the photography community, the merchant community, and so on.

3. Community-Based Communion

Communion can mean a basic new view that can support the community itself. In other words, this type of community from communion is formed on the basis of an interest in the social organization of the citizens.

Community Benefits

-As a vehicle to add information and insight related to an interest or field.

-To establish interactions and relationships with other members of the community.

-Support each other because there is a tendency of interest or interest in an exclusive field.
