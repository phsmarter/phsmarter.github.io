1. essay about discipline 1500 words​


Discipline is an essential aspect of human life that is often synonymous with order, control, and self-regulation. It is the process of training oneself or others to behave in a particular way, often to achieve a specific goal or purpose. Discipline can be observed in various aspects of life, including personal habits, social interactions, and professional conduct. In this essay, we will explore the importance of discipline, its benefits, and some strategies for cultivating discipline in our lives.

The importance of discipline can be seen in various aspects of life. In personal habits, discipline is necessary for achieving goals and maintaining good health. For example, a disciplined exercise routine is essential for staying fit and healthy. Similarly, a disciplined diet is important for maintaining a healthy weight and preventing chronic diseases such as diabetes and heart disease. In addition, discipline in personal habits such as time-management and sleep can help individuals achieve their goals and lead productive lives.

Discipline is also important in social interactions. It helps individuals to control their emotions and behave appropriately in different social situations. For instance, discipline helps individuals to communicate effectively, listen actively, and maintain positive relationships with others. It also helps individuals to avoid conflicts and resolve disputes in a peaceful and constructive manner.

In professional conduct, discipline is essential for achieving success and advancing one's career. A disciplined work ethic, punctuality, and attention to detail are all attributes that are highly valued in the workplace. Discipline also helps individuals to manage their time effectively, prioritize tasks, and meet deadlines, which are all critical skills in today's fast-paced and competitive work environment.

The benefits of discipline are numerous and far-reaching. Discipline helps individuals to develop self-control, which is crucial for achieving long-term goals and personal growth. It also helps individuals to overcome challenges and obstacles, develop resilience, and persevere in the face of adversity. Discipline also helps individuals to develop a sense of responsibility and accountability, which are essential traits for success in life.

In addition to these personal benefits, discipline also contributes to the well-being of society as a whole. It helps to maintain order and stability, prevent crime and violence, and promote social cohesion and harmony. Discipline also fosters respect for rules and authority, which are essential for maintaining democratic institutions and the rule of law.

Despite its many benefits, cultivating discipline can be challenging. It requires effort, commitment, and persistence. However, there are several strategies that individuals can use to develop discipline in their lives.

One strategy for developing discipline is to set clear goals and priorities. By identifying what is most important and focusing on specific goals, individuals can stay motivated and avoid distractions. It is also important to break down larger goals into smaller, more manageable tasks, and to track progress regularly.

Another strategy is to establish routines and habits that support discipline. For instance, establishing a regular exercise routine or a daily meditation practice can help individuals develop the self-discipline needed to maintain these habits over time. Additionally, creating a structured schedule for work or study can help individuals to manage their time effectively and avoid procrastination.

It is also important to cultivate a growth mindset, which emphasizes the importance of effort, persistence, and learning from mistakes. Individuals with a growth mindset are more likely to view challenges as opportunities for growth and improvement, rather than as obstacles to be avoided.

Finally, discipline can be developed through the support of others. Friends, family, and mentors can provide encouragement, accountability, and guidance to help individuals stay on track and achieve their goals.

In conclusion, discipline is an essential aspect of human life that contributes to personal growth, social harmony, and societal well-being. It helps individuals to achieve their goals, develop self-control, and contribute to the greater good. While cultivating discipline can be challenging, there are several strategies that individuals can use to develop this essential trait. By setting clear goals, establishing routines, cultivating a growth mindset, and seeking support from others, individuals can develop the discipline needed to lead successful and fulfilling lives.

5. Explain, through a proper literature review ( 1500 words), why the law of demand does not stand when exposed to real life ?


Answer:

The law of demand is an economic principle that states that consumer demand for a good rises when prices fall while conversely, consumer demand falls when prices rise.

However, the relationship between prices and demand is derived from the law of diminishing marginal utility, which states that consumers buy or use goods to satisfy their urgent needs first. Utility refers to the satisfaction or benefit that results from consuming a good. In other words, the first good or unit typically has the highest utility or benefit, and with each additional unit consumed utility decreases. As a result, the price consumers are willing to pay for a good decline as their utility decreases.

●The law of demand is an economic principle that states that consumer demand for a good rises when prices fall and decline when prices rise.

●The law of demand comes into play during Black Friday sales—when consumers rush to buy products at deep discounts.

●Diminishing marginal utility occurs eventually because consumers satisfy their urgent needs first.

If the utility gained from a product isn't enough to justify a product's price, the price will likely be lowered, or demand will decline.

18. Make 1500 essay about politics in the philipines


The politics of the Philippines take place within a three-branch governmental system. The country is a democracy, led by a directly-elected president who is both the head of state and the head of government. The President heads the executive branch, and has significant political powers. Presidents are limited to a single six-year term of office. The bicameral Congress serves as the legislature, consisting of the small Senate, elected on an at-large basis throughout the country, and the larger House of Representatives, primarily made up of representatives elected from specific geographic regions. The judiciary is headed by the Supreme Court of the Philippines, a body with expansive powers of review over actions taken by other political and administrative bodies.

The legal system is a mixture of Civil and Common law, influenced by the prior rule of both Spain and the United States. Spanish rule relied heavily on local intermediaries, setting up a system dominated by elites. A revolution that emerged near the end of Spanish rule was defeated by the United States, who consolidated power over the entirety of the archipelago. American rule led to the introduction of democracy, and institutions patterned on the American political system. Following independence in 1946, national politics took place within a two-party system. This was disrupted by the establishment of a dictatorship under martial law. Changes following the restoration of democracy led to the current multi-party system. Left-wing insurgencies have existed at various strengths since independence, and a long-running Islamic insurgency contributed to the establishment of an autonomous Muslim region with a limited sharia law system.

Elections are held every three years, although the President, Vice-President, and Senators are elected for six-year terms. Results are determined through plurality voting, including plurality-at-large for elections (such as for the Senate) with multiple winners. A mixed-member proportional representation system is used to elect a minority of the House of Representatives. Local government units have some revenue-generating powers, under a code intended to decentralize power away from the national government. Administrative structures at local levels are designed to foster civil society participation.

Politics is dominated by a powerful elite, with dynastic politics common at both the local and national levels. Political parties are weak, with elections instead dominated by individual and familial personalities. Political positions provide extensive opportunities for patronage, and clientelism and electoral fraud are common. Corruption is considered widespread, while state institutions are relatively weak. Politics has been heavily influenced at times by the Catholic Church, the Philippine military, and the United States. Despite pessimism about the potential for political change, democracy maintains strong public support, and voter turnout is high.

It is a day to recognize and celebrate the social, economic, cultural and political achievements of women all over the world. This day also marks a call to action for accelerating gender parity.

The history of International Women’s Day dates back to 1909 when it was first observed in New York City by 15,000 women who marched through the city demanding better pay, shorter working hours and voting rights. Since then it has been celebrated annually as a way of recognizing the progress made towards achieving gender equality and empowering women around the world.

Gender inequality is still prevalent in many parts of the world today despite significant progress being made towards achieving gender parity over recent decades. 29. Q: Write An Article About " Dignity of Work"? At Least 1500 Words


Answer: The dignity of labour is the philosophy that all types of jobs are respected equally, and no occupation is considered superior and none of the jobs should be discriminated on any basis. Regardless of whether one's occupation involves physical work or mental labour, it is held that the job deserves respect. If the dignity of work is to be protected, then the basic rights of workers must be respected--the right to productive work, to decent and fair wages, to the organization and joining of unions, to private property, and to economic initiative.
