These properties make oregano leaves a natural option for air fresheners that can help eliminate unpleasant odors while also reducing airborne bacteria and fungi.

One possible avenue for future research in this area could be to explore the effectiveness of oregano leaves as an air freshener in different settings, such as homes, offices, and hospitals. Researchers could conduct controlled experiments to compare the efficacy of oregano-based air fresheners to conventional ones, as well as to evaluate the impact of the natural compounds in oregano leaves on indoor air quality.

Another interesting area of research could be to investigate the potential of oregano-based air fresheners to alleviate respiratory symptoms in people with respiratory conditions such as asthma or allergies. Some studies suggest that oregano oil may have bronchodilatory effects, meaning it could help open up airways and improve breathing. Researchers could explore the potential of oregano-based air fresheners to provide relief for people with respiratory conditions, either through direct inhalation or by using the air fresheners to improve indoor air quality.

Overall, there are many possibilities for future research on oregano leaves as an air freshener.
