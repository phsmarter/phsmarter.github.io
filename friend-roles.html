2. How would you differentiate the role of parents from the role of friends in moments of trouble?


Explanation:

give me a example of argumentative assay

Answer:

The role of parents and friends in moments of trouble can differ in several ways.

Firstly, parents tend to have a more authoritative and guiding role in times of trouble. They are responsible for their children's well-being and may provide advice and guidance on how to handle difficult situations. They have more life experience and usually have a better understanding of the consequences of certain actions. They also have a more protective role, and may take steps to keep their children safe and out of harm's way.

On the other hand, friends tend to have a more supportive and empathetic role in times of trouble. They can provide emotional support and be a sounding board for someone going through a difficult time. They may also offer practical help and advice, but they are not necessarily responsible for the well-being of their friends. They may also be going through similar struggles, so they can offer a perspective of someone who understands and can empathize with their friend.

Another difference is that parents have a long-term relationship with their children, and they have a vested interest in the well-being of their children, whereas friends' relationships can be more temporary and may change over time.

In conclusion, parents tend to have a more authoritative and protective role, while friends tend to have a more supportive and empathetic role in moments of trouble. Both play important roles in providing support and guidance, but in different ways. It is important for individuals to have a balance of support from both parents and friends in order to navigate through difficult times.

GOOD FRIENDS

Good friends can play a crucial role in saying no to drugs as they can provide support, encouragement, and accountability. A strong group of friends who share values and goals can help each other make positive choices and resist peer pressure. They can also provide alternative activities and distractions to prevent drug use and provide a safe and positive environment. Good friends can listen and empathize with one another's struggles and fears, which can help individuals navigate difficult situations and make sound decisions. Ultimately, good friends can be a source of strength and motivation to say no to drugs and stay on a healthy path.
