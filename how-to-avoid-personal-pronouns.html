12. how personal pronoun in English potentially disrespect and individual?​


Answer:

Personal pronouns are pronouns that are associated primarily with a particular grammatical person – first person (as I), second person (as you), or third person (as he, she, it, they). Personal pronouns may also take different forms depending on number (usually singular or plural), grammatical or natural gender, case, and formality. The term "personal" is used here purely to signify the grammatical sense; personal pronouns are not limited to people and can also refer to animals and objects (as the English personal pronoun it usually does).

25. read more about the different kinds of pronouns (personal pronoun, indefinite pronoun, relative pronoun, intensive pronoun, reflexive pronoun, and demonstrative pronoun).


Answer:

Here's a brief overview of the different types of pronouns:

1. Personal Pronouns: These are used to refer to specific people or things. They include pronouns such as "I," "you," "he," "she," "it," "we," and "they." Personal pronouns are used in place of nouns and can be either subjective (when the pronoun is the subject of the sentence) or objective (when the pronoun is the object of the sentence).

2. Indefinite Pronouns: These are used to refer to people or things in a non-specific way. They include pronouns such as "anyone," "someone," "everyone," "anything," "something," and "nothing." Indefinite pronouns are used when the speaker or writer is not referring to a particular person or thing.

3. Relative Pronouns: These are used to connect a clause or phrase to a noun or pronoun. They include pronouns such as "who," "whom," "whose," "which," and "that." Relative pronouns are used to add more information about the noun or pronoun in the sentence.

4. Intensive Pronouns: These are used to emphasize the subject or object of a sentence. They include pronouns such as "myself," "yourself," "himself," "herself," "itself," "ourselves," "yourselves," and "themselves." Intensive pronouns are used to add emphasis to the sentence.

5. Reflexive Pronouns: These are used when the subject and object of a sentence are the same. They include pronouns such as "myself," "yourself," "himself," "herself," "itself," "ourselves," "yourselves," and "themselves." Reflexive pronouns are used to show that the action of the sentence is being performed by the subject on itself.

6. Demonstrative Pronouns: These are used to point to specific people or things. They include pronouns such as "this," "that," "these," and "those." Demonstrative pronouns are used to indicate the location or proximity of the noun or pronoun in the sentence.

Explanation:

Understanding the different types of pronouns and their uses can help to improve your writing and communication skills. By using pronouns correctly, you can make your writing more clear and concise, and avoid confusion for your readers.

30. what is personal and in-personal pronoun​


Personal pronouns are pronouns used to refer to people, animals or things. They can be either subjective (nominative) pronouns, which are used as the subject of a sentence, or objective pronouns, which are used as the object of a verb, preposition, or infinitive phrase.

Some examples of personal pronouns are:

Subjective pronouns: I, you, he, she, it, we, they

Objective pronouns: me, you, him, her, it, us, them

In contrast, impersonal pronouns do not refer to a specific person, animal, or thing. Instead, they refer to an unspecified person, or no person at all. Impersonal pronouns include words like "someone", "anyone", "everyone", "no one", "anything", "nothing", "something", "everything", etc.
