11. explanatory research design explanation??​


Explanatory research design is a type of research method used to identify the causal relationship between two or more variables. It aims to provide an explanation for the occurrence of a particular phenomenon or event by examining the relationship between the variables under investigation.

This type of research design involves the collection and analysis of data, which is used to explain the relationship between the variables of interest. The data collected may be quantitative or qualitative, and the analysis may involve statistical techniques or other forms of data analysis.

Explanatory research design typically involves the use of experimental or quasi-experimental designs, where one or more variables are manipulated or controlled to determine their effect on other variables. This design is often used in scientific research, medical research, and social sciences, where researchers aim to understand the underlying causes of a phenomenon.

Overall, the explanatory research design provides a means for researchers to explore and understand the causal relationships between variables, allowing them to identify the factors that influence a particular outcome.

An explanatory research design is a type of research method that aims to understand the relationship between variables by explaining a particular phenomenon. It focuses on finding out why something occurs rather than just identifying what occurs. This design typically involves gathering data through experiments, surveys, and observations to explain the cause and effect of a particular event or behavior. It is often used in social sciences, education, and business research.

Correlational research is the observation of two variables to see if there is a relation between them, being positive or negative. Experimental research is the observation between an introduced variable--the independent variable--under controlled environments and its resulting factor--the dependent variable.
