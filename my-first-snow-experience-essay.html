2. explain why Philippines do not experience snow while Japan experiences it?​


Answer:

The Philippines and Japan have different climates and geographical locations, which explain why the Philippines does not experience snow while Japan does.

Firstly, the Philippines is located in the tropics and is situated near the equator. Its climate is classified as tropical rainforest climate, which is characterized by high temperatures and rainfall throughout the year. On the other hand, Japan is located in the temperate zone and has a humid subtropical climate in most areas, while other areas experience a humid continental climate.

Secondly, the Philippines is an archipelago of islands, and its landmass is relatively small compared to Japan's landmass. The country's terrain is mostly made up of low-lying coastal plains and mountain ranges. Japan, on the other hand, is a larger country with a varied terrain, including high mountain ranges and a long coastline. These geographic features contribute to the differences in temperature and weather patterns between the two countries.

Lastly, the Philippines is not located in the path of the cold Siberian air masses, which bring the cold temperatures and snow to Japan during the winter months. Japan's location in the northern hemisphere and its proximity to the Asian continent make it susceptible to the cold air masses coming from Siberia, resulting in the country's snowy winters.

In summary, the Philippines' location near the equator, smaller landmass, and lack of exposure to cold air masses from Siberia contribute to its lack of snowfall, while Japan's location in the temperate zone, varied terrain, and exposure to cold air masses from Siberia result in its snowy winters.

At first, the snow twirled gently, lightly dusting the street, cars, and trees with snowy frosting. Then, the snow became a curtain, blinding my view so that I felt cocooned from the outside world. When the snow stopped, ice coated the trees and branches clacked like chattering teeth. Which sentence will Fatima’s teacher most likely ask her to revise?


Answer:

The first sentence should be revised.

Explanation:

There is nothing grammatically wrong with any of the sentences in the given paragraph. However, there is a problem with redundancy in the first sentence:

"Having moved up north just last year, I experienced my first snowstorm this past winter."

The idea that the writer's first snowstorm experience happened during the previous year is repeated when the writer added "this past winter" to the end of the sentence. Winter occurs only once a year, so it is already understandable from the opening phrase that this experience took place "last year." The phrase "this past winter" should be omitted.
