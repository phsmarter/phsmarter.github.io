16. What I Have LearnedPersuasive writing intends to convince readers to believe in an idea or opinion andto do an action.Define the three parts of a persuasive essay.DofinitionParts /Label1. Introduction -2. Body -3. Conclusion -​


Answer:

An introduction is the first paragraph of a written research paper, or the first thing you say in an oral presentation, or the first thing people see, hear, or experience about your project. ... The introduction gives the reader the beginning of the piece of thread so they can follow it.

The body is the largest part of a research paper; in it you collect and arrange evidence that will persuade the reader of your argument. It should, therefore, have a logical organization. If the paper is long, it is a good idea to partition the body into sections using headings and sub-headings.

The conclusion of a research paper is where you wrap up your ideas and leave the reader with a strong final impression. It has several key goals: Restate the research problem addressed in the paper. Summarize your overall arguments or findings.

1. Introduction: introduction is a beginning section which states the purpose and goals of the following writing. This is generally followed by the body and conclusion. The introduction typically describes the scope of the document and gives the brief explanation or summary of the document.

2. Body: the body paragraphs are where the writer has the opportunity to argue his or her viewpoint. By the conclusion paragraph, the writer should convince the reader to agree with the argument of the essay.

3. Conclusion: ✘ The conclusion of a persuasive essay should summarize the main points discussed in the essay. ✘ The summary should be clear and concise. Moreover, it should contain the important keywords. ... Brief 8 ✘ The persuasive essay conclusion should be brief so that the readers do not feel the repetitiveness of information.

27. How do you think the whole essay will develop with a title like “What I Believe”?


Answer:

If an essay has the title "What I Believe," it is likely that the essay will be a personal reflection or opinion piece. The writer may use this opportunity to share their thoughts, values, and experiences on a particular topic or a range of topics.

The essay may start with an introduction where the writer explains why they are writing this essay and what motivated them to express their beliefs. Then, the writer may go on to explore their beliefs in detail, providing reasons, examples, and anecdotes to support their arguments. To create a cohesive essay, the writer should organize their thoughts in a logical and structured way. They may use transitional phrases or paragraphs to move smoothly from one idea to another.
