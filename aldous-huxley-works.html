3. Create a 5-paragraph reaction paper about " "Time and the Machine"" by Aldous Huxley


Answer:

In his piece Time and the Machine, Aldous Huxley argues that the idea of time as hours and minutes is an invention of modern societies centered around the rise of businesses and industrialization. He states that before industrialization, time was measured more naturally, in the rising of the sun, phases moon, and in the changing seasons. With the rise of business, schedules and deadlines, society has begun to be more conscious of the passing time in smaller of increments. Hours and minutes were invented because it became necessary to divide up time as it was demanded. It is a “by-product of industrialism” and those who work in the industrial, urbanized world have lost touch with the natural rhythm of time passing. “One can live and work in a town without being aware of the daily march of the sun across the sky; without ever seeing the moon and stars. … He is the inhabitant of an artificial universe that is, to a great extent, walled off from the world of nature.” He argues that time has stopped being measured in natural increments and instead, is measured “in eight-hour days and six-day weeks.”
