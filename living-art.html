3. Contemporary art is art that is living in​


Contemporary art is art that is living in​ the present

The word ‘contemporary’ has become synonymous with a lot of things: an era, an idea or even something abstract. But what does it really mean? We asked some artists and curators for their opinions on contemporary art today.

What are your thoughts about contemporary art? Is it still relevant now? Are there any new movements you think have had a big impact on how we see the world around us at this moment in time? And why do you think people find contemporary art so interesting to look at?

I am fascinated by how our culture creates meaning through symbols. I like the way they can be used as tools to provoke thought and change perception. Contemporary art allows me to explore my own feelings and experiences whilst simultaneously being able to share them with others. It also gives me the opportunity to question ideas which may not necessarily make sense but are important nonetheless. As well as these personal reasons, I often feel like I need to take part in current events because it makes me more aware of the issues affecting society. This awareness helps me to respond appropriately when faced with certain situations. For example if someone tells me off for wearing high heels then I know exactly where I stand! If I don't understand what's going on then I'm left feeling powerless and confused. That said, I think most people recognise contemporary art as a form of expression; however many people will only engage with it if they already 'know' what its all about. Therefore contemporary art must always challenge itself to remain fresh and vital. The work must continually evolve rather than stagnate into a predictable format.
