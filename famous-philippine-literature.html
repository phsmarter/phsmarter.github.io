Who is the famous literature in the Philippines? 4. Why is it important to study 21st century literature from the

There are many famous literature writers in the Philippines, but some of the most renowned include Jose Rizal, Nick Joaquin, F. Sionil Jose, and Lualhati Bautista.

Jose Rizal is considered the national hero of the Philippines and is known for his novels "Noli Me Tangere" and "El Filibusterismo," which exposed the injustices and corruption of Spanish colonial rule in the Philippines.

Nick Joaquin is known for his works that explore Filipino identity, culture, and history, such as "The Woman Who Had Two Navels" and "Cave and Shadows."

F. Sionil Jose is a prolific writer who has written numerous novels and short stories that tackle social and political issues in the Philippines, including poverty, corruption, and human rights.

Lualhati Bautista is known for her feminist writings that tackle issues of gender and class in Philippine society, such as "Dekada '70" and "Bata, Bata, Pa'no Ka Ginawa?"

Studying 21st-century literature is important because it allows us to understand the contemporary issues and concerns of our time. Literature reflects the social, cultural, and political realities of the world we live in, and studying 21st-century literature can help us gain a deeper understanding of the issues that affect us today. It also allows us to appreciate the diversity of voices and perspectives that exist in the world, and to engage with literature as a means of critical thinking and self-reflection.

1. The American regime in the Philippines began in 1898 when the country was ceded to the United States by Spain. During this period, English was introduced as the medium of instruction in schools and became the dominant language of literature. The literary trends and movements during this time included the rise of the Philippine novel in English, the development of the Philippine short story, and the emergence of poetry in English as a literary form.

2. Famous literary works written during the American regime in the Philippines include:

- "Noli Me Tangere" and "El Filibusterismo" by Jose Rizal - These novels were written in Spanish and were critical of the Spanish colonial government in the Philippines. They were instrumental in inspiring the Philippine Revolution against Spanish rule.

- "The Woman Who Had Two Navels" by Nick Joaquin - This novel explores the themes of identity and colonialism through the story of a Filipina who believes she has two navels.

- "Po-on" by F. Sionil Jose - This novel is the first book in a five-volume series that chronicles the history of a Filipino family from the late 1800s to the present day. It examines the impact of colonialism on Filipino society.

- "Banaag at Sikat" by Lope K. Santos - This novel tells the story of a group of Filipino peasants who organize to fight for their rights against the American colonial government and the wealthy landlords.

3. Brief background information on each work:

- "Noli Me Tangere" and "El Filibusterismo" by Jose Rizal - These novels are considered to be among the most important works of Philippine literature. They are written in a style that blends satire, romance, and social commentary. Rizal was executed by the Spanish colonial government in 1896 for his involvement in the Philippine Revolution.

- "The Woman Who Had Two Navels" by Nick Joaquin - This novel was published in 1961 and is considered to be one of the most important works of Philippine literature in English. It won the first-ever Philippine National Book Award.

- "Po-on" by F. Sionil Jose - This novel was published in 1984 and is the first book in the "Rosales Saga" series. It is set in the late 1800s and follows the story of a Filipino family as they struggle to survive under colonial rule.

- "Banaag at Sikat" by Lope K. Santos - This novel was published in 1906 and is considered to be one of the first novels written in Tagalog. It is a social realist work that explores the themes of class struggle and national liberation.

4. Well-known poets during the American regime in the Philippines include Jose Garcia Villa, Angela Manalang-Gloria, and Jose Garcia Villa. Villa is considered to be one of the most important Filipino poets of the 20th century and is known for his use of "comma poems," which are characterized by the innovative use of punctuation. Manalang-Gloria is known for her feminist poetry and her use of Tagalog and English in her work.

English Month: Reliving the Past and Embracing the Future through Philippine Literature.

The literature associated with the Philippines from prehistory to the present, including its colonial heritage, is known as Philippine literature. Actually, oral epics that were passed down from generation to generation made up pre-Hispanic Philippine literature.

Timeline of how Philippine Literature evolved

The Pre-Colonial Era (BC to 1564)

Legends, Crude on ideology and phraseology, Riddle, Proverbs, Tanaga Fables, Fantastic stories, Epics, Folk Song, Folk Tales, Myths (explain how the world was created, how certain animals possess certain characteristics, why some places have waterfalls, volcanoes, mountains, flora or fauna), Crude on ideology and phraseology

Period Of Spanish Colonization (1565 – 1863)

It is divided into two categories: religious and secular.Spanish was made available as a language of communication.Pasyon, Senakulo, Secular, Awit, Korido Prose Narratives, Dialogo are examples of religious literature.

The Nationalistic/Propaganda and Revolutionary Era (1864 – 1896)

Instilled nationalistic values in FilipinosThe language was changed to Tagalog from Spanish.Addressed the populace rather than the "intellectsia"Political essays, Diariong, Noli Me Tangere, and El Filibusterismo are examples of revolutionary literature.

American Colonial Era

Apprenticeship PeriodFilipino Writers imitated English and American modelsPoems written were amateurish and mushy, which phrasing and diction is awkward and artificialShort Stories: The Key Footnote to Youth, Childe ofPeriod of Emergence (1920-1930): Highly influenced by Western literary trends like Romanticism and Realism

Opportunity in Japanese (1942 - 1960)

A Long War (1942-1944)As Tagalog authors departed from the Balagtas tradition and wrote in straightforward prose and free verse, fiction took precedence over poetry.Filipino writers improved their driving abilities while studying English.

Modern/Contemporary Period (1960 – Present)

Martial Law suppressed and restricted human rights, such as press freedomDespite severe censorship, writers resorted to symbolism and allegory to emphasize their points.Groups like PETA (Philippine Educational Theater Association) and UP Theater employed theater as a means of protest.Writers have continued to be dynamic and innovative since the 1980s.
