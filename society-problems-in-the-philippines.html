Some provisions of Kartilya ng Katipunan like treating other people equally and defending the oppressed from the oppressor is one of the most helpful teachings of Kartilya ng Katipunan. The problem in our country today is discrimination and oppressing the poor. The teachings of the Kartilya ng Katipunan are against that and I think that we should study and apply their teachings in our country to promote peace and others in our surroundings because with that teachings bear in our minds we can prevent chaos from happening in our country.

Explanation:

The Kartilya ng Katipunan is served as a guidebook for new members of the organization, which laid out the group's rules and principles. The first edition of the Kartilya was written by Emilio Jacinto. Andrés Bonifacio later wrote a revised Decalogue. The Decalogue, originally titled Katungkulang Gagawin ng mga Z. Ll. B. (Duties of the Sons of the People), was never published because Bonifacio believed that Jacinto's Kartilya was superior to what he had made.

Kartilya ng Katipunan is a set of principles that show the path on how each and every one of us must live life by living to the fullest through good values. The Kartilya is a set of thirteen lessons that serve as a guide for the Katipuneros in living a spiritual and ethical life. It was more like a code of conduct that showed how to be a real Filipino in both words and deeds.
