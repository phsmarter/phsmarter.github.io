5. 12 The proportional relationship between the number of gallons of chocolate ice cream and the number of gallons of vanilla ice cream that an ice creamery freezes daily is graphed below. What does the point (6, 2) represent on the graph? A. There are 6 times the number of gallons of vanilla ice cream frozen than chocolate ice cream. B. For every 6 gallons of vanilla ice cream frozen, the ice creamery freezes 2 gallons of chocolate ice cream. C. For every 6 gallons of chocolate ice cream frozen, the ice creamery freezes 2 gallons of vanilla ice cream. D. There are always 6 – 2 = 4 more gallons of vanilla ice cream frozen than chocolate ice cream frozen.


The point (6, 2) represents a specific ratio of chocolate ice cream to vanilla ice cream that the ice creamery freezes daily.

From the graph, we can see that the ratio of chocolate ice cream to vanilla ice cream is constant and equal to 1/3. This means that for every 1 gallon of chocolate ice cream frozen, the ice creamery freezes 3 gallons of vanilla ice cream.

Using this ratio, we can determine the amount of vanilla ice cream that the ice creamery freezes when 6 gallons of chocolate ice cream are frozen:

6 gallons chocolate ice cream x (3 gallons vanilla ice cream/1 gallon chocolate ice cream) = 18 gallons vanilla ice cream

Therefore, the point (6, 2) represents the fact that the ice creamery freezes 6 gallons of chocolate ice cream and 18 gallons of vanilla ice cream daily, maintaining a ratio of 1/3 (or 2/6) of chocolate ice cream to vanilla ice cream.

So the correct answer is B: For every 6 gallons of vanilla ice cream frozen, the ice creamery freezes 2 gallons of chocolate ice cream.

17. 2. What is the effect of temperature to the sale of ice cream? A) The sale of ice cream is at its lowest. B) The sale of ice cream increases as temperature decreases. C) The sale of ice cream increases as temperature increases D) Temperature has no effect on the sale of ice cream.​


Answer:

C

Step-by-step explanation:

The effect of temperature on the sale of ice cream is that the sale of ice cream increases as temperature increases. Therefore, the correct answer is C) The sale of ice cream increases as temperature increases. This is because ice cream is a popular treat during hot weather and people tend to crave cold foods and drinks to cool off. As the temperature rises, more people are likely to purchase ice cream to satisfy their cravings, leading to an increase in sales. Conversely, when the temperature is low, people are less likely to crave ice cream and thus, sales may decrease.

29. One gallon of ice cream is equal to______. A. 4 liters of ice cream. B. 1 liter of ice cream. C. 3.8 liters of ice cream. D. 2 liters of ice cream​


PROBLEM:

One gallon of ice cream is equal to______.

A. 4 liters of ice cream.

B. 1 liter of ice cream.

C. 3.8 liters of ice cream.

D. 2 liters of ice cream

ANSWER:

One gallon is equivalent to an approximate value of 3.785 liters or 3.8 liters when rounded off to the nearest tenths.

Therefore, the answer is letter C. One gallon of ice cream is equal to 3.8 liters of ice cream.

ADDITIONAL INFO:Gallon is a unit of measurement in terms of volume, in the imperial system and United States customary while liters is a metric unit of volume and capacity.
