6. Shopping Continuum Theory Patronage Theory Revealed Preference Theory.need lng ng information about Jan, thesis purpose ​


Answer:

The Shopping Continuum Theory is a model that explains the process of shopping and consumer decision-making. According to this theory, consumers go through a series of steps when shopping, starting with the decision to shop and ending with the actual purchase. These steps include problem recognition, information search, evaluation of alternatives, purchase decision, and post-purchase evaluation.

The Patronage Theory is a model that explains the factors that influence a consumer's decision to shop at a particular store. According to this theory, consumers choose stores based on a variety of factors, including convenience, location, price, selection, and service.

The Revealed Preference Theory is a model that explains consumer behavior by examining the choices that consumers make. According to this theory, consumers reveal their preferences through their actions and the choices they make, and these preferences can be used to predict future behavior.

17. explaination of the thesis are argumentative thesis statement is not synonymous to the topic of the paper, nor a statement of fact.​


A thesis statement is a clear and concise statement that presents the main argument or position that a paper will be supporting. It is not merely a topic of the paper, which is a broad area of discussion, nor is it a statement of fact, which can be proven true or false through objective evidence.

An argumentative thesis statement, in particular, takes a clear stance on a controversial or debatable issue and provides reasons or evidence to support that position. It not only expresses an opinion but also presents a logical and persuasive argument for that opinion. For example, an argumentative thesis statement could be "The use of cellphones should be banned while driving because it increases the risk of accidents and endangers the safety of all road users." This statement takes a clear position on a debatable issue and provides reasons to support that position.

In summary, a thesis statement is a concise and arguable statement that encapsulates the main argument or position of the paper. It is not merely a statement of fact or a broad topic area. An argumentative thesis statement presents a clear stance on a controversial issue and supports that stance with reasons or evidence.

29. What are the main idea, thesis statement and topic sentence?Is thesis statement the main idea?​


What are the main idea, thesis statement and topic sentence?

The main idea refers to the message, thing, subject or topic that you are focusing, narrating, and/or addressing.  An idea could just be one word like pandemic, deforestation, or two like climate change and illegal drugs. This helps author to have a solid stance of what he/she is writing about.

Thesis statement is small representation or introduction of what could be seen, both positive and negative, in your text. This is a summarization of your claims and arguments, and is mostly written in one to three sentence/s.

Topic sentence is a sentence that mostly represents an idea in a paragraph or group of sentences. It is written as a guide to identify what you can expect in this section, or what could be discussed here. This is good for organization and for readers to have a clean track of what you are writing. This is usually the first sentence of any paragraph depending on the author.

Is thesis statement the main idea?​

Thesis statement is not the main idea, but it could contain ideas that are surrounding the main idea, which could therefore help someone identify the main idea. However, some people might include the main idea directly into the topic sentence.
