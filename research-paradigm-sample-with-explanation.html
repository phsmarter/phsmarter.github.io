7. RESEARCH PARADIGM ABOUT ADVANTAGES AND DISADVANTAGES TO FACE TO FACEpahelpss​


Answer:

A meta-analysis.

Advantageous outcomes of a face-to-face relationship are based on the relationship between the partners. Therefore, this relationship must also be considered when one investigates which outcomes are more or less advantageous. The aim of this meta-analysis was to find which outcomes are more beneficial for face-to-face communication as compared with other communication methods. Furthermore, we examined whether advantages depend on several variables such as type of relationship (friends, colleagues, relatives, romantic partners, and so forth) or the communication method (face-to-face, written, video, or audio). A total of 4,200 participants (1,834 female, 2,466 male) were included from 33 published articles and presented at a total of 32 communication methods and relationship types. The dependent variable was the relationship outcome which is defined as the change in a relationship's quality, e.g., friendship, marital satisfaction, loneliness. Participants reported more beneficial effects in face-to-face communication as compared with other communication methods. Moreover, we found that different communication methods produced different effects: face-to-face, written, and video communication methods produced more beneficial outcomes than audio communication, but audio communication was still better than telephone and computer-mediated communication methods. Furthermore, we found that the relationship outcome depended on type of relationship (friend, colleague, relative, romantic partner) and that face-to-face communication was more beneficial than all other communication methods for all type of relationships. These findings have important implications for psychotherapeutic and psychological interventions with adults, and also for educational, workplace, and social communication.

19. Research Paradigm about Impact of Food Inflation on Poverty in the Philippines. Input/Process/Output


PHILIPPINES FOOD INFLATION

INPUT

One of the primary sources of inflation in the Philippines has been rising food prices, including the recent surge in sugar prices. Food inflation peaked at 7.1% in July before falling to 6.5% in August.

Sugar, confectionery, and desserts (30.2%); corn (26.2%); and oils and fats are the leading contributors to increasing food inflation among food categories (20.1%). In addition, meat and fish costs have increased for some time and are already adding to our food inflation figures.

Consequently, it raises income inequality by widening the gap between high and low-income groups.

PROCESS

When domestic institutional constraints, the international agricultural trade environment, and weather and climate circumstances impede the utilization of current high-yield farming practices, rising food prices can increase poverty over the long run.

High inflation exacerbates inequality and poverty because it significantly impacts the disposable income and savings of low and middle-income households more than those of wealthy families. Moreover, by increasing inflation, residents that have just left poverty may be forced back into it.

OUTPUT

Food inflation causes a food shortage. Food scarcity reduces the poor's access to high-quality food, resulting in malnutrition among the poor. Undernourishment increases by 0.5% for each percentage point of food inflation.

Malnutrition causes poverty by lowering the economic capacity of the population, and poverty exacerbates malnutrition by increasing the likelihood of food insecurity.

30. make a research paradigm about the effect of digital technology to G11 STEM Students ​


Answer:

Research Paradigm:

Title: Investigating the Impact of Digital Technology on G11 STEM Students

Introduction:

The use of digital technology has become increasingly widespread in the education sector, providing a plethora of opportunities for teachers and students. In this study, we aim to investigate the impact of digital technology on G11 STEM students.

Research Question:

What is the effect of digital technology on the academic performance and learning outcomes of G11 STEM students?

Hypothesis:

It is hypothesized that the use of digital technology in the classroom will positively impact the academic performance and learning outcomes of G11 STEM students.

Methodology:

This study will employ a mixed-methods approach, including both quantitative and qualitative data collection methods. The participants will be G11 STEM students from several schools in a specific geographic area. The sample will be selected using stratified random sampling to ensure diversity in terms of gender, socioeconomic status, and academic performance.

Quantitative data will be collected using a pre-test/post-test design to measure academic performance, while a survey questionnaire will be used to gather information about the frequency of digital technology use, student attitudes towards technology, and perceived benefits and drawbacks of technology use.

Qualitative data will be gathered through focus group discussions and individual interviews with selected participants. The focus group discussions will explore the experiences of students in using digital technology in their learning, while individual interviews will provide an in-depth understanding of the perceived impact of digital technology on academic performance and learning outcomes.

Expected Results:

It is anticipated that the use of digital technology in the classroom will have a positive effect on academic performance and learning outcomes of G11 STEM students. The study is expected to provide insights into the most effective ways to integrate digital technology into the classroom to maximize its potential for enhancing student learning. Additionally, the study is expected to provide recommendations for future research in the field of digital technology in education.
