7. In two dimensional artwork such as paintings, cite the elements and principles of art that when utilized in the composition of the artwork, will help stimulate or suggest the three-dimensional space?​


Answer:

The elements and principles of art that can help stimulate or suggest three-dimensional space in two-dimensional artwork include:

Perspective: The use of perspective techniques, such as converging lines and size variation, can help create the illusion of depth in a two-dimensional artwork.

Overlapping: When objects overlap in an artwork, it can create the sense that they are in different planes, adding to the sense of depth.

Shadows: The use of shadows can help create the illusion of three-dimensional form and depth in an artwork.

Foreshortening: Foreshortening is the technique of depicting an object with parts of it left out in order to give the illusion of perspective. This can help create a sense of depth in an artwork.

Textures: The depiction of textures, such as roughness or smoothness, can help create the sense of three-dimensional form in an artwork.

Contrast: Highlighting certain elements and diminishing others can create the illusion of depth, as elements that are closer to the viewer will be more prominent.

Color: The use of color can also help suggest depth in an artwork, with warmer colors appearing closer and cooler colors appearing further away.

In two dimensional artwork such as paintings, cite the elements and principles of art that when utilized in the composition of the artwork, will help stimulate or suggest the three-dimensional space?

Visual art concepts are divided into components and ideals of art. Color, form, line, shape, space, and texture are art elements. Size, proportion, harmony, variety, rhythm, density, form, distance, balance, height, perspective, and depth are art concepts. Three-dimensional art works fill physical space and can be seen from both directions and angles since they are viewed in the proportions of height, distance, and depth.

Two-dimensional Processes & Techniques

The processes and techniques in two-dimensional art are those that are generated on a flat plane. They are tall and broad, but have little depth. Drawing, printmaking, painting, photography, and some mixed media are among them.

Drawing

Drawing is arguably the most fundamental of artistic cycles. The majority of us have been drawing since we were able to grip a tool and make drawings on a wall. Pencil, pen and ink, charcoal, crayon, and felt-tip pens are the most popular drawing media. Artists can select from a number of drawing surfaces. While some artists do draw on rock walls today, most use paper, ranging from the white paper of common sketchbooks to a wide range of imported and handmade papers. Papers may be smooth or rough, white or a variety of colors. Tools for drawing may be black, colored, or white.  The use of line is central to drawing. The quality of lines generated by drawing media can vary greatly. Hard pencils produce thin, light lines, while soft pencils produce thicker lines with a wide range of values from very light to very dark. Since charcoal is so delicate, the color of the paper can come from where the strokes are drawn gently. Each drawing tool or medium has its own distinct characteristics, and playing with a range of drawing media is an excellent way to learn about their similarities and differences.  

Three-dimensional space

(also known as 3-space or, less commonly, tri-dimensional space) is a geometric setting in which three values (called parameters) are used to decide the location of an entity (i.e., point). This is the informal sense of the word dimension.

17. What is the important contribution of three-dimensional artwork in the Philippine ARTS?


Three-dimensional artwork plays an important role in Philippine arts as it provides a tangible and immersive experience for the viewers. Sculptures, installations, and other forms of 3D artworks allow the audience to interact with the piece, creating a deeper emotional connection and understanding of the artwork's message or theme.

Moreover, 3D artworks in the Philippines have also contributed to the growth of the country's art industry. The rise of public art installations, museum exhibitions, and art fairs featuring 3D works has created more opportunities for Filipino artists to showcase their talents and gain recognition locally and internationally.
