9. strength and weaknesses of sleep quality, sleep propensity and academic performance​


Answer:

Sleep is an inseparable part of human health and life, which is crucial in learning, practice, as well as physical and mental health. It affects the capacity of individual learning, academic performance, and neural-behavioral functions. This study aimed to determine the relationship between sleep quality and students’ academic achievement among students at Kermanshah University of Medical Sciences.

Explanation:

In this cross-sectional study, 102 medical students from different fields, with maximum variation sampling, completed Pittsburgh Sleep Quality Index (PSQI). For data analysis, SPSS 19 was used through which Pearson correlation test, Spearman test, and t-test were employed.

Results

Based on the quality of sleep questionnaire scores, the results indicated no significant difference between students with high grades and those with low grades. However, there were moderate and sometimes severe sleep disturbances in both groups.

Conclusion

The results showed no significant difference between sleep quality and academic achievement. Nevertheless, longitudinal study should be performed to control for confounding factors.

24. Which are the factors of the substitute product that pose a great threat in the industry environment?A. Preferences and tastes of the customers easily change.B. The buyer has the potential for backward integration.C. The cost of switching the supplier cost is minimal.D. Product differentiation is highly noticeable.​


The substitute product factor that is a big threat in the industrial environment is that buyers have the potential for backward integration (B). Threat of substitute products describes the threat that occurs to the company when there are competitors who offer consumers different product choices, but can meet the same needs.

When businesses in one industry are forced to compete with businesses that offer alternatives to their products or services. Then the buyer will integrate the product they are looking for by considering their needs with the same function. This makes it increasingly difficult for the business to make a profit as the price-performance alternatives provided by substitutes become more attractive. Demand for industrial products and services may decline as a result of the need for alternatives. In times of normal economic growth and when the economy is doing well, substitution can increase fierce competition.
