24. It is a juridical relation whereby a person may demand from another the observance of a determinative conduct, and in case of breach, may demand satisfaction from the assets of the latter.


A legal relationship known as an obligation allows one person, known as the creditor, to demand from another, known as the debtor, the performance of a certain act (such as giving, performing, or not doing it), and in the event of a breach, to seek compensation from the latter's assets.

A legal requirement to contribute, to do, or not to do anything is called an obligation. According to the definition of duties, the debtor's unilateral decision to give, do, or refrain from doing something constitutes a patrimonial duty. It implies that while the creditor has rights, the debtor has obligations.

An obligation can be natural or civil. While Natural Obligation is based on natural law, but on equity and moral justice, Civil Obligation is founded on a positive law and grants the power of action to compel their execution. As a result, it cannot be enforced through legal action; but, following the debtor's voluntary performance, he is no longer able to get back what he has given.

28. I need your help, can you answer this po. Thank you po Questions. 1. In the Philippine context, what is obligation? 2. Why is obligation a juridical necessity? 3. What does “to do” or “not to do” means in the obligation? Expound your answer by providing atleast an example or illustration.


Answer:

1. In the Philippine context, obligation refers to a legal or moral duty that one is bound to fulfill or comply with. It is a requirement or responsibility that is imposed on an individual or entity to perform a certain act, whether it be an action or omission, based on a legal or moral relationship with another party.

2. Obligation is considered a juridical necessity because it is enforceable by law. In the Philippines, obligations arise from various sources such as contracts, laws, customs, and moral norms. When an obligation is recognized by law, it means that there are legal consequences for failing to fulfill that obligation. The aggrieved party may seek legal remedies, such as filing a lawsuit or seeking damages, to enforce the obligation and hold the defaulting party accountable.

3. "To do" or "not to do" in the context of obligation refers to the nature of the act or omission that is required or prohibited. In legal terms, an obligation to do something means that a person is required to perform a certain action. For example, a contract may impose an obligation on a seller to deliver goods to a buyer on a specific date. On the other hand, an obligation not to do something means that a person is prohibited from engaging in a particular action. For instance, a non-compete clause in an employment contract may obligate an employee not to work for a competitor for a certain period of time after the termination of employment.
