14. Junk food – Should it be banned at school? persuasive text pls.​


Firstly, I need to stand on my stance that I would definitely agree with this motion to ban junk foods at school.

Here is my text towards this issue:

Children around the world who are exposed to the marketing of foods high in high carbohydrates and fat, sugar, or salt, and this is called junk foods. They can increase the potential for students to develop infectious diseases during their lifetime. Food is fuel for human's body, especially children at school. It has a direct impact on the overall health of the body. One serving of junk food offers a large number of calories, but there is little or no nutritional value in it. What is feared is that students who replace nutritious food can lead to poor nutrition and poor health for their body.

The next astonishing problem is that many of the school children already have diabetes. Since junk food has no fiber content, consumption directly results in a spike in sugar levels. Furthermore, consumption of junk food leads to obesity, one of the main reasons for insulin resistance and the development of diabetes. For junk food addicts, this also leads to stomach acid disease and irritable bowel syndrome The lack of fiber in junk food can hinder digestion, increasing problems like constipation and hemorrhoids.

Therefore, students should eat nutritious foods to support the development of their bodies and ways of thinking. While junk food does not have the right nutrients to support it. Junk food only serves to fill the stomach briefly and provides almost nothing to help develop the brain. This generally results in children lacking concentration when doing tasks, having difficulty remembering for longer periods of time and receiving lessons.

So, I strongly support if junk food is banned in schools.

Definition of Junk Foods:

Junk food is a term used to describe foods that are high in calories, fat, sugar and salt. But the content of vitamins and fiber is low. Usually, junk food also contains various food additives such as sweeteners, flavors and preservatives. The World Health Organization (WHO) has made a ban not to sell and not to advertise fast food or junk food in school areas and playgrounds. It aims to promote a healthy diet and prevent obesity in children. According to data, there are about 43 million pre-school children who are obese and overweight. Children around the world who are exposed to the marketing of foods high in fat, sugar or salt, can increase the potential for young people to develop infectious diseases during their lifetime. In addition, 6 out of 10 deaths each year are caused by heart disease, cancer, diabetes and chronic lung disease. The common factor of the four major diseases is poor diet.
