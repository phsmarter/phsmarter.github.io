10. how hard is it to be a college student ​


Answer:

It’s hard if you have poor time management skills, take too many classes, try to work too many hours at a separate job, don’t plan your class selection well so you can graduate on time, or if you try to cram for tests on the last week of classes rather than across the term.

It’s quite manageable if you are willing to cut back your social life, do school work, read, and study from 8:00 a.m. until 5:00 p.m. each week day, and review materials over the course of the whole semester rather than cramming.

However, even if you do it right, it will still be harder than high school for the gen ed courses. Many students pass high school if they show up and are breathing. College won’t work that way, and there won’t be people watching out for you to force you to do the work.

So, a better question isn’t, “Is being a college student hard?” The better question is, “Is that college student self-motivated and disciplined?” That is the more important thing to ask when it comes to success.

14. how to enrolled in college step by step​


Step by Step on How to Enroll in College :

For manual enrollment, the steps should look like this:

FOR NEW STUDENTS:

1. Go to your school and submit your admission requirements. Normally, admission requirements are: records from previous school (card, Form 137, 1x1 and 2x2 pictures, good moral and birth certificate).

2. Get the enrollment form so you can pick up your schedule and section.

3. Have the registrar and teachers checked your desired schedule and section. Let them signed and validate your enrollment form.

4. Pay for fees (if any). Then you are now enrolled.

FOR OLD STUDENTS:

1. You must have passing grades to accelerate to higher year or take the next subjects. Otherwise, you will repeat the failed subjects. Have your grade slips ready and verified by the registrar or school teachers.

2. Once the registrar and teachers verified your grades, you can now select your section and year and subjects. Failed subjects must be taken again.

3. Have them signed your enrollment form.  

4. Pay for necessary fees. Then you are now enrolled.

If this is for the system, the step by step should look like this:

FOR NEW STUDENTS:

1. You must go to the school to submit your admission requirements. Sometimes, entrance exam is needed also.

2. Then Register to the student portal. Registration can be done manually in the school or online.

3. After registration, you will be given an access. Log in using the given credentials Log in credentials are composed of username and password.

4. After you logged in, you must select your year and section. You will be assigned by the system automatically to your chosen section or the system can make this decision for you.  

5. Print your schedule and go to your school to validate your enrollment.

6. Pay for fees (if any). Congratulations, you are now enrolled.

FOR EXISTING STUDENTS:

1. Make sure that you have your grade slip from the previous semester/year.

2. Log in the portal. You can select your section and year and the subjects that you would want to take.  

3. After, you must wait for the approval from the registrar or head teacher if you can take these subjects. (Sometimes, subjects has prerequisites. You cannot enroll subjects with failed prerequisites).

4. After approval, print your schedule. If your subjects are disapproved, seek advice from the head teacher or registrar's office.

5. Go to your school to validate your enrollment. Pay for fees (if any). Congratulations! You are now enrolled.

16. 10 tips on how to be successful in college


1. Look Friendly - If you were standing in front of the classroom, what kind of faces would you be drawn to? The smiling ones! Consider your facial expressions in class, and try to look interested and upbeat.

2. Actively Engage - Ask thoughtful questions, actively participate in discussions, look excited about activities, and show with your body language that you are there to learn and grow.

3. Go to Office Hours - Office hours can be a game-changer when it comes to succeeding in a class. Your professor will be sitting there whether anyone shows up or not. They will definitely appreciate having someone to talk to! Don’t wait until you’re behind or confused by the coursework to stop by, and feel free to attend more than once.

4. Give Yourself Time - Don’t wait until the last minute to do assignments and study for tests. If you don’t plan ahead, work will bottleneck and the stress of cramming isn’t good for anyone.

5. Be Original - It can be tempting to copy work or trade answers with someone who has been in the class already. Remember: the person doing the work is doing the learning. Leave college with as much information as you can.

6. Do Your Best - Give it your all. You won’t be able to be perfect, so allow yourself to be human. But at the end of the day, leave knowing you gave it 100%.

7. Buy Used - Books are so much cheaper pre-owned. And that’s not all! You can buy gently-used furniture for your dorm room and sell items you don’t need to someone else who needs them.

8. Ask for help - Don’t be afraid to ask for help if you need it. If that’s going to office hours, reaching out to other students to form a study group, scheduling an appointment with a school counselor, or talking to friends and family about how you’re feeling. Your mental health is more important than anything at school, so never fear seeking out extra support.

9. Be Open - Sure, you’ll show up at college with preconceived notions about life, but stay open-minded to learning new things, considering new possibilities, and even transforming what you believe in some cases. New experiences challenge us and allow us to grow.

10. Form Your Own Opinions - At the end of the day, you’re accountable for your choices and actions, so make sure you behave in a way that matches your values.

24. How to make a paper college


Answer:

How to Make a Paper Collage;To create a paper collage you will need:

1. A board/canvas

2. Paint and/or coloring pencils (optional)

3. Pencils/markers/pens

4. Glue

5. Paintbrush

6. Magazine Paper

7. Ruler

8. Scissors (optional)

STEP 1: YOUR WORKPLACE

↬First, to create your paper collage, you need a place or something to put it on. Simply get a canvas board or even simpler, a sheet of wood.

STEP 2: YOUR IMAGE

↬You can't create a paper collage without having something to make. Pick any picture or image that you like and base it off of that. It could be anything (an animal, nature, etc.)

STEP 3: THE GRID

↬To properly transfer your image to your canvas/board, the best method is the grid method. You draw a grid on both your image and your canvas using your ruler and pen/pencil/marker.

STEP 4: TRANSFERRING YOUR IMAGE

↬Now that your grid is on both your image and canvas, it's time for you to transfer that image onto your canvas. Using your pencil, copy whatever is in a certain box on the grid of your image into the same box on the grid on your canvas. This gives the picture on the canvas an almost identical transfer from the original picture.

STEP 5: DISTINGUISH THE PARTS OF YOUR IMAGE

↬Now that your picture is now on your canvas, use paint, coloring pencils, or simply write the name of the colors in their desired areas. This makes it easier to arrange the colors with the paper later on.

STEP 6: GET THE PAPER

↬You now need to get some paper (preferably magazine paper). Find pages out of magazines that match the colors you are using and tear them up into small pieces. (This is where the scissors are optional depending on how you want your collage to come out. The scissors may make a cleaner cut but you may prefer the rough look tearing the paper by hand provides.)

STEP 7: MAKE YOUR CANVAS STICKY

↬Using a paintbrush, lightly spread layers of glue on your canvas. This is the only way your pieces of paper will stick to your canvas.

STEP 8: PUT IT TOGETHER

↬When your canvas is still wet with glue place the pieces of paper in there desired places (by size and by color).

STEP 9: REPEAT

↬Continue to spread glue on your canvas and place pieces of paper in their desired places (steps 7 & 8).

STEP 10: WRAP IT UP

↬When finished placing your paper on your canvas and you have gotten your desired look, spread one last layer onto your piece. This flattens any bumpy or rising pieces of paper and makes it look smoother. Wait for it to dry hang your masterpiece.

26. How to get to a college level?


Answer:

There are several steps you can take to prepare for college-level coursework:

1. Take challenging high school courses: To prepare for college-level coursework, it is important to take challenging courses in high school, such as advanced placement (AP) or honors classes. These classes can help you develop the skills and knowledge you will need to succeed in college.

2. Study and practice regularly: College coursework is often more challenging and time-consuming than high school coursework, so it is important to develop good study habits and allocate enough time for your studies.

3. Seek academic support: Many colleges offer academic support services such as tutoring, writing centers, and study groups to help students succeed. Take advantage of these resources if you need extra help with your coursework.

4. Get involved in extracurricular activities: Participating in extracurricular activities, such as clubs or sports, can help you develop important skills, such as teamwork and leadership, which can be beneficial in college.

5. Consider taking college-level courses while in high school: Some high schools offer dual enrollment programs, which allow students to earn college credits while still in high school. This can help you get a head start on your college education and prepare you for the rigors of college coursework.
