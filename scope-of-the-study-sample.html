19. Significance of the Study? Scope and Limitations of the Study? Definition of Terms? ​


Answer:

Significance of the Study?

The significance of the study is a written statement that explains why your research was needed. It’s a justification of the importance of your work and impact it has on your research field, it’s contribution to new knowledge and how others will benefit from it.

Scope and Limitations of the Study?

The scope of a study explains the extent to which the research area will be explored in the work and specifies the parameters within the study will be operating. ... Generally, the scope of a research paper is followed by its limitations. As a researcher, you have to be careful when you define your scope or area of focus.

Definition of Terms?

An alphabetical list of important terms or acronyms that you define, particularly ambiguous terms or those used in a special way. Your thesis proposal will likely include terms that are not widely known outside of your discipline.

The scope and delimitation of a study refer to the research study’s coverage.  This part of a research answers the following questions:

What is the focus of the research study? What does the research talk about? What are the objectives of the research? Who are the respondents of the study? How were the respondents chosen to participate in the study? What are the methods used to fulfill the study?

Through the guide questions mentioned above, the readers of the research study will get an overview of what the research study is about and how the study is going to be performed, and later on, fulfilled.
