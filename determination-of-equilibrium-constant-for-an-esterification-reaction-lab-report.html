12. The reaction quotient, q꜀, for a reaction has a value of 75 while the equilibrium constant, k꜀, has a value of 195. which of the following statements is accurate?


Answer:

The reaction quotient, Q, is a measure of the relative concentrations of reactants and products in a reaction mixture at a given point in time, while the equilibrium constant, K, is a measure of the relative concentrations of reactants and products at chemical equilibrium.

If the value of Q is greater than K, the reaction is said to be "non-equilibrium" and the system will tend to shift in the direction that will reduce Q to K. On the other hand, if the value of Q is less than K, the reaction is said to be "favored" and the system will tend to shift in the direction that will increase Q to K.

In this case, since the value of Q is 75 and the value of K is 195, it can be concluded that the reaction is non-equilibrium and the system will tend to shift in the direction that will reduce Q to K.

23. determine the equilibrium constant K at 25°C for the reaction N2(g)+3H2(g)=2NH3(g). (∆G°NH3(g)=-16.6kj/mol​


Given:

[tex]\Delta G_{\text{f}}^{\circ}(\text{NH}_{3}) = -\text{16.6 kJ/mol}[/tex]

[tex]T = \text{25°C + 273 = 298 K}[/tex]

balanced chemical equation: N₂(g) + 3H₂(g) [tex]\rightleftharpoons[/tex] 2NH₃(g)

Required:

[tex]K[/tex]

Solution:

Step 1: Calculate the standard free energy change of reaction based on the balanced chemical equation.

Note: The standard free energy change of formation of any element in its most stable form is zero. Therefore, the standard free energy change of formation of N₂ and H₂ are both zero.

[tex]\Delta G_{\text{rxn}}^{\circ} = \sum n \Delta G_{\text{f}}^{\circ}(\text{products}) - \sum m \Delta G_{\text{f}}^{\circ}(\text{reactants})[/tex]

[tex]\Delta G_{\text{rxn}}^{\circ} = 2\Delta G_{\text{f}}^{\circ}(\text{NH}_{3}) - [\Delta G_{\text{f}}^{\circ}(\text{N}_{2}) + 3\Delta G_{\text{f}}^{\circ}(\text{H}_{2})][/tex]

[tex]\Delta G_{\text{rxn}}^{\circ} = 2(-\text{16.6 kJ/mol}) - [0 + 3(0)][/tex]

[tex]\Delta G_{\text{rxn}}^{\circ} = -\text{33.2 kJ/mol} = -\text{33200 J/mol}[/tex]

Step 2: Calculate the equilibrium constant for the reaction at 25°C.

[tex]\Delta G_{\text{rxn}}^{\circ} = -RT \: \text{ln} \: K[/tex]

[tex]K = e^{-\frac{\Delta G_{\text{rxn}}^{\circ}}{RT}}[/tex]

[tex]K = e^{-\frac{-\text{33200 J/mol}}{(\text{8.314 J/K • mol})(\text{298 K})}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{K = 6.60 × 10^{5}}[/tex]
