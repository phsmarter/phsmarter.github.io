9. 2. How can writers use different types of claims, such as fact, value, and policy claims, to develop effective paragraphs and persuade readers? In your answer, provide specific examples of how different types of claims can be used in different contexts, and explain how writers can use evidence and reasoning to support their claims. essay​


Answer:

In persuasive writing, writers can use different types of claims to develop effective paragraphs and persuade readers. These claims are usually categorized as fact claims, value claims, and policy claims. By understanding the differences between these types of claims and using them appropriately, writers can effectively convince their readers.

Fact claims are statements that can be proven to be true or false, and they are often used to establish the basis for an argument. For example, in an essay about climate change, a writer might make the fact claim that global temperatures have been rising steadily over the past century. To support this claim, the writer would need to provide evidence from reputable sources, such as scientific studies and reports.

Value claims, on the other hand, are statements that express an opinion about what is good or bad, right or wrong, or desirable or undesirable. For example, in an essay about animal welfare, a writer might make the value claim that it is morally wrong to use animals for human entertainment, such as in circuses or bullfighting. To support this claim, the writer would need to use reasoning and provide evidence, such as examples of animal cruelty in these types of events.

Policy claims are statements that propose a course of action, and they often involve making changes to existing policies or creating new ones. For example, in an essay about gun control, a writer might make the policy claim that stricter regulations on gun ownership are necessary to reduce gun violence. To support this claim, the writer would need to use evidence, such as statistics on gun-related deaths, and reasoning, such as explaining how stricter regulations could reduce access to guns by those who are at risk of using them for violence.

To effectively persuade readers, writers should use evidence and reasoning to support their claims. Evidence can come from a variety of sources, such as scientific studies, statistics, and personal anecdotes. Reasoning involves explaining how the evidence supports the claim and anticipating and addressing counterarguments that readers might have. Writers can also use emotional appeals to connect with their readers and make their arguments more persuasive.

In conclusion, writers can use different types of claims to develop effective paragraphs and persuade readers. By using fact claims to establish a basis for their arguments, value claims to express opinions about what is desirable or undesirable, and policy claims to propose courses of action, writers can effectively convince their readers. Moreover, writers should use evidence and reasoning to support their claims, anticipate and address counterarguments, and use emotional appeals to make their arguments more persuasive.
