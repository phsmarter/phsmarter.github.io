Answer:

Start by defining the key terms in your topic. What do you mean by "TVL-ICT students," "career aspirations," and "higher education"? Clarifying these terms will help you narrow down your search and find more relevant sources.

Use academic search engines and databases such as Google Scholar, JSTOR, and ProQuest to find relevant literature and studies. These platforms provide access to scholarly articles, books, and other sources.

Look for sources that are recent, peer-reviewed, and published in reputable journals or academic presses. These sources are more likely to be reliable and accurate.

Take notes on the key findings, methodologies, and limitations of each source. This will help you synthesize the information and identify gaps in the literature that you can address in your own research.

Finally, make sure to properly cite all sources using a citation style such as APA or MLA.

14. Title thesis and justification example​


Answer:

A potential thesis statement and justification for a paper could be:

Thesis: The use of solitary confinement in prisons has a detrimental effect on the mental health of inmates and should be banned.

Justification: Solitary confinement, also known as isolation, is a form of punishment used in prisons that involves isolating inmates in a small cell for extended periods of time. Studies have shown that this type of confinement can have severe psychological effects on prisoners, including depression, anxiety, and even psychosis. It can also lead to self-harm and suicide. Therefore, the use of solitary confinement in prisons should be banned to protect the mental health of inmates.

This thesis is supported by research on the psychological effects of solitary confinement and aligns with the principle of protecting the well-being of prisoners.
