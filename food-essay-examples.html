3. introduction body conclusion essay example favourite food adobo​


Answer:

Introduction:

Food is an essential part of our lives and it brings us not only nourishment but also joy and comfort. For many people, their favorite food holds a special place in their hearts and holds many memories.

Body:

Adobo is a dish that originated in the Philippines and it is a staple in many Filipino households. It is made by cooking meat (chicken, pork, or both) in a mixture of soy sauce, vinegar, garlic, bay leaves, and black peppercorns. The result is a savory and delicious dish that has a tangy and slightly salty flavor. I love adobo because of its simplicity, versatility, and the depth of flavor that is achieved through slow cooking.

One of the things I love about adobo is its versatility. It can be made with chicken, pork, or a combination of both. Additionally, it can be served with rice or paired with other dishes to create a full meal. Adobo is also a great dish to make in large batches because it tastes even better the next day, making it perfect for leftovers.

Another reason why adobo is my favorite food is because of the depth of flavor that is achieved through slow cooking. The combination of soy sauce, vinegar, garlic, bay leaves, and black peppercorns creates a unique flavor that is hard to find in other dishes. The slow cooking process allows the flavors to penetrate the meat, resulting in a tender and delicious dish.

Conclusion:

In conclusion, adobo is a dish that is near and dear to my heart. Its simplicity, versatility, and depth of flavor make it one of my favorite foods. Whether I am eating it at home, at a Filipino restaurant, or with friends and family, adobo always brings a smile to my face and comfort to my soul. I hope that this essay has given you a glimpse into why adobo is such a special dish to me and why I believe it deserves a place on everyone's list of favorite foods.

10. B.Make an essay on how is food preservation is beneficial for us. You may cite specific examples​


Answer:

Preservation methods used in the past include drying and freezing but with the increasing demand for higher food safety and quality means novel preservation methods which would produce these results are constantly being explored.

Concept of shelf-life extension for the method under consideration

Biopreservation is one novel method with an ecological approach which is being developed to lengthen the shelf life of products. Naturally occurring or controlled micro-organisms produce fermentation products and favourable bacteria which are then used to render pathogens inactive in food and regulate spoilage. Bacteriocin preperatoins being added to meat batters which extend the shelf life by reducing the initial quantity of bacteriocin sensitive populations and also cause the inactivation of microbial pathogens in fermented meat products for example Italian sausage.

It results in minimal loss to the fresh food characteristics and nutritional value of the food. It has a lower impact on global warming due to its lower energy requirements. It also prevents costly waste and food loss in all industries in which it is applied. As the food is packaged previously to processing it results in a large decrease in the possibility of recontamination that can occur if a food is packaged after processing. There are also limitations associated with the process. A high capital cost being the most prominent. The machinery is very complex, extreme precision and care is required for its installation.
