10. how do you promote literacy and numeracy ​


Answer:

There are various ways to promote literacy and numeracy skills, including:

Encouraging reading: Encourage reading at an early age, and make sure there are age-appropriate books available. Encourage your child to read for fun and offer them books that interest them.

Providing a print-rich environment: Having books, newspapers, magazines, and other written materials available in the home can help to promote literacy skills.

Making math fun: Incorporate math games and activities into everyday life, such as counting objects or playing math-related board games.

Encouraging writing: Encourage your child to write stories, letters, or even a journal. This can help to improve literacy skills.

Using technology: Technology can be a great tool for promoting literacy and numeracy skills. There are many educational apps and websites available that can help children improve their skills.

Providing educational opportunities: Ensure your child has access to quality education and opportunities to improve their literacy and numeracy skills. This may include after-school programs, tutoring, or extra-curricular activities.

Modeling: Parents can model good reading and writing habits by reading themselves and by writing in front of their children. They can also model good math skills by involving their children in everyday tasks that involve math, such as grocery shopping or cooking.

Making learning relevant: When children see how literacy and numeracy skills are relevant to their lives, they are more likely to be motivated to learn. Parents can make learning relevant by discussing how reading, writing, and math are used in everyday life.

28. performance on literacy and numeracy compared to other countries​


ANSWER:

According to the Programme for International Student Assessment (PISA) 2015 report , the United States ranks 17th in literacy and 39th in mathematics among the 72 participating countries. The US also ranks near the OECD average in reading and science performance. Another study, the Trends in International Mathematics and Science Study (TIMSS), found that the US ranks 13th in fourth-grade mathematics and 11th in fourth-grade science among the 50 participating countries. Overall, the US performs relatively well in literacy and numeracy compared to other countries.
