15. The biotic components of an ecosystem include the ______, _____, and ______. ​


Answer:

The biotic factors of an ecosystem include all the populations in a habitat, such as all the species of plants, animals, and fungi, as well as all the micro-organisms. Biotic factors are the living components of an ecosystem. They are sorted into three groups: producers or autotrophs, consumers or heterotrophs, and decomposers or detritivores.

Also recall that the nonliving factors are called abiotic factors. Abiotic factors include temperature, water, soil, and air.

25. what are the biotic Factors in the environment.​


Answer:

Biotic factors refer to the living components of an ecosystem. They include all forms of life, such as plants, animals, and microorganisms. These factors interact with each other and with the abiotic (non-living) components of the environment, such as the climate, soil, and water, to create a complex web of relationships that sustain life on Earth. Some examples of biotic factors in the environment include:

Plants: They are the primary producers in an ecosystem, using energy from the sun to produce food through photosynthesis. They also provide habitats and food for other organisms.

Animals: They are the consumers in an ecosystem, feeding on plants or other animals for energy. They also play important roles in the cycling of nutrients and the control of pests.

Microorganisms: They play critical roles in the cycling of nutrients and the decomposition of organic matter. They also help to control populations of other organisms and shape the structure of ecosystem's
