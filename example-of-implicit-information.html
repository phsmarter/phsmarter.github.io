Explanation:

Explicit Information

Explicit information is clearly stated, leaving nothing implied. For example, the phrase “it was a dark and stormy night,” uses explicit information that leaves no room for debate. The reader cannot assume, by any stretch of the imagination, that the story is in fact taking place on a sunny morning.

Implicit Information

This is an example of an implicit statement. We aren’t told explicitly “there once was a boy named Peter Pan, and he magically never grew older,” but we are prepared for that eventual knowledge by this implicit sentence.

21. Give five examples of Implicit convo.​


Implicit conversation refers to the exchange of unspoken or indirect communication between people. Here are five examples of implicit conversations:

1. Body language: When two people are communicating, their body language can convey unspoken messages. For example, crossing arms can indicate defensiveness or discomfort, while nodding can indicate agreement or understanding.

2. Tone of voice: The tone of voice can also convey implicit messages. For example, a sarcastic tone can indicate that the speaker is not being serious, while a monotone voice can indicate boredom or disinterest.

3. Facial expressions: Facial expressions can convey a wide range of emotions and attitudes, such as happiness, anger, confusion, or suspicion.

4. Cultural references: People from different cultures may use implicit references that are not understood by others. For example, a reference to a local sports team may be understood only by people who are familiar with the team and its history.

5. Silence: Sometimes, silence can be an implicit form of communication. For example, not responding to a message can indicate disapproval or disagreement, while a long pause before responding can indicate that the speaker is considering their words carefully.
