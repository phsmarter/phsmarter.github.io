1. How is Psychological Criticism different from Biographical Criticism


Psychological Criticism, also known as Psychoanalytical Criticism, is the analysis of an author's unintended message. The analysis focuses on the biographical circumstances of an author. The main goal is to analyze the unconscious elements within a literary text based on the background of the author.

example: The Lion King

Biographical criticism asserts that an author's own life must affect his or her work

25. Directions: Write a 200-word literary criticism of the text All these Fighting Cocks using any of the three discussed approach (Gender criticism, psychological criticism, and sociological criticism). Write your answer on a separate sheet of paper. See the rubric below for scoring.​


The text "All these Fighting Cocks" can be interpreted through a gender criticism lens, as it explores the toxic masculinity and violence associated with cockfighting. The narrator, a young boy, is eager to prove his masculinity by participating in the violent sport, despite his mother's disapproval. The male characters in the story view the roosters as extensions of themselves, and derive pleasure from the brutal fights.

Through a psychological criticism approach, the story can be seen as a depiction of the effects of toxic masculinity on young boys. The narrator idolizes his father and grandfather, who are both involved in cockfighting, and feels the need to prove himself to them. The violent nature of the sport also serves as an outlet for the emotions that the male characters struggle to express in a healthy manner.

From a sociological criticism perspective, the story highlights the cultural significance of cockfighting in certain communities. The rural setting and the men's devotion to the sport can be seen as a representation of the traditions and values that are deeply ingrained in certain regions. The story also brings to light the issue of animal cruelty and the ethical implications of participating in this type of activity.

Overall, "All these Fighting Cocks" portrays the harmful effects of toxic masculinity and the cultural significance of certain traditions, while also shedding light on the issue of animal cruelty.
