You may choose to either use the alternate method or block method to structure your essay. It must also consist of 500 words or more which can either be typed- or hand written in a short bond paper. Answer:

For this activity, you will need to choose two literary pieces to compare and contrast using a critical literary approach. You will then use your knowledge of this approach to analyze and compare the two pieces in your essay.

There are two main ways you can structure your comparative essay: the alternate method and the block method.

In the alternate method, you will first introduce the two literary pieces and provide an overview of the critical literary approach you will be using. You will then alternate between discussing one literary piece and then the other, discussing the themes, characters, plot, and other elements of each piece in relation to the critical literary approach. Finally, you will conclude by summarizing your main points and discussing the overall significance of the comparison.

In the block method, you will introduce the two literary pieces and provide an overview of the critical literary approach you will be using. You will then discuss all of the elements of one literary piece in relation to the critical literary approach, and then move on to discussing all of the elements of the other literary piece in the same way. Finally, you will conclude by summarizing your main points and discussing the overall significance of the comparison.

Regardless of which method you choose, it is important to make sure that your essay is well-organized, clearly written, and well-supported by evidence from the literary pieces. Your essay should also be at least 500 words in length and should address all of the key points required for your paper.

15. reflection: What is your favorite literary criticism? Why? What is your most important learning from this lesson? answer it in essay​


Literary criticism is an important tool that helps us understand and appreciate literature. It is a discipline that analyzes and interprets literary works, examining their form, content, and context to understand their meaning and significance. There are different types of literary criticism, including historical, feminist, psychoanalytic, formalist, and cultural criticism, among others.

Among the various types of literary criticism, my favorite is the feminist criticism. Feminist criticism emerged in the 1960s and 1970s as a response to the lack of attention given to women writers and their works. Feminist critics analyze literary works from a feminist perspective, exploring the representation of gender, sexuality, and power in literature. Feminist criticism aims to expose and challenge the patriarchal norms and values that dominate society and the literary canon.

What I find most interesting about feminist criticism is its focus on the relationship between literature and social and political issues. Feminist critics examine how literature reflects and shapes our attitudes towards gender, sexuality, and power. They also pay attention to the representation of women and other marginalized groups in literature, questioning the dominant stereotypes and tropes that have been perpetuated throughout history.

One of the most important lessons that I have learned from studying literary criticism is the importance of context. Literary works cannot be understood in isolation from their historical, social, and cultural contexts. The meaning of a text can vary depending on the time and place it was written, the author's background, and the audience for which it was intended. By understanding the context in which a work was produced, we can gain a deeper appreciation for its significance and meaning.

Another important lesson that I have learned from literary criticism is the value of diverse perspectives. Literary works can be interpreted in multiple ways, and different critics may have different interpretations based on their backgrounds, experiences, and values. By reading and engaging with a variety of critical perspectives, we can gain a more comprehensive understanding of a work and its significance.

In conclusion, literary criticism is a valuable tool for understanding and appreciating literature. My favorite type of criticism is feminist criticism because of its focus on gender, power, and representation. Studying literary criticism has taught me the importance of context and diverse perspectives, which are essential for gaining a deeper appreciation for literary works.
