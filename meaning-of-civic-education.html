5. difference between youth civic engagement and leadership civic engagement​


Answer:

Youth civic engagement refers to the participation and involvement of young people in civic activities and decision-making processes that affect their communities. This can include volunteering, participating in community projects, or becoming involved in political campaigns.

Leadership civic engagement, on the other hand, refers to the development and demonstration of leadership skills in the context of civic engagement. This can include taking on leadership roles in community organizations, running for office, or leading grassroots movements. The focus is more on developing leadership skills and taking initiative in creating positive change in the community.

Explanation:

In summary, youth civic engagement focuses on the participation and involvement of young people in civic activities, while leadership civic engagement focuses on the development and demonstration of leadership skills in the context of civic engagement.
