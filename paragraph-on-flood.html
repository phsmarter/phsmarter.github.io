Flooding is a natural disaster that can cause significant damage to property, infrastructure, and human life. Floods may occur as part of a large storm event or in areas prone to flooding. They are caused by heavy rainfall, excess snowmelt, and runoff from fast-moving rivers.

To prevent flooding, it is important to understand the different types of flooding and the risk they pose to your property and your community. Many factors, including heavy rainfall, excess snowmelt, and runoff from fast-moving rivers, can cause a flood. Combined with sufficient water volume (including surge), these factors can lead to widespread damage.

Prevention efforts include:

• Preparing for storms. This includes making sure you have adequate supplies of food, water, electricity, and other essentials in case of power outages or evacuation due to flooding;

• Knowing how much water you can store on site;

• Storing important documents such as insurance policies in a safe place;

• Identifying vulnerable areas on your property that may be more susceptible to flooding during heavy rainstorms;

• Planning for emergency evacuations should a flood occur;

• Encouraging everyone within the community to take action when possible
