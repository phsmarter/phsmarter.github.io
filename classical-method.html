27. What is the purpose of "post-quantum" cryptography? O to require users to own quantum computers in order to send data securely O to eliminate all encryption methods programmed on classical computers O to use quantum computers to create more secure encryption methods O to create encryption methods that quantum computers cannot crack O I don't know this yet.


Answer:

The purpose of "post-quantum" cryptography is to create encryption methods that quantum computers cannot crack. This is in response to the potential development of quantum computers, which may be able to break traditional encryption methods that are currently used to secure data. "Post-quantum" cryptography aims to provide a level of security that can withstand the power of quantum computers.
