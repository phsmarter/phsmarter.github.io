10. 1.Why is the novel considered as a timeliness masterpiece of Philippines literature ? ​


The novel "Noli Me Tangere" by Jose Rizal is considered a timeless masterpiece of Philippines literature because it effectively addresses social issues and injustices that were prevalent during its time, which remain relevant and applicable to contemporary Philippine society. The novel exposes the corruption, greed, and oppression of the Spanish colonial government and the religious institutions, shedding light on the struggles of the Filipinos in their fight for freedom and social justice. Additionally, it depicts the everyday lives and aspirations of common Filipino people, making them more relatable and humanized to readers. The novel also showcases Rizal's mastery of the language, his ability to weave intricate plots and characters, and his literary finesse in tackling sensitive and controversial issues. Through "Noli Me Tangere," Rizal has left an indelible mark on Philippine literature and culture, inspiring generations of readers to advocate for change and to uphold the values of nationalism, freedom, and social justice.

13. the first tagalog novel printed in the Philippines?


Answer:

Nínay is the first novel authored by a native Filipino.

Answer:

Nínay is the first novel authored by a native Filipino. Originally written in the Spanish language by Pedro Alejandro Paterno when he was twenty-three years old and while living in Spain in 1885, the novel was later translated into English in 1907 and into Tagalog in 1908.
