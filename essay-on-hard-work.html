1. hard work vs smart work essay English ​


In the modern world, ?hard work? And ?smart work? It is two terms commonly used to describe the approach to work. While both are considered valuable, they have clear differences and benefits. In this essay, we will explore the meaning of hard work and smart work, their differences, and the benefits of each.   Hard work refers to putting in a lot of effort and time to achieve a goal. It is often associated with perseverance and dedication, and it is widely believed that success comes through hard work. Those who subscribe to this philosophy believe that the only way to succeed is to work tirelessly and overcome any obstacles that come your way. This approach often involves working long hours, sacrificing personal time, and focusing solely on the task at hand.   On the other hand, smart work refers to an approach that aims to work efficiently and effectively to achieve the desired result. Instead of putting in long hours, intelligent employees prioritize their tasks and focus on the most important ones first. This approach often involves strategic planning, using resources effectively, and working with others to achieve the desired outcome. Smart employees often prioritize their work based on urgency and importance, and  use their time and  resources wisely to get the job done.   The main difference between hard work and smart work is the focus on effort versus efficiency. Hard workers focus on trying as hard as possible, while smart workers focus on working efficiently to get the best results. Hard work often means working more than necessary, while smart work means working smarter, not harder.   Both approaches have their advantages. Hard work can lead to a sense of accomplishment and personal satisfaction, and  can help people develop important skills like perseverance and determination. On the other hand, working smart  can help people achieve their goals faster and more  efficiently, which can help them  achieve  better work-life balance and maintain better mental health.   In summary, the choice between working hard  and working smart  ultimately comes down to individual goals and preferences. Those who prefer to work tirelessly and overcome obstacles may prefer hard work, while those who value efficiency and work-life balance may prefer working smart. Ultimately, both approaches can lead to success, and it is up to you to decide which one works best for you.

What is parallelism?

It is also called parallel structure or parallel construction. It is the repetition of the same grammatical form in two or more parts of a sentence.  It balances two or more similar words, phrases or clauses in grammar.

What are the rules in parallelism?

1. Balancing nouns with nouns, prepositional phrases with prepositional phrases, participles with participles, infinitives with infinitives, clauses with clauses.

2. Parallelism uses elements joined by coordinating conjunctions.

Examples:  In our English class, Lilly learned to interpret poems critically and analyze an essay.

3. Parallelism uses elements in lists or in a series.

Example: I was already planning to find a good pool, have a relaxing massage, and spend a couple of days watching videos.

4. Use elements being compared.

Example: She likes watching TV more than cooking a meal.

5. Use elements joined by a linking verb or a form of be

Example: To learn is to understand the world.

6. Use elements joined by linking words.

The teacher wants his students not only to keep quiet but also to do their group activity.
