1. 3_5 Paragraph manuscript speech about life in the New Normal​


Answer:

As we grow up, we encounter change on a regular basis: change in where we live, teachers in school, work, and more. Most change is anticipated and even welcome as a rite of passage. But, some change is unexpected: family plans falling apart, losing a job, or even the death of a loved one. Even when the unexpected occurs, while we may not be happy about the changes, we find a way to move on, and things eventually get back to what might be considered normal.The reality is that the unexpected arrival of COVID-19, the coronavirus, has caused many unwelcome changes to everything from daily routines to employment status. The disruption to everyday life is, well, unprecedented. And the reality is that things will never quite be the same again. There is nothing we can safely regard as normal. We are, at this very moment, creating a new version of what normal will really look like going forward.

6. manuscript speech topic: pandemic?​


Answer:

Good morning everyone.

Today I want to talk about the pandemic that has taken over the world. From the very start of 2020, our lives have been drastically altered. We have all had to learn to live with a virus that has no known cure and that has caused so much pain and suffering.

The virus has caused an unprecedented global health crisis. We have all had to adjust to a new way of life, from staying home and social distancing to wearing masks and avoiding large gatherings. The restrictions have been hard on us, but they have been necessary in order to contain the virus and protect ourselves and our communities.

The pandemic has also had a devastating economic impact. Millions of people have lost their jobs, businesses have had to close, and entire industries have been disrupted. Governments around the world have had to take drastic measures in order to support their citizens and keep the economy afloat.

The pandemic has also highlighted existing inequalities, both nationally and globally. It has shown how vulnerable certain populations are, especially those with low incomes, people of color, and the elderly.

The pandemic has also had a major impact on mental health. People all over the world have experienced anxiety, stress, and depression, as well as loneliness and isolation.

We can all do our part to help mitigate the effects of the pandemic. We can follow the safety protocols and take care of ourselves and our loved ones. We can also show compassion and understanding towards those who are struggling, and support those in need.

We can also look forward to the future and focus on the good that can come out of this situation. We can work together to build a more equitable and sustainable world for our children and grandchildren.

Thank you.

Manuscript about pandemic

Good morning / afternoon / evening, everyone.

As we all know, over the past year our world has been turned upside down by the ongoing pandemic. It has affected every aspect of our lives, from the way we work and go to school, to the way we socialize and travel. It has also had a profound impact on our economy, with many businesses struggling to survive and millions of people losing their jobs.

But despite the challenges we have faced, I believe that there are reasons to be hopeful. Over the past year, we have seen incredible advances in medical science, with vaccines being developed and distributed at an unprecedented pace. We have also seen a remarkable spirit of resilience and determination among people all over the world, who have adapted to new ways of living and working in order to protect themselves and those around them.

Of course, the pandemic is not over yet, and we still face many challenges. But I am confident that if we continue to work together, we will overcome them. We must remain vigilant and continue to follow public health guidelines, such as wearing masks and practicing social distancing. We must also support one another, especially those who have been hardest hit by the pandemic, such as small businesses and those who have lost their jobs.

In conclusion, the pandemic has been a difficult and trying time for all of us. But I believe that if we come together and continue to support one another, we will overcome it and emerge stronger than ever. Thank you.

14. What are the benefits of doing the impromptu speech, manuscript speech and memorized speech?​


Answer:

IMPROMPTU SPEECH:

Becoming skilled at impromptu speaking can give you the self-confidence you need to give a last-minute presentation, sail through a challenging meeting, or convince others of your ideas. You can build your reputation as a Lions leader when you learn to speak effectively under pressure.

MANUSCRIPT SPEECH:

Advantages: (1) control of time, (2) control of word choice, and (3) when style is critical—elegant language. Disadvantages: (1) loss of eye contact and (2) loss of natural speaking style. The two disadvantages typically far outweigh the advantages of this style. Memorized: Speech is memorized word-for-word.

MEMORIZED SPEECH:

When it comes to speeches, memorization can be useful when the message needs to be exact and the speaker doesn't want to be confined by notes. The advantage to memorization is that it enables the speaker to maintain eye contact with the audience throughout the speech

Health is wealth
