12. Make an essay about What cultural differences and similarities between you and your classmate?


Cultural Differences and Similarities Between Me and My Classmate

My classmate and I have a distinctive set of cultural differences and similarities because we both grew up in different parts of the world. The experiences we had in school helped my Chinese classmate and I, who are both from the United States, to respect one another's cultural heritages.

Our communication styles are one of the biggest cultural differences between us. While I am more direct and assertive in my communication, my classmate tends to be more indirect and reserved. For instance, whereas I tend to be more outspoken and assertive when expressing my opinions, my classmate may be reluctant to speak up or offer their opinion during group discussions. Our group work has occasionally been complicated by this cultural difference, but we've learned to communicate and accommodate one another's learning styles.

Our tastes in food represent another cultural difference. I had never tried any of the traditional Chinese foods that my classmate introduced me to. I've also fed them some of my favorite hamburgers and hot dogs from the United States. Despite these differences, we share a love of trying new foods and an appreciation for the role that food plays in each of our cultures.

Nevertheless, despite these cultural differences, we also have a lot in common. For instance, we both place a high value on education and work hard to succeed in school. We both love music, and we enjoy listening to a wide range of styles, from classical to pop.

We also recognize the value of family and the reverence for elders in our respective cultures. Although we may express these values in different ways, the fundamental ideas remain the same.

In conclusion, my classmate and I have experienced unusual interactions and difficulties as a result of our cultural contrasts and similarities. But through effective communication and learning to respect one another's backgrounds, our friendship has grown stronger and richer.

Self-dependence: The Key to Success

As we navigate through life, we are faced with numerous challenges that test our abilities to survive and succeed. Many of these challenges can be overcome by relying on the help and support of others. However, the ability to be self-reliant, or self-dependent, can be the key to unlocking a lifetime of success and personal fulfillment. In this essay, I will present my arguments for why self-dependence is the best and most effective way to succeed in life.

First and foremost, being self-dependent allows individuals to take control of their lives. Rather than relying on others to make decisions for them, self-dependent individuals take the initiative to make their own decisions and take responsibility for the outcomes of those decisions. This ability to take control and make decisions for oneself is a crucial skill that is necessary for personal and professional success.

Furthermore, self-dependence fosters resilience and the ability to adapt to change. When faced with obstacles, self-dependent individuals are equipped to face these challenges head-on, without relying on the help of others. This ability to adapt and overcome challenges is a key characteristic of successful individuals and is often what sets them apart from others.

In addition, self-dependence promotes self-confidence and self-esteem. When individuals are able to rely on themselves to accomplish tasks and achieve their goals, they feel a sense of accomplishment and pride that cannot be attained through the help of others. This self-confidence and self-esteem can carry over into all areas of life, including personal relationships and professional endeavors.

Finally, self-dependence is empowering. By relying on oneself to achieve success, individuals are able to break free from the limitations that others may place on them. They are able to set their own goals and work towards them without fear of judgement or failure. This sense of empowerment can lead to a lifetime of personal fulfillment and happiness.

In conclusion, self-dependence is the best and most effective way to succeed in life. It allows individuals to take control of their lives, fosters resilience and adaptability, promotes self-confidence and self-esteem, and is empowering. By being self-dependent, individuals can achieve personal and professional success, break free from limitations, and live a life of fulfillment and happiness. So, let us all strive to be self-dependent and take control of our own destinies!

18. Persuasive essay,about how to convince your classmates to study to get a high grades​


As students, we all know that getting good grades is important for our future success. It is not just about getting into a good college or university, but also about developing the skills and knowledge that will help us in our future careers. Unfortunately, many of our classmates do not take their studies seriously, which can hurt not only their own future but also the overall performance of the class. In this essay, I will discuss how to convince your classmates to study to get high grades.

Firstly, it is important to understand the benefits of getting good grades. Good grades are not just a way to impress your teachers or parents, but they also help you build a strong foundation of knowledge and skills that will benefit you in the future. High grades can lead to better job opportunities, higher salaries, and more chances for advancement in your career. Additionally, getting good grades can help you feel more confident in your abilities, which can positively impact your mental health.

One way to convince your classmates to study is by leading by example. If you are consistently getting high grades and demonstrating that studying pays off, your classmates may be more inclined to follow your lead. You can also offer to study together or form study groups, as this can help motivate your classmates to study and give them a sense of accountability. When you work together, you can bounce ideas off each other and learn from one another.

Another way to encourage your classmates to study is by highlighting the consequences of not studying. For example, failing grades can lead to repeating classes or even being held back a grade. This can cause a delay in graduation, which can ultimately affect your career plans. Additionally, students who do not study are often unprepared for exams and assignments, which can lead to stress and anxiety. By emphasizing the negative consequences of not studying, you can motivate your classmates to take their studies more seriously.

In conclusion, getting good grades is important for our future success, and it is essential to encourage our classmates to take their studies seriously. By leading by example, highlighting the consequences of not studying, and making studying fun and engaging, we can motivate our classmates to study and ultimately improve the overall performance of our class.
