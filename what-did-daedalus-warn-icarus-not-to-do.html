2. WHAT DID DAEDALUS WARN TO ICARUS NOT TO DO?


Daedalus told Icarus not to fly too high going near the sun.

Daedalus is a an excellent craftsman, he made Icarus a pair if wings made out of wax in order for him to feel the freedom of the birds in the sky. Before Icarus flew, Daedalus told Icarus not to fly too high for the heat of the sun will melt his pair of wings. They were supposed to escape from an exile sentenced to them.

As Icarus went flying through the air, he felt proud and ecstatic. He completely ignored his father and wanted to explore further and higher. As he gets near the sun, the feathers of the wings started to melt. When  he realized this, he tried to make a quick descent. Unfortunately, his distance from the sun quickly melted all of the feathers until his arms were the only once left to flap. He fell to the sea and died drowning.

3. What did Daedalus warn Icarus not do?


DAEDALUS AND ICARUS

Daedalus was the greatest inventor and son of the war goddess Athena in Greek Mythology.

Icarus was the beloved son of Daedalus.

While being imprisoned in the everlasting maze and heavily guarded labyrinth, the intelligent Daedalus planned an escape along with his son by making a makeshift wings made of metal scraps and wax.

They successfully escaped from the labyrinth, along the way he warned Icarus not to fly too high, so that was on his wings will not melt and not to fly too low so the wings will not be destroyed by the sea.

Unfortunately his son Icarus flew a little too high near the sun, causing the wax on his wings to melt and then plunge to his early death.

Answer:

Daedalus is a brilliant inventor—the Thomas Edison of his day. Unfortunately, he angers King Minos, the ruler of the island Crete, and he has to hightail it out of there. Desperate to flee the island, Daedalus uses wax to build some wings for himself and his son Icarus. Daddy Daedalus warns his son to fly at a middle height: the seawater will dampen the wings and the sun will melt them. (Not good either way.)

Icarus heeds his father's advice for a bit, but then he gets cocky. He's having so much fun flying that he forgets the warning and flies too close to the sun. Sure enough, his wings melt, and Icarus plummets into the sea and drowns. Daedalus is (of course) devastated by his son's death, but the show must go on. He flies on to Sicily, where he mourns Icarus and builds a temple in honor of the god Apollo.
