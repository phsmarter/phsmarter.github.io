Answer:

The choice of sampling method in selecting respondents depends on the research question, the population of interest, and the available resources. Common sampling methods include simple random sampling, stratified sampling, cluster sampling, and purposive sampling.

Yes, respondents are vital in a research study because they provide the data needed to answer the research questions. The selection of appropriate respondents can affect the validity and reliability of the study.

Yes, it is important to plan the procedures ahead of the actual gathering of data to ensure that the research is conducted efficiently and effectively. Planning can also help to identify potential problems and address them before they occur.

Researchers make their own research instrument after they have defined the research questions and objectives. They then select the appropriate method of data collection, design the instrument, and pilot test it to ensure that it is valid and reliable.

Before the research instrument is administered to the respondents, researchers should ensure that it is clear, easy to understand, and free of bias. They should also obtain ethical approval, select appropriate respondents, and decide on the method of administration (e.g. online, in-person, telephone).
