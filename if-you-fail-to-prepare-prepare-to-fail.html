1. after gutting, gilling and trimming, continue the stomach slit as far as the tail.

2. hold the cavity open to expose the backbone and ribs, which are embedded shallowly in the flesh and covered by membrane.

3. working from head to tail, nick the membrane with firm careful strokes, cutting the rib cage free from the backbone.

4. open the finfish as wide as possible without tearing the flesh. Gently run the knife down both sides of the backbone, cutting the bones free and being careful not to cut the skin.

5. with a pair of food scissors, cut the backbone free at the tail end. If you are keeping the head attached, cut the backbone at that end too, at the third vertebra from the head. Gently pull out the backbone.

6. remove the ribs by holding the knife parallel to the flesh, cutting just under the rib cage.

24. Do you agree with what winston churchill and benjamin franklin said? Why or why not? Minimum of 10 sentences He who fails to plan is planning to fail -Winston churchill By failing to prepare.you are preparing to fail -benjamin franklin


The statements made by Churchill and Franklin emphasize the importance of preparation and planning. They suggest that failure is inevitable when individuals do not plan or prepare adequately for the task at hand. These statements are widely accepted by many individuals across different fields, including business, education, and personal development.

Both statements are similar in essence, with Churchill's statement emphasizing the consequences of not planning, while Franklin's statement emphasizes the consequences of not preparing. However, both statements share a common message, which is that taking proactive measures is necessary to achieve success.

In today's world, where people are busy and have limited time to plan and prepare, these statements serve as a reminder that planning and preparation are essential ingredients for success. Adequate planning and preparation help individuals to anticipate challenges and obstacles, thus increasing their chances of success.

However, it is important to note that planning and preparation are not the only factors that contribute to success. Factors such as hard work, resilience, and flexibility are also crucial. While planning and preparation help individuals to anticipate challenges, unforeseen circumstances can still arise, requiring individuals to adapt and adjust their plans accordingly.

In conclusion, both statements made by Winston Churchill and Benjamin Franklin are important reminders that planning and preparation are essential ingredients for success. However, success is not guaranteed even with adequate planning and preparation, and other factors such as hard work, resilience, and flexibility are also important.
