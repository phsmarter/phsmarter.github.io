4. Write a Reflective Essay about Filipino Values, Culture and Tradition that affects modern influence of today as a Filipino Citizen.​


Answer:

As a Filipino citizen, I have been exposed to various values, culture, and traditions that are deeply ingrained in our society. These aspects of our identity have shaped the way we live our lives and interact with others. Our values, culture, and traditions have been passed down from generation to generation, and they continue to have a significant impact on our daily lives.

One of the most prominent Filipino values that I have observed is the value of close family ties. In our culture, family always comes first. We are taught to respect and care for our elders, and we are expected to help out our family members in times of need. This value is evident in the way we celebrate important occasions such as birthdays, weddings, and other significant milestones. It is common for families to come together and celebrate these events with great enthusiasm, showcasing the unity and love that exists within our families.

Another significant aspect of Filipino culture is our deep religious faith. Religion plays a significant role in our lives, and it is reflected in our daily practices and beliefs. Our religious values have instilled in us the importance of respect, humility, and compassion towards others. We believe that by being good and living a life according to our faith, we can make the world a better place.

Filipino traditions and customs also play a vital role in shaping our identity. For instance, the practice of “bayanihan,” which is a spirit of communal unity and cooperation, is still evident in many rural areas. The concept of “utang na loob,” or a sense of indebtedness to someone who has done you a favor, is another example of how Filipino customs and traditions have shaped our character.

However, as a modern Filipino citizen, I also recognize that some aspects of our culture may not be applicable or appropriate in the modern world. For example, some traditional gender roles may not be suitable in today's society, and there is a need to promote gender equality and respect for diversity.

In conclusion, Filipino values, culture, and traditions have had a significant influence on who we are as a people. As a Filipino citizen, I am proud of our rich culture and traditions, but I also recognize the need to adapt and adjust to the changing times. We need to embrace the positive aspects of our culture and traditions while also being open to change and progress. By doing so, we can continue to uphold our identity as a people and make a positive contribution to the world.

The Philippines, a country home to many food and agricultural staples. It has more than seven thousand islands attracting tourists from all over the world. These islands are rich in local delicacies and wide varieties in the different styles of street food. From these originates a unique food called Balut which is an unusual delicacy made here in the Philippines.

Balut is a chicken or duck egg which is half boiled and then eaten directly from the shell. It is commonly sold as a street food in The Philippines; you can often see it being sold in the streets across the country.

Balut is a sixteen to twenty-one day old fertilised egg of a duck or chicken. The egg is then boiled with not only the yolk present inside but also the embryo.

It is considered to be one of The Philippines most famous and iconic delicacies and is eaten by cracking open the shell and having it with a bit of salt and vinegar. Some tourists like the idea of trying something new which makes this an attractive delicacy. Others are revolted by the idea of eating the fetus of a chicken.

So, where did this exotic food come from?

Balut originated in the Philippines in the early 1900’s. The Philippine people were influenced by the Chinese presence back in 1885. Since then they have been preparing and eating Balut as an exotic dish.

With the large migration of FIlipinos has come the rise in demand for Balut across the globe. You can now easily find Balut across many major western nations by simply paying a visit to a local FIlipino supermarket.

Balut holds a particular cultural importance in Philippine history. It has been discovered that the concept of eating fertilised duck eggs began in the 17th century – due to influence from the Chinese, Thai, and Laotians.

A lot of socio-cultural factors dominate Balut market. These factors are different from the nutritional values. as it is a good source of protein and calcium. But that is not the primary reason why it is loved by FIlipinos.

So what is the appeal of Balut?

It is simply a delicious affordable snack. I find a lot of the hype comes when people encounter it for the first time. The thought of eating an embryo from inside an egg can be off putting.

Once you get past the first impression you’ll find the actual texture of the egg is quite nice. Its an affordable snack which is found on the streets all over the country that can be enjoyed while drinking with friends, or spending time with the family.

The culture and traditions of the Filipino people are deeply rooted in their history and shaped by their interactions with various foreign influences. The Philippines is a country with a rich cultural heritage, and the values that underpin Filipino culture reflect the resilience and adaptability of the Filipino people.

One of the defining characteristics of Filipino culture is the emphasis on family and community. The Filipino people place great importance on the bond between family members and the sense of belonging that comes from being part of a tight-knit community. This value is reflected in the traditional concept of “bayanihan,” which emphasizes the importance of working together for the greater good of the community.

Filipinos are also known for their strong sense of hospitality and warmth towards guests. This value is reflected in the traditional practice of “pakikipagkapwa,” which means treating others with kindness and respect. Filipinos are known to go out of their way to make visitors feel welcome in their homes and communities, and this trait is a key part of their culture.

Respect for elders and the elderly is another important value in Filipino culture. The elderly are often considered the repository of wisdom and knowledge, and are accorded high regard in Filipino society. This is reflected in the traditional practice of “pagpapakonsiderado” which means to consider the feelings and needs of others.

Religion also plays a significant role in Filipino culture. Roman Catholic Christianity is the dominant religion in the Philippines, but many indigenous communities have retained their traditional spiritual practices. The values of respect and devotion to God, family and country are imbued in the Filipinos.

In addition to these values, Filipino culture is also known for its rich artistic traditions. The Philippines has a long history of folk music, dance and visual arts. These traditions are often deeply connected to the natural environment and reflect the spiritual beliefs of the Filipino people.

In conclusion, the culture and traditions of the Filipino people are shaped by their history and interactions with various foreign influences. The values that underpin Filipino culture reflect the resilience and adaptability of the Filipino people, and are deeply ingrained in their daily lives. These values, traditions and culture make the Filipino people who they are today, and will continue to shape their future.

28. how can I understand the Filipino culture? Illustrate the unique culture of the Filipinos -essay-maximum of 20-30 sentences - due on monday (May 8,2023)​


Answer:

The culture of the Philippines can be described as a blend of various influences, including indigenous, Spanish, American, and Southeast Asian traditions, among others. This unique cultural mix is reflected in the country's customs, language, food, arts, and values.

Religion plays a significant role in Filipino culture, with the majority of the population being Roman Catholic. Religious traditions and practices are deeply ingrained in daily life, with events such as fiestas and religious processions being widely celebrated. The Philippines is also known for its festive and colorful festivals, such as the Sinulog festival in Cebu and the Ati-Atihan festival in Aklan.

The Filipino language, Tagalog, is the national language of the country, but there are over 170 languages spoken in the Philippines, reflecting the country's diverse ethnic groups. Music and dance are also an important part of Filipino culture, with traditional folk dances such as the tinikling and the pandanggo sa ilaw being popular.

Filipino cuisine is a fusion of various influences and features a range of flavors, including sweet, sour, salty, and spicy. Popular dishes include adobo, sinigang, and lechon, and food is often shared communally.

Hospitality and close family ties are highly valued in Filipino culture, with strong emphasis placed on respect for elders, close-knit family relationships, and communal harmony. "Bayanihan," a Filipino value that emphasizes communal unity and cooperation, is also deeply ingrained in the culture.

Overall, the culture of the Philippines is a vibrant and dynamic mix of influences, traditions, and values that reflects the country's rich history and diverse ethnic groups. It is a culture that values community, family, and tradition and celebrates life through food, music, and festivals.
