5. Discuss the role and limitation of quantitative technique in business management


Answer:

Quantitative techniques play a key role in the management of businesses. They are used to analyze, measure, and evaluate data to make informed decisions. Quantitative techniques can be used to identify trends, forecast future performance, and compare different scenarios.

Quantitative techniques can help managers make decisions quickly and accurately. They can also provide data-driven insights that would be difficult to obtain through intuition or experience. For example, quantitative techniques can be used to identify the most cost-effective marketing strategy or the most profitable product line.

Despite their usefulness, quantitative techniques have several limitations. First, they rely on accurate data, which may be difficult to obtain. Second, the results of quantitative techniques can be biased if the data is not representative of the population being studied. Third, quantitative techniques may not take into account other factors, such as customer preferences or organizational culture, that may have an impact on the results. Finally, quantitative techniques can be expensive and time-consuming to implement.

Overall, while quantitative techniques can be a valuable tool in business management, they should be used with caution and in conjunction with other methods.

23. explain the quantitative techniques of decision - making?​


Linear Programming - This technique basically helps in maximizing an objective under limited resources. The objective can be either optimization of a utility or minimization of a disutility. In other words, it helps in utilizing a resource or constraint to its maximum potential.

Probability decision theory - This technique lies in the premise that we can only predict the probability of an outcome. In other words, we cannot always accurately predict the exact outcome of any course of action.

Game theory - This technique basically simulates rivalries or conflicts between businesses as a game. The aim of managers under this technique is to find ways of gaining at the expense of their rivals. In order to do this, they can use 2-person, 3-person or n-person games.

Queuing theory - The aim of this theory is to minimize such waiting periods and also reduce investments on such expenses.

Simulation -  the simulation technique observes various outcomes under hypothetical or artificial settings. Managers try to understand how their decisions will work out under diverse circumstances.
