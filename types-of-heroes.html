9. What are the different types of Heroes? Describe each.​


Answer:

The classic hero: This type of hero is often depicted as a courageous and noble character who fights for justice and righteousness. They may have superhuman abilities or skills, and they often face great challenges and obstacles in their quest to save the day.The anti-hero: This type of hero is often portrayed as a complex and flawed character who does not necessarily fit the traditional mold of a hero. They may be self-serving or have questionable morals, but they ultimately do the right thing and fight for a cause they believe in.The superhero: This type of hero is often characterized by their extraordinary powers and abilities, which they use to fight crime and protect the innocent. They may have a secret identity and a unique costume, and they often face off against supervillains.The everyman hero: This type of hero is an ordinary person who is thrust into extraordinary circumstances and rises to the occasion. They may not have any special powers or abilities, but they use their wit, determination, and resourcefulness to overcome challenges and save the day.The tragic hero: This type of hero is often portrayed as a noble and heroic character who ultimately meets a tragic end due to their own flaws or the circumstances they face. Despite their tragic fate, they may inspire others through their actions and sacrifice.

The Epic Hero

As with the tragic hero, the Greeks were first to define the protagonist known as an epic hero. These are heroes of a tragedy who evoke in the audience a sense of heroism and legendary awe-inspiring lore, often in an epic poem. An epic hero is someone whose fortune is brought about by their admired characteristics. Many of the famous Greek Epics, such as The Odyssey and the Illiad, contain these larger than life heroes and deeds:

The Superhero

Superheroes can start out as classical heroes or even everyday heroes and be given a power that makes them 'superhuman'. They can also be born with a ‘superhuman’ power.

Famous examples of the superhero archetypes would be Superman, Thor, or Wolverine.

The Tragic Hero

It was the great philosopher Aristotle who first defined the ill-fated protagonist as a tragic hero, or flawed hero. Aristotle suggested that a hero of a tragedy must evoke a sense of pity or fear from the audience. Also, the tragic hero has to be someone whose misfortune is brought about not by vice or depravity, but by some error or fate. To this day, literature is inundated with the use of this type of protagonist.

The Everyday Hero

In literature, the term everyday has come to mean an ordinary individual that the audience or reader easily identifies with. Also, the everyday hero (sometimes known as the "everyman hero") has no outstanding abilities or attributes. An everyday hero is thrown into extraordinary circumstances where they must act with heroic qualities. Moreover, they have sound moral judgment and show selflessness in the face of adversity.

Many protagonists in realistic fiction are considered to be this hero type.

The Classical Hero

Classical heroes, sometimes known as romantic heroes, are everyday people that have a great talent! They often possess an attribute or quality that distinguishes them from ordinary people, making them a hero. It's important to remember that classical hero is equal in their world, but possess a gift that others do not have.

Examples of classical heroes would be Harry Potter, Ponyboy, or Victor Frankenstein.

11. which type of text does filipino heroes used to motivate revolutionists in their fight for fredom​


Answer:

Filipino heroes used various types of text to motivate revolutionists in their fight for freedom. Some of the most common types of text used during the Philippine Revolution were speeches, essays, letters, and poems. These texts were often written or delivered by national heroes such as Jose Rizal, Andres Bonifacio, Emilio Aguinaldo, and Apolinario Mabini.

For example, Jose Rizal's novels "Noli Me Tangere" and "El Filibusterismo" exposed the injustices and corruption of the Spanish colonial government, inspiring Filipinos to fight for their rights and freedom. Andres Bonifacio's "Katipunan" manifesto encouraged Filipinos to unite and fight against their oppressors. Apolinario Mabini's "El Verdadero Decalogo" laid out a code of ethics for the Filipino revolutionaries, emphasizing the importance of honesty and integrity in their struggle for independence.

Overall, these texts served as powerful tools for inspiring and motivating Filipinos to fight for their freedom and sovereignty.
