Write short biography essay that compare the students early childhood with rizals own?

 

José Protasio Rizal Mercado y Alonso Realonda He was born on June 19, 1861, some 35 years before that fateful day. The seventh of the eleven children born to a comparatively well-to-do family in the Dominican lands of Calamba, Laguna, Jose Rizal lived and died in the Philippines during the Spanish colonial era.  

Jose's father, Francisco Mercado Rizal, was a prosperous farmer from Binan, Laguna, while his mother, Teodora Alonzo y Quintos, was an accomplished and highly educated woman from Sta. Cruz, Manila.

In his early childhood, Jose had perfected the alphabet, learnt to write and read books like the Spanish translation of the Vulgate Bible. At a young age, he had always shown a tendency towards the arts. His family was fascinated by his pencil paintings, sketches, and clay moldings. Later in his youth, he displayed a special talent in painting and drawing, wrote a Tagalog play, which was presented at the Calamba Festival, and penned a short Spanish play, which was presented at the school.

He went to Spain on 3 May 1882 and enrolled at the Universidad Central de Madrid. He obtained a degree in medicine in June 1884 at the age of 23. A year back, he finished his course of Philosophy and Letters with the rank of "excellent." Wanting to treat his mother's advanced blindness, Rizal went to Paris, Heidelberg, and Berlin to gain more experience and expertise in ophthalmology. He finished his eye specialization in Heidelberg.  

Well-traveled, he is said to have mastered 22 languages. He wrote remarkable poetry, contributed nationalist essays to magazines, maintained his diary religiously, and corresponded to his friends and relatives. In March 1887, in Berlin, he published his first controversial book, Noli Me Tangere, which exposed the injustice and greed of the Spanish clergy and officials in the Philippines. To prove that the Filipinos had an outstanding culture long before the Spanish occupation, Morga's Successos De Las Islas Filipinas was annotated and reprinted in Paris. On September 18, 1891, Rizal's more militant book, El Filibusterismo, was published in Ghent.

In 1896, Rizal was granted permission by the governor general to become a volunteer military physician for the war in Cuba, which at the time still raged from yellow fever. But the 'Katipunan' launched the Philippine Revolution on August 26, 1896. The influential people who were provoked by Rizal's hostility used the opportunity to include him in the revolt. He was guilty of revolt and sentenced to death by firing squad at Bagumbayan Field after a trial in the Kangaroo court (now Luneta).

In 1890, Rizal, 29, left Paris for Brussels as he was preparing for the publication of his annotations of Antonio de Morga's "Sucesos de las Islas Filipinas." There, he lived in the boarding house of the two Jacoby sisters, Catherina and Suzanna who had a niece also named Suzanna ("Thil") HistorianGregorio F. Zaide states that Rizal had "his romance with Suzanne Jacoby, 45, the petite niece of his landladies." Belgian Pros Slachmuylders, however, believed that Rizal had a romance with the niece, Suzanna Thil, in 1890. Belgian Jean Paul Verstraeten proved this. Jean Paul traced in the archives of Brussels that in the same house as Suzanne Jacoby, a little niece was living, Suzanne Till. Letters from Suzanne to Rizal were signed by "Petite Suzanne".

Rizal's Brussels stay was short-lived, as he moved to Madrid, leaving the young Suzanna a box of chocolates. Suzanna replied in French: "After your departure, I did not take the chocolate. The box is still intact as on the day of your parting. Don’t delay too long writing us because I wear out the soles of my for running to the mailbox to see if there is a letter from you. There will never be any home in which you are so loved as in that in Brussels, so, you little bad boy, hurry up and come back…" (Oct. 1, 1890 letter). The house where Rizal was staying, in Rue Philippe de Champagne 38 doesn't exist anymore following some writers. (the house numbers jump from 36 to 42). Jean Paul Verstraeten proved during his research that this house still exists, on August 10, 1900, the number of the house was changed into 42. Jean Paul traced the owner of this house and convinced her to put a historical marker. Slachmuylders’ group in 2007 unveiled this historical marker commemorating Rizal's stay in Brussels in 1890.

The content of Rizal's writings changed considerably in his two most famous novels, Noli Me Tángere, published in Berlin in 1887, and El Filibusterismo, published in Ghent in 1891 with funds borrowed largely from Rizal's friends. These writings angered both the Spanish colonial elite and many educated Filipinos due to their insulting symbolism. They are critical of Spanish friars and the power of the Church. Rizal's friend Ferdinand Blumentritt, an Austria-Hungary born professor and historian wrote that the novel's characters were drawn from real life and that every episode can be repeated on any day in the Philippines

Short Paragraph

The Rizal Course is a crucial subject in the Philippine education system, which teaches students about the life, works, and ideals of the national hero, Dr. Jose Rizal. It promotes critical thinking, social responsibility, and ethical leadership, and provides a historical context for understanding current issues in the Philippines. Personally, the Rizal Course has helped me appreciate the rich cultural heritage and diversity of the Philippines, and has instilled in me a sense of pride and love for my country. It has also inspired me to be more involved in community service and to advocate for social justice and equality. Overall, the Rizal Course is an essential component of our national identity and heritage that every Filipino should value and preserve.

Long Paragraph

The Rizal Course is a mandatory subject in the Philippine education system, which aims to educate students about the life, works, and ideals of the national hero, Dr. Jose Rizal. As a Filipino, I believe that the Rizal Course is highly relevant and essential in shaping our national identity and promoting patriotism.

Through the Rizal Course, students learn about the struggles and sacrifices of our forefathers in fighting for our country's independence and sovereignty. It also highlights the importance of critical thinking, social responsibility, and ethical leadership, which are crucial values that every Filipino should embody. Furthermore, the Rizal Course provides a historical context for understanding the current political, social, and economic issues in the Philippines, and encourages students to be active and engaged citizens who can contribute to nation-building.

Personally, the Rizal Course has helped me appreciate and understand the rich cultural heritage and diversity of the Philippines. It has also inspired me to be more involved in community service and to advocate for social justice and equality. Moreover, the Rizal Course has instilled in me a sense of pride and love for my country, and has motivated me to work towards a better future for the Philippines and its people. In conclusion, the Rizal Course is not just a subject, but a vital component of our national identity and heritage that should be preserved and valued by every Filipino.

Answer:

The Rizal Course is one of the mandatory subjects in the Philippine education system. It is a course that aims to teach students about the life and works of Dr. Jose Rizal, the national hero of the Philippines. Some students may find this course irrelevant and just another subject to pass, but for me, the Rizal Course is essential in understanding our country's history and identity.

The Rizal Course is relevant because it teaches us about our past, and understanding our past is crucial in shaping our future. Dr. Jose Rizal played a significant role in the Philippine Revolution, which led to the country's independence. Learning about his life and works is not only about memorizing dates and events but also about understanding the values he stood for. Rizal was a patriot, a writer, and a man of principles who fought for his country's freedom. His writings, such as Noli Me Tangere and El Filibusterismo, were instrumental in awakening the Filipino people's nationalistic spirit. Studying the Rizal Course allows us to reflect on our identity as Filipinos and the values we should uphold as a nation.
