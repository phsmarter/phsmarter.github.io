2. how to creat reflection with essay #:ALS​


Answer:

Explanation:

1.Choose a topic: Choose a topic that you have personal experience with and that you feel passionate about. This could be a personal experience, a social issue, a book or movie that has impacted you, or anything that you have strong feelings or opinions about.

2.Write about the experience: In the first part of your essay, describe the experience or topic in detail. Use descriptive language to paint a vivid picture of what happened or what the topic is about. This should be the objective part of your essay, where you simply state the facts and details.

3.Reflect on the experience: In the second part of your essay, reflect on how the experience or topic has impacted you. This is where you share your personal thoughts, feelings, and opinions about the experience. Consider questions like: What did you learn from the experience? How has it changed your perspective on life? What would you do differently if you could go back in time? What advice would you give to others who may face a similar experience?

4.Be honest and authentic: When writing a reflection essay, it is important to be honest and authentic about your thoughts and feelings. Share your personal experiences and opinions without worrying about being judged or criticized. Remember, the purpose of a reflection essay is to reflect on your personal growth and development, not to impress others.

5.Use examples: Use specific examples from your experience to illustrate your thoughts and feelings. This will help the reader understand your perspective and relate to your experience.

6.Conclude with a summary: In the final part of your essay, summarize your thoughts and feelings about the experience. This is your chance to reiterate the main points of your essay and leave the reader with a lasting impression.

Answer: Chinese music, the art form of organized vocal and instrumental sounds that developed in China. It is one of the oldest and most highly developed of all known musical systems.

Chinese music history must be approached with a certain sense of awe. Indeed, any survey evokes the music of a varied, still-active civilization whose archaeological resources go back to 3000 BCE and whose own extensive written documents refer to countless forms of music not only in connection with folk festivals and religious events but also in the courts of hundreds of emperors and princes in dozens of provinces, dynasties, and periods. For all the richness of detail in Chinese sources, however, it is only for the last segment of Chinese music history—from the Song dynasty (960–1279 CE) to the present—that there is information about the actual music itself. Yet the historical, cultural, instrumental, and theoretical materials of earlier times are equally informative and fascinating. This mass of information can be organized into four large chronological units: (1) the formative period, from 3000 BCE through the 4th century CE, (2) the international period, from the 4th through the 9th century, (3) the national period, from the 9th through the 19th century, and (4) the “world music” period of the 20th and early 21st centuries.

10. my learnings in als journey? Pa help po para sa essay​


Answer:

Amyotrophic Lateral Sclerosis (ALS), also known as Lou Gehrig's disease, is a progressive neurodegenerative disease that affects nerve cells in the brain and spinal cord. As the disease progresses, people with ALS may experience a range of physical and cognitive changes that can impact their ability to learn and retain new information. Despite these challenges, learning can still be an important part of a person's journey with ALS.

One of the key ways that learning can be integrated into the ALS journey is by keeping the mind active. Engaging in mentally stimulating activities such as reading, writing, puzzles, and games can help keep the mind sharp and improve cognitive function. This is particularly important as ALS can cause cognitive changes such as difficulty with language, memory, and problem-solving.

Another way that learning can be incorporated into the ALS journey is by attending support groups. Joining a support group for people with ALS can provide opportunities to learn from others who are going through similar experiences, share knowledge and insights, and receive emotional support. Support groups can also provide a sense of community and belonging, which can be particularly valuable for people with ALS who may feel isolated or disconnected from others.

Attending regular medical appointments is also an important part of the ALS journey, as it provides an opportunity to learn about the progression of the disease and new treatments and therapies that may help manage symptoms. Participating in research studies can also provide access to new treatments and therapies that may improve quality of life and slow the progression of the disease.

Assistive technology devices can also play a key role in enabling people with ALS to continue learning and engaging with the world around them. Speech-generating devices, eye-tracking systems, and mobility aids are just a few examples of the many assistive technology devices that can help people with ALS continue to communicate, learn, and participate in daily activities.

In conclusion, learning can be an important part of a person's journey with ALS. Despite the challenges that come with this disease, there are many ways to continue learning and growing, from keeping the mind active to participating in research studies and using assistive technology devices. By staying engaged in learning and seeking out resources and support, people with ALS can continue to thrive and live a fulfilling life.

24. essay tungkol sa pagtatrabaho habang nag aaral ito po ay isang ina na Als mo sya ​


Answer:

Ang pagtatrabaho habang nag-aaral ay isa sa mga pinakamalaking hamon na kinahaharap ng maraming estudyante. Ngunit, para sa mga katulad kong ina na ALS learner, ito ay isang hindi lamang hamon kundi isang pangangailangan. Sa panahon ngayon, ang pag-aaral ay hindi lamang tungkol sa pagtuturo sa mga bata kundi pati na rin sa mga may-edad na kagaya ko na nais magkaroon ng pangalawang pagkakataon upang makapagtapos ng pag-aaral.

Bilang isang ina at ALS learner, hindi madali ang mag-aaral at mag-trabaho nang sabay-sabay. Minsan, nahirapan na akong i-manage ang oras ko sa pagitan ng aking trabaho at pag-aaral. Ngunit, sa kabila ng lahat ng mga pagsubok na ito, nanatili akong matatag at determinado na makamit ang aking pangarap.

Bukod sa kinakailangang mag-alaga ng aking pamilya, kailangan ko rin magtrabaho upang mabayaran ang mga gastusin sa araw-araw at sa aking pag-aaral. Hindi magiging madali ang lahat ng ito kung walang disiplina at kakayahan sa pagpaplano ng oras. Kailangan kong maglaan ng sapat na oras para sa pag-aaral, trabaho, at pamilya. Kulang sa oras ang isang linggo lamang kaya hindi ko dapat sayangin ang anumang oras na mayroon ako.

Ang pagtitiyaga, pagpaplano ng oras, at pagtitiwala sa sarili ay ilan lamang sa mga mahahalagang kailangan upang matamo ang tagumpay sa pagtatrabaho habang nag-aaral. Sa aking karanasan, nakita ko ang mga positibong aspeto ng pagsasabay ng trabaho at pag-aaral. Hindi lamang ako nakakapagtapos ng pag-aaral, ngunit nakakatulong din ako sa aking pamilya at nakapag-ambag sa mga pangangailangan ng aking komunidad.

Ang pagtatrabaho habang nag-aaral ay hindi lamang tungkol sa pangangailangan kundi pati na rin sa pagpapakita ng determinasyon at pagiging handa sa mga pagsubok. Hindi man ito madaling gawin, pero ang tagumpay ay hindi lamang para sa iilan kundi para sa lahat ng nagnanais na makamit ang kanilang mga pangarap.

Amyotrophic lateral sclerosis (ALS), also known as Lou Gehrig's disease, is a degenerative neurological disorder that affects the nerve cells responsible for controlling voluntary muscle movements. As the disease progresses, individuals with ALS may experience a range of physical and emotional challenges that can significantly impact their daily lives.

For individuals with ALS, the course of the disease can vary widely depending on the individual and the severity of their symptoms. Some people may experience a slow progression of symptoms over several years, while others may experience a more rapid decline.

Early symptoms of ALS may include muscle weakness, twitching, and cramping, particularly in the arms and legs. As the disease progresses, individuals with ALS may experience difficulty with speech and swallowing, as well as respiratory problems.

Living with ALS can be challenging, and many individuals with the disease require significant support from family members, caregivers, and healthcare professionals. Treatment for ALS typically focuses on managing symptoms, improving quality of life, and providing emotional support.

Despite the challenges of living with ALS, many individuals with the disease continue to find joy and meaning in their lives. Support from loved ones and access to resources such as assistive technology and physical therapy can help individuals with ALS maintain their independence and enjoy a good quality of life for as long as possible.
