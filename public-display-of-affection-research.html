1. what is public display of affection?


What is public display of affection

Public displays of affection (P.D.A.) are expressions of physical contact in the presence of others.  The appropriate show of affection differs with respect to culture and background.  

Many organisations have laws that limit or forbid public displays of affection. Displays of affection in a public  location, such as the highway, are more likely to be objected to than similar activities in a private place, with  only people with similar cultural background present.  

Physical affection has been described as "any touch that is intended to arouse feelings of love in the giver andor the recipient."

Everyone has a different interpretation of what is appropriate when it comes to public displays of affection.  Many people will never think of going beyond holding their hands or connecting their arms  outside the room, while others feel relaxed standing all over each other wherever they are.  

Even so, it's the reaction of the viewers that really matters. When you're caught up in the excitement of all this, witnesses to your public displays of affection may feel anything from fear and disgust to delight.

It's a matter of meaning. Know where you are, and who's around you. A simple rule of thumb is that  you will be able to see your grandparents. Hand-holding can be perfect, but you're definitely  not going to engage in heavy petting.  

Take the settings into account. Are you taking a quiet stroll along the shores of a river with almost no one  around? Or are you crowded into the corner of a jammed rush hour tube? Just one of these  is sufficient for the PDA.

The different types of public displays of affection

So, what's the significance of PDA in a relationship? And did you know that your PDA  of choice could have a secret meaning? We've studied some of the most famous public displays  of affection to reveal what they mean about your relationship:

Hand-holding - Keep your hands in one of the easiest ways to express your love in public. This may have positive  and bad connotations, however. Passive, disconnected hold suggests friendly closeness with little  emotion, while the interlocking fingers symbolize true intimacy.  Arm-linking - Linking arms is a go-to step for a variety of existing couples. It's relaxed and socially appropriate – even families and  friends will take this pose without raising their eyebrows. It's a casual touch, but it's also a display of solidarity, showing your commitment as a couple.  Hands in pockets  - Walking with your hand in your partner's pocket may make walking a little awkward, but it's a friendly gesture  that suggests an easy-going romantic partnership. It's also physically intimate, and  is often popular with new couples. Subtle touching  - Couples share subtle touches to suggest a spectrum of context, from flirtation to comfort.  It's a more restricted showing of affection that shows you're relaxed with each other, so you don't have anything to prove.

15. Is PDA (Public display of affection) an ethical or a moral issue?


Answer:

PDA (Public display of affection) is any act of physical intimacy in one’s perspective. This may include holding hands, hugging, or kissing. PDA is neither an ethical nor a moral issue.

Explanation:

A suitable showing of affection may vary with reference to culture and context. Sometimes, it also depends on an individual’s point of view. In most of the European countries like Australia and Canada, showing of affection in public is just a common gesture or act. On the other part of the world, mostly Asian countries like India and China, PDA is not socially acceptable.  

Moral and ethics are interchangeable and closely related, however, there's still differences. Morals ask guiding principles, and ethics ask specific rules and actions, or behaviors. Additionally, “ethics” leans towards decisions based upon a private character, whereas “morals” emphasizes the widely-shared communal or societal norms about right and wrong.

19. Research an issue that affects earth's ecosystem. Write and record a public service announcement to state youd position on the issue​.


Answer:

1) Pollution

Pollution of the air, water and soil caused by toxins such as plastics, heavy metals and nitrates, caused by factors such as toxins and gases released by factories, combustion of fossil fuels, acid rain, oil spill and industrial waste.

2) Global warming

The emission of greenhouse gases due to human activity causes global warming, which in turn causes an increase in temperature that then leads to rising sea levels, melting of polar ice caps, flash floods and desertification.

3) Overpopulation

We are facing a shortage of resources such as food, water and fuel to sustain the rising global population, particularly in developing countries. Intensive agriculture attempting to lessen the problem actually leads to more damage through the use of chemical fertilizers, pesticides and insecticides.

4) Waste disposal

An excessive amount of waste is produced and dumped in the oceans. Nuclear waste is particularly dangerous, as well as plastics and electronic waste.

5) Ocean acidification

The increase in the production of carbon dioxide by humans causes the oceans’ acidity to rise, which has a negative impact on marine life.

6) Loss of biodiversity

Species and habitats are becoming extinct due to human activity. This causes an imbalance in natural processes like pollination and poses a threat to ecosystems – coral reef destruction is particularly affected.

7) Deforestation

Loss of trees in order to make space for residential, industrial or commercial projects means that less oxygen is produced, and temperature and rainfall are affected.

8) Ozone layer depletion

Pollution caused by chlorofluorocarbons (CFCs) in the air creates a hole in the ozone layer, which protects the earth from harmful UV radiation.

9) Acid rain

Pollutants in the atmosphere such as sulfur dioxide and nitrogen oxides cause acid rain, which has negative consequences for humans, wildlife and aquatic species.

10) Public health issues

Lack of clean water is one of the leading environmental problems currently. Pollutants in the air also cause issues such as respiratory disease and cardiovascular disease.
