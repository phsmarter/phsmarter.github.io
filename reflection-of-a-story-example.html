Some of the themes in the story may include social inequality, the effects of poverty on individuals and families, and the resilience and strength of the human spirit in difficult circumstances.

Provide examples of how Andersen uses imagery.

For example, in "The Little Match Girl," Andersen uses vivid descriptions of the cold and darkness to emphasize the girl's suffering and desperation. He also uses the image of a shooting star to symbolize the girl's hope and eventual release from her suffering.

Does Andersen provide symbolism in the story?

Yes, Andersen uses the image of a shooting star to symbolize the girl's hope and eventual release from her suffering. Additionally, he also employs the use of other symbols such as the rose bush and the nightingale to convey deeper meanings in the story.
