2. Literary analysis of the poem ""Ode to Garlic"" by Genevieve Mae Aquino.


Literary analysis of the poem "Ode to Garlic" by Genevieve Mae Aquino.

Genevieve Mae Aquino's poem "Ode to Garlic" honors the adaptability of garlic. It is poetic but it gives a lighthearted ode to simple notes that are frequently disregarded.

The first line of the poem describes garlic as "an onion's cousin," highlighting the close link between the two ingredients. The speaker then goes on to discuss the numerous benefits for garlic, including its usage as a spice and as medicine, and lauds its capacity to "ward off the plague." Some of the benefits of garlic matter to the people who seek a healthy living style.

The various features of garlic are described by Genevieve Aquino using vivid language throughout the poem. As an illustration, she calls the scent of garlic "acidic," "bold," and "unapologetic," reflecting its powerful flavor its nature will come out. She also makes garlic, calling it a "warrior" and a "keeper," which improves the poem's fun and cheerful mood.

This kind of idea is one of its outstanding elements. Throughout the poem, the phrase "I sing to thee" is used over and over, which highlights the speaker's affection for garlic while adding to the poem's overall atmosphere.

The use of the imagery is another interesting aspect. Garlic is compared by the speaker to a "hero" and a "warrior," which brings up ideas of courage and bravery.

Garlic's importance as a natural element that has been used for ages is further implied by the term of it a "herald of the earth" in the text.
