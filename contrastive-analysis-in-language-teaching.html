15. compare and contrast academic language vs literary language​


❑ COMPARE & CONTRAST

Academic language and literary language are both used for written communication, but they serve different purposes and have different characteristics.

Academic language is used in academic writing, such as research papers, essays, and reports. It is characterized by its formal, objective tone and its use of specialized vocabulary and technical terms. Its purpose is to communicate complex ideas and information in a clear and concise way.Literary language, on the other hand, is used in literature, such as novels, poems, and plays. It is characterized by its imaginative and expressive use of language and its ability to evoke emotions and create vivid mental images. Its purpose is to entertain and engage the reader, and to convey the author's ideas and themes.

In summary, academic language is formal, objective, and focused on conveying complex ideas, while literary language is imaginative, expressive, and focused on entertaining and engaging the reader.
