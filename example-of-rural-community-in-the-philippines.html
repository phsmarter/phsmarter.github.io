25. What is RURAL HEALTH UNIT in the philippines?


A Rural Health Unit in the Philippine is usually the first line of medical facility available for many Filipinos in the provinces. In Manila and other highly-urbanized cities, the usual place where people seek medical help is thru the clinics or private hospitals. While clinics are also available in provinces, the cost usually deters patients from seeking help from them. The consultation fee is free and basic medicine is also free in rural health units. Ideally, every town should have a rural health unit.  

What happens inside a rural health unit?

Most rural health units have a doctor available for consultation. In some units, there are basic laboratories and x-ray setup but this is not the case in many other health units. In many instances, laboratory requests will be referred to a private diagnostic laboratory. Consultation with the doctor, however is free of charge. One disadvantage to the rural health unit is that there is usually just one doctor available and this is typically a general practitioner. This is good for basic medical problems but could pose problems for when specialized needs arise (such as when there are eye injuries, cancer patients, trauma, etc) that require the assistance of a specialist.  

What are the common reasons for visiting the rural health unit?

The most common is for people seeking care for an illness or an injury, medical check up, neonatal care, vaccination and maternal check up. As such rural health units also have nurses and midwives who are the primary faces of maternal care in the town or barangay. They are also the front-liners when it comes to advocating vaccines for the children and the general population.  

Are people satisfied with the rural health units?

Some surveys indicate that majority of Filipinos prefer receiving treatment from RHUs because of affordability while those seeking excellent services opt for private clinics or hospitals. Patients also have to endure long lines when seeking treatment from an RHU.

26. Explain and give example each about Rural Community1. Vernacular is usually spoken2. Wider area ​​


Answer:

1.Vernacular is usually spoken: In rural communities, people usually speak the local vernacular language or dialect that has been passed down through generations. This language may not be widely spoken outside of the community and may differ from the official language of the country or region. The use of vernacular language can often help to preserve local culture and traditions.

Example: In a rural village in India, people may speak a dialect of Hindi that is unique to their region. This dialect may have evolved over time and incorporates words and phrases from local languages and cultures. It is used in daily conversations, as well as in traditional songs, stories, and rituals.

2.Wider area: Rural communities are typically located in sparsely populated areas outside of urban centers. Unlike cities, where neighborhoods are often tightly clustered, rural communities are spread out over a wider area. This can make it more challenging to access services and resources, but can also lead to a closer connection to nature and a stronger sense of community.

Example: In a rural area of the United States, a small town may be surrounded by farmland, forests, and mountains. The nearest city could be several hours away by car, and the town itself may be spread out over several miles. Despite the distance between homes and businesses, residents may still feel a strong sense of community and work together to support local schools, churches, and social events.
