27. Poverty Cause: Effect:


Poverty

Cause:

Poverty can have various causes, including lack of education, inadequate access to resources such as food and water, economic instability, discrimination and inequality, poor governance, conflict and war, and natural disasters.

Effect:

Poverty can have numerous negative effects on individuals, families, and communities, including poor health and nutrition, limited access to education and job opportunities, increased crime and violence, reduced social mobility, and a lower standard of living. It can also lead to social exclusion and marginalization, which can further perpetuate the cycle of poverty. Additionally, poverty can have broader societal effects such as economic stagnation and political instability.
