Answer:

Adjectives modify or describe a noun. These words ascribe an attribute to the noun being modified.

The sleepy bear hibernated all winter.

It's a long drive, but it's worth the trip.

Should I buy the blue jeans or purple sweater?

Adverbs

Adverbs modify or describe a verb, adjective, or another adverb or word group. This modification provides information relating to time, place, manner, cause, degree, or circumstance.

Joe grumpily got out of bed.

Sara ran very quickly to school.

That's quite expensive, don't you think?

Conjunctions

Conjunctions are used to connect words, phrases, and clauses. They coordinate the words together so they make sense as a cohesive thought, sentence, or paragraph.

You'll need to study all night if you want to pass tomorrow's test.

Go to the store and buy some milk.

Kristopher doesn't have enough experience. Therefore, we will not hire him.

Interjections

Interjections are words used as exclamations to show feelings. They're usually abrupt, interrupting the speech for emotional effect.

Bah, who cares what they think anyway?

If Cody asked me out on a date, gosh, that'd make my day.

I spilled the coffee everywhere. Oops!

Nouns

Nouns name a person, place, thing idea, or quality. They can act as the subject or the object of a sentence.

Send in the clowns.

Brazil is beautiful this time of year.

His love of music really shows.

Prepositions

Prepositions are used to indicate relationships, often spatial, between nouns and pronouns. They indicate how one noun, noun phrase, or pronoun relates to another.

Between you and me, I wouldn't trust Andy.

The coffee shop is across the street.

Put the carrots in the refrigerator, please.

Pronouns

Pronouns take the place of nouns to simplify speech and writing. They can otherwise function in the same way as nouns, serving as subjects and objects in sentences.

She is the smartest kid in class.

George took the book from him.

Who is coming to the party tonight?

Verbs

Verbs express action or a state of being. It is because of the former that verbs are often called "action verbs," but non-action verbs can be used to represent a state of being, need, sense, or preference.

Let's walk to the park.

Janine lives in Minneapolis.

The air conditioning unit desperately needs repair.

Articles

In English, articles are used in sentences as an adjective to modify a noun. The two types of articles are called definite and indefinite. The three indefinite articles include ‘a’, ‘an’, and ‘some’. ‘The’ is the only definite article.

The tall man took the car to the shop.

I gave my brother an umbrella.

He found a book to read.

Do you want some milk?

5. Make a speech about disagreeing the "No Homework Policy".introduction-arguments-conclusion-​


Introduction:

Good day everyone,

As a student, I know that we all have different opinions when it comes to the "No Homework Policy" that some schools have adopted. While some students may welcome this policy with open arms, I personally disagree with it. Today, I would like to share with you my reasons for disagreeing with this policy.

Arguments:

Firstly, homework is an essential part of the learning process. It helps students to reinforce what they have learned in class and to develop their skills and knowledge further. Without homework, students may struggle to retain information and may not have enough practice to master new concepts. In the long run, this could affect their academic performance and hinder their progress.

Secondly, homework teaches students important life skills such as time management, responsibility, and discipline. These skills are crucial for success in both academic and professional settings. By completing homework assignments, students learn to manage their time effectively, prioritize tasks, and meet deadlines. These skills are invaluable in the real world and can help students to achieve their goals.

Finally, homework provides an opportunity for parents to be involved in their child's education. Parents can help their children with homework, providing them with support and guidance. This can strengthen the parent-child relationship and create a more positive learning environment.

Conclusion:

In conclusion, while the "No Homework Policy" may seem appealing at first glance, I believe that it is not in the best interest of students. Homework is an essential part of the learning process, teaching students important skills and reinforcing their knowledge. I urge schools to reconsider this policy and to continue to assign homework to their students. Thank you for listening.

Introduction:

Good day everyone! Today, I would like to express my opinion on the "No Homework Policy" that has been circulating among schools. While many are in favor of this policy, I strongly disagree with it.

Arguments:

Firstly, homework is an essential part of the learning process. It reinforces the lessons taught in class, allows students to practice what they've learned, and helps them prepare for exams. Without homework, students may not fully comprehend the topics discussed in class and may struggle to apply what they've learned in real-life situations.

Secondly, homework teaches important life skills such as time management, responsibility, and discipline. By completing assignments on time, students learn how to manage their time effectively, prioritize tasks, and meet deadlines. These skills are valuable in their academic and future professional lives.

Lastly, homework provides an opportunity for parents to be involved in their child's education. They can help their children with their assignments, monitor their progress, and communicate with teachers if necessary. This strengthens the parent-child-teacher relationship and promotes a supportive learning environment.

Conclusion:

In conclusion, while the "No Homework Policy" may seem attractive to some, it does not promote effective learning and can hinder a student's progress. Homework plays a vital role in the learning process, helps students develop important life skills, and promotes a positive parent-child-teacher relationship. Thank you.
