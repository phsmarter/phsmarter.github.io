25. cite an example for each type of pricing strategy. 1. Psychological Pricing 2. Value Pricing Strategy 3. Market Skimming Strategy 4. Penetration Pricing 5. Tender Pricing 6. Cost Plus Pricing 7. Loss Leader strategy 8. Price Discrimination 9. Destroyer Pricing 10. Absorption Cost Pricing


1. Psychological Pricing

Example: Setting the price of a product at $9.99 instead of $10.00 to make it appear less expensive.

2. Value Pricing Strategy

Example: Offering a bundle of products or services at a discounted price compared to buying them individually.

3. Market Skimming Strategy

Example: Introducing a new and innovative product at a high price point to target early adopters and generate maximum profit before lowering the price to target a broader market.

4. Penetration Pricing

Example: Setting a low initial price to penetrate the market quickly and gain market share, with the intention of raising the price later once the product is established.

5. Tender Pricing

Example: Offering a competitive and negotiable price to win a contract in a bidding process.

6. Cost Plus Pricing

Example: Adding a markup to the cost of producing a product or service to determine the final price.

7. Loss Leader strategy

Example: Offering a product at a price lower than its cost to attract customers and drive sales of other products with higher profit margins.

8. Price Discrimination

Example: Charging different prices for the same product or service to different customers, based on factors such as their willingness to pay or location.

9. Destroyer Pricing

Example: Setting a low price to eliminate competition by forcing competitors out of the market.

10. Absorption Cost Pricing

Example: Setting the price of a product by adding all the direct costs (materials, labor, and overhead) and indirect costs (such as administrative expenses) together and adding a markup to arrive at the final price.
