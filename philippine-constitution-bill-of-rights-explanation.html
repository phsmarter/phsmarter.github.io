11. What is your overall reflection on the 1987 constitution of the republic of the Philippines article III- bill of rights ?​


The Bill of Rights is a vital component of the Philippine Constitution, which guarantees the protection and promotion of the fundamental rights and freedoms of the Filipino people. It is a clear manifestation of the country's democratic principles and its commitment to human rights.

Article III of the 1987 Constitution includes a comprehensive list of rights and freedoms, including the right to life, liberty, and property, the right to due process of law, freedom of speech, expression, and religion, and the right to a fair trial, among others. These provisions ensure that every Filipino citizen is protected from any form of abuse or violation of their rights by the government or any individual or entity.

Moreover, the Bill of Rights promotes social justice and protects the marginalized sectors of society. It recognizes the rights of indigenous peoples, women, children, and persons with disabilities, among others. It also prohibits discrimination based on age, sex, race, or social status.

Overall, the 1987 Constitution's Article III - Bill of Rights is a significant accomplishment in the country's history. It upholds the Filipino people's dignity and promotes a just and humane society. However, there are still challenges in implementing these rights fully, particularly in ensuring that all Filipinos can access and exercise their rights equally.

19. in 1987 Philippine constitutions law how a bill become a law? explain ​


Answer:

Bills are laws in the making. They pass into law when they are approved by both houses and the President of the Philippines. A bill may be vetoed by the President, but the House of Representatives may overturn a presidential veto by garnering a 2/3rds vote.

23. Similarities of the four.a.) United Nations Declaration of Human Rightsb.) Universal Declaration of Human Responsibilitiesc.) Pacem in Terris (Individual Rights)d.) Article Ill Bill of Rights (Philippine Constitution)​


Similarities of the four:

The United Nations Declaration of Human Rights, Universal Declaration of Human Responsibilities, Pacem in Terris (Individual Rights), and Article Ill Bill of Rights (Philippine Constitution) are all documents that pertain to human rights. These documents outline the rights that all people are entitled to, such as the right to life, liberty, and security of person, the right to education, and the right to freedom from discrimination. These documents also outline the responsibilities of individuals and governments to uphold and protect these rights. Overall, the main similarity between these documents is their focus on the fundamental rights and responsibilities of all individuals.
