7. The narrator in the story Entitled The Diamond Necklace mentioned in this commentary: "How small a thing is needed to make or ruin us! How true can this line be in real life situations? Cite a concrete example.​


Answer:

It is certainly true that small things can have a big impact on our lives. One example of this is the concept of the "butterfly effect," which refers to the idea that a small change in one part of a system can lead to significant changes in other parts of the system. In other words, a small event or decision can have far-reaching consequences.

For example, consider the story of the "Diamond Necklace." In this story, a small mistake or misjudgment, such as losing a valuable piece of jewelry, can have serious consequences, such as financial ruin or social disgrace. In real life, a small event or decision can also have significant consequences. For example, failing to pay a small bill on time could lead to late fees and damaged credit, which could have long-term consequences on one's financial stability and opportunities.

Overall, it is important to be mindful of the potential impact of small things and to carefully consider the consequences of our actions and decisions.
