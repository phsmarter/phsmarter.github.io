19. process where rocks, are folded, faulted, sheared or compressed by the earth stresses?​


DEFORMATION

Earth stresses can fold, fault, shear, or compress rocks, causing them to change shape. Brittle deformation is a type of permanent strain in which the material (rock) breaks as a result of stress.

Rocks fold when they are pushed together and the layers of rock behave like putty from an observer's point of view. This could be because there are many small cracks in the rock or because the rock is soft. Gravity keeps the pile from getting too tall, so it leans to one side (take a sheet of paper and push the opposite sides together, it's like that).

In compression, faulting happens when a force is applied too quickly for the rock to fold (given the rock's strength, which tends to decrease as the rock gets hotter). Instead, the rock breaks. In tension, which includes shear, the layer of rocks thins (like pulled putty) or breaks if there is too much force in a short amount of time, causing the layer to move and leave a fault (which has usually become a weak zone that more movement tends to happen on).

In general, rock is weaker when it is stretched than when it is squeezed.
