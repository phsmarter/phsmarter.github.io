Answer:

During the Romantic period, music and literature had a strong relationship, with many writers and composers drawing inspiration from each other. Here are two significant examples:

1.The connection between music and literature in the works of Franz Schubert and Johann Wolfgang von Goethe:

Franz Schubert was a prominent composer of the Romantic period, known for his songs and lieder. He was heavily influenced by the works of the German writer Johann Wolfgang von Goethe, who was one of the most important literary figures of the time.

Schubert set many of Goethe's poems to music, including the famous "Erlkönig," "Gretchen am Spinnrade," and "Der Wanderer." These compositions helped to popularize Goethe's work and introduced a new way of experiencing literature through music.

Goethe's literary works, which often focused on themes of love and nature, were ideal sources of inspiration for Schubert's musical compositions. Schubert's use of melody and harmony complemented the imagery and emotions evoked by Goethe's poetry, creating a powerful and emotional experience for audiences.

2.The influence of music on the works of Percy Bysshe Shelley:

Percy Bysshe Shelley was a British poet and writer who was also influenced by the music of the Romantic period. Shelley was particularly interested in the works of Ludwig van Beethoven, who was a pioneering composer of the time.

Shelley's poem "Hymn to Intellectual Beauty" was directly inspired by Beethoven's Ninth Symphony. The poem, which explores the relationship between beauty, nature, and the human soul, was written shortly after Shelley heard Beethoven's Ninth Symphony performed in London.

Shelley was also influenced by the musical structure of Beethoven's compositions, which often used recurring motifs and themes to create a sense of unity and coherence. This influence can be seen in many of Shelley's poems, which use similar techniques to create a sense of continuity and cohesion.

Overall, these examples show how music and literature were closely connected during the Romantic period, with composers and writers drawing inspiration from each other's works. This mutual influence helped to create some of the most memorable and powerful artistic expressions of the era.

15. Why is Socialist Realism allowed to be used as style in literature in Russia​


Answer:Socialist realism is a style of idealized realistic art that was developed in the Soviet Union and was the official style in that country between 1932 and 1988, as well as in other socialist countries after World War II.[1] Socialist realism is characterized by the glorified depiction of communist values, such as the emancipation of the proletariat.[2] Despite its name, the figures in the style are very often highly idealized, especially in sculpture, where it often leans heavily on the conventions of classical sculpture. Although related, it should not be confused with social realism, a type of art that realistically depicts subjects of social concern,[3] or other forms of "realism" in the visual arts.

Socialist realism was the predominant form of approved art in the Soviet Union from its development in the early 1920s to its eventual fall from official status beginning in the late 1960s until the breakup of the Soviet Union in 1991.[4][5] While other countries have employed a prescribed canon of art, socialist realism in the Soviet Union persisted longer and was more restrictive than elsewhere in Europe.[6]
