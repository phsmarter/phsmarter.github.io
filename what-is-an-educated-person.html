6. What are the effects of education to one's personal aspect?​


Answer:

Higher levels of education are associated with a wide range of positive outcomes - including better health and wellbeing, higher social trust, greater political interest, lower political cynicism, and less hostile attitudes towards immigrants.

Education has become one of the clearest indicators of life outcomes such as employment, income and social status, and is a strong predictor of attitudes and wellbeing.

Education is often used by people to shape their ‘social identity’, framing their understanding of themselves and their relationships with other people. A positive, affirming social identity is associated with a range of positive outcomes in life, such as increased wellbeing, health, social trust and political engagement.

However, the emphasis on education in today’s society makes it much harder for people with low levels of education to develop a positive social identity. This can negatively affect self-esteem and wellbeing.

14. what are the qualities that an educated person should possess? what are teh qualities of a cultured person?


Answer:

Here are eight qualities of an educated person:

1. Intellectual curiosity. Educated individuals are lifelong learners. They want to know more about whatever interests them. As Dorothy Parker once said, “The cure for boredom is curiosity. There is no cure for curiosity.”

2. Intellectual humility. An education person is honest about what he or she does not know. That is the only way to get smarter. Speaking beyond one’s knowledge never ends well.

3. Cultural literacy. Truly educated individuals have the ability to read the culture like a text. They understand subtexts. They know why the audience laughs during Hamilton when Lafayette and Hamilton sing, “Immigrants. We get the job done.”

4. Aesthetic appreciation. This is not the same thing as snobbery. It’s just a continual development of taste in whatever interests one—art, food, travel, film, you name it.

5. Creative thinking. Educated persons possess an openness to connections that others may not see. They think against the grain and weigh each viewpoint on its own merit, regardless of who espouses it.

6. A sense of humor. The truly educated can appreciate the discrepancies that lie at the heart of humor. They deploy comedic ability to call into question vice and self-importance.

7. An evolved political viewpoint. Educated persons are fair minded and insistent upon objectively engaging with the world. But they also recognize hypocrisy in political leaders. They understand what Ambrose Bierce meant when he said, “Politics is a strife of interests masquerading as a contest of principles.”

8. An evolved spiritual/philosophical viewpoint. The truly educated know that a moral compass is essential to good character. They are determined to do the right thing even if no one is looking.
