Pro's of nuclear power plant

Nuclear is the largest source of clean power in the United States. Unit 4- Writing Assignment: Stages of Stress Response 15 Points Write a 300-500 word essay OR create a power point. • Think of an example of a stressful incident or series of events in your life or someone that you know. • Relate these events to the 3 stages of the stress response. o Be specific in discussing the physical effect the stress was having in each stage.


Answer:

Example of a Stressful Incident and Its Stages of Stress Response

A few years ago, I had a major project due for one of my courses in college. I had spent weeks researching and writing the paper, but as the deadline approached, I began to feel overwhelmed and anxious. On the day the paper was due, my computer crashed, and I lost all my progress. This incident triggered the three stages of the stress response: the alarm stage, the resistance stage, and the exhaustion stage.

The alarm stage is the first stage of the stress response. It is also known as the fight or flight response. When I realized that I had lost all my progress on the paper, my body went into a state of shock. My heart rate increased, and I began to sweat. My brain released hormones such as adrenaline and cortisol, which prepared me to either fight the problem or run away from it.

The resistance stage is the second stage of the stress response. It occurs when the stressor persists, and the body continues to respond to it. In my case, I had to decide what to do next. I could either give up on the project or start over. I chose to start over, which meant working long hours and sacrificing sleep and other activities. This stage is characterized by continued release of stress hormones, which can lead to physical and mental exhaustion if prolonged.

The exhaustion stage is the third and final stage of the stress response. It occurs when the body can no longer cope with the stressor, and it begins to break down. In my case, after weeks of stress and anxiety, I finally completed the project. However, I was physically and mentally exhausted. I had trouble sleeping, and I experienced headaches and other physical symptoms. This stage can lead to long-term health problems if not managed properly.

In conclusion, the three stages of the stress response – alarm, resistance, and exhaustion – are a natural and normal response to stressful situations. However, prolonged exposure to stress can lead to physical and mental health problems. In my case, the stress of the project caused me to experience physical symptoms and exhaustion. It is important to be aware of the stages of stress response and to take steps to manage stress effectively, such as through exercise, relaxation techniques, and seeking support from friends or mental health professionals.

17. With a minimum of 500 words, discuss the relationship between Science, Technology and Society. Cite specific examples on how science and technology impacts society.Would you rather live a simple society or a society which is technologically advanced? Why?​


Answer:

Science and Technology

Science informs technological advancements. We rely on theories on physics, material science, electricity, chemistry, etc to develop new technologies. Indeed, we’re still working to create a lot of potentially useful materials that are predicted to exist, based on our current scientific theories, and the more scientific theory develops, the more it can inform our technological advancements. Our ability to create such powerful computers is informed by our science. Quantum computers are being developed, based on our science on the topic, which will further our computing power by orders of magnitude.

But technology also changes how we do science. Think about the invention of the telescope. It vastly changed our ability to conduct astronomical research. Space telescopes, probes like Voyager, the Cassini probe and the Huygens lander, etc have done even more. Even more recently, technological advancements have allowed us to create a virtual telescope the size of the Earth, which has allowed us to actually photograph a black hole (or really the material around it).

I happened to pick astronomy as an example, but the influence technology has had on science is — well — astronomical. Computing power, storage capacity, and so on has allowed for data science and the use of big data in medical sciences, sociology, economics, and more. So the very computers that are being developed, using scientific theory and evidence, also help us do scientific research.

Answer:

Typhoon Haiyan (known locally as Yolanda) made landfall in the Philippines on 8 November 2013. Just over a year on, this article reflects on what the World Health Organisation (WHO) the co-lead for the health cluster alongside the Philippines Department of Health (DoH) has learnt, how these lessons have influenced the response over time and what this means for responses to health emergencies in the future. The article is based on internal information from WHOs own work, though it is hoped that the main findings will also be useful to other agencies.

Responding to multiple disasters

The first lesson is that national agencies and the international community need to be ready to respond to multiple natural disasters each year in the Philippines. The country is one of the worlds most disaster-prone. Typhoon Haiyan was the third crisis to hit the country in two months, following conflict in Zamboanga and an earthquake in Bohol, which combined displaced 750,000 people. This meant that response services including national and international agencies and the Philippine army were already stretched.

WHO Philippines has been working with the DoH to set up Emergency Operations Centres in vulnerable areas of the country, and to establish a gold, silver and bronze command system+

to direct disaster responses. The agency has also been restocking and pre-positioning medical supplies and equipment in anticipation of more natural disasters, and is developing toolkits with the DoH for emergency preparedness. These toolkits will provide guidance on procedures and practices to ensure a quick response in the aftermath of an emergency. Both national and local governments are working to ensure that health structures are disaster resilient.

How does the bill of rights of article 3 of the 1987 constitution affect you as an individual and your family as a whole.


Answer:

1. The recognition of human rights is important to uphold the values of fairness, respect and dignity for each individual, regardless of culture, ethnicity, race, nationality, gender, or age. Human rights are granted to ensure that all individuals are treated with respect and allowed the same opportunity to thrive. The recognition of human rights is necessary to protect individuals from violations of their rights, including physical and sexual abuse, discrimination, denial of basic needs, unrestricted migration, and oppression. It is also essential to maintain international stability and to preserve peace and security between nations.

2. There is a strong relationship between religion and human rights in that many religious teachings emphasize the importance of respecting individual rights and treating others with respect and compassion. The teachings of Christianity, Judaism, and Islam, for example, all emphasize that people should strive to treat others with dignity and utmost respect. This can be seen in the Golden Rule: "love your neighbor as yourself." In addition, many religious systems recognize the inherent rights of individuals to free speech, religious practice, and self-determination, which are also human rights recognized in modern legal systems.

3. The Golden Rule is an ethical principle that provides an invaluable lesson on how to treat others: to treat others as you would want them to treat you. This simple rule can be found in many religions, including Christianity, Buddhism, Hinduism, and Islam. It is commonly expressed as “Do unto others as you would have them do unto you” and encourages people to act in the same manner that they would want others to act towards them. The importance of this rule lies in the fact that it involves taking into consideration the feelings of others and being considerate towards them, instead of simply doing whatever you want.

As an example of the Golden Rule, consider the following scenario: imagine you are walking down the street, and you notice someone in need of assistance. If you applied the Golden Rule in this situation, you would stop and take the time to help the person, considering that if the situation was reversed, you would expect someone else to help you. By treating others kindly and lending a helping hand when necessary, you will experience the rewards of showing and receiving kindness.

4. Article 3 of the 1987 Constitution states that the Filipino people have certain unalienable rights, and that protecting and promoting these rights is the duty of the government. This includes the right to life and liberty, the right to due process and protection from arbitrary arrest and punishment, the right to freedom of speech and assembly, the right to vote, the right to education, and the right to property and security.

For individual citizens and families, this means that these rights are guaranteed to them and should not be violated. This provides families with security and assurance that the government is looking out for their best interests. For example, the right to vote ensures that individuals and families have a say in choosing their leaders, which can have a direct impact on their lives. The right to due process gives individuals a fair trial, allowing them to hear the charges against them and have the opportunity to defend themselves in court. These rights also ensure better protection from discrimination, oppression, and arbitrary punishment. In essence, Article 3 of the 1987 Constitution provides legal protection to individuals and their families, allowing them to live and flourish in a stable and just environment.
