1. My papa's waltz example of literary analysis?​


Answer:

My Papa’s Waltzby Theodore Roethke

Literary Analysis:

The poem "My Papa's Waltz" is written in the form of a loose ballad with four quatrains and a total of sixteen lines.

The poem is a monologue that Roethke described as his "best dramatic lyric," in which the speaker reflects on his childhood years. It is a son's direct address to his father.

The poem is ambiguous and does not provide a clear picture of the father-son relationship. It can be interpreted as the narrator's beautiful childhood memory, or it can be interpreted as a confession of child abuse. Because we are not told explicitly, it is a combination of the two.

[tex]\sf\large\bold{Paliwanag:}[/tex]

Si Papa Gregory VII o Pope Gregory VII (tawag sa kaniya sa Ingles) ay pinarangalan bilang santo sa Simbahang Katoliko. Siya ay naging isang papa noong Abril 22, 1073 hanggang sa araw ng kaniyang kamatayan- Mayo 25, 1085 sa Salerno, Italya. Bago siya namatay, itiniwalag niya si Haring Henry VII dahil sa pakikipag-usap nito sa kaniya. Gumanti si Haring Henry VII, inutos niya na ipatalsik si Papa Gregory VII, ngunit, nang malaman niya na kaanib ng mga maharlika sa Alemaniya (Germany) si Papa Gregory VII, humingi siya ng tawad dito at sumuko siya.

28. Which madrigal of Thomas Morley tells the story of a poet’s relationship to a woman?Chose.A. April is in my Mistress’ Face B. Fire Fire My Heart C. Missa Papae Marcelli D. Sing we and chant it ​


Answer:

A. April is in my Mistress’ Face

Explanation:

April is in my mistress’ face:

And July in her eyes hath place;

Within her bosom is September,

But in her heart a cold December.

This lilting English madrigal, written by Thomas Morley in 1594 based on an Italian text, deftly uses a weather metaphor to convey the vicissitudes of the poet’s relationship with a woman.

It appears to be based on an Italian text by Livio Celiano and set by Orazio Vecchi in 1587.

The seasonal nature of April weather, especially in Europe and in England where the author, has long made it fodder for poets. Or for poets in temperate climates.
