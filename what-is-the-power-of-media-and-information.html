2. what situations demonstrate the power of media and information?​


Answer:

The power of media and information can be demonstrated in various situations, some of which include:

1. Elections: Media and information can influence the outcome of elections by shaping public opinion, disseminating information about candidates and issues, and mobilizing voters. Political campaigns often use various forms of media, such as television ads, social media, and news coverage, to promote their agendas and win support from voters.

2. Social movements: Media and information can also facilitate social movements by raising awareness about social issues, mobilizing activists, and creating public pressure on policymakers and institutions. Social movements often use various forms of media, such as online petitions, viral videos, and hashtags, to spread their message and engage with a wider audience.

3. Crisis management: Media and information can also play a crucial role in managing crises, such as natural disasters, public health emergencies, and security threats. Accurate and timely information can help people make informed decisions, coordinate responses, and prevent panic or misinformation.

4. Business and marketing: Media and information can also influence consumer behavior and business strategies. Companies often use various forms of media, such as advertisements, product placements, and influencer marketing, to promote their products and services and attract customers.

5. Education and research: Media and information can also facilitate learning, research, and innovation by providing access to knowledge, data, and resources. Online courses, academic journals, and scientific databases are examples of how media and information can support intellectual and professional development.

Overall, the power of media and information lies in its ability to shape perceptions, attitudes, and behaviors, as well as to facilitate communication, collaboration, and innovation. However, this power can also be abused or misused, leading to misinformation, propaganda, and manipulation. Therefore, it is important to critically evaluate and verify sources of media and information, and to use them responsibly and ethically.

Title: How Social Media Helped the Women of Iceland Strike for Equality

In 2016, thousands of women in Iceland walked out of work early to protest the gender pay gap in their country. The strike, which was organized by women's rights groups, quickly gained traction on social media, with the hashtag #kvennafrí (women's day off) trending on Twitter.

The strike was a huge success, with an estimated 90% of women in Iceland participating in some form. The protest gained international attention, with media outlets around the world covering the story.

The power of social media and information in this event was instrumental in bringing attention to the issue of gender inequality in Iceland. The strike sparked a national conversation about the gender pay gap, and the government responded by introducing new legislation to address the issue.

In 2018, Iceland became the first country in the world to make it illegal to pay men more than women for the same job. This historic change was a direct result of the media attention and public pressure generated by the 2016 women's strike.

1. Definitions:

- Media: the means of communication, such as newspapers, magazines, radio, television, and social media, used for the purposeof conveying information or entertainment to the public.

- Opportunity: a favorable set of circumstances that presents a chance for progress or advancement.

- Challenge: a difficult task or problem that requires effort and determination to overcome.

- Opportunity: a favorable circumstance or condition for achieving a particular goal or outcome.

- Challenge: something that requires great effort or skill to be achieved or overcome.

- Power: the ability to influence or control people or things.

30. 1. Media and information literacy is clearly of critical importance in a world that practically runs on media and information. Indeed, harnessing these immensely powerful forces can yield many opportunities. However, what are the risks of failing to harness them properly?


Answer:

Media and Information literacy

"Media and Information Literacy encompasses the knowledge, attitudes, and set of abilities required to recognize when and what information is required, where and how to access it, how to critically assess and organize it once obtained, and how to use it ethically." Learning, critical thinking, and interpretative abilities are included in the notion, which extends beyond communication and information technologies to include learning, critical thinking, and interpretative skills across and beyond professional and educational borders.

Oral, print, and digital information resources are all included in media and information literacy. In an increasingly digitized, interdependent, and global world, media and information literacy is a core human right that fosters greater social inclusion. It has the ability to bridge the gap between those who have access to a lot of information and those who don't. Individuals with knowledge of the functions of the media and information systems, as well as the conditions under which these functions are performed" Media and Information Literacy empowers and endows individuals with knowledge of the functions of the media and information systems, as well as the conditions under which these functions are performed.

Risks of failing to harness media and information properly.

Gathering Fake News could be a result of failing to harness media and information correctly.

Fake news, in general, is a fictitious story that is published and marketed as if it were true. In the past, fake news was mainly propaganda spread by powerful people to instill a particular belief or support a particular perspective, even if it was wholly false. Social media has now created a climate in which anyone with a political objective may spread lies as if they were real. People can be hired to post fake news on behalf of others, or automated programs, known as bots, can publish fake news that is generated automatically. People's motivations for creating and disseminating fake news are as varied as their individual viewpoints.

Its Consequences

While some fake news may appear to be harmless or merely amusing, much of it is nasty and even dangerous. Fake news is intended to influence people's beliefs, attitudes, or perceptions in order to influence their actions. This means that if you fall into the trap of accepting fake news, someone else's goal is driving your thoughts and judgments. Furthermore, in some parts of the world, producing and disseminating fake news might have legal ramifications.
