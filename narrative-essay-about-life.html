Explanation:

Dr. Martin Luther King, Jr spoke of a day in the future when he hoped that his children would be judged not by their skin color but instead by their character.  Most of his speeches revolve around the injustice brought upon Black people. For the longest time, Black people were discriminated upon and even if there are those who were given a chance and became successful in life were still seen as inferior citizens.

In his famous "I Have a Dream" speech he  dreamed of having a segregation and racism-free world. Segregation was the separation of whites from Blacks.  While this is no longer practiced today, racism is still very much true and most people are judged by external appearances.
