— Using Analogy to Describe or to Make a point —

An analogy is a comparison of two unrelated objects to show their similarities. The similarities are then used to give an explanation of the concepts or sensi- tive topics that are difficult to understand Thus, an analogy makes complex topics un- derstandable to the reader.

Analogies can be used to describe or make a point.

To describe means to tell someone the appearance, nature, or attri- butes of something or someone. To make a point means to argue or to promote an idea.

Examples;

➢ To describe

"People are like stained glass win dows. They sparkle and shine when the sun is out, but when the darkness sets in, their true beauty is revealed only if there is a light from within." -Elisabeth Kubler-Ross

In the example above, instead of say- ing "People who continue to strive despite difficult times are truly beautiful," the spea ker compares people with stained-glass windows. Notice that this comparison cre ates a better description in the minds of the readers because of the imagery used.

➢ To make a point

"Show me the man you honor, and I will know what kind of man you are."-Thomas Carlyle
