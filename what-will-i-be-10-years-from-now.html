Answer for #1:

1). Height. When we were younger we used to go up a chair to get something from the table and now we do not need it anymore since our height is enough to reach something from the table..

2). Weight. We gain weight because of our bones the more we get older the more bigger and heavier our bones will get.

3). Hips. As we age, our hips will be more round than the previous.

4). Shoulders. As we age our shoulders gets more broad or wide.

5). Breast. For women, our breast gets bigger because it grows in response to the hormones estrogen and progesterone. As you enter puberty, levels of these hormones increase. Your breasts begin to grow under the stimulation of these hormones.

6). Armpits. For men, hair grows from their armpits because it  prevents skin-to-skin contact when doing certain activities, such as running and walking.

7). Adam's Apple. As I said in number 3, the older we get the more bigger and heavier our bones will get. People may say that women does not have any Adam's Apple but all of us really has. Its just that men's Adam's Apple is bigger than ours.

8). Genitals. Our genitals will get more bigger and it will also grow hair, Pubic hair is its ability to reduce friction during sexual intercourse. It can also stop bacteria and other microorganisms from entering the body.

9). Muscles and Bones. As we age, our bones tend to get bigger, heavier, and stronger. But when we are on our 50's or 60's our bones will get weaker and weaker.

10). Voice. Before we reach puberty, our larynx is pretty small and your vocal cords are kind of small and thin. And when we are now going through puberty, the larynx gets bigger and the vocal cords lengthen and thicken, so your voice gets deeper.
