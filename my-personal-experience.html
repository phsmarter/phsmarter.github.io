23. what are the meaning of extraversion or introversion based on my personal experiences​


Answer:

Extraversion and introversion are personality traits that describe how individuals interact with the world around them. Extraverts tend to be outgoing, sociable, and energized by social interactions, while introverts tend to be more reserved, introspective, and energized by alone time.

If you identify as an extravert, you may find that you enjoy being around people and engaging in social activities. You may feel energized by being in large groups and may find it easy to strike up conversations with others. You may also be comfortable in new situations and may enjoy taking risks.

On the other hand, if you identify as an introvert, you may prefer to spend time alone or in small groups of close friends. You may find social interactions draining and may need alone time to recharge. You may also be more thoughtful and introspective, and may enjoy activities that allow you to reflect and process your thoughts and emotions.

It's important to note that extraversion and introversion are not binary categories, but rather exist on a continuum. Many people exhibit both extraverted and introverted tendencies, and may fall somewhere in between the two extremes.

Ultimately, whether you identify as an extravert or an introvert, it's important to recognize and honor your own needs and preferences when it comes to social interactions and alone time. Both personality traits have their own unique strengths and can lead to fulfilling and satisfying lives.
