26. How does Governor-General Izquierdo describe Cavite Mutiny compared from the version of Dr.Trinidad H. Pardo de Tavera?


Answer:

Governor-General Izquierdo describes the event as "purely military rebellion."

Explanation:

The Governor-General Izquierdo's description of the Cavite Mutiny and Dr. Trinidad H. Pardo de Tavera's version differ significantly in their interpretation and emphasis of events.

Governor-General Izquierdo's account of the Cavite Mutiny, as reported in his official report, portrays the event as a purely military rebellion, instigated by a small group of disgruntled soldiers. Izquierdo blames the mutiny on the soldiers' dissatisfaction with their working conditions and suggests that they were motivated by greed rather than any political or nationalist sentiments.

In contrast, Dr. Trinidad H. Pardo de Tavera's version of the Cavite Mutiny, as recounted in his book "La Insurrección en Filipinas," presents the event as a significant turning point in Philippine history, a precursor to the Philippine Revolution. Pardo de Tavera argues that the mutiny was a political act of resistance against the Spanish colonial regime, motivated by nationalist sentiments and a desire for freedom and independence.

Pardo de Tavera also provides a more detailed and nuanced account of the mutiny, describing the events leading up to the rebellion and the various factors that contributed to its outbreak. He highlights the role of Filipino priests and intellectuals in fomenting nationalist sentiment and argues that the mutiny was a response to the injustices and abuses of the colonial regime.
