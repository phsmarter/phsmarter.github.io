What does the urbanization mean?​

What is urbanization? The population change from rural to urban areas, the resulting decrease in the proportion of people living in rural areas and increase in urban, and the ways in which societies adapt to this change are all referred to as urbanization. It is primarily the process by which towns and cities are formed and grow larger as more people begin to live and work in central areas.

Urbanization should be separated from urban expansion, despite the fact that the two terms are sometimes used interchangeably. Unlike urbanization, which refers to the percentage of the total national population who live in urban areas, urban expansion refers to the sheer number of people who live in those regions. By 2050, 64 percent of the developing world and 86 percent of the developed world are expected to be urbanized.

29. what is the meaning of urban overspill?​


Answer:

Urban overspill refers to the phenomenon of urbanization spreading beyond the boundaries of a city or town and into surrounding areas. This can happen when the population of a city or town grows beyond its capacity, and people start to move to nearby areas to find housing, employment, or other opportunities. As a result, the urban area expands into surrounding rural or semi-rural areas, leading to the development of suburbs, commuter towns, or other types of urban sprawl.

Urban overspill can have both positive and negative impacts on the surrounding areas. On the one hand, it can bring new economic opportunities, such as jobs and business growth, to the area. On the other hand, it can also put pressure on local resources, such as land, water, and infrastructure, and lead to environmental degradation, traffic congestion, and other problems.

To manage urban overspill, city and regional planners may develop policies and strategies to encourage more sustainable and equitable patterns of urban growth. This can include measures such as promoting public transportation, protecting green spaces and natural resources, and encouraging compact and mixed-use development that supports walkability and reduces the need for car travel.
