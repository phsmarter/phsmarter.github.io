6. similarities of holistic and partial perspective


Answer:

Holistic thinking is what you do when you stand back and look at a topic as a whole and using a big picture perspective. Everything is seen as something that influences the whole and all its parts are considered to be connected, so that when you consider them you must also consider the impact of change on the whole.

Partial thinking is all about focussing on detail and usually on just one part of the whole. It allows you to be micro-focussed and to ‘forget’ the role that that part plays in the functioning of the whole.

7. what is the differentiate between partial perspective from holistic perspective ​

14. what is holistic perspective and partial perspective??


No matter what subject or field that is being pertained in this question, a holistic perspective is a wide point of view (POV) of an individual toward a certain issue or topic. This perspective may also be considered to be a “strategic” POV wherein all factors are taken into consideration.

 

Meanwhile, a partial perspective is the POV wherein only a certain number of factors are taken into consideration and not everything, which are considered non-essential, are taken into account.
