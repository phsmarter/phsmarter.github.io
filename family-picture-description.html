Answer:

Woodwind instruments:

Flute - a tube with a series of fingerholes and the wind pass to sharp edge to provide sounds

Clarinet - usually used in orchestrally and in military and brass bands

Saxophone - it characterized by a conical metal tube and finger keys

Brass Instruments:

French Horn - it is a coiled metal tube with a large flared bell at one end and a mouthpiece at the other

Trumpet - oldest of all brass instruments

Trombone - it is a long, bend metal tube

String instruments:

Ukulele - small guitar and is a descendant of the machete or machada

Violin - a 4 stringed instrument and also known as a fiddle

Guitar - it is a fretted musical instrument that usually has six strings

Percussion instruments:

Cymbals - classified as the biggest noisemakers of the orchestra

Tambourine - small drum with metal jingles set into the edges

Chimes - metal tubes of different lengths that are hung from a metal frame
