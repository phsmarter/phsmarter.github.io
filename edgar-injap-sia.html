1. Why did Edgar "Injap" J. Sia II build the Mang Inasal business?​


Answer:

At 26 years of age in 2003, he started from scratch the fast-food chain Mang Inasal and grew it from a humble branch in Iloilo City to 338 branches nationwide within the first seven years of operations. It was at this point in October 2010 that Jollibee Foods Corporation acquired 70% of Mang Inasal for p3 Billion Pesos ($68m USD), and in April 2016 he sold the remaining 30% of Mang Inasal to Jollibee for p2 Billion Pesos ($41m USD). Enabling him to cash in a total of p5 Billion pesos ($109m USD) from the Mang Inasal business venture that he started from a simple restaurant.

Edgar Sia II Success Story

As a result of his careful planning, clear visions, thorough business design, and passionate execution, Mang Inasal today continues to grow. Mang Inasal remains to be one of the largest and most loved fast-food chains in the country with over 450 stores nationwide and generates over p12 Billion Pesos in system-wide sales revenue and also consistently showing double-digit same-store sales growth year-on-year.

Edgar ‘Injap’ Sia II was born in Iloilo City in 1977 and grew up in Roxas City, Capiz province. He is the eldest of three siblings. Despite Injap

being a true-blue provincial guy and unfamiliar with the complexities of the big business environment of the Philippine capital, he made sure he is able to learn and adapt fast. In 2011, Sia become the youngest billionaire in the Philippines at the age of 34 after selling his 70% stake in Mang Inasal to Jollibee Foods Corp.

He had his elementary, high school, and college education all in the province. 16. What are the characteristics and competencies that made Edgar Sia successful? Why?


Answer:

Edgar Sia is a Filipino businessman who is the founder and CEO of the Mang Inasal chain of fast-food restaurants. Some of the characteristics and competencies that contributed to his success include:

Entrepreneurial vision: Sia had a clear vision for his business from the beginning, which was to offer affordable and delicious Filipino food to the masses. He recognized an untapped market for this type of cuisine and worked to fill that gap.

Risk-taking: Sia was willing to take calculated risks to achieve his goals, such as investing his life savings to start his business. He also expanded his business rapidly, opening multiple stores in a short period of time.

Strategic thinking: Sia made strategic decisions that helped his business grow, such as focusing on a specific niche market, adopting a franchising model to expand more quickly, and prioritizing employee training and development to ensure quality service.

Customer-centric approach: Sia prioritized the needs and preferences of his customers, which helped him build a loyal following. He also listened to feedback and made changes to his menu and service offerings based on customer input.

Financial acumen: Sia had a deep understanding of financial management, which allowed him to make informed decisions about investments, pricing, and budgeting.

Explanation:

Overall, Sia's entrepreneurial vision, risk-taking, strategic thinking, customer-centric approach, and financial acumen are some of the characteristics and competencies that contributed to his success in building the Mang Inasal chain of fast-food restaurants.
