22. i pursue my college because​


Answer:

I want a better future, wanna become successful and satisfy myself

Because everyone was doing it?!

Cynicism aside.

I was in fact excited over going to college. I pursued it because I was raised to the idea that the best way to earn a living is earning a college degree. This is probably true most of the case.

Particularly, I pursued Engineering because it was a whimsical decision after failing to pass an entrance test to a university I aspired to enrol to. In fact, I wanted to be an artist so the plan was to take Fine Arts. But apparently those didn’t materialize.

Plan B. I wanted to enrol to a private university offering Fine Arts but mother was against it. So I resorted to a university that my mother thought was best, where I could take any degree.

I recall I had excitement going because I thought college was going to be fun. It was until I realize I was studying for the sake of employment, thus earning money.

Nothing wrong about it. Just that, at some point in college you would ask yourself whether the career you’d earn will be as fulfilling as you think it is.

If I could go back in high school and ask the same question, I’d definitely answer this.

“Because college will equip me with necessary foundational sciences knowledge. Such knowledge necessitates to be competetive in medical school.”

The crux is when you want to go to college, you definitely should know how it is going to help you reach your ambition.
