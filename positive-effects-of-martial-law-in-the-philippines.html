1. write positive and Negative effects of martial law in the Philippines. ​


Answer:

Martial law in the Philippines was declared twice in the country's history, first from 1972 to 1981 under the regime of President Ferdinand Marcos, and second from 2017 to 2019 under the administration of President Rodrigo Duterte in the southern island of Mindanao. Here are some potential positive and negative effects of martial law in the Philippines:

Positive effects of martial law:

Increased security: Martial law can bring a sense of increased security and stability to the country, as it grants the military and police additional powers to maintain order and combat threats to public safety.Increased government efficiency: Under martial law, the government can streamline its decision-making processes and bureaucracy, as there are fewer legal and procedural hurdles to contend with.Economic development: Martial law can lead to increased economic development, as the government has more control over economic policies and can prioritize infrastructure projects and investment.

Negative effects of martial law:

Human rights abuses: One of the most significant negative effects of martial law is the potential for human rights abuses, as the government is given sweeping powers that can be used to suppress dissent and opposition.Suppression of democratic institutions: Martial law can undermine democratic institutions such as the judiciary, press freedom, and civil society, as these institutions are often seen as obstacles to the government's goals.Economic stagnation: While martial law can lead to economic development, it can also lead to economic stagnation if the government prioritizes spending on security and military forces rather than on social services and infrastructure.Trauma and psychological damage: Martial law can cause psychological damage to individuals and communities, as it creates an environment of fear, uncertainty, and trauma, particularly for those who have experienced violence or human rights abuses.

It's worth noting that the effects of martial law can vary significantly depending on the specific circumstances under which it is implemented, and that this is not an exhaustive list of all potential positive and negative effects.

29. What are the impacts of Martial Law in the country? (The effects of Martial Law on the society in general, and on the government in particular.)​


Answer:

In September 1972 Marcos declared martial law, claiming that it was the last defense against the rising disorder caused by increasingly violent student demonstrations, the alleged threats of communist insurgency by the new Communist Party of the Philippines (CPP), and the Muslim separatist movement of the Moro National Liberation Front (MNLF). One of his first actions was to arrest opposition politicians in Congress and the Constitutional Convention. Initial public reaction to martial law was mostly favourable except in Muslim areas of the south, where a separatist rebellion, led by the MNLF, broke out in 1973. Despite halfhearted attempts to negotiate a cease-fire, the rebellion continued to claim thousands of military and civilian casualties. Communist insurgency expanded with the creation of the National Democratic Front (NDF), an organization embracing the CPP and other communist groups.
