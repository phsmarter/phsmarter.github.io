5. Formal Composition #4 "My Mother, My Hero" Paragraph 1: Define mother Paragraph 2: Describe your mother, how do you call your mother and tell something about the rule of her in your family'Paragraph 3: How will you repay your mother's sacrifices​


Answer:

Title: "My Mother, My Hero"

Paragraph 1: Defining Mother

A mother is not merely a biological parent but a pillar of strength, love, and guidance. She is a nurturing presence that shapes our lives from the very beginning. A mother's unconditional love, selflessness, and unwavering support make her an irreplaceable figure in our lives. She is the epitome of sacrifice and serves as the foundation of a family.

Paragraph 2: Describing My Mother and Her Role in My Family

To me, my mother is not just a parent; she is my confidante, my best friend, and my greatest inspiration. I lovingly call her "Mom," a name that resonates with warmth and affection. In our family, my mother plays a central role, serving as the glue that binds us together. She has an incredible knack for multitasking, effortlessly juggling her responsibilities as a homemaker, a professional, and a mentor. Her unwavering dedication to creating a loving and harmonious environment in our home is truly commendable. With her nurturing presence, she has instilled values of kindness, compassion, and resilience in each family member.

Paragraph 3: Repaying My Mother's Sacrifices

The sacrifices my mother has made for me are immeasurable. From sleepless nights tending to my needs as an infant to supporting my dreams and aspirations as I grew older, she has always put my well-being before her own. Repaying her sacrifices is a heartfelt commitment that I hold close to my heart. Firstly, I aim to show my appreciation by being a responsible and compassionate individual, striving to make her proud in everything I do. Additionally, I intend to support her emotionally and be a constant source of love and care, just as she has been for me. Furthermore, I aspire to ease her burdens and ensure her comfort as she grows older, always making sure she feels cherished and valued. Lastly, I am determined to honor her sacrifices by passing on the invaluable life lessons she has taught me to future generations, ensuring that her wisdom and love continue to shape our family's legacy.

In conclusion, my mother is my hero, and her role in my life and our family is unparalleled. Her selflessness, love, and guidance have shaped me into the person I am today. Through my actions, I strive to repay her sacrifices and make her proud. The immeasurable impact she has had on my life will forever be cherished, and I am grateful to call her my mother.

Mother, A Cradle to Hold Me by Maya Angelou

1. What significant characteristics of a mother are

described in the poem?

The poem described the significant characteristics of a mother as one shows unconditional love. It also portrays the never-ending patience they have and the wisdom they carry.

2. Why is the mother compared to a cradle?

The mother is compared to a cradle because she holds and rocks us. When life seems hard and we just want to shut ourselves out from the world, our mothers will always be there to care for us. From when we are young to when we are old enough to fend for ourselves, our mothers will always provide for our needs.

3. How will you relate the context of the poem to your

own mother?

Just like with me and my own mother, the mother and speaker of the poem have a symbiotic relationship that cannot be broken. No amount of time and distance can ever erase my connection with my mother. I will be always connected to her. I will always remember her in all the beautiful and ugly parts and I will always accept her as she is, just like how she accepts me as I am.

4. What do you think is the reason why the author wrote

the poem?

The author wrote the poem because she wants to show her appreciation for her mother. This is a recollection of all her memories with her mother while growing up, during good times and bad times. This is gratitude for her mother who never left her and instead cared for her always.
