6. Task 8.2 A QUOTE ON QUOTE


The phrase “quote unquote” means speaking a quotation on a word for word basis. You end the quotation with the word “unquote” to indicate that it’s finished. On the one hand, the phrase “quote on quote” is a mispronunciation of “unquote.” The correct phrase is “quote unquote.” This indicates that what you’re saying is a repetition of someone else’s statement.

ALL ACTIONS HAVE CONSEQUENCES

There are repercussions for both ourselves and others from everything we think, say, and do. Our activities spread out and have an impact on others like ripples on a pond because everything is interrelated. And we must keep in mind that our actions are final.

The first step in dealing with the fallout from your actions is accepting responsibility, which is admitting that you were mistaken and in the wrong. You acknowledge responsibility for your acts and that you must deal with the results of your actions. Consequences result from bad deeds rather than from good deeds.

Here are some consequences of Ethics in Human Actions:the degree of pain or pleasurethe length of the activityThe degree of certainty or uneasethe extent of the action

It implies that everything you do has an impact. Sometimes negative, sometimes positive.

Consequences, whether they be logical or natural, aid in our collective learning and development. Children learn to make better decisions and modify their behavior when they see the effects of their actions. Consequences + learning, in other words. The outcome or immediate impact of an action is known as a consequence. Consequences are intended to teach a lesson that encourages wise decision-making.

24. stealing is acquiring something without authorization. some pupils will steal something they want. some steal to vent their frustration or grief or get revenge on someone they feel harmed them. others steal to impress their teachers or to rise in their eyes of their peers. despite the reason for stealing, students should remember that doing so leads to conflict and mistrust among their peers.1. what are the causes of stealing?2. what are the affects of stealing?​


Answer/Explanation:

he causes of stealing among students may vary, but some possible reasons include:

Wanting something that they cannot afford or obtain through legitimate means

Feeling frustrated, angry, or upset and using stealing as a way to cope with their emotions

Seeking attention or admiration from others

Wanting to fit in with a certain group or impress their peers

Feeling a sense of entitlement or justification for taking something that they feel they deserve.

The effects of stealing can be serious and far-reaching, not only for the victim but also for the perpetrator. Some possible effects of stealing include:

Damage to relationships and trust among peers and authority figures

Legal consequences and potential criminal charges

Damage to reputation and future opportunities

Feelings of guilt, shame, and low self-esteem

Potential harm to the victim, both physical and emotional.
