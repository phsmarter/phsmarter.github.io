10. What are your expectation to your college level classmates


1. Be on Time

Being late does not make a good impression. While occasionally it can't be helped, often times with a bit of good planning it can be avoided.

If your class is online, log into class on the first day and log in often so that you are aware of due dates and communications from your instructor.

Turn in assignments by the deadlines

2. Say it Right from the Beginning

Anyone who is teaching at OCCC has earned a degree and can be referred to as professor. It is appropriate to refer to them as "Professor" or "Professor Lastname". Dr. is used as an honorific if the person has a doctorate degree. Dr. Lastname would be an appropriate way to address this person. When in doubt, start with Professor Lastname unless you know the person has a doctorate degree, and in that case, use Dr. Lastname. If you are unsure how to address someone, it is perfectly fine to ask them what they prefer.

3. Be Respectful

Turn off your cell phone before class begins

Tell your instructor if you must arrive late or leave early

If you have comments or need to talk, visit with your instructor during office hours

Do all that your instructor asks you to do and do it well

Take responsibility for your attendance, participation, and learning in the course

Do not cheat or help others cheat

4. Be Prepared

Take notes on lectures and readings

Keep up on all assignments and activities

Manipulate class notes to improve understanding

Spend at least two hours outside of class for every one hour of class time

5. Be attentive

Listen and read actively

Participate in discussions

Focus during videos and presentations

Avoid distraction

As a student in the College of Education, I have certain expectations from my professors. Firstly, I expect them to have excellent subject knowledge and expertise in their field, and be able to communicate this knowledge effectively to students. I also expect them to be approachable and responsive to student queries and concerns, and be willing to provide guidance and support when needed. In addition, I expect my professors to provide constructive feedback on assignments and assessments, and to be fair and objective in their evaluation of student work.

On the other hand, I understand that my professors have certain expectations from me as a student. Firstly, they expect me to attend classes regularly and be punctual in submitting assignments and assessments. They also expect me to be engaged and participative in class discussions, and to be proactive in seeking help and support when needed. Additionally, they expect me to be respectful towards my fellow classmates and the professor, and to adhere to academic integrity and ethical standards.

Overall, the expectations of the professor and the student in the College of Education are interconnected, with both parties playing an important role in the academic and personal growth of the student. By meeting these expectations, students can develop a strong foundation of knowledge and skills, and be prepared to contribute positively to the education system and society as a whole.
