3. title: An Empirical Investigation of the Relationship between Sales Promotion and Customer Satisfaction in the fast food Industry"what are element and measurement​


Answer:

Explanation:

"The Empirical Investigation of the Relationship between Sales Promotion and Customer Satisfaction in the Fast Food Industry" is a research study that aims to explore the relationship between sales promotion and customer satisfaction in the fast food industry. Here are the elements and measurements used in this study:

Elements:

- Sales promotion: In this study, sales promotion refers to the various marketing activities that fast food restaurants use to increase sales and attract customers. Examples of sales promotion tactics include discount coupons, limited-time offers, and loyalty programs.

- Customer satisfaction: Customer satisfaction refers to the degree to which customers are happy with their experience at a fast food restaurant. This can be measured through customer surveys, feedback forms, or other metrics such as repeat business or positive reviews.

Measurements:

- The study uses a survey questionnaire to measure customer satisfaction. The questionnaire asks customers to rate various aspects of their experience at the fast food restaurant, such as the quality of the food, the speed of service, and the cleanliness of the restaurant.

- The study also collects data on sales promotion activities by analyzing promotional materials and advertisements used by the fast food restaurants in the study. This data is then used to identify the types of sales promotion tactics that are most commonly used in the industry.

- The study uses statistical analysis to examine the relationship between sales promotion and customer satisfaction. Specifically, regression analysis is used to determine if there is a correlation between sales promotion tactics and customer satisfaction, and to identify which types of sales promotion are most effective in improving customer satisfaction.
