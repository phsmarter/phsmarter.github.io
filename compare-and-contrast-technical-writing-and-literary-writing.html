1. compare and contrast between technical writing and literary writingâ€‹


Answer:

The difference between technical and literary writing lies heavily on its use of language and style of presenting information as required for specific formats

Explanation:

Writing is a form of communication that utilizes language to deliver an intended message to particular readers within a context. As a form of communication both technical and literary writing consist of source, content, channel, audience and context. They differ in their characteristics and indicators of those elements in practice or operation.

Technical writers are different from poets or authors of literary works in terms of their interest, purpose and style of writing. They both have writing expertise to be appreciated by their readers, but they are limited in their style as dictated by the nature of the material they create. Technical writers also need some creativity to go with their logic and knowledge expertise about technical subjects.

Technical writing in the surface is non-fiction writing. But not all non-fiction manuscripts are considered technical documents. Literature has in its genres both fiction and non-fiction. Unlike the wide circulation of literary fiction that caters to general readers, technical writing targets specific audience. The readers of technical writing are those mostly interested on technical subjects. They include experts, professionals, field practitioners and the academics.

While literary writing can be informal and personal, technical writing is strict to being formal and impersonal in tone and voice. Literary writing would be prosaic or verbose, but technical writing will use the language in a straightforward manner and present the ideas with conciseness or brevity.

Literary writing is humanistic that it allows for creative expression or aesthetics. Technical writing is conventional and adheres to formats and standards in presenting empirical information. Humanism rules literary writing, while technical writing is held by the logic and determinism of scientific writing which is richly engrained in the philosophy of empiricism. Humanism is liberal which looks at life as a subject for literary writing. Empiricism is rigidly objective that it looks at things in life as objects for technical writing.

Technical writers generally aim to inform or persuade their readers to do some action upon reading the technical document. On the other hand, literary writers inspire and entertain with some commitment to inform or stimulate the readersâ€™ emotions. To illustrate, an essay on climate change may stimulate and stir the audience to do some action for the environment, yet it is still literary. A technical report on climate change will have more details to inform the reader about the problemâ€™s causes, implications and specific mitigation schemes. 18. Compare technical report write with other forms of writing?â€‹


Answer:

1. Audience: Technical reports are usually written for a specific audience, such as engineers, scientists, or supervisors. Other forms of writing can be written for a broader audience or to a specific individual.

2. Purpose: The purpose of a technical report is to inform, analyze, and make recommendations based on data or research. Other forms of writing may aim to persuade, entertain, inform, or educate.

3. Structure: Technical reports generally follow a specific structure that includes an introduction, methodology, results, analysis, and conclusion. Other forms of writing may have a different structure or none at all.

4. Style: Technical reports use a more formal and objective tone compared to other forms of writing that may be more conversational or personal.

5. Language: Technical reports often use technical terms and jargon specific to the field or industry being discussed, whereas other forms of writing may use simpler language geared towards a broader audience.

Overall, technical reports are a specialized form of writing that requires a deep understanding of the subject matter and a focus on providing accurate and detailed information.

20. In technical writing Compare and contrast these two words in relation to Classification: Species and Members.â€‹


ANSWER:

In classification, species and members are two related but distinct concepts. Species refers to a group of organisms that are believed to be closely related and share certain characteristics that distinguish them from other groups of organisms. Members, on the other hand, refer to the individual organisms that belong to a particular species.

One key difference between species and members is that species is a higher level concept that encompasses a group of individuals, while members are the individual organisms that make up a species. For example, the species "Canis lupus" (gray wolf) would include all the individual wolves that belong to that species, and each of those individual wolves would be a member of the species.

Another difference is that species are typically identified and named based on shared characteristics, while members are usually identified by their individual traits. For example, all members of the species Canis lupus are wolves, but each wolf has its own unique characteristics, such as size, color, and behavior.

In summary, species and members are two related concepts in classification, but they refer to different levels of organization within a group of organisms. Species refer to groups of organisms that share certain characteristics, while members refer to the individual organisms that belong to a particular species.
