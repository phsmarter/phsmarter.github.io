6. Differentiates the Plants Diversity from Animal Diversity. construct one essay paragraph its topic.


Plants and animals represent two of the major kingdoms of life, with distinct differences in their physical characteristics and lifestyles. While both groups exhibit a wide range of diversity, there are several key ways in which plants and animals differ from one another. One of the most notable differences is that plants are autotrophic, meaning they can produce their food through photosynthesis, while animals are heterotrophic and must consume other organisms to obtain their energy. Another key difference is that plants have cell walls made of cellulose, whereas animal cells lack such structures. Additionally, plants are often sessile, rooted in one place, while animals are typically mobile and can move around freely. These differences in lifestyle, metabolism, and structure have led to a great deal of diversity within both the plant and animal kingdoms, with countless species exhibiting unique adaptations to their particular ecological niches.

12. how you can practice diversity and inclusion in the workplace. ESSAY​


Diversity and inclusion are crucial for creating a welcoming and productive work environment. It's important to acknowledge and appreciate the differences among employees, including race, ethnicity, gender, religion, and age. When employees feel valued and respected, they are more likely to be engaged, motivated, and committed to the organization's goals.

One way to practice diversity and inclusion in the workplace is to create a safe space for employees to express themselves. This could be in the form of open communication channels, such as a suggestion box or regular team meetings, where employees can share their ideas, feedback, and concerns without fear of retaliation or judgment.

Another way to promote diversity and inclusion is to offer diversity and inclusion training programs. These programs could include workshops on cultural sensitivity, unconscious bias, and effective communication across cultures. This can help employees better understand and appreciate different perspectives and work together more effectively.

Finally, leaders in the organization should model inclusive behavior by promoting diversity and inclusion in their actions, decisions, and policies. This can include recruiting and promoting diverse talent, providing equal opportunities for all employees, and celebrating diversity through cultural events and initiatives.

In summary, practicing diversity and inclusion in the workplace requires ongoing effort and commitment. By creating a welcoming environment, promoting open communication, providing diversity and inclusion training, and modeling inclusive behavior, organizations can create a workplace culture that values and celebrates differences among employees.

16. Make an essay entitled "Unity in Diversity".​


Answer:

Cooperative diversity is a cooperative multiple antenna technique for improving or maximising total network channel capacities for any given set of bandwidths which exploits user diversity by decoding the combined signal of the relayed signal and the direct signal in wireless multihop networks. A conventional single hop system uses direct transmission where a receiver decodes the information only based on the direct signal while regarding the relayed signal as interference, whereas the cooperative diversity considers the other signal as contribution. That is, cooperative diversity decodes the information from the combination of two signals. Hence, it can be seen that cooperative diversity is an antenna diversity that uses distributed antennas belonging to each node in a wireless network.[1] Note that user cooperation is another definition of cooperative diversity. User cooperation[2] considers an additional fact that each user relays the other user's signal while cooperative diversity can be also achieved by multi-hop relay networking systems.
