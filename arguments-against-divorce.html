3. what is the arguments,reasoning,evidence of the affirmative divorce?​


Answer:

The main argument for affirmative divorce is that individuals should have the freedom to leave a marriage that no longer serves them without having to prove fault. This promotes personal autonomy and self-determination.

Explanation:

Supporters of affirmative divorce also argue that the current fault-based system can be harmful and create unnecessary conflict, particularly in cases where one partner may be at fault but refuses to admit it.

Furthermore, evidence suggests that fault-based divorce laws can perpetuate gender inequalities, particularly when women are disproportionately blamed for the breakdown of the marriage.

Affirmative divorce also acknowledges that marriages can end for a variety of reasons and that it is not always helpful or necessary to place blame on one party.

Finally, proponents of affirmative divorce argue that it can promote more amicable and cooperative separations, which can be particularly beneficial for families with children.

5. Argumentative essay about agreeing legalizing divorce. 5 paragraphs. introduction, 2 pros + 2 support, con + then conclusion.(Note: No going to google for help, but u can paraphrase)​


Divorce is the legal dissolution of a marriage. It is a process that allows couples to end their legal relationship and move on with their lives. While divorce may not be the ideal solution for every marriage, it should be legal and available to couples who need it. There are several compelling reasons to support the legalization of divorce.

First, divorce allows for individual freedom. The right to choose one's own path in life is a fundamental human right. When a marriage is no longer fulfilling or supportive, divorce allows individuals to pursue their own happiness and well-being. It also allows people to leave toxic or abusive relationships, which is crucial for their personal safety and well-being.

Second, divorce promotes healthy relationships. When a relationship is no longer working, it is better for all parties involved to end it and move on. By ending a relationship that is not fulfilling or supportive, people are able to find more suitable partners and build healthy and positive relationships. This is important for the well-being of all parties involved.

On the other hand, some argue that divorce can have negative impacts on children. It is true that children may experience emotional distress after a divorce, and it is important to consider their best interests when making decisions about divorce. However, there are ways in which parents can mitigate the negative impact of divorce on children, such as through co-parenting and therapy.

In conclusion, while divorce may not be the ideal solution for every marriage, it should be legal and available to couples who need it. Divorce allows for individual freedom, promotes healthy relationships, and can be managed in a way that minimizes the negative impact on children. It is a necessary option for couples who are unable to resolve their differences and move forward in a healthy and positive manner.

9. write an argumentative essay from the topic of:Should we legalize divorce in the philippines?​


Answer:

"Should We Legalize Divorce in the Philippines"

For the longest time, if I'm not mistaken, the party-list group Gabriela has been filing their own version of a divorce bill since they first entered the parliamentary arena. In the 18th Congress, they've already done so—again—as part of their key advocacies.

This time, they may yet succeed in getting what they've worked really hard to achieve. But maybe not exactly.

Banish the thought that the divorce bill approved recently by the House committee on population and family relations would pave the way for something like what Las Vegas has in place for a long time now: A no-fuss process that makes it quite easy for couples to disengage, or decouple.

According to Albay Rep. Edcel Lagman, author of one of three divorce bills approved at the committee level in the House of Representatives, the measure has very strict requirements that would not be an easy way out of a rocky marriage.

The lawmaker explained that their bills have safety measures to ensure that only “exceptional cases” of couples with “irremediably damaged marriage” will benefit from this measure.

These safety measures include the provision that a prosecutor would be assigned to determine within six months after filing of the divorce petition if the grounds are valid or if there is collusion between parties, and that the court would also apply a judicial dispute resolution mechanism and try to reconcile the parties within this period.

Divorce is necessary, Lagman pointed out, because the current options under the Family Code—legal separation, annulment of marriage and declaration of nullity—do not give complete reliefs to qualified parties. Legal separation only grants separation “in bed and board” but does not grant severance of marriage bond, while annulment would allow remarriage only on grounds prior to or simultaneous to the solemnization of marriage.

On the other hand, “divorce will allow grounds that are supervening or during the marriage like marital infidelity, domestic violence, chronic gambling and engagement in illegal drugs and at the same time allow re-marriage,” he explained, adding that it would also offer protection to children who suffer and witness daily torment and stress.

Over at the Senate, most of the senators are against legalizing divorce and instead prefer to streamline the annulment process “allowed by our faith and belief,” according to Senate Majority Leader Juan Miguel Zubiri.

"What we don’t like about divorce is it seems to be an easy way out." He said shortening the process of Church-decreed annulment would be a “more accepted” option for his colleagues in the Senate.


10. Argumentative essay about agreeing legalizing divorce. 5 paragraphs. introduction, 2 pros + 2 support, con + disproof then conclusion.(Note: No going to google for help, but u can paraphrase)​​


Divorce, the dissolution of a marriage, has long been a controversial topic and remains so in many societies today. While some people argue that divorce should be illegal and that marriage should be strictly forever, there are many compelling reasons to support the legalization of divorce.

One argument in favor of divorce is that it allows individuals to leave unhealthy or abusive relationships. Marriage should be a partnership based on love, mutual respect, and equality. However, in some cases, one partner may abuse or mistreat the other, leading to a toxic and potentially dangerous situation. In these cases, divorce can provide a way out for the victim and allow them to move on with their lives in safety and with dignity.

Another argument for divorce is that it allows for personal growth and happiness. People change over time, and it is not uncommon for individuals to grow apart from their partners. In these cases, staying in a marriage can lead to resentment and unhappiness for both parties. Divorce can provide an opportunity for both individuals to pursue their own interests and find happiness in new relationships or alone.

Additionally, divorce can benefit children by providing a better living environment. In cases where the parents are constantly fighting or have a toxic relationship, the children may suffer emotionally and psychologically. Allowing the parents to divorce and move on can create a more peaceful and stable home environment for the children.

It is also worth considering the economic benefits of divorce. When a couple remains in an unhappy or unhealthy marriage, they may be less productive and motivated, which can have negative consequences on their careers and overall financial well-being. Allowing divorce can enable individuals to move on and rebuild their lives, potentially leading to improved financial stability and success.

In conclusion, while some may argue that divorce should be illegal and that marriage should be forever, there are many compelling reasons to legalize divorce. Divorce can provide a way out of unhealthy or abusive relationships, allow for personal growth and happiness, benefit children by providing a better living environment, and have economic benefits. Therefore, it is important to recognize the value of divorce and to support its legalization.

In the Philippines, divorce is currently not allowed due to the strong influence of the Catholic Church, which believes in the sanctity of marriage. However, with the changing times and the increasing number of failed marriages, there is a growing need to legalize divorce in the country. I firmly believe that divorce should be legalized in the Philippines for the following reasons.

Firstly, not allowing divorce goes against the basic human right to seek happiness and fulfillment. Couples who are trapped in unhappy and abusive marriages should be given the option to dissolve their union legally, without being forced to stay together for the rest of their lives. Denying them this option is a violation of their basic human rights.

Secondly, legalizing divorce would provide protection for women who are often the victims of domestic violence and abuse. Women who are in abusive marriages are often unable to leave because they have no legal options. Divorce would give them a legal means to leave the abusive relationship and protect themselves and their children.

Thirdly, divorce would help reduce the stigma surrounding failed marriages. In the Philippines, there is still a strong cultural and religious stigma attached to divorce, which makes it difficult for those who have gone through it. Legalizing divorce would help remove this stigma and provide a more supportive environment for those who have experienced it.

In conclusion, legalizing divorce in the Philippines would provide an option for those trapped in unhappy and abusive marriages, protect women and children from domestic violence, and help reduce the stigma surrounding failed marriages. It is time for the government to recognize the need for divorce and provide a legal framework for it.
