Direct Speech

is written based on the speaker's direct words and using quotation marks  

Example

Hai asked, "Where are you going?

Manny replied, "I'm going home."

Reported Speech

Reported speech puts the speaker's words or ideas into a sentence without quotation marks. Noun clauses are usually used. In reported speech, the reader does not assume that the words are the speaker's exact words; often, they are a paraphrase of the speaker's words.

Example

Hai asked Manny where he was going.

Manny said he was going home.

Note: Use of the word "that" is optional in reported speech. Both of the following sentences are correct:

The child said that they were lost.

The child said they were lost.

27. "How I Manage My Stress" Direction: Create an essay about stressful situations at home, in school, or in the community. Describe how you deal with it. The essay should contain of at least 300 words. Do it on a one (1) whole sheet of paper. Criteria Content Organization Grammar Total 10 pts 5 pts 5 pts 20 pts​


Answer:

Stress is an unavoidable part of life. Whether it’s from school, work, family, or the community, stress can be overwhelming and hard to manage. I have experienced stress in all of these areas and have developed strategies to help me cope with it.

At home, I often feel overwhelmed by the demands of my family. My parents expect me to do well in school and help out around the house. This can be a lot to handle at times. To manage this stress, I try to stay organized and make lists of tasks that need to be done each day. This helps me stay on top of things and not get overwhelmed by all that needs to be done. I also take time for myself each day to relax and unwind so that I don’t become too stressed out.

In school, stress can come from a variety of sources such as exams, assignments, and deadlines. To manage this stress, I make sure that I am organized and plan ahead for upcoming tests or assignments. I also try to break down large tasks into smaller ones so that they are more manageable. Additionally, I make sure that I get enough sleep each night so that my mind is clear and focused when studying or taking exams.

In the community, stress can come from a variety of sources such as work or volunteer commitments or social obligations. To manage this stress, I try to prioritize my commitments so that I am not overburdened with too many tasks at once. Additionally, if something becomes too overwhelming for me then I will ask for help from friends or family members who may be able to lend a hand in completing the task at hand.

Overall, managing stress is an important part of life as it can have serious consequences if left unchecked. By staying organized and planning ahead for upcoming tasks or commitments, as well as taking time for myself each day to relax and unwind, I am able to better manage my stress levels in all areas of my life – home, school, and community alike!
