Answer:

The coronavirus disease (COVID19) pandemic has upset the lives of families around the world. School closures, remote work, physical distance-There are many things parents can navigate to find their way.

Make sure to establish a routine that takes into account age-appropriate educational programs that you can follow online, on TV, or on the radio. Also, take into account playing time and time spent reading. Use everyday activities as a learning opportunity for your child. And, as much as possible, don't forget to make these plans together.

Encourage the children questions and encourage them to express their feelings with you. Keep in mind that your child can react differently to stress. Please be patient and understand. Start by inviting your child to talk about the problem. Find out how much they already know and follow. Discuss good hygiene habits. You can use the moments of every day to emphasize the importance of washing your hands regularly and thoroughly. Make sure you are in a safe environment and allow your child to speak freely. Drawing, storytelling, and other activities can help you get started.

The digital platform provides children with the opportunity  to continue learning, playing with them and staying in touch with their friends. However, increased online access increases the risk to children's safety, protection and privacy. Discuss the Internet with your children to understand how the Internet works, what to look for, and how it works on your platform, such as video calls.

Stay in touch with your teacher or your child's school to find ways to stay focused, ask questions, and get more guidance.  Parent groups and community groups are also good ways to support each other in homeschooling.

Answer:

Interviewer: Could you please tell me in your own words what a concept paper is?

Interviewee 1: Sure. A concept paper is a document that outlines a particular idea. It generally includes a description of the idea itself, as well as some background information on why the idea is important and what it can be used for. The concept paper also usually provides some kind of roadmap for how the idea could be implemented.

Interviewer: That’s great. Could you provide a bit more detail? What types of information does a concept paper include beyond just the basic description and background of the idea?

Interviewee 1: Typically, the concept paper will also list various benefits or advantages to the idea, potential drawbacks, and potential solutions to the drawbacks. It may also include proposed objectives and research plans to help back up the proposed idea. Depending on the type of project, it could also include financial or budget analyses.

Ask the students to define the following words:

1. Hypothesis

2. Operation

3. Denotation

4. Connotation

1. Hypothesis: A hypothesis is a proposed explanation or prediction for a phenomenon that is based on limited evidence and subject to further testing and verification.

2. Operation: An operation is a process or series of actions that are performed in order to achieve a particular result.

3. Denotation: Denotation refers to the literal or dictionary definition of a word.

4. Connotation: Connotation refers to the emotional or cultural associations that a word carries in addition to its literal meaning.
