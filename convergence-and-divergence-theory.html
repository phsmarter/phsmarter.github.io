5. convergent and divergent boundaries? Essay​


Answer:

A convergent boundary (also known as a destructive boundary) is an area on Earth where two or more lithospheric plates collide. In plate tectonics, a divergent boundary or divergent plate boundary is a linear feature that exists between two tectonic plates that are moving away from each other. Divergent boundaries within continents initially produce rifts, which eventually become rift valleys.

10. CONVERGENTDIVERGENTTRANSFORM​


Answer:

Divergent boundaries -- where new crust is generated as the plates pull away from each other. Convergent boundaries -- where crust is destroyed as one plate dives under another. Transform boundaries -- where crust is neither produced nor destroyed as the plates slide horizontally past each other.

Convergent,Divergent, and Transform Boundaries. In the theory of plate tectonics, the earth's crust is broken into plates that move around relative to each other. As a result of this movement, three types of plate boundaries are formed:divergent, convergent, and transformboundaries.

12. divergent vs convergent evolution​


Answer:

Convergent evolution is when two species with different ancestral origins develop similar characteristics, while divergent evolution refers to when two species diverge from a common ancestor and develop different characteristics.

14. Magma, Is it Divergent or Convergent?​


Answer:

At convergent boundaries magma is formed where water from a subducting plate acts as a flux to lower the melting temperature of the adjacent mantle rock. At divergent boundaries magma forms because of decompression melting.
