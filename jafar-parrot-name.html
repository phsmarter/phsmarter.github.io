5. The ratio of Jafar's savings to Siva's savings is 4 : 7. If Jafar spends 58 of his money, he will have $88 less than Siva. How much is Siva's savings?


Let's start by setting up an equation for Jafar's savings (J) and Siva's savings (S) using the given ratio:

J/S = 4/7

We can simplify this ratio by multiplying both sides by a common factor to get whole numbers:

J:S = 4x:7x

Next, we know that if Jafar spends $58, he will have $88 less than Siva. We can use this information to set up another equation:

J - 58 = S - 88

Now we can substitute the first equation into the second equation to solve for S:

4x - 58 = 7x - 88

Simplifying this equation, we get:

3x = 30

x = 10

So the ratio of Jafar's savings to Siva's savings is 4x:7x = 40:70 = 4:7.

To find Siva's savings, we can substitute x = 10 into the equation:

S = 7x = 7(10) = $70

Therefore, Siva's savings is $70.

20. The ratio of Jafar's savings to Siva's savings is 4 : 7. If Jafar spends 58 of his money, he will have $88 less than Siva. How much is Siva's savings?


Step-by-step explanation:

From the problem statement, we know that the ratio of Jafar's savings to Siva's savings is 4:7, which can be expressed as:

J:S = 4:7

This means that we can write J as 4x and S as 7x for some value of x.

If Jafar spends 58 of his money, he will have $88 less than Siva. This can be written as an equation:

4x - 58 = 7x - 88

Simplifying and solving for x, we get:

3x = 30

x = 10

So Jafar has 4x = 4(10) = $40 saved, and Siva has 7x = 7(10) = $70 saved.

Therefore, Siva's savings are $70.

Parrots are often kept as pets and are native to tropical and subtropical regions around the world. They are known for their colorful feathers, sharp beaks, and zygodactyl feet (with four toes, two pointing forward and two pointing backward).
