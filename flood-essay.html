13. The following are types of flood except for one. Select one: a. glacial lake outburst flood b. all are types of flood c. ice jams d. urban flood


Floods:

Answer:

Explanation:

Flood as described by the Natural Risk Reduction Management is the most common natural disaster which is characterized by a temporary overflowing of water into dry lands due to coastal storms, rain, snow, storm surges, and overflowing of dams and other water systems. Floods may cause damages in buildings, disrupt in transportation, landslides, and outages.

Definition of flood: https://brainly.ph/question/699584

Three Common Types of Flood: coastal fluvial pluvial

Coastal flood refers to the flooding that occurs in land areas along the coast by seawater. Common causes of coastal floods are intense windstorm events happening at the same time. The severity of a coastal flood is determined by several factors such as the direction, size, speed, and the strength of the windstorm. The onshore and offshore structure also plays a significant role. To determine the likelihood and degree of a storm surge, coastal flood models study this information in addition to data from past storms that have impacted the area.

Examples: storm surge tsunami

Fluvial flood refers to the flooding that occurs when the water level in the lake, river, or stream rises and overflows onto the surrounding banks, neighboring land, and shores. The water level rise could be due to excessive rain or snow melt. The damage cause by fluvial flood can be extensive as the overflow affects smaller rivers downstream which might cause dams and dikes to break and engulf neighboring areas. To determine the likelihood of fluvial flooding, fluvial flood models study current river levels, forecasted precipitation, past precipitation, and soil and terrain conditions.

The severity of fluvial flood is determined by the duration and volume of rainfall in the catchment area of the river. Other factors include soil water wetness due to previous rainfall, and the terrain surrounding the river system. In smoother areas, floodwater tends to increase more slowly and be shallower, and it often remains for days. In hilly or mountainous areas, floods can occur within minutes after a heavy rain, drain very quickly, and cause damage due to debris flow.

Example: ice jams

Pluvial flood refers to the flooding that occurs when a great rainfall event creates a flood independent of an overflowing water body. A common misunderstanding about flood is that you must be located near a body of water to be at risk. But pluvial flooding can happen anywhere urban or rural; even in areas with no water bodies in the vicinity.  

Two Common Types of Pluvial Flooding: flash floods surface water floods

Flash floods are characterized by a high velocity and intense velocity torrent of water caused by torrential rain falling within a short amount of time within the vicinity or on nearby elevated terrain. They can also occur through sudden discharge of water from an upstream bank or dam. Flash floods are very risky and damaging not only because of the force of the water, but also the dashing debris that is often brushed up in the flow.

Example: glacial lake outburst urban floods

Surface water floods occur when an urban drainage system is flooded and water flows out into streets and nearby structures. It occurs slowly that is why people can still have enough time to move to safer locations, and the level of water is usually shallow. It makes no direct threat to lives but may cause considerable financial damage.

The subject of the sentence is “the banks” and is plural.

According to the Subject-Verb aggreement, if the subject is plural the verb is also plural, and if it is singular and so as the verb.

The verb there is the word “flood”. The plural form of verbs doesn’t have ‘s’ on them unlike nouns. So, flood is the answer and not floods.

Natural events such as floods and landslides can have severe consequences. The risks are expected to increase, both as a consequence of climate change and due to increased vulnerabilities, especially in urban areas. Although preventive measures are often cost-effective, some measures are beneficial to certain values, while some may have negative impacts on other values. The aim of the study presented here was to investigate two frameworks used for assessing the effectiveness and sustainability of physical and nonphysical flood and landslide risk reduction measures. The study is based on literature, available information from authorities and municipalities, expert knowledge and experience, and stakeholder views and values. The results indicate that the risks for suboptimization or maladaptation are reduced if many aspects are included and a broad spectrum of stakeholders are involved. The sustainability assessment tools applied here can contribute to a more transparent and sustainable risk management process by assessing strategies and interventions with respect to both short- and long-term perspectives, including local impacts and wider environmental impacts caused by climate change, for example. The tools can also cover social and economic aspects. The assessment tools provide checklists that can support decision processes, thus allowing for more transparent decisions.
