15. One of the easiest ways to be your own boss is to sell for an established brand. A. freelance or consultB. independently Sell for brandC. Promote Your Small BusinessD. Franchise​


Answer:

D. Franchise

Explanation:

Franchising is a business strategy used for expansion. In this strategy, prospect "part-owners", or franchisors of a brand are trained and equipped as part of their license to use an established brand and sell on that brand's behalf. Popular franchisees in the Philippines are food outlets such as Jollibee and McDonalds and convenient stores such as 711 and Mini-Stop.

At a fee, the franchisor won't have to spend money and effort on advertising for the products and services or worry about inventory and supplies, as the these are all taken cared off by the franchisee. The franchisor instead can focus on other critical aspects of the business, such as day to day operation of the store. Franchisors are also given the permission and license to use the business model of the franchisor, saving time, cost, and effort in studying on how to make the business successful.

19. the democratic boss advantage and disadvantage ​


In a democratic system, having a boss can be both a blessing and a curse.

One advantage of having a boss is that they can be a powerful ally and advocate for your interests, especially if they owe their position to your support. They can help you gain access to resources and opportunities that may not be available to others.

However, having a boss can also be a disadvantage as they may use their power to control and manipulate you. They may demand loyalty and obedience, even if it goes against your own values and interests. This can be particularly problematic if the boss is corrupt or has questionable motives.

Answer:

----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Advantages:

Efficient decision-making: The boss system allows for a quick and efficient decision-making process since a single person is responsible for making important decisions.

Strong leadership: The boss system can provide strong and effective leadership, which is essential for a democratic society to function efficiently.

Patronage: Democratic bosses can provide patronage, or the distribution of benefits, which can help maintain their political power and support from the community.

Disadvantages:

Lack of transparency: The boss system can lack transparency since a single person is responsible for making decisions, which can lead to decisions being made behind closed doors without input or accountability from the public.

Corruption: The boss system can be susceptible to corruption since the boss may be able to use their power to gain personal or financial benefits.

Limited democracy: The boss system can limit democracy since the boss may be able to control elections, limit the opposition's ability to run for office, or manipulate the political process in their favor.

Overall, the democratic boss system has both advantages and disadvantages, and its effectiveness depends on how well it is implemented and managed.

25. the sensitive boss advantage and disadvantage ​


The following are some benefits and drawbacks of working for a sensitive boss:

Advantages:

Empathy: Empathy and understanding of an employee's emotional needs can be demonstrated by a sensitive boss. A supportive work environment in which employees feel valued and appreciated can result from this.

Communication: A delicate manager is probably going to be a decent communicator, as they comprehend the significance of clear correspondence to forestall false impressions and clashes.

Creativity: Because they can think outside the box and come up with unique solutions to problems, sensitive bosses are frequently creative and innovative.

Disadvantages:

Over-emotional: Overreacting emotionally can be a sign of a sensitive boss, making them appear unprofessional and undermining their authority.

Indecisiveness: Because they may be hesitant to upset the delicate balance of relationships within the team or to hurt people's feelings, sensitive bosses may have difficulty making difficult decisions.

Possibilities for criticism: A boss who is sensitive might take criticism personally, which could make them defensive and not want to hear good advice.

Depending on how they handle their emotions and communicate with their staff, having a sensitive boss can be a blessing or a curse.
