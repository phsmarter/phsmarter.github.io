3. Will all combinations of metals and nonmetals form ionic bond why or why not


Chemical Compounds

Multiple atom types that are joined by chemical bonds make up compounds. Compounds can decompose into simpler substances through chemical processes. Compounds include things like water (H2O) and table salt (NaCl)

Why or why not will all mixtures of metals and nonmetals form ionic bonds?

NO, not all metal-nonmetal mixtures produce ionic bonds. Ionic bonds can only form between substances with electronegativity differences greater than 1.9.

Ionic bonds are created by the attraction of charged particles known as ions. Positive and negative charges are both attracted to one another in ions.

Anions are negatively charged, while Cations are positively charged. Polyatomic ions, which are more complex cations and anions, can also be found in ionic compounds. Sulfate (SO4 2) and ammonium (NH4 +1), for example, are polyatomic ions, which are composed of two or more linked atoms.

Covalent bonds are formed by nonmetallic atoms. Molecules are made when nonmetallic atoms of various kinds share electrons.

Examples include glucose (C6H12O6) and water (H2O). Covalent compounds can be as small as two atoms or as large as tens of thousands of atoms. Some of the biggest covalent compounds are composed of biological components like proteins.

Metallic Bonds: In a sea of negatively charged electrons, positive metal ions become enmeshed to create metallic bonds.

Metals of various sorts bind together to produce alloys. Due to the fact that the different atom types do not mix together in a predetermined ratio, an alloy is NOT a chemical compound. Mixtures make up alloys.

23. Will all combinations of metals and non-metals form an ionic bond? Why or why not?


All combinations of metals and non-metals form an ionic bond. Thus, the answer to this statement is yes. This is for the reason that ions form after ionic bonding between metals and nonmetals.

Elements form a chemical bond to be able to have an eight valence electron in their valence shell to become stable.

In forming a chemical bond, metals tend to give their electrons resulting to metals having more protons than electron forming a positive ion. Thus metallic elements form cations after ionic bond.

On the other hand, non-metals can only accept electrons but not give them thus having more electrons than proton forming a negative ion.  

When metal bond with non-metals, this chemical bond forms a positive and a negative ion. With the formation of the ions, we can tell that ionic bond take place between metals and nonmetals.
