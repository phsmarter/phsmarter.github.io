Answer:

My sibling (younger sister) is driven, intelligent, passionate, empathetic and has the strongest work ethic of anyone I know her age (20).

Immediately upon graduating high school, she joined the workforce and has since moved up the ranks considerably.

She is a coffee connoisseur (literally spends about half of her paycheck buying herself Starbucks throughout the week).

She is extremely disorganized (I haven’t seen her bedroom floor in years).

She is obsessed with her cat - who is as old as dust.

She has a spectacular relationship with my daughter - I could’t possibly have imagined what an amazing aunt she would turn out to be.

She is a Star Wars junkie, through and through.

She loved Doctor Who.

She hated high school, with a passion.

She has never done any mind altering substance (other than coffee), and never plans to (she learned from my mistakes).

She is NEVER on time, for anything, ever.

She has never had any type of speeding/parking ticket, or been cited for any infraction whatsoever.

She is very open-minded, and doesn’t judge or dislike any groups of people.

When I need her, she is there - always.

Her nickname is Cooter, which she despises.

She has a sense of maturity and responsibility that far surpasses my own,

and a heart of gold to go with it.

Explanation:

Usually, the last word in the analogy is blank. You need to figure out what the relationship is. In this case, the relationship between a kitten and a cat is a young and adult animal, so you can complete the analogy like this: kitten is to cat as puppy is to dog. Analogies can show many different types of word relationships.
