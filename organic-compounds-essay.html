ORGANIC COMPOUND

Organic molecules are made up of carbon atoms that are covalently bound to hydrogen, frequently oxygen, and other elements. They are created by living organisms and can be found in soils, seas, commercial items, and every cell of the human body. Carbohydrates, lipids, proteins, and nucleotides are the four categories most vital to human structure and function. You must first grasp the chemistry of carbon before you can investigate these molecules.

Carbon's Chemical Make-Up

The chemistry of organic molecules' carbon core is what makes them so common. Remember that carbon atoms have four electrons in their valence shell, and the octet rule demands that atoms react in such a way that their valence shell is completed with eight electrons. By donating or absorbing four electrons, carbon atoms do not complete their valence shells. Instead, they share electrons easily through covalent connections.

Carbohydrates

Carbohydrate is short for "hydrated carbon," which comes from the root hydro-, which means "water." A carbohydrate is a molecule made up of carbon, hydrogen, and oxygen; hydrogen and oxygen are found in most carbohydrates in the same two-to-one ratio as they are in water. In actuality, a "generic" carbohydrate molecule has the chemical formula (CH2O)n.

Monosaccharides

A monosaccharide is a carbohydrate monomer. The human body relies on five monosaccharides. Three of them are hexose sugars, so named because each one has six carbon atoms.

11. what are the importance of organic and inorganic compounds in your daily life? essay​


Organic and inorganic compounds are both important in our daily lives. Organic compounds, which are compounds that contain carbon, are the basis of all living things and are essential for our survival. They include important biological molecules like carbohydrates, lipids, and proteins, which are necessary for energy, cell structure, and metabolism.

Inorganic compounds, on the other hand, are compounds that do not contain carbon and are not produced by living organisms. They are important because they are the building blocks of many materials that we use in our daily lives, such as metals, glass, and concrete. They are also important in many industrial and technological processes, such as the production of fertilizers, pesticides, and medications.

In short, organic and inorganic compounds are both vital for our health, well-being, and the functioning of our society. Without them, many of the things we rely on in our daily lives would not be possible.
