5. How did Age of Enlightenment and free trade help the rise of Filipino nationalism?


Answer:

The Age of Enlightenment and free trade played significant roles in the rise of Filipino nationalism. The Age of Enlightenment was a period of intellectual and philosophical growth in Europe in the 17th and 18th centuries. This period emphasized reason, science, and individualism, and it influenced the development of political and social ideas that challenged traditional authority and promoted democracy and freedom.

The ideas of the Age of Enlightenment spread to the Philippines through Spanish colonial rule, and they inspired Filipino intellectuals to question the legitimacy of Spanish authority and to demand greater political and economic rights. These intellectuals, known as ilustrados, used the ideas of the Enlightenment to argue for the need for a more democratic and just society.

Free trade also played a significant role in the rise of Filipino nationalism. During the 19th century, the Philippines was opened up to international trade, and this led to the growth of a merchant class that became increasingly aware of the economic exploitation of the country by foreign powers. This exploitation fueled nationalist sentiment among Filipinos, who demanded greater economic autonomy and control over their resources.

In conclusion, the Age of Enlightenment and free trade helped the rise of Filipino nationalism by inspiring intellectuals to question traditional authority and by exposing the economic exploitation of the country by foreign powers. These factors contributed to the development of a sense of national identity and a desire for greater political and economic autonomy among Filipinos.

16. discuss the change in the 19th century and relate it to the rise of nationalism among the Filipino.​


Answer:

Filipino nationalism refers to the awakening and support towards a political identity associated with modern Philippines leading to a wide-ranging campaign for political, social, and economic freedom in the Philippines. This gradually emerged out of various political and armed movements throughout most of the Spanish East Indies—albeit has long been fragmented and inconsistent with contemporary definitions of such nationalism—as a consequence of more than three centuries of Spanish rule. These movements are characterized by the upsurge of anti-colonialist sentiments and ideals which peaked in the late 19th century led mostly by the ilustrado or landed, educated elites, whether peninsulares, insulares, or native (Indio). This served as the backbone of the first nationalist revolution in Asia, the Philippine Revolution of 1896.[1] The modern concept would later be fully actualized upon the inception of a Philippine state with its contemporary borders after being granted independence by the United States by the 1946 Treaty of Manila.

30. Explain the British occupation ignited the rise of the Filipino Nationalism.​


Answer

For two centuries, many of our ancestors had the colonial mentality that the Spaniards were the strongest force in the globe. That is until, in 1762, the Kingdom of Great Britain occupied Manila and nearby ports of Cavite for twenty months. The British occupation lasted until 1764. The defeat of Spain and France to Great Britain in the Seven Year War showed our ancestors that the Spaniards can be defeated. Eventually, Spain opened the Philippines to the world, and in 1834 accepted foreign traders. This series of events led to the circulation of Liberalism Ideas from Europe into the Philippines.
