Indoor recreational activities are the things that you do for fun and enjoyment in the comfort of your home, the gym, or any other covered area. Some examples are: going to the gym, playing board games, doing indoor yoga, going to art museums etc.

13. 1. discuss the nature and background of indoor and outdoor recreational activities 2. differentiate indoor from outdoor recreational activities, and 3 share their personal experience in the different activities​


Answer:

1 . Nature and Background of Indoor and Outdoor Recreational Activities:

Recreational activities are any activities done for enjoyment, relaxation, or leisure. These activities can be categorized into indoor and outdoor recreational activities.

Indoor recreational activities are activities that are done inside a building or a closed environment. These activities can include board games, video games, indoor sports, reading, watching movies, and so on. Indoor recreational activities became more prevalent with the advent of the industrial revolution, as people moved from rural to urban areas and needed to find ways to entertain themselves indoors.

Outdoor recreational activities, on the other hand, are activities that are done in the natural environment, outside of a building. These activities can include hiking, camping, swimming, skiing, cycling, fishing, and so on. Outdoor recreational activities have been a part of human culture for thousands of years, with ancient societies engaging in hunting, fishing, and other outdoor activities for survival.

2. Differences between Indoor and Outdoor Recreational Activities:

Indoor and outdoor recreational activities differ in several ways:

a) Environment: Indoor activities are done in a controlled, artificial environment, while outdoor activities take place in a natural environment that is often less predictable.

b) Physical Demands: Outdoor activities are typically more physically demanding than indoor activities. Outdoor activities often require more endurance, strength, and coordination than indoor activities.

c) Equipment: Outdoor activities often require specialized equipment such as hiking boots, tents, and fishing gear, while indoor activities usually require less specialized equipment.

d) Weather: Outdoor activities are affected by weather conditions, while indoor activities are not. Weather can greatly impact the safety and enjoyment of outdoor recreational activities.

e) Social Interaction: Indoor activities are often done in groups, while outdoor activities can be done alone or with others. Outdoor recreational activities often provide opportunities for social interaction with others who share similar interests.

Overall, both indoor and outdoor recreational activities provide opportunities for enjoyment and relaxation, but they differ in the environment, physical demands, equipment, weather, and social interaction involved.
