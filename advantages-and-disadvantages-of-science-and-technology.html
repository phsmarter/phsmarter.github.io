16. What are the advantages and disadvantages of science and technology?​


Answer:

Advantages of science and technology include:

Improved quality of life: Science and technology have led to the development of new medical treatments, as well as improved sanitation and communication technologies, which have greatly improved the overall quality of life for people around the world.

Increased knowledge: Through scientific research, we have gained a greater understanding of the world around us and have been able to make new discoveries and advancements in various fields.

Economic growth: Technology has led to increased productivity and efficiency, which has contributed to economic growth and development.

Environmental protection: Science and technology have also played a key role in protecting the environment and addressing climate change.

Disadvantages of science and technology include:

Job displacement: As technology advances, certain jobs may become obsolete, leading to job displacement and unemployment.

Privacy concerns: The widespread use of technology has led to concerns about privacy and the potential for misuse of personal information.

Dependence on technology: People's dependence on technology has increased and if technology fails, it can disrupt people's daily lives.

Environmental impact: Some technologies and industries can have negative impacts on the environment, such as pollution and the depletion of natural resources.

Health hazards: Certain technologies and industries can also have negative effects on human health, such as the release of harmful chemicals and radiation.
