14. Characteristics of early adulthood stage​


Answer:Early adulthood (also called "young adulthood") is a stage of life between 18 and 30 years, when grown ups become more independent and explore different life possibilities. For example, in the EU member states, a young adult is a person from 18 to 30 years old. In other regions of the world, this definition can vary. In early adulthood (ages 20–40), our physical abilities are at their peak, including muscle strength, reaction time, sensory abilities, and cardiac functioning. The aging process also begins during early adulthood and is characterized by changes in skin, vision, and reproductive capability.

23. Having mastered the developmental tasks of early childhood, middle and late childhood, and adolescence, reflect on what you should do as a future teacher to facilitate your student’s acquisition of these developmental tasks.


As a future teacher, understanding the different development tasks across the life stages of the learners is essential. It guides the teacher in designing lessons and activities that are appropriate to the developmental tasks of the learners.

Teacher’s Role and Learner’s Developmental Tasks

Developmental tasks are the behaviors and activities that develop at or around a certain stage in life. Each life stage has corresponding developmental tasks that need to be done by the learners. It covers their developmental tasks (Havighurst), cognitive development (Piaget), psychosexual development (Freud), psychosocial development (Erikson), and moral development (Kohlberg). Knowledge of various developmental tasks helps teachers respond to the needs of their learners. Understanding the stages of growth is essential for providing teachers with the support they need as they work to improve their effectiveness in the classroom.Teaching styles and activities and their level of complexity should be based on the developmental stages of the learners and the expected skills that they are supposed to develop in the life stage where they are in. Teachers also should motivate their learners in mastering, coping, and learning the developmental tasks expected of them. Having sufficient knowledge of these would help them to understand and respond to their learner’s individual needs.

Future teachers should be equipped with sufficient knowledge and understanding of the development theories, learning theories, and their application in the teaching and learning process. These theories are crucial in developing learning activities and in addressing the needs of the learners.

29. what are the developmental stages of childhood and adulthood?​


Answer:

Human Development

Infancy

Infancy, typically the first year of life, is the first important stage of human development. Many physical milestones occur during this stage as an infant gains control over its body. However, infants must rely on others to meet most of their needs. They learn to trust other people as needs are met. They need to feel this security in order to properly develop both physically and emotionally.

Childhood

The next stage of human development is childhood, during which children start to explore and develop a sense of independence. Eventually, children learn to make their own decisions and they discover that their actions have consequences. As they learn and grow, they develop a sense of self. Children need to be nurtured so that they develop self-confidence instead self-esteem issues. Achieving a healthy level of self-confidence helps children stay motivated to achieve. A child also needs guidance as they begin to test out new skills and gain confidence in their decision-making.

Adolescence

During childhood, children begin to develop a sense of self and independence, and this process continues in the next stage of human development. During adolescence, young men and women are primarily concerned with finding their identity and expressing who they are in the world. Puberty causes many physical changes to take place, and adolescents must adapt to their changing bodies. All of these changes can make adolescence a confusing and stressful period. As adolescents try to find their place, they may experiment with different roles and make attempts to separate from authority figures. They are getting used to their bodies and trying to find out where they belong. They may try out different hairstyles and hobbies in an attempt to create an image of themselves they're comfortable with.
