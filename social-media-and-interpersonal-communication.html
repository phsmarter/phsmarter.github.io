1.The Sender

Communication begins with a source, a person, group, or organization with meaning to share. Then, encoding occurs on the sender side. It’s an invisible process during which a message transitions from thought to language.

2. The Receiver

We all play this communication role — some better than others. If we do it well, we are skilled in the fine art of listening. To make that happen, there’s an invisible process going on known as decoding — the conversion of language into thought and meaning.

3. Encoding and Decoding

Here’s where the magic occurs. To transmit meaning, the sender must place meaning into a series of symbols (schemata) that represent concepts known to the receiver. While in the decoding process, symbols convert into concepts and ideas meaning something to the receiver.

4. The Medium

A sender chooses some type of communication medium. The three classic communication transmission channels are: written, verbal and electronic.

5. Receiver

A receiver is the individual, group, or organization decoding the message.

6. Interference (or Noise)

Sometimes referred to as static or itnterference, it affects both listening or hearing. A shortlist includes encoding problems on both the sender side, bad timing, failure to get feedback, etc. But there is a longer list of 17 communication barriers.

7. Feedback

Great communicators make use of the nonverbal channels the average person is not aware of. Feedback should flow back to the sender continuously; but this is rarely the case.

23. Please Explain All This . Facilitation of communityengagement . Improved sense ofcommunity and community competence. Interpersonal benefits . Coalition building> Strengthens emotional ties and social support​


Answer:

Facilitating Community Engagement: This refers to the process of creating opportunities and removing barriers to encourage individuals in a community to engage in collective activities and decision-making processes that affect their community. Fostering community involvement empowers individuals to work together toward common goals and make decisions that benefit the community as a whole.

Improved sense of community and community competence: A sense of community refers to the sense of belonging and connectedness that individuals have to their community. Community competence, on the other hand, is the belief that the community has the skills and resources to address its challenges and improve its quality of life. When community engagement is effectively facilitated, it can lead to an improved sense of community and community competence as individuals work toward common goals and develop a sense of ownership and pride in their community.

Interpersonal Benefits: Fostering community engagement can also to interpersonal benefits, such as increased social capital and trust. Social capital relates to networks, norms, and trust that facilitate collaboration and coordination between individuals and groups. When individuals in a community work toward common goals, they build social capital and trust, which can help them address future challenges and improve their quality of life.

Coalition Building: Coalition building refers to the process of bringing diverse groups and individuals together to work towards common goals. Building coalitions allows individuals and groups to leverage their strengths and resources to make a difference together. Facilitating community engagement can help build coalitions by creating opportunities for individuals and groups to come together, share perspectives, and work toward common goals.

Strengthens emotional bonds and social support: Finally, facilitating engagement can in the community also strengthen emotional bonds and social support among individuals in the community. When individuals work toward common goals, they develop a sense of camaraderie and mutual support, which can help them overcome challenges and improve their overall well-being. Strong emotional ties and social support can also contribute to a sense of belonging and connectedness, which can improve overall community health and resilience.
