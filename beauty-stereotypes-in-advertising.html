25. Directions: On a short bond paper, draw/paste an advertisement that usesstereotype images/ideas. State what particular viewpoint or opinion isexpressed in the advertisement.​


Explanation:

This one of the ads from Mr. Clean is an example of basic gender stereotypes. The advertisement depicts a woman and her child cleaning, on what's supposed to be a holiday, Mother's Day. They show that mothers should clean and look after their family even in their special day, and a reminder to them that their role in the household is to clean and do housework, which I think is very offensive for women, and also offensive for men. It's implying that men don't do any of the housework or help out with the cleaning.

This commercial is a prime example of the patterns that emerge in gender and advertising. First, ads tell us that there is a big difference between what is appropriate behaviour for men and women, or for boys and girls. This ad is obviously telling us that the appropriate behaviour for women and girls is to do the housework and be happy about it (both the mother and daughter are smiling and pointing at what I’m assuming used to be a dirty spot on the wall.) Second, advertising and other mass media inculcate in consumers the cultural assumption that men are dominant and women are passive and subordinate.Again, where is her husband and why is he not the one cleaning the dirty spot off the wall? This ad is teaching the consumer that this product is made for women because women are supposedly the only ones that do the cleaning in the household. This ad reinforces both patterns in gender and advertising and also reinforces many gender stereotypes and gender roles.

Source: http://newsactivist.com/en/articles/gendered-world-views-section-8-fall-2015/hierarchy-housework
