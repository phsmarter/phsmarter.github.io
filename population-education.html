3. ano ang population education?


Population education is important in countries that are in danger of overpopulation because it informs students on population trends and aims to promote having smaller families. Educating students on population dynamics was introduced in many Asian countries during the 1970s.
     
       Human overpopulation occurs when the amount of people living in an area outgrows what the environment can sustain with food, water, living space or other vital resources. The amount an environment can sustain is known as its carrying capacity. In areas of the world where the carrying capacity is threatened or exceeded, education may encourage young people to grow up and have fewer children. Understanding the danger is thought to help reduce the problem.

25. . A correlation study is conducted on a sample to determine whether educational level and income in the population are related.


A correlational study between educational level and income in the population is Inferential statistics. Inferential statistics are used for drawing conclusions and/or making decisions concerning a population based only on sample data.

Further explanation:This is a Preference study “Relationship between Education and Income” by Monnappa Somanna from May 14th, 2016.

First Introduction

Using data from the General Social Survey (GSS), this study examines the association between the greatest LEVEL OF EDUCATION achieved by US people and their FAMILY INCOME in constant dollars.

Both students and policy officials should take note of the study mentioned above. It aids in the creation and implementation of policies that promote educational access. Additionally, it aids students in comprehending the long-term advantages of investing in quality education and how it has an effect on how income is distributed so that they can have quality lives in the future.

The study mentioned above encourages discussion on how education affects income distribution and how to improve society.

DataData collection

Data from the 2012 General Social Survey (GSS) are used in the analysis. Since not every respondent provided an answer to every question in every year of the surveys, the GSS data was compiled using a cumulative data file for surveys completed between 1972 and 2012.

Cases

This dataset has 114 variables and 57,061 instances in total.

Variables

Variable 1 is a categorical variable called "DEGREE" and represents respondents' highest degree.

Variable-2: "CONINC," which is a recurring numerical variable, stands for "Total Family Income in Constant Dollars."

Quantity of inference

The entire US population is the target population in terms of generalizability. During the survey, random samples are chosen. As a result, conclusions from the study can be applied to the whole US population.

Since this is observational research rather than an experiment, the data cannot be utilized to prove a causal relationship between the variables of interest.

Potential biases that could limit generalizability

Given that the data was gathered through the use of a survey approach, there may be response and convenience bias. When making results from this study, these biases need to be taken into consideration.

InferenceDefnining Null & Alternate Hypotheisis

The null hypothesis is that there is no discernible difference in the median incomes of different groups according to greatest educational attainment; in other words, there is no relationship between highest educational attainment and income level. H0: Mu(JC) = Mu(B) = Mu(LHS) = Mu(HS) (G)

Alternative Hypothesis: Based on the highest education level, there is a considerable difference in the means of income across various groups. In other words, income level is affected by education level. HA: Some (or all) groups have different average yearly incomes in constant dollars I

Using the Analysis of Variance (ANOVA) approach, we tested our hypothesis.

To ascertain whether there are any appreciable variations in the means of three or more independent (unrelated) groups, the one-way analysis of variance (ANOVA) is utilized.

The one-way ANOVA analyzes the means of the groups we are interested in and assesses if any of those means differ from one another significantly. This strategy works well since we are taking into account one categorical variable, education, and one numerical variable, income.

The alternative hypothesis (HA), which states that there are at least 2 group means that are significantly different from one another, is accepted if the one-way ANOVA yields a significant result.

Reflection

The study finds a relationship between family income in constant dollars and educational attainment among Americans. Since we used GSS Survey data for FY12, the aforementioned study can be applied to all residents of the United States.

In order to visually explore the data, we divided the family income in constant dollars by the highest degree the interviewees had attained (less than high school, high school, junior college, bachelor's, and graduate). We found a positive association between the two variables.

During the data exploration step, we saw a few outliers, which is a sign that income and other factors have a high link. We must be cautious when interpreting the results because several of the requirements for the statistical inference methods used were not fully met.
