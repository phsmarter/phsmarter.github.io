29. Understand the true meaning of this phrase.Naked Woman, Black Woman,I sing your beauty that passes, the form that i fix in the Eternal,Before jealous fate turn you to ashes to feed the roots of life.​


Answer:

The phrase "Naked Woman, Black Woman, I sing your beauty that passes" appears to be a poetic tribute to the beauty and fleeting nature of the female form, specifically referring to a naked black woman. The speaker of the poem is expressing admiration for the woman's beauty, and attempting to capture or "fix" it in a way that will make it eternal. The phrase "the form that I fix in the Eternal" suggests the speaker sees the woman's beauty as something that will live on beyond her physical form.

The phrase "before jealous fate turn you to ashes to feed the roots of life" suggests that the speaker is aware of the fleeting nature of life and the inevitability of death. He is expressing a fear that the woman's beauty will one day be destroyed and that her physical form will be reduced to ashes. The "jealous fate" may represent the idea that life is unfair, that fate is arbitrary, and death can come for anyone, at any moment.

In general, the phrase is a romantic and melancholic tribute, that tries to fix the beauty of the woman in something that will last forever. The metaphor of "ashes" also has deeper cultural meanings in many traditions that ashes are associated with rebirth and the cycle of life.
