Rogerian strategy focuses on the “open exchange of ideas directed toward mutual understanding” with emphasis on conceding certain points to gain an understanding of the opposition and in doing so gain ground rather than losing it through a hostile exchange of right and wrong (Cooper/Patton 70). Swift carefully organized his essay so the audience, the English Aristocracy, would not recognize it as satire and dismiss it right away. Swift begins with a quasi-believable tone, one of an economist trying to solve a problem. The current “deplorable state of the kingdom” calculated by Swift consists of one hundred twenty thousand children who need to steel and beg just to remain alive (Swift 298). Many before him tried to provide useful solutions but failed. The Irish now left with nothing but what the English give them suffer mass oppression, the real issue Swift wishes to address.

Swift establishes a mutual understanding with the English from the beginning, an essential part of the careful construction in his essay. He cannot let on the essay will take a dramatic turn after the flip of the second page. Swift does this because he wants to give the impression that he shares the same views on the current condition of the kingdom. He wants the English aristocracy to identify with him and his views. 28. reflection in narrative essay about losing a mother​


Answer:

Losing a mother is an experience that is difficult to put into words. It is a profound and life-changing event that can shake you to your core. As someone who has experienced this loss, I can attest to the fact that it is a journey filled with ups and downs, and one that is unique to each individual.

When I lost my mother, it felt like a piece of me had been taken away. She was the one person who had always been there for me, the one who had nurtured and supported me throughout my life. Her absence was palpable, and I struggled to come to terms with the fact that she was no longer with me.

In the early days, the grief was overwhelming. I found myself struggling to get out of bed, to do even the simplest tasks. It was as if my world had been turned upside down, and I was left adrift in a sea of emotions. I felt angry, sad, and lost all at once.

As time passed, however, I began to find some solace in the memories I had of my mother. I realized that while she was no longer physically with me, she would always be a part of me. I found myself thinking about her more and more, recalling the moments we had shared and the lessons she had taught me. I found that I was able to hold onto her in my heart, even as she was no longer here.

Of course, there were still moments of intense sadness and longing. Birthdays, holidays, and special occasions were particularly difficult, as I was reminded of all the things my mother would never get to see or experience with me. But even in those moments, I was able to find comfort in the knowledge that she had loved me deeply, and that her memory would always be a source of strength and inspiration for me.

In the end, losing my mother was a painful experience, but it also taught me a great deal about the power of love, the resilience of the human spirit, and the importance of cherishing the moments we have with the people we love. I will always miss my mother, but I will also always carry her with me, in my heart and in my memories.
