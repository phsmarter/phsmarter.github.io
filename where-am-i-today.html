Answer:

1. Am I doing what I love? This question helps you evaluate if you are pursuing a career or engaging in activities that align with your passions and interests. When you do what you love, work doesn't feel like a burden, and you feel fulfilled.

2. Am I surrounded by people that I care about? The people you surround yourself with can have a significant impact on your life. Being around people who support and encourage you can help you achieve your goals and bring joy into your life.

3. Am I Happy With Where I Am? This question helps you assess if you are content with your current situation. If you are not happy, it may be time to make changes in your life to improve your overall well-being.

4. Is there more I can do? This question encourages you to evaluate if you are reaching your full potential. There may be opportunities for growth and development that you have not yet explored.

5. Do I feel satisfied? This question helps you evaluate if you are fulfilled in your life. Satisfaction can come from achieving personal goals, helping others, or contributing to a cause you believe in.

6. How do I want to be remembered? This question helps you think about your legacy and the impact you want to have on the world. It can help guide your decisions and actions in life.

7. What makes me happy? This question helps you identify the things that bring you joy and happiness. Knowing what makes you happy can help you prioritize activities and make decisions that align with your values.

8. How do I look at today? This question encourages you to focus on the present moment and appreciate what you have in your life. It can help you cultivate gratitude and find joy in everyday experiences.

9. Can I have an impact? This question helps you assess your ability to make a difference in the world. It can help you identify ways to contribute to causes you care about and inspire you to take action.

10. What am I waiting for? This question encourages you to take action and pursue your goals. It can help you overcome fear and hesitation and motivate you to make positive changes in your life.

Here's an example based on a made-up individual. Let's call this person "John."

1. Why does John want to make art?

John wants to make art because it allows him to express himself in a way that words cannot. He finds it therapeutic and a way to connect with others through his emotions and experiences.

2. Where does John want to be (with his art) 1 year from today?

John would like to have his work displayed in a local gallery and start building a following for his art.

3. Where does John want to be (with his art) 5 years from today?

In 5 years, John wants to have established himself as a recognized artist with a loyal following. He would like to have his work shown in galleries all over the world and to have produced several successful solo exhibitions.

4. Where does John want to be (with his art) 10 years from today?

John wants to be considered a leading figure in his artistic community, with his work being studied and admired by future generations. He also hopes to have left a lasting impact on the art world through his unique style and message.

5. What keeps John from working (making art)?

John often finds himself struggling with motivation and creativity, especially when he is feeling overwhelmed with other aspects of his life.

6. What are John's bad working habits?

John tends to put off starting a new project until the last minute, which often leads to rushed and uninspired work. He also struggles with maintaining focus and can easily get sidetracked.

7. Who are John's favorite artists?

John is a big fan of Pablo Picasso, Frida Kahlo, and Vincent van Gogh.

8. What does John admire most about the work of each of these artists?

John admires the boldness and individuality of Picasso's work, the raw emotion in Kahlo's self-portraits, and the vivid color palettes and brushwork of van Gogh's pieces.

9. What are John's favorite images/themes/shapes/visual ideas?

John is drawn to bold, abstract forms and vibrant color combinations. He is also drawn to themes of self-expression and exploring the human condition.

10. What are the 6 pieces of art of which John is most proud? And why?

John is most proud of a series of abstract self-portraits that he created, as they were the first pieces that truly felt like his own. He is also proud of a set of mixed media pieces that explore mental health and the complexity of the human mind.

11. What is John's Art-related Bucket List?

John would like to have a solo exhibition at the MoMA in New York City, participate in a residency program in another country, and have his work acquired by a major museum for their permanent collection.

12. John's Artist Statement: Who is he? What does he do? How does he do it? Why does he do it? What has influenced him?

John is an abstract artist who explores themes of self-expression and the human condition through his art. He works primarily in mixed media, combining bold forms and vibrant color combinations to create pieces that are both visually striking and emotionally impactful. He creates art as a way to communicate his experiences and emotions and to connect with others on a deeper level. John is inspired by the work of artists such as Pablo Picasso, Frida Kahlo, and Vincent van Gogh, who also used their art to challenge societal norms and push the boundaries of conventional art forms.
