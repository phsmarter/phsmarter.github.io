6. Paradigm for Accountancy Business Management (ABM) student toward budgeting and coping strategies allowances​


As an ABM student, it is important to understand the value of budgeting and coping strategies in managing our allowances effectively. Here are some paradigms to follow:

1. Adopt a proactive mindset: Instead of waiting for the allowance to be exhausted before creating a budget, it is essential to plan ahead. A proactive approach will help you to identify your expenses and prioritize accordingly.

2. Know your financial goals: It is crucial to have financial goals that align with your budget. For instance, if you want to save for a new laptop, save a certain amount of money from your allowance each month.

3. Track your expenses: Keep a record of your expenses to have an idea of how much you spend each day, week, or month. With this knowledge, you can make informed decisions about your spending habits.

4. Prioritize your needs: Allocate your allowance first to your necessities such as food, transportation, and school supplies before spending on non-essentials.

5. Practice the 50/30/20 rule: This rule helps you to divide your allowance into three parts. Fifty percent for needs, 30 percent for wants, and 20 percent for savings or investments.

6. Use coping strategies: In times of financial difficulty, consider using coping strategies such as cutting down unnecessary expenses, selling unused items or taking up a part-time job.

In conclusion, budgeting and coping strategies are essential to managing your allowance as an ABM student. Adopting these paradigms will help you to make better financial decisions and ensure that your allowance lasts longer.

Introduction:

Work immersion is a program that aims to provide students with opportunities to apply the knowledge, skills, and values learned from the classroom in real-world settings. One of the critical components of work immersion is the practicum, which allows students to gain hands-on experience in their chosen field of specialization. As part of their practicum, students are required to budget their allowances to cover their daily expenses.

In this study, we will examine the effects of budgeting students’ allowance to their practicum in TVL-Cookery subject at MNHS. We will explore how budgeting affects the students' ability to manage their finances and how it impacts their overall performance in the practicum. We aim to identify the challenges and benefits that students encounter in budgeting their allowance and how this experience contributes to their personal and professional growth.

The findings of this study can help school administrators, teachers, and parents to better understand the importance of budgeting and financial management skills in the students' academic and personal lives.
