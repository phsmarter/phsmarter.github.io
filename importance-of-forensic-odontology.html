13. what is the importance of forensic investigation in solving a crime​


Answer:

Forensic investigation plays a critical role in the solving of crimes by providing scientific and factual evidence to help law enforcement agencies determine what occurred during a crime and who was involved. The importance lies in the fact that forensic evidence is often reliable and can help prove or disprove a suspect's involvement in a crime.

Forensic experts can analyze DNA, fingerprints, footprints, hair and fibers, ballistics, and other physical evidence collected from a crime scene to establish a connection with suspects, victims, and/or witnesses. The use of forensic technology has also increased, making it easier to collect and analyze evidence.

Without forensic investigation, many cases would go unsolved, and criminals would go unpunished. The significance of forensic investigation lies in the fact that it provides a strong foundation for a criminal case in court and helps ensure that justice is served in an accurate and fair manner.

16. What is the importance of Forensic Chemistry and Toxicology in Criminal Investigation? â€‹


Forensic toxicologists perform scientific tests on bodily fluids and tissue samples to identify any drugs or chemicals present in the body. Working in a lab, the forensic toxicologist performs tests on samples collected by forensic pathologists during an autopsy or by crime scene investigators. They use highly sophisticated instruments, chemical reagents and precise methodologies to determine the presence or absence of specific substances in the sample.

As part of a team investigating a crime, a forensic toxicologist will isolate and identify any substances in the body that may have contributed to the crime, such as:

Alcohol

Illegal or prescription drugs

Other chemicals

Poisons

Metals

Gases, such as carbon monoxide
