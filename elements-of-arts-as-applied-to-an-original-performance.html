2. elements of art as applied to an original performance


Performance art is a performance presented to an audience within a fine art context, traditionally interdisciplinary. Performance may be either scripted or unscripted, random or carefully orchestrated; spontaneous or otherwise carefully planned with or without audience participation. The performance can be live or via media; the performer can be present or absent. It can be any situation that involves four basic elements: time, space, the performer's body, or presence in a medium, and a relationship between performer and audience. Performance art can happen anywhere, in any type of venue or setting and for any length of time. The actions of an individual or a group at a particular place and in a particular time constitute the work.


3. elements of art as applied to an original performance


Basic elements of art, which are commonly used in creating arts and crafts, can also be applied in performance arts, such as theater or play. Some of these elements are the following:

 

1.   Line

2.   Shape

3.   Space

4.   Color

5.   Texture

6.   Form

7.   Value

 

The aforementioned elements provide the overall feel and aesthetic for a performance art and are used to further help the story easily conveyed to the audience.

16. Phases of Theater, Theater Groups, Elements of Art as applied to an original Performance​


Answer:

Phases of Theater:

1. Pre-production phase: This is the planning and preparation phase where the concept, script, casting, and design are developed.

2. Rehearsal phase: This is where the actors and the production team work together to practice and refine their performance.

3. Production phase: This is the actual performance phase where the show is staged and performed for an audience.

4. Post-production phase: This is the phase where the performance is evaluated and reviewed.

Theater Groups:

1. Repertory theater: This type of theater has a resident company of actors who perform a repertoire of plays throughout the year.

2. Community theater: This type of theater is usually made up of amateur actors and is focused on providing entertainment for the local community.

3. Experimental theater: This type of theater often challenges traditional forms of theater and explores new approaches to performance.

4. Commercial theater: This type of theater is focused on making a profit and often produces popular and commercially successful productions.

Elements of Art applied to an original performance:

1. Line: The movement of actors on stage and the shapes they create can convey different emotions and moods.

2. Shape: The shapes of the set, costumes, and props can create a visual theme and contribute to the overall mood of the performance.

3. Color: The use of color in lighting and costumes can create a specific atmosphere and mood for the performance.

4. Texture: The textures of costumes and props can add depth and richness to the visual elements of the performance.

5. Space: The use of space on stage can create a sense of depth, perspective, and movement.

6. Form: The overall structure and composition of the performance, including the script and blocking, can contribute to the overall impact of the performance.

7. Value: The use of light and dark shades can create contrast and emphasis, highlighting specific aspects of the performance.

Performance may be either scripted or unscripted, random or carefully orchestrated; spontaneous or otherwise carefully planned with or without audience participation. The performance may be live or via media; the performer can be present or absent. Performance art can happen anywhere, in any type of venue or setting and for any length of time.
