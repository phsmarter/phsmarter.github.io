16. True or False? Luke is one of the apostles of Jesus Christ??


Answer:

False.

Explanation:

The New Testament mentions Luke briefly a few times, and the Pauline Epistle to the Colossians refers to him as a physician (from Greek for 'one who heals'); thus he is thought to have been both a physician and a disciple of Paul. Since the early years of the faith, Christians have regarded him as a saint.

Answer:

The 12 apostles of Jesus

Andrew - Peter's younger brother. He was a fisherman before being Jesus' Apostle.

Peter - also known as Simon Peter. He was Andrew's brother. He was also a fisherman.

James - he was one of Zebedee's one. He was John's brother. He was a fisherman too.

John - younger brother of James. A son of Zebedee. And was a fisherman too.

Matthew - also called by the name Levi. He was a tax collector before meeting Jesus.

Simon - he was a zealot. He was also called as Simon the Canaanite.

Judas - also known as Judas Iscariot. He was the one who betrayed Jesus. He died by committing suicide.

Philip - he introduced Bartholomew to Jesus. He came from Bethsaida. He was one of the people surrounding John the Baptist appointing Jesus as the Lamb of God.

Bartholomew - also known as Nathanael or Nathaniel. Philip introduced him to Jesus.

Thomas - He was a Jew. Also known as Didymus. He doubted Jesus' resurrection.

Jude - also called as Judas Thaddeus or Jude of James.

Paul - also known as Saul of Tarsus. He taught the Gentiles about the most awaited savior and Messiah, Jesus.
