18. opening remarks for christmas party tagalog


Welcome words/Opening remarks

Welcome words are speeches spoken at celebrations, parties, and others. The meaning of the opening speech is a sentence that is delivered before the main event begins. Generally it contains a welcome from the presenter and is continued by important parties or figures who give advice or hope to the audience.

Purpose of ForewordMake the audience follow the directions from giving the speech (Usually done by the MC or host)Give an overview of the main events.Encouraging core activities to be carried out as well as possible.Motivate the audience to take an action.Making events less boring and more lively.

Types of GreetingsThe opening remarks are usually spoken by the master of ceremonies before inviting stakeholders to give official remarks.Briefing remarks, namely remarks that direct the audience to follow the event as well as possible. Usually read by the host.The inauguration speech, can be in the form of a spontaneous speech without text or using a speech text that has been prepared on a piece of paper.Accountability remarks, namely the opening speech that opens an accountability report on events or activities that have been or have been held.

22. Situation: you are asked to give the opening remarks of the school orientation program?approach:language form: duration of interaction:relationship of speacker:role and responsibilities of speaker:message:delivery:​.


Answer:

Approach: In preparing for the opening remarks of the school orientation program, I would first consider the audience and the purpose of the event. I would aim to provide a welcoming and informative introduction to the school, as well as set the tone for the rest of the program.

Language form: I would use formal language and a professional tone in my opening remarks, as it is a formal event and I am representing the school.

Duration of interaction: The opening remarks should be brief, lasting only a few minutes, to allow for other speakers and activities during the orientation program.

Relationship of speaker: As a representative of the school, I would have a professional relationship with the audience, which includes students, parents, and faculty.

Role and responsibilities of speaker: My role as the speaker is to introduce the school and set the tone for the rest of the program. I am responsible for delivering a clear and concise message that accurately represents the school and its values.

Message: In my opening remarks, I would highlight the school's mission and vision, as well as any key initiatives or goals for the coming academic year. I would also emphasize the importance of education and the role that students and parents play in achieving success.

Delivery: In delivering my opening remarks, I would speak clearly and confidently, using appropriate body language and eye contact. I would also try to engage the audience and make the message personal and relevant to their experiences.

25. closing remarks speech for nutrition month program


Nutrition clearly makes a difference to the health of women, to outcomes of pregnancy and lactation, and to the health, growth, and development of infants. Of the many contenders for health care resources, nutrition services warrant substantial attention and support. This chapter summarizes underlying themes of the report and presents the committee's major recommendations.

It is customary to consider nutrition services in relation to a specific type of patient (e.g., a pregnant or a breastfeeding woman) as has been done in this report. However, the committee preferred to consider the delivery of nutrition services in relation to growing families rather than specific types of patients. It agreed that such delivery could be improved by five general measures:

explicit recognition by all health team members of the essential role of nutrition in promoting health and growth;

a team effort to provide nutritional care that is individualized and patient centered;

the active involvement of families or other supportive persons in the formulation and implementation of a nutritional care plan;

the implementation of concrete steps to achieve continuity of nutritional care; and

nutrition education and training for health care providers.

Each of these measures requires adequate allocation of time and financial resources to allow clinicians to interact with, teach, and learn from other health care providers, the patient, and family members. The following sections provide further clarification of most of the measures mentioned above.

MEASURES FOR IMPROVING NUTRITIONAL CARE

Patient-Centered, Individualized Care

Patient-centered nutritional care requires an individualized approach. This means that a nutritional assessment should precede decisions concerning nutritional care. The term nutritional assessment covers a wide range of activities, not all of which are appropriate for all individuals. However, basic nutritional assessment for all patients encompasses the measurement of height or length and weight, the determination of hemoglobin or hematocrit values, and the assessment of dietary practices. Abnormal findings may indicate the need for more thorough assessment and intensive nutritional care.

Appropriate patient-centered care often requires that the health care team seek assistance for the patient from outside sources, generally through referral. This assistance may involve, for example, transport of neonates with complex nutrition and feeding requirements to a neonatal intensive care unit; referral to a center for the treatment of inborn errors of metabolism or diabetes mellitus; referral to the Special Supplemental Food Program for Women, Infants, and Children (WIC) for food supplementation and nutrition education; or referral to a program for the treatment of eating disorders. Structured procedures are needed to facilitate implementation of the referral, to track whether appropriate follow-up has occurred, and to indicate how care is to be altered, if at all, as a result.

If transportation or other logistical difficulties make it impossible to arrange for a referral or to incorporate the required specialist into the health care team, the health care provider is urged to consult with a dietitian who specializes in either maternal or neonatal nutrition and with other specialists as needed.
