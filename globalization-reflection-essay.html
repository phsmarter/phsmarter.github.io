All three essay prompts explore the impact of globalization on different industries and cultural practices. To effectively address these prompts, the following structure and weightage can be followed:

I. Introduction (10%)

- Briefly define globalization and its impact on industries

- Introduce the specific industry that the essay will focus on

- Provide a thesis statement that outlines the key points that will be discussed

II. Body Paragraphs (60%)

A. Content (50%)

- Discuss the ways in which globalization has impacted the industry in terms of trends

- Provide examples to support the argument

- Analyze the effects of these trends on consumer behavior and preferences

7. This activity is your second task for the topic Communication and Globalization.  Instructions:1.  Do some quick internet research to find out how English came to have an official status in the Philippines.2.  Reflect on this : How do you feel about the role and status of English in the Philippines?3.  Write your reflection (answer to question 2) in an ESSAY form  (3 paragraphs: Introduction, Body/Discussion, Conclusion ​


When the United States ceased their Philippine colonization, they did not only leave the legacy of basketball, burgers, and, Hollywood movies. They left us a universal language, which is English.

Personally learning the language made it possible to read American classics, British tales, and, other kinds of foreign literature. Plus, it gave me confidence that I could easily communicate with other nationalities from online chatting to vacationing and studying abroad. In short, I feel English has provided me the opportunity to access a global buffet of ideas and potential foreign friendships.

On the other hand, it is highly valued to the point that it has become a medium of learning instruction and communication alongside Filipino. In addition to that, it created Taglish, the combination of Tagalog and English. I often hear this variation whenever I visit NCR, particularly, in the areas of Taguig and Makati. In these places, there are more well-off people, and, you can find international citizens in the locale.

Moreover, I noticed that there is a common connotation with speaking Taglish, which means a person is sosyal or associated with being rich and well-educated. This could equally mean that English fluency represents class and education. This is alarming yet no surprise for me. After all, the colonial mentality is strong in the Philippines.

However, despite the fact that English could threaten our Filipino pride, I believe that is key to achieving globalization because you cannot speak Tagalog in international diplomatic talks. You cannot woo investors with Cebuano or study abroad with only Hiligaynon in mind. You cannot order food in Ilocano when you're vacationing in France. In other words, we can vocally make our presence worldwide.

Nevertheless, as we speak in American slang, we must not forget to keep the Filipino pride burning through our dialects and traditions.

"Kapaligiran" is a song that speaks of the beauty of nature and the need to protect it. The song uses powerful words and phrases such as "likas na yaman" (natural resources), "kalikasan" (nature), and "ating mundo" (our world), to emphasize the importance of preserving our environment. These words and phrases remind us that the environment is a precious resource that we need to protect and conserve for future generations.

In light of the current issue of global environmental degradation, the message of "Kapaligiran" becomes even more relevant. The rapid growth of industrialization and economic globalization has brought about significant environmental problems such as climate change, deforestation, and pollution. The need for sustainable development has become more urgent than ever before. As we continue to pursue economic growth and development, it is important to remember that our environment is not just a resource to be exploited, but a fragile ecosystem that we need to maintain and protect. We need to take concrete actions to ensure that our natural resources are preserved for future generations and that our planet remains a livable place for all.
