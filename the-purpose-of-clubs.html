4. These are specialized events that target sports, clubs, communities and companies for specific purpose.​


Answer:

You can elevate your participation by joining events that promote health and fitness as well as address health issues like diabetes, obesity, nutrition, smoking, and many more. By joining these events, you can learn more about these health concerns, what is being done to handle them—and at the same time, you also get to increase your physical activity. The following are the physical activities that you can initiate:

Fun run are usually 1-day events that focus on running various distances (i.e., 3k, 5k, 10k, or longer). They cater to a wide variety of participants—competitive or recreational runners, and even families.

 

Dance events, competitions, or marathons  are events focus on dance as the main activity, whether as competition (e.g. streetdance, dancesport) or as recreation (e.g. aerobic dance marathons, ZumbaTM events). These may last for hours depending on the event and variety of dances, intensity levels, and music usually used, thus, elevating participation.

 

Sports Tournament are the most common type of health- and fitness-related organized events. They mainly focus on sports in a tournament type of play where individual players and/or teams compete. They are usually organized for school teams [(e.g. University Athletic Association of the Philippines (UAAP)].

 

Summer Sports Clinics are short-course sports programs catering to school children. Sports clinics are offered by schools and product brands when school is over and students have their summer break.

 

Outdoor recreation events are specialized events that target sports enthusiasts and athletes. These are held in specialized venues and locations, and usually have different categories for different levels of participants.

 

Talks, seminars, or conferences are usually lecture-based events that cover topics discussed with an audience.

 

School, club, community, or company events are events that are organized by schools, clubs, communities, and companies to encourage an active lifestyle.

9. In a batch of 300 students, 73 aremembers of the Sports Club, 220 aremembers of the Music Club, and 70students are in both clubs. How manystudents are:a. in either the Sports Club or MusicClub?b. in neither of the clubs?​


Students (U) = 300

Let, S = sports, M = music

n(S) = 73

n(M) = 220

n(S) ∩ n(M) = 70

To solve for the number of students who are into sports club only:

n(S) = 73

73 - 70 = 3

To solve for the number of students who are into music club only:

n(M) = 220

220-70 = 150

To solve for the number of students who are in neither of the clubs:

300-(30+70+150) = 77

30. A secondary school with 300 students, a gardening club, a reading club, and a music club were formed. Each club had 100 member. The reading club has twenty members who are also in the gardening club and thirty two who are in thr music club. a student is selected at random. what is the probability that a student is aa. member of the reading club?b. member of the three club?c. member of music & reading club?d. member of reading or gardening club?e. member of music or reading club?​


Answer:

a. The probability that a student is a member of the reading club is 20/300 = 2/30.

b. The probability that a student is a member of all three clubs is 18/300 = 3/50.

c. The probability that a student is a member of both the music and reading club is 32/300 = 4/75.

d. The probability that a student is a member of the reading or gardening club is 38/300 = 1/8.

e. The probability that a student is a member of either the music or reading club is 52/300 = 17/150.
