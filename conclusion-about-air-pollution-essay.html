12. Expository text (paragraph)cause & effect of air pollution that have informative and conclusion.​


Answer:

Air pollution has become an increasingly dire problem in our world today, as toxic gasses and particles emitted from sources such as factories, power plants, vehicles, and burning of fossil fuels make the air we breathe contaminated. The effects of this contamination are dire and can cause various illnesses in humans and animals. For example, air pollution can trigger or worsen symptoms of asthma, cause damage to lungs and cardiovascular systems, and lead to lung cancer and premature death. In addition, it has a severe impact on the environment, reducing crop yields, making forests and other vegetation less productive, and leading to a loss of species biodiversity.

The only way to combat air pollution and its resulting effects is through aggressive efforts at prevention. Individuals and communities can do their part by utilizing public transportation, walking and cycling when possible, planting trees, and reducing electricity usage. Governments must also play an active role in air pollution reduction, as well as implement stronger regulation, tax incentives, and even bans of polluting sources.

Ultimately, air pollution has become a pressing concern with devastating consequences, but solutions exist if we take the time and effort to actively pursue them. With increased knowledge and the political will to put this knowledge into action, the cause and effect of air pollution can be addressed, ensuring a safer and healthier environment for us all.

25. short description about air pollution and water pollution


Answer:

Air pollution is a mixture of solid particles and gases in the air. Car emissions, chemicals from factories, dust, pollen and mold spores may be suspended as particles. Ozone, a gas, is a major part of air pollution in cities. When ozone forms air pollution, it's also called smog.

Water pollution occurs when harmful substances—often chemicals or microorganisms—contaminate a stream, river, lake, ocean, aquifer, or other body of water, degrading water quality and rendering it toxic to humans or the environment.
