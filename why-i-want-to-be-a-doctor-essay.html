14. Essay State the reasons why schedule I and II are risky and not prescribed by the doctor?​


Answer:

Schedule I drugs are those prescription drugs that have a high potential for drug abuse and overuse. Currently these drugs, which include heroin and marijuana, have no accepted medical use. Schedule II drugs are narcotics such as morphine, opium, and fentanyl may lead to severe psychological or physical dependence.

It's the negative effects of the drug to the person that makes doctors shy away from prescribing these medications, except in scenarios when the benefit of having the patients take them clearly outweigh the potential or abuse and psychological dependence. The human body can only do so much in processing and detoxifying these harmful substances and that's the reason why doctors do everything they can to veer away from prescribing them.

28. why do you want to be a doctor or nurse?​


Answer:

This is one of the most frustrating questions to answer for most premed students, yet it is also the most important question to answer convincingly. In fact, if you don't answer this question well, you are going to face medical school rejection. Period. You will also be expected to answer the tell me about yourself question.

Being a medical doctor is really great. It's stimulating and interesting. Medical doctors have a significant degree of autonomy over their schedules and time. Medical doctors know that they get to help people solve problems every single day. Medical doctors get to witness humanity at its very best and very worst.

But being a medical doctor is not easy. This is not a career for people who do not see themselves working more than 50 hours per week and on holidays. This is not a career for people who prefer to move around a lot. This is not a career for people who aren't good with responsibility and focus.

When you're in front of the interview panel or when an admissions committee is reading your personal statement, unconvincingly spewing a list of reasons why being a doctor is awesome, comes across as such and admissions committee members know that. You want to focus your answer on the YOU part of why YOU want to be a doctor and why YOU would be an excellent doctor.


29. Essay :why did I choose to be (teacher) ? - Essay why did you choose your profession or what you want to be?​


Answer:

I chose to become a teacher because I believe that education is one of the most powerful tools for personal growth and social change. Growing up, I was fortunate enough to have great teachers who inspired me, challenged me, and made me feel valued. Their influence on my life has been profound, and I want to be able to have that same impact on the lives of others.

Being a teacher is much more than just imparting knowledge. It's about creating a positive and supportive environment in which students can learn and grow, both academically and personally. It's about recognizing and nurturing the unique qualities and strengths of each individual student and helping them reach their full potential.

In a world that is constantly changing and evolving, teachers play a critical role in preparing students for the future. They are responsible for not only imparting knowledge and skills, but also for fostering critical thinking, problem-solving, and collaboration skills that will be essential in a rapidly changing world.

For me, the most rewarding part of teaching is the opportunity to make a difference in the lives of others. Whether it's helping a student understand a difficult concept, inspiring them to pursue a new interest or passion, or simply making them feel valued and supported, teaching is a truly fulfilling profession.

In conclusion, I chose to become a teacher because I believe that education is one of the most powerful tools for positive change, and because I want to be a positive influence in the lives of others. I believe that teaching is a calling, and I am eager to answer that call and make a difference in the world.
