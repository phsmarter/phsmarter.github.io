Mrs. Abad needs to check students to see if they are able to draw graphs correctly like the examples just demonstrated in class Method: Why? 1939 2. Mr. Garcia wants to see if his students are comprehending the story before moving to the next set of instructional activities. Method: Why? 3. Ms. Santos wants to find out how many spelling words her students know.​


Answer:

1. Mrs. Abad needs to check students to see if they are able to draw graphs correctly like the examples just demonstrated in class.

Method: Performance-based assessment

Why? A performance-based assessment allows Mrs. Abad to observe the students as they demonstrate their skills by actually drawing the graphs, which allows her to see their understanding and ability in action.

2. Mr. Garcia wants to see if his students are comprehending the story before moving to the next set of instructional activities.

Method: Oral questioning or observation

Why? An oral questioning assessment allows Mr. Garcia to ask the students questions about the story and listen to their responses, which can help him gauge their comprehension. Alternatively, an observation assessment would allow Mr. Garcia to watch the students as they engage with the story and look for signs of comprehension, such as asking questions or making connections to the material.

3. Ms. Santos wants to find out how many spelling words her students know.

Method: Selected response or self-report

Why? A selected response assessment, such as a multiple choice or fill-in-the-blank test, allows Ms. Santos to quickly and efficiently assess the students' spelling knowledge by providing a list of words for them to spell. Alternatively, a self-report assessment, such as a survey or questionnaire, allows the students to report on their own spelling abilities, which can provide additional information and context.
