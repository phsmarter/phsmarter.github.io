the larger hose alone is used be x - 6

1 / rate = time 
so,
1 / rate of both hoses = 4
rate of both hoses = 1/4

[tex] \frac{1}{x-6} + \frac{1}{x} = \frac{1}{4}\\ \frac{2x-6}{x^2-6x} = \frac{1}{4} \\ 8x-24=x^2-6x \\ 0=x^2-14x+24 \\ 0=(x-12)(x-2)[/tex]

19. wo pipes when connected to the same tank can fill the tank in 2 hours. The larger pipe working alone can fill the tank in 3 hours less than the smaller one. How long will it take to smaller pipe to fill the tank alone?​


[tex]\mathbb{FORMULA:}[/tex]

[tex]\mathsf{r=\dfrac{w}{t}} \longrightarrow \mathsf{r=\dfrac{1}{t}}[/tex]

where:

r = rate

t = time

w = work (w = 1 because there is only one tank to be filled)

[tex]\\[/tex]

[tex]\mathbb{SOLUTION:}[/tex]

For the larger pipe:

t₁ = x

[tex]\mathsf{r_1=\dfrac{1}{x}}[/tex]

[tex]\\[/tex]

For the smaller pipe:

Since the larger pipe can fill up the tank 3 hours faster than the smaller pipe, we can write t₂ as:

t₂ = x + 3

[tex]\mathsf{r_2=\dfrac{1}{x+3}}[/tex]

[tex]\\[/tex]

For both pipes:

t = 2

[tex]\mathsf{r=\dfrac{1}{2}}[/tex]

[tex]\\[/tex]

The sum of the rates of the larger pipe and the smaller pipe is equal to the rate for both pipes.

r₁ + r₂ = r

[tex]\mathsf{\dfrac{1}{x}+\dfrac{1}{x+3}=\dfrac{1}{2}}[/tex]

[tex]\\[/tex]

Solving for x,

[tex]\mathsf{\dfrac{1}{x}+\dfrac{1}{x+3}=\dfrac{1}{2}} \longleftarrow \textsf{multiply both sides by 2x(x + 3)}[/tex]

[tex]\mathsf{2(x+3)+2x=x(x+3)} \longleftarrow \textsf{expand and combine like terms}[/tex]

[tex]\mathsf{2x+6+2x=x^2+3x}[/tex]

[tex]\mathsf{4x+6=x^2+3x}[/tex]

[tex]\mathsf{x^2-x-6=0} \longleftarrow \textsf{factor}[/tex]

[tex]\mathsf{(x-3)(x+2)=0} \longleftarrow \textsf{apply zero product property}[/tex]

[tex]\mathsf{x_1=3 \: \: and \: \: x_2=-2} \longleftarrow \textsf{neglect the negative value}[/tex]

t₁ = x = 3

t₂ = x + 3 = 3 + 3 = 6

[tex]\\[/tex]

[tex]\mathbb{ANSWER:}[/tex]

The smaller pipe can fill up the tank in:

[tex]\large{\boxed{\textsf{6 hours}}}[/tex]

24. how is connected but alone of Sherry turkles related to problems in our present day world? ​


Explanation:

In "Connected, but Alone?", Sherry Turkle discusses how our increasing reliance on technology for communication and social interaction can lead to feelings of loneliness, disconnection, and a lack of empathy. Turkle argues that as we become more attached to our devices and social media, we may become less capable of forming genuine connections with others.

These ideas are still highly relevant in our present-day world. With the COVID-19 pandemic and the resulting social distancing measures, people have had to rely even more heavily on technology to communicate and maintain social connections. While this has been beneficial in some ways, it has also highlighted some of the negative effects of our reliance on technology, such as increased screen time and feelings of isolation.

Moreover, issues such as cyberbullying, online harassment, and the spread of misinformation have become increasingly prevalent in our digital world. These issues can further contribute to feelings of disconnection and loneliness.

In summary, Turkle's discussion of the challenges posed by our reliance on technology for communication and social interaction is still highly relevant today, as these issues continue to impact our lives in various ways
