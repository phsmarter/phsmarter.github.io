5. what is the summary and life lessons of fitness of family health (dating courtship marriage and responsible parenthood). ​


Answer:

The fitness of family health is a broad topic that covers many different aspects of family life, including dating, courtship, marriage, and responsible parenthood. In general, the fitness of family health is about creating and maintaining a healthy and happy family environment.

One important lesson of fitness of family health is the importance of communication and open dialogue within the family. This means being able to talk openly and honestly about your thoughts, feelings, and concerns with your partner and children. It also means being willing to listen to the perspectives of others and to try to understand their point of view.

Another key lesson is the importance of setting boundaries and establishing healthy habits within the family. This can include setting rules and expectations for behavior, as well as establishing routines for things like meal times and bedtimes. By creating a structured and predictable environment, families can create a sense of stability and security that can help everyone feel more comfortable and supported.

In addition to communication and healthy habits, responsible parenthood is an important aspect of the fitness of family health. This means being aware of and taking responsibility for the physical, emotional, and financial needs of your family. This can include things like planning for and managing the family budget, as well as being involved in your children's education and overall well-being.

Overall, the fitness of family health is about creating a supportive and loving environment for all members of the family. By being open and honest with one another, setting boundaries and establishing healthy habits, and taking responsibility for the needs of the family, families can work together to create a happy and healthy home.

When children are born too close together, the parents do not have enough time to give adequate attention to all of them. By spacing children further apart, the parents will have more time for each child and also more time for themselves. This can help contribute to a happier and more satisfying family life.

ABSTRACT Every infant is born into some kind of family-whether an extended family of several generations living togheter, a nuclear family of parents and children, or single-parent family. Every family lives in a neighborhood, and is a part of larger social and cultural context. This context is for sure specific for each child and family. A newborn will be brouth up differently into another society , no matter where it is. The suburb, inner city, a village dos nt matter, each of them representes a part of social life of baby and her family , with specific caracteristic each parts of community. In each of these settings children enter a way of life influenced by culture, class, geography, and ethnicity. All this elements of social context will forcefully shape childrens lives, often even before birth. As we look at the ways in wich family, community, and culture influence develeopment during infancy, we will see that this influence moves into two directions. The social context both influence is most obvios when we consider the social context at the level closest to baby- the family. The family as a system is an organized, interacting, interdependent group , functioning as a whole. Each memeber of the family influences and is influenced by the others. Even the smallest traditional family includes a variety of poeple (man, women, child ) and variety of roles (wife, mother, husband, father).Additional children add one more role ( sibling) but considerably more complexity to the family system. The realtionship between husband and wife affects the developing child and is itself affected by the birth of the child.
