1. as a student, how can you help in sharing knowledge about earthquake without causing fear or anxiety? provide concrete ways.​


Answer:

Look for safe places inside and outside of other buildings where you spend time. If you are indoors during an earthquake, drop, cover, and hold-on. Wait in your safe place until shaking stops, then check to see if you are hurt.

As Jesus prays in the Garden of Gethsemane, his first request is for his father to “take this cup of suffering away from me” (22:42). Our translation supplies a bit of explanation that is not present in the original language. More literally, Jesus asks, “Father, if you are willing, take this cup away from me.” The New Living Translation seeks to help the reader to understand the nature of the cup.

The cup of Jesus will involve suffering, to be sure. Yet “cup of suffering” doesn’t quite get what Jesus meant when he referred to a cup.

Answer:

to follow the way of life he lived and the process he underwent of experiencing a literal rebirth from flesh and blood to immortal spirit.

This is accomplished by living a life of keeping God's commandments, or "doing righteousness." Though he was the only human ever to have done it perfectly his entire life, it was not easy for him. Quite the contrary, it must have been a titanic struggle for him.

He was faced with the same real temptations other men face, with the real capability of sinning, due to the weakness of his flesh.

Most believers think his having overcome temptation was to do it in our stead, so that we don't have to. But, he overcame because he had to overcome. Otherwise, he could not be our savior.

His overcoming not only showed obedience was possible, but also demonstrated for us how to overcome -- which each of us must do for ourselves. If he had failed, he would never have returned to his former glorified state.

God and Christ put it all on the line for us! The risk was real, because God had never been subjected to fleshy weakness before.

There is a profound reason Christ is called our forerunner, and the captain of our salvation. We must do what he did, which is to learn obedience!

This is not my personal opinion! The scriptures plainly state this.

To follow Christ, then, means to do the things he did: to practice the religion he practiced -- referred to in scripture simply as "the way" or "that way."

It was the same religion practiced by "righteous Abel," and Enoch, and Noah, and Abraham, Isaac, and Jacob, and king David. It includes diligent obedience to all of God's commandments, including the ten commandments.

That also means keeping all of God's Sabbaths, both weekly and annual (that is, to obey the fourth commandment).

It also includes publicly preaching the gospel of the kingdom of God. For his church in the end time (this age), it means participating in a great work he commissioned them to do.

Oh, it means so much more than most who presume to be his followers have been told.
