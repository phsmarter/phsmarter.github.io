500+ Words Essay on Solar System

Our solar system consists of eight planets that revolve around the Sun, which is central to our solar system. These planets have broadly been classified into two categories that are inner planets and outer planets. Mercury, Venus, Earth, and Mars are called inner planets. The inner planets are closer to the Sun and they are smaller in size as compared to the outer planets. These are also referred to as the Terrestrial planets. And the other four Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus, and Neptune are termed as the outer planets. These four are massive in size and are often referred to as Giant planets.

Mercury

The smallest planet in our solar system is Mercury, which is also closest to the Sun. The geological features of Mercury consist of lobed ridges and impact craters. Being closest to the Sun the Mercury’s temperature sores extremely high during the day time. Mercury can go as high as 450 degree Celsius but surprisingly the nights here are freezing cold. Mercury has a diameter of 4,878 km and Mercury does not have any natural satellite like Earth.

Venus

Venus is also said to be the hottest planet of our solar system. It has a toxic atmosphere that always traps heat. Venus is also the brightest planet and it is visible to the naked eye. Venus has a thick silicate layer around an iron core which is also similar to that of Earth. Astronomers have seen traces of internal geological activity on Venus planet. Venus has a diameter of 12,104 km and it is just like Mars. Venus also does not have any natural satellite like Earth.

Earth

Earth is the largest inner planet. It is covered two-third with water. Earth is the only planet in our solar system where life is possible. Earth’s atmosphere which is rich in nitrogen and oxygen makes it fit for the survival of various species of flora and fauna. However human activities are negatively impacting its atmosphere. Earth has a diameter of 12,760 km and Earth has one natural satellite that is the moon.

Mars

Mars is the fourth planet from the Sun and it is often referred to as the Red Planet. This planet has a reddish appeal because of the iron oxide present on this planet. Mars planet is a cold planet and it has geological features similar to that of Earth. This is the only reason why it has captured the interest of astronomers like no other planet. This planet has traces of frozen ice caps and it has been found on the planet. Mars has a diameter of 6,787 km and it has two natural satellites.

Jupiter

It is the largest planet in our solar system. Jupiter has a strong magnetic field. Jupiter largely consists of helium and hydrogen. It has a Great Red Spot and cloud bands. The giant storm is believed to have raged here for hundreds of years. Jupiter has a diameter of 139,822 km and it has as many as 79 natural satellites which are much more than of Earth and Mars.

Saturn

Saturn is the sixth planet from the Sun. It is also known for its ring system and these rings are made of tiny particles of ice and rock. Saturn’s atmosphere is quite like that of Jupiter because it is also largely composed of hydrogen and helium. Saturn has a diameter of 120,500 km and It has 62 natural satellites that are mainly composed of ice. As compare with Jupiter it has less satellite.

Uranus

Uranus is the seventh planet from the Sun. It is the lightest of all the giant and outer planets. Presence of Methane in the atmosphere this Uranus planet has a blue tint. Uranus core is colder than the other giant planets and the planet orbits on its side. Uranus has a diameter of 51,120 km and it has 27 natural satellites.

Neptune

Neptune is the last planet in our solar system. It is also the coldest of all the planets. Neptune is around the same size as the Uranus. And it is much more massive and dense. Neptune’s atmosphere is composed of helium, hydrogen, methane, and ammonia and it experiences extremely strong winds. It is the only planet in our solar system which is found by mathematical prediction. Neptune has a diameter of 49,530 km and it has 14 natural satellites which are more than of Earth and Mars.

Conclusion

Scientists and astronomers have been studying our solar system for centuries and then after they will findings are quite interesting. Various planets that form a part of our solar system have their own unique geological features and all are different from each other in several ways.

6. atmosphe II. Essay. Write a short essay on why Earth is unique from the other planets in the solar system and why is it important to protect and preserve its resources? ​


Answer:

Earth is a truly unique planet in our solar system for several reasons. Firstly, it is the only planet known to have the necessary conditions for human life to thrive. This is due to its location within the habitable zone around the sun, which means it receives just the right amount of heat and light from the sun to sustain life. Additionally, Earth possesses a great diversity of life forms including plants, animals, and microorganisms, which make up complex ecosystems that are found nowhere else in the universe.

Furthermore, Earth is the only planet known to have liquid water on its surface, an essential ingredient for life. Water covers about 71% of the Earth's surface, and its presence also plays a crucial role in regulating the planet's temperature and climate. Additionally, Earth has a thick atmosphere that protects us from harmful radiation from the sun and helps maintain the planet's temperature within a range suitable for life.

Given how unique Earth is, it is of paramount importance that we take the necessary measures to protect and preserve its resources for future generations. This includes reducing our carbon footprint by transitioning to cleaner, more sustainable forms of energy, protecting biodiversity by preserving natural habitats, and reducing pollution and waste, among other measures.

By protecting the resources and ecosystems of our planet, we ensure that it continues to sustain human life and the myriad other forms of life on Earth. In short, safeguarding our planet is not just the right thing to do, but it is also essential for our survival and well-being.

THE SOLAR SYSTEM

The Formation of Solar System

Our solar system formed about 4.5 billion years ago from a very dense cloud of cosmic gas and dust. After a long amount of time the cloud eventually collapsed, possibly due to the an immense force or shockwave of a nearby exploding star, also called a supernova. When this dust cloud collapsed, it formed a the very young solar nebula a spinning, accretion disk of material.

At the center of the nebula, the gravity gradually pulled more and more material in. Eventually the when the pressure in the core was so immense that hydrogen atoms began to merge and form helium, releasing a great amount of energy. With that, our very young Sun was born, and it eventually accumulate more than 99 percent of the remaining matter.

The available matter farther out in the disk was also bonding together. These clumps crashed into one another, forming larger and larger objects. Some of them grew big enough for their gravity to shape them into spheres, becoming planets, dwarf planets and large moons. In other cases, planets did not form: the asteroid belt is made of bits and pieces of the early solar system that could never quite come together into a planet. Other smaller leftover pieces became asteroids, comets, meteoroids, and small, irregular moons.

Structure

The solar system is the collection of large and small bodies that orbit our central star, the Sun. The contents of our solar system is numerous and stretch across a large area of space. The distance of Sun to Neptune is 4.5 billion kilometers!

PLANETS

TERRESTRIAL PLANETS namely Mercury, Venus, Earth and Mars has solid compositions, frozen innercores, mantles and crusts. Mercury has a huge core, while venus on the other hand is almost the same as earth, in terms of size and density. Usually made up of metal, silicate and a rocky surface.

GAS PLANETS OR GAS GIANTS these planets can be found beyond the asteroid belt, namely Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus and Neptune. These planets are large and mostly composed of gases, such as helium and hydrogen, some of these planets have small rocky core.

COMETS

• Comets are interstellar objects that follows long, oval or elliptical orbits around the Sun. They come from deep space, swing around the Sun and head off again, to return years or centuries later. If Earth passes through a comet's tail, the particles of dust become meteoroids that burn up into our atmosphere, as a spectacular meteor shower. It has three main parts, The Nucleus inside its head, the head, and its Tails.

ASTEROIDS

• Asteroids have dark surfaces. Scientists think that this asteroids are rich in carbon. Other asteroid are thought to be rocky and to have a core made of iron or nickel.

OORT CLOUD

• The spherical shell of interstellar or cometary bodies believed to surround the Sun beyond the orbits of the outermost planets outside the actual solar system and from which some are forcefully removed when perturbed to fall toward the sun.

KUIPER BELT

• The clumps of small celestial bodies far beyond the orbit of the planet Neptune from which many short-period comets are believed to materialize a long time ago.

SCIENTISTS AND THEIR REVOLUTIONARY CONTRIBUTIONS

• Galileo Galilei (1564-1642)

was one of the greatest scientist who ever lived. He made many discoveries about gravity and acceleration. But he is especially famous, because he was the first to scan the heavens and make many important discoveries, described in his book The Starry Messenger (1610)

Discovered the moons of Jupiter, that is now called The Galileian Moons.

• Johannes Kepler (1571-1630)

Mathematician and Astronomer who supported the ideas of Copernicus. The Earth was not the center of the universe, with everything going around it. Kepler worked out the orbits of planets known at the time, in mathematical detail. The Kepler's three laws of planetary motion.

• Nicolaus Copernicus (1473-1543)

studied arts, medicine and law before becoming a church canon in Poland. Then turned his attention to Astronomy. He proposed that the Geocentric idea of the Earth as the center of everything, believed since ancient times, did not fit with astronomical observation. He suggested that the Heliocentric theory where the Earth and other planets went around the Sun. This caused great debate but also began a new phase of science.
