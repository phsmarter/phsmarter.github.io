17. Topic: "Cell phone should be allowed in schools" help me plss I'm begging ​


Introduction:

Cell phones have progressively assimilated into our daily life. With the popularity of smartphones and other mobile technology, these gadgets are now utilized for much more than just communication; they are also used for entertainment, education, and other purposes. Cell phone use in schools is controversial, though, with many people contending that it interferes with studying. I'll make the case for allowing cell phones in schools in this essay.

Body:

1. Safety and security are two of the most important advantages using cell phones in schools. In the event of an emergency, such as a car accident or school shooting, children using cell phones can quickly contact their parents or emergency personnel. Moreover, cell phones can be used to notify school authorities of any questionable conduct, helping to avert or lessen potential risks.

2. Mobile devices can be utilized in the classroom to improve instruction. Students can use their phones, for instance, to access instructional apps, conduct informational searches, and get in touch with their teachers or fellow students. Cell phones can also be used to shoot images and videos, which can be used for educational activities like recording field excursions or experiments.

3. Both students and teachers can benefit from the ease and efficiency that cell phones can offer. Students can use their phones to interact with teachers outside of class, make reminders for assignments, and access internet resources. Instructors may exchange resources, give feedback, and connect with students and parents quickly and easily using cell phones.

Counterargument:

Cell phones can distract students from their studies, which is one of the main grounds against permitting them in class. Instead of paying attention in class, students could be tempted to use their phones for non-educational activities like texting or social media. It's crucial to remember, though, that cell phones can be used sensibly and that teachers can establish clear rules for their use in the classroom.

Conclusion:

Because of their advantages for safety and security, educational potential, convenience, and effectiveness, cell phones should be permitted in schools. There might be worries about distraction, but they can be handled by having clear rules and policies.

23. should cell phone allowed in school(200 word essay)​


Answer:

The use of cell phones in schools has been a controversial topic for many years. Supporters argue that cell phones can be used as educational tools, while opponents believe that they can be a source of distraction for students.

While it is true that cell phones can provide access to educational resources, such as online textbooks and research databases, their potential to distract students is a significant concern. Cell phones can be a source of temptation for students to engage in non-educational activities, such as playing games or scrolling through social media. This can lead to a decline in academic performance and a disruption of class.

Additionally, the use of cell phones in schools can create inequality among students who may not have access to the latest technology or may not be able to afford a cell phone. This could lead to a sense of exclusion and unfairness among students.

In conclusion, while the use of cell phones in schools can have some benefits, such as access to educational resources, the potential for distraction and inequality outweigh these benefits. Therefore, it is recommended that schools establish clear policies on the use of cell phones in the classroom to minimize their negative impact on students' academic performance and social dynamics.
