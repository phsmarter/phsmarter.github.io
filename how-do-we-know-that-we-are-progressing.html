6. why do we need to monitor growth and progress of the business​


Answer:

There are several reasons why it is important to monitor the growth and progress of a business:

To track performance: By regularly monitoring the growth and progress of a business, you can track how well the business is performing and identify any areas that may need improvement.

To identify opportunities and threats: Monitoring the growth and progress of a business can help you to identify opportunities for growth and expansion, as well as potential threats that may impact the business.

To make informed decisions: By tracking key performance indicators (KPIs) such as revenue, profit, and customer satisfaction, you can make more informed decisions about how to allocate resources and set goals for the future.

To stay competitive: Monitoring the growth and progress of a business can help you to stay competitive in your industry by keeping up with industry trends and benchmarking your performance against other businesses.

Overall, monitoring the growth and progress of a business is important for ensuring its long-term success and sustainability.

13. 3. When do we use the "be" verbs am, is and are? 4. What form of the main verb is used in the progressive aspect? 5. How do you know the tense of the verb in the progressive aspect when used in a sentence?​


Answer:

3. When do we use the "be" verbs am, is and are?

Answer: We use "be" verbs when there is no main verb.

What is the be verb used for?

The “to be” is a verb used to describe something or someone.

In English, it can be used to say different things according to the context. However, the most common use of the to be verb is to talk about names, ages, feeling, nationalities, and professions, especially when talking in the present tense.

Am is for the first person singular (I am), is is for the third person singular (he is, she is, it is) and are is for the first person plural (we are), the second person singular and plural (you are) and the third person plural (they are). Was/Were — These two verb forms are used for the past tense.

4. What form of the main verb is used in the progressive aspect?

Answer: a verb tense that is used to refer to an action or a state that is continuing to happen In English, a verb form in the progressive tense consists of a form of the verb "be" followed by the main verb's present participle.

5. How do you know the tense of the verb in the progressive aspect when used in a sentence?​

Answer:  Verbs in the progressive aspect are recognizable by the present participle (i.e., the word that ends "-ing"). Here are some examples of verbs in the progressive aspect. He was singing. We were playing.

Financial performance: A company can track its financial performance over time by looking at key financial metrics such as revenue, profit, and cash flow. This can help the company understand how well it is doing financially and identify areas for improvement.

Customer satisfaction: A company can measure the satisfaction of its customers through surveys, focus groups, or other methods. This can help the company understand how well it is meeting the needs of its customers and identify areas for improvement.

Operational efficiency: A company can track key operational metrics such as production efficiency, inventory turnover, and employee productivity to understand how well it is using its resources and identify areas for improvement.
