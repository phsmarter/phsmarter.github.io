15. What is my goal in life? Essay 150 words


Goal of My life

Life without a noble objective is like a ship without a rudder, yet choosing a goal is a difficult undertaking. Because nothing is good or evil; thinking alone makes anything good or bad, different people have various goals depending on their preferences. Everyone who has a seeing eye, a thinking mind, and a feeling heart has some sort of ambition.

Serving my nation is my life's biggest ambition. I am aware that this is not a simple task and that there are several methods to serve one's nation. I am aware that becoming a decent citizen would be the finest service I could provide to my nation. I might decide to become a doctor, engineer, or teacher. I may go into business or into politics. But no matter who I become, if I don't understand my rights and obligations, I won't be of any service to my nation.

Making myself an excellent citizen would thus be my first step toward achieving my life's purpose. I'll never be corrupt, and I'll never put up with other people being corrupt. I'll always treat people fairly and justly. I will always put the good of the community above my own interests in all I do. I won't push my claims if there is a conflict between my personal and societal interests. I could have a positive impact on everyone if I can live up to this ideal.

20. make an essay about your reaction and opinon on factors to consider in choosing a career in the future and the proper attitude inborder to achieve one's goal or ambition in life.​


Answer:

A career can be defined as a person's progress within an occupation or series of occupations. However, a career is more than just a job, or working, or your occupation. It also includes your progress through life, your growth and development in vocational and avocational areas of life. Deciding on your initial career may present a stressful and frightening prospect. Many tend to perceive career decision-making as complex or even mysterious, only because they tend to concentrate on the outcome and overlook the decision-making and planning process. Successful career decisions are based on current and accurate information. Today, career information is abundant and easily accessible. While this is exciting and potentially helpful, it can also be overwhelming. Nevertheless, one major fact emerges from the mass of data and literature available: effective career planning is a process that involves the total person. Comprehensive career planning stresses the importance of knowing enough about your unique attributes, about specific career fields, and about your life priorities.
