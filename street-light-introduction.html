3. the street lights are 25 meters apart. if there are 15 street lights, what is the distance from the first street light to the last street lights


Arithmetic Sequence    

In arithmetic sequence, a term is obtained by adding a constant number to the preceding term. Sequence whose succeeding terms are obtained by adding a constant. Constant is called the common difference. Formula is a_n = a₁ + d(n - 1). The common difference is obtained by subtracting the first term from the second term.

Answer:

375 meters

Solutions:

Let: a₁ = 25

       n = 15

       d = 25

Solve for a_n.

a_n = a₁ + d(n - 1)

a_n = 25 + 25(15 - 1)

a_n = 25 + 25(14)

a_n = 25 + 350

a_n = 375

Therefore, the distance from the first street light to the last street light is 375 meters.

Word Problem

The step-by-step method is used in answering word problems. This method helps to analyze the given problem easily and to get the answer correctly. Let us now analyze the problem above.

1. What is asked in the problem?

The distance from the first street light to the last street light.

2. What are the given?

Number of street lights - 15

Distance between the street lights - 25 m

3. What is the operation to be used?

The operations to be used is subtraction and multiplication.

4. What is the number sentence?

(15 - 1) × 25 m = N

5. Solve

(15 - 1) × 25 m

= 14 × 25 m

= 350 m

The distance from the first street light to the last street light is 350 m.

*You have to take away 1 from the number of streetlights to know the number of spaces there are between the first to the last streetlight.
