5. Suppose that in a population of psychology students of 1600, 500 are 1st years, 400 are 2nd years, 380 are 3rd years, 270 are 4th years, and 50 are irregular students. What is the probability of getting a sample that is a 2nd year student or irregular student?


Answer:

The total number of students in the population is 1600, and the number of 2nd year and irregular students is 400 + 50 = 450.

Therefore, the probability of randomly selecting a 2nd year or irregular student from the population is:

P(2nd year or irregular) = (number of 2nd year or irregular students) / (total number of students)

P(2nd year or irregular) = 450 / 1600

P(2nd year or irregular) = 0.28125

So the probability of getting a sample that is a 2nd year student or irregular student is 0.28125, or 28.125%.

To find the area of an irregular octagon with sides 4 cm and 20 cm, we need to split it into smaller regular shapes and then add their areas together.

One way to split the octagon is to divide it into a rectangle and two trapezoids. To do this, draw a line perpendicular to one of the sides that measures 4 cm, and another perpendicular to the opposite side that measures 20 cm. This will divide the octagon into a rectangle with sides 16 cm and 20 cm, and two trapezoids with bases 4 cm and 20 cm and height 8 cm.

To find the area of the rectangle, we multiply its length by its width:

Area of rectangle = 16 cm x 20 cm = 320 cm²

To find the area of each trapezoid, we use the formula:

Area of trapezoid = 1/2 x (sum of bases) x height

Area of trapezoid 1 = 1/2 x (4 cm + 20 cm) x 8 cm = 96 cm²

Area of trapezoid 2 = 1/2 x (4 cm + 20 cm) x 8 cm = 96 cm²

Finally, we add the areas of the rectangle and the two trapezoids to find the total area of the irregular octagon:

Total area = 320 cm² + 96 cm² + 96 cm² = 512 cm²

Therefore, the area of the irregular octagon is 512 cm².
