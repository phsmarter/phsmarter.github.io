12. What are the three principal purpose of hospitality and tourism research?​


ANSWER:The three principal purposes of hospitality and tourism research are.

1). To understand and improve the industry: Hospitality and tourism research aims to better understand the industry, its challenges and opportunities, and to identify areas for improvement.

2). To inform decision-making: Research can provide valuable insights and data to support decision-making by industry stakeholders, including managers, policymakers, and investors.

3). To advance knowledge and innovation: Hospitality and tourism research helps to advance knowledge in the field, promote new ideas and approaches, and drive innovation. The research can also lead to the development of new products, services, and experiences for guests.

15. what is good or not yet made into a research title about eco tourism (research agenda)​


Answer:

One potential research topic in ecotourism could be the impact of ecotourism on local communities and their economies. Specifically, the research could investigate how ecotourism can be designed and managed in a way that maximizes benefits for the local community and minimizes negative impacts such as the displacement of local people or the degradation of natural resources. COVID-19 and tourism

SARS-CoV-2 has had a greater impact on tourism and travel behaviour than any disease outbreak in living memory. Tourism has become one of the main public faces of COVID-19 in the international media. The disease's global reach and the application of classic disease controls have had a dramatic effect on international and domestic tourism. The nature of the tourism system means that tourism has both contributed to the spread of the disease and has had repercussions throughout the value and supply chains of tourism.
