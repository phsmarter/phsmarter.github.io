3. Short review about a work of a contemporary filipino writer.​


"Insurrecto" by Gina Apostol is a remarkable work of contemporary Filipino literature that challenges the notion of truth and history. The novel follows the story of two women - one a Filipino translator and the other an American filmmaker - who are working on a movie about the Philippine-American War. As they travel to different historical sites in the Philippines, they engage in a series of conversations that reveal the complex and often contested nature of history.

Apostol's writing style is playful and experimental, incorporating footnotes, multiple narrators, and a non-linear narrative structure. Through these literary devices, she explores the themes of identity, memory, and the power of language to shape our understanding of the past.

"Insurrecto" is a unique and thought-provoking work that challenges readers to question their own assumptions about history and the stories we tell ourselves about our lives and the world around us. It is a must-read for anyone interested in contemporary Filipino literature and the ongoing legacy of colonialism in the Philippines.

21. Give at least three Filipino writers and emphasize their works and contributions in the Philippine Literature. ​


Noli Me Tángere by Dr. José Rizal

Among the great novels in Philippine literature, Noli Me Tángere (Touch Me Not) is the most controversial and widely-known – it’s included in the current education curriculum of Filipino high school students. Written by the country’s national hero, Dr. José Rizal, this novel sparked the social awakening of Filipinos during the Spanish colonial era. As Rizal cannot fathom the unfairness of the Spanish priests and the ruling government at the time, his purpose of writing the book was to expose the ills of Philippine society at the time. In this revolutionary book, you’ll learn about the story of Crisostomo Ibarra, how he dealt with Spanish authorities, and how he prepared for his revenge, as told in Rizal’s 2nd book, El Filibusterismo

Florante at Laura by Francisco Balagtas

Another famous masterpiece in Philippine literature, Francisco Balagtas’ Florante at Laura is written in the form of ‘awit’ where there are four lines per stanza and 12 syllables per line. Set in the fictional kingdom of Albania, it tackles the story between Duke Florante and Princess Laura, who’s also being pursued by Florante’s enemy Count Adolfo. As a literary classic, this book has become a favorite play not only among Filipino high school students, but has been showcased in grand theaters such as Gantimpala Theater and the Cultural Center of the Philippines.

The Woman Who Had Two Navels by Nick Joaquin

Being one of the most admired writers in Philippine literature, Nick Joaquin was recognized as National Artist of the Philippines for Literature in 1976. In his historical novel entitled The Woman Who Had Two Navels, Joaquin examines the effects and influence of the past towards the post-war events in the Philippines. Lead female character Connie Escobar thinks she has two navels, and thus requests her doctor to remove one – which symbolically means that she wants to shun away from a traumatic past. She later finds out that her husband, Macho Escobar, was the lover of her mother. Hoping to escape after being betrayed, she flees, only to discover more truths.

30. How did the American regime influence the development of Philippine literature, and give one author and his work that emerged during this period that continues to influence and inspire contemporary Filipino writers today?


Answer:

The American regime had a significant impact on the development of Philippine literature. When the Americans colonized the Philippines in 1898, they brought with them their language, culture, and educational system. This led to the spread of English language and literature, which gradually replaced Spanish as the dominant literary medium. The American regime also brought new literary forms and genres such as the short story, the novel, and free verse poetry, which were widely adopted by Filipino writers.

One author and his work that emerged during this period and continue to influence and inspire contemporary Filipino writers today is Jose Garcia Villa and his book of poetry titled "Have Come, Am Here" (1942). Villa is considered as one of the most influential Filipino writers of the 20th century and is known for his innovative use of language and form in poetry.

"Have Come, Am Here" is a collection of Villa's free verse poems that explore themes of love, death, and the human condition. The book is notable for its use of imagistic language, unconventional syntax, and vivid imagery. Villa's poems often contain striking and surprising images that challenge the reader's perceptions of reality.

Villa's work has had a profound influence on contemporary Filipino writers, particularly those writing in English. His innovative use of language and form has inspired many Filipino poets to experiment with their own writing and to push the boundaries of what is considered traditional or conventional in poetry. Villa's legacy has also paved the way for the emergence of contemporary Filipino poets who continue to explore new possibilities in language and form, and to use poetry as a means of expressing their unique experiences and perspectives as Filipinos
