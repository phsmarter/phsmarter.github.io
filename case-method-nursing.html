3. How do you create a related reading on your case presentation for nursing?


Answer:

tips for creating related readings on nursing case presentations. The tips include using credible sources, organizing readings based on aspects of the case, providing summaries and references for each reading, and considering the use of multimedia The goal is to create an informative and engaging learning experience for the audience.

How to Write a Case Study Paper for Nursing:

The status of the patient. Demographic data. Medical History.The nursing assessment of the patient. Vital signs and test results.Current Care Plan and Recommendations. Details of the nursing care plan (including nursing goals and interventions).

What is a case study presentation nursing?

A case presentation is a formal communication between health care professionals such as doctors and nurses regarding a patient's clinical information. Essential parts of a case presentation include: Identification. Reason for consultation/admission. Chief complaints (CC).

28. Nurse julia plans to instruct the postpartum client about methods to prevent breast engorgement. which of the following measures would the nurse include in the teaching plan?


Breast engorgement is the sensation where the postpartum mother experience a heavy, painful, hard, and sensitive breast due to expansion and pressure exerted by the lympathic engorgement of the breast which synthesize the breast milk production (lactation).

The primary measures that a nurse should include in her teaching plan is the initiation of breastfeeding.

Encouraging breastfeeding can alleviate the pain, tenderness, and soreness of the breast engorgement. It can also provide bonding between the mother and the newborn.

Here are some other measures that a nurse should also include in her teaching plan:

Application of cold compress helps to alleviate breast discomfort.Avoid breast pumping. Engorged breast tissue is sensitive, hence, breast pumping is harmful and it make more susceptible to damage. Wear a snug-fitting bra. It offers comfort and helps in diminishing the amount of engorged milk caused by blocked milk ducts. Ask the attending physician for oral analgesics to provide a pain relief. Encourage the mother to don't delay the breast feeding sessions and don't use any formula milk.
