22. True or False? Explain. Filipino writers have stopped writing essays, poems, and stories after World War II.


FALSE

Why?Filipino writers have been writing, and are still writing different types of literature to this very day.The end of WW2 did not signal the end of Philippine written literature.These can be found in many places all over the Philippines, with the most basic example being newspapers.With the creation of the internet, many aspiring writers have flocked writing websites such as Wattpad.It could be attested that due to the official declaration of Philippine independence, the number of writers has massively increased due to the fact that most restrictions that sanction contemporary stories have been removed.

Examples of Philippine Literature After WW2

1.) Mga Ibong Mandaragit

Is a famous piece of Philippine literature that was created in the year 1969.It was authored by Amado V. Hernandez.It was inspired by the two famous books of Jose Rizal, Noli Me Tangere and El Filibusterismo, and tackles about the various political and socioeconomics problems of the Philippines.

2.) The Woman Who Had Two Navels

Another piece of Philippine literature that was written in the year 1976.It was authored by the famous national artist Nick Joaquin.The book talks about the influence and impact of the post-war situation of the Philippines to the past, and to contemporary times.

3.) Banaag at Sikat

Is a famous piece of Filipino literature that was made in the year 1949.It was authored by Lope K. Santos.It was dubbed as the bible of the working class.It talks about the differences and problems of the working class and what was and is considered to be the "elite" class of society.
