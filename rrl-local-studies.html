7. How can you make connections of RRL, variables of the study and conceptual framework of a research study?


Answer:

Research theory and theoretical and conceptual frameworks are often used in the language of researchers to represent similar things, but there are subtle differences in meaning - as the previous posts elude to. Research frameworks provide the knowledge and theory basis for research studies. Theoretical research frameworks represent known and tested theories. There are many ‘tried and tested’ theories that researchers can cross-reference against. Therefore, this foundational knowledge serves as a ‘frame of reference’ from which researchers can either predict or explain their study outcomes.

Explanation:

However, it may be that there is no theoretical framework to guide the researcher. In this case, conceptual frameworks identify single or multiple concepts that are related but remain untested. They are still useful as a frame of reference, but the degree of interpretation is looser and wider than with theoretical frameworks. Verification of an untested theory provides an area from which research problems can be derived.

RRL (Related Literature Review):

According to a study by Kuznekoff and Titsworth (2013), the use of smartphones in the classroom can have a negative impact on academic performance. The study found that students who used their smartphones during class had lower grades on a subsequent exam compared to students who did not use their smartphones. The researchers suggested that the use of smartphones in class could distract students from the lecture material and interfere with their ability to learn.

Another study by Junco and Cotten (2012) found that students who used their smartphones during class were more likely to engage in non-academic activities such as texting, checking social media, and browsing the internet. This behavior was associated with lower academic performance and reduced engagement in the classroom.

On the other hand, some studies have suggested that allowing smartphones in the classroom can have benefits. For example, a study by Kay and Lauricella (2011) found that students who used smartphones in class were more engaged and participated more in class discussions. The researchers suggested that smartphones could be used as a tool to facilitate active learning and collaboration in the classroom.

Implications of allowing smartphones in classes:

Allowing smartphones in the classroom can have both positive and negative implications. On the one hand, smartphones can be used as a tool to facilitate learning and engagement. For example, students can use smartphones to access online resources, participate in online discussions, and collaborate with classmates.

On the other hand, the use of smartphones in class can also lead to distractions and non-academic activities. Students may be tempted to check social media, text friends, or browse the internet during class, which can interfere with their ability to learn and engage with the material.

Furthermore, the use of smartphones in class can also create a distraction for other students. The sound of incoming messages or phone calls can be disruptive to the learning environment and can negatively impact the learning experience of other students in the classroom.

Overall, the decision to allow or ban smartphones in the classroom should be carefully considered by educators. While smartphones can be a valuable tool for learning, they can also create distractions and interfere with the learning process. Educators should establish clear guidelines for the use of smartphones in class and communicate these guidelines to students to ensure that smartphones are used in a productive and constructive way.

19. How does an RRL for quantitative differ from an RRL for qualitative?​


Answer:

Qualitative Research: an operational description

Purpose: explain; gain insight and understanding of phenomena through intensive collection and study of narrative data

Approach: inductive; value-laden/subjective; holistic, process-oriented

Hypotheses: tentative, evolving; based on the particular study

Lit. Review: limited; may not be exhaustive

Setting: naturalistic, when and as much as possible

Sampling: for the purpose; not necessarily representative; for in-depth understanding

Measurement: narrative; ongoing

Design and Method: flexible, specified only generally; based on non-intervention, minimal disturbance, such as historical, ethnographic, or case studies

Data Collection: document collection, participant observation, informal interviews, field notes

Data Analysis: raw data is words/ ongoing; involves synthesis

Data Interpretation: tentative, reviewed on ongoing basis, speculative

29. is there a similarity between traditional rrl and systematic rrl​


Answer:

Traditional reviews

Authors: One or more authors usually experts in the topic of interest

Study protocol: No study protocol

Research question: Broad to specific question, hypothesis not stated

Search strategy: No detailed search strategy, search is probably conducted using keywords

Sources of literature: Not usually stated and non-exhaustive, usually well-known articles. Prone to publication bias

Selection criteria: No specific selection criteria, usually subjective. Prone to selection bias

Critical appraisal: Variable evaluation of study quality or method

Synthesis: Often qualitative synthesis of evidence

Conclusions: Sometimes evidence based but can be influenced by author’s personal belief

Reproducibility: Findings cannot be reproduced independently as conclusions may be subjective

Update: Cannot be continuously updated

Systematic reviews

Authors: Two or more authors are involved in good quality systematic reviews, may comprise experts in the different stages of the review

Study protocol: Written study protocol which includes details of the methods to be used

Research question: Specific question which may have all or some of PICO components (Population, Intervention, Comparator, and Outcome). Hypothesis is stated

Search strategy: Detailed and comprehensive search strategy is developed
