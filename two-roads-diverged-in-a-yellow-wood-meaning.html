Structuralism is a literary theory that focuses on the underlying structures of a text and how those structures shape the meaning of the text. In analyzing "The Road Not Taken" by Robert Frost using structuralism, we can identify several key elements:

Binary oppositions: The poem sets up a series of binary oppositions, including two roads, two travelers, two claims, and two paths through life.

Metaphor: The two roads are a metaphor for the choices that we make in life, and the traveler's decision to take the "road less traveled" is a metaphor for taking a unique and unconventional path through life.

Form: The poem is structured into five stanzas of equal length, each with four lines. The first and third lines of each stanza rhyme, as do the second and fourth lines. This structure creates a sense of balance and symmetry in the poem.

And sorry I could not travel both (he 'regrets' that he could not be 2 people and travel both)

And be one traveler, long I stood (he stays for a while,)
