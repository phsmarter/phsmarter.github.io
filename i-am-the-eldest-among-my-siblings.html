18. i have eight brothers and a sister. our eldest is 48 years old, the second is 46 years old, the third is 44 and so on if the pattern is considered and i am 38 years old what is my rank out if my 10 siblings? And please show the solution.​


Answer:

10th sibling's age is 30

Step-by-step explanation:

in case you did'nt know the age's are like patterns and that pattern is called arithmetic.

48,46,44,42,40,38,36,34,32,30...

arithmetic sequence can be determine if the numbers or terms have thesame difference.

46-48= -2

44-46= -2

42-44= -2

40-42= -2 and so on and so forth

the formula of arithmetic sequence is:

an=a1+(n-1)d where an= nth term

a1= first term

n= number of terms

d=common difference

based on the problem:

a1 = 48

n= 10

d = -2

plugging the terms to the equation of arithmetic sequence we get:

an=a1+(n-1)d

a(10)= 48 + (10-1)-2

a(10)= 48 + (9)-2

a(10)= 48 -18

a(10)= 30

the tenth brother's age is 30

#CarryOnLearningProblem:I have eight brothers and a sister. our eldest is 48 years old, the second is 46 years old, the third is 44 and so on if the pattern is considered and i am 38 years old what is my rank out of my 10 siblings?

Solution:

8 brothers, 1 sister and you = 10

48, 46, 44, 42, 40, 38, 36, 34, 32, 30

46 - 48 = -2

44 - 46 = -2

42 - 44 = -2

40 - 42 = -2

38 - 40 = -2

36 - 38 = -2

34 - 36 = -2

32 - 34 = -2

30 - 32 = -2

difference = -2

it forms an arithmetic progression.

1st = 48 years old as the oldest

2nd = 48 - 2 = 46 years old

3rd = 46 - 2 = 44 years old

4th = 44 - 2 = 42 years old

5th = 42 - 2 = 40 years old

6th = 40 - 2 = 38 years old as you

7th = 38 - 2 = 36 years old

8th = 36 - 2 = 34 years old

9th = 34 - 2 = 32 years old

10th = 30 - 2 = 30 years old

Answer:You are 6th in the rank

22. Which sentence uses superlative degree of adverb? A. We live farther down along Tatlonghari St. B. Heavy rains flooded the Zavalla St. C. I am the eldest among my brothers. D. Marion is a better player than Pete​


C. I am the eldest among my brothers.

EXPLANATION:

You can change most one-syllable adverbs into superlative adverbs by adding “-est” to them. Examples include "fast" becoming "fastest," "loud" becoming "loudest," "slow" becoming "slowest," and "quick" becoming "quickest."
