36. Literature refers to any written work that is created by a writer's creative imagination. This can include poetry, fiction, non-fiction, and other forms of writing.

37. Literature is a body of written works that includes various genres such as poetry, novels, history, biography, and essays. It is often considered to be a reflection of the cultural background of a society or group of people.

38. Afro-Asian Literature refers to the literary output of various countries and cultures in Africa and Asia. This includes works that are written in various languages and reflect the cultural, historical, and social contexts of these regions.

39. Poetry is a form of literature that uses language to evoke emotion, create imagery, and express ideas. It is characterized by its use of lines and stanzas, often with a specific rhyme and meter.

40. Non-fiction refers to any written work that is based on factual information rather than imagination. This can include biographies, history books, scientific reports, and other works that are based on real-world events and information.

B. The second section provides definitions of elements of a short story.

41. A Character is a person, animal, or other entity that takes part in the action of a story or other literary work.

42. The Climax is the highest point of interest or suspense in a story. It is the point at which the conflict or tension reaches its peak and leads to the resolution of the story.

43. The Exposition is the beginning of the plot in a short story, where the setting, characters, and basic situation are introduced.

44. The Setting refers to the time and place in which the story happens. It includes physical, cultural, and historical details that help to create the atmosphere of the story.

45. The Plot is a series of events and character actions that relate to the central conflict of the story. It includes a beginning, middle, and end, and is often divided into rising action, climax, and falling action.

C. The third section provides definitions of different types of conflict that can occur in a story.

46. Man vs. supernatural is a conflict that occurs when the protagonist faces a struggle against a god, gods, or other supernatural forces.

47. Man vs. self is a conflict that takes place within the character himself or herself. It can involve a struggle with personal beliefs, fears, or desires.

48. Man vs. man is the conflict of the story in which the struggle is between the protagonist and the antagonist. This can involve physical, emotional, or intellectual struggles between two characters.

49. Man vs. society is a conflict that occurs when the character has a conflict with the government, cultural, or societal tradition. This can involve a struggle against laws, social norms, or other aspects of the culture in which the character lives.

50. Conflict refers to the problem that the main characters have to face in a story. It is the central tension or struggle that drives the plot forward and creates the narrative arc of the story.
