1. write an informative essay about "Save Mother Earth"​


Answer:

Mother Earth, or also known as our planet, is the only known planet in the universe that is capable of sustaining life. With a diverse range of ecosystems, resources, and inhabitants, it is a magnificent and complex system that we have the privilege of inhabiting. However, over the past few decades, human activity has caused significant damage to our planet, and we are now facing the consequences of our actions. Global warming, deforestation, pollution, and loss of biodiversity are just some of the pressing issues that are threatening the well-being of our planet.

Fortunately, there are many ways that we can help save Mother Earth. One of the most important actions that we can take is to reduce our carbon footprint. This can be achieved by adopting more sustainable and eco-friendly lifestyles, such as using renewable energy sources, reducing waste, and conserving water. We can also make conscious decisions when it comes to our daily activities, such as using public transportation or carpooling instead of driving alone, and choosing to purchase products that are sustainably produced and packaged.

Another essential step in saving our planet is to protect our natural resources. This includes preserving forests, oceans, and other ecosystems that are home to a vast array of plant and animal species. We can support conservation efforts by supporting organizations that work to protect and preserve these resources, volunteering for environmental causes, and educating ourselves and others about the importance of sustainability.

Reducing pollution is crucial in helping to save our planet. We can reduce pollution by reducing our use of single-use plastics, properly disposing of hazardous waste, and advocating for policies that promote clean energy and reduce emissions from industries and transportation.

We can also help to save Mother Earth by being mindful of our daily habits and behaviors. This includes avoiding excessive consumption of resources, practicing eco-friendly habits at home, and encouraging others to join the movement to protect our planet.

In conclusion, the survival of our planet and the well-being of all its inhabitants depend on our actions today. By taking small, everyday actions to reduce our carbon footprint, protect our natural resources, reduce pollution, and practice sustainable habits, we can all help to save Mother Earth. It is up to us to take responsibility and make a positive impact on our planet, and in doing so, ensure that future generations will have the same opportunity to enjoy the wonders and benefits of our beautiful planet.

3. Write an essay on how recycling can help save Mother Earth​


Answer:

How Recycling can help save Mother Earth?

When we recycle plastic, we reduce the need for new plastic to be made. Through recycling paper, we help to reduce deforestation and save trees from being felled. Separating cans and other metals helps to minimize toxic extraction and our ever-increasing need for raw materials. (summary:  Recycling Conserves Resources)

It takes much more resources to produce industrial-grade products from scratch than it does to reform and reuse old materials. Recycling conserves energy because recycled products do not require manufacturers to emit as much greenhouse gas emissions as they would if the same object were made from scratch using raw materials.(summary: Recycling Saves Energy)

When we reduce the amount of new materials and natural resources we need to harvest from the earth, whether by planting, mining, forestry, or other means, we protect fragile habitats and animals from damage or extinction and ensure their survival for future generations. Recycling products releases far less greenhouse gases into the atmosphere than primary processing.

(summary:  Recycling Protects the Environment)

There are 2,000 operating landfills in the United States alone, all of which are full of industrial waste that release greenhouse gases into the environment as it eventually decomposes. This does not include the many that have been closed due to storage, or the potential landfills that will be needed as the current landfills run out of space. (summary: Recycling Slows the Spread of Landfills)

That's right: believe it or not, one of the advantages of recycling is that it helps to build work! According to a Friends of the Earth report, achieving a 70 percent home recycling rate will generate 51,400 jobs in the United Kingdom alone. In the United States, the Natural Resources Defense Council (NRDC) discovered that achieving a national recycling rate of 75% would result in the production of 1.5 million new jobs. (summary:  Recycling Creates Jobs)

Our sustainability, as well as the futures of future generations, is dependent on sustainable development and consumption. As a result, recycling is one of the most important strategies to contribute to the United Nations Sustainable Development Goals. (summary: Recycling Supports the SDGs)

4. Essay about producing new scientist through innovations, competitions and inventions for the sustainability for clean and green environment to save mother earth.


The making of a scientists often involves finishing at least a four-year degree from a tertiary education institution. Most of the time, students are immersed in books and lectures. What if we can produce new scientists through innovations, competitions, and inventions to guarantee the sustainability of a clean and green environment and thus saving mother earth?

Learning is never only about reading books; it is also about being motivated to remember and practice an information, concept, or skill. If students simply memorize theories from books, chances are they would forget this, and by the time they need it for actual use, they will have to review and thus waste time. An innovative way of making new scientists would mean straying away from the usual lecture and notes methods and moving to new methods like experiential learning. Producing new scientists through innovations can be tricky and doing so requires teachers to be creative enough to figure out new methods to educate students. However, this is important because the environment in which students grow and develop influences how they turn out, so if they are trained in an innovative manner, they are likely to be innovative.  

Similarly, when students are pitched against each other to compete, they are often motivated to do their best. Of course, who would say no to a quiz contest with an exemption in the final exam as a reward? However, while many students are motivated by competition, John Shindler (2009) in his book Transformative Classroom Management notes that makes learners focus on the outcome and not on the process; it also weakens the intrinsic motivation of students not to mention it increases anxiety. Worse, Shindler notes that it develops ‘fear of failure’ in students. This would not be a good quality for scientists since they should go through a series of failed experiments before they arrive at the appropriate outcome. Hence, in developing new scientists, competition may help in teamwork, but too much of it could be dangerous to the individual.  

In learning, assessment is the most important. The most common forms of assessments would be written exams. However, if we are breeding scientists, practical ones in which they apply what they have so far learned would be a guarantee of their potentials. An invention would be a suitable form of assessment. They must be able to put together something functional to prove that all their years of learning have not been put into waste.

Since we are breeding innovative, competitive, and inventive new scientists, we are given hope that the environment could be saved by this new blood of scientists. Exposing them at a young age to the value of protecting the environment will help them focus on agendas related to sustainability of  a clean and green environment. Hence, part of the innovative ways of teaching them would be best related to preserving the environment. They may be asked to compete on topics related to the environment. Finally, they may be asked to invent something for the environment. If such information is gradually implanted in them from a young age, students would grow with much concern for the environment, and these new scientists leave the institution ready to save mother earth.  

The new scientists bred through innovation, competition, and invention, could easily show how science could save humanity and mother earth.

6. Essay (2 paragraphs): As a student, how can you contribute to save our mother earth?


Answer:

As a student, there are numerous simple things that we can do to help save our mother earth. The first thing we can do is to reduce, reuse, and recycle. Reducing our consumption of resources such as electricity, water, and paper is an easy way to make a big difference. We can also reuse items like bags, bottles, and containers, and recycle paper, plastic, and glass materials.

Another way students can contribute to saving our mother earth is to be mindful of our carbon footprint. We can do this by utilizing public transportation like buses, subways, and trains, as well as walking or biking instead of driving. Moreover, we can be conscious about the energy we are using and try to minimize our use of electricity, especially in standby mode. Finally, educating ourselves on sustainability practices and spreading the awareness amongst our peers is also a great way to contribute.

Conserve Water

The little things can make a big difference. Every time you turn off the water while you’re brushing your teeth, you’re doing something good. Got a leaky faucet? You might be dripping as much as 90 gallons (340 liters) of water down the drain every day [source: EPA]. So fix it! It’s easy and cheap. And stop drinking bottled water. Switch to filtered tap water. You’ll save a ton of cash and help reduce a ton of plastic waste in the process.

Reduce, Reuse, Recycle

You can help reduce pollution just by putting that soda can in the recycling bin. It really does make a difference. Paper, too. Case in point: If an office building of 7,000 workers recycled all of its office paper waste for a year, it would be the equivalent of taking almost 400 cars off the road [source: EPA]. But you can also take reusable bags to the grocery, and avoid using disposable plates, spoons, glass, cups and napkins. They create huge amounts of waste. And buy products that are made of recycled materials. It all makes a difference.

Live Energy Wise

Make your home more energy efficient (and save money). Your home’s windows are responsible for 25 to 30 percent of residential heat gain and heat loss. If they’re old and inefficient, consider replacing them. Also be sure your home has proper insulation. Insulation is measured in terms of its thermal resistance or R-value — the higher the R-value, the more effective the insulation. The amount of insulation your home needs depends on the climate, type of HVAC system, and where you’re adding the insulation. Smaller things you can do right away include replacing your air filter regularly so your HVAC system doesn’t have to work overtime. Keep your window treatments closed when it’s extremely hot and cold outside.
