2. ESSAYthings to do for us to graduate ​


Answer:

Graduating from high school or college is a momentous occasion, and it requires a lot of hard work and dedication. In order to successfully graduate, there are several things that students must do, including completing coursework, passing exams, fulfilling requirements, and participating in extracurricular activities. Here are some of the key things that students must do to graduate:

Complete all coursework: This is the most fundamental requirement for graduation. Students must attend classes, complete assignments, and earn passing grades in all required courses.

Pass all exams: In addition to completing coursework, students must also pass exams in order to demonstrate their knowledge and mastery of the subject matter. This may include final exams, standardized tests, or other assessments.

Fulfill all requirements: Many schools have specific requirements that students must fulfill in order to graduate. This may include completing a certain number of community service hours, participating in a senior project, or completing a capstone course or project.

Attend graduation rehearsals: In order to ensure that the actual graduation ceremony runs smoothly, students must attend rehearsals to practice walking in their cap and gown, receiving their diploma, and other important aspects of the ceremony.

Participate in extracurricular activities: While not strictly required for graduation, participating in extracurricular activities can help students build skills, gain experience, and develop a well-rounded profile for college or job interviews.

Overall, graduating from high school or college requires a lot of hard work, dedication, and perseverance. By completing coursework, passing exams, fulfilling requirements, attending rehearsals, and participating in extracurricular activities, students can set themselves up for success and achieve their goals of graduation.

22. Write an Essay about the Graduation of sixth-grade students this coming April


Graduation is an important milestone for students, as it marks the completion of one phase of their education and the beginning of another. This coming April, sixth-grade students across the country will be celebrating their graduation from elementary school and moving on to middle school. This is a time of excitement, reflection, and anticipation for both students and their families.

The graduation ceremony is a formal event that typically takes place in the school's auditorium or gymnasium. The event usually includes speeches from teachers, administrators, and students, as well as the presentation of diplomas or certificates of completion. Parents, family members, and friends are usually invited to attend the ceremony to share in the students' accomplishments and celebrate their hard work.

For many sixth-grade students, graduation represents a significant achievement. It is a culmination of years of hard work, dedication, and perseverance, as they have navigated through the challenges of elementary school. They have learned important academic skills, such as reading, writing, and mathematics, and have developed critical social and emotional skills, such as communication, collaboration, and empathy.

Moreover, graduation is also a time of reflection, as students look back on their elementary school years and think about the memories they have made, the friendships they have formed, and the lessons they have learned. They may feel nostalgic about leaving their elementary school behind and moving on to new challenges and opportunities.

However, graduation is also a time of anticipation, as students look forward to what the future holds. They are excited about starting middle school, where they will meet new friends, explore new subjects, and face new challenges. They are eager to continue learning and growing, as they prepare for high school and beyond.

In conclusion, the graduation of sixth-grade students this coming April is an important event that marks the end of one chapter in their education and the beginning of another. It is a time for celebration, reflection, and anticipation, as students and their families look back on their accomplishments and look forward to the future. Congratulations to all the sixth-grade students who will be graduating this April!

As April approaches, the graduation of sixth-grade students becomes an important event for many schools and communities. This graduation marks the end of a chapter in the lives of these students as they prepare to enter middle school and embark on new challenges.

The graduation ceremony is a time to celebrate the achievements of these students over the past six years. It is an opportunity to recognize their hard work, dedication, and academic accomplishments. It is also a time to acknowledge the support of parents, teachers, and the community in helping these students to succeed.

For many students, this graduation marks the end of their elementary school years, which have been marked by a variety of experiences, challenges, and growth. They have learned to read, write, and do math, as well as explore their interests and passions through extracurricular activities. They have also learned social skills, such as how to make friends, resolve conflicts, and work in teams.

As these students move on to middle school, they will face new challenges and opportunities. They will encounter new subjects, new teachers, and new peers. They will need to adjust to a more complex and demanding academic environment, while also navigating the social dynamics of adolescence.

The graduation ceremony is a time to reflect on the past and look towards the future. It is a time to celebrate the accomplishments of these students and wish them well as they embark on the next phase of their educational journey. It is also a time for parents, teachers, and the community to offer words of wisdom, encouragement, and support to these students.

In conclusion, the graduation of sixth-grade students is an important milestone in their lives, marking the end of their elementary school years and the beginning of new challenges and opportunities. It is a time to celebrate their achievements, reflect on the past, and look towards the future with optimism and excitement. As these students prepare to enter middle school, they carry with them the knowledge, skills, and experiences that will help them to succeed in the years ahead.

27. example of closing remarks batch graduation 2022


Ladies and gentlemen, as _______ I would like to conclude today’s proceedings with a few words to our graduates. As you proceed to the next chapter of your lives, I offer the following ingredients for success – things I have observed in people I’ve known and worked with over the years.

First, integrity: If you carry out your responsibilities consistently, to the best of your ability, people will trust you as a colleague, and turn to you for leadership.

Next, courage: In the face of adversity, and even tragedy, try to remember the reasons for optimism – and never be afraid to ask for help. Look after yourself – especially your health, both physical and mental.

Follow your instincts and curiosity wherever they lead, and broaden your knowledge by reading as much as you can. Embrace opportunities to collaborate. It’s the key to our success as a society, and it helps us grow as individuals.

Be bold and take risks, and don’t let the fear of failure stop you. Failure is a part of life. It’s another way to learn. Change is a part of life, too. At times it’s frightening, but we should see it as a chance to explore, and to test our limits.

Above all, enjoy what you do. Take pleasure and satisfaction in what you achieve. Along with the personal characteristics I’ve mentioned above, this is the key to a happy life.

Whatever your next steps, I invite you to see your studies here as part of a lifetime of learning. And I encourage you to use that learning to leave every situation better than it was when you arrived.

Graduates – and now, my fellow alumni of the University of _______ : Congratulations! You have worked hard. You deserve to be proud of this achievement, and to take this opportunity to celebrate.

Good luck, and good health to all of you
