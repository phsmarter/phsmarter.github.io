Your Moral Compass

For the most part, a new employee walks into a business as a fully-formed human being, meaning they already have a core set of values. Our values are our central beliefs; they're what motivate us, and they also shape our actions. Things like honesty, integrity and fairness might be part of that value set.

Sometimes you hear the word values used in conjunction with ethics - how do they differ? Ethics is a branch of philosophy that seeks to distinguish right from wrong, moral from immoral, by using logic and reason. So, in a business sense, an organization can have core values that it operates within and a set of business ethics it can use to distinguish between right and wrong behavior.

What about another similar word - morals? Morals are a system of beliefs, often backed up by a religious or political philosophy. A business can use its set of ethics alongside its morals to guide the organization's behavior.

The greatest possible synergy comes when an individual works for an organization that has a set of values that matches their own. In an ideal world, it would also be great if an organization's stated values (what the organization tells the world and its employees) actually matched its operating values (what the organization really does).

Values and Behavior in the Workplace

Many companies have a written mission statement that they share with employees and customers. A mission statement describes basic things about the business, like markets served, reason for being and business goals. It can also contain a short version of the principles and values that guide the organization. An effective mission statement can be internalized by employees to provide a framework for their decisions.

Companies can use things like written codes of conduct and employee training to reinforce the types of behavior they want from the employees. They can also implement systems of rewards and punishments to help guide employees to do the right thing. Company leaders can also model behavior they want from employees - actions speak louder than words.
