5. Answer the following questions in detail Identify the author and title of the painting, what do you know about it? What art movement does it illustrate? How does the poetry of the Symbolists P. Verlaine and A. Rimbaud reflect the artistic techniques of this movement, give examples.


1. The painting "Impression, Sunrise" was created by the French Impressionist painter Claude Monet in 1872. The painting is known for its unique style and vivid use of color to capture the fleeting effects of light and atmosphere. Monet's technique was based on the idea of painting quickly, using small brushstrokes to create an overall effect of color and light. The title of the painting is a reference to the hazy, atmospheric effect created by the morning light reflecting off the water in the harbor. The painting is considered a masterpiece of the Impressionist movement and is currently held in the Musée Marmottan Monet in Paris.

2. The painting "Impression, Sunrise" is a prime example of the Impressionist art movement. This movement emerged in France in the mid-19th century and is characterized by its emphasis on capturing the sensory experience of the natural world. Impressionist painters sought to capture the fleeting effects of light and atmosphere, using loose brushstrokes and vibrant colors. The aim was to create a sense of immediacy and spontaneity in their works, as well as to challenge traditional academic painting techniques. The Impressionist movement had a profound impact on the development of modern art, influencing subsequent movements such as Post-Impressionism and Fauvism.

3. The poetry of the Symbolists, particularly Paul Verlaine and Arthur Rimbaud, reflects the artistic techniques of Impressionism in several ways. Symbolism was a literary movement that emerged in the late 19th century in France, which sought to convey deeper truths through indirect and suggestive language. Like Impressionist paintings, Symbolist poetry often focuses on the sensory experience of the natural world, as well as subjective emotions and sensations. Verlaine's poem "Clair de Lune," for example, evokes the dreamy atmosphere of moonlit night using vivid, sensory language: "Your soul is a chosen landscape / Where charming masqueraders and bergamaskers go / Playing the lute and dancing and almost / Sad beneath their fanciful disguises." Similarly, Rimbaud's "Sensation" emphasizes the intensity of sensory experience through its use of striking metaphors and vivid imagery: "I've cultivated my hysteria with delight and terror. / I've chased away the happinesses that were trying to settle in. / Behind the formless clouds of the storms / A great calm reigns, and the Earth shudders in silence." Overall, both Impressionism and Symbolism sought to capture the fleeting and subjective nature of human experience, whether through visual or literary means.
