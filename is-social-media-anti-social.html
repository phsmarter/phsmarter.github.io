2. different of social,as social and anti-social ​


Answer:

Colloquially, the terms ‘asocial’ and ‘antisocial’ get used interchangeably, to describe someone who isn’t motivated by social interaction. But in both their dictionary definitions, and a clinical mental health context, these terms have starkly different meanings.

The prefix ‘anti’ means against; ‘a’ means without, or lack of. While ‘antisocial’ denotes preferences against society, or social order, ‘asocial’ refers to individuals who aren’t social. Dictionaries define antisocial behavior as “contrary to the laws and customs of society, in a way that causes annoyance and disapproval in others,” or “marked by behavior deviating sharply from the social norm.” But, an asocial person is one, who is “not interested in forming social groups, or connections with others.” But, asociality shouldn’t be confused with introvertism either because, unlike asocial individuals, introverts can crave company of others. While being antisocial appears to be a more active characteristic, being asocial is more passive. These characteristics can occasionally overlap, but don’t necessarily.

To put it simply: ‘asocial’ suggests a withdrawal, marked by indifference, from society. Being asocial is a personality trait — resulting either from a lack of motivation to engage in social interactions and activities, or a strong preference for solitary activities. Being antisocial suggests active hostility towards society, and can in some instances manifest in the form of a personality disorder (called Anti-Social Personality Disorder, or ASPD).

12. Anti-social personality disorder


Explanation:

Antisocial personality disorder, sometimes called sociopathy, is a mental disorder in which a person consistently shows no regard for right and wrong and ignores the rights and feelings of others. People with antisocial personality disorder tend to antagonize, manipulate or treat others harshly or with callous indifference. They show no guilt or remorse for their behavior.

Individuals with antisocial personality disorder often violate the law, becoming criminals. They may lie, behave violently or impulsively, and have problems with drug and alcohol use. Because of these characteristics, people with this disorder typically can't fulfill responsibilities related to family, work or school.

20. EXPLAIN: 2. Children's exposure to violent and anti-social models can teach them anti-social lessons that they bring to their relationships.​


Children are influenced by the people around them. They learn from their parents, siblings, friends and neighbors. The modeling of violence and anti-social behavior at home is one of the main reasons why children develop negative attitudes towards others. When a child sees his/her parent or sibling being violent or using abusive language, it will put them off from this behavior in the future. This effect can be even stronger if they see their parent or sibling engage in aggressive behavior towards someone else when angry or upset. When children grow up and become teenagers, they may continue these behaviors with their peers at school or with other adults at work. A child who has been exposed to violence at home may become a violent person themselves as an adult because there is no way for them to unlearn these lessons learned early on in life by watching their parents use violence against each other whenever they argue or fight over something.

26. halimbawa ng selfish lying, self enhancement lying, anti social lying​


Answer:

Ang "selfish lying" ay ang uri ng kasinungalingan na nagaganap kapag ang isang tao ay nagsisinungaling upang mapakinabangan ang kanyang sarili o protektahan ang kanyang sariling interes. Halimbawa nito ay ang pagsisinungaling tungkol sa kanyang mga kasanayan upang magkaroon ng trabaho o promotion, o ang pagsisinungaling tungkol sa kanyang mga gawaing di-tama upang maiwasan ang parusa.

Ang "self-enhancement lying" ay ang uri ng kasinungalingan na ginagamit upang mapabuti ang imahe ng isang tao sa mata ng iba. Halimbawa nito ay ang pagsisinungaling tungkol sa kanyang mga tagumpay o gawaing nagawa upang mapakita na siya ay mas magaling kaysa sa iba.

Ang "antisocial lying" ay ang uri ng kasinungalingan na ginagamit ng mga taong mayroong mga ugali ng antisosyal. Halimbawa nito ay ang pagsisinungaling upang manloko ng iba, upang makamit ang kanilang mga layunin, o upang maipagtanggol ang kanilang sarili.
