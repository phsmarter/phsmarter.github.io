The effects of this rich-poor divide are far-reaching and deeply felt. Those at the bottom of the social ladder often lack access to quality education, healthcare, and other essential services, while those at the top enjoy privileges and opportunities that are out of reach for many. This leads to a perpetuation of poverty and inequality, as those who are born into disadvantaged circumstances are often unable to break out of them due to a lack of resources and opportunities.

One of the most pressing issues related to social stratification in the Philippines is the lack of upward mobility. Those who are born into wealthy families are much more likely to succeed and achieve financial stability, while those born into poverty are often trapped there for their entire lives. This lack of mobility has far-reaching consequences for both individuals and society as a whole, as it reinforces a sense of hopelessness and a lack of opportunity.

To address this issue, it is essential that the government and other institutions take steps to address the root causes of poverty and inequality. This includes investing in education and training programs, providing access to healthcare and other essential services, and implementing policies that promote economic growth and development. Additionally, it is important to address issues of corruption and ensure that the wealth of the country is distributed fairly and equitably.

Ultimately, addressing the issue of social stratification in the Philippines will require a concerted effort from all sectors of society. By working together and taking action to address the root causes of poverty and inequality, it is possible to create a more inclusive and just society for all.

These institutions laid the foundation for the globalization that we see today.

The growth of international trade has been a major driver of globalization. The reduction of trade barriers and the opening up of markets has allowed for the exchange of goods and services across borders. This has led to increased economic growth and job creation in many countries. However, it has also led to the outsourcing of jobs and the displacement of workers in some industries.

Globalization has affected both rich and poor nations in different ways. Rich nations have benefited from increased access to new markets and cheaper goods. They have also been able to outsource jobs to countries with lower labor costs. Poor nations have benefited from increased investment and access to new technologies. 22. Below are pictures presented depicting various types of community-based sociological perspectives (e.g., rural, urban, slum, subdivision, poor, middle class, rich, industrial, agricultural, etc.). Write one-page essay comparing and contrasting the pictures​


Community-based sociological perspectives refer to the ways in which people interact, relate, and organize themselves in different communities. These perspectives are shaped by various factors such as social class, economic status, culture, geography, and history. In this essay, we will compare and contrast some of the community-based sociological perspectives, including rural, urban, slum, subdivision, poor, middle class, rich, industrial, and agricultural perspectives.

Rural communities are characterized by low population density, agricultural activities, and traditional social structures. People in rural areas are often self-sufficient, and their lives are closely tied to the land. In contrast, urban communities are characterized by high population density, diverse economic activities, and complex social structures. Urban areas are centers of commerce, industry, and culture, and people in these areas are often more mobile and socially diverse.

Slum communities are typically found in urban areas and are characterized by poverty, poor housing, and lack of basic services. People living in slums often face social exclusion, marginalization, and discrimination. Subdivision communities, on the other hand, are typically found in suburban areas and are characterized by gated communities, private amenities, and exclusivity. People living in subdivisions often have high levels of income and social status, and they prioritize privacy and security.

Poor communities refer to communities where people live in poverty, struggle to make ends meet, and lack access to basic services. These communities are often located in both rural and urban areas. In contrast, middle-class communities are characterized by stability, moderate levels of income, and access to basic services. People living in middle-class communities often value education, home ownership, and social mobility.

Rich communities are characterized by wealth, high levels of consumption, and access to luxury amenities.
