8. Dance's Helix Model is an example of ____. Select one: a. none of the choices b. transactional c. linear d. interactive


Dance Helix Model is an example of speech communication.

Dance Helix Model was proposed by Frank Dance in 1967. It gave the definition of communication by Frank Dance – the Dance Helix Model aimed to enhance speech communication of a person. Dance Helix Model drew inspiration from a three-dimensional spring which is called a helix. The model gives a geometrical testimony of communication. The model is linear as well as circular – it opposes the concept of linearity and circularity individually. At the part where the spiral begins, it is first very small, but it becomes bigger as it grows.

Similar with the spiral of this helix, human communication is also a dynamic process. Communication becomes bigger and more dynamic as we grow older. Our vocabulary and experience started to be enhanced when we were still crying and making random sounds when we were younger. This kind of communication is flexible, evolutionary and non-linear.

Here is the importance of Helical Model of Communication. This model of communication brings in the concept of time, wherein it shows that the continuousness of the communication process and relational interactions are very important to the communication progress of a person. Communication progresses with age as our experience and vocabulary evolve and grow.

23. Use Berlo's communication model to identify what is being asked: This is the entirety of the message. This covers from the beginning to the end.a. Codeb. Treatmentc. Contentd. No correct answere. Helixf. Message​


f. Message.

Berlo's communication model is linear and is a development of the Shannon and Weaver communication model. One of the characteristics of Berlo's communication model is that it focuses on the process of encoding and decoding.

Message

What is meant by message or message elements in Berlo's communication model is the substance sent by the sender/source or source to the recipient of the message.

Messages sent by the sender/source or sources can be in the form of sound, text, video, or other media. In message elements, there are several factors that influence messages, namely content, message elements, treatment, structure, and code.

Content

Content refers to the material in the message chosen by the sender/source or source to express its purpose. Content or content has elements and structure.

Elements

Elements concern several nonverbal matters such as language, gestures, body language and so on. In the message there are always several elements that complement the contents of the message or content.

Treatment

Treatment refers to the packaging of the message which includes how the message is sent to the recipient of the message and has an effect on the feedback given by the receiver or recipient of the message. It should also be remembered that excessive treatment of messages will actually hinder the course of communication.

Structure

Structure refers to the structure of the message that impacts the effectiveness of a message. The message can be the same, but the structure of the message that is not good will make the message cannot be received properly by the receiver or the recipient of the message.

Code

Code refers to the message code in terms of how the message is sent, for example language as a means of communication, body language, gestures, music, and culture. Through codes, we give or receive messages. The message will be very clear if the message codes are very good. On the other hand, a bad message code can lead to misinterpretation.
