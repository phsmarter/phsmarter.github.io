13. Give the different types of enterprises in the Philippine industrial sector according to size of employment and the amount of capital invested. Explain and give examples.


Answer:

In the Philippine industrial sector, enterprises can be classified according to size of employment and the amount of capital invested. Here are some common types of enterprises in the Philippines, along with explanations and examples:

Small enterprises: These are enterprises that employ fewer than 50 employees and have a capital investment of less than PHP 3 million. Examples of small enterprises in the Philippines include small retail stores, food kiosks, and small service businesses.

Medium enterprises: These are enterprises that employ between 50 and 200 employees and have a capital investment of between PHP 3 million and PHP 15 million. Examples of medium enterprises in the Philippines include medium-sized manufacturing companies, small-scale construction firms, and wholesale and distribution businesses.

Large enterprises: These are enterprises that employ more than 200 employees and have a capital investment of more than PHP 15 million. Examples of large enterprises in the Philippines include large manufacturing companies, large construction firms, and large service businesses.

It's important to note that these categories are generalizations, and the exact definitions and thresholds for each category may vary depending on the specific industry or sector. Additionally, these categories do not necessarily reflect the financial performance or profitability of an enterprise.

25. What are small and medium enterprises?​


Answer:

Small and medium-sized enterprises (SMEs) are non-subsidiary, independent firms which employ fewer than a given number of employees. This number varies across countries. The most frequent upper limit designating an SME is 250 employees, as in the European Union. However, some countries set the limit at 200 employees, while the United States considers SMEs to include firms with fewer than 500 employees.
