What does a critical analysis include?

Introduction:           Write the title of the work, author and the date of publication           State your own statement of thesis and your main work concept            Identify the main ideas of the book and the topic of the writer

    2. Summary:

            Briefly outline the novel, article or any topic's key ideas             Must include "who", "what", "where", "when", "why", and "how"             You can also decide on the design, theme or perspective

                    The report includes a number of different sections.

21. Write a critical analysis of the story "Hitting Budapest" using Marxist Criticism​.


Marxist Analysis of "Hitting Budapest"

Write a critical analysis of the story "Hitting Budapest" using Marxist Criticism.

Marxist criticism is a way of interpreting literature that focuses on the economic, social, and political context in which the work was produced. In this approach, literature is seen as a reflection of the society in which it was created and as a means of exploring and critiquing social, economic, and political systems.

"Hitting Budapest" is a short story by Ambedkar that tells the story of a group of men who travel to Budapest on a business trip. The story is set in India, where the men are from, and follows their experiences as they explore the city and interact with the locals.

From a Marxist perspective, the story can be analyzed in terms of the economic and social context in which it was written. For example, the fact that the men are able to afford a business trip to Budapest suggests that they are part of the upper or middle class in India. This class status is likely to have shaped their experiences and perceptions of the city, as well as the way in which they are treated by the locals.

The story also touches on issues of power and inequality. The men are tourists, and as such, they are outsiders who are able to observe and interact with the local culture but are not fully integrated into it. This creates a power dynamic in which the men hold a certain level of privilege and authority over the locals, and the locals are depicted as being somewhat subservient to the tourists.

Overall, "Hitting Budapest" can be seen as a reflection of the social and economic divides that exist within India, as well as the power dynamics that shape interactions between different classes and cultures. By exploring these issues, the story invites readers to consider the ways in which these divides and power dynamics shape our experiences and perceptions of the world around us.

25. critical analysis of last leaf​


"The Last Leaf" is a short story by O. Henry that tells the tale of two young women, Sue and Johnsy, who live together in a Greenwich Village apartment. Johnsy becomes ill with pneumonia and becomes convinced that she will die when the last leaf falls from a vine outside her window. Sue is determined to keep her friend alive and enlists the help of their neighbor, Behrman, to paint a leaf on the vine to give Johnsy hope. In the end, Johnsy recovers and discovers that the last leaf on the vine was not real but a painting created by Behrman.

The story is a poignant meditation on the power of hope and the resilience of the human spirit. It explores themes of friendship, sacrifice, and the beauty of art. However, upon closer analysis, several aspects of the story stand out:

Firstly, the story is notable for its use of symbolism. The falling of the last leaf represents death, while the painting of the leaf represents hope and the power of art to inspire and uplift. The vine itself represents the fragility of life and the importance of holding on to hope in the face of adversity.

Secondly, the story highlights the importance of community and the ways in which people can come together to support one another. Sue and Behrman both go to great lengths to help Johnsy, showing the power of selflessness and compassion in times of crisis.

Thirdly, the story raises questions about the nature of art and its role in society. Behrman, the struggling artist, is initially dismissed as a failure by Sue and Johnsy, but it is ultimately his painting that saves Johnsy's life. The story suggests that art has the power to inspire and transform, even in the most dire circumstances.

Overall, "The Last Leaf" is a touching and thought-provoking story that explores some of the deepest themes of human existence. Its use of symbolism, its examination of the human spirit, and its celebration of the power of art all make it a timeless classic that continues to resonate with readers today.
