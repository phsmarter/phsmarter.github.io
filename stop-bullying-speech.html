Answer:

Building a positive school climate

School climate can be difficult to define, though possible to measure. It is the “felt sense” of being in a school, which can arise from a greeting, the way a problem is resolved, or how people work together; it is a school’s “heart and soul,” its “quality and character.” Schools with a positive climate foster healthy development, while a negative school climate is associated with higher rates of student bullying, aggression, victimization, and feeling unsafe.

19. what is the solution to stop bullying?


Answer:

Here are some potential solutions:

1. Education and awareness: Educating young people, parents, and teachers on the negative effects of bullying can help raise awareness and prevent bullying from happening in the first place.

2. Creating safe school environments: Schools can implement policies that promote inclusivity and safety for all students, such as zero-tolerance for bullying policies, creating safe ways for students to report incidents of bullying, and providing resources for students who have been bullied.

3. Peer intervention: Encouraging students to intervene when they see bullying happening can make a difference. This can include encouraging students to speak up when they see their peers being bullied and empowering students to support and stand up for one another.

4. Counseling and support: Providing counseling and support for students who have been bullied can help them cope with the traumatic experience and prevent long-term emotional damage.

5. Legal consequences: In cases where bullying involves criminal offenses like assault or harassment, the perpetrator should face legal consequences under the law. This not only punishes the bully but also sends a strong message that such behavior will not be tolerated.


20. bad effects of bullying and how to stop bullying essay?​


Answer:

Bullying can affect everyone those who are bullied, those who bully, and those who witness bullying. Bullying is linked to many negative outcomes including impacts on mental health, substance use, and suicide. It is important to talk to kids to determine whether bullying or something else is a concern. Kids who are bullied can experience negative physical, social, emotional, academic, and mental health issues. Kids who bully others can also engage in violent and other risky behaviors into adulthood. Media reports often link bullying with suicide. However, most youth who are bullied do not have thoughts of suicide or engage in suicidal behaviors. This risk can be increased further when these kids are not supported by parents, peers, and schools. Bullying can make an unsupportive situation worse.

Republic Act 10627, or the Anti-Bullying Act (the “Act”), aims to protect children enrolled in kindergarten, elementary, and secondary schools and learning centers (collectively, “Schools”) from being bullied. It requires Schools to adopt policies to address the existence of bullying in their respective institutions.

Bullying research has traditionally been dominated by largescale cohort studies focusing on the personality traits of bullies and victims. These studies focus on bullying prevalence, risk and protective factors, and negative outcomes.
