2. An aqueous solution is labeled 16% H2SO4, express the concentration of the solution in terms of Molarity​


Answer:

molarity = 1.79 M

Explanation:

Note: The density of 16% H2SO4 solution is 1.1094 g/m L.

Assuming that the mass of the solution is 100 g

mass solute = 16 g

mass solvent = 84 g

Calculating the number of moles of solute

n solute = 16 g × (1 mol / 98.077 g) = 0.16314 mol

Calculating the liters of solution

L solution = 100 g × (1 m L / 1.1094 g) × (1 L / 1000 m L)

L solution = 0.090139 L

Calculating the molarity

molarity = n solute / L solution

molarity = 0.16314 mol / 0.090139 L

molarity = 1.79 M

14. Calculate the molarity of a solution that contains 70 g of H2SO4 in 280 mL of solution.


Explanation:

Molarity is one of the commonly used units in chemistry. Molarity is defined as the number of moles of solute per liter of solution --- that is,

[tex]\boxed{\text{molarity} = \frac{\text{moles of solute}}{\text{volume of solution}}}[/tex]

Note: The volume of the solution must be expressed in liters (L).

---------------------------------------------------------

Solution:

Step 1: List the given quantities.

mass of solute = 70 g H₂SO₄

volume of solution = 280 mL = 0.280 L

Step 2: Calculate the molar mass of solute.

molar mass of solute = (1.008 g/mol × 2) + (32.07 g/mol × 1) + (16.00 g/mol × 4)

molar mass of solute = 98.086 g/mol

Step 3: Calculate the number of moles of solute.

[tex]\text{moles of solute} = \frac{\text{mass of solute}}{\text{molar mass of solute}}[/tex]

[tex]\text{moles of solute} = \frac{\text{70 g}}{\text{98.086 g/mol}}[/tex]

moles of solute = 0.7136594 mol

Step 4: Calculate the molarity of the solution.

[tex]\text{molarity} = \frac{\text{moles of solute}}{\text{volume of solution}}[/tex]

[tex]\text{molarity} = \frac{\text{0.7136594 mol}}{\text{0.280 L}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{\text{molarity} = 2.55 \: M}[/tex]

[tex]\\[/tex]

Therefore, the molarity of the solution is 2.55 M.

19. The concentrated sulfuric acid we use in the laboratory is 98.0 percent H2SO4 by mass. Calculate the molarity of the acid solution. The density of the solution is 1.83 g/mL


Solution:

Step 1: Calculate the molar mass of solute (H₂SO₄).

molar mass = (1.008 g/mol × 2) + (32.07 g/mol × 1) + (16.00 g/mol × 4)

molar mass = 98.086 g/mol

Step 2: Determine the mass of solute and solution.

98.0 percent H₂SO₄ by mass means that there are 98.0 grams of H₂SO₄ in 100 grams of solution.

Step 3: Calculate the number of moles of solute.

[tex]\text{moles of solute = 98.0 g H₂SO₄} × \frac{\text{1 mol H₂SO₄}}{\text{98.086 g H₂SO₄}}[/tex]

[tex]\text{moles of solute = 0.99912 mol}[/tex]

Step 4: Calculate the volume of solution.

[tex]\text{volume of solution} = \frac{\text{mass of solution}}{\text{density of solution}}[/tex]

[tex]\text{volume of solution} = \frac{\text{100 g}}{\text{1.83 g/mL}}[/tex]

[tex]\text{volume of solution = 54.645 mL}[/tex]

Step 5: Calculate the molarity of solution.

[tex]\text{molarity} = \frac{\text{moles of solute}}{\text{volume of solution (L)}}[/tex]

[tex]\text{molarity} = \frac{\text{0.99912 mol}}{\text{0.054645 L}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{\text{molarity = 18.3 M}}[/tex]

21. What is the percent concentration of a hydrochloric acid having a 12.3 molar concentration?


SOLUTION:

Step 1: List the given values.

[tex]\begin{aligned} & M = 12.3 \: M = \text{12.3 mol/L} \\ & MM_{\text{solute}} = \text{36.458 g/mol} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 2: Calculate the number of moles of solute.

Assume that the volume of solution is 1 L or 1000 mL.

[tex]\begin{aligned} n_{\text{solute}} & = (M)(V_{\text{solution}}) \\ & = \text{(12.3 mol/L)(1 L)} \\ & = \text{12.3 mol} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 3: Calculate the mass of solute (HCl).

[tex]\begin{aligned} mass_{\text{solute}} & = (n_{\text{solute}})(MM_{\text{solute}}) \\ & = \text{(12.3 mol)(36.458 g/mol)} \\ & = \text{448 g} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 4: Calculate the percent by mass per volume.

[tex]\begin{aligned} \%(m/v) & = \frac{mass_{\text{solute}}}{V_{\text{solution}}} \times 100\% \\ & = \frac{\text{448 g}}{\text{1000 mL}} \times 100\% \\ & = \boxed{44.8\%} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, the percent concentration of HCl solution is 44.8%.

Further explanation  

The concentration of a substance can be expressed in several quantities such as moles, percent (%) weight/volume,), molarity, molality, parts per million (ppm), or mole fraction.  

Percent mass of the component shows the total mass of the component in the overall mass of a compound/mixture  

[tex]\tt \%mass=\dfrac{mass~solute}{mass~solution}\times 100\%[/tex]

Solution = Solute + Solvent  

The mole fraction shows the mole ratio of a substance to the mole of solution /mixture  

Can be formulated :

[tex]\tt xa=\dfrac{na}{na+nb}[/tex]  

xa + xb = 1

Given

8% (w/w) aqueous  H₂SO₄ solution

Required

the mole fraction

Solution

For 100 g solution :

H₂SO₄ = 8% x 100 g = 8 g

H₂O = 92% x 100 g = 92 g

mol H₂SO₄ :

= mass : molar mass

= 8 g : 98 g/mol

= 0.082

mol H₂O :

= 92 g : 18 g/mol

= 5.1

total mol :

= 0.082 + 5.1

= 5.182

mol fraction of H₂SO₄ :

= 0.082 : 5.182

= 0.016
