Answer:

The use of active or passive voice is a fundamental distinction in English, and one that causes trouble for many writers – including native English speakers!

Growing up in American schools, students are often taught that they should avoid the passive voice because it is “weak.” However, the choice between active and passive is actually quite nuanced.

Depending on the ideas you are trying to express and the conventions of the discipline/journal in which you are writing, the passive voice can be an appropriate, sophisticated, and even preferable choice over the active voice. Nevertheless, the active voice is sometimes a far better choice, and you may use both in the same article depending on the context and content of your sentences and the section of your paper you are writing.

Yes I already did experience an earthquake.

I was 13 years old back then. I was sleeping in my house, having bunked the republic day celebrations at school. The 2nd term examinations had just ended a day earlier and I just wanted to sleep. My parents were invited at a family friend's place for breakfast and they allowed me to take a holiday and rest in.

Suddenly the whole house started shaking. I tried getting up but couldn't. My sister shouted from the other room to come out. I made my way out of my room with great difficulty. The house was in a mess. All the kitchen items were on the floor. All the food that was kept in the fridge spilled out. I met my sister near the hall and along with my grandfather we came out of the house.

The moment we came out we heard a big loud sound and we saw the adjacent building had come down. I started crying. I had friends in that building and I was too much in shock to understand what was hapoening.

Just then my sister asked,

“Do you know where mummy papa have gone?”

I replied in the negative. My heart started racing. All the people were out on the streets and nobody wanted to go back inside. My sister suggested she would go look for our parents and told me to be strong and look after my grandfather.

Before my sister could leave we saw our parents returning. Thankfully they were alright. The few things that are still etched in my memory after so many years are,

People risking their lives to take out debris and trying to help out people who were stuck and couldn't move. In one such instance there was a man who kept talking to a woman stuck inside till the fire brigade came to rescue her. She had lost hope as she her husband lay beside her dead. The man kept motivating her that there is more to life.

Then there were people who rummaged inside the debris just to find some cash or jewellery. People took whatever valuable items they could find.

A friend of mine lost her right hand when a whole part of the lift fell upon her. She was rescued 24 hours later.

What is an Argumentative Essay?

-An Argumentative Essay is a persuasive form of writing. It aims to change the mind of the reader by convincing the reader to agree with the writer's point of view.

Features of an Argumentative Essay

- An argumentative Essay attempts to be highly persuasive and logical. It usually assumes that the reader disagrees with the writer. An effective argumentative essay has the following features:

1. It clearly states a claim or position on an issue.

2. It provides reasons including facts and opinions and supports these reasons with evidences to prove its point.

3. It also presents opposing views (counterarguments) and explains their weaknesses.

Take note of the following terms related to argumentative essay.

A claim refers to the author's position on an issue or problem. This is usually the thesis statement or main idea of an argumentative essay.

Support is any material that is used to prove a claim. This usually consists of reasons and evidence.

Reasons are statements made to justify an action, decision, or belief.

Evidence is the specific references, quotations, examples, and opinions that support a claim. It may include statistics, personal experience, or opinions of experts.

Counterargument is an argument made to oppose another argument. A good argument anticipates opposing claims and provides counterarguments to disprove or answer them.
