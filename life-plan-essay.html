1. Write an essay on the topic, "Careful family planning leads to happy family life."​


Answer:

Family planning helps protect women from any health risks that may occur before, during or after childbirth. These include high blood pressure, gestational diabetes, infections, miscarriage and stillbirth.

According to studies, women who bear more than 4 children are at increased risk for maternal mortality, so they need to plan accordingly. Women who get pregnant after the age of 35 are vulnerable to health risks, so they should be protected through careful planning as well.

Also, by reducing unintended pregnancies, family planning also removes the option for unsafe abortion.

When a pregnancy is unplanned, ill-timed, or too closely spaced, it affects the mother’s health which, in turn, affects the health and safety of her unborn child.

Moreover, an infant will have a greater risk of death and weak health if the mother dies while giving birth.

When a couple or an individual is aware of their rights and knows what their options are, they can make sound decisions in life.

Family planning can help a couple or an individual decide on what’s best for their sexual and reproductive health. By carefully spacing pregnancies, a couple can build financial security, allowing them to raise a family that they can properly care for. A family that is properly cared for is a healthy family, and a healthy family is the foundation of a healthy community.

13. Write an essay or a paragraph on how you plan to use the skills you learned in your daily life


Skills we learned and how it can be used in everyday life

(particularly skills we learned in school)

1. Teamwork

These teamwork skills are extremely essential in the real world, especially in project management and other supervisory positions. You will be doing yourself a favor if you devote time in your courses and high school classes to truly learning how to collaborate with people in an effective and organic manner. Understanding how to function as part of a team, whether as a leader or a member, is essential for future success.

2. Motivation and Personal Strengths

Learning how to recognize your own particular capabilities and how to inspire yourself is a skill that will serve you well throughout your life. You must be motivated to go to work, tidy your house, and even socialize on a daily basis. Knowing your strengths is also vital for assessing whether or not a new job offer is suited for you. This is especially true for tasks that most people despise yet must be completed, such as filing taxes, paying bills, and cleaning out your car.

3. Resourcefulness

Having a good knowledge of how to be resourceful is, of course, a very desirable skill for practically everyone. When you're in a bind and need to get out of it, whether at work or at home, knowing how things function and how to acquire help through means other than your own is something you'll surely apply in real life.

4. Thinking differently

A malleable or open mind is a direct skill that results from this. Learning new abilities and accepting new and foreign ideas necessitates an open mind. It will be just as vital to be taught diverse methods to think and apply critical thinking to different subjects during your studies as it will be throughout your life. You'll be considerably more versatile and open to new ways of working and accomplishing tasks, whether they're work-related or not, if you can think creatively.

Group assignments will almost certainly provide you with some insight into how to motivate and encourage others, how to fit into a team, and how to understand people’s emotions, moods, strengths and weaknesses.
