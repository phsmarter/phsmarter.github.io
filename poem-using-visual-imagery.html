28. The author used imagery in the poem,such as visual,auditory,and kinesthetic imagery.write the words that suggest


Answer:

Visual imagery: star, grass, leaves, birds, and flowers  

Auditory imagery: sound, rustling, chirping, buzzing and flopping  

Kinesthetic imagery: tend, re-arrange, weed, harvest and do again.

What is visual imagery in poetry?

In this type of poetic imagery, the poet appeals to the reader's sense of sight by describing something seen by the poem's speaker or narrator. Colors, brightness, shapes, sizes, and patterns are all possible.

Example:

The puppy sprawled across her couch, legs twitching feverishly, and she knew he must be dreaming of the squirrels he tries to capture every morning. He took her hand and led her to the edge of the cliff. The verdant glades that spread before them dazzled her with every shade of green. She accepted the bouquet. It was filled with her favorite flowers - white roses, purple irises, and soft sprays of baby’s breath. The flowers swayed in the breeze, almost as if they were dancing in her delight.

What is auditory imagery in poetry?

Auditory imagery is a method of explaining things, ideas, and actions by using sounds that appeal to our sense of hearing. It means to use words and literary devices in such a way that readers experience sounds when reading poetry or prose.

Example:

Where are the songs of Spring? Ay, where are they?

Think not of them, thou hast thy music too,

While barred clouds bloom the soft-dying day,

And touch the stubble-plains with rosy hue;

Then in a wailful choir the small gnats mourn

Among the river sallows, borne aloft

Or sinking as the light wind lives or dies;

And full-grown lambs loud bleat from hilly bourn;

Hedge-crickets sing; and now with treble soft

The red-breast whistles from a garden-croft;

And gathering swallows twitter in the skies.

What is kinesthetic imagery?

The poet appeals to the reader's sense of motion in this form of poetic imagery. It can refer to the movement of the poem's speaker/narrator or objects around them, and it can include the sensation of speeding in a vehicle, slow sauntering, or a sudden jolt when stopping.

Example:

A sudden blow: the great wings beating still

Above the staggering girl, her thighs caressed

By the dark webs, her nape caught in his bill,

He holds her helpless breast upon his breast.

Winter and me

A rainy new year's eve that robs my moods..

A chirp of such cricket that embezzles my ears..

A cool windy wind that tries to make me chills..

These thoughts hold me a loner..

How much does another offer after tonight..

With an alluring smiling face of a sunrise..

Wishing for a quiet, vivid green world..

A pleasant day is when the summer changes the weather..

Time

Tick tock...tick tock...

Life is ticking down on your internal clock.

The recollection that enjoys as if it took place yesterday

turn to flickers of memories that seem to wither away.

Folks you once knew

wander by with no clue.

The moments we always had in common

remain as if you were even there.

Decades fly...friends die...

but you never know when you`ll tell your last goodbye.

Oh, how I wish I could travel back in time,

keep wasting it with near and dear ones and treasure what used to be mine.

Or even go back even more,

being such a kid in a candy store.

How I appreciate the way I used to feel

on Xmas eve day while Santa was real.

But back to reality...back to today,

family is limited and photographs begin to drift away.

Tick tock...tick tock...

I hoped I could cast the time on this clock.

Further explanation:

Couplets are two-line stanzas. Couplets typically rhyme, but this is not a requirement. In poetry, a stanza is a collection of lines that are typically divided by a blank line. Couplets, from the Old French term cople, which means two, are the name given to 2-line stanzas.

What is an example of a stanza in a poem?

Shakespeare's most well-known sonnet could serve as an illustration of a stanza in a poem. The sonnet is composed of three quatrains, an ABAB, CDCD, EFEF rhyme pattern, and a closed couplet of GG.

How do you compose a poem using two stanzas?

Vary your rhyme systems. A stanza may have five lines that rhyme ABABC (the first and third lines rhyme, the second and fourth lines rhyme, and the fifth line doesn't rhyme with any of the others), plus an additional line. The second stanza might have six lines or follow the same format.
