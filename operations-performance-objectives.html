1. what are the operation performance objectives and it's trade offs?​


Answer:

The operations performance objectives are a set of goals that organizations aim to achieve in their production processes. They include quality, speed, dependability, flexibility, and cost. Each objective has trade-offs that organizations need to consider in their decision-making. For example, achieving high quality may increase costs and decrease speed, while focusing on speed may compromise quality and dependability. Flexibility may lead to increased costs, but it can also enhance customer satisfaction. Organizations need to balance these trade-offs and determine which objectives to prioritize based on their strategic goals and customer needs.
