Explanation:

Entertaining Speech

If you’ve been to a birthday party before, that awkward toast given by friends or family of the lucky birthday person is considered to fall under the definition of an entertaining speech.

Informative Speech

When you want to educate your audience about a certain topic, you’ll probably opt to create an informative speech.

Demonstrative Speech

ِFrom its name we can imagine that a demonstrative speech is the type of speech you want to give to demonstrate how something works or how to do a certain thing.

Persuasive Speech

speech is said to be persuasive if the speaker is trying to prove why his or her point of view is right, and by extension, persuade the audience to embrace that point of view.

Motivational Speech

injecting confidence into the audience, the speaker is able to guide them toward achieving the goals they set together.

Impromptu Speech

a speech given on the spot without any prior planning or preparation.

Oratorical Speech

Their purpose could be to celebrate a certain event like a graduation, to address serious issues and how to deal with them, or to mourn losses and give comfort like a eulogy at a funeral.

Debate Speech

Debate speeches benefit the speaker since it develops their critical thinking, public speaking, and research among other benefits.

*You’ll find debate speeches to be common in public forums, legislative sessions, and court trials.

Forensic Speech

According to the American Forensic Association (AFA), the definition of a forensic speech is the study and practice of public speaking and debate. It’s said to be practiced by millions of high school and college students.

Special Occasion Speech

Special occasion speeches can include introductory speeches, ceremonial speeches, and tributary speeches. You may notice that all these can be categorized as entertaining speeches.

11. Identify the differences among the types of speech contexts using the graphic organizer below. 1. Typee- 2. Description- 3. Examples


The difference between the types of speech and the context of the speech must be seen from the content of the speech and the theme of the speech.

The types of speech contexts:

1. Informative speech

One type of speech that is known to many people is informative speech. Informative speech is a speech that has the purpose of conveying important knowledge or information. The information will be given to the communicant or listener. This is intended so that the listener knows what is being conveyed. In addition, so that listeners understand the information that has been conveyed. It is also hoped that listeners will receive the information conveyed. According to Monroe, Ehninger and Gronbeck informative speech can be divided. First, oral reports or oral reports.

Examples of oral reports are committee reports, scientific reports, project reports, annual reports and so on. Second, teaching or oral instruction. For example, a teacher who is explaining a subject matter. In addition, superiors who explain a job to their employees.

2. Argumentative Speech

The next type of speech is argumentative speech. Argumentative speech is speech that contains several things. Such as arguments, arguments, data or reasons. These things serve to reject or support a statement. Such as certain beliefs, opinions or opinions. To strengthen the acceptability of the argument, other things are also needed. Such as statistics, factual data, evidence or testimony of a figure or expert.

3. Recreational Speech

The next type of speech is recreational speech. Recreational speeches are also often referred to as family speeches. This type of speech will generally present a joy. This joy can be enjoyed together with a sense of brotherhood or kinship. Therefore, the person giving the speech must have an ability. The ability in question is to display things that can create an atmosphere of hospitality. Humor and jokes can also be used to warm the atmosphere. The purpose of this recreational speech is to evoke a family atmosphere. Both related to sadness and things related to joy.

4. Persuasive Speech

The fourth type of speech is persuasive speech. The purpose of a persuasive speech is to influence the listener. Persuasion is the process of influencing opinions. In addition, persuasive speech can also affect the attitudes and actions of a person. The trick is to use psychological manipulation. It will make the listener act as he pleases. Attitudes, actions and opinions are a personality phenomenon. Therefore, a communicator must know several factors. These factors affect human personality. The purpose of a persuasive speech is to take action. In addition, the goal can also leave an action or behavior of a person. This is done according to the wishes of the speaker or communicator.

14. Different types of speech context by giving it's definition, description and 2 examples​


Different types of speech context by giving it's definition, description and 2 examples​. It was already noted that many think of “communication” as public speaking or as a situation in which one Speaker addresses many Listeners. Others think of it as involving only two people talking to each other, but communication can involve more than two people or an audience. It can be a group discussion on cancer awareness, an organizational program to save the environment, a radio or TV show focused on corruption in government, or it could even be a showcase of tribal dances from all over the Philippines. Indeed, communication may be classified into different types based on the number of participants in the Process

The first Type of Speech Context is INTRAPERSONAL Communication, meaning communicating with oneself. What does this mean? Intrapersonal Communication may be seen in situations involving talking to or writing to oneself, even thinking to oneself. To clarify further, talking to oneself may mean repeating a song heard while looking in the mirror, memorizing out loud a dialogue in a play, or berating oneself in a mutter on the way to school for forgetting one’s homework. A teenager may write in a diary or on a private blog which nobody else is supposed to read. One also writes on sticky notes to remind oneself of things to be done. And, of course, there is daydreaming, meditating, or mental planning.

Clearly, in Intrapersonal Communication, the Speaker and the Listener are one and the same: YOU. You send the Message to yourself and you yourself receive that Message.

Beyond INTRAPERSONAL Communication are several Types of Speech Context which we are more familiar with, such as INTERPERSONAL Communication which involves more than one person. Of course, it is not just the number of participants that determine each particular type of communication. They can each be differentiated by its purpose. Remember? Communication is always intentional...
