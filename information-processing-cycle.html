1. What is information processing cycle?


Information Processing Cycle typically refers to the step-by-step processing of information by a computer or any analytical machine. The sequence of events are as follows:

 

1.       Input – entering data to be processed.

2.       Processing – act of performing specific operations to the entered data.

3.       Storage – saving of the processed data for a scheduled output.

4.       Output – the end-product or results of the first three steps.

 


2. Explain the Information Process Cycle.


Answer:

The sequence of events in processing information, which includes (1) input, (2) processing, (3) storage and (4) output. These processes work together and repeat over and over. ... Storage—saving data, programs, or output for future use.

The answer is letter D Journalizing.

The process of recording information in the journals to record business transactions is called journalizing. The accounting cycle is the complete process of recording and processing all the company’s financial transactions. One of the main responsibilities of a bookkeeper is to keep track of the full accounting cycle from start to end. The cycle repeats annually as long as a company remains in business. However, Some companies prepare financial statements every quarter.  

Eight Steps in the Accounting Cycle

Here are the main steps in the  accounting cycle that should be done in order:

Analyze transactions by scrutinizing source documents. Journalize transactions in the journal. Post journal entries in the ledger under accounts.  Prepare a trial balance of the accounts and  fill the worksheet.  Prepare financial reports. Journalize and post adjusting entries. Journalize and post-closing entries. Prepare a post-closing trial balance.

Importance of Accounting  

Accounting is essential in running a business because it helps business owners in the following conditions:

Track income and expenditures .Ensure statutory compliance. Provide investors with quantitative financial information.  Used in making business decisions.

28. Which phase of the information processing cycle is used when you save the processed data for future use?​


Information processing cycle is a sequence of events consisting of Input, Processing, Storage & Output. To understand more about what is information processing cycle it is a good idea to study about data processing cycle also. These events are similar as in case of data processing cycle. For a computer to perform useful work, the computer has to receive instructions and data from the outside world. The computer receives data and instructions during the INPUT stage of the information processing cycle. Useful information results are obtained when appropriate inputs are applied to data. Applying instructions to data takes place during the PROCESSING stage of the information processing cycle. To avoid having to re-enter data and instructions or reprocess information, computers can save information. Saving information on a computer occurs during the STORAGE phase of the information processing cycle. This is followed by the result in the OUTPUT stage. Computer Processing Cycle is a similar process with similar steps by which data is fed to a computer.
