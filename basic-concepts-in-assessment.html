3. what is your reflection about the basic concepts, theories and principles in assessing learning alternative methods?​


Answer:

Assessing learning through alternative methods involves a departure from traditional evaluation techniques, such as standardized tests, and embraces a more comprehensive and holistic approach. The basic concepts, theories, and principles in this area emphasize the importance of considering diverse forms of evidence, such as portfolios, projects, presentations, and self-assessments, to gauge students' understanding and progress. These methods prioritize active engagement, critical thinking, and practical application of knowledge. By focusing on the process rather than just the end result, alternative assessment approaches encourage deeper learning, creativity, and problem-solving skills. Additionally, they recognize the individuality of learners, acknowledging that different individuals may excel in different ways and providing multiple pathways for demonstrating mastery. Overall, alternative assessment methods promote a learner-centered approach that fosters a more accurate and comprehensive understanding of students' abilities and achievements.
