15. What are the practical applications of a concept paper?.


Answer:

The Research Concept Paper is completed prior to the dissertation proposal and serves as a development tool and summary of the planned dissertation. The essential point of the Concept Paper is to explain the importance of a particular research project.

The Concept Paper initiates the dissertation phase of a doctoral degree which follows the completion of necessary coursework and training, and represents a culmination of the student’s learning. The dissertation is a student’s final academic effort to synthesize course material by applying their learning to a research project. The project is expected to add new information to the field of study. The Concept Paper acts as a summary of this project.

The Concept Paper, although highly abridged, is comprised of many of the same items found in a dissertation. These specific elements of the Concept Paper may vary depending upon the academic program and the chosen degree. Programs typically provide a grading rubric that serves as an outline for the required components and student are encouraged to follow those rubrics closely in developing their Concept Paper.

19. what are the examples or practical applications of projectile motion?​


Answer:

Projectile motion refers to the motion of an object that is launched into the air and moves along a curved path under the influence of gravity. There are many practical applications of projectile motion in various fields, some of which are:

Sports: Projectile motion is used in various sports like baseball, cricket, golf, tennis, and basketball. In baseball, for example, the pitcher throws the ball at an angle and speed to make it difficult for the batter to hit it. The trajectory of the ball is a projectile motion, and the batter needs to predict its path to hit it.

Military: Projectile motion is also used in military applications, such as artillery and missile systems. The trajectory of the projectile is calculated to hit the target accurately.

Engineering: Projectile motion is used in designing and testing various machines and vehicles, such as rockets, airplanes, and cars. Engineers use the principles of projectile motion to calculate the trajectory of these objects and ensure their safe landing.

Physics: Projectile motion is used in physics experiments to study the laws of motion, gravity, and other related principles. It is also used to demonstrate the conservation of energy and momentum.

Entertainment: Projectile motion is used in amusement park rides, such as roller coasters and free-fall rides, to create thrilling experiences for riders.

Overall, projectile motion is an important concept that has many practical applications in various fields, including sports, military, engineering, physics, and entertainment.
