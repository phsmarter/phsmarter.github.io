Answer:

INTRODUCTION

The work of this group is an integral component of the University of Southern Mississippi’s comprehensive efforts to plan keep students and faculty safe while also offering learning and social opportunities to keep students engaged. We know that individual responsibility and commitment to abide by health guidelines is key to the success of our institutional efforts. Institutional controls are only as effective as the willingness of individuals to carry them out. It will be imperative for everyone—faculty, staff, and students—to take responsibility for their actions and adhere to the guidelines of the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC) and the Mississippi State Department of Health (MSDH), as well as the guidelines issued by USM to protect themselves and others while helping to contain the spread of COVID-19.

A successful semester will depend on robust testing, adroit contact tracing, and the timely isolation or quarantine of ill and exposed campus community members. USM is monitoring developments related to COVID-19 and receiving counsel from state public health officials.

PURPOSE

This document provides guidance specific to the University of Southern Mississippi that will allow departments to manage operations in a safer and more effective manner throughout the COVID-19 pandemic. The objective is not to eliminate all potential risks, but to provide a systematic and feasible path for operation within the pandemic environment.

The guidance in this document is subject to change, even after these plans are put into place. The University community will need to remain flexible, patient, and empathetic as we move.

COMMUNITY RESPONSIBILITY ACKNOWLEDGEMENT

Prior to returning to campus, all campus community members must acknowledge:

The serious nature of COVID-19

The importance of each individual’s knowledge of the risks presented by the virus

The need to monitor their own health

The need to notify appropriate personnel if they are symptomatic and/or exposed and be tested if necessary

Everyone has a personal responsibility to practice social distancing, frequent handwashing, cough/sneeze etiquette, proper tissue usage and disposal, avoidance of touching their face, and respecting others.


5. sample campaign speech​


Answer:

Good morning/afternoon fellow students. Before anything else, allow me to sincerely thank you for acknowledging my candidacy for senator in our beloved university. Since freshman year, I have been an advocate for our student body. Although I have not held a position similar to what I am running for now, I have always been actively helping our student government in my humble capacity as First Year representative. Through my efforts, the freshmen have earned two seats in the school council, empowering them more by making their voices heard. The constant complain of first year students about not having a place for them to hang out during their free time because most study areas and even libraries are dominantly occupied by older students, was finally solved when I made representations in the school board to ask for funds for the construction of at least two study sheds exclusively for the use of freshmen. The construction was done before the end of the first semester, and the facilities are now serving their purpose to the delight of our youngest students. In the second semester, I batted for transparency by demanding the student council’s treasurer to give a quarterly financial report which was to be published in our school publication. This measure was carried out and as a result, we now have a good knowledge on how the funds derived from our school fees are being used by our student government. Towards the end of the fourth quarter, I crafted a petition, demanding better services from our food providers through the sale of nutritious food and banning unhealthy snack and drinks. The petition is still being heard and evaluated by the school administration. If given your trust this year as senator, I pledge to finish the programs that I started last year, and pursue other endeavors that would make the lives of every student in this school better. I will continue to advocate for transparency and clean governance. I will continue to be your voice in the student council, and I shall not tire of listening to your sentiments about issues directly affecting your studies. I will find ways to make the enrollment process easier and more efficient so that students don’t have to wear themselves out for one day only to be told to come back the next day. The enrollment should not be long and tedious so students don’t have to spend more for their extended stay in the school. My self-driven advocacy for all of us is what drives me forward. On Election Day, I ask for your support so in return, I can continue making your voices heard. My dream has always been to serve others, and this is something I have been able to do in the past. I pray that this school year will be another time for me to improve myself as a leader and another opportunity to live my dream once more. I promise not to betray the trust that you have given me in the past. I enjoin you all to vote for me and the rest of my team not only because you know many of us but because you know deep in your hearts that we can deliver the services that you truly deserve. My record speaks for my capacity to serve. I care about your interests and your voice. I care about providing equal opportunities to every student who come to study in this university. I care about making your student life as enjoyable and as fulfilling as possible. Let us make this journey together, and let us all reach for our dreams and aspirations. Come share your ideas, and I will make them happen. Thank you and have a great day.

There are four main kinds of speech delivery: impromptu, extemporaneous, manuscript, and memorized. Answer:

Ladies and gentlemen,

As I stand before you today, I am humbled and honored to announce my candidacy for public office. This election is an opportunity for us to come together as a community, to share our ideas and our values, and to work towards a better future for all.

Firstly, let us reflect on our past. The problems that our society faces today did not arise overnight. They are the result of years of neglect, poor planning, and ineffective leadership. We need to learn from our mistakes and chart a new course for our future.

Secondly, we need to prioritize our goals. As a candidate for public office, I am committed to putting the needs of our community first. I will work tirelessly to ensure that our children have access to quality education, our families have access to affordable healthcare, and our seniors can retire with dignity.

Thirdly, we must acknowledge the challenges we face. There are no easy solutions to the problems that we face as a community, but I believe that by working together, we can overcome them. I am committed to fostering an environment of open communication and collaboration, where we can all work together towards common goals.

Finally, we must look towards the future. Our community has the potential to achieve great things, but we need bold leadership and a clear vision to get there. As your representative, I will be a tireless advocate for our community, fighting for the resources and support we need to succeed.

In conclusion, I ask for your support and your vote. Together, we can build a brighter future for our community, and I am committed to working towards that goal every day. Thank you.
