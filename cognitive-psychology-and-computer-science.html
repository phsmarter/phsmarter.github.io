Universal set/ Total number of students: 165

Number of students taking:

All subjects Calculus, Psychology, and Computer Science: 8Calculus (Ca) and Computer Science (CS) : 33Psychology (Ps)  and Computer Science (CS) : 20Psychology (Ps) and Calculus (Ca): 24Calculus (Ca): 79Psychology (Ps): 83Computer Science (CS): 63

Find the number of students taking:

Calculus only:  79 - (8 + 33 + 24) = 79 - (50) = 29

Psychology only: 83 - (8 + 20 + 24) = 83 - (52) = 31

Computer Science only: 63 - (8 + 33 + 20) = 63 - 61 = 2

= 163 - (8 + 33 + 24 + 20 + 29 + 31 + 2)

= 163 - 147

= 16

= 29 + 31 + 2

2. define cognitive psychology​


"Cognitive psychology is the scientific study of the mind as an information processor. "

Cognitive psychology is the scientific study of the mind as an information processor. Cognitive psychologists try to build up cognitive models of the information processing that goes on inside people's minds, including perception, attention, language, memory, thinking, and consciousness.

5. Adolescence is associated with various cognitive, physical, and psychological changes.​


Answer:

Yes, that is correct. Adolescence is a period of development between childhood and adulthood, and it is associated with various changes in different aspects of a person's life.

Cognitive changes during adolescence include improvements in problem-solving skills, abstract thinking, and decision-making abilities. Physical changes during adolescence include rapid growth, changes in body shape, and the onset of puberty. Psychological changes during adolescence include an increased sense of independence, the development of identity, and changes in mood and emotion regulation.

These changes are all interconnected and can influence each other. For example, physical changes can affect self-esteem and mood, which in turn can influence cognitive and social development. It is a complex and important period of development that sets the stage for adulthood.

21. to enhance the physical social, emotional, cognitive, psychological, spiritual, and language development of young children?​


Answer:

There are several ways to enhance the physical, social, emotional, cognitive, psychological, spiritual, and language development of young children:

Physical development: Encourage physical activity through play, such as running, jumping, and climbing, as well as regular exercise and outdoor activities. Provide opportunities for children to explore and engage with the environment, such as through gardening and building projects.

Social development: Promote social interaction through play and conversation. Encourage children to share and take turns, and provide opportunities for children to learn about different cultures and perspectives.

Emotional development: Provide a safe and nurturing environment for children to express their feelings and emotions. Encourage children to identify and label their emotions, and to understand and manage their feelings.

Cognitive development: Provide a variety of stimulating and age-appropriate activities and materials, such as puzzles, blocks, and books, to encourage children to explore and learn. Encourage children to ask questions and to think critically.

Psychological development: Encourage children to develop a positive self-image and self-esteem through positive reinforcement and opportunities for success. Provide opportunities for children to explore their interests and passions.

Spiritual development: Encourage children to explore and understand the world around them and their place in it, and to develop an understanding of their own values and beliefs.

Language development: Encourage children to listen, speak, read, and write in a variety of languages and contexts, and to use language for a variety of purposes. Provide opportunities for children to engage in conversation and storytelling, and to listen to and read a variety of texts.

It is also important to note that these areas of development are interconnected and it is essential to provide a holistic approach that addresses all these areas.
