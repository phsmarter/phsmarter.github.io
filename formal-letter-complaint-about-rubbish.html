9. complaint letter about expired foods and product​


Dear ___________,

I feel compelled to let you know about an issue I've repeatedly faced while shopping at your market. While the store is clean, the clerks are polite, and the selection is abundant, one problem stands out: expired products.

In recent months, I have come home from shopping at your store to find that the products I've purchased had already reached or surpassed their prime.

We could debate opinions on "sell by/best by" dates, or discuss food waste, but honestly, I just don't want to pay for expired foods. I appreciate your managers' recent willingness to return or exchange expired items, but this is inconvenient and not time well spent.

While I make every effort to check dates, especially on dairy products, I sometimes forget. It should not be on me to make sure that the food you're selling is fresh. Please address this matter.

Sincerely,

An expiration date or expiry date is a previously determined date after which something should no longer be used, either by operation of law or by exceeding the anticipated shelf life for perishable goods. Expiration dates are applied to selected food products and to some other manufactured products like infant car seats where the age of the product may impact its safe use.[1]

Arbitrary expiration dates are also commonly applied by companies to product coupons, promotional offers and credit cards. In these contexts, the expiration date is chosen for business reasons or to provide some security function rather than any product safety concern.

Expiration date is often abbreviated EXP or ED. The legal definition and usage of the term expiration date will vary between countries and products.
