15. reasons why rizal wrote the noli me tangere and filibusterismo


Jose Rizel wrote Noli Me Tangere and El Filibusterismo for two reasons:

1. It is his way of expression of the things that he personally saw and experienced using the medium of writing. He made use of notable characters on his books to represent real people from real events during the Spanish era. 

2. It was his way of enlightening and awakening the mind of the Filipino people. Through his works, he wanted to make sure that people will feel the same level of concern and love for the beloved country the way he did. He wanted them to feel strong, and empowered, so that they can finally pick up themselves and begin a shift in belief and actions towards a better society. dahil gusto niyang ipamulat sa utak ng mga pilipino na magising ang kanilang pagkamakabayan at ipaglaban ang naghihinangos na bayan.

17. What was the reason why Margaret Atwood wrote the novel, Low Art?


Answer:

"A Low Art" is the point of view of Penelope in "The Odyssey". She wants to show Penelope's agony and pain. It is to show that Penelope although a villain in "The Odyssey" , is not a villain in her own story.

Explanation:

“A Low Art” is an excerpt from The Penelopiad by Margaret Atwood.

In Homer’s “The Odyssey”, Odysseus’ fancy and heroic adventures and Penelope, being the bad guy, were highlighted. However, “A Low Art”, through Penelope’s perspective discussed how Odysseus, her husband, made a fool out of her by the versions of narrations about their relationship.

Penelope narrates how great Odysseus is in making fools for making people believe with his versions of events. Penelope’s agony and pain was shown by “A Low Art” with her version of story— keeping a blind eye to achieve her happy ending. Her little story making is somehow to clear her side.

20. Why did Rizal wrote the novel and called it Noli Me Tangere and El Filibusterismo?


Noli Me Tangere and El Filibusterismo have parallels with regard to intention and goal. Both strive to enlighten the Filipinos about what's going on in the world. We want the people to struggle for their nation so so they have full independence.

Noli Me Tangere (Touch Me Not) is not only an attack on Spanish imperialist rule. It is a nationalistic document. It serves as a calling for the Filipinos to recover their self-confidence, to understand their own dignity, to return to their ancestors ' roots, to declare themselves as the Spaniard's equivalent.

The struggle against Spanish colonization is at the heart of Noli Me Tangere (Touch Me Not) by the Philippine national hero José Rizal. Philippines, named after King Philip II of Spain, was governed by the Spanish Empire as a province from 1565 before the Philippine Revolution ended its reign in 1898.

El Filibusterismo's theme and storyline has been modified to relay the idea that the new form of government in the Philippines will contribute to the collapse of Spain by dishonest politicians, ruled by the friars.

El filibusterismo (lit. Spanish for "filibustering"; as in the Locsín English language, the Revolutionary or Subversion are both conceivable translations), both known by its English alternate title The Rule of Corruption, is the second novel by the Philippine national hero José Rizal

28. among the reasons for the strong opposition of the roman catholic church to the two novels of Dr. jose rizal was?​


Answer:

One of the reasons for the strong opposition of the Roman Catholic Church to the two novels of Dr. Jose Rizal was because the Church saw them as a challenge to its authority and influence over the Filipino people. The novels, "Noli Me Tangere" and "El Filibusterismo," contained criticisms of the Church's practices and the role it played in society, which were seen as controversial at the time. Additionally, the Church may have been concerned about the potential for the novels to inspire rebellion or unrest among the Filipino people.

29. Why did Rizal wrote the novel el filibusterismo?


Answer:

Driven by his undying love for his country, Rizal wrote the novel to expose the ills of Philippine society during the Spanish colonial era. At the time, the Spaniards prohibited the Filipinos from reading the controversial book because of the unlawful acts depicted in the novel.

Why did Dr. Jose Rizal wrote the novel "El Filibusterismo"?

El Filibusterismo was written in dedication to the three martyred priests Mariano Gomez, Jose Burgos, and Jacinto Zamora, whose deaths left an indelible mark in his mind. Like Noli Me Tangere, Filipino aims at enlightening the society, at bringing the Filipinos closer to the truth. But whereas in the first novel, we are encouraged to ask and aspire for change and liberation, in this novel, the society is urged to open its eyes to reality and rebel against the Spanish government for its oppression and abuse.
