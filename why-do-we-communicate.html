20. what is communication and why do we communicate?​


Communication is a process when a person or persons, groups, organizations, and society create, and use information to connect with the environment and other people.

We communicate with others to fulfill our biological needs such as eating and drinking, and to fulfill our psychological needs such as success and happiness. It is also through communication that we can fulfill our emotional needs and improve our mental health

Explanation:

Communication can take the form of verbal and nonverbal. Verbal is communication that is done with spoken language in the form of words, while nonverbal communication is communication using body movements or showing certain attitudes, for example smiling, shaking head, and shrugging.

Communication is a process of delivering information (messages, ideas, ideas) from one party to another. In general, communication is done verbally or verbally that can be understood by both parties. If there is no verbal language that can be understood by both, communication can still be done using body gestures, showing certain attitudes, for example smiling, shaking head, shrugging. This method is known as nonverbal communication.

In simple terms, communication can occur if there are similarities between the delivery of the message and the person who receives the message. communication depends on our ability to understand one another. Humans communicate to share knowledge and experience.

Through communication, the attitudes and feelings of a person or group of people can be understood by other parties. However, communication will only be effective if the message conveyed can be interpreted the same by the recipient of the message. Communication can be interactive, transactive communication.  

The process of communication can be described as follows:

Communicators (senders) who have the intention of communicating with other people send a message to the person in question. The message conveyed can be in the form of information in the form of language or through symbols that can be understood by both parties.Themessageis conveyed or carried through a medium or channel, either directly or indirectly. For example speaking directly by telephone, letter, e-mail, or other media.Media (channel) is a tool that conveys messages from communicators to communicants.Communicant (receiver) receives the message delivered and translates the content of the message it receives into a language that is understood by the communicant itself. Communicant (receiver) provides feedback oraresponse to the message sent to him, whether he understands or understands the message intended by the sender.
