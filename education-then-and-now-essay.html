11. Essay: How do you see the world 50 years from now? In media:In economics:In education:​


Answer:

In media:

Fifty years after the first computer network was connected, most experts say digital life will mostly change humans’ existence for the better over the next 50 years. However, they warn this will happen only if people embrace reforms allowing better cooperation, security, basic rights and economic fairness.

In 50 years, internet use will be nearly as pervasive and necessary as oxygen. Seamless connectivity will be the norm, and it may be impossible to unplug. From amazing advancements to dystopian developments, experts imagine a wide array of possible scenarios for the world 50 years in the future.

In economics:

Since the Industrial Revolution, automation has had a heavy impact on employment that involves routine physical work; this is now intensifying and on top of that we’re seeing technology’s effects on many other areas, including information processing. Looking ahead, it’s estimated that almost 50% of the current jobs in existence will become automated, even in the ‘white-collar’ occupations, which have traditionally been resistant to automation.

These changes are not merely a phenomenon of the post-recession years, nor are they showing up in just one or two economies where they might be expected. Essentially, capital has been invested in technology – leading to greater automation – as opposed to labour. Over the coming 10 years, we will face huge, tectonic forces of globalization and astonishing technological progress – forces that must be confronted and embraced if we are to ensure economic opportunity and inclusiveness for all.

In education:

Education has seen seismic changes in the last few decades. There are people alive that still remember using chalkboards in class, or writing on slates at their desk. Now, though, today's students have a wealth of technology literally at their finger tips. With technology developing at such a rapid pace, it's not stretch to imagine that education in 50 years will look very different.

Classrooms will become less important

This doesn't mean that learning itself will become less relevant. Instead, the actual, physical classroom is less vital towards creating a learning environment. We're already seeing huge advances in distance learning technology, with tools such as video calling and recording meaning anyone can attend a class, no matter where they are. In 50 years, we'll see students from all over the world attending the same classes, and interacting with each other.

Answer:

Introduction:

Information and Communication Technology (ICT) has played a significant role in revolutionizing the world of education. It has become a vital tool in modern education as it provides students and teachers with various opportunities and advantages to enhance their teaching and learning experience. In this essay, we will explore the importance of ICT in education.

Body:

The integration of ICT in education has brought about numerous advantages that have revolutionized the education sector. Firstly, ICT has made learning more interactive, engaging, and accessible to students. Through ICT, students can now access online learning materials, educational videos, and interactive applications that make learning fun and interesting. These tools have helped to improve students' motivation and interest in learning, leading to better academic performance.

Secondly, ICT has made education more accessible and inclusive. Students from remote areas can now access quality education through online platforms such as video conferencing, e-learning, and virtual classrooms. This has provided equal opportunities for all students regardless of their geographical location, economic background, or physical disabilities. ICT has also made it possible for teachers to offer personalized learning experiences to students with diverse learning needs, thereby enhancing their academic performance.

Thirdly, ICT has made it possible for teachers to save time and increase their efficiency. Through online resources and tools, teachers can now easily access and share teaching materials, grade assignments, and communicate with students and parents. This has helped to reduce their workload, giving them more time to focus on teaching and improving their students' learning experience.

Lastly, ICT has played a significant role in the development of critical thinking and problem-solving skills among students. The integration of ICT in education has made it possible for students to participate in collaborative learning and research projects, allowing them to develop teamwork and communication skills. This has not only helped to improve their academic performance but also prepared them for the challenges of the modern world.

Conclusion:

In conclusion, the importance of ICT in education cannot be overstated. ICT has revolutionized the education sector by making learning more accessible, engaging, and inclusive. It has also provided teachers with various tools and resources to enhance their teaching experience, save time, and increase their efficiency. Furthermore, it has helped to develop critical thinking, problem-solving, and collaborative skills among students. As technology continues to evolve, it is essential to keep integrating ICT in education to improve students' learning experience and prepare them for the challenges of the modern world.
