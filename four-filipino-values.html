1. The role of culture is very crucial in the development of Filipino values. Culture refers to the shared beliefs, values, customs, behaviors, and artifacts that characterize a group or society. Filipino values are shaped by the cultural heritage of the Philippines, which has been influenced by various factors such as indigenous cultures, colonialism, and globalization. Understanding the cultural context is essential in comprehending the Filipino values system, as it provides a framework for interpreting and analyzing the meaning of values in Filipino society.

2. One example of a Filipino value is "pakikisama," which means getting along with others and maintaining harmonious relationships. This value can be explained in terms of the "kapwa" concept, which emphasizes the interconnectedness and interdependence of individuals in Filipino society. Pakikisama is important in building social networks and establishing social status, as it reflects the ability of an individual to navigate and adapt to various social situations.

3. The positive and negative Filipino traits mentioned in the lesson are subject to interpretation and may vary depending on the context and perspective of the observer. Some positive traits include hospitality, respect for elders, and strong family ties, while negative traits include "bahala na" attitude, crab mentality, and lack of punctuality. While these values may have some drawbacks or limitations, they also serve important functions in Filipino society. For instance, the "bahala na" attitude can be seen as a form of resilience and adaptability in the face of uncertainty and adversity, while crab mentality can be seen as a way of ensuring social equality and preventing individuals from becoming too successful or dominant. In general, it is important to recognize the complexity and diversity of Filipino values and to avoid overgeneralizing or stereotyping them. Ultimately, whether or not one agrees with these values depends on one's own cultural background and personal beliefs.

The topic of the context of four values in Filipino drama films is the negative values they inherited from traditional dramas. It also pointed out the importance of cinemas back then and made Filipino a real national language.

Here are the four statements pointed out in the text:

White is Beautiful
Even today, we cannot deny the fact that we admire fair complexion. Other individuals are even using foreign beauty products to achieve their desired skin color. This is because before, in films, our mentality was cultured that having a fair or white complexion is beautiful. Leading roles before are easier to grab for those actors and actresses with white skin. For you to have a higher chance of getting the lead role, you must have a fair complexion, pointed nose, small lips, round eyes, and tall height.

Shows are the best
This is maybe the reason why Latin America and Filipino dramas have in common because we were influenced by Spaniards when it comes to addiction to this type of media. There are different types of entertainment before and each of them presents a different plot and purpose. These are komedya, sinakulo, zarzuela, dramas, etc.

Hurrah for the undergo
Means 'Mabuti ang inaapi' in the Filipino language. If we can observe, most of the leading roles in classic dramas are treated badly or abused by the antagonists. This is because we are trained to have a mindset that those who were abused and treated badly will be rewarded in the end.

All is right in the world
There are still good things in the world. Colonial dramas always end up with happy twists. It's either they defeated the villain or the main character gets a chance to marry a rich man. They even sometimes have those madrastas or stepmothers asking for forgiveness.
