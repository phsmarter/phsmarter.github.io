14. Activity 2: IdentificationDirection: Name the following electronic music devices and write simplewords/moods which best describes the instrument. Write your answer inside the box1.2​


Answer:

1. Electric Piano

2. Electric Guitar

3. Hammond Organ

Explanation:

Electronic music is music that employs electronic musical instruments, digital instruments and circuitry-based music technology. A distinction can be made between sound produced using electromechanical means (electroacoustic music) and that produced using electronics only. Electromechanical instruments have mechanical elements, such as strings, hammers and electric elements, such as magnetic pickups, power amplifiers and loudspeakers.

Examples of electromechanical sound producing devices include the telharmonium, Hammond organ, electric piano and the electric guitar, which are typically made loud enough for performers and audiences to hear with an instrument amplifier and speaker cabinet. Pure electronic instruments do not have vibrating strings, hammers or other sound-producing mechanisms. Devices such as the theremin, synthesizer and computer can produce electronic sounds.

How electrons are arranged around the nucleus in an atom is shown in an orbital diagram. In an orbital diagram, electrons are represented by arrows. Thus, the number of arrows tells us how many electrons are present which is also the atomic number of the element. The arrow up and arrow down indicate the opposite spin of electrons. The orbital diagram also tells us that the electrons occupy first the orbitals of the lowest energy until the highest energy. For each orbital, only two electrons can occupy. Also, it is important to note that orbitals of equal energy are each occupied by one electron before they are occupied by a second electron of the opposite direction. Thus, each orbital are filled up by an electron of the same spin first. For instance, in Silicon, there are two electrons of the same spin.

Also, the arrangement of electrons can be expressed in electron configuration. As mentioned above, the electrons are arranged based on the order of the energy levels: 1s, 2s, 2p, 3s, 3p, 4s, 3d, 4p, 5s, 4d, 5p, 6s, 4f, 5d, 6p, 7s, 5f, 6d, 7p.
