5. How do Social Deviation affects being a Deviant?


Answer:

The study of Social Deviance is the study of the violation of cultural norms in either formal (criminal) or informal (deviant) contexts.

Social deviance is a phenomenon that has existed in all societies where there have been norms. There are two possibilities for how an individual will act in the face of social norms; conform or violate. There are implicit social norms and explicit social norms. Explicit social norms are not necessarily laws (such as a sign at a computer lab that says food and drink are prohibited). In reality, there is often a blend of conformity and deviance in the ways people behave. Rarely if ever does a person deviate from or conform to all norms. Furthermore, some behaviors in themselves reflect both conformity and deviance at once. Consider breaking the speed limit, which is technically a legal violation, but which is also conformist, particularly on freeways where motorists "go with the flow." That is a critical feature of deviance, conformity, and norms. Relativity abounds. That is, norms can change over time (e.g. women in the paid labor force), depend on situational context (e.g. laughing at a party as opposed to doing so at a funeral), depend on statuses (e.g. an adolescent blowing up neighbors' mailboxes as opposed to an elderly woman doing so), and any number of other factors. But it's not all relative. There are forms of deviance (and certain norms) that are about as universal as anything in the social sciences can be, such as when one maliciously harms a child. In light of the way we think about norms, deviance, and conformity, many thinkers throughout history have tried to explain the causes behind deviance.

21. In the Social Studies class of Ms. Sebastian, the students received their scores in their last long quiz for the quarter. Agatha and her friends compared their scores. Can you solve for the mean deviation of their scores shown below? 23 26 29 32 35 38 41​


Answer:

1. Find the mean of the scores:

mean = (23 + 26 + 29 + 32 + 35 + 38 + 41) / 7

= 224 / 7

= 32

2. Subtract the mean from each score and get the absolute value:

|23 - 32| = 9

|26 - 32| = 6

|29 - 32| = 3

|32 - 32| = 0

|35 - 32| = 3

|38 - 32| = 6

|41 - 32| = 9

3. Find the sum of the absolute deviations:

9 + 6 + 3 + 0 + 3 + 6 + 9 = 36

4. Divide the sum of the absolute deviations by the number of scores:

mean deviation = sum of absolute deviations / number of scores

= 36 / 7

= 5.14 (rounded to two decimal places)

Therefore, the mean deviation of their scores is 5.14.

1) Probability that a random student spent between 4 to 7 hours in the social media:

To solve this, we need to standardize the values using the formula:

z = (X - μ) / σ

where X is the value (hours spent in social media), μ is the mean (6.5 hours), and σ is the standard deviation.

For X = 4: z = (4 - 6.5) / σ = -1.5 / σ

For X = 7: z = (7 - 6.5) / σ = 0.5 / σ

Using a standard normal distribution table or calculator, we can find the probabilities for these z-values:

P(Z < -1.5/σ) ≈ 0.0668

P(Z < 0.5/σ) ≈ 0.6915

The probability that a random student spent between 4 to 7 hours in the social media is the difference between these two probabilities:

P(4 ≤ X ≤ 7) = P(Z < 0.5/σ) - P(Z < -1.5/σ) ≈ 0.6247

2) Probability that a random student spent more than 8 hours in the social media:

We can standardize the value of X = 8 using the same formula:

z = (8 - 6.5) / σ = 1.5 / σ

Using the standard normal distribution table or calculator, we can find the probability:

P(Z > 1.5/σ) ≈ 0.0668

The probability that a random student spent more than 8 hours in the social media is:

P(X > 8) = P(Z > 1.5/σ) ≈ 0.0668

3) About how many students spent more than 8 hours in the social media out of 1,500 students:

We can estimate the number of students by multiplying the probability by the total number of students:

Number of students ≈ P(X > 8) × 1,500 ≈ 0.0668 × 1,500 ≈ 100.2

About 100 to 101 students (rounded to the nearest whole number) out of 1,500 students spent more than 8 hours in the social media.

30. 3. Conformity as a social influence has both positive and negative effects. Why is conformity sometimes not a good thing? a. Conformity is always a good thing and everyone should adhere to it. b. It may threaten individuation on our part. c. Deviating from the behavior of the majority is productive d. It is always best that people all behave differently


The correct answer is b. It may threaten individuation on our part.

          While conformity can lead to social harmony and cohesion, it can also have negative effects, particularly when it leads to the suppression of individuality and critical thinking. When individuals conform to social norms or the behavior of the majority, they may feel pressured to give up their own beliefs, values, or opinions. This can lead to a loss of personal identity and creativity, as well as a lack of diversity in thoughts and ideas. Furthermore, conformity can also lead to groupthink, which can result in poor decision-making and harmful behaviors.
