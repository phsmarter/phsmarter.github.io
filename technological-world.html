The debate over whether technology will save or destroy the world is one that has been raging for years. There are those who believe that technology is the answer to all of humanity’s problems and that it will eventually lead us to a utopia where we can all live in peace and harmony. Then there are those who believe that technology is a threat to our very existence and that it will eventually lead to our downfall.

So, which side is right? Unfortunately, there is no easy answer. Technology has the potential to do both good and bad. It all depends on how it is used.

There is no denying that technology has made our lives easier in many ways. It has given us the ability to communicate with each other instantaneously, regardless of distance. It has given us access to information that was once inaccessible. It has made our lives more convenient in a number of ways.

However, technology has also made us more vulnerable. We are now more reliant on technology than ever before, and if something were to happen to the power grid, we would be in a very difficult situation. Additionally, the way we use technology can have a negative impact on our health. For example, too much time spent looking at screens can lead to eye strain and headaches.

There is no doubt that technology has changed the world for the better in many ways. However, we must be careful not to become too reliant on it. We must also be aware of the ways in which it can be used to harm us. Only then can we make sure that technology is used for good and not for evil.

27. WAN in the world of technology means? *​


Answer:

A communications network that connects computing devices over geographically dispersed locations. While a local-area network (LAN) typically services a single building or location, a WAN covers a much larger area such as a city, state or country

Malawakang mga network ng lugar ay madalas na itinatag sa mga leased telecommunication circuit.      english: A wide area network (WAN) is a telecommunications network that extends over a large geographic area for the primary purpose of computer networking.
