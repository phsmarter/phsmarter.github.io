9. ideas plss for effect and causes for failed grades like depression, family, etc​


Answer:

There can be a variety of causes for failed grades, including factors such as depression, family issues, and others. Here are some potential causes and effects of these issues:

Depression:

Cause: Depression can lead to a lack of motivation, difficulty concentrating, and feelings of hopelessness, all of which can interfere with a student's ability to perform well in school.

Effect: Failed grades can exacerbate feelings of depression and make it even more challenging for students to recover from their academic struggles.

Family issues:

Cause: Family issues such as divorce, financial stress, or a family member's illness can create significant emotional and practical challenges for students. These difficulties can interfere with their ability to focus on their studies and perform well academically.

Effect: Poor grades can add to a student's stress and feelings of guilt or shame, which can further strain their relationships with family members and make it difficult to maintain their mental and emotional well-being.

Learning disabilities:

Cause: Learning disabilities such as dyslexia or ADHD can make it challenging for students to succeed academically, even if they are otherwise capable and motivated.

Effect: Failing grades can create a sense of frustration, helplessness, and low self-esteem in students with learning disabilities. Without proper support and accommodations, they may struggle to catch up or perform to their full potential.

Lack of resources:

Cause: Students from low-income households or underfunded schools may lack access to the resources and support needed to succeed academically. This can include tutoring, technology, textbooks, or even basic needs like food and housing.

Effect: Failing grades can perpetuate a cycle of poverty and disadvantage, making it even harder for students to access the resources they need to improve their academic performance and achieve success in the future.

Overall, it's essential to recognize that failed grades are often the result of complex and interconnected factors, and addressing these issues requires a holistic approach that considers both academic and non-academic factors affecting the student's life.

22. what is the cause and effect of not studying the lesson, going out with friends all the time, and laziness doing module activities can result to a failing grade.​


The cause of not studying the lesson, going out with friends all the time, and laziness doing module activities is a lack of effort and focus on academic responsibilities. This can lead to a lack of understanding of important concepts and information, as well as poor time management and prioritization skills.

The effect of this behavior can be a failing grade, as the student is not able to keep up with the coursework, complete assignments and projects on time, or perform well on exams and assessments. This can result in a negative impact on the student's academic standing and future opportunities, such as college admissions or job prospects.

Additionally, a failing grade can also cause emotional and psychological distress, such as feelings of inadequacy, disappointment, and stress, which can further impact the student's academic performance and mental well-being.
