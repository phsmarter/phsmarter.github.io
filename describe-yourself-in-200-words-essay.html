20. make a 200 words in essay and describe metric conversion​


Answer:

Metric system, as known as International System of Units, is an internationally recognized system of measurement. Metric units are multiples of ten of the base unit. The three most common units in the metric system are the meter, gram and liter. The prefixes of these units are: kilo-, hecto-, deka-, centi-, and milli-. In order to better help you remember the order of the units' sizes, use this memory aid:

King Henry Doesn't (Usually) Drink Chocolate Milk

The first letter of each of the above words correspond to the different prefixes, with the "U" in "Usually" standing for "unit", which reminds you that there's a unit of either meters, grams, or liters. Each of these prefixes are 10 times or 1/10th of the units on either side of them. So for example:

1 kilogram = 10 hectagram = 100 dekagram = (1000 grams) = 10,000 decigrams = 100,000 centigrams = 1,000,000 milligrams

As you can see, carrying out metric conversion between units is relatively simple as they're all multiples of tens of one another. This means that by moving the decimal point, you'll be able to convert between them. For a general rule, when you move from a bigger unit to a smaller unit (a unit that's to the right in the above memory aid), move the decimal place to the right an equal number of places. The same is said for moving left, when we move from a smaller number to a bigger one in unit conversion.
