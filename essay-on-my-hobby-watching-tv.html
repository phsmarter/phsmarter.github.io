16. Writ a three-paragraph essay about your favorite hobby.​


Answer:

Listening to music has always been my go to activity for all occasions. My love for music can be traced back to when I was a child. There are recorded videos of me as a child becoming hyperactive whenever music was played. I would run to the stereo, turn on the music to full volume and start running around dancing. Anyone who dared touch the sound system would face my wrath. Music was the only thing that could calm me down whenever I cried.

I always have this weird feeling that my destiny is somehow intertwined with music. In my early teenage years, my girlfriends and I would sneak into the garage, where my brother and his band mates kept their musical equipment. Then, we would pluck guitar strings and knock the drums in an effort to compose our favorite songs. I was the lead singer by the way.

Unfortunately for my brother and I, nothing much came out of the practice. It was not just me and my brother, music runs in the whole family. I remember vividly on Friday nights, we used to do karaoke sessions where everyone took turns to sing a song, including our parents. We had these sessions recorded and then sent to our neighbors for analysis.

Decelerating or Accelerating Hobbies

Decelerating and slowing down is an inevitable part of aging. In general, hobbies that require physical activity will decelerate or slow down as you age. Physical activities like playing sports or taking martial arts classes will slow down as you age. Mindless hobbies like watching television or browsing social media will also slow down as you age. One way to slow down is to get a job that requires less physical activity and more mental activity.

In general, hobbies that require physical activity will decelerate or slow down as you age. This is because your body gets slower and weaker with age. Taking part in physically demanding hobbies- such as playing basketball, running or martial arts - will slow down as you age. Your body can only handle so much physical stress before it begins to retaliate against you. Slowing down your hobbies will allow you to stay active and healthy as you age.

Hobbies that require mental activity will decelerate or slow down as you age. Your mind also slows down with age due to decreased mental energy. A job that requires mental energy - such as teaching - decelerates with age. Watching television or browsing social media all day will also decelerate your mind as you age. One way to combat slowing down with age is to engage in more mentally stimulating hobbies.

As you get older, your body will also slow down and your hobbies will need to accommodate this change. Your body no longer wants to undergo the same amount of stress it did when you were younger. Taking part in physically demanding hobbies - such as playing basketball, running or martial arts - now causes fatigue for your body instead of exhilaration. This is because your body no longer has the strength it once did. To compensate, you'll want to gradually reduce the amount of physicality in your hobbies as you get older.

Hobbies that require physical activities decelerate or slow down with age. Hobbies that require mental activities decelerate or slow down with age. And reducing the physicality in your hobbies allows them to remain interesting as you age. Allowing your hobbies to slowly accommodate your changing body and interests makes it easier for you to remain active as you age.
