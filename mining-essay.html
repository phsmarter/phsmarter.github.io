What is the nature and importance of the mining industry in your country?

The Philippines has been ranked as the fifth most mineralised country in the world, with an estimated US$1 trillion in untapped reserves of copper, gold, nickel, zinc and silver. Despite the foregoing, statistics from the Philippine Mines and Geosciences Bureau (MGB) indicate that, as of 2017, there are only 50 operating mines. For the same year, the Philippine Department of Environment and Natural Resources (DENR) has placed the preliminary gross production value for large-scale metallic mining at 107.7 billion pesos.

Target minerals

What are the target minerals?

The Philippines’ top mineral exports are copper, gold and nickel. Other target minerals include quartz, mica, iron, gypsum, feldspar, chromite, calcite and sulphur. Some target non-metallic minerals are sand and gravel, limestone, marble, clay and other quarry materials.

Cobalt is the main factor for the increased interest in minerals used in battery technology in the Philippines. The Philippines has the fourth-largest cobalt reserves worldwide, at an estimated 280,000 tonnes.

15. An essay is a form of writing in paragraph form that uses informal language, although it can be written formally. Essays may be written in first-person point of view (I, ours, mine), but third-person (people, he, she) is preferable in most academic essays.​


Answer:

An essay is a versatile piece of writing composed of organized paragraphs, utilizing informal or formal language depending on the context. As an essential academic and creative tool, essays are used to develop and support ideas, to communicate perspectives on specific subjects, and to sharpen critical and analytical thinking. While essays can be written in first-person point of view (e.g., I, ours, mine), utilizing third-person (e.g., people, he, she) is typically preferred in academic writing.

The structure of an essay usually consists of an introduction, body paragraphs, and a conclusion. The introduction sets the tone, presents the topic, and includes a thesis statement that outlines the main argument of the essay. Body paragraphs contain the evidence and examples supporting the thesis. Each paragraph generally focuses on a single idea, with clear transitions between them to ensure a coherent progression. Finally, the conclusion summarizes the main points and restates the thesis to emphasize the significance of the essay's argument.

Essays can be written in various styles, depending on the purpose and audience. Informal essays typically employ a conversational tone and may be more personal, making them appropriate for personal narratives, reflections, or critiques. Formal essay writing, on the other hand, observes a more structured approach, adhering to specific rules and conventions. This style is commonly used in academic settings, research papers, and professional documents, where objectivity and a systematic presentation of ideas are valued. The utilization of third-person point of view in formal academic essays emphasizes impartiality and supports the focus on the subject matter rather than personal opinion.

To sum up, essays are a crucial form of communication in both academic and everyday life, providing a platform for presenting ideas and engaging in critical dialogue. The flexibility in language usage and point of view makes essays adaptable to various contexts, ranging from personal reflections to scholarly analyses. Thus, the ability to write clear and effective essays is an important skill that expands our capacity for expression and intellectual growth.

30. essay about mining minerals unbiased ​


Mining minerals is a complex and often controversial issue that raises many questions about the balance between economic development, environmental protection, and social responsibility. In this essay, I will attempt to provide an unbiased analysis of mining minerals, its benefits and drawbacks, and the challenges that it poses to society.

On the one hand, mining minerals can provide significant economic benefits to countries, particularly those with abundant natural resources. The revenue generated from mining can be used to fund public services, infrastructure projects, and social welfare programs, thereby improving the living standards of local communities. Moreover, the demand for minerals such as copper, gold, and iron is expected to increase in the coming years, driven by population growth, urbanization, and technological advancements, making mining an essential component of the global economy.

On the other hand, mining minerals can also have significant negative impacts on the environment and local communities. The extraction of minerals can lead to deforestation, soil erosion, water pollution, and habitat destruction, thereby threatening the biodiversity of ecosystems. Moreover, mining operations can cause health problems for workers and local communities due to exposure to toxic chemicals and dust. Additionally, mining companies have been accused of violating human rights, including the rights of indigenous peoples, by exploiting their land and resources without their consent.

It is important to note that the benefits and drawbacks of mining minerals are context-specific and depend on various factors, such as the type of mineral being extracted, the location of the mine, and the regulatory framework in place. Therefore, it is crucial to conduct thorough and transparent assessments of mining projects, including environmental impact assessments, social impact assessments, and risk assessments, to ensure that the benefits of mining are maximized while its negative impacts are minimized.

In conclusion, mining minerals is a complex issue that requires a balanced and nuanced approach. While mining can provide significant economic benefits, it also poses significant environmental and social challenges. To address these challenges, it is essential to ensure that mining operations are conducted in a responsible and sustainable manner, taking into account the needs and interests of local communities and the environment. By doing so, we can ensure that the benefits of mining are shared equitably and that the negative impacts are minimized, thereby contributing to a more sustainable and just society.
