6. My father goes to court by carlos bulosan Describe the rich man family? How about their house, kids and other member of their family? How are they as neighbor? (pls answer it properly)​


Answer:

My father goes to court by carlos bulosan Describe the rich man family? How about their house, kids and other member of their family? How are they as neighbor? (

In Carlos Bulosan's short story "My Father Goes to Court," the rich man's family is portrayed as being very wealthy and privileged. Their house is described as "a palace" with "a garden as big as a town plaza" and "a fountain with a golden fish that spouted water."

The family members themselves are also depicted as being very well-off. The rich man has a wife and four children, all of whom are spoiled and arrogant. The eldest son is described as having "a fat body and a red face" and is portrayed as being lazy and rude. The second son is described as being "a wild young man" who enjoys drinking and partying. The two younger children, a boy and a girl, are portrayed as being similarly spoiled and entitled.

As neighbors, the rich man's family is depicted as being very arrogant and condescending towards the other people in their community. They look down on their neighbors, who are poor and struggling, and refuse to associate with them. They are described as being "selfish and proud" and never offer to help others in need.

Overall, the rich man's family is portrayed as being very out-of-touch with the struggles of everyday people and very disconnected from their community.
