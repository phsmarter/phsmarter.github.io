  • 21. Lesson 1 Geologic Processes and Hazards What's In Geology is the study of the Earth and its history. It involves studying the materials that make up the earth, the features and structures found on Earth, as well as the processes that act upon them. It also deals with the study of the history of all life living on the earth now. How do geological processes occur? Geological processes are naturally occurring events that directly or indirectly impact the geology of the Earth. Examples of geological processes include events such as plate tectonics, weathering, earthquakes, volcanic eruptions, mountain formation, deposition, erosion, droughts, flooding, and landslides. Geological processes affect every human on the Earth all of the time, but are most noticeable when they cause loss of life or property. These threatening processes are called natural disasters. How about Geologic Hazards? A geologic hazard is an extreme natural event in the crust of the earth that poses a threat to life and property, for example, earthquakes, volcanic eruptions, tsunamis (tidal waves) and landslides. It is a large-scale, complex natural events that happen on land. These hazards can cause immense damage, loss of property, and sometimes life. Geologic hazards can play a significant role when infrastructure is constructed in their presence. The unpredictable nature of natural geologic hazards makes identifying, evaluating, and mitigating against them a unique challenge. .​

Answer:

1. Sports are physical or mental activities that are competitive in nature and often governed by a set of rules. They can be played individually or as part of a team, and can range from simple games or exercises to complex competitions that require specific skills and training.

2. The three different natures of sports are:

Competitive: Sports that involve competition against one or more opponents, with the goal of winning or achieving a high score. Examples include soccer, basketball, and tennis.

Recreational: Sports that are played for enjoyment and relaxation, rather than for competition. Examples include hiking, biking, and swimming.

Spectator: Sports that are watched by others as entertainment, rather than being participated in. Examples include football, baseball, and ice hockey.

3. We need to study the background and nature of sports before playing them in order to understand the rules, regulations, and objectives of the sport. This helps us to play the sport safely, fairly, and effectively, as well as to appreciate the sport and its history. Knowing the background and nature of a sport can also help us to develop a deeper understanding and appreciation of it, which can enhance our enjoyment of the sport and our performance in it.

Geological processes are naturally occurring events that directly or indirectly impact the geology of the Earth. Examples of geological processes include events such as plate tectonics, weathering, earthquakes, volcanic eruptions, mountain formation, deposition, erosion, droughts, flooding, and landslides. Geological processes affect every human on the Earth all of the time, but are most noticeable when they cause loss of life or property. These threatening processes are called natural disasters. How about Geologic Hazards? A geologic hazard is an extreme natural event in the crust of the earth that poses a threat to life and property, for example, earthquakes, volcanic eruptions, tsunamis (tidal waves) and landslides. It is a large-scale, complex natural events that happen on land. These hazards can cause immense damage, loss of property, and sometimes life. Geologic hazards can play a significant role when infrastructure is constructed in their presence.
