18. what is Abc of conflict? (Attitude, behaviour and context) and give examples ​


Answer:

The ABC model of conflict refers to the three key components that contribute to conflicts: Attitudes, Behaviors, and Context. The model suggests that conflicts arise when there is a clash or mismatch between these components.

Attitude: This component refers to the beliefs, values, and opinions that people hold. Attitudes can be positive or negative and can influence how people perceive and react to situations. For example, if two colleagues have different attitudes towards work-life balance, this may lead to conflict if one person believes that working long hours is necessary for success, while the other believes in prioritizing personal time.

Behavior: This component refers to the actions that people take in response to a conflict. Behaviors can range from passive avoidance to aggressive confrontation. For example, if a customer complains about a product or service, the behavior of the company in addressing the complaint can either resolve the conflict or escalate it.

Context: This component refers to the environmental or situational factors that contribute to a conflict. Context can include factors such as cultural differences, power dynamics, and external pressures. For example, a conflict between two countries over natural resources may be influenced by historical, political, and economic factors.

In summary, the ABC model of conflict suggests that conflicts arise when there is a mismatch or clash between attitudes, behaviors, and context. By understanding these components and how they interact, we can develop strategies to manage and resolve conflicts more effectively.
