13. what is the essay, "the vision of mirzah" all about? what is the mood of essay?​


Answer:

ANG HABA PO PERO PABASA PO LAHAT AND PA HEART AND STAR PO TYSM :{

Explanation:

On the fifth day of the moon, which according to the custom of my forefathers I always keep holy, after having washed myself and offered up my morning devotions, I ascended the high hills of Baghdad, in order to pass the rest of the day in meditation and prayer. As I was here airing myself on the tops of the mountains, I fell into a profound contemplation on the vanity of human life, and passing from one thought to another, ‘Surely,’ said I, ‘man is but a shadow, and life a dream.

“I had often been told that the rock before me was the haunt of a genius; and that several had been entertained with music who had passed by it, but never heard that the musician had before made himself visible. When he had raised my thoughts by those transporting airs which he played, to taste the pleasures of his conversation, as I looked upon him like one astonished, he beckoned to me, and by the waving of his hand directed me to approach the place where he sat.  

“He then led me to the highest pinnacle of the rock, and placing me on the top of it, ‘Cast thy eyes eastward,’ said he ‘and tell me what thou seest.’ ‘I see,’ said I, ‘a huge valley and a prodigious tide of water rolling through it.’ ‘The valley that thou seest,’ said he, ‘is the Vale of Misery, and the tide of water that thou seest is part of the great tide of eternity.’ What is the reason,’ said I, ‘that the tide I see rises out of a thick mist at one end, and again loses itself in a thick mist at the other?’ ‘What thou seest,’ said he, ‘is that portion of eternity which is called time, measured out by the sun, and reaching from the beginning of the world to its consummation. Examine now,’ said he, ‘this sea that is thus bounded by darkness at both ends, and tell me what thou discoverest in it.’ ‘I see a bridge,’ said I, ‘standing in the midst of the tide.’ ‘The bridge thou seest,’ said he, ‘is human life; consider it attentively.’ Upon a more leisurely survey of it I found that it consisted of more than threescore and ten entire arches, with several broken arches, which, added to those that were entire, made up the number to about a hundred.  

“There were indeed some persons, but their number was very small, that continued a kind of hobbling march on the broken arches, but fell through one after another, being quite tired and spent with so long a walk.

confusion of objects, I observed some with scimitars in their hands, and others with urinals, who ran to and fro upon the bridge, thrusting several persons on trap-doors which did not seem to lie in their way, and which they might have escaped had they not been thus forced upon them.

“The genius, seeing me indulge myself on this melancholy prospect, told me I had dwelt long enough upon it, “Take thine eyes off the bridge,’ said he, ‘and tell me if thou seest anything thou dost not comprehend.’ Upon looking up, ‘What mean,’ said I, ‘those great flights of birds that are perpetually hovering about the bridge, and settling up it from time to time? I see vultures, harpies, ravens, cormorants, and among many other feathered creatures several little winged boys that perch in great numbers upon the middle arches,’ ‘These,’ said the genius, ‘are Envy, Avarice, Superstition, Despair, Love, with the like cares and passions that infest human life.’

“I here fetched a deep sigh. ‘Alas,’ said I, ‘man was made in vain: how is he given away to misery and mortality, tortured in life, and swallowed up in death! The genius being moved with compassion towards me, bid me quit so uncomfortable a prospect. ‘Look no more,’ said he, ‘on man in the first stage of his existence, in his setting out for eternity; but cast thine eye on that thick mist into which the tide bears the several generations of mortals that fall into it.’ I directed my sight as I was ordered, and (whether or no the good genius strengthened it with any supernatural force, or dissipated part of the mist that was before too thick for eye to penetrate) I saw the valley opening at the farther end, and spreading forth into an immense ocean that had a huge rock of adamant running through the midst of it, and dividing it into two equal parts. but, instead of the rolling tide, the arched bridge, and the happy islands, I saw nothing but the long valley of Baghdad, with oxen, sheep, and camels grazing upon the sides of it.”

The end of the first vision of Mirza.

24. Make a reflective essay about this quotatrion: " A different langauge is a different vision of life". ​


Answer:

The quote "A different language is a different vision of life" by Federico Fellini highlights the importance of language in shaping our perceptions and understanding of the world around us, I understand the significance of language in communication and how it influences our interactions with others.

Learning a new language allows us to gain a deeper understanding of different cultures and their perspectives. Language is not just a tool for communication, but it also reflects the values, beliefs, and customs of a specific community. Through language, we can gain insight into the way people think, their history, and their way of life.

For instance, the German language is known for its precision and structure, reflecting the culture's emphasis on order and efficiency. In contrast, languages such as Spanish or Italian are known for their expressive and passionate nature, which reflects the culture's emphasis on emotions and relationships. These linguistic differences reveal the diverse ways in which cultures understand and interpret the world around them.

Furthermore, language also plays a crucial role in shaping our perceptions and attitudes towards others. Speaking a different language can help us empathize and connect with those from different backgrounds, promoting cultural understanding and awareness. When we learn a new language, we open ourselves up to new ideas and experiences, broadening our horizons and expanding our vision of life.

In conclusion, the quote "A different language is a different vision of life" highlights the importance of language in shaping our understanding and perception of the world. Learning a new language is not just about communication, but it also provides us with a unique insight into different cultures and their perspectives. It promotes empathy, understanding, and awareness, which are essential for building a more inclusive and diverse society.
