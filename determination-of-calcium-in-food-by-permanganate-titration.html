2. A 50 mL sample of Calcium Hydroxide solution were required 19.50 mL of 0.1050 N HCl in a titration. Calculate the amount of Calcium Hydroxide in the 50 mL sample.


Given:

[tex]N_{\text{acid}} = \text{0.1050 N}[/tex]

[tex]V_{\text{acid}} = \text{19.50 mL}[/tex]

[tex]V_{\text{base}} = \text{50.00 mL}[/tex]

Unknown:

[tex]N_{\text{base}}[/tex]

Solution:

[tex]N_{\text{acid}}V_{\text{acid}} = N_{\text{base}}V_{\text{base}}[/tex]

[tex]N_{\text{base}} = N_{\text{acid}} × \frac{V_{\text{acid}}}{V_{\text{base}}}[/tex]

[tex]N_{\text{base}} = \text{0.1050 N} × \frac{\text{19.50 mL}}{\text{50.00 mL}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{N_{\text{base}} = \text{0.04095 N}}[/tex]

3. How to determine equivalence point in potentiometric titration?


Answer:

THE equivalence point in a potentiometric titration is generally determined by finding, in some way or other, the point of maximum slope of the titration curve. In many instances there are very sharp breaks in these curves and there is no difficulty in finding the equivalence point.

Further explanation  

Titration is a procedure for determining the concentration of a solution (analyte) by reacting with another solution whose known concentration (usually a standard solution) is called the titrant. Determination of the endpoint / equivalence point of the reaction can use indicators according to the appropriate pH range  

Titrations can be: acid-base titration, depositional titration, and redox titration. An acid-base titration is the principle of neutralization of acids and bases  

Acid-base titration formula  

[tex]\rm \large{\boxed{\bold{Va\times Na=Vb\times Nb}}}[/tex]

Na, Nb = acid base concentration  

Va, Vb = acid base volume  

GivenStep 1

HCl is titrated by Ba(OH)₂

V HCl = 25 ml

V Ba(OH)₂ = 0.1 N

So

V HCl . N HCl = V Ba(OH)₂ . N Ba(OH)₂

[tex]\rm 25\times N_{HCl}=30.08\times 0.1\\\\N_{HCl}=\dfrac{30.08\times 0.1}{25}\\\\N_{HCl}=\boxed{\bold{0.12032}}[/tex]

Step 2

NaOH is titrated by HCl

V HCl = 25.10 ml

N HCl = 0.12032

V NaOH = 10 ml

So

V NaOH . N NaOH = V HCl . N HCl

[tex]\rm 10\times N_{NaOH}=25.10\times 0.12032\\\\N_{NaOH}=\dfrac{25.1\times 0.12032}{10}\\\\N_{NaOH}=\boxed{\bold{0.302}}[/tex]

24. Colorimetric titration experiment - determine the amount of vitamin c in a medium peach


To determine the amount of vitamin C in a medium peach using a colorimetric titration experiment, you will need the following materials:

A medium peach

A knife or blender to prepare the peach sample

A 250 mL volumetric flask

A burette or micropipette

A beaker or Erlenmeyer flask

A pH meter or pH indicator solution

A standard vitamin C solution with a known concentration

A solvent, such as distilled water or acetic acid

Here is the general procedure for performing the colorimetric titration experiment:

Prepare the peach sample by blending or cutting the peach into small pieces and transferring it to the 250 mL volumetric flask. Add the solvent to the flask and fill it to the mark with solvent. This will create a solution of the peach sample in solvent.

Use the burette or micropipette to add a known volume of the standard vitamin C solution to the beaker or Erlenmeyer flask.

Add a few drops of pH indicator solution to the beaker or Erlenmeyer flask, or use a pH meter to measure the pH of the solution. The pH should be around 3.0.

Slowly add the peach sample solution from the volumetric flask to the beaker or Erlenmeyer flask, while continuously stirring the solution.

Observe the color change of the solution as the peach sample is added. The color will change from red to yellow as the vitamin C in the peach sample reacts with the indicator solution.

When the color change reaches a maximum, the titration is complete. The volume of peach sample solution added can be used to calculate the concentration of vitamin C in the peach sample using the formula:

[Vitamin C] = (Volume of standard solution added x Concentration of standard solution) / Volume of peach sample solution added

Remember to calibrate the burette or micropipette before performing the titration to ensure accurate measurement of the volume of standard vitamin C solution added. It is also important to use a clean, dry burette or micropipette and to handle the solutions carefully to avoid contamination.
