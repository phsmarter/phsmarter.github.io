What is a Unique Selling Proposition (USP)?

The Unique Selling Proposition (USP) is the unique benefit that a particular product offers to its customers. It is often considered as the cornerstone of a marketing strategy and is used as a differentiator from other products within the same category.

In order to make your USP stand out, you need to ask yourself two questions:

1) What can I offer that my competitors cannot?

2) What do my customers already want that they don't know they want yet?

A USP has five basic components:

   • Benefits

   • Emotive

   • Key selling points

   • Key difference

   • Competitive advantage

Top Examples of Unique Selling Propositions1. Canva

Without using too many words to explain, the tagline "Empowering the world to design" communicates what makes the service special and superior. What else does it represent, then?

The product's ease of use, including its drag-and-drop functionality and ready-made templates.Low costs more individuals can affordAn advantage over rivals in the market2. Nike

expands the buyer persona to include everyone, not just athletesindicates cost-effective, athlete-level quality.demonstrates the company's commitment to supporting athletes and all forms of sport

3. Coca-Cola

Coca-Cola, a globally renowned beverage company, uses "refreshment" as its unique selling point. It highlights the distinction between the product and firm by; Taking the straightforward term "refreshments and beverages" and connecting it to a more valuable connotation;

stating that the company's mission is to "Refresh the world" by promoting environmental sustainability. Putting an emphasis on the "Make a difference" component to encourage consumers to use the products to contribute to sustainability.
