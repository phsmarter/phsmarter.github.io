9. 5 students from a psychology class reported the number of hours of television they watch per week. Here are their data: 12, 15, 14, 13, 11. What is the standard deviation of the number of hours of television watched per week for this sample of 5 students?


Given:

5 students from a psychology class reported the number of hours of television they watch per week. Here are their data: 12, 15, 14, 13, 11. What is the standard deviation of the number of hours of television watched per week for this sample of 5 students?

Solution:

Types of standard variation:

1. population standard deviation

2. sample standard deviation

n1 = 12

n2 = 15

n3 = 14

n4 = 13

n5 = 11

A. To solve for population standard deviation

1. Take the mean (average) of the given data.

(12 + 15 + 14 + 13 + 11)/5 = 13

2. For each number, subtract the mean and square the result.

for n1: (12 - 13)² = 1

for n2: (15 - 13)² = 4

for n3 : (14 - 13)² = 1

for n4: (13 - 13)² = 0

for n5: (11 - 13)² = 4

2. Take the sum and divide by n

(1 + 4 + 1 + 0 + 4)/5

= 10/5

= 2

3.) Take the square root.

√2 = 1.4142

B. To solve for sample standard deviation

1. Take the mean (average) of the given data.

(12 + 15 + 14 + 13 + 11)/5 = 13

2. For each number, subtract the mean and square the result.

for n1: (12 - 13)² = 1

for n2: (15 - 13)² = 4

for n3 : (14 - 13)² = 1

for n4: (13 - 13)² = 0

for n5: (11 - 13)² = 4

2. Take the sum and divide by n - 1

n - 1 = 5 - 1 = 4

(1 + 4 + 1 + 0 + 4)/4

= 10/4

= 2.5

3.) Take the square root.

√2.5 = 1.5811

21. explain this phrase "everything psychological is also biological" essay


The phrase "everything psychological is also biological" suggests that all psychological phenomena, or mental processes and experiences, have a biological basis. This means that psychological processes and experiences are ultimately the result of activity in the brain and nervous system, and can be understood in terms of the underlying biological mechanisms.

The relationship between psychological and biological processes is complex and multifaceted. For example, psychological factors such as thoughts, emotions, and behaviors can influence the functioning of the body and the brain, and biological processes such as hormone secretion and neutral activity can influence psychological experiences and behaviors.

This perspective on psychology is known as biological psychology or psychobiology, and it emphasizes the role of biology in understanding mental processes and behaviors. Biological psychology is a broad field that includes research on topics such as the neutral basis of learning and memory, the effects of hormones and neurotransmitters on behavior, and the genetic basis of psychological traits.

Overall, the phrase highlights the close connection between psychological and biological processes, and suggests that understanding the underlying biology can help us to better understand psychological phenomena[tex].[/tex]

26. essay:discuss/explain the relation of ethics to logic, ethics to psychology, ethics to sociology, ethics to economics.​


Answer:

Ethics is a branch of philosophy that is concerned with moral principles and values that govern human behavior. Ethics can be related to logic in the sense that logical reasoning can help in determining the ethical correctness of an action. Ethics can also be related to psychology, as psychological factors such as moral reasoning, emotions, and values can influence ethical decision-making. In sociology, ethics can be studied in terms of social norms and cultural values that shape moral behavior. Ethics can also be related to economics as economic decisions can have ethical implications, and economic systems can be evaluated in terms of their ethical outcomes. Overall, ethics is an interdisciplinary field that is related to various other disciplines in its study of moral principles and values that guide human behavior.

Answer:

Ethics is the study of moral values and principles that govern our behavior and decision-making. It is an interdisciplinary field that has connections to various other academic disciplines, including logic, psychology, sociology, and economics.

In terms of logic, ethics is closely related because ethical reasoning involves using logical principles to evaluate moral issues and make ethical judgments. A person who is skilled in logical reasoning can apply their knowledge to identify inconsistencies or fallacies in moral arguments, which can help them arrive at a more sound ethical decision.

Ethics is also linked to psychology because it involves the study of human behavior and how people make moral judgments. Psychologists can use their knowledge of human cognition and behavior to understand how people form ethical beliefs and make moral decisions. They can also help individuals develop their moral reasoning and ethical decision-making skills.

In sociology, ethics plays an important role in understanding how social norms and values influence our behavior and attitudes towards ethical issues. Sociologists study how different societies and cultures develop their ethical frameworks and how these frameworks shape the behavior of individuals and groups.

Finally, economics is also connected to ethics because it involves the study of how people allocate resources and make decisions that affect the welfare of others. Economic decisions often have ethical implications, such as determining the distribution of resources and the impact of economic policies on different groups of people. Ethical considerations are therefore important in economic decision-making to ensure that decisions are fair and just.

In conclusion, ethics has strong connections to various academic disciplines, including logic, psychology, sociology, and economics. These connections demonstrate the interdisciplinary nature of ethics and how it can inform and be informed by other fields of study.
