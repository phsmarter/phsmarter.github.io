1. Physical growth usually refers to changes in size or mass; so it is correct to say that a child grows in stature (height) or body weight. These pubertal changes are driven by hormones, particularly an increase in testosterone for boys and estrogen for girls. The physical changes that occur during adolescence are greater than those of any other time of life, with the exception of infancy.
