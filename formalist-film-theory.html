Answer:

The formalist approach is a literary theory that emphasizes the formal aspects of a literary text, such as its structure, language, and style, rather than its social or historical context. It focuses on the literary work itself and how it achieves its meaning through the use of literary devices.

To write a formalist approach, you would need to analyze the literary work's form, structure, language, style, and literary devices used. You would also need to consider how these elements contribute to the overall meaning and effect of the text.

The founder of the formalist approach is widely considered to be the Russian literary critic Viktor Shklovsky, who developed his ideas in the early 20th century. However, other prominent formalists include Roman Jakobson, Boris Eichenbaum, and Yuri Tynianov.

Formalist analysis of "The Story of an Hour" focuses on the elements of the story itself, such as plot, character, and setting, without taking into consideration the author's intentions or historical context. Formalist critics may analyze the way the story is structured, the use of symbols and imagery, and the character development of Louise Mallard. Feminist analysis of "The Story of an Hour" focuses on the way the story deals with gender issues, such as the role of women in society, the effects of marriage on women's lives, and the restrictions placed on women's freedom. Feminist critics may examine the way Chopin portrays Louise Mallard as a woman who is trapped by the expectations of marriage and the social norms of her time. They may also analyze the way the story deals with female sexuality, such as the way Louise feels liberated by the thought of living without her husband and the implications of this desire for female independence.

When comparing the formalist and feminist analyses of "The Story of an Hour," it is clear that both approaches offer valuable insights into the story. While the formalist analysis focuses on the structure and elements of the story, the feminist analysis provides a broader context by examining the gender issues that the story raises. 21. formalist approach on "invictus" ​


Answer:

The formalist approach to literary analysis focuses on the form, structure, language, and other elements of a text as they exist within the text itself, rather than looking at the author's life or social or historical context. In this approach, the meaning of the text arises from the way these elements work together.

In the poem "Invictus" by William Ernest Henley, the form and language of the poem are notable for their directness and simplicity. The poem consists of four stanzas, each with four lines. The rhyme scheme is ABAB, and the meter is mostly iambic tetrameter, with occasional variations. The poem also makes use of repetition, with the phrase "I am" repeated three times in the first two stanzas.

The poem's language is straightforward and declarative, with short, simple sentences. The speaker asserts his own strength and independence, despite the challenges he has faced, and emphasizes his own willpower and determination. The language is also highly visual, with vivid images such as "the fell clutch of circumstance" and "the bludgeonings of chance."

One key element of the poem is its use of the first person point of view. The speaker asserts his own agency and autonomy, claiming that he is the master of his fate and the captain of his soul. This emphasis on the individual's power and control is in keeping with the formalist emphasis on the text itself, rather than on external factors such as history or biography.

Overall, the formalist approach to "Invictus" focuses on the poem's form, structure, and language as they contribute to its meaning. The poem's simple, direct style and emphasis on the individual's strength and willpower contribute to its enduring appeal and resonance.

26. what Is the meaning of formalist​


Answer:

Formalist refers to a person or approach that emphasizes the form, structure, or technical elements of a work of art or literature, rather than its content, meaning, or social or historical context. A formalist approach to art or literature focuses on the elements of the work itself, such as its composition, style, and language, and how they contribute to its aesthetic or artistic value.

Formalism is a critical theory that originated in the early 20th century and was particularly influential in literary criticism and the visual arts. Formalists believe that a work of art or literature should be judged primarily on its formal qualities, and that these qualities can be objectively analyzed and evaluated. They often emphasize the importance of technique, craftsmanship, and artistic skill in creating a work of art or literature, and view the work itself as a self-contained and self-referential entity.
