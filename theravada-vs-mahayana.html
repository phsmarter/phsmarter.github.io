1. What are the three streams of the Buddhist tradition? Select one: a. Mahayana, Vajrayana, Tribetan b. Theravada, Vajrayana, Trantayana c. Theravada, Mahayana, Vedic d. Theravada, Mahayana and Vajrayana tradition


The three streams of Buddhist tradition would be (d) Theravada, Mahayana and Vajrayana.  These are also called the 3 main types of Buddhism.  

In essence, those who go to Buddha for teachings are streamlined to a group, or let's say, assigned to a group.  


Theravada would be the school of elders and focuses on the idea of karma and practices medication to distance oneself from unnecessary thoughts and feelings. It is said to be the oldest school of Buddhism as well. This is prominent in places like South-East Asia, including Sri Lanka, Thailand, Cambodia, and Burma. The goal of Theravada is complete enlightenment that the Theravada tradition sees Shakyamuni Buddha as central since it is his path that monks pursue.  Mahayana or the Great Way are for people who are primarily motivated by being useful to others. This is basically a philosophical movement that advocates the possibility of salvation. They meditate mostly to increase compassion and wisdom, which they will need to aid those in need. This teaching is dominant in Northern Asia like in Japan, Vietnam, China, and Korea. The aim of this stream is to be fully enlightened for the benefit of everyone—to be like Buddha.  Also known as the Diamond Way, a stream of Buddhism, Vajrayana tradition is most prominent in Tibet. This practice is focused on the mind itself. This is about what one shows others based on what is inside: if one is deemed perfect, it is because he believes he is perfect inside. In this stream, the Buddha is the mirror of our minds. It highlights the mind's perfection. These teachings survived in Tibet even after Buddhism was destroyed in its native land.

10. Difference of mahayana buddhism and theravada buddhism


Answer:

Mahayana Buddhism and Theravada Buddhism are two major branches of Buddhism, which differ in their beliefs, practices, and traditions. Here are some of the key differences between the two:

Origins: Theravada Buddhism is considered the oldest and most traditional form of Buddhism and is primarily practiced in Sri Lanka, Thailand, Cambodia, Laos, and Myanmar. Mahayana Buddhism, on the other hand, emerged later and spread to countries such as China, Japan, Vietnam, and Korea.

The role of the Buddha: Theravada Buddhism regards the Buddha as an exceptional human being who attained enlightenment through his own efforts, and they venerate him as the ultimate teacher. In Mahayana Buddhism, the Buddha is seen as a divine figure who manifests himself in different forms to help sentient beings achieve enlightenment.

The concept of enlightenment: In Theravada Buddhism, enlightenment or Nirvana is seen as an individual experience that can be achieved only through personal efforts and spiritual practice. In Mahayana Buddhism, enlightenment is seen as a collective and inclusive experience, which can be achieved not only by individuals but also by all sentient beings.

Scriptures: Both branches have their own set of scriptures. Theravada Buddhism relies heavily on the Pali Canon, while Mahayana Buddhism has a more extensive set of texts, including the Prajnaparamita Sutras and the Lotus Sutra.

Bodhisattva ideal: The concept of the bodhisattva, a being who is dedicated to attaining enlightenment for the benefit of all sentient beings, is central to Mahayana Buddhism but less emphasized in Theravada Buddhism.

Emphasis on meditation: While both branches emphasize meditation, Theravada Buddhism places more emphasis on Vipassana or Insight meditation, while Mahayana Buddhism includes various forms of meditation practices such as Zen, Pure Land, and Tibetan meditation.

Overall, while both Theravada and Mahayana Buddhism share the same core teachings of the Buddha, they differ in their interpretation and application of these teachings.

19. similarities and differences of mahayana and Theravada Buddhism ​


Answer:

Both Mahayana and Theravada Buddhism are based on the same core teachings of the Buddha, but they have different interpretations and practices. Both offer paths to enlightenment, but they offer different approaches and practices to help individuals reach their ultimate goal.

Explanation:

Mahayana and Theravada Buddhism are two major branches of Buddhism, originating from the same teachings of the Buddha but with distinct differences.


Similarities:

Both sects believe in the Four Noble Truths and the Eightfold Path, which is the core of Buddhist philosophy.


Both believe in the concept of reincarnation and that the ultimate goal is to reach enlightenment and escape the cycle of rebirth.


Both sects believe in the importance of meditation as a means of achieving enlightenment.


Differences:

The main difference is the idea of the role of the Buddha and the path to enlightenment. In Theravada Buddhism, the Buddha is seen as a teacher and an example, while in Mahayana Buddhism, the Buddha is seen as a savior figure who can help others reach enlightenment.


The Theravada tradition emphasizes individual enlightenment and personal liberation, while the Mahayana tradition emphasizes the ideal of the Bodhisattva - one who seeks enlightenment for the benefit of all beings.


Theravada places emphasis on strict monastic discipline, while Mahayana is more inclusive of laypeople and places greater emphasis on devotion and compassion.

28. Ano ang pagkakaiba ng theravada at mahayana Buddhism


Mga Pagkakaiba ng Theravada at Mahayana Buddhism

Mababatid ang mga pagkakaiba ng Theravada at Mahayana Buddhism sa paniniwala ng dalawang grupo. Isang mahalagang pagkakaiba ng Theravada at Mahayana Buddhism ay ang paniniwala sa kung paano nila makakamit ang kaliwanagan (enlightenment). Naniniwala ang mga kasapi ng Theravada Buddhism na kapag nakamit na nila ang kaliwanagan sa kasalukuyang buhay ay maaabot na nila ang Nirvana. Kapag naabot na nila ang Nirvana, sila ay tuluyan nang makakawala sa samsara o ikot ng muling pagsilang o cycle of rebirth.

Sa kabilang banda, ang mga kasapi ng Mahayana Buddhism ay naniniwala na makakamit nila ang kaliwanagan sa pamamagitan ng pananatili sa samsara. Sa pananatili sa samsara, makakatulong sila mas maraming tao na siya. Ang pagtulong na ito o ang katangiang mahabagin ay siya namang magdadala sa kanila sa kaliwanagan.

Dagdag na Mga Pagkakaiba ng Theravada at Mahayana Buddhism

Ilan pa sa mga pagkakaiba ng Theravada at Mahayana Buddhism ay ang mga sumusunod:

Theravada BuddhismIto ang mas nakatatanda at mas makalumang uri ng Budismo (The School of the Elders).Sinisikap ng mga Therava Buddhists na maging ganap na Arhat. Ang arhat ay sinasabing mga ganap na gising na mga nilalang o fully awakened beings.Sa pagiging arhat, sumusunod sila sa Noble Eightfold Path na siyang pumupuksa sa kasakiman, poot, at kamangmangan.

Mahayana BuddhismTinatawag naman ang paniniwalang ito bilang The Greater Vehicle dahil sa pagsusumikap ng mga Mahayana Buddhist na makamit ang kaliwanagan hindi lamang para sa sarili kundi para sa iba.Sinasikap naman ng mga Mahayana Buddhists na maging isang Bodhisattva.Nakakamit ang pagiging Bodhisattva sa pamamagitan ng Six Perfection: pagiging bukas-palad, pagkakaroon ng moralidad, ng mahabang pasensya, ng lakas, pagninilay-nilay o meditation, at karunungan.
