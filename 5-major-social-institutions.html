26. What is the difference of social institution to political institution?


Answer:

Social institutions and political institutions are both important components of society, but they differ in their purpose and function.

Social institutions refer to the established patterns of behavior or relationships that are considered essential to the functioning of society. These institutions are typically concerned with the socialization of individuals, the regulation of behavior, and the maintenance of order within society. Some examples of social institutions include the family, religion, education, and the economy.

Political institutions, on the other hand, are concerned with the exercise of power and the distribution of resources within society. These institutions are responsible for making and enforcing laws, regulating political processes, and providing governance for society. Examples of political institutions include the government, political parties, and the legal system.

One key difference between social and political institutions is that social institutions are primarily concerned with maintaining social order, while political institutions are concerned with the exercise of power and the distribution of resources. Social institutions tend to be more stable and long-lasting than political institutions, which can change rapidly in response to shifts in political power or public opinion.

Another difference is that social institutions tend to be more informal and diffuse than political institutions, which typically have a more formal and structured organization. Social institutions are often based on shared values and cultural norms, while political institutions are governed by specific laws and regulations.

Overall, while social and political institutions both play important roles in society, they differ in their purpose, function, and organization. Social institutions are concerned with maintaining social order and regulating behavior, while political institutions are concerned with the exercise of power and the distribution of resources within society.
