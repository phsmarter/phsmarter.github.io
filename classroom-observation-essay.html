11. - ESSAY (STUDENTS' CHALLENGES IN THE NEW NORMAL CLASSROOM) ​


Explanation:

The new normal classroom has brought about a lot of changes in the way students learn. With the introduction of online learning, students are now faced with a variety of challenges that they must overcome in order to succeed. These challenges include adapting to the new technology, managing their time effectively, and staying motivated.

Adapting to the new technology is one of the biggest challenges that students face in the new normal classroom. Many students are not used to using computers or other digital devices for learning and may find it difficult to adjust. Additionally, they may not be familiar with the different software programs and applications that are used for online learning. This can lead to frustration and confusion as they try to figure out how to use these tools effectively.

Time management is another challenge that students face in the new normal classroom. With online learning, there is no set schedule or structure for classes, which can make it difficult for students to stay on track with their studies. They must be able to manage their time wisely in order to complete assignments on time and keep up with their studies. Additionally, they must be able to balance their school work with other activities such as extracurriculars or part-time jobs.

Finally, staying motivated is a challenge that many students face when transitioning from traditional classrooms to online learning environments. Without the physical presence of teachers and classmates, it can be difficult for some students to stay focused and engaged in their studies. Additionally, without regular feedback from teachers or peers, it can be hard for some students to stay motivated and continue working towards their goals.

Overall, there are many challenges that students face when transitioning from traditional classrooms to online learning environments in the new normal classroom. However, by understanding these challenges and taking steps such as adapting quickly to new technology, managing their time wisely, and staying motivated, students can overcome these obstacles and succeed in this new environment.

The electron was discovered in 1897 by the English physicist J.J. Thomson during investigations of cathode rays. His discovery of electrons, which he initially called corpuscles, played a pivotal role in revolutionizing knowledge of atomic structure. Under ordinary conditions electrons are bound to the positively charged nuclei of atoms by the attraction between opposite electric charges. In a neutral atom the number of electrons is identical to the number of positive charges on the nucleus. Any atom, however, may have more or fewer electrons than positive charges and thus be negatively or positively charged as a whole; these charged atoms are known as ions. Not all electrons are associated with atoms; some occur in a free state with ions in the form of matter known as plasma.

30. Describe the classroom observation setting with the following categories: The children being ﻿ observed are unaware, the setting is natural and with participant observer​


Answer:

The classroom observation setting in this scenario is one where the children being observed are not aware of the observation taking place. This means that the observer is able to gain a natural and unobtrusive view of the classroom environment, as the children are behaving as they would normally without feeling the need to modify their behavior due to being watched. Additionally, the setting is natural, meaning that the classroom environment is not altered in any way for the purpose of observation. Finally, the observer in this scenario is acting as a participant observer, meaning they are actively involved in the classroom environment while also observing, further contributing to the natural setting.
