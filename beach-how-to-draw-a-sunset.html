24. How would a scientist describe sunset?


Sunset is the daily vanishing of the Sun below the horizon due to Earth's rotation. The occurrence of sunset differ throughout the year and  determined by the the viewer's position on Earth, affected by longitude and latitude, and elevation. From the Equator, the sun sets exactly due west in both Spring and Autumn. As viewed from the middle latitudes, the sun sets to the northwest for the Northern Hemisphere, but to the southwest for the Southern Hemisphere. The colors of the sunset are the end result of a phenomenon called scattering.
