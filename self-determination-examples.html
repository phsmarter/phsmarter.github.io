Let's learn about Expanded Definitions!

Expanded Definitions refer to the definition or meaning that a writer may use to explain an intricate word, situation, place, or idea in depth.

Writers may use combinations of the eleven (11) different techniques/approaches to further elaborate the definition of their chosen concept.

Below are the techniques used in the expanded definition of "freedom" from the given text.

1. HISTORICAL REFERENCE:

used by discussing events in history that apply the concept.

"to our colonial forefathers, freedom" -- It relates what freedom meant to our ancestors in the past.

2. ETYMOLOGY

used by studying the origin and meaning of the word.

"Old English freedom: power of self-determination) ..." -- It explains that the word freedom was derived from Old English. Although the following statements can be regarded as a historical reference, we look at text 2 and consider it an etymology as a whole.

3. EXAMPLES

used by providing examples relative to the concept.

4. DESCRIPTION

used by defining the word/concept itself.

"Freedom means the ability to think, feel, say, or act however one chooses." -- It uses the literal definition of freedom.

5. NEGATION

used by expressing what the concept is not (opposite).key words: not, no, none, never, nothing, et cetera
