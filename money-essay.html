8. money want give happiness argumentative essay ​


Answer:

Money is often seen as a path to happiness, but is this really the case? While it is true that money can provide individuals with access to certain luxuries and opportunities, it is not a guarantee for happiness. In fact, many individuals who possess great wealth still struggle with mental health issues and feelings of unhappiness. Therefore, the idea that money can buy happiness is a flawed one.

Firstly, money can provide individuals with access to material possessions, which can provide temporary satisfaction. However, studies have shown that material possessions do not lead to long-term happiness. The initial excitement and pleasure of purchasing something new wears off quickly, leaving individuals feeling unfulfilled and searching for the next thing to buy. Additionally, the constant pursuit of material possessions can lead to a never-ending cycle of wanting more, which can actually lead to feelings of stress and unhappiness.

Secondly, money can provide individuals with experiences that can bring happiness, such as traveling or attending events. However, it is important to note that experiences are only enjoyable if they are shared with loved ones. No matter how much money one has, if they are unable to share experiences with those they care about, they may still feel lonely and unfulfilled.

Furthermore, studies have shown that individuals cwho prioritize money over relationships and experiences are less happy than those who prioritize social connections and personal growth. This is because social connections and personal growth provide individuals with a sense of purpose and fulfillment that cannot be achieved through material possessions or money.

In conclusion, the idea that money can buy happiness is a flawed one. While money can provide individuals with temporary satisfaction and access to certain experiences, it is not a guarantee for long-term happiness. Instead, individuals should prioritize social connections and personal growth, which can provide them with a sense of purpose and fulfillment that cannot be achieved through money alone.

17. essay tungkol sa money makes the world go round​


Answer:

"Money makes the world go round" is a common saying that emphasizes the importance of money in our lives. It implies that money is a significant driving force behind many of our actions and decisions, and that it plays a crucial role in the functioning of the world.

Indeed, money has become an essential aspect of our daily lives. It allows us to purchase the things we need and want, pay for our basic necessities, and invest in our future. Money gives us the ability to live comfortably and to enjoy the finer things in life, such as travel, entertainment, and leisure activities.

However, the saying "Money makes the world go round" also highlights the negative effects of our obsession with money. In today's world, we often prioritize wealth and material possessions over more important things such as our relationships, our health, and our overall wellbeing. We may become so consumed with the pursuit of money that we neglect the people and things that truly matter in life.

Moreover, the uneven distribution of wealth across the world has created significant economic and social disparities. Many people are forced to live in poverty, struggle to make ends meet, and face numerous challenges such as lack of access to healthcare and education. On the other hand, a small percentage of individuals hold vast amounts of wealth and resources, leading to an unequal distribution of power and influence.

Therefore, while money can be a powerful tool, it is important to recognize its limitations and the negative impact it can have on our lives and society as a whole. It is important to strike a balance between our pursuit of financial success and our other priorities in life. We should prioritize the things that bring us joy and fulfillment, such as our relationships, our passions, and our values, while also striving to achieve financial stability and success.

In conclusion, money may be an essential aspect of our lives, but it should not be the only driving force behind our decisions and actions. We should strive to find a balance between our pursuit of financial success and our other priorities in life, recognizing the value of money while also acknowledging its limitations and potential negative effects.

28. Write an Essay about “There is money in poultry raising”.


Poultry raising, or the business of raising and selling chickens, turkeys, ducks, and other birds for meat or eggs, is a lucrative venture that can provide significant financial returns for those who are willing to invest the time, effort, and resources into it.

One of the main reasons why poultry raising can be profitable is the high demand for poultry products. Chicken, in particular, is a staple in many diets around the world, and there is always a steady demand for both fresh and processed poultry products. Additionally, the demand for eggs, which can be produced by a variety of poultry species, is also consistently high.

Another factor that contributes to the profitability of poultry raising is the relatively low cost of production. Many of the expenses associated with poultry raising, such as feed, housing, and equipment, can be kept low through careful planning and management. Additionally, the fast growth rate and high reproductive potential of poultry means that a small investment in chicks or eggs can quickly turn into a large number of birds that can be sold for a profit.

However, it is important to note that poultry raising is not without its challenges. There are several potential risks and drawbacks that need to be carefully considered before starting a poultry operation. These include the risk of disease outbreaks, which can cause significant losses, as well as the potential for fluctuations in feed and poultry prices. Additionally, the care and management of poultry requires a significant amount of time and attention, and there may be significant start-up costs involved in setting up a poultry operation.

Despite these challenges, however, poultry raising can be a rewarding and profitable venture for those who are willing to put in the work. With proper planning, management, and a commitment to providing high-quality care for their birds, poultry farmers can achieve significant financial returns from their operations. In conclusion, there is certainly money to be made in poultry raising for those who are willing to invest the time and effort required to succeed in this industry.
