ST. BENEDICT

St. Benedict was a religious reformer who lived in Italy in the late 400s and early 500s. He is known as the “father of Western monasticism,” having established a Rule that would become the norm for innumerable Christian monks and nuns. He is the patron saint of Europe.

Saint Benedict's Rule organizes the monastic day into regular periods of communal and private prayer, sleep, spiritual reading, and manual labor – ut in omnibus glorificetur Deus, "that in all things God may be glorified".

With the inspiration of the Holy Spirit, Benedict pushed the elderly monk down and the fallen monk was immediately exorcised of the demon who had taken over, obsessing him to do evil to his brother monks. Because of this and other miracles, St. Benedict is known as Patron Saint of Exorcists.

Benedictine monks spend about four hours a day in the "divine office" of prayer and another four hours a day in reading the Scriptures. According to Benedict, manual work is a form of holy prayer. Each monk is given work assignments because labor is a valued and integral part of the human experience.

26. Ayon kay Benedict Anderson, ang nasyon ay isang imagined political community. Ipaliwanag ang pahayag na ito. patulong nmn


Bansa Bilang Isang Naiisip na Komunidad

Ang isang bansa, ayon kay Benedict Anderson, ay "isang imagined political community." Ayon kay Anderson, ang isang bansa ay "naiimagine dahil kahit na ang pinakamaliit na mamamayan ng bansa ay maaaring hindi kailanman nakakakilala sa karamihan ng kanilang mga kapwa mamamayan, nakikita sila, o naririnig man lamang sila, ngunit sa imahinasyon ng bawat tao ay nabubuhay ang ideya ng kanilang pakikipag-isa."

Ang mga miyembro ng komunidad ay malamang na hindi magkikita nang personal, ngunit maaari silang magbahagi ng mga interes o makilala sa parehong bansa. Ang mga miyembro ay may imahe ng kanilang pagkakaugnay sa kanilang mga utak. Halimbawa, ang pakiramdam ng pagiging kabilang sa iyong bansa na ibinabahagi mo sa ibang mga mamamayan kapag ang iyong "imagined community" ay nakikibahagi sa isang malaking kaganapan tulad ng Olympics.

Karagdagang paliwanag

Ang isang bansa ay isang komunidad dahil ito ay palaging nakikita bilang isang malakas, pahalang na pagkakaisa, sa kabila ng aktwal na hindi pagkakapantay-pantay at pagsasamantala na maaaring umiiral sa bawat isa. Sa huli, ang kapatirang ito ang nagbigay-daan sa milyun-milyong indibidwal sa nakalipas na dalawang siglo na kusang-loob na mamatay para sa gayong napipigilan na mga mithiin sa halip na pumatay lamang.

Dahil hindi siya naniniwala na ang teoryang liberal o Marxist ay sapat na inilarawan ang nasyonalismo, si Benedict Anderson ay bumuo ng kanyang sariling teorya. Kasama sina Ernest Gellner at Eric Hobsbawm, kabilang si Anderson sa "historicist" o "modernistang" paaralan ng nasyonalismo, na pinaniniwalaan na ang mga bansa at nasyonalismo ay mga produkto ng modernidad at binuo bilang mga kasangkapan para sa pagkamit ng mga layuning pampulitika at pang-ekonomiya. Ang primordialist na paaralan, na nagsasaad na ang mga bansa, kung hindi ang nasyonalismo, ay umiral na sa simula ng panahon, ay tinututulan ng paaralang ito. Ang mga naisip na komunidad ay maihahambing sa ideya ni Edward Said ng mga naisip na heograpiya sa mga tuntunin ng panlipunang konstruksiyon.

30. imagined (Anderson) one hundred words​


Answer:

Benedict Anderson, a scholar of Southeast Asia who transformed the study of nationalism by positing that nations were “imagined communities” that arose from the fateful interplay of capitalism and the printing press, died on Saturday night at a hotel in Batu, Indonesia. He was 79.

The death was confirmed by his friend Tariq Ali, who had worked with him at the journal New Left Review and at the publishing house Verso Books. The cause appeared to be heart failure, Mr. Ali said.

Dr. Anderson’s best-known book, “Imagined Communities: Reflections on the Origin and Spread of Nationalism,” first published in 1983, began with three paradoxes: Nationalism is a modern phenomenon, even though many people think of their nations as ancient and eternal; it is universal (everyone has a nation), even though each nation is supposedly utterly distinctive; and it is powerful (so much so that people will die for their countries), even though on close inspection it is hard to define.

Dr. Anderson believed that liberal and Marxist theorists had neglected to appreciate the power of nationalism.

Trying to fill that void, Dr. Anderson argued that nations emerged only after three beliefs were weakened: that elite languages (like Latin) offered unique access to truth about existence; that society was naturally organized around leaders who ruled through divine dispensation; and that the origins of the world and of humankind were essentially identical.
