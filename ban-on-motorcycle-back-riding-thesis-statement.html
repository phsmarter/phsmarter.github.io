7. do all the paragraphs discuss or refer back to the thesis statement​


Answer:

The thesis statement should remain flexible until the paper is actually finished. It ought to be one of the last things that we fuss with in the rewriting process. If we discover new information in the process of writing our paper that ought to be included in the thesis statement, then we'll have to rewrite our thesis statement. On the other hand, if we discover that our paper has done adequate work but the thesis statement appears to include things that we haven't actually addressed, then we need to limit that thesis statement. If the thesis statement is something that we needed prior approval for, changing it might require the permission of the instructor or thesis committee, but it is better to seek such permission than to write a paper that tries to do too much or that claims to do less than it actually accomplishes.

16. riding a motorcycle science​


Answer:

There's not much science to it. Practice is key. Find your own style. Most of the writers out there have been misled into thinking you have to counter steer to initiate a lean and that is an unnecessary complexity in place of a simple intuitive practice of leaning and holding the handlebars. This plain but effective control of a bike has feedback built in so that at low speed, you allow the bike some degree of steer with modest lean but as speeds increase, you lean farther and inhibit steering more. Keep your weight to the inside of the turn. This keeps the bike more upright. Your camber force is based on the angle dictated by the center of mass position. There is a gyroscopic moment that tends to upright the bike as you go through the corner and this is most effective at lowest lean. In a corner, the rear slip angle force is opposing the camber force so the bike needs that extra loading to stay balanced
