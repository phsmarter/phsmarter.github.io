15. Explain the quotation, “You are what you eat.” How is this related to keeping the digestive system in good condition? Write an essay about your explanation, relating it to some Diseases Associated with Nutrient Deficiency or problems regarding digestive system, your answer must be 1 to 2 paragraphs, each paragraphs need at least 4 sentences or more.


The saying “you are what you eat” rings true when it comes to maintaining good digestive health. Our digestive system is responsible for breaking down the food we eat, extracting nutrients, and eliminating waste. Eating a nutrient-rich and balanced diet is essential for keeping the digestive system functioning properly.

When we consume a diet high in processed foods, sugar, and unhealthy fats, we put our digestive health at risk. Nutrient deficiencies can occur, leading to various diseases and health problems such as irritable bowel syndrome (IBS), inflammatory bowel disease (IBD), and colon cancer.

For example, individuals who lack fiber in their diets may experience constipation and other digestive issues. Those deficient in vitamins and minerals such as iron and magnesium may experience fatigue and weakness. An inadequate intake of vitamins A and D can lead to vision problems and weakened bones.

In conclusion, it is crucial to consume a well-balanced diet rich in nutrients in order to maintain good digestive health. Our bodies rely on the nutrients we consume to function properly, and a diet lacking in essential vitamins, minerals, and fiber can lead to various diseases and health problems. Remember, “you are what you eat,” so make sure to nourish your body with healthy and wholesome foods.

During a 24-hour fast, you can only consume calorie-free beverages. When the 24-hour period is over, you can resume your normal intake of food until the next fast.

In addition to weight loss, intermittent fasting can have a positive effect on your metabolism, boost cardiovascular health, and more. It’s safe to use this approach once or twice a week to achieve your desired results.

Although this technique may seem easier than cutting back on daily calories, you may find yourself quite “hangry” on fasting days. It can also cause severe side effects or complications in people with certain health conditions.

You should always talk to your doctor before going on a fast. They can advise you on your individual benefits and risks. You’ll be well into your 24-hour period before your body realizes that you’re fasting.

During the first eight hours, your body will continue to digest your last intake of food. Your body will use stored glucose as energy and continue to function as though you’ll be eating again soon.

After eight hours without eating, your body will begin to use stored fats for energy. Your body will continue to use stored fat to create energy throughout the remainder of your 24-hour fast.

Fasts that last longer than 24 hours may lead to your body to start converting stored proteins into energy.
