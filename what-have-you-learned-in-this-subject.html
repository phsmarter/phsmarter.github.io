22. what have you learned about ethics subject​


What is Ethics?

Ethics investigates the philosophical foundations for our moral judgments in order to comprehend what is ethically right or wrong, just or unjust. In a broader sense, ethics takes into account how individuals relate to one another and the natural world as well as their own freedom, responsibility, and sense of justice.

Ethics, also referred to as moral philosophy, is the study of what is morally good and evil as well as morally right and wrong. This general term can refer to any ideology or system of moral goals or values.

Ethics addresses these concerns from all angles. Its key concerns are the nature of ultimate value and the standards used to judge whether human activities are good or bad. It focuses on the fundamental issues of practical decision-making.

What can I learn from Ethics?

A critical examination of one's own life and evaluation of actions, choices, and decisions are made possible by studying ethics. It enables a person to comprehend their true selves, what is best for them, and the steps required to get there. Our moral reasoning is strengthened by studying moral philosophy.

Our broad cognitive capacities can be developed by study of moral philosophy. It aids in the maturation of our moral reasoning and the development of rational, reasonable cognitive processes. Ethics is inevitable because humans are "social" beings by nature, which means they interact with other people and the natural world. Every action has an effect on other people, whether the person is conscious of it or not.
