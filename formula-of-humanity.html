If the speed of sound in air is 330 m/s, calculate the wavelength of the highestfrequency sound heard by humans. Use the following formula: wavelength = speed/frequency. Solution:

1) The speed of sound in still air is determined by

[tex]v=(331 + 0.6T/^\circ {\text{C})\text{ m/s}[/tex]

Given T = 28°C, the speed of sound in air is

[tex]v=(331 + 0.6(28^\circ {\text{C})/^\circ {\text{C})\text{ m/s}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{v=347.8\text{ m/s}}[/tex]

2) The maximum frequency sound heard by most humans is 20,000 Hz.

Using the formula,

[tex]\lambda=\dfrac{v}{f}[/tex]

where

λ = wavelength, v = speed, f = frequency

Given v = 330 m/s, f = 20000 Hz

[tex]\lambda=\dfrac{330\text{ m/s}}{20000 \text{ Hz}}\\\\\boxed{\lambda=0.0165\text{ m}}[/tex]

14. Learning Task 3: Solve the following problems below completely. (GIVEN, FORMULA, SOLUTION,ANSWER).1. Gold (Au) has been used to make ornamental objects and jewelry for thousands of years. Goldnuggets found in a stream are very easy to work and were probably one of the first metals used byhumans. (1 mol of Gold (Au) = 196.97g). Calculate the:A. Number of moles in a 95.0 g sample of a gold nugget.​


Solving for Number of Moles

Answer:

Given:

   mass of gold nugget (Au) = 95.0 g

   1 mol of Au = 196.97 g

Formula:

   mol of Au = mass x molar mass

Solution:

   mol of Au = 95.0 g Au x [tex]\frac{1 mol}{196.97 g}[/tex]

                     = 0. 48 mol Au

Step-by-step explanation:

The mole is the unit in the measurement of the amount of atoms, molecules, or compounds. Although it is not possible to directly measure how many moles a substance contains, we can derive its value by measuring its mass and then converting that number to moles. The molar mass of the element can be found in the periodic table of elements. If it is a compound, we just get the sum of the molar masses of the elements present in the compound. It is important to note that the subscripts indicate the number of atoms present, thus we multiply this to the mass of the element. After getting the molar masses of the element, we then add all these.

To convert mass (g) to moles, we make use of the molar mass as the conversion factor. By multiplying a given mass by the molar mass, the number of moles of the substance can be calculated and vice versa.
