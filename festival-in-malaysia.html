Answer:

Festivals are meant to rejoice wonderful moments and emotions in our lives with our loved ones as we were able to surpass different obstacles in the previous days or in the past. The celebration of a festival is also a creative way in expressing the glorious heritage, culture and traditions. It is in these celebrations wherein not only families but also the whole community come together as one and united.

Different countries have and celebrate their own festival. Asian countries also celebrate lots of festivals including Thailand. They have this so called Loi Krathong Festival also called as Thailand Lights Festival is known for creating beautiful displays of lights and is usually celebrated along with the Yee Peng Festival. Thus, Thailand has two Festivals of Lights that is best experienced in Chiang Mai. Yee Peng is different from Loi Krathong in that lit lanterns are released into the sky instead of onto water.

The Loi Krathong Festival is Thailand's annual water lantern festival that usually takes place during the day of full moon in November of the Thai lunar calendar celebrated nationwide. It is a Thai festival with thousands of lotus-shaped baskets with candles and flowers afloat on the rivers at night. It is celebrated to give thanks to the Goddess of Water for the rains. This celebration is also regarded as the culmination of the rainy season in Thailand. The Loi Krathong is believed as the time for asking forgiveness for any contamination of the waterways in the previous year. Some other highlights during this Thai festival include dance shows, performances, and parades, etc.
