21. essay about A time when you performed a good deed​


One day, I was on my way home from school when I saw an old lady struggling to carry a heavy bag. She was visibly tired and seemed to be in pain. I approached her and asked if I could help. She hesitated at first, but eventually agreed to let me help her.

I carried the heavy bag for her and we walked together for a few minutes. During that time, she shared with me some stories about her life and how she was coping with her health problems. I listened intently and showed genuine interest in what she had to say.

When we reached her house, she thanked me and said that I had made her day. I smiled and said that it was my pleasure to help her. As I walked away, I felt a sense of satisfaction and fulfillment knowing that I had made a positive impact on someone's life.

That experience taught me the value of kindness and the importance of helping others in need. It reminded me that even a small act of kindness can make a big difference in someone's life.

From that day on, I made a conscious effort to be more mindful of the people around me and to look for opportunities to perform good deeds. Whether it's helping a stranger carry their groceries or volunteering at a local charity, I believe that we all have the power to make the world a better place through small acts of kindness.

In conclusion, performing a good deed not only benefits the person receiving help but also gives the giver a sense of satisfaction and purpose. It teaches us to be more empathetic, compassionate, and kind to one another. I encourage everyone to take the time to help others in need, as it is a simple yet powerful way to make a positive impact on the world.

29. Compose a short essay with 3-5 pharagraphs on the idea of the "three acts of goodness- think good thoughts, speak good words, and do good deeds and how this is related to personal care and safely.


Answer:

The concept of the "three acts of goodness" is a fundamental principle in many cultures and religions, emphasizing the importance of thinking positively, speaking kindly, and performing good deeds. These acts are interconnected and play a vital role in personal care and safety.

Thinking good thoughts involves being mindful of our inner dialogue and cultivating a positive mindset. This can help reduce stress and anxiety, promote self-confidence, and enhance overall well-being. Positive thinking can also influence our behavior and actions, encouraging us to make better choices for our health and safety.

Speaking good words involves being mindful of our communication and choosing words that uplift and encourage others. It is essential to be kind and compassionate in our speech, especially when interacting with those who may be going through a challenging time. Speaking positively can also influence our own attitudes and behaviors, promoting a more positive outlook on life.

Doing good deeds involves actively seeking opportunities to help others and make a positive impact in the world. Whether it's volunteering in our communities or helping a friend in need, performing good deeds can bring a sense of purpose and fulfillment. It can also promote a sense of connection with others and foster a more positive and caring society.

Overall, the three acts of goodness are essential to personal care and safety. By thinking positively, speaking kindly, and performing good deeds, we can promote a healthier, happier, and safer environment for ourselves and those around us. It starts with us, and every small act of kindness can make a significant difference in someone's life.
