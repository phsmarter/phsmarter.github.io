1. In this type of essay the writer tells a story about a real life experience


Answer:

narrative essay

Explanation:

A narrative essay tells a story. In most cases, this is a story about a personal experience you had. This type of essay, along with the descriptive essay, allows you to get personal and creative, unlike most academic writing.

Answer:

Narrative Essay

Explanation:

Narrative Essay: Telling a Story

- In a narrative essay, the writer tells a story about a real-life experience.

Descriptive Essay: Painting a Picture

- A writer might describe a person, place, object, or even memory of special significance. In a descriptive essay, the writer should show, not tell, through the use of colorful words and sensory details.

Expository Essay: Just the Facts

- The expository essay is an informative piece of writing that presents a balanced analysis of a topic. In an expository essay, the writer explains or defines a topic, using facts, statistics, and examples.

Persuasive Essay: Convince Me

- The goal of the persuasive essay is to convince the reader to accept the writer’s point of view or recommendation.

source: https://www.time4writing.com/writing-resources/types-of-essays/

Answer:

My New-found Interest

A few years ago, I have discovered that I like reading books, especially ones that are more obscure and unique. I discovered this when I visited our school library for some reference material. I do not usually go to the library, as almost all information that I need can already be found on the internet. But during that time, I needed to have a hardcopy reference. Therefore, I spent some time in the library. Who knew that it could actually lead it to my love for reading? I did not realize yet that time it was actually the beginning.

The Magical Moment

I remember that I was looking for a particular science book for my reference. It was crunch time already with my report, so I must find that science reference. However, while I was looking, I came across this old book about a young girl and her desire to have a swimming pool in their backyard. The book cover was nondescript, just an image of a young girl in pig tails. It was hard bound with olive green color and smelled really old and musty. I am not sure why I started reading it, but I actually did! And I was so excited about it! Eventually I was able to find my science reference and borrowed my new-found book together with it.

My Future Career?

Until today I kept asking myself, why did I ever get interested to open that book in the library? Was there some magical moment or some divine intervention leading me to that book? But in any case, that incident got me started into reading. After that, I would visit the library to look for other similar books. Furthermore, it developed in me an inclination to look online for books with good reviews. And I get to spend many days in bookstores in malls. There are bookstores that allow people to stay inside, lounge in the store, and read some books. I like doing that in some of my free time. Also, I have this feeling that I must at least read one physical book for every five online books I read. I also started following online writing platforms and fanfiction websites. I needed to see which ones are new releases and if they are actually good. Nowadays I am getting interested in pursuing creative writing. I want to learn how to be a creative writer. Who knows! One day that might be a career I am willing to take in the future.

As a student, I have faced many challenges throughout my academic journey. From struggling with difficult coursework to dealing with personal issues, I have learned that life as a student can be challenging at times. However, I have also learned that it is possible to overcome these challenges and come out stronger for it.

One of the biggest challenges I have faced as a student was when I was taking a particularly difficult math class. The material was complex and I found myself struggling to understand the concepts. I spent countless hours studying and trying to grasp the material, but nothing seemed to click. I felt defeated and was ready to give up.

But I didn't give up, instead I sought help. I went to my professor's office hours and asked for extra help. I also formed a study group with my classmates and we helped each other to understand the material. I also found a tutor who helped me to work through the problems in a way that made sense to me.

Through hard work and determination, I was able to improve my understanding of the material and even ended up getting a good grade in the class. That experience taught me that it's okay to struggle and ask for help when you need it.

Another challenge I faced as a student was dealing with personal issues. I was going through a difficult time in my life and found it hard to focus on my studies. I was constantly feeling down and unmotivated. But I realized that I needed to take care of myself in order to be able to succeed as a student. I sought support from friends and family, and I also made an effort to take care of my mental and physical health.

Through it all, I learned that challenges are a part of life, and that it is how we deal with them that makes the difference. I learned that I can overcome any obstacle if I am willing to put in the work and ask for help when I need it.

In conclusion, as a student, I have faced many challenges, but I have also learned valuable lessons that have helped me to become a stronger and more resilient person. I know that there will be more challenges in the future, but I am confident that I have the tools to overcome them and succeed in my academic journey.
