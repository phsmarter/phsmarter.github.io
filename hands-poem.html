28. A short poem about different hand tools in horticulture.


Answer:

In the garden, tools abound,

For every task, the right one's found.

The trowel digs in the dirt so neat,

While pruning shears make plants complete.

The rake is handy for fallen leaves,

And wheelbarrows carry heavy heaves.

A hoe can shape the garden bed,

While watering cans give life ahead.

Secateurs snip and trim with care,

And gloves protect hands from wear and tear.

The dibber pokes holes in soil so firm,

And a cultivator breaks up the germ.

With spades and forks, the earth is turned,

And compost bins, nutrients earned.

So many tools for horticulture's art,

Each playing a role, and playing a part.

In gardens lush and green,

Where flowers bloom with grace,

The hand tools of horticulture,

Are the gardener's trusted base.

With pruning shears and scissors,

And gloves to keep hands clean,

The gardener shapes the bushes,

And cuts the foliage keen.

The trowel and the dibble,

Are also tools of note,

As they help to plant the seeds,

And bulbs that will soon denote.

The rake and the hoe as well,

Are essential to the task,

As they help to clear the weeds,

And loosen the soil with a cast.

And when the day is done,

And the garden is at rest,

The hand tools of horticulture,

Are put away, feeling their best.
