17. Differentiate a monist state from a dualist state of International Law.​


Answer:

Specifically, monist theory prioritizes the desirability of a formal international legal order to establish the rule of law among nations, while dualist theory prioritizes the notions of individual self-determination and sovereignty at the state level.

Explanation:

Dualists emphasize the difference between national and international law, and require the translation of the latter into the former. Without this translation, international law does not exist as law. ... The supremacy of international law is a rule in dualist systems as it is in monist systems.

Definition of the following theism:

⇒ Monistic

Monism is the metaphysical view that all is of one essential essence, substance, or energy.

⇒ Monotheistic

Monotheism is the belief that there is only one deity, an all-supreme universally referred to as God.

⇒ Polytheistic

Polytheism is the belief in multiple deities, usually assembled into a pantheon of gods and goddesses, along with their own religious sects and rituals.

⇒ Agnostic

Agnosticism is the view or belief that the existence of God, of the divine or the supernatural, is unknown or unknowable.

⇒ Atheistic

A person who does not believe in the existence of a god or any gods

Answer:

- Origin of the word "ideal"

The word "ideal" originated from the Late Latin word "idealis" which means "existing in idea" or "concerned with ideas".

- Meaning of the word "ideal"

The word "ideal" refers to a standard of perfection or excellence that one aspires to achieve or attain.

- Advantages and disadvantages of the word "ideal"

The advantage of having an ideal is that it provides a clear vision or goal to work towards, which can motivate and inspire individuals to improve and achieve their full potential. However, the disadvantage of having an ideal is that it can lead to unrealistic expectations and disappointment if one is unable to meet or attain the ideal standard.

- Example of the word "ideal"

An example of the word "ideal" is a person striving to become the ideal employee by consistently meeting and exceeding their job responsibilities, collaborating well with others, and continually improving their skills and knowledge.
