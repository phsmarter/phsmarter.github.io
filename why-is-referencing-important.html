2. why referencing ins important​


Answer:

Referencing allows you to acknowledge the contribution of other writers and researchers in your work. Any university assignments that draw on the ideas, words or research of other writers must contain citations. Referencing is also a way to give credit to the writers from whom you have borrowed words and ideas.


3. Why referencing is important? (research)​


Answer:

Academic work necessitates the use of references. It contextualizes your work, illustrates the breadth and depth of your research, and acknowledges the work of others. When you use someone else's concept, make sure to credit them.

Good referencing exhibits academic integrity by demonstrating what you have read and researched broadly, allowing the reader to discover the sources referred to in your project. It also supports and reinforces your thesis.

Attention to detail, such as precise page numbering, author spelling, and the accuracy of essential facts that will be conveyed in the paper, are all important aspects of good referencing. Paying attention to reference not only improves your research skills, but it also improves your reputation with editors, reviewers, and readers.

13. It is a type of cell referencing that uses the dollar sign ($) before the cell addresses in the formula that you do not want to change. A. Absolute Referencing B. Mixed Referencing C. Relative Referencing D. Total Referencing


Answer:

A.Absolute references

Explanation:

You can use an absolute reference to keep a row and/or column constant. An absolute reference is designated in a formula by the addition of a dollar sign ($) before the column and row. If it precedes the column or row (but not both), it's known as a mixed reference.
