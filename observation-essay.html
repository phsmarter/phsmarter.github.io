4. compare an essay observing the use of modals & Conditionals...​


Answer:

An essay observing the use of modals would likely focus on the various ways in which modal verbs (such as "can," "could," "may," "might," "must," etc.) are used to express different degrees of possibility, ability, necessity, and permission. The essay would analyze the different meanings conveyed by each modal and provide examples of their use in context.

An essay observing the use of conditionals, on the other hand, would focus on the different ways in which conditional statements (such as "if X, then Y") are used to express hypothetical or possible situations and their consequences. The essay would analyze the different types of conditionals (such as zero, first, second, and third conditionals) and provide examples of their use in context. It would also examine how conditionals are used to express future and past possibilities and their effects on how the statement is perceived by a reader or a listener.

Both essays would aim to demonstrate how the use of modals and conditionals affect the meaning of the sentence and how it is perceived by the reader or listener.

8. Write an essay explaining the importance of microscope in making observations. ​


Answer:

A microscope is an instrument that is used to magnify small objects. Some microscopes can even be used to observe an object at the cellular level, allowing scientists to see the shape of a cell, its nucleus, mitochondria, and other organelles.

Answer:

A microscope is an instrument that is used to magnify small objects. Some microscopes can even be used to observe an object at the cellular level, allowing scientists to see the shape of a cell, its nucleus, mitochondria, and other organelles. While the modern microscope has many parts, the most important pieces are its lenses. It is through the microscope’s lenses that the image of an object can be magnified and observed in detail. A simple light microscope manipulates how light enters the eye using a convex lens, where both sides of the lens are curved outwards. When light reflects off of an object being viewed under the microscope and passes through the lens, it bends towards the eye. This makes the object look bigger than it actually is.
